'''Michael P. Delaney''', better known by his stage name '''Dub-L''', is an American record producer, recording artist and filmmaker.

==Early life==
Delaney was born and raised in the [[East Village, Manhattan|East Village]] in [[New York City]].<ref name="urbansmarts">[http://www.urbansmarts.com/interviews/dubl.htm “Dub-L interview,”] Urban Smarts, November 10, 2004.</ref><ref name="apatil">Adwait Patil, [http://www.redbull.com/in/en/music/stories/1331675885067/in-conversation-with-dub-l "In Conversation With Dub L,"] RedBull.com, September 2, 2014.</ref> He began writing graffiti in 1991.<ref name="apatil"/> The name Dub-L comes from his last name, Delaney, which he shortened to Dell for his tag name in high school when he was doing graffiti. People started calling him Double-L, which turned into Dub-L.<ref name="africas">[http://www.africasgateway.com/2003/10/14/interview-with-dub-l-everybody-gather-around-the-dubs-in-town/ “Interview With Dub L – Everybody Gather Around – The Dubs In Town,”] africasgateway.com, October 14, 2003.</ref>

==Career==
===Music===
Dub-L produced [[Aesop Rock]]'s first album, ''[[Music for Earthworms]]'', in 1997. Dub-L has said that he is in possession of the masters but will not re-release it, because he likes the fact that it's "rare and legendary."<ref name="urbansmarts"/>

Also in 1997, Dub-L met fellow New York City native and DJ JS-1, who was a member of the [[Rock Steady Crew]]. They immediately began collaborating as Ground Original, releasing their debut album in 2002, also called ''Ground Original'', which featured well-known MCs such as [[KRS-One]], [[Masta Ace]], [[Kool G Rap]] and [[Big Daddy Kane]], as well as underground MCs such as [[C-Rayz Walz]] and [[Planet Asia]].<ref name="mcusenza">Michael Cusenza, “Double Standard: Independent Hip-Hop’s Dynamic Duo,” ''Tablist'', Vol. 2, Issue 1, 2005.</ref><ref>Leah Rose, “Ill Techniques: DJ JS-1,” ''[[XXL (magazine)|XXL]]'', June 2003.</ref> They released ''Claimstake'' the next year, which included appearances from [[Common (rapper)|Common]], [[J-Live]] and [[Jeru the Damaja]].<ref name="mcusenza"/>

In 2003, Dub-L released his debut solo album, ''Dub-L's In Town'',<ref name="africas"/> following it up in 2004 with his second album, ''Day of the Mega Beast!'' It features guest appearances from artists including [[Rahzel]], [[Professor X]], [[Hangar 18 (band)|Hangar 18]], DJ JS-1 and [[MF Grimm]].<ref>HeneC, “Dub-L: Day of the Mega Beast,” ''[[YRB Magazine|YRB]]'', Issue #46, October 2004, p. 108.</ref><ref>Joshua Glazer, “dub-L: Day of the Mega Beast,” ''[[URB (magazine)|URB]]'', January/February 2005, p. 95.</ref><ref name="ugrap">[http://www.ugrap.de/interview.php?id=51 “Interviews: dub-L,”] ugrap.de, April 2005.</ref> Dub-L produced and rapped on his two solo albums.<ref name="ugrap"/>

Dub-L's other work includes the trip hop group The Controls, which he formed with singer Ann Colville in 1998, putting out their debut album the following year. An instrumental version of the album was released in 2012.<ref>Neil Drumming, [http://www.washingtoncitypaper.com/articles/17172/haphazardous-material “Music Review,”] ''[[Washington City Paper]]'', April 23, 1999.</ref> In 2004, he produced two tracks from [[MF Doom]]'s second Viktor Vaughn album, ''[[Venomous Villain]]''.<ref name="ugrap"/> He has remixed artists including [[Jennifer Lopez]] and [[Jessica Simpson]].<ref name="apatil"/> Dub-L and [[DJ Cassidy]] occasionally team up as Cass & Dubs; in 2008, they produced all of O'Neal McKnight's singles, including "Check Your Coat", featuring [[Greg Nice]].<ref>“Singles,” ''URB'', January/February 2009, p. 110.</ref> After disbanding Cass & Dubs in 2012, he and rapper Chase Phoenix formed the group Monday Morning High.<ref name="apatil"/>

===Film===
His short film ''Baby Show'', based on his [[Public-access television]] comedy sketches from the late-1990s, was an official selection at the 2016 International Filmmaker Festival of World Cinema Nice, where Delaney won Best Editing of a Short Film<ref>[http://filmfestinternational.com/nice-iff-2016-awards-winners/ "Nice IFF 2016 Award Winners,"] Film Fest International, June 2016.</ref> and was nominated for Best Comedy.<ref>[http://filmfestinternational.com/best-comedy-nice-iff-2016/ "Best Comedy,"] Film Festival International, March 2016.</ref> It was also selected for the NewFilmmakers New York Spring 2016 festival, winning the Audience Choice Award for Best Short Film.<ref>[http://newfilmmakersonline.com/movie-download/148635,8028/Michael-Delaney-BABY-SHOW---EPISODE-ONE "Baby Show – Episode One,"] newfilmmakersonline.com. Accessed October 5, 2016.</ref>
