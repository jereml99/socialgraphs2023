'''Kimetrius Christopher Foose''' (born 1997 or 1998),<ref name=genius/> known professionally as '''Lil Skies''', is an American rapper and singer from [[Waynesboro, Pennsylvania]] signed to All We Got Entertainment through [[Atlantic Records]]. 

His three highest charting tracks on the [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]] are "[[I (Lil Skies song)|I]]" (number 39), "[[Nowadays]]" (number 55), and "[[Red Roses (song)|Red Roses]]" (number 69). His major label debut mixtape, ''[[Life of a Dark Rose]]'', was released in 2018 and peaked at number 10 on the [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]] chart. The mixtape was certified Platinum by the [[RIAA]] in 2020.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.riaa.com/gold-platinum/?share=19182&t=t|title=LIL SKIES – LIFE OF A DARK ROSE|website=RIAA}}</ref> He released his debut studio album, [[Shelby (album)|''Shelby'']], in 2019. He followed it up by releasing his second studio album, ''[[Unbothered]]'', in 2021.

== Early life ==
Kimetrius Christopher Foose was born in 1997 or 1998 in [[Chambersburg, Pennsylvania]].<ref name=genius>{{cite news | last=Mench| first=Chris| title =From Waynesboro To The Web: Lil Skies Is On The Verge Of Breaking Out| date =December 5, 2017 | url =https://genius.com/a/from-waynesboro-to-the-web-lil-skies-is-on-the-verge-of-breaking-out|website=Genius.com| access-date =February 12, 2018}}</ref><ref name="Stokey">{{cite news| last =Adams| first =Joi S.| title =New TSP Artist Alert: Lil Skies Releases Video For "Some Way"| publisher =The Stokey Project| date =April 1, 2017| url =https://www.thestokeyproject.com/single-post/2017/04/01/New-TSP-Artist-Alert-Lil-Skies-Releases-Video-For-%E2%80%9CSome-Way%E2%80%9D| access-date =February 12, 2018}}{{dead link|date=January 2019 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}</ref> He began [[Freestyle rap|freestyling]] at age three. He was introduced to music by his father, Michael Burton Jr., a hip hop artist formerly known as Dark Skies, now as BurntMan, and his mother Shelby Foose. Foose's stage name is a play on his father's.<ref name="Elevator">{{cite news | last=McCarthy| first=Liam| title =Going Up w/ Lil Skies| work =Elevator Magazine| date = September 5, 2017| url =http://www.elevatormag.com/going-up-w-lil-skies/| access-date =February 12, 2018}}</ref><ref name="ShipNC">{{cite news | last=Bonura| first=Denise| title =Reaching for the stars: Lil Skies to open for Fetty Wap | newspaper =Shippensburg News Chronicle| date =October 20, 2016 | url =http://www.shipnc.com/news/local/article_0b5d2f38-9639-11e6-9a86-bf8da9fc1cd0.html| access-date =February 12, 2018}}</ref> Foose and his family moved to [[Waynesboro, Pennsylvania]] when he was in third grade.<ref name="Stokey" /> When he was 11, his father was injured in a workplace chemical explosion at the Rust-Oleum plant near [[Williamsport, Maryland]].<ref>{{Cite web |last=Herald |first=Denise Bonura/The Record |title=Michael Burton Jr. of Waynesboro emerges stronger after suffering third-degree burns in Rust-Oleum chemical explosion |url=https://www.therecordherald.com/story/news/accident/2014/03/28/michael-burton-jr-waynesboro-emerges/37930735007/ |access-date=2022-02-26 |website=Waynesboro Record Herald |language=en-US}}</ref>

== Career ==
===2016–2018: Career beginnings and ''Life of a Dark Rose''===
Skies claims he used to have to sell marijuana and work "little jobs" to pay for studio time and music videos.<ref name="youtube.com">{{Cite web|title=Lil Skies on 'Shelby', Dealing w/ Depression, XXXTentacion + More! - YouTube|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dc5I72Z7YDE|access-date=December 16, 2020|website=www.youtube.com}}</ref> He also worked at a McDonald's and a Japanese restaurant in his hometown.<ref name="youtube.com" /> In the meantime, he would spend the summer at a relative's house; it was at that point in time he worked in collaboration with his dad on his first project released on August 14, entitled, "Birth of Skies Vol. 1". Foose and his father would write an album the following year, ''Father-Son Talk'', discussing the recovery process.<ref name="RH">{{cite news | last=Bonura| first=Denise| title =Michael Burton Jr. of Waynesboro emerges stronger after suffering third-degree burns in Rust-Oleum chemical…| newspaper =The Record Herald| date =March 28, 2014 | url =http://www.therecordherald.com/article/20140328/news/140329752| access-date =February 12, 2018}}</ref> Foose graduated from [[Waynesboro Area Senior High School]] in 2016<ref name="RH2">{{cite news | title =Waynesboro Area Senior High School Class of 2016| newspaper =The Record Herald| date =May 27, 2016| url =http://www.therecordherald.com/article/20160527/NEWS/160529855| access-date =February 12, 2018}}</ref> and briefly attended [[Shippensburg University of Pennsylvania]] before dropping out to focus on his rap career.<ref name="Genius">{{cite news | last=Mench| first=Chris| title =From Waynesboro To The Web: Lil Skies Is On The Verge Of Breaking Out| date =December 5, 2017 | url =https://genius.com/a/from-waynesboro-to-the-web-lil-skies-is-on-the-verge-of-breaking-out| access-date =February 12, 2018}}</ref> His musical inspirations are [[Lil Wayne]], [[Eminem]], [[Wiz Khalifa]], [[The Offspring]], [[Punchline (band)|Punchline]], [[MxPx]], [[Audiovent]] and [[50 Cent]].<ref>{{Cite magazine|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/hip-hop/8240344/lil-skies-interview-nowadays-rapper-video|title = Get to Know 'Nowadays' Rapper Lil Skies|magazine = [[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|date = March 12, 2018}}</ref>

In October 2016, Foose opened for [[Fetty Wap]] at Shippensburg University<ref name="ShipNC" /> and later released his first mixtape, ''Alone'', in January 2017.<ref name="Drill">{{cite web | last=Jackson| first=Shawn| title =Lil Skies Drops 'Alone' An AyRap Delicacy| website=Drill Kings| date =January 25, 2017 | url =https://www.drillking.org/lil-skies-drops-alone-an-ayrap-delicacy/| access-date =February 12, 2018}}</ref> In July 2017, he released two songs, "Red Roses" featuring Landon Cube<ref name="DrillRoses">{{cite web | last=Jackson| first=Shawn| title =Lil Skies Teams Up With Landon Cube For 'Red Roses'| website=Drill Kings| date =July 24, 2017 | url =https://www.drillking.org/lil-skies-teams-up-with-landon-cube-for-red-roses/| access-date =February 12, 2018}}</ref> and "Off The Goop" featuring Sprite Lee.<ref name="ElevatorSprite">{{cite news | last=Hef| first=Hollywood| title =Lil Skies & Sprite Lee unleash catchy new jam "Off The Goop"| work=Elevator Magazine| date =July 17, 2017 | url =http://www.elevatormag.com/lil-skies-sprite-lee-unleash-catchy-new-jam-off-the-goop/| access-date =February 12, 2018}}</ref> He would later release other songs that year, including "Rude" and "Signs of Jealousy."<ref name="Complex">{{cite news | last=Ortiz| first=Edwin| title =Premiere: Lil Skies Stunts in "Signs of Jealousy" Video| work=Complex| date =November 14, 2017 | url =http://www.complex.com/music/2017/11/lil-skies-signs-of-jealousy-video| access-date =February 12, 2018}}</ref>

===2019: ''Shelby''===
{{Main|Shelby (album)}}
On March 1, 2019, Lil Skies released his album ''Shelby'', named after his mother, while simultaneously debuting the music video for lead single "[[I (Lil Skies song)|I]]".<ref>{{cite magazine|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/hip-hop/8500617/lil-skies-shelby-album-stream|title=Lil Skies Surprise Drops Debut Album 'Shelby' Featuring Gunna, Gucci Mane: Listen|date=March 1, 2019|last=Saponara|first=Michael|access-date=March 5, 2019|magazine=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]}}</ref> He made a short documentary for the album on his YouTube channel by interviewing her and surprising her during it by telling her he was going to name it after her.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|title=Lil Skies – Shelby (The Documentary) – YouTube|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qJk0fvt6jBs|access-date=December 16, 2020|website=www.youtube.com}}</ref> On May 21, 2019, Lil Skies released a video for "Breathe", a song from his album, ''Shelby''.<ref name="Top40-Charts">{{cite web |url=https://top40-charts.com/news.php?nid=146821 |title=Lil Skies Drops New Video For 'Breathe' |website=Top40-Charts.com |access-date=May 22, 2019}}</ref> On July 14, 2019, Lil Skies collaborated with rapper [[Machine Gun Kelly (musician)|Machine Gun Kelly]] on the song "Burning Memories".

===2020–present: ''Unbothered''===
{{Main|Unbothered}}
On March 4, 2020, Skies released the song "[[Havin My Way]]", which features close friend [[Lil Durk]] and serves as the lead single from his second studio album, ''[[Unbothered]]'' (2021).<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/lil-skies-and-lil-durk-dont-miss-on-havin-my-way-new-song.1986406.html|title=Lil Skies & Lil Durk Don't Miss On "Havin My Way"|last=Zidel|first=Alex|date=March 4, 2020|website=HotNewHipHop|access-date=March 20, 2020}}</ref> It was followed by the second single, "Riot", which was released on May 14, 2020.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/lil-skies-is-ready-to-riot-new-song.1987207.html|title=Lil Skies Is Ready To "Riot"|publisher=HotNewHipHop|last=Lilah|first=Rose|date=May 14, 2020|access-date=December 16, 2020}}</ref> "On Sight", the third single, was released on November 6, 2020.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://rhymehiphop.com/lil-skies-on-sight-stream-video/|title=Lil Skies - "On Sight" [STREAM + VIDEO]|publisher=Rhyme Hip Hop|last=Pickus|first=Brandon|date=November 6, 2020|access-date=December 16, 2020}}</ref> On July 10, 2020, he released the track "Red & Yellow" from the ''[[F9 (film)|Road to Fast 9]]'' mixtape.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://music.apple.com/us/album/red-yellow-from-road-to-fast-9-mixtape-single/1522554118|title=Red & Yellow (From Road To Fast 9 Mixtape) – Single by Lil Skies on Apple Music|date=July 9, 2020|via=[[Apple Music]]|access-date=July 11, 2020}}</ref> On December 16, 2020, Foose announced ''Unbothered'' and its complete details along with the single "Ok". The album was released on January 22, 2021.

== Personal life ==
Foose resides in Waynesboro, Pennsylvania. In July 2019, Foose and his longtime girlfriend, Jacey Fugate, had a son.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qJk0fvt6jBs|title=Lil Skies – Shelby (The Documentary)|last=Lil Skies|date=March 13, 2019|via=YouTube}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.fuse.tv/videos/2019/05/lil-skies-mind-massage-interview|title = Fuse|website = [[Fuse (TV channel)|Fuse]]}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.pinterest.ca/pin/350858627220951247/|title=Baby skies &#124; Lil skies, Cute rappers, Lil pump}}</ref>

Foose cites his own song "[[Red Roses (song)|Red Roses]]" as his personal favorite song, referring to the musical combination with him and Landon Cube as "epic".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.elevatormag.com/going-up-w-lil-skies/|title=Going Up w/ Lil Skies|date=September 6, 2017}}</ref> He cites [[Lil Wayne]] as his biggest music inspiration.<ref name="highsnobiety.com">{{cite web|url=https://www.highsnobiety.com/p/lil-skies-interivew/|title=Lil Skies Talks His Favorite Tattoo & Touring With Lil Uzi|date=December 28, 2017|website=Highsnobiety}}</ref>

Foose tries to avoid drugs as much as possible after seeing it negatively impact the lives of his old friends.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://djbooth.net/features/2018-02-21-lil-skies-scared-of-drugs|title=Lil Skies Explains Why He Avoids Lean & Xanax: "I'm Scared, Bro"|first=Brian "Z"|last=Zisook|website=DJBooth|date=October 13, 2018 }}</ref>

In an interview, Skies claims if he was not rapping, he would like to do some "[[Bear Grylls]] type, outdoor stuff" and that his favorite hobby is fishing.<ref>{{cite web|access-date=December 16, 2020|title=Mom Interviews Lil Skies – YouTube|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=FMpoY9yozp4|website=www.youtube.com}}</ref>
