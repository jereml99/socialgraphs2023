'''Al Sherrod Lambert''' (pronounced ''æl ʃɛrəd læmbərt''; born June 29, 1985), sometimes known as simply '''A-Rod''', is a [[Grammy Award]] winning American producer, singer, songwriter, and musician.<ref name="bloomfield.edu">{{cite web|url=https://bloomfield.edu/about-us/news/grammy-nominated-bloomfield-college-alumnus-speaks-freshman-class|title=Grammy Nominated Bloomfield College Alumnus Speaks to Freshman Class - Bloomfield College|website=bloomfield.edu}}</ref> He has won recognition as a gospel, pop, hip hop and R&B producer. He is signed to [[Kobalt Music Group]]<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.kobaltmusic.com/roster/all|title=All Roster|last=Kobalt|date=February 1, 2019|website=Kobalt}}</ref> and is a registered [[Broadcast Music, Inc.|BMI]] songwriter.<ref name="bmi.com">{{cite web|url=https://www.bmi.com/news/entry/meet_a-rod_the_bmi_songwriter_behind_fantasias_new_hit_album|title=Meet A-Rod, the BMI Songwriter Behind Fantasia's New Hit Album|date=May 10, 2013|website=BMI.com}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Lambert was born in [[Newark, New Jersey]]. He grew up in nearby [[Irvington, New Jersey|Irvington]] and attended [[Irvington High School (New Jersey)|Irvington High School]]<ref>{{cite web|url=http://irvingtonhighschool.net/irvington-nj/class-of-2003.html|title=Irvington High School Class of 2003|website=irvingtonhighschool.net}}</ref><ref>Sykes, Chris. [https://essexnewsdaily.com/headlne-news/ihs-2016-graduation-builds-on-tradition "IHS 2016 graduation builds on tradition"], ''Essex News Daily'', June 30, 2016. Accessed April 6, 2022. "Irvington High School held its 137th commencement ceremony in the Richard Codey Arena in West Orange on Thursday, June 23. Keia Smith was the valedictorian of the Irvington High School Class of 2016, and the guest speaker was music writer, producer and Irvington High School alumnus Al Sherrod Lambert."</ref> and [[Bloomfield College]] where he graduated in 2008.<ref name="bloomfield.edu"/> In 2010 Lambert moved to Los Angeles where he maintains residency with his wife and daughter.<ref name="bloomfield.edu"/>

==Career==

===Producing and songwriting===
Lambert started his music career in 2010 when he worked on the songs "Sleeping Pills" and "All Said and Done" on the [[Jamie Foxx]] album ''[[Best Night of My Life]]'' with fellow New Jersey producer and songwriter [[Eric Hudson]].<ref name="bmi.com" /><ref name="ratedrnb.com">{{Cite web | url=http://ratedrnb.com/2014/06/interview-songwriter-al-sherrod-lambert-talks-michelle-williams-new-album-lesson-learned-sevyn-streeter/ | title=INTERVIEW: Songwriter Al-Sherrod Lambert Talks Michelle Williams' New Album + Lesson Learned from Sevyn Streeter| date=June 18, 2014}}</ref> The Following year he wrote "[[25/8 (song)|25/8]]" for [[Mary J Blige]]  as well as the title track for the film ''[[Think Like a Man]]''. The song was co written by [[Harmony Samuels]] and [[Eric Bellinger]] and features the vocals of [[Jennifer Hudson]], [[Ne Yo]], and [[Rick Ross]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.allmusic.com/song/think-like-a-man-mt0043958569|title=Think Like a Man - Jennifer Hudson, Ne-Yo, Harmony Samuels - Song Info|website=AllMusic}}</ref>

In 2013, Lambert wrote several songs for the [[Fantasia Barrino]] album ''[[Side Effects of You]]''. The album was nominated for a [[Grammy Award]] for [[Best Urban Contemporary Album]] at the 52nd Annual [[Grammy Awards]], as well as a nomination for [[Best Traditional R&B Performance]] for the single "Get it Right" and a nomination for [[Best R&B Song]] for "[[Without Me (Fantasia Barrino song)|Without Me]]". That same year he received two Billboard Pop Awards at the [[BMI Awards]] for his work on the [[Ariana Grande]] singles "[[The Way (Ariana Grande song)|The Way]]" and "[[Right There (Ariana Grande song)|Right There]]". [[The Way (Ariana Grande song)|"The Way]]" peaked at #9 on the [[Billboard hot 100]] and spent 28 weeks on the charts.<ref>{{cite magazine|url=https://www.billboard.com/music/ariana-grande|title=Ariana Grande The Way Chart History|magazine=Billboard}}</ref> The song is [[RIAA]] certified double platinum. [[Right There (Ariana Grande song)|"Right There]]" peaked at #84 on the [[Billboard Hot 100]] and spent 8 weeks on the charts.<ref>{{cite magazine|url=https://www.billboard.com/music/ariana-grande|title=Ariana Grande Right There Chart History|magazine=Billboard}}</ref> The single is [[RIAA]] certified gold.

In 2014 Lamber co-wrote the [[Michelle Williams (singer)|Michelle Williams]] song "Say Yes". The song features backing vocals by former [[Destiny's Child]] members [[Kelly Rowland]]  and [[Beyonce Knowles]]. The song peaked at #1, staying on the [[Billboard hot 100|Billboard Hot 100]] chart for 38 weeks.<ref>{{cite magazine|url=https://www.billboard.com/music/michelle-williams|title=Michelle Williams Say Yes Chart History|magazine=Billboard}}</ref> It was nominated for a [[Soul Train Award]] for best Gospel/Inspirational Song, and received four nominations at the 2015 [[Stellar Awards]] including a win for Music Video of the Year.

In 2017 Lambert was a songwriter on the [[Lalah Hathaway]] album ''Lalah Hathaway Live'' which received a [[Grammy Award]] for R&B Album of the Year.<ref name="grammy.com">{{cite web|url=https://www.grammy.com/grammys/news/making-something-learn-lalah-hathaways-special-talent|title=How did Lalah Hathaway make 'Something'?|date=March 3, 2014|publisher=National Academy of Recording Arts and Sciences}}</ref> The album peaked at #2 on the [[Billboard Hot 100]] and stayed on the charts for 12 weeks.<ref>{{cite magazine|url=https://www.billboard.com/music/lalah-hathaway|title=Lalah Hathaway Chart History|magazine=Billboard}}</ref> He also wrote the single "Sexy Body" by [[Pitbull (rapper)|Pitbull]],<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.allmusic.com/song/sexy-body-mt0054134992|title=Sexy Body - Jennifer Lopez, Pitbull - Song Info|website=AllMusic}}</ref> and the singles "What is Love" and "Good Woman" on the [[La'Porsha Renae]] album ''[[Already All Ready]]''. 
Later Lambert would keep his hit songwriting and producitin career moving full steam ahead by co-producing “March 14”’on Drakes Scorpion album, co writing “Made for Now” for the legendary Janet Jackson and co—writing “Borderline” for r and b superstar Brandy in 2020.
In 2021 Lambert would become a main writer for the legendary [[Ronald Isley]] and [[The Isley Brothers]] penning three songs for the Rock & Roll hall of famers. 
He now is releasing his on music under his artist name POINTS for his record label Points On The Board.
