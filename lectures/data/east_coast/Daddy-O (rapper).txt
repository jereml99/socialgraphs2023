'''Glenn K. Bolton''', widely known as '''Daddy-O''', is an American rapper and record producer. He began his career by founding the hip-hop group [[Stetsasonic]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://thesource.com/2021/03/12/today-in-hip-hop-history-cult-classic-flick-cb4-opened-in-theaters-28-years-ago/|title=TODAY IN HIP-HOP HISTORY: CULT CLASSIC FLICK 'CB4' OPENED IN THEATERS 28 YEARS AGO|publisher=The Source|date=March 12, 2021|access-date= April 26, 2021}}</ref> He is known as an early advocate of [[Sampling (music)|sampling]] in music.<ref>{{cite book |title=Icons of Hip Hop: An Encyclopedia of the Movement, Music, and Culture |editor=Mickey Hess |page=152 |date=2007 |publisher=ABC-CLIO |isbn=9780313339028}}</ref>

==Career==
In 1981, Daddy-O began his career as the lead member of the band [[Stetsasonic]], which included [[Prince Paul (producer)|Prince Paul]], in [[Brooklyn]], New York. He produced and remixed songs for musical acts such as [[Mary J. Blige]], [[Queen Latifah]], [[Red Hot Chili Peppers]], [[The B-52's]], [[Jeffrey Osborne]], [[Chante Moore]], and [[Lil' Kim]].<ref name="LarkinDM">{{cite book|title=[[Encyclopedia of Popular Music|The Virgin Encyclopedia of Dance Music]]|editor=Colin Larkin|editor-link=Colin Larkin (writer)|publisher=[[Virgin Books]]|date=1998|edition=First|isbn=0-7535-0252-6|page=73}}</ref>

From 1994 to 1998, he was a Senior Director for [[MCA Records]]. After leaving MCA, he worked for [[Motown Records]] and various other entertainment agencies, where he helped to produce and discover talent. Other acts with which he has been associated include [[Sonic Youth]], [[Sublime (band)|Sublime]], [[Barry White]], [[They Might Be Giants]], [[Camron]], [[Pizzicato 5]], and [[Third World (band)|Third World]].<ref>{{Cite web|title="Forbidden Love" THIRD WORLD with Stetsasonic - YouTube|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=99h4HRDntsI|access-date=2020-11-11|website=www.youtube.com}}</ref>

In 2015, Daddy-O joined the CloudSpray team as a management and marketing consultant.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.kickstarter.com/projects/cloudsprayproject/cloudspraymail-invented-for-the-military-can-now-b|title=CloudSprayMail - Invented for the military, can now be yours|website=Kickstarter.com}}</ref> In 2016, Daddy-O and his partner Lion Lindwedel (founder of True School Entertainment) formed their co-owned label, Odad Truth Records. He released his solo albums on the OTR Label with digital distribution on Chuck D's distribution company SpitDigital.  The first release was ''#EverybodyButKRS'', followed in February 2016 with ''The Odad, the Gun, & the Children, El Dolor De Las Calles, No Tablecloths in 2018, From My Hood 2 U in 2019, and the most recent solo release of G.O.A.T. Antidote in 2020''.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://slamjamz.com/news/the-legend-daddy-o-loud-truthful|title=The Legend of Daddy O-Loud & Truthful - The SpitSlam Record Label Group|website=Slamjamz.com}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://slamjamz.com/news/meet-lion-lindwedel-not-from-adams-north-dakota-but-lucky-enough-be-here-you|title=Meet Lion Lindwedel – Not from Adams, North Dakota, But Lucky Enough To Be Here With You - The SpitSlam Record Label Group|website=Slamjamz.com}}</ref>
