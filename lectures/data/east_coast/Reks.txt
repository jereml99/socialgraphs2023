'''Corey Isiah Christie''' (born August 24, 1977), better known as '''Reks''' (styled in [[all caps]] as '''REKS'''), is an American [[Hip hop music|rapper]]. Reks emerged from [[Lawrence, Massachusetts|Lawrence]], [[Massachusetts]]' [[underground rap]] scene. His debut underground album, ''[[Along Came The Chosen]]'', was released on Brick Records in 2001. Reks has released eight additional albums and has appeared on various albums and [[mixtape]]s.

==Early career==
Reks was a [[breakdancing|breakdancer]] in his teens and was part of a local B-boy crew called Funk Town Connection.{{citation needed|date=May 2012}} By the time he entered college at [[University of Massachusetts Amherst]], Reks had built a reputation for himself in the local [[Boston]] rap scene, and eventually quit school to begin recording for Brick Records. A few 12" singles, "I Could Have Done More" and "Fearless," arrived first in early 2001, before Brick issued his debut LP, ''Along Came the Chosen'', later that year. This earned Reks nominations for Hip-Hop Album and Artist of the Year by the [[Boston Music Awards]].<ref>[http://www.bostonmusicspotlight.com/localspotlight-news-boston-music-awards-reveal-2011-nominations/ Boston Music Awards reveal 2011 nominations]</ref> Reks worked with [[DJ Premier]], [[Styles P]], [[Alchemist (producer)|Alchemist]], [[Hi-Tek]], and many others on his 2011 album [[Rhythmatic Eternal King Supreme]].<ref>{{cite web| url = http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/album-reviews/id.1635/title.reks-reks-rhythmatic-eternal-king-supreme| url-status = dead| archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20110308230143/http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/album-reviews/id.1635/title.reks-reks-rhythmatic-eternal-king-supreme| archive-date = 2011-03-08| title = REKS - R.E.K.S. (Rhythmatic Eternal King Supreme) {{!}} Read Hip Hop Reviews, Rap Reviews & Hip Hop Album Reviews {{!}} HipHopDX}}</ref> On October 14, 2016 he released the critically acclaimed album "[[The Greatest X]]"<ref>{{Cite web |title=On 10th Album, Boston Rapper Reks Mulls Nature Of Success And Barriers For Black Men |url=https://www.wbur.org/news/2016/10/13/boston-rapper-reks |access-date=2022-06-15 |website=www.wbur.org |language=en}}</ref>
