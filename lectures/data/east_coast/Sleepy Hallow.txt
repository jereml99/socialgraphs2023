'''Tegan Joshua Anthony Chambers''' (born December 20, 1999), known professionally as '''Sleepy Hallow''',<ref>{{Cite web |last=Chad |first=Mauli |date=August 12, 2023 |title=The Life of The Brooklyn Nightmare, Sleepy Hallow |url=https://raptology.com/documentary/the-life-of-the-brooklyn-nightmare-sleepy-hallow/ |url-status=live |website=Raptology.com}}</ref> is an American rapper making [[Brooklyn drill]] music.<ref>{{cite web | url=https://www.musicindustryhowto.com/what-is-drill-music/ | title=What is Drill Music? With 7 Top Examples & History | date=December 15, 2021 }}</ref> Originally from Jamaica, Chambers grew up in the [[Flatbush, Brooklyn|Flatbush]] section of Brooklyn in New York City alongside frequent collaborator [[Sheff G]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=Sleepy Hallow {{!}} HipHopMyWay|url=https://www.hiphopmyway.com/artists/underground/sleepy-hallow/|access-date=August 13, 2020|website=www.hiphopmyway.com|language=en-US}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|date=October 31, 2019|title=Sleepy Hallow Redefines the Sound of Brooklyn With His Debut Mixtape Don't Sleep|url=https://audibletreats.com/sleepy-hallow_pr2/|access-date=August 15, 2020|website=Audible Treats}}</ref> 

In 2018, he had his first breakthrough viral single with "Flows", which also featured Sheff G.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Reneau|first=Steven|date=August 1, 2019|title=Sheff G & Sleepy Hallow Break Down The Meaning Of "Flows"|url=https://genius.com/videos/Sheff-g-sleepy-hallow-break-down-the-meaning-of-flows|url-status=live|access-date=September 14, 2020|website=Genius}}</ref> In August 2020, his single "[[Deep End Freestyle]]" featuring [[Fousheé]] was certified platinum by the [[Recording Industry Association of America|RIAA]].<ref name="riaa.com">{{Cite web|title=SLEEPY HALLOW – DEEP END FREESTYLE (FEAT. FOUSHEÉ)|url=http://www.riaa.com/gold-platinum/?share=21656&t=t|access-date=August 15, 2020|website=RIAA}}</ref> In August 2021, his single "[[2055 (song)|2055]]" peaked at number 51 on the US [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]], becoming his highest-charting song. He is currently signed to Winners Circle Entertainment and [[RCA Records]].<ref>{{Cite magazine|date=August 12, 2020|title=Winner Circle Entertainment's Sleepy Hallow & Sheff G Announce Partnership With RCA Records: Exclusive|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/9433088/sleepy-hallow-sheff-g-rca-records-partnership|access-date=August 15, 2020|magazine=Billboard}}</ref>

On May 16, 2023, Chambers was indicted along with 32 other alleged gang members including Sheff G for a multitude of gang conspiracy charges.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Santia • • |first=Marc |date=2023-05-16 |title=NYC Rappers Sheff G and Sleepy Hallow Arrested in Brooklyn Gang Bust |url=https://www.nbcnewyork.com/news/local/crime-and-courts/nyc-rappers-sheff-g-and-sleepy-hallow-arrested-in-brooklyn-gang-bust/4338346/ |access-date=2023-05-19 |website=NBC New York |language=en-US}}</ref>

On May 30, 2023, Chambers announced on his Instagram story that he got bailed out, and is now waiting for the trial to start.

== Early life ==
As a child, Chambers immigrated from Jamaica to the [[Flatbush, Brooklyn|Flatbush]] section of [[Brooklyn]], New York City. Around the age of 12, he started to rap. He later picked the name "Sleepy Hallow" as his rap name as that was his name in the streets. Chambers dropped out of school in the ninth grade.<ref>{{Cite magazine|last=Lee|first=Cydney|date=2021-09-21|title=Sleepy Hallow: September's R&B/Hip-Hop Rookie of the Month|url=https://www.billboard.com/music/rb-hip-hop/sleepy-hallow-september-rb-hip-hop-rookie-9632769/|access-date=2021-11-30|magazine=Billboard|language=en-US}}</ref> He first met friend and collaborator [[Sheff G]] when the two were preparing to fight, but stopped when Sheff G was impressed with how Chambers carried himself.<ref>{{Citation|title=Sleepy Hallow & Sheff G Exclusive Interview How did they meet & more|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=EGcjVLwiXko|language=en|access-date=2021-11-30}}</ref>

== Career ==
He, [[Sheff G]], and DizzyGotBands were signed to former [[National Football League|NFL]] player, [[Junior Galette]]'s music record label, Nula Entertainment from 2017 to 2018.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Romaker|first=Ian|date=May 4, 2018|title=Shimmy Shimmy Ya – Sheff G climbs from the New York underground|url=https://lemonwire.com/2018/05/04/shimmy-shimmy-ya-sheff-g-climbs-from-the-new-york-underground/|access-date=August 15, 2020|website=LemonWire}}</ref>

In 2019, Sleepy Hallow released his debut mixtape, ''Don't Sleep''.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Brooklyn's Sleepy Hallow Drops Off Debut Project "Don't Sleep" Ft. Sheff G|url=https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/brooklyns-sleepy-hallow-drops-off-debut-project-dont-sleep-ft-sheff-g-new-mixtape.119724.html|access-date=August 13, 2020|website=HotNewHipHop|date=November 2019}}</ref> In the same year, Winners Circle Entertainment was founded, with Sleepy Hallow as one of the first signees.<ref>{{Cite web|title=WINNERS CIRCLE ENTERTAINMENT ANNOUNCES PARTNERSHIP WITH RCA RECORDS SHEFF G & SLEEPY HALLOW RELEASE NEW RECORD "TIP TOE" – Sony Music Canada|url=https://www.sonymusic.ca/press_release/winners-circle-entertainment-announces-partnership-with-rca-records-sheff-g-sleepy-hallow-release-new-record-tip-toe|access-date=2021-11-30|website=www.sonymusic.ca}}</ref>

In 2020, he released his second mixtape, ''Sleepy for President''.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Sleepy Hallow: Sleepy For President|url=https://pitchfork.com/reviews/albums/sleepy-hallow-sleepy-for-president/|access-date=August 13, 2020|website=Pitchfork}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=Listen to Sleepy Hallow's Project 'Sleepy for President'|url=https://www.complex.com/music/2020/06/sleepy-hallow-sleepy-for-president|access-date=August 13, 2020|website=Complex}}</ref> The mixtape featured the hit single "[[Deep End Freestyle]]" featuring [[Fousheé]], which peaked on the US ''Billboard'' Hot 100 chart at number 80 on June 20, 2020.<ref>{{Cite magazine|title=The Billboard Hot 100 Chart|url=https://www.billboard.com/charts/the-billboard-hot-100/2020-06-20|access-date=August 13, 2020|magazine=Billboard}}</ref> The single has over 85 million streams and was [[Music recording certification|certified gold]] by [[Recording Industry Association of America|RIAA]] in August 2020.<ref>{{Cite web|date=August 12, 2020|title=NY Drill Stars Sheff G & Sleepy Hallow Ink RCA Deal|url=https://hiphopdx.com/news/id.57299/title.ny-drill-stars-sheff-g-sleepy-hallow-ink-rca-deal|access-date=August 15, 2020|website=HipHopDX}}</ref><ref name="riaa.com" />

In August 2020, Winners Circle Entertainment started a partnership with [[RCA Records]].<ref>{{Cite magazine|date=August 12, 2020|title=Winner Circle Entertainment's Sleepy Hallow & Sheff G Announce Partnership With RCA Records: Exclusive|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/9433088/sleepy-hallow-sheff-g-rca-records-partnership|access-date=August 13, 2020|magazine=Billboard}}</ref> Sleepy Hallow and Sheff G released the single "Tip Toe", produced by Great John.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Sheff G and Sleepy Hallow Announce RCA Records Partnership, Share New Song "Tip Toe"|url=https://www.complex.com/music/2020/08/sheff-g-sleepy-hallow-rca-records-partnership-tip-toe|access-date=August 23, 2020|website=Complex}}</ref>

In June 2021, he released his first studio album, ''Still Sleep?''<ref name="Revolt">{{cite web|url=https://www.revolt.tv/platform/amp/new-music/2021/4/16/22388046/sleepy-hallow-2055-video|title=Sleepy Hallow ventures into the year "2055" in new visual|work=[[Revolt (TV network)|Revolt]]|last=Cho|first=Regina|date=April 16, 2021|access-date=July 16, 2021}}</ref> The album includes the single, "[[2055 (song)|2055]]", which was released alongside a video that sees an animated Sleepy Hallow in an alternate, futuristic universe.<ref name="Revolt" /> The single would be certified double platinum in March 2022.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Gold & Platinum|url=https://www.riaa.com/gold-platinum/?tab_active=default-award&ar=Sleepy+Hallow&ti=2055&format=Single&type=#search_section|url-status=live|access-date=2021-11-30|website=RIAA|language=en-US}}</ref>

In May 2022, he released his single "[[Die Young (Sleepy Hallow song)|Die Young]]", which features Canadian rapper [[347aidan]].

In September 2022, he released his single "2 Mins of Pain".<ref>{{Cite web |title=Sleepy Hallow narrates "2 Minutes of Pain" in latest single |url=https://www.revolt.tv/article/2022-09-08/190846/sleepy-hallow-2-minutes-of-pain-song/ |access-date=2022-12-04 |website=REVOLT |language=en-US}}</ref>

In November 2022, he released the single "Marie".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.revolt.tv/article/2022-11-02/250133/sleepy-hallow-marie-song/|title=Sleepy Hallow drops new "Marie" single|last=Cho|first=Regina|date=November 2, 2022|website=Revolt|access-date=November 3, 2022}}</ref>

In May 2023, Sleepy Hallow was charged in a 140-count indictment, as the district attorney's office said the gang is linked to 12 shootings and a murder that was caught on camera.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Santia • • |first=Marc |date=2023-05-16 |title=NYC Rappers Sheff G and Sleepy Hallow Arrested in Brooklyn Gang Bust |url=https://www.nbcnewyork.com/news/local/crime-and-courts/nyc-rappers-sheff-g-and-sleepy-hallow-arrested-in-brooklyn-gang-bust/4338346/ |access-date=2023-05-17 |website=NBC New York |language=en-US}}</ref>

On September 15, 2023, Sleepy Hallow released his first album since getting out of jail “Boy Meets World". It features songs such as "GBG" featuring [[Marshmello]] and "For You" featuring TaTa.{{citation need|date=September 2023}}
