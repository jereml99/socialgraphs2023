'''Paul Nathaniel Barman''' (born October 30, 1974), better known by his stage name '''MC Paul Barman''', is an American rapper and illustrator. Having emerged during the early 2000s, Barman became a definitive voice within the realm of [[alternative hip hop]], noted for his intricate rhyme schemes and humorous narrative style, which he infuses with an array of literary devices. He is particularly recognized for his usage of {{Cl|Word games|word games}}, including [[Acrostic|acrostics]]<ref name="SeattleT09">{{cite web|url=https://www.seattletimes.com/entertainment/music/listening-to-paul-barman-will-make-you-smarter-than-listening-to-mozart/|title=Listening to Paul Barman will make you smarter than listening to Mozart|work=[[The Seattle Times]]|first=Andrew|last=Matson|date=December 3, 2009|access-date=May 20, 2023|archive-date=December 3, 2021|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20211203111236/https://www.seattletimes.com/entertainment/music/listening-to-paul-barman-will-make-you-smarter-than-listening-to-mozart/|url-status=live}}</ref> and [[Palindrome|palindromes]],<ref name="Brown03">{{cite web|url=https://www.brownalumnimagazine.com/articles/2007-06-22/rapping-in-palindromes/|title=Rapping in Palindromes|work=[[Brown Alumni Magazine]]|first=Jake|last=Miller|date=April 2003|access-date=May 20, 2023|archive-date=May 20, 2023|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230520004530/https://www.brownalumnimagazine.com/articles/2007-06-22/rapping-in-palindromes/|url-status=live}}</ref><ref name="Crimson03">{{cite web|url=https://www.thecrimson.com/article/2003/2/21/sometimes-cerebral-rapper-mixes-palindromes-politics-paul/|title=Sometimes-Cerebral Rapper Mixes Palindromes, Politics|work=[[The Harvard Crimson]]|first=Michael|last=Hoffman|date=February 21, 2003|access-date=May 20, 2023|archive-date=May 20, 2023|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230520003538/https://www.thecrimson.com/article/2003/2/21/sometimes-cerebral-rapper-mixes-palindromes-politics-paul/|url-status=live}}</ref> in crafting his rap lyrics. The SymmyS Awards, a competition once organized by [[The Palindromist]], had Barman as a past panel judge.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.usatoday.com/story/popcandy/2014/04/15/palindromes/7740239/|title=It's the SymmyS: Awards honor best new palindromes|work=[[USA Today]]|first=Whitney|last=Matheson|date=April 15, 2014|access-date=May 20, 2023|archive-date=May 20, 2023|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230520011045/https://www.usatoday.com/story/popcandy/2014/04/15/palindromes/7740239/|url-status=live}}</ref> Barman's complex and multilayered approach to songwriting has earned him both acclaim and attention, marking him as a distinctive and influential figure within the alternative hip hop scene.

==Early life==
Barman is from [[Ridgewood, New Jersey]]. He is Jewish. He attended [[Brown University]].<ref name=nytimes>{{cite web|last=Rosen|first=Jody|url=https://query.nytimes.com/gst/fullpage.html?res=9405E0D91231F930A15757C0A9669C8B63|title=MUSIC; Rapping in Whiteface (for Laughs)|work=[[The New York Times]]|date=April 23, 2000|access-date=July 30, 2008}}</ref>

==Career==
MC Paul Barman released his debut EP, ''[[It's Very Stimulating]]'', on Wordsound in 2000; it was produced by [[Prince Paul (producer)|Prince Paul]].<ref>{{cite web|first=Thomas|last=Quinlan|url=http://exclaim.ca/music/article/mc_paul_barman-its_very_stimulating|title=MC Paul Barman - It's Very Stimulating|work=[[Exclaim!]]|date=April 1, 2000|access-date=March 27, 2018}}</ref>

His first studio album, ''[[Paullelujah!]]'', was released on Coup d'État in 2002.<ref>{{cite web|first=Matt|last=LeMay|url=http://pitchfork.com/reviews/albums/565-paullelujah/|title=MC Paul Barman: Paullelujah!|work=[[Pitchfork (website)|Pitchfork]]|date=November 20, 2002|access-date=March 27, 2018}}</ref>

He released his second studio album, ''[[Thought Balloon Mushroom Cloud]]'', on Househusband in 2009.<ref>{{cite web|first=Nicholas|last=Candiotto|url=http://potholesinmyblog.com/album-review-mc-paul-barman-%E2%80%93-thought-balloon-mushroom-cloud/|title=MC Paul Barman - Thought Balloon Mushroom Cloud|work=Potholes in My Blog|date=June 5, 2010|access-date=March 27, 2018}}</ref>

He has toured with [[Blackalicious]].<ref>{{cite web|first=Margaret|last=Murray|url=http://www.xlr8r.com/reviews/mc-paul-barman/paullelujah|title=Paullelujah!|work=[[XLR8R]]|date=January 14, 2003|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131027233632/http://www.xlr8r.com/reviews/mc-paul-barman/paullelujah|archive-date=October 27, 2013|access-date=March 27, 2018}}</ref> He has taught a hip hop class to high school kids at the [[Bank Street College of Education]].<ref>{{cite web|first=Ben|last=Westhoff|url=http://www.villagevoice.com/2010/01/05/mc-paul-barman-perseveres/|title=MC Paul Barman Perseveres|work=[[The Village Voice]]|date=January 5, 2010|access-date=March 27, 2018}}</ref>

In 2018, he released ''(((Echo Chamber)))'', his first studio album since 2009's ''Thought Balloon Mushroom Cloud'', on [[Mello Music Group]].<ref>{{cite web|first1=Noah|last1=Yoo|first2=Matthew|last2=Strauss|url=http://pitchfork.com/news/mf-doom-questlove-mark-ronson-more-produce-new-mc-paul-barman-album/|title=MF Doom, Questlove, Mark Ronson, More Produce New MC Paul Barman Album|work=[[Pitchfork (website)|Pitchfork]]|date=March 12, 2018|access-date=March 27, 2018}}</ref> It included productions from [[MF Doom]], [[Questlove]], and [[Mark Ronson]],<ref>{{cite web|first=Kyle|last=Mullin|url=http://exclaim.ca/music/article/mc_paul_barman_discusses_writing_in_the_dark_making_his_kids_proud_and_his_return_to_music_on_echo_chamber|title=MC Paul Barman Discusses Writing in the Dark, Making His Kids Proud and His Return to Music on '(((Echo Chamber)))'|work=[[Exclaim!]]|date=May 18, 2018|access-date=May 20, 2018}}</ref> as well as guest appearances from [[Open Mike Eagle]] and [[Masta Ace]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://uproxx.com/hiphop/playboi-carti-mc-paul-barman-die-lit-echo-chamber/2/|title=Within Hip-Hop, Playboi Carti And MC Paul Barman Aren't As Different As They Might Seem|work=[[Uproxx]]|first=Aaron|last=Williams|date=May 22, 2018|access-date=May 23, 2018}}</ref>

==Musical style and influences==
''[[The New York Times]]'' called his music "a surreal departure from the rap norm".<ref name=nytimes/> His music has been influenced by [[Boogie Down Productions]], [[Jungle Brothers]], [[Wu-Tang Clan]], [[MC Lyte]], and [[De La Soul]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.indyweek.com/indyweek/mc-white-intellectual/Content?oid=1183750|title=MC White Intellectual|work=[[Indy Week]]|first=Jamie|last=Rogus|date=May 16, 2011|access-date=May 23, 2018}}</ref>
