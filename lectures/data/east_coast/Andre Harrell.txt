'''Andre O’Neal Harrell''' (September 26, 1960&nbsp;– May 7, 2020) was an American music executive and multimedia [[Executive producer|producer]].<ref>Janine McAdams, "[[Uptown Records|Uptown]], [[MCA Inc.|MCA]] seal multimedia deal: Harrell looks to 'synergize' music, film, TV", [[Billboard (magazine)|''Billboard'']], 1992 Jun 20;'''104'''(25):[https://books.google.com/books?id=qRAEAAAAMBAJ&pg=PA8&dq=Andre+Harrell+multimedia 8],[https://books.google.com/books?id=qRAEAAAAMBAJ&pg=PA89&dq=Harrell 89].  For a briefer take, see Dimitri Ehrlich, [https://books.google.com/books?id=3uQCAAAAMBAJ&pg=PA26&dq=Harrell+Uptown "Andre the giant"], [[New York (magazine)|''New York'']], 1992 Sep 21;'''25'''(37):26.</ref> In 1986, recently a rapper, he formed [[Uptown Records]], soon a leader in R&B, rap, and their fusion, "[[hip hop soul]]" and "[[new jack swing]]."<ref name=":0">Kiki Mason, [https://books.google.com/books?id=QeMCAAAAMBAJ&pg=PA37 "Pop goes the ghetto"], ''[[New York (magazine)|New York]]'', 1995 Oct 23;'''28'''(42):37–43, whereby [https://books.google.com/books?id=QeMCAAAAMBAJ&pg=PA38&dq=Harrell p 38] offers a Harrell portrait prioritizing the recent, [https://books.google.com/books?id=QeMCAAAAMBAJ&pg=PA40&dq=Harrell p 40] covers Harrell's early life and segues into his start in the music business, [https://books.google.com/books?id=QeMCAAAAMBAJ&pg=PA41&dq=Harrell p 41] includes his own successes in it, [https://books.google.com/books?id=QeMCAAAAMBAJ&pg=PA42&dq=Harrell+Puffy+Combs+fired p 42] introduces Sean "Puffy" Combs, and [https://books.google.com/books?id=QeMCAAAAMBAJ&pg=PA43&dq=Harrell p 43] returns to Harrell in the present, 1995, including recent multimedia ventures.</ref> He gave [[Sean Combs|Sean "Diddy" Combs]] his 1990 start in the industry,<ref>Natasha S Alford, interviewer, [https://thegrio.com/2016/05/20/andre-harrell-dishes-on-how-helped-take-diddy-from-shirtless-bad-boy-to-music-mogul/ "Andre Harrell dishes on how he helped take Diddy from shirtless ‘bad boy’ to music mogul"], ''TheGrio.com'', [[TheGrio]], 20 May 2016.</ref> and later was [[Motown Records]]' CEO.<ref name=":0" />

==Early life==
Harrell was born in New York City borough the [[Bronx]] on September 26, 1960.<ref name=NYT>{{cite news|title=Andre Harrell, Executive Who Bridged Hip-Hop and R&B, Dies at 59|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2020/05/09/arts/music/andre-harrell-dead.html|first=Jon|last=Caramanica|date=May 9, 2020|access-date=May 9, 2020|newspaper=The New York Times|quote=Andre Harrell, an innovative music executive who in the late 1980s founded Uptown Records, a crucial bridge between the worlds of hip-hop and R&B, and who gave the first career break to Sean Combs, today one of hip-hop’s signature moguls and global ambassadors, died on Thursday night at his home in West Hollywood, Calif.}}</ref><ref name=ABC>{{cite news|title=Legendary hip hop and R&B record label founder Andre Harrell has died|url=https://abcnews.go.com/US/legendary-hip-hop-rb-record-label-founder-andre/story?id=70595687|first=Christina|last=Carrega|date=May 9, 2020|access-date=May 9, 2020|work=ABC News}}</ref> His father, Bernie, worked at a [[Greengrocer|produce market]] in the Bronx's [[Hunts Point, Bronx|Hunts Point]] section; his mother, Hattie, was a nurse's aide.

While a teenager, Harrell formed with Alonzo Brown, a friend from high school, a rap duo, [[Dr. Jeckyll & Mr. Hyde (group)|Dr. Jeckyll & Mr. Hyde]]—Harrell as Jeckyll, Brown as Hyde<ref name="ABC" />—and reportedly achieved minor hit songs, "Genius Rap" in 1981 and "AM/PM" in 1984.<ref>{{cite news|title=Andre Harrell Dies: Hitmaking Music Entrepreneur And Executive For Several Record Labels Was 59|url=https://deadline.com/2020/05/andre-harrell-dies-music-executive-uptown-records-motown-obituary-1202930560/|first=Bruce|last=Haring|date=May 9, 2020|access-date=May 9, 2020|magazine=Deadline}}</ref>

Harrell graduated from [[Bayard Rustin Educational Complex|Charles Evans Hughes High School]] in 1978.<ref name="NYT" /> He attended [[Baruch College]], transferred to [[Lehman College]], and, aiming to be a newscaster, majored in communications and business management.<ref name="HistoryMakers">{{cite web|title=Andre Harrell's Biography|url=https://www.thehistorymakers.org/biography/andre-harrell|access-date=May 9, 2020|publisher=The HistoryMakers}}</ref><ref name="NYT" /> Third-year, he withdrew and took work at a local radio station.<ref name="NYT" /><ref name="HistoryMakers" />

==Career==
In 1983, Harrell met [[Russell Simmons]], a co-founder of [[Def Jam Recordings]]. He went to work for Def Jam and within two years became vice-president and general manager. After a few years working at Def Jam, Harrell left and founded his own label called [[Uptown Records]].<ref name=NYT/>

Harrell is credited with having discovered and signed [[Sean Combs|Sean "Puffy" Combs]].{{sfn|Traugh|2010|p=23}} In 1988, [[Mary J. Blige]] recorded an impromptu cover of [[Anita Baker]]'s "[[Caught Up in the Rapture]]" at a recording booth in a local mall.<ref>{{cite news|title=Oprah Talks to Mary J. Blige|url=https://www.oprah.com/omagazine/oprah-interviews-mary-j-blige|date=May 2006|access-date=May 9, 2020|magazine=O, The Oprah Magazine}}</ref> Her mother's boyfriend at the time later played the cassette for [[Jeff Redd]], a recording artist and A&R runner for Uptown Records. Redd sent it to Harrell, who met with Blige. In 1989, she was signed to the label and became the company's youngest and first female solo artist.<ref>{{cite news|title=Mary J. Blige's most iconic '90s hits collected in new box set|url=https://thevinylfactory.com/news/mary-j-bliges-iconic-90s-hits-collected-in-new-box-set/|first=Lazlo|last=Rugoff|date=October 11, 2019|access-date=May 9, 2020|magazine=The Vinyl Factory}}</ref>

In 1988, Harrell was offered a label deal [[MCA Records|MCA Music Entertainment Group]]. After he had multiple successful releases, in 1992, MCA offered Harrell a [[multimedia]] deal, which involved film and television productions. They developed the feature film ''[[Strictly Business (1991 film)|Strictly Business]]'' and [[Fox Broadcasting Company|FOX]]'s hit police drama series, ''[[New York Undercover]],'' which aired from 1994 until 1998.<ref name=NYT/>

Harrell renamed Uptown Records as Uptown Enterprises, and its records were featured in productions for [[Universal Pictures]] and [[Universal Television]].<ref>{{cite news|title=Making a Difference; It's a Sweet $50 Million For Andre Harrell's Lineup|url=https://www.nytimes.com/1992/11/15/business/making-a-difference-it-s-a-sweet-50-million-for-andre-harrell-s-lineup.html|first=Anita M.|last=Samuels|date=November 15, 1992|access-date=May 9, 2020|newspaper=The New York Times}}</ref> In 1994, Harrell had a son with Wendy Credle, a music attorney. They named him Gianni Credle-Harrell.<ref>{{cite news|title=Music Executive Andre Harrell Dead at 59: 'He Made His Living Uplifting Others,' Russell Simmons Says|url=https://people.com/music/music-executive-andre-harrell-dies-at-59/|first=Ally|last=Mauch|date=May 9, 2020|access-date=May 9, 2020|magazine=People}}</ref>

In 1995, Harrell was appointed CEO of [[Motown Records]] and remained there until 1997.<ref name=NYT/> He also hosted ''Champagne & Bubbles'' on Sunday nights from 6 to 9pm on Emmis Urban AC WRKS ([[98.7 Kiss FM]])/New York.<ref>{{cite news|title=RIP: Hip Hop Executive Andre Harrell Passes Away At 59|url=https://www.hot97.com/hot-highlights/rip-hip-hop-executive-andre-harrell-passes-away-at-59/|first=King|last=Louie|date=May 9, 2020|access-date=May 9, 2020|work=Hot 97|publisher=WQHT}}</ref> Harrell was the CEO of Harrell Records, which is distributed through [[Atlantic Records]].<ref>{{cite magazine|last1=Mitchell|first1=Gail|date=October 1, 2011|title=Love Jones|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=jim-YANOn5AC&q=%22andre+harrell%22+%22L7+Entertainment%22&pg=RA2-PA43|magazine=Billboard|publisher=Nielsen Business Media, Inc.|volume=123|issue=35|page=43|issn=0006-2510}}</ref> He partnered with budding Atlanta-based production company L7 Entertainment for the release of their new artists Hamilton Park and Netta Brielle.<ref>{{cite news|title=Andre Harrell Speaks on Music Being the Pulse of America|url=https://singersroom.com/content/2012-01-22/andre-harrell-speaks-on-music-being-the-pulse-of-america/|date=January 22, 2012|access-date=May 9, 2020|magazine=Singersroom}}</ref>

Harrell was the Vice Chairman of Revolt, [[Sean Combs|Diddy]]'s multi-platform music network. On October 17, 2014, he was instrumental in launching the Revolt Music Conference in [[Miami, Florida]], at the [[Fontainebleau Miami Beach|Fontainebleau Hotel]]. The event was attended by such entertainment figures as [[Guy Oseary]], [[Russell Simmons]], and [[L.A. Reid]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/6289075/revolt-music-conference-miami-begins-sean-combs-andre-harrell-craig-kallman-daniel-glass|title=Revolt's Inaugural Music Conference Kicks Off in Miami with Sean Combs, Guy Oseary, Craig Kallman and Many More|website=Billboard|date=October 17, 2014}}</ref>

==Death==
Harrell died on May 7, 2020, at his home in [[West Hollywood, California]].<ref name=NYT/> He was 59, and news of his death was first announced on [[Instagram]] by [[D-Nice]].<ref name=ABC/><ref>{{cite news|title=Usher, Mariah Carey, John Legend & More React to Andre Harrell's Death|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/hip-hop/9374750/musicians-celebrities-react-to-uptown-records-andre-harrell-death|first=Mitchell|last=Peters|date=May 9, 2020|access-date=May 9, 2020|magazine=Billboard}}</ref> According to Wendy Credle, Harrell's ex-partner, he had been suffering from [[Cardiovascular disease|heart problems]] in the time leading up to his death.<ref name=NYT/> At his funeral, held on May 23, among notable attendees were singers [[Mary J. Blige]], [[Mariah Carey]] and record producer [[L.A. Reid]].<ref>{{cite news|title=Mariah Carey, Chris Rock, Mary J. Blige Attend Andre Harrell Funeral; TV Tribute Follows|url=https://variety.com/2020/music/news/mariah-carey-chris-rock-mary-j-blige-andre-harrell-funeral-tv-tribute-1234616847/|first=Jeff |last=Vasishta|date=May 26, 2020|access-date=July 18, 2022|publisher=Variety}}</ref>
