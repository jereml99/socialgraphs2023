'''Tariq Amar Devega''' (born July 26, 1990), known professionally as '''ASAP Nast''' (stylized '''A$AP Nast'''), is an American rapper, songwriter and model from [[Harlem, New York]]. Nast is best known as a member of the hip hop collective [[ASAP Mob|A$AP Mob]]. As part of A$AP Mob, they released their first project as a group, a mixtape titled ''[[Lords Never Worry|Lord$ Never Worry]]'' in 2012. His breakout song, "[[Trillmatic]]" featuring [[Wu-Tang Clan]]'s [[Method Man]] was released on December 4, 2013.

== Early life and education ==
ASAP Nast was raised in [[Harlem|West Harlem]], [[New York City]] on 116th and Morningside Avenue, and frequently spent time with his cousin [[ASAP Rocky]] when they were children.<ref name="hotnewhiphop1"/><ref name="villagevoice2">{{cite web|author=J. Pablo Tue., Aug. 28 2012 at 2:00 PM |url=http://blogs.villagevoice.com/music/2012/08/meet_asap_mob.php?page=2 |title=Meet The A$AP Mob: Talking To Ant, Ferg, J. Scott, Nast, Twelvy, And Yams |publisher=Blogs.villagevoice.com |date=2012-08-28 |access-date=2014-03-25}}</ref> When Rocky moved to [[The Bronx]] as a teen, the two lost contact, but were reunited by ASAP Bari, one of [[ASAP Mob]]'s founding members.<ref name="hotnewhiphop1"/> Nast went to [[Martin Luther King, Jr. Educational Campus|Martin Luther King, Jr. High School]] in [[Manhattan]] but dropped out before  graduating  to pursue a  career in music. After dropping out of high school, he worked at the [[Sneakers|sneaker]] store Atmos.<ref name="villagevoice2"/>

== Musical career ==

===2007–12: Career beginnings with ASAP Mob===
ASAP Nast was one of the first members to join the hip hop collective [[ASAP Mob]], that was formed by ASAP Yams, ASAP Bari and ASAP Illz in Harlem during 2007. He was originally known by the name New York Nast, however he adjusted his stage name to include the ASAP prefix, as all the other Mob members have; aside from [[Dash (rapper)|Dash]].<ref name="autogenerated1">{{cite web|url=http://www.vibe.com/article/vibe-tv-meet-asap-mob |title=TV: Meet the ASAP Mob |publisher=Vibe |date=2013-09-10 |access-date=2014-03-25}}</ref> Nast made his official debut on ASAP Rocky's early song "Uptown" and then he appeared on two ''[[Live.Love.ASAP]]'' songs, "Purple Swag: Chapter 2" and "Trilla".<ref name="hotnewhiphop1"/><ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.djbooth.net/index/tracks/review/asap-rocky-trilla |title=ASAP Rocky ft. ASAP Twelvy & ASAP Nast - Trilla &#124; Stream & Download |publisher=DJBooth.net |access-date=2014-03-25}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|author=PEDRO says |url=http://www.thefader.com/2011/09/19/asap-rocky-f-asap-nast-and-asap-twelvy-trilla-mp3/ |title=ASAP Rocky f. ASAP Nast and ASAP Twelvy, "Trillamatic" MP3 |publisher=The Fader |date=2011-09-19 |access-date=2014-03-25}}</ref>

On August 27, 2012, ASAP Mob released their debut project, a mixtape titled ''[[Lords Never Worry]]'', as a free download.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://pitchfork.com/news/47648-download-the-new-mixtape-lords-never-worry-from-aap-rockys-aap-mob/|title=Download the New Mixtape Lords Never Worry From A$AP Rocky's A$AP Mob|publisher=Pitchfork Media|last=Pelly|first=Jenn|date=August 27, 2012|access-date=August 28, 2012}}</ref> Nast appeared on four of the mixtape's tracks, including the standout "Black Mane".<ref name="complex1">{{cite web|url=http://www.complex.com/music/2013/07/25-new-rappers-to-watch-out-for/asap-nast |title=A$AP Nast — 25 New Rappers to Watch Out For |publisher=Complex |access-date=2014-03-25}}</ref> Following the mixtape's release, Nast toured with the rest of ASAP Mob. Throughout that tour they performed 48 shows in 61 days, the majority of which were sold out.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.vibe.com/article/mob-ties-meet-aap-nast |title=Mob Ties: Meet A$Ap Nast |publisher=Vibe |date=2012-11-28 |access-date=2014-03-25}}</ref>

===2013–14: ''L.O.R.D.''===
Singer [[Tinashe]] released the remix to her song "Who Am I Working For?" on April 16, 2013, featuring Nast.<ref name="complex2">{{cite web|url=http://www.complex.com/music/2013/04/premiere-tinashe-f-aap-nast-who-am-i-working-for-remix |title=Premiere: Tinashe f/ A$AP Nast "Who Am I Working For? (Remix)" |publisher=Complex |date=2013-04-16 |access-date=2014-03-25}}</ref> In July 2013, [[ASAP Ferg]] announced that ASAP Mob would release their debut album after the release of his solo debut album ''[[Trap Lord]]''.<ref>{{cite web|last=Buyanovsky |first=Dan |url=http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2013/07/asap-ferg-teases-new-asap-mob-album/ |title=A$AP Ferg Teases New A$AP Mob Album - XXL |publisher=Xxlmag.com |date=2013-07-10 |access-date=2014-03-25}}</ref> During October 2013, ASAP Nast along with ASAP Rocky, Ferg, Twelvy and Ant, participated in the ASAP Mob's cypher during the [[2013 BET Hip Hop Awards]].<ref>{{cite web|last=Baker |first=Soren |url=http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/news/id.25592/title.kendrick-lamar-a-ap-rocky-slaughterhouse-among-bet-hip-hop-awards-cypher-participants |title=Kendrick Lamar, A$AP Rocky, Slaughterhouse Among BET Hip Hop Awards Cypher Participants &#124; Get The Latest Hip Hop News, Rap News & Hip Hop Album Sales |publisher=HipHop DX |date=2013-09-29 |access-date=2014-03-25}}</ref> On December 3, 2013, it was announced that the album would be released on March 4, 2014.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.rap-up.com/2013/12/03/video-asap-mob-f-asap-nast-method-man-trillmatic/ |title=Video: A$AP Mob f/ A$AP Nast & Method Man – 'Trillmatic' |publisher=Rap-Up.com |access-date=2014-03-25}}</ref> The following day, the album's first [[Single (music)|single]], "[[Trillmatic]]" by ASAP Nast, was released along with an accompanying music video. The single, produced by ASAP Ty Beats, features a [[Verse (popular music)|verse]] from American rapper and New York City-native, [[Wu-Tang Clan]]'s [[Method Man]].<ref name="mv">{{cite web|url=http://pitchfork.com/news/53247-video-aap-mob-trillmatic-ft-aap-nast-and-method-man/|title=Video: A$AP Mob: "Trillmatic" [ft. A$AP Nast and Method Man]|publisher=Pitchfork Media|last=Zoladz|first=Lindsay|date=December 5, 2013|access-date=January 17, 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.complex.com/music/2013/12/asap-mob-asap-nast-method-man-trillmatic |title=A$AP Mob A$AP Nast Method Man "Trillmatic" |publisher=Complex |date=2013-12-03 |access-date=2014-03-25}}</ref> It had previously been previewed at the end of the music video for ASAP Ant's song "The Way It Go".<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.complex.com/music/2013/07/video-asap-ant-asap-the-way-it-gotrillmatic |title=A$AP Ant & A$AP Nast "The Way It Go/Trillmatic" |publisher=Complex |date=2013-07-09 |access-date=2014-03-25}}</ref> The single was met with generally positive reviews from [[music critic]]s.<ref name="complex2"/><ref>{{cite web|first=Billy|last=Johnson, Jr. |url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/news/a-ap-mob-s-trillmatic-video-count-the-aa99-s-references-20131204 |title=AAP Mobs Trillmatic Video: Count the 1990s References &#124; Music News |publisher=Rolling Stone |date=2013-12-04 |access-date=2014-03-25}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://theboombox.com/asap-nast-method-man-trillmatic-video/ |title=A$AP Nast, Method Man Take It to the '90s With 'Trillmatic' |publisher=Theboombox.com |date=2013-12-04 |access-date=2014-03-25}}</ref> The album was then changed to be titled ''[[L.O.R.D. (album)|L.O.R.D.]]''.<ref>{{cite web|last=Meara |first=Paul |url=http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/news/id.27038/title.rap-release-dates-juicy-j-meek-mill-murs-curtiss-king-killa-sha |title=Rap Release Dates: Juicy J, Meek Mill, Murs & Curtiss King, Killa Sha &#124; Get The Latest Hip Hop News, Rap News & Hip Hop Album Sales |publisher=HipHop DX |date=2014-01-14 |access-date=2014-03-25}}</ref>

It has been predicted that ''L.O.R.D.'' could've resulted in Nast being the next breakout member of the group.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.complex.com/music/2014/01/15-artists-to-watch-out-for-in-2014/asap-nast |title=A$AP Nast — 15 Artists To Watch Out For In 2014 |publisher=Complex |access-date=2014-03-25}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.complex.com/music/2014/01/most-anticipated-albums-2014/asap-mob-lords |title=16. A$AP Mob, Lords — The Most Anticipated Albums of 2014 |publisher=Complex |date=2014-01-06 |access-date=2014-03-25}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://atlantablackstar.com/2013/12/04/straight-mobbin-aap-nast-trillmatic-video-feat-method-man/ |title=Straight Mobbin': A$AP Nast Drops "Trillmatic" Video Featuring Method Man |publisher=Atlanta Black Star |date=2013-12-04 |access-date=2014-03-25}}</ref> Originally, he was being considered for the 2014 ''[[XXL (magazine)|XXL]]'' freshman class,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/10spot-artist/2014/03/aap-nast/ |title=A$AP Nast - XXL |publisher=Xxlmag.com |access-date=2014-03-25}}</ref> but was not added. ''L.O.R.D.'' was expected for a 2014 release, however, ASAP Mob leader, the late ASAP Yams announced on his [[Tumblr]] account that the album was officially scrapped.<ref name="tumblr">{{cite web|url=http://yamborghini.tumblr.com/post/98450145494/you-my-dude-and-a-ap-is-my-fav-rap-fashionable-but|title=You my dude and A$AP Is my fav rap / fashionable ...|publisher=Yamborghini.tumblr.com|access-date=19 January 2015}}</ref>

== Musical style ==
''[[Complex (magazine)|Complex]]'' said that ASAP Nast's style is deeply rooted in 1990's [[East Coast hip hop]], omitting the [[Trap music (hip hop)|trap]] and [[southern hip hop]] influence that some other ASAP Mob members have.<ref name="complex1"/> ''[[Vibe (magazine)|Vibe]]'' called him a brash, rapid-fire rhymer.<ref name="autogenerated1"/> He has been credited as able to "adapt his flow to match beats of all styles and eras." His verses are usually boastful and full of multi-syllable rhyme schemes.<ref name="hotnewhiphop1"/>

== Other Ventures ==

=== Collaborations ===
Various brands such as [[Converse (shoe company)|Converse]] and [[Reebok]] have acknowledged his influence and thus granted him the opportunity to design sneakers that merge heritage with his street-style sensibilities.<ref>{{Cite web |date=2022-02-26 |title=Sole Mates: A$AP Nast and the Reebok Zig Kinetica II Edge |url=https://hypebeast.com/2022/2/asap-nast-reebok-zig-kinetica-ii-edge-sole-mates-interview |access-date=2022-05-30 |website=HYPEBEAST}}</ref> The rapper has released a number of collaborative pairs with Converse as they played with silhouettes from One Stars and Chuck 70s to the Jack Purcell Mid.<ref>{{Cite web |date=2017-11-26 |title=The A$AP Nast x Converse Collaboration Is Set for an Online Rerelease |url=https://hypebeast.com/2017/11/asap-nast-converse-collaboration-footlocker-online-rerelease |access-date=2022-05-30 |website=HYPEBEAST}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |date=2019-12-02 |title=A$AP Nast and Converse Turn up the Heat With Second Jack Purcell Mid Collaboration |url=https://hypebeast.com/2019/12/asap-nast-converse-jack-purcell-167378c-167379c-release-date |access-date=2022-05-30 |website=HYPEBEAST}}</ref> In Spring of 2021, ASAP Nast formed a relationship with Reebok as they dropped a new Classic Leather Legacy colorway followed by a Zig Kinetica II Edge.<ref>{{Cite web |date=2021-03-29 |title=A$AP Nast Links With Reebok For Earth Toned Classic Leather Legacy Collaboration |url=https://hypebeast.com/2021/3/asap-nast-reebok-classic-leather-legacy-h01280-release-date |access-date=2022-05-30 |website=HYPEBEAST}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |date=2021-11-18 |title=A$AP Nast and Reebok Reunite for a Zig Kinetica II Edge Collaboration |url=https://hypebeast.com/2021/11/aap-nast-reebok-zig-kinetica-ii-edge-collaboration-gy5805-release-info |access-date=2022-05-30 |website=HYPEBEAST}}</ref>
