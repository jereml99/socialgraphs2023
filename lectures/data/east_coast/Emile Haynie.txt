'''Emile Haynie''' (born July 13, 1980), often credited simply as '''Emile''', is an American record producer. Born and raised in [[Buffalo, New York]], his range of production includes [[alternative rock]], [[hip hop music|hip hop]], [[indie music|indie]] and [[pop music]]. Haynie has worked with several prominent artists in the music industry including [[Kanye West]], [[Kid Cudi]], [[Eminem]], [[Lana Del Rey]], [[Bruno Mars]], [[Linkin Park]], [[FKA Twigs]], [[Florence Welch]] and [[Camila Cabello]], among others. Additionally, Haynie received two [[Grammy Awards]] for his work with English singer [[Adele]], namely [[Grammy Award for Album of the Year|Album of the Year]] for the [[RIAA certification|diamond certified]] ''[[25 (Adele album)|25]]'' as well as [[Grammy Award for Record of the Year|Record of the Year]] for "[[Hello (Adele song)|Hello]]".<ref name="GrammysHistory" />

==Biography==
===2003–2008: Early life and career beginnings===
Emile Haynie is a native of [[Buffalo, New York]]. He started primarily as a [[Sampling (music)|sample]]-driven [[hip hop production|hip-hop producer]] and got his first big break after handing off a [[Instrumental hip hop|beat]] [[CD]] to now-deceased Detroit [[rapper]] [[Proof (rapper)|Proof]].<ref name="complex">{{Cite news |first1=Daniel |last1=Isenberg |first2=Joe |last2=La Puma |date=October 28, 2011 |title=Emile Tells All: The Stories Behind His Classic Records |magazine=[[Complex (magazine)|Complex]]
|url=http://www.complex.com/music/2011/10/emile-tells-all-the-stories-behind-his-classic-records/ |access-date=February 4, 2012 }}</ref> He then began producing for various members of [[Eminem]]’s Detroit camp, as well as [[New York City]] rappers [[Raekwon]], [[Cormega]], and [[C-Rayz Walz]].<ref name="complex"/> He relocated to [[New York City]] and got his start as a [[hip hop music|hip hop]] producer in the early 2000s, collaborating with rappers [[Obie Trice]], [[Ghostface Killah]], [[The Roots]], [[Cormega]], [[M.O.P.]], [[Rhymefest]], and [[AZ (rapper)|AZ]]. His career grew and, by the second half of 2000's first decade, he had worked with [[Ice Cube]], [[Slaughterhouse (group)|Slaughterhouse]], Eminem, [[Kanye West]] and [[Kid Cudi]].<ref name="complex"/>

===2008–2010: Dream On era===
In 2007, Haynie, along with record producer [[Plain Pat|Patrick “Plain Pat” Reynolds]], began co-managing the career of then-up-and-coming musician Kid Cudi. The two would go on to executive produce Cudi’s debut mixtape ''[[A Kid Named Cudi]]'' (2008). In late 2008, Haynie, Plain Pat and Kid Cudi launched their [[record label]], [[Dream On (record label)|Dream On]], in partnership with Kanye West's [[GOOD Music]] and [[Universal Motown]]. Haynie [[remix]]ed [[Michael Jackson]]'s 1972 song, "Maria (You Were the Only One)", for the 2009 album ''[[The Remix Suite|Michael Jackson: The Remix Suite]]''.<ref name="levine">{{Cite news |first=Mike |last=Levine |date=October 13, 2011 |title=Talking Shop With Emile |magazine=[[Electronic Musician]] |url=http://www.emusician.com/engineers-producers/0989/talking-shop-with-emile/143730 |access-date=February 4, 2012 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120208214924/http://www.emusician.com/engineers-producers/0989/talking-shop-with-emile/143730 |archive-date=February 8, 2012 |url-status=dead }}</ref> In February 2011 Kid Cudi announced Dream On had been dissolved.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://rapfix.mtv.com/2011/02/27/kid-cudi-splits-with-manager-launching-new-label/ |title=Kid Cudi Splits With Manager, Launching New Label |date=February 27, 2011 |publisher=MTV |access-date=October 17, 2011}}</ref> Cudi stated to ''[[Complex (magazine)|Complex]]'' magazine that they were still on good terms: "I wanted to try something new, and I wanted to take control of things myself.[...] There’s no hard feelings."<ref>{{cite news |url=http://www.complex.com/music/2011/09/kid-cudi-puzzling-2011-cover-story/page/2 |title=Kid Cudi: Puzzling (2011 Cover Story) |magazine=Complex |date=September 19, 2011 |access-date=October 17, 2011 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20111014054427/http://www.complex.com/music/2011/09/kid-cudi-puzzling-2011-cover-story/page/2 |archive-date=October 14, 2011 }}</ref> The label released Kid Cudi's albums, ''[[Man on the Moon: The End of Day]]'' (2009) and ''[[Man on the Moon II: The Legend of Mr. Rager]]'' (2010).<ref>{{Cite news |date=February 28, 2011 |title=Kid Cudi Splits With Label and Managers, Starts New Label |magazine=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] |url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/472845/kid-cudi-splits-with-label-and-managers-starts-new-label |access-date=February 4, 2012 }}</ref>

===2010–2014: Mainstream hits===
Haynie was nominated for the 2010 [[Grammy Award for Album of the Year]] for [[Eminem]]'s ''[[Recovery (Eminem album)|Recovery]]''.<ref>{{cite web |title=Nominees And Winners |publisher=[[Grammy.com]] |url=http://www.grammy.com/nominees?year=2010&genre=All |access-date=February 4, 2012 }}</ref> He co-produced Kanye West's 2010 single, "[[Runaway (Kanye West song)|Runaway]]", and expanded into [[pop music|pop]] and [[indie music]], working with [[Lana Del Rey]], [[Bruno Mars]] and [[Fun (band)|Fun]].<ref name="complex"/> Haynie produced Lana Del Rey's 2012 album, ''[[Born to Die (Lana Del Rey album)|Born to Die]]'', which debuted on the [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]] chart at number two, and at number one in Britain, Germany, Ireland, Switzerland and Austria, respectively.<ref>{{Cite news |first=Jacob |last=Brown |date=February 9, 2012 |title=A Star Is Born (and Scorned) |magazine=[[T (New York Times)|T]] |url=http://tmagazine.blogs.nytimes.com/2012/02/09/a-star-is-born-and-scorned/ |access-date=February 11, 2012 }}</ref> Alongside [[Jeff Bhasker]], Haynie also produced [[Fun (band)|Fun]]'s second album ''[[Some Nights (album)|Some Nights]]'' (2012). Haynie also co-wrote [[Lady Antebellum]]'s 2013's single, "[[Compass (Lady Antebellum song)|Compass]]".

===2015–present: ''We Fall''===
{{Main|We Fall}}

On January 19, 2015, Haynie announced he would be releasing his debut [[studio album]], titled ''[[We Fall]]'', featuring [[guest appearance]]s from [[Andrew Wyatt]], [[Brian Wilson]], [[Rufus Wainwright]], [[Lana Del Rey]], [[Charlotte Gainsbourg]], [[Sampha]], [[Devonte Hynes]], [[Nate Ruess]], [[Colin Blunstone]], [[Lykke Li]], [[Romy Madley Croft]], [[Randy Newman]], [[Father John Misty]] and [[Julia Holter]].<ref>{{cite web|last=Cliff |first=Aimee |url=http://www.thefader.com/2015/01/19/emile-haynie-album-lana-del-rey-wait-for-life |title=Producer Emile Haynie Announces Star-Studded Album, Shares New Track With Lana Del Rey |publisher=Thefader.com |date=2015-01-19 |access-date=2015-04-03}}</ref> The album, which was recorded over the course of six months in [[Los Angeles]]' [[Chateau Marmont]], was made available for pre-order the following day and was revealed to be released February 23, 2015, under [[Interscope Records]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.mtv.com/news/2053345/lana-del-rey-wait-for-life-emile-haynie/ |title=Lana Del Rey Is In Full-On Jazz Singer Mode On Emile Haynie's 'Wait For Life' |publisher=MTV.com |date=2015-01-19 |access-date=2015-04-03}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://pigeonsandplanes.com/2015/01/emile-haynie-ft-lana-del-rey-wait-for-life/ |title=Emile Haynie ft. Lana Del Rey - "Wait For Life" &#124; Pigeons & Planes |publisher=Pigeonsandplanes.com |access-date=2015-04-03}}</ref>

In January 2020, [[Hipgnosis Songs Fund]] confirmed the acquisition of Haynie’s music catalog. Details of the acquisition were first revealed in the [[Merck Mercuriadis]]-led, UK-based company’s interim report, published the month prior. Hipgnosis acquired 100% of Haynie’s worldwide copyrights, including publishing and writer share, as well as  producer royalty income streams, of his catalog comprising 122 songs.<ref name=mbww/>

==Personal life==
Haynie works out of his studio in the New York City neighborhood of [[Chelsea, Manhattan|Chelsea]].<ref name="levine"/>

==Legacy==
In 2020, Canadian music executive [[Merck Mercuriadis]], founder of Hipgnosis Songs Fund, called Haynie “one of the most influential producers of the last decade and with Lana Del Rey, Kid Cudi, Eminem, Bruno Mars, Fun and many others he has made important records that have inspired so many other great creators.”<ref name=mbww>{{Cite web|url=https://www.musicbusinessworldwide.com/hipgnosis-confirms-catalog-acquisition-from-lana-del-rey-kid-cudi-producer-emile-haynie/|title = Hipgnosis confirms catalog acquisition from Lana del Rey & Kid Cudi producer Emile Haynie|date = 9 January 2020}}</ref>
