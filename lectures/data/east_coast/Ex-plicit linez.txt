'''Brandon Jermaine Yun''', better known by his [[stage name]] '''EXP''' (pronounced "e-ex-pee"), formerly '''EX-PLICIT LINEZ''', is an  American [[rapper]] and former [[radio personality]] for New York Korean radio ''NYKR 1660 AM'', a mutual protégé of [[Drunken Tiger]] and [[Lee Hyun Do]] a.k.a. D.O. EXP first appeared on the underground rap scene in The Bronx in the mid 1990s on a DJ Platinum's mixtape series, ''Hate in Da Blood''. He is one of the first generation of Asian American rappers to be involved in the Hip-Hop scene along with Jamez, John "shogunna" Chee and [[Mountain Brothers]]. He is also known as one of the first Korean rappers to mix Korean and English words to rhyme in the song. He is of Korean descent.

Aside from releasing multiple mixtapes that gained heavy rotations in college radio stations and internet radios, EXP has gained notoriety for involvement in feuds with other Korean artists.

==Music career==
He became a member of '''The Movement''' in 2001. In 2007 he released his first United States radio single, ''Like That'', which was produced by former member of ([[Deux (band)|DEUX]]), [[Lee Hyun Do]] a.k.a. D.O. The single received spins throughout [[Atlanta]] radio stations. In addition to his solo works, EXP made numerous guest appearances internationally throughout Japan,<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.castle-records.net/products/detail.php?product_id=616 |title=Castle Records/商品詳細 Crazy T / 朝帰り |website=www.castle-records.net |access-date=27 January 2022 |archive-url=https://archive.today/20140928214759/http://www.castle-records.net/products/detail.php?product_id=616 |archive-date=28 September 2014 |url-status=dead}}</ref> China, France and [[Philippine]].

EXP's Supreme Hustle set a record for most downloaded Hip Hop album in Chinese music sites in 2005.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.565656.com/musiclist2/mmc_23109.htm|title=Ex-plicit Linez所有歌曲 Ex-plicit Linez新歌 Ex-plicit Linez最经典好听的歌曲 Ex-plicit Linez歌曲大全下载|website=565656.com|accessdate=19 April 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.yyy3g.com/yr/sclass.asp?sclass=3 |title=伊人音乐传播 |website=www.yyy3g.com |access-date=17 January 2022 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120402052633/http://www.yyy3g.com/yr/sclass.asp?sclass=3 |archive-date=2 April 2012 |url-status=dead}}</ref>

In 2007, prior to releasing his 7th album, [[Rebirth of YSJ]], [[Yoo Seung-jun]] showed interest in working with EXP, whom expressed his interest in making music in China.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.mtv123.com/zhuanjilist/13876.shtml|title=刘承俊最新专辑Rebirth of YSJ - 叮当音乐网|website=Mtv123.com|accessdate=19 April 2018}}</ref>

EXP was briefly signed to [[Tiger JK]]'s Jungle Entertainment in 2008 and Warner Music Korea under D.O from Deux. In 2010, [[Lee Hyun Do]] a.k.a. D.O. announced that he was producing for EXP.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://mlbpark.donga.com/bbs/view.php?bbs=mpark_bbs_bullpen09&idx=220906&cpage=14910&s_work=&select=&keyword= |title=↗ 파크에 오면 즐겁다 |website=mlbpark.donga.com |access-date=27 January 2022 |archive-url=https://archive.today/20140928214815/http://mlbpark.donga.com/bbs/view.php?bbs=mpark_bbs_bullpen09&idx=220906&cpage=14910&s_work=&select=&keyword= |archive-date=28 September 2014 |url-status=dead}}</ref> He also worked with K-Pop producer Seung Wook Yang (Producer of [[Click-B]]'s Oh Jong Hyuk and ezllife)

In December 2010, EXP became Urban A&R at [[S.M. Entertainment]] and [[Mnet (Korea)]] affiliate, MarCan Entertainment. He left the company due to mutual Differences.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://twitter.com/marcanent|title=Marcan Entertainment (@marcanent) - Twitter|website=Twitter.com|accessdate=19 April 2018}}</ref>

EXP recorded an entrance song "Baki's Theme" for Korean MMA Fighter Won sik "Parky" Park <ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.sherdog.com/fighter/Won-Sik-Park-24865|title=Won Sik|last=Sherdog.com|website=Sherdog|accessdate=19 April 2018}}</ref> for his bout against Kosuke Umeda. Park lost the bout via Unanimous Decision.

In 2014, EXP coached and prepared [[Sang A Yim-Propp]] for her performance on [[Seoul Broadcasting System|SBS]] Channel's Thanksgiving special show "Yul chang Club Some-Sing".<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.wowtv.co.kr/newscenter/news/view.asp?bcode=T32001000&wowcode=W014&artid=X20140911004239&compcode=HK|title='썸씽' 임상아 ＂이혼 후 10살 딸에게 의지＂|website=Wowtv.co.kr|accessdate=19 April 2018}}</ref>

==Personal life==
{{like resume|date=September 2023}}
In 2009, EXP supported and performed for Relay for Life (American Cancer Society) event in East Village, New York.

EXP is a fan of [[Mixed Martial Arts]]. He is a practitioner of Russian Martial Arts [[Sambo (martial art)]] and holds 5th Dan (Godan) Black belt in [[Aikido]].

In July 2014, EXP announced his involvement in upcoming horror movie ''Vamp Bikers DOS''.<ref name="imdb.com">{{cite web|url=https://www.imdb.com/title/tt3652744/|title=Vamp Bikers Dos|date=19 September 2015|website=IMDb.com|accessdate=19 April 2018}}</ref> The movie also featured [[Melle Mel]] and [[Grandmaster Caz]].

In October 2018, EXP announced his acting appearance in ''Amazon Original'' produced ''Japanese Borscht'' <ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.imdb.com/title/tt8718114/|title=Japanese Borscht 2019 |publisher=IMDb|access-date=3 February 2020}}</ref> Remake with distribution by ''Sony''. He also appears on the original soundtrack for the film.

In November 2020, EXP launched a clothing company, ''Thick Bad Wolf''.<ref name="Thickbadwolf.com">{{cite web|url=https://www.thickbadwolf.com/|title=Thick Bad Wolf |date=5 November 2020|website=Thickbadwolf.com}}</ref>

EXP also has hinted a return to the music scene from long hiatus.
