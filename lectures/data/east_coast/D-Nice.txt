'''Derrick T. Jones''' (born June 19, 1970), better known by his stage name '''D-Nice''', is an American DJ, record producer, and rapper who began his career in the mid-1980s with the [[Hip hop music|hip hop]] group [[Boogie Down Productions]].<ref>{{cite web|url={{AllMusic|class=artist|id=p24|pure_url=yes}}|title=Biography: D-Nice|last=Kellman|first=Andy|website=[[AllMusic]]|access-date=May 4, 2010}}</ref> He discovered [[Kid Rock]] in 1988, landing him a deal with [[Jive Records]].

== Life and career ==
Jones was born and raised in the [[Harlem]] neighborhood of [[Manhattan]], [[New York City]].<ref name="D-NiceAM">{{cite web|author=Andy Kellman|url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/d-nice-mn0000140270|title=D-Nice|publisher=[[AllMusic]]|access-date=June 11, 2020}}</ref> He later moved to [[The Bronx]] as a teenager. At age 15, it was there that he met [[Scott La Rock]] and later formed [[Boogie Down Productions]] along with [[KRS-One]] in 1986.<ref name="D-NiceAM" /> In his early career with the group, he was given the nicknames as "the Human TR-808" and "the 808". D-Nice also gained significant popularity when he produced the song "Self-Destruction" for the [[KRS-One#Stop the Violence Movement|Stop the Violence Movement]].<ref name="D-NiceAM" />

Soon after the song released, D-Nice signed a solo deal [[Jive Records]] and released his debut studio album called ''[[Call Me D-Nice]]'' in 1990.<ref name="D-NiceAM" /> The album peaked at #75 on the [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' Top Pop Albums]] and #12 on the [[Top R&B/Hip-Hop Albums|Top Black Albums]] chart. It was also rated 3.5 out of 5 mics by ''[[The Source]]'' magazine.<ref name="D-NiceAM" /> In 1991, D-Nice released his second studio album called ''[[To tha Rescue]]''. The album peaked at #137 on the Top Pop Albums chart and #27 on the Top R&B Albums chart. He collaborated with KRS-One, [[Naughty by Nature]], and [[Too Short]] for the album.<ref name="D-NiceAM" />

D-Nice clashed with Jive Records over his stylistic direction and soon stopped releasing music as a recording artist.<ref name="D-NiceAM" /> In 1996, his first daughter, Ashli Lyric Jones, was born.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://photos.essence.com/galleries/malinda_williams_wedding_photos_the_ceremony#42682 |title=Essence.com: Derrick Jones posed with his new family |access-date=August 18, 2010 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110224062828/http://photos.essence.com/galleries/malinda_williams_wedding_photos_the_ceremony#42682 |archive-date=February 24, 2011 |url-status=dead }}</ref> D-Nice became a web developer in the late 1990s and started his own creative services company in 2000.

He later had another daughter named Dylan Coleman-Jones with Kelli M. Coleman, Executive Vice President for [[GlobalHue]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://instagram.com/p/wwkKdYHUMr/?modal=true |archive-url=https://ghostarchive.org/iarchive/s/instagram/wwkKdYHUMr |archive-date=2021-12-24 |url-access=limited|title = D-Nice on Instagram: "I made it back from the @BETnetworks event in DC this morning to take the lil one to school. Although I love my job, I wish I could do this…"|website=Instagram.com}}{{cbignore}}</ref>

D-Nice began working as a photographer in the 2000s while also still being a DJ and shot the album covers for [[Carl Thomas (singer)|Carl Thomas]]' 2007 album ''[[So Much Better (album)|So Much Better]]''<ref>Carl Thomas – So Much Better (CD liner notes). Bungalo Records. 02097 01182</ref> and [[Pharoahe Monch]]'s 2011 album ''[[W.A.R. (We Are Renegades)]]''.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Bark|first1=Theo|title=Photosynthesis With D-Nice: A History of Hip-Hop Photography|url=http://theboombox.com/photosynthesis-with-d-nice-a-history-of-hip-hop-photography/|website=The Boombox|publisher=theboombox.com|access-date=June 8, 2014}}</ref> He also photographed the album cover for [[Kenny Lattimore]]'s 2017 album, ''[[Vulnerable (Kenny Lattimore album)|Vulnerable]]''.<ref>{{cite web|title=Kenny Lattimore To Get 'Vulnerable' On New Album As He Reveals Cover Art & Tracklist|url=http://www.soulbounce.com/2017/09/kenny-lattimore-get-vulnerable/|website=soulbounce.com|publisher=Kimberly Shines Media|access-date=March 24, 2018}}</ref>

[[File:MalindaWilliamsD-NiceJun06.jpg|thumb|D-Nice with [[Malinda Williams]]]]
In August 2008, he married actress [[Malinda Williams]], but the couple separated in October 2009. In February 2010, the couple filed for divorce<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.vibe.com/2010/02/malinda-x-d-nice-we-wanted-one-last|title=Malinda x D-Nice: We Wanted This One To Last|date=February 24, 2010|website=Vibe.com|access-date=February 14, 2020}}</ref> and the divorce was finalized on June 14, 2010.

In March 2020, D-Nice began hosting Homeschool at Club Quarantine on Instagram Live from his home as a way for people to come together and help others cope with the [[COVID-19]] global health crisis. The 3/21 Saturday night dance party ran 9 hours and D-Nice urged people to "take care of one another and wash their hands."<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.forbes.com/sites/adriennegibbs/2020/03/21/dj-d-nice-hosts-an-ig-live-uplift-coronavirus-party-and-everybody-shows-up/#5e9c8777229c|title= DJ D-Nice Hosts An IG Live Social Distancing Uplift Party And Everybody Shows Up|work=Forbes}}</ref> It drew over 100,000 viewers, including [[Rihanna]], [[Lenny Kravitz]], [[Nile Rodgers]], [[Lalah Hathaway]], [[Bernie Sanders]], [[Joe Biden]], [[Dwyane Wade]], [[Janet Jackson]], [[Fab Five Freddy]], [[Michelle Obama]], [[Mark Zuckerberg]], [[Donnie Wahlberg]], [[Angela Bassett]], and [[Sheila E.]], among others.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.complex.com/music/2020/03/dj-d-nice-instagram-social-distancing-party|title=Rihanna, Dwyane Wade, Bernie Sanders, and More Attend DJ D-Nice's Virtual 'Social Distancing Dance Party'|work=Complex}}</ref>  The following day, March 22, his stream capped at over 150,000 simultaneous viewers.{{citation needed|date=March 2020}} D-Nice won the [[2020 Webby Awards|2020 Webby Award]] for Artist of the Year in the category Special Achievement.<ref name="Kastrenakes">{{cite web |last1=Kastrenakes |first1=Jacob |title=Here are all the winners of the 2020 Webby Awards |url=https://www.theverge.com/2020/5/20/21263445/2020-webby-awards-winners-lil-nas-x-nasa-jon-krasinski |website=The Verge |access-date=May 22, 2020 |language=en |date=May 20, 2020}}</ref> He also was one of the Honoree Recipients for the Shine a Light Award during the [[BET Awards 2020|2020 BET Awards]] for his contributions of ''Club Quarantine,'' alongside co-recipients with ''[[Verzuz]]'' creators [[Timbaland]] and [[Swizz Beatz]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=BET Awards Highlight: BET Awards 2020 Shine a Light Award Honorees|url=https://www.bet.com/video/betawards/2020/highlights/bet-awards-2020-shine-a-light-award-honorees.html?cid=facebook|access-date=June 29, 2020|website=BET.com}}</ref>
On March 27, 2021, he was awarded Entertainer of the Year at the [[52nd NAACP Image Awards]]. On June 22, 2021, he was awarded the ASCAP Voice of the Culture Award for being a beacon of hope and source of inspiration during the COVID-19 pandemic. His livestreamed DJ sets remain a popular online destination for thousands weekly, and he has more than 2.6 million followers on [[Instagram]].{{citation needed|date=June 2021}}
