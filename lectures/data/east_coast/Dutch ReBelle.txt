{{Short description|American rapper}}
'''Vanda Bernadeau''', professionally known as '''Dutch ReBelle''', is a rapper from [[Boston|Boston, Massachusetts]].<ref>{{Cite web |title=ReBelle with a cause |url=https://www.bostonglobe.com/arts/2012/10/11/scene-and-heard-rebelle-with-cause/VSrHmh7qvvDCv65BmBRweJ/story.html |access-date=2023-07-22 |website=The Boston Globe |language=en-US}}</ref>

== Early life ==
ReBelle was born in [[Hinche]], [[Haiti]]. She was raised in [[Mattapan]] and [[Milton, Massachusetts|Milton]][[Massachusetts|.]] ReBelle began rapping at age 9. In college, she decided to pursue a music career. ReBelle was discovered while [[Hairdresser|doing]] [[Dreadlocks|locs]] at the house of a customer whose roommate had a radio show, when she rapped spontaneously [[Radio broadcasting|on air]].<ref name=":1">{{Cite web |title=Lyricist Dutch ReBelle on cusp of a breakout year {{!}} Dorchester Reporter |url=https://www.dotnews.com/2013/lyricist-dutch-rebelle-cusp-breakout-year |access-date=2023-07-22 |website=www.dotnews.com |language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |title=Boston's ReBelle braces herself for the big time |url=https://www.bostonglobe.com/arts/music/2014/09/11/boston-rapper-rebelle-braces-herself-for-big-time/2jg6zrelZUzuplK0QSKYpO/story.html |access-date=2023-07-22 |website=The Boston Globe |language=en-US}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |date=2023 |title=Northampton to host its second Power of Truths Arts & Education Festival |url=https://www.gazettenet.com/Northampton-to-host-its-second-Power-of-Truths-Arts---Education-Festival-50701569 |website=Daily Hampshire Gazette}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |date=2018-10-26 |title=Local Female Musicians and Their Upcoming Boston Shows |url=https://www.exhalelifestyle.com/local-female-musicians-and-their-upcoming-boston-shows/ |access-date=2023-07-22 |website=Exhale Lifestyle |language=en-US}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |last=Bielagus |first=Abby |date=2019-06-19 |title=Boston's New Creative Guard |url=https://www.bostonmagazine.com/arts-entertainment/2019/06/19/boston-creative-guard/ |access-date=2023-07-22 |website=Boston Magazine |language=en-US}}</ref>

== Career ==
Regarding her stage name, ReBelle has stated, “When I was in high school, it kind of just happened that people started calling me [[Von Dutch]] after the trucker hat line. Then it got shortened to Dutch in college, and when I started taking music seriously, I added the ReBelle to the end. It was an ode to my country, but it was also from the Lauryn Hill song “I Find It Hard To Say (Rebel)”. ReBelle played her first gig at [[Boston University]]. She then went on to perform at the [[House of Blues]] and the [[Hard Rock Cafe|Hard Rock Café]].<ref name=":1" />

''KillerBoomBox.com'' wrote, "If [[Ghostface Killah]] and [[Lauryn Hill|Lauryn]] had a baby, it would be Dutch."<ref name=":1" /> She was named ''[[Boston (magazine)|Boston]]''<nowiki/>'s Best Music Artist of 2018.<ref>{{Cite web |title=BEST OF BOSTON: Best Music Artist |url=https://www.bostonmagazine.com/best-of-boston-archive/award/music-artist/ |access-date=2023-07-22 |website=Boston Magazine |language=en-US}}</ref> ''[[WBUR-FM|WBUR]]'' dubbed her album, ''Bang Bang,'' one of the best local hip-hop albums in 2018.<ref>{{Cite web |title=7 Best Albums From Boston's Hip-Hop Scene This Year |url=https://www.wbur.org/news/2018/12/21/bostons-best-hip-hop-2018 |access-date=2023-07-22 |website=www.wbur.org |language=en}}</ref> In 2020, ''[[Bandcamp]]'' wrote, "ReBelle lays bars over [[Trap music|trap beats]], with a hard edge comparable to [[Memphis, Tennessee|Memphis]]’s [[Gangsta Boo]] or Diamond from [[Atlanta]]’s [[Crime Mob]]."<ref>{{Cite web |date=2020-05-12 |title=In Boston, Women and Non-Binary Rappers Own the Hip Hop Scene |url=https://daily.bandcamp.com/scene-report/boston-rap-list |access-date=2023-07-22 |website=Bandcamp Daily}}</ref>

ReBelle has stated she is often the only woman in the room in hip-hop spaces and has to create her own space within these environments.<ref>{{Cite web |date=2020 |title=City Scenes: How Boston's DIY Hip-Hop Community Is Fending For Itself |url=https://www.npr.org/2020/10/14/922245184/city-scenes-how-bostons-diy-hip-hop-community-is-fending-for-itself |website=NPR}}</ref> She has said working in a music industry dominated by men is much easier than surviving other traumas she's experienced.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Drive: Dutch ReBelle's Rocky Road |url=https://www.wbur.org/news/2013/04/04/dutch-rebelle-sunday |access-date=2023-07-22 |website=www.wbur.org |language=en}}</ref> She has faced discrimination when trying to book Boston venues as a hip-hop artist.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Is Boston Hostile To Hip-Hop? |url=https://www.wbur.org/news/2018/02/28/boston-hip-hop |access-date=2023-07-22 |website=www.wbur.org |language=en}}</ref>
