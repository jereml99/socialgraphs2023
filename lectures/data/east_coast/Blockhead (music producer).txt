'''Tony Simon''' (born October 8, 1976),<ref>{{cite tweet|user=AesopRockWins|number=651992519543287808|date=October 7, 2015|title=Happy Birthday to my old friend @BlockheadNYC. That's my G right there.}}</ref><ref>{{cite tweet|user=BlockheadNYC|number=118540971075047424|date=September 26, 2011|title=Just found out Nick Cannon and I share the same birthday. Fuck my parents.}}</ref><ref>{{cite tweet|user=BlockheadNYC|number=405719256911716352|date=November 27, 2013|title=@ArmchairHater I'm 37 years old. I have no clue who any of those people are.}}</ref> better known by his stage name '''Blockhead''', and formerly known as '''Funky Omelet''', is an American hip hop record producer and DJ from [[Manhattan]], [[New York (state)|New York]]. Aside from his solo efforts released on the [[Ninja Tune]] label, he is most associated with producing tracks for [[Aesop Rock]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://hiphopdx.com/news/id.24080/title.blockhead-names-his-top-5-hip-hop-albums|title=Blockhead Names His Top 5 Hip Hop Albums|work=[[HipHopDX]]|first=Jay|last=Balfour|date=May 26, 2013|access-date=June 7, 2017}}</ref> He has been a member of the groups such as [[Party Fun Action Committee]]<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.allmusic.com/artist/party-fun-action-committee-mn0002303893/biography|title=Party Fun Action Committee - Biography|work=[[AllMusic]]|first=John|last=Bush|access-date=June 7, 2017}}</ref> and The Mighty Jones.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://phatfriend.com/2015/12/01/the-mighty-jones-album-gone-long-is-here/|title=The Mighty Jones Album "Gone Long" is here!|work=Phat Friend|date=December 1, 2015|access-date=June 7, 2017|archive-date=August 31, 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170831002754/https://phatfriend.com/2015/12/01/the-mighty-jones-album-gone-long-is-here/|url-status=dead}}</ref>

==Biography==
Simon was born and raised in [[Downtown Manhattan]].<ref name=ninjatune/> He is the son of the late [[Sidney Simon]], a well-known sculptor in [[New York City]], and Renee Adriance, a social worker.<ref name=nytimes>{{cite web|url=https://www.nytimes.com/1997/08/08/arts/sidney-simon-sculptor-80-founded-school.html|title=Sidney Simon, Sculptor, 80; Founded School|work=[[The New York Times]]|first=Judith H.|last=Dobrzynski|date=August 8, 1997|access-date=June 7, 2017}}</ref> He has one brother and five half siblings.<ref name=nytimes/> At a young age, he loved hip hop in both the lyrical and musical aspects.<ref name=ninjatune>{{cite web|url=http://ninjatune.net/artist/blockhead|title=Blockhead|work=[[Ninja Tune]]|access-date=June 7, 2017}}</ref> In his early teens, Simon and his friends formed a group called the Overground, where he started making music.<ref name=ninjatune/> As an aspiring rapper, he enrolled in [[Boston University]] and encountered [[Aesop Rock]], another BU student.<ref name=prefixmag2>{{cite web|url=http://www.prefixmag.com/features/blockhead/feeling-his-way-part-2-of-2/12570/|title=Blockhead: Feeling his way (Part 2 of 2)|work=Prefix|first=Bryan|last=Whitefield|date=December 6, 2005|access-date=June 7, 2017}}</ref> At this point, Simon quit rapping and focused on producing music.<ref name=prefixmag2/> According to him, his stage name comes from the shape of his head: "While it's not square, it's pretty close."<ref name=artistdirect>{{cite web|url=http://www.artistdirect.com/artist/bio/blockhead/2628020|title=Blockhead Biography|work=[[Artistdirect]]|access-date=June 7, 2017}}</ref>
