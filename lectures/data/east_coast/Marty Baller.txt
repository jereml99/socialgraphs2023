'''Milton Delano Martin III''', better known by his [[stage name]] '''Marty Baller''', is an American rapper, songwriter, dancer, actor, and model from the [[Harlem]] neighborhood of [[Manhattan]], [[New York (state)|New York]]. Aside from his solo career, he was a member of the hip-hop group [[ASAP Mob|A$AP Mob]] and the official tour [[hype man]] to [[RCA Records]] recording artist [[ASAP Ferg]].<ref>{{cite web|title=A$AP Mob|url=http://www.asapmob.com/tag/marty-baller/|publisher=www.asapmob.com|accessdate=25 April 2017}}</ref> Marty Baller is often referred to as "Ferg's protégé" and the "Face of [[Traplord]]".<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.complex.com/music/2016/12/asap-ferg-new-video-uzi-gang-feat-lil-uzi-vert-marty-baller/|title= Watch ASAP Ferg's New Video Uzi Gang Feat Lil Uzi Vert and Marty Baller|publisher=[[Complex (magazine)|Complex]]}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|title=You Need to Be Paying Attention to The Trap Lord's Marty Baller|url=http://www.asapferg.com/you-need-to-be-paying-attention-to-trap-lords-marty-baller/|accessdate=25 April 2017|publisher=[[ASAP Ferg]]}}</ref>

Marty Baller gained recognition after releasing his debut [[single (music)|single]], "Big Timers" featuring [[ASAP Ferg]], and the track "I'm A Dog", in collaboration with ASAP Ferg and [[Migos]], on his debut mixtape ''Marty G Raw''.<ref>{{cite web|title=Migos and ASAP Ferg Join Marty Baller on 'I'm a Dog'|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2016/11/migos-asap-ferg-marty-baller-im-a-dog/|publisher=[[XXL (magazine)|XXL]]|accessdate=25 April 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|title=A$AP Ferg Introduces Marty Baller to the World with a New Short Film|url=https://noisey.vice.com/en_us/article/adollarap-ferg-introduces-marty-baller-to-the-world-with-a-new-short-film|accessdate=25 April 2017|publisher=[[Noisey]]|language=en-us}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Marty Baller - Marty G Raw|url=http://www.hotnewhiphop.com/marty-baller-marty-g-raw-new-mixtape.116631.html|publisher=HotNewHipHop|accessdate=25 April 2017}}</ref>

Shortly after launching his solo career, Marty Baller collaborated with a variety of rappers including [[Lil Uzi Vert]] on ''[[Always Strive and Prosper]]'', [[Rich The Kid]], [[Smoke DZA]] and [[Fatman Scoop]].<ref>{{cite news|title=A$AP Ferg, Lil Uzi Vert, and Marty Baller Go Punk in New Video "Uzi Gang"|url=https://noisey.vice.com/en_us/article/adollarap-ferg-lil-uzi-vert-i-marty-baller-punkowo-w-nowym-klipie-uzi-gang|accessdate=25 April 2017|publisher=[[Noisey]]|language=en-us}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Marty Baller and Rich The Kid Connect on "Rambo"|url=https://hypebeast.com/2017/1/marty-baller-rich-the-kid-rambo|website=HYPEBEAST|accessdate=25 April 2017|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Marty Baller Shows You How "Roll The Bud Up" With Smoke DZA & Fatman Scoop|url=https://hypebeast.com/2016/4/marty-baller-roll-the-bud-up|website=HYPEBEAST|accessdate=25 April 2017|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|title=Video: A$AP Ferg – "Uzi Gang" ft. Lil Uzi Vert and Marty Baller|url=http://www.spin.com/2016/12/asap-ferg-uzi-gang-lil-uzi-vert-marty-baller/|accessdate=25 April 2017|publisher=[[Spin (magazine)|Spin]]|date=20 December 2016}}</ref>

==Career==
In 2007, Marty Baller joined the [[ASAP Mob|A$AP Mob]] crew, a Harlem-based collective of rappers, producers, music video directors, fashion designers, and bikers who shared similar interests in music, fashion, style, and art.<ref name="hypetrak1">{{cite web |last=Huynh |first=Davis |url=http://hypetrak.com/2012/01/asap-rocky-always-strive-and-prosper/ |title=A$AP Rocky – Always $trive and Prosper |publisher=Hypetrak |date= |accessdate=2013-07-04 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131016011341/http://hypetrak.com/2012/01/asap-rocky-always-strive-and-prosper/ |archive-date=2013-10-16 |url-status=dead }}</ref> It had been formed by A$AP Yams, A$AP Bari and A$AP Illz.<ref name="vvblogs">{{cite web|author= |url=http://blogs.villagevoice.com/music/2012/08/meet_asap_mob.php |title=Meet The A$AP Mob: Talking To Ant, Ferg, J. Scott, Nast, Twelvy, And a compilation of his YouTube hits compiled by a fan in Paris.|publisher=[[The Village Voice]]}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|last=Roos|first=Brandon E.|date=November 29, 2011|url=http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/interviews/id.1810/title.asap-rocky-says-deluxe-version-of-liveloveaap-is-coming-reveals-identity-of-purple-swag-video-girl|title=ASAP Rocky Says Deluxe Version Of "LiveLoveA$AP" Is Coming, Reveals Identity Of "Purple Swag" Video Girl|publisher=[[HipHopDX]]|accessdate=June 4, 2012}}</ref>

Marty Baller's rapping skills were noticed by [[ASAP Ferg]] while growing up in Harlem.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://noisey.vice.com/read/lil-uzi-vert-noisey-next|title=Marty Baller is living in the future of rap - Noisey|publisher=[[Vice (magazine)|Vice]]}}</ref> Marty also gained wider attention and notoriety after being mentioned on [[ASAP Ferg]]'s single [[Shabba (song)|"Shabba"]] and "[[New Level]]".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://genius.com/1938317|title=Shabba by A$AP Ferg|publisher=[[Genius (website)|Genius]]}}</ref> He also gained greater notoriety through weekly installments of Wavy Wednesdays through ''[[The Fader]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.thefader.com/2016/03/09/asap-ferg-shares-i-dont-care-with-marty-baller-for-wavy-wednesdays/|title= A$AP Ferg Shares "I Don't Care" With Marty Baller For Wavy Wednesdays|publisher=[[The Fader]]}}</ref> In 2015, drawing up inspiration from the [[Big Tymers]], Marty released the track "Big Timers" featuring [[ASAP Ferg]] to critical acclaim and later followed up the single.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.thefader.com/2015/12/28/marty-baller-asap-ferg-big-timers/|title= Mart Baller And A$AP Ferg Link Up on "Big Timers"|publisher=[[The Fader]]}}</ref>

Marty then released his solo mixtape ''Marty G Raw'' on November 11, 2016 with guest features from [[ASAP Ferg]] and [[Migos]].<ref>{{cite web|url= http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2016/11/marty-baller-marty-g-raw-mixtape/ |title=Marty Baller Drops 'Marty G Raw' Mixtape]|publisher=[[XXL (magazine)|XXL]]}}</ref>

==Musical style==
According to ''[[XXL (magazine)|XXL]]'', Marty Baller "is carving out his own path in hip-hop.<ref>{{cite web|title=Migos and ASAP Ferg Join Marty Baller on 'I'm a Dog'|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2016/11/migos-asap-ferg-marty-baller-im-a-dog/|publisher=[[XXL (magazine)|XXL]]|accessdate=25 April 2017}}</ref> ''[[Vice (magazine)|Noisey]]'' wrote "Marty Baller is breaking through. Previously known for a supporting role in A$AP Mob, he’s…putting out promising tracks and [is] ready".<ref>{{cite news|title=A$AP Ferg Introduces Marty Baller to the World with a New Short Film|url=https://noisey.vice.com/en_us/article/adollarap-ferg-introduces-marty-baller-to-the-world-with-a-new-short-film|accessdate=25 April 2017|publisher=[[Noisey]]|language=en-us}}</ref>

==Influences==
In an interview with [[The Hundreds]], Marty Baller stated his influences are [[ASAP Rocky]], [[ASAP Ferg]], [[Jay-Z]], [[Lil Wayne]], [[Andre 3000]] and [[Michael Jackson]].<ref>{{cite web|title=You Need to Be Paying Attention to Trap Lord's Marty Baller|url=https://thehundreds.com/blogs/content/marty-baller|publisher=[[The Hundreds]]|accessdate=25 April 2017}}</ref>
