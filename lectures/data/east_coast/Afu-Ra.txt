'''Aaron Ocosice Phillip''' (born January 31, 1974), better known by his stage name '''Afu-Ra''', is an American [[Underground hip hop|underground rapper]]. A member of the [[Gang Starr Foundation]], he has worked closely with its members, including [[Gang Starr]], [[Jeru the Damaja]], [[Big Shug]] and [[Group Home]].<ref>[{{AllMusic|class=artist|id=p370024/biography|pure_url=yes}} allmusic ((( Afu-Ra > Biography )))<!-- Bot generated title -->]</ref>

==Career==
Afu-Ra grew up in [[Brooklyn]], with frequent collaborator [[Jeru the Damaja]].<ref name="Jeru interview">{{Cite web|url=https://hiphopdx.com/news/id.24076/title.jeru-the-damaja-names-his-5-favorite-hip-hop-albums|title=Jeru The Damaja Names His 5 Favorite Hip Hop Albums|date=May 24, 2013|website=HipHopDX}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.buzz.ie/music/jeru-damaja-interview-thought-save-world-246808|title=Jeru the Damaja Interview: 'I thought I could save the world'|first=Conor|last=McCaffrey|website=Buzz.ie}}</ref>

Afu-Ra's first studio appearance was on Jeru's 1994 album ''[[The Sun Rises in the East]]'', on the song "Mental Stamina". He made another appearance on Jeru's second album ''[[Wrath of the Math]]'', on the track "Physical Stamina", in 1996. Afu's debut single, "Whirlwind Thru Cities", was released in 1998, reaching the top 20 on ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]''<nowiki>'</nowiki>s [[Hot Rap Tracks|Hot Rap Singles]] chart. In 1999, he released his second single, "Defeat" b/w "Mortal Kombat". His debut album, ''[[Body of the Life Force]]'', was released in October 2000.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://hiphopdx.com/reviews/id.4/title.afu-ra-the-body-of-the-life-force|title=Afu-Ra - The Body of the Life Force|date=February 18, 2001|website=HipHopDX}}</ref> The album featured production from [[DJ Premier]], [[DJ Muggs]], [[True Master]] and [[Da Beatminerz]]. Guests included [[Wu-Tang Clan]]'s [[GZA]] and [[Masta Killa]], [[M.O.P.]], [[Ky-Mani Marley]], and the [[Cocoa Brovaz]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.nme.com/reviews/reviews-nme-3219-338125|title=Afu-Ra: 'Body Of The Life Force' &#124; NME|website=[[NME]] |date=September 12, 2005}}</ref> The album peaked at 183 on the [[Billboard 200]],<ref name="BB 200">{{Cite magazine|url=https://www.billboard.com/artist/afu-ra/chart-history/tlp/|title=Afu-Ra|magazine=Billboard}}</ref> 13 on the [[Independent Albums|independent]] chart,<ref name="BB IND">{{Cite magazine|url=https://www.billboard.com/artist/afu-ra/chart-history/ind/|title=Afu-Ra|magazine=Billboard}}</ref> and 42 on [[Top R&B/Hip-Hop Albums|hip hop]].<ref name="BB HH">{{Cite magazine|url=https://www.billboard.com/artist/afu-ra/chart-history/blp/|title=Afu-Ra|magazine=Billboard}}</ref>

Afu released his second effort ''[[Life Force Radio]]'' in May 2002 on [[Koch Records]], featuring production from DJ Premier, [[True Master]], [[Easy Mo Bee]], [[Domingo (producer)|Domingo]] and [[Ayatollah (producer)|Ayatollah]]. Guests included [[Guru (rapper)|Guru]], [[Big Daddy Kane]], [[RZA]], and [[Teena Marie]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://hiphopdx.com/reviews/id.177/title.afu-ra-life-force-radio|title=Afu-Ra - Life Force Radio|date=June 18, 2002|website=HipHopDX}}</ref> The album was slightly less successful, peaking at 184 on the ''Billboard'' 200,<ref name="BB 200"/> 17 on the independent album chart,<ref name="BB IND"/> though 29 on hip hop.<ref name="BB HH"/>

In 2004, he released a compilation album titled ''Afu-Ra presents Perverted Monks'', followed by a studio album in 2005, titled ''[[State of the Arts]]'' on [[Decon Records]].<ref name="Jeru interview"/> The latter featured production by DJ Premier and [[Bronze Nazareth]] and guest vocals from Masta Killa, [[Royce da 5'9"]], and [[Gentleman (musician)|Gentleman]]. In 2012, he independently released ''Body Of The Life Force 2'', which was poorly received.
