'''Robert Alan Diaz'''  (September 17, 1975 – June 9, 2015)<ref>[http://www.legacy.com/ns/-pumpkinhead-obituary/175045053 Robert Alan Diaz Obituary] Legacy.com</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=avtPL5jq_ag|title = - YouTube|website = [[YouTube]]}}</ref> known by his stage name '''Pumpkinhead''' or '''P.H.''', was an American [[rapper]] and [[Hip hop music|hip hop]] artist. He grew up in the [[Park Slope]] area of [[Brooklyn]], [[New York City|New York]] with his mother and younger sister.

==Music career==

Pumpkinhead started writing rap lyrics at age 10 during his 6th grade language arts class after hearing the song "Nightmares" by [[Dana Dane]]. He also stated he was influenced by [[Redman (rapper)|Redman]] and the [[Leaders of the New School]], as well as [[Doug E. Fresh]].<ref name="Latin">{{cite web|url=http://www.latinrapper.com/featurednews8.html|title=Rapper Pumpkinhead Interview - Brooklyn Academy LatinRapper.com|work=latinrapper.com}}</ref> He also cites his experience of meeting [[Tupac Shakur|2Pac]] and witnessing his ability to write and record songs on the spot as a motivating factor in his emceeing.<ref name="Latin" />

Pumpkinhead got a break in New York City hip hop when a friend of his gave a demo tape to [[Bobbito Garcia]] of the ''Stretch & Bobbito Show'' on [[WKCR]].<ref>[http://www.hiphopcanada.com/2015/06/pumpkinhead-aka-ph-brooklyn-rapper-passes-away-at-39-news/ Pumpkinhead aka PH, Brooklyn rapper passes away at 39] hiphopcanada.com</ref>

Pumpkinhead's breakthrough single was 1997's "Dynamic," which became a staple of [[mixtape]]s in the late 1990s. The [[Beat Junkies|World Famous Beat Junkies]] featured the remix—featuring guest appearances by Poka Face, Meat Pie, Ocean, [[Jean Grae|What? What?]], CES, DCQ and The Bad Seed—on their second mix CD, ''The World Famous Beat Junkies, Vol. 2'' in 1998.

Pumpkinhead has been a guest on several hip hop albums by other artists. He appears on [[Immortal Technique]]'s ''[[Revolutionary Vol. 1]]'' (2001) and ''[[Revolutionary Vol. 2]]'' (2003).
He guest starred on [[Jean Grae]]'s 2003 [[extended play|EP]] ''[[The Bootleg of the Bootleg EP|The Bootleg of the Bootleg]]'', appearing on the track "Code Red" with  [[Block McCloud]]. In 2013 he appeared on the track "Welcome To The Show" alongside Long Island emcees the Lab Ratz (Dave Z and Lantz).<ref>[http://www.lantz.bandcamp.com Lantz on Bandcamp]</ref> In 2009, he started going by PH and released ''My Era (No Skinny Jeans Allowed)'', a mixtape remixing several 90's classics.<ref>{{cite web|title=PH (The Artist Formerly Known As Pumpkinhead) - My Era (No Skinny Jeans Allowed)|url=http://www.thatsthatish.com/2009/08/ph-artist-formerly-known-as-pumpkinhead.html|publisher=thatsthatish.com|accessdate=5 May 2013}}</ref>

==Death==

Pumpkinhead died  on June 9, 2015, in a New Jersey hospital, while waiting to undergo a gall stone surgery. The cause of death has not been disclosed.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.ballerstatus.com/2015/06/09/ny-rap-vet-pumpkinhead-dead-at-38/ |title=NY Rap Vet Pumpkinhead Passes Away |publisher=BallerStatus.com |date=June 9, 2015 |access-date=June 9, 2015 |archive-date=October 27, 2015 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20151027010634/http://www.ballerstatus.com/2015/06/09/ny-rap-vet-pumpkinhead-dead-at-38/ |url-status=dead }}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://hiphopdx.com/news/id.34179/title.pharoahe-monch-marco-polo-react-to-pumpkindhead-passing-away|title=Talib Kweli & Pharoahe Monch React To Pumpkinhead Passing Away|date=June 9, 2015|work=HipHopDX}}</ref>
