'''Heather B. Gardner''' (born November 13, 1970),<ref>{{cite book|first=Joel|last=Whitburn|author-link=Joel Whitburn|title=Joel Whitburn's Music Stars: Brief Bios of Every Recording Artist Who Ever Charted|year=2009|publisher=Hal Leonard Corporation|page=114|isbn=978-0-8982-0176-5}}</ref> billed professionally as '''Heather B.''', is an American rapper, reality television personality, and radio host who first gained fame as a member of the hip hop group [[Boogie Down Productions]] before becoming a cast member on ''[[The Real World: New York]]'', the 1992 inaugural season of [[MTV]]'s reality show ''[[The Real World (TV series)|The Real World]]''.<ref name=allmusic>{{cite web |url={{AllMusic|class=artist|id=p26505|pure_url=yes}} |title=Heather B. |access-date=February 21, 2008 |last=Kurutz |first=Steve |year=2008 |work=[[Allmusic]] |publisher=[[All Media Guide]]}}</ref>

==Career==
From February 16 to May 18, 1992, Gardner filmed ''[[The Real World: New York]]'', the first season of MTV's reality television series, ''[[The Real World (TV series)|The Real World]]'', on which she appeared as a cast member.<ref name=RWHouses>[http://www.realworldhouses.com/realworld1.html "SoHo Loft"]. realworldhouses.com. November 28, 2010</ref> The series premiered May 21 that year. On the show, she was depicted as a [[Hip hop music|hip-hop]] artist with the group [[Boogie Down Productions]], on the verge of getting her big career break.<ref name=MTVBio>{{cite web|url=http://www.mtv.com/shows/realworld-season1/cast_member.jhtml?personalityId=1023|title=The Real World: New York: Cast: Heather B|publisher=[[MTV]]|date=1992|accessdate=September 14, 2011|archivedate=November 8, 2010|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20101108011608/https://www.mtv.com/shows/realworld-season1/cast_member.jhtml?personalityId=1023}}</ref> During the season, she is seen recording her first album.<ref name=MTVBio/><ref>{{cite episode|title=Julie and Eric...Could it be Love?|series=[[The Real World: New York]]|season=1|number=2|network=MTV}}</ref> According to MTV's biography for her: "She has a lot of drive and dedication to whatever she is doing. She makes friends quickly and always speaks her mind regardless of the consequences."<ref name=MTVBio/>

Gardner was affiliated with the rap group Boogie Down Productions,<ref name=allmusic/> and was signed to [[Pendulum Records]] in 1995. In 1996, she released her first album, ''[[Takin' Mine]]''. The single "All Glocks Down", an anti-gun violence anthem, received radio play, as did the follow-up single "If Headz Only Knew."<ref>{{cite magazine |url=http://www.billboard.com/charts/1993-02-06/rap-song |title=Chart Listing For The Week Of Feb 06 1993 |access-date=February 21, 2008 |date=February 6, 1993 |magazine=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] |publisher=[[Nielsen Company|Nielsen Business Media, Inc.]] |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080526085820/http://www.billboard.com/bbcom/esearch/chart_display.jsp?cfi=369&cfgn=Singles&cfn=Hot+Rap+Singles&ci=3013919&cdi=6695214&cid=02%2F06%2F1993 |archive-date=May 26, 2008 |url-status=dead }}</ref>

Gardner scored a minor role in the 1995 film ''[[Dead Presidents]]'' as "Peaches". In 1997, Gardner signed with [[MCA Records]] and in 1998 she released the single, "Do You," which had a considerable amount of television and radio airplay. In 2002 Gardner produced her second album, ''Eternal Affairs'', with production from Pete Rock and DJ Premier. The album met with positive reviews despite not charting on ''Billboard''.<ref name=AllHipHop>AbduSalaam, Ismael. [http://allhiphop.com/stories/news/archive/2010/03/12/22145013.aspx "Heather B. Returns With DJ Premier For 'Open Bar'"]. [[AllHipHop]]. March 12, 2010</ref>

Outside of her music career, Gardner has appeared in various projects and television specials related to ''The Real World''. She also appeared in the film ''[[The Wedding Video (2003 film)|The Wedding Video]]'' directed by ''Real World'' alum [[Norman Korpi]], and a 2005 television commercial for [[America Online]] anti-virus software.<ref>Cooper, Gael Fashingbauer. [https://web.archive.org/web/20121005085755/http://today.msnbc.msn.com/id/7046596/ns/today-entertainment/t/will-reality-tv-return-paradise/#.TnA1fE8yS2Y "Will reality TV return to 'Paradise'?"]. Today Reality TV. [[MSNBC]]. March 15, 2005</ref> Gardner's third studio album, ''Open Bar'', which was executive-produced by DJ Premier.<ref name=AllHipHop/>

As of 2012, Gardner is the co-host of the [[Sirius Satellite Radio]] show ''Sway in the Morning'' with former [[MTV]] reporter [[Sway Calloway]].<ref>{{cite web|last=Chudnofsky|first=Lisa|title=Heather B From The First Season Of 'Real World': Where Is She Now?|url=http://remotecontrol.mtv.com/2013/03/20/real-world-new-york-heather-b/|publisher=[[MTV]]|access-date=January 20, 2014}}</ref> In 2016 the duo appeared in the twelfth episode, "Soliloquy of Chaos,"  of ''[[Luke Cage (TV series)|Luke Cage]]'', interviewing [[Method Man]], about being rescued by Cage during a hold-up of a Harlem bodega.<ref>Cecchini, Mike (October 1, 2016). [http://www.denofgeek.com/us/books-comics/luke-cage/258700/luke-cage-complete-marvel-universe-easter-eggs-and-reference-guide/page/0/11 "Luke Cage: Complete Marvel Comics Easter Eggs and Reference Guide – Luke Cage Episode 12: Soliloquy of Chaos"]. Den of Geek.</ref>

She appeared as herself in the 2018 [[Bruce Willis]] film ''[[Death Wish (2018 film)|Death Wish]]''.

In 2021, she portrayed the character Aunt Ruth in the [[Jussie Smollett]] movie ''B-Boy Blues'', Smollett's adaptation of the novel series written by [[James Earl Hardy]].

==Personal life==
Gardner married fellow hiphop artist born E. Gray, a former member of the group [[Bravehearts]] who goes by the professional name Horse. In a 2010  interview with ''The Urban Daily'' Gardner related that she met Gray on a subway train 15 years prior, and that they had been married for nine years, commenting, "It's like everybody else's house and we're a real family. We got a dog and pain-in-the-ass neighbors. He did music and I do music."<ref>{{cite web|url=https://shop.rockthebells.com/blogs/articles/heather-b|title=Heather B: Rap Infamy, Radio and 'The Real World'|publisher=Rock the Bells|language=en-US|url-status=live|date=March 4, 2021|access-date=June 5, 2023|archive-date=January 20, 2022|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20220120051426/https://shop.rockthebells.com/blogs/articles/heather-b}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0IPdXsvh0xc|title=Heather B On Her Marriage To Horse From the Bravehearts|publisher=TheUrbanDaily|via=[[YouTube]]|language=en-US|url-status=live|date=May 24, 2010|access-date=June 5, 2023|archive-date=June 5, 2023|archive-url=https://ghostarchive.org/varchive/0IPdXsvh0xc}}</ref>
