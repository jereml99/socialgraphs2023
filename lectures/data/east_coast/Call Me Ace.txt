'''Anthony “Ace” Patterson''' (August 15, 1989), also known by his stage name '''Call Me Ace''', is an American rapper from [[Bridgeport, Connecticut]], and currently lives in the [[San Francisco Bay Area]], [[California]].<ref name="dailycal070519">{{cite web|title='I just want to be real': Local rapper and Haas alumnus Call Me Ace talks motivations, recent album |url=https://www.dailycal.org/2019/07/05/i-just-want-to-be-real-local-rapper-and-haas-alumnus-call-me-ace-talks-motivations-recent-album/ |publisher=Daily Cal |access-date=July 5, 2019}}</ref> In December 2016, Call Me Ace released his first EP, ''Misinterpretations'' while still a business consultant at [[Deloitte Consulting LLP]].<ref name="nookandkranny112816">{{cite web|title=The Undiscovered Bay: Call Me Ace |date=28 November 2016 |url=https://nookandkranny.wordpress.com/2016/11/28/call-me-ace-2/ |publisher=Nook and Kranny |access-date=November 28, 2016}}</ref><ref name="poetsandquants022018">{{cite web|title=Haas MBA Balances Job At Facebook & Rap |url=https://poetsandquants.com/2018/02/20/berkeley-haas-mba-balances-consulting-rap-career/ |publisher=Poets&Quants |access-date=February 20, 2018}}</ref> In March 2019 he released ''Airplane Mode'', which debuted at #3 on the [[iTunes]] Top 40 US Hip-Hop Album Chart and at #50 on the ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' R&B / Hip-Hop Album Sales Chart.<ref name="diymusician">{{cite web|title=How an indie hip-hop artist, Call Me Ace, charted on Billboard and iTunes |date=29 April 2019 |url=https://diymusician.cdbaby.com/music-promotion/how-to-chart-on-billboard/ |publisher=DIY Musician |access-date=April 29, 2019}}</ref>

In 2016, Call Me Ace released his first EP, ''Misinterpretations'', solely produced by Bay Area legend producer, [[Sean T]], who has produced for [[Mac Dre]], [[Mistah F.A.B.]], and [[E-40]].<ref name="nookandkranny112816" /> ''Misinterpretations'' ended up being the #9 most-spun album on KUSF radio in February 2017.<ref>{{cite web|title=KUSF's Top Ten Spins for February |url=https://www.facebook.com/KUSF.org/photos/a.77510049626/10154912155959627/?type=3&sfnsw=cl |publisher=KUSF |access-date=February 21, 2017}}</ref> In August 2017, Call Me Ace released the ''2interpretations'' mixtape, which reached #19 on ''Thizzler'''s Bay Area Music Chart that same month (rank of 50).<ref>{{cite web|title=The Bay Area Music Chart: August 2017 |date=3 September 2017 |url=https://www.thizzler.com/blog/2017/09/03/the-bay-area-music-chart-august-2017.html |publisher=Thizzler |access-date=September 3, 2017}}</ref> In March 2019 he released his fourth project and first independent studio album, ''Airplane Mode'', which debuted at #50 on the ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' R&B / Hip-Hop Album Sales Chart.<ref>{{cite web |title=R&B/Hip-Hop Album Sales (Week of April 6, 2019) |url=https://www.billboard.com/biz/charts/randb-hip-hop-album-sales |publisher=Billboard |access-date=April 2, 2019 |archive-date=July 19, 2019 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190719163411/https://www.billboard.com/biz/charts/randb-hip-hop-album-sales |url-status=dead }}</ref><ref name="diymusician" /> In February 2020, Call Me Ace released his 2nd EP, ''Working From Home'', which reached over 1 million streams on Spotify, despite having recently changed jobs from Facebook to YouTube.<ref>{{cite web|title=Call Me Ace Raps About Life And The Career Grind In 'Working From Home' |url=https://www.kalw.org/post/call-me-ace-raps-about-life-and-career-grind-working-home#stream/0 |publisher=KALW |access-date=May 12, 2020}}</ref> In June 2020, he quickly followed up with the EP, ''Working From Home: Extended'' - inspired by the COVID-19 global pandemic and recent social unrest - and used his album campaign as a way to fundraise over $10,000 in effort to combat racial injustice towards black and brown communities in the United States.<ref>{{cite web|title=Call Me Ace Donates $10k+ To End the Criminalization of Black and Brown Communities |date=6 July 2020 |url=https://enspiremag.com/2020/07/call-me-ace-donates-10k-to-end-the-criminalization-of-black-and-brown-communities/ |publisher=Enspire Magazine |access-date=July 6, 2020}}</ref>

In February 2021, Call Me Ace released his second studio album, ''Out Of Office'', which is his third project created and released during the pandemic.<ref>{{cite web|title=Bay Area Rapper Call Me Ace Releases New Project 'Out Of Office' |url=https://losangelesleakers.com/2021/02/bay-area-rapper-call-me-ace-releases-new-project-out-of-office-stream/ |publisher=L.A. Leakers |access-date=February 5, 2021}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Call Me Ace – "Out Of Office" Album Release Announcement |date=5 February 2021 |url=https://bodegasync.com/2021/02/05/call-me-ace-out-of-office/ |publisher=Bodega Sync |access-date=February 5, 2021}}</ref> In the same month, Call Me Ace was also recognized in Symphonic Distribution's "20 Black Artists Who Are On The Rise in 2021" list.<ref>{{cite web|title=20 Black Artists Who Are On The Rise in 2021 |date=2 February 2021 |url=https://blog.symphonicdistribution.com/2021/02/20-black-artists-who-are-on-the-rise/ |publisher=Symphonic Distribution |access-date=February 9, 2021}}</ref> In April 2021, Call Me Ace reached the Top 10 of [[BET]] Music's national emerging talent search competition, BET AmpliFind.<ref>{{cite web|title=Call Me Ace on his Indie Rap Career, Building His Brand, and Still Working Full-Time |date=2 July 2021 |url=https://trapital.co/2021/07/02/call-me-ace-on-building-a-brand-new-opportunities-and-vanity-metrics/ |publisher=Trapital |access-date=July 2, 2021}}</ref>

== Early life, education, and career ==
Ace Patterson was born in [[Bridgeport, Connecticut]].<ref name="dailycal070519" /> His parents emigrated from [[Jamaica]] before they had him.<ref name="dailycal070519" /> Growing up, Ace was interested in drawing, acting, poetry, and, eventually, rapping by the time he finished middle school. He went to Greens Farms Academy for high school and middle school<ref>{{cite web|title=Ace Patterson '07: A Life in Balance |url=https://www.gfacademy.org/community/gfa-magazine/single-post-magazine/~board/magazine-stories/post/ace-patterson-07-a-life-in-balance |publisher=Greens Farms Academy |access-date=June 24, 2019}}</ref>

In 2007, Ace enrolled at [[Columbia University]] in [[New York City]].<ref name="columbiaspectator051811">{{cite web|title=Senior profile: Ace Patterson |url=https://www.columbiaspectator.com/2011/05/18/senior-profile-ace-patterson/ |publisher=Columbia Spectator |access-date=March 27, 2013}}</ref> In 2009, he co-founded the student group, ''Columbia University Society of Hip-Hop'' (CUSH).<ref>{{cite web|title=Navigating Burnout? Rapper Call Me Ace Has a Few Ideas |url=https://www.kqed.org/arts/13860961/navigating-burnout-rapper-call-me-ace-has-a-few-ideas |publisher=KQED Arts |access-date=July 10, 2019}}</ref><ref name="poetsandquants022018" /> Before graduating, he and his group CUSH opened up for [[Snoop Dogg]] at the annual Bacchanal concert.<ref name="columbiaspectator051811" /> In 2011, Ace received his [[Bachelor of Arts]] in [[Anthropology]].<ref>{{cite web|title=With a Positive Message, this Consortium Alum and Facebook Marketer Is Making a Name for Himself in Hip-Hop |date=July 2019 |url=https://cgsm.org/this-facebook-marketer-is-making-a-name-for-himself-in-hip-hop/ |publisher=The Consortium |access-date=July 1, 2019}}</ref>

In 2013, Ace was quoted and referenced in a chapter of the Oxford Handbook of Critical Improvisation titled, "Improvisation in Freestyle Rap," written by Ellie Hisama.<ref>{{cite book|title=Oxford Handbook of Critical Improvisation, "Improvisation in Freestyle Rap" |year=2016 |url=https://www.oxfordhandbooks.com/view/10.1093/oxfordhb/9780199892921.001.0001/oxfordhb-9780199892921-e-24 |publisher=Oxford University Press |doi=10.1093/oxfordhb/9780199892921.013.24 |isbn=978-0-19-989292-1 |access-date=September 1, 2016|editor1-last=Piekut |editor1-first=Benjamin |editor2-first=George E |editor2-last=Lewis |last1=Hisama |first1=Ellie M. }}</ref>

In 2014, Ace left his role at [[Success Academy Charter Schools]] to attend graduate school at the [[University of California, Berkeley]], [[Haas School of Business]].<ref name="poetsandquants022018" /> During his last semester, he and a classmate made a parody of Drake's “[[The Motto (Drake song)|The Motto]]” in honor of the school and called it, “YOHO (You Only Haas Once)”. The music video was featured on ''Poets & Quants'' the day of release and has over 10,000 views to date.<ref>{{cite web|title=Haas Drops MBA Hype Music Video |date=19 May 2016 |url=https://poetsandquants.com/2016/05/19/haas-drops-mba-hype-music-video/ |publisher=Poets&Quants |access-date=May 19, 2016}}</ref> In 2016, Ace received his [[Master of Business Administration]] and joined [[Deloitte Consulting]].<ref name="poetsandquants022018" />

Ace worked in [[Marketing]] at [[Facebook]] and is part of the 2019 class of ADCOLOR FUTURES, a nationally selective program that celebrates diverse influencers that excel in their careers and also give back to the community.<ref>{{cite web|title=THE 13TH ANNUAL ADCOLOR AWARDS TAKES PLACE SEPTEMBER 8TH, 2019 IN LOS ANGELES
 |date=11 June 2019 |url=http://adcolor.org/the-13th-annual-adcolor-awards-takes-place-september-8th-2019-in-los-angeles/ |publisher=ADCOLOR |access-date=June 19, 2019}}</ref> Currently, Ace works on the Creator & Artist Development team at [[YouTube]] as the Global Program Manager for Music Label Partnerships.<ref>{{cite web|title=Call Me Ace Is Thriving In The Midst Of Crisis |url=https://medium.com/@ellingtonandre7/call-me-ace-is-thriving-in-the-midst-of-crisis-2df5252773da/ |publisher=Medium |access-date=April 17, 2020}}</ref>

== Musical influences ==
Call Me Ace has noted [[Ludacris]], [[Lil Wayne]], [[Snoop Dogg]], [[Busta Rhymes]], and [[Eminem]] as rappers that inspired him when he was younger.<ref name="nookandkranny112816" /> Ace has eclectic music tastes, having referenced genres like [[Cuban music]], [[Blues]], [[Jazz]], [[Dancehall]], [[Afrobeats]], and [[Ethiopian music]] as being additional sources of inspiration.<ref name="nookandkranny112816" /><ref name="dailycal070519" /> His music style is influenced by his Jamaican roots, the East Coast, the [[Bay Area]], and his international travel experiences.<ref name="dailycal070519" /> Call Me Ace has stated that he and his wife have visited over 100 international cities across 50 countries, are both multilingual, and are both children of immigrants - all of which have given him a global, cross-cultural outlook on life and in his music.<ref name="diymusician" />
