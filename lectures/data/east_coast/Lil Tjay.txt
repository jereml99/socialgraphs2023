'''Tione Jayden Merritt'''{{Needs IPA|lang=en|date=August 2023}} (born April 30, 2001), known professionally as '''Lil Tjay''' ({{IPAc-en|t|i| í|e…™}} {{respell|TEE|jay}}),<ref name="Tjay">{{cite web|url=https://amp.www.complex.com/music/2019/10/lil-tjay-everything-you-need-to-know|title=Everything You Need to Know About Lil Tjay|website=[[Complex (website)|Complex]]|last=Callas|first=Brad|date=October 11, 2019|accessdate=April 4, 2021}}</ref> is an American rapper and singer. He rose to prominence in 2017 with his song "Resume", and then the release of his breakthrough song "Brothers" which led him to sign with [[Columbia Records]] later during that year. Lil Tjay's debut studio album, ''[[True 2 Myself]]'', was released in 2019 and debuted at number 5 on the [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]], marking the album his first charting project. The following year, he released the mixtape ''[[State of Emergency (Lil Tjay EP)|State of Emergency]]'', that featured only [[Brooklyn drill|drill]] artists from [[New York City]]. 

His second studio album, ''[[Destined 2 Win]]'', was released in 2021 and included the single "[[Calling My Phone]]" (with [[6lack]]); his highest-charting song to date. In 2023, released the song "[[Gangsta Boo (song)|Gangsta Boo]]" with fellow New York artist [[Ice Spice]], and released his third studio album, ''[[222 (album)|222]]'', in July of that year.<ref name=":0" />

==Early life==
Merritt was born and raised in the [[Fordham, Bronx|Fordham]] neighborhood of [[the Bronx]], [[New York City]].<ref name="Tjay" /> He was raised by a single mother and has two younger siblings in what was described as a "roomy" apartment. Merritt called himself the problematic child out of the three, as he would often get himself into petty robberies and school fights. At the age of 15, Merritt was arrested for one of his robberies and was sentenced for a year to a [[youth detention center]] where he would begin to write raps; they included one of his hit songs "Resume", which he released on [[SoundCloud]].<ref name="pitchfork.com" />

Merritt's childhood friend, Smelly, was fatally stabbed in August 2016. Merritt pays tribute to his childhood friend in a number of his songs referencing his name, "Smelly".<ref>{{Cite web |date=2021-08-17 |title=Lil Tjay pays tribute to deceased friend with 'Forever In My Heart' |url=https://ourgenerationmusic.com/new-music-spotlight/lil-tjay-pays-tribute-to-deceased-friend-with-forever-in-my-heart/ |access-date=2022-12-05 |website=Our Generation Music |language=en-US}}</ref>

==Career==
===2016‚Äì2019: Beginnings, record deal, and ''True 2 Myself''===
In 2016, Merritt began releasing music on [[SoundCloud]].<ref name="pitchfork.com">{{Cite web|url=https://pitchfork.com/features/rising/get-to-know-hip-hop-heartthrob-lil-tjay-who-wants-to-be-the-next-usher/|title=Get to Know Hip-Hop Heartthrob Lil Tjay, Who Wants to Be the Next Usher|website=Pitchfork|date=January 18, 2019 |access-date=February 10, 2019}}</ref> "Resume", one of his first songs, was released when Merritt was 16 years old, and quickly began spreading online.

On March 10, 2018, Merritt competed and placed first at the NYC All Ages Edition of Coast 2 Coast LIVE, where his performance gained the attention of a record label [[Artists and repertoire|A&R]] who was in attendance to judge the showcase.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://coast2coastlive.com/video/lil-tjay-performs-at-coast-2-coast-live-nyc-all-ages-edition-3-10-18-1st-place|title=Lil tjay ‚Äì Lil tjay Performs at Coast 2 Coast LIVE {{!}} NYC All Ages Edition 3/10/18 ‚Äì 1st Place {{!}} Videos {{!}} Coast 2 Coast LIVE {{!}} Largest Artist Showcase in the World|website=Coast 2 Coast Live|access-date=April 5, 2019}}</ref> Soon after, Merritt released the single "[[Brothers (Lil Tjay song)|Brothers]]", which became his biggest song at the time and led to him signing with [[Columbia Records]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.forbes.com/sites/garysuarez/2018/12/26/5-hip-hop-singles-to-watch-this-winter-on-the-billboard-hot-100/|title=5 Hip-Hop Singles To Watch This Winter On The Billboard Hot 100|last=Suarez|first=Gary|website=Forbes|access-date=February 10, 2019}}</ref>

Released on [[YouTube]] in 2018, Merritt earned himself recognition through his sampling of [[Justin Bieber]]'s "[[Baby (Justin Bieber song)|Baby]]" on his single, "None of Your Love".<ref>{{Cite web |date=2020-12-09 |title=Lil Tjay Flips Justin Bieber's "Baby" for "None of Your Love" |url=https://hypebeast.com/2020/12/lil-tjay-none-of-your-love-single-stream |access-date=2022-12-05 |website=Hypebeast}}</ref>

Within the first ten months of Merritt's music career, he had released five tracks that recorded over one million plays on SoundCloud.<ref name="Elevator Exploding">{{Cite web|url=https://www.elevatormag.com/exploding-nyc-rapper-lil-tjay-doesnt-want-none-of-your-love/|title=Exploding NYC rapper Lil Tjay doesn't want 'None Of Your Love'|date=August 2, 2018|website=Elevator|access-date=April 5, 2019}}</ref><ref name="AllMusic Bio" /> "Brothers", "Resume", "Goat", and "[[Leaked (song)|Leaked]]" each accumulated tens of millions of streams.<ref name="SoundCloud Lil Tjay">{{Cite web|url=https://soundcloud.com/liltjay|title=Lil Tjay|website=SoundCloud|access-date=April 2, 2019}}</ref> In July 2018, Merritt worked with producer [[CashMoneyAP]] to release "None of Your Love", which also amassed tens of millions of plays.<ref name="Elevator Exploding" /> Merritt released his debut EP ''No Comparison'' at the end of 2018.<ref name="AllMusic Bio" />

In January 2019, Merritt was featured on fellow rapper [[Polo G]]'s single "[[Pop Out]]", which peaked at number 11 on the [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]].<ref name="Itunes Lil Tjay">{{Cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/artist/lil-tjay/1436446949|title=Lil Tjay on Apple Music|website=Apple Music|access-date=April 2, 2019}}</ref> In July, he was featured on boy band [[PrettyMuch]]'s "Lying".<ref>{{Cite magazine|last=Spanos|first=Brittany|date=July 19, 2019|title=Watch PrettyMuch, Lil Tjay's Graffiti-Covered 'Lying' Video|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-news/prettymuch-lying-lil-tjay-new-video-fomo-tour-861070/|access-date=March 5, 2021|magazine=Rolling Stone|language=en-US}}</ref> Later that year, he released an EP titled ''F.N''; the project's lead single, "[[F.N (song)|F.N]]", peaked at number 56 on the ''Billboard'' Hot 100 and became his first solo charting song. Merritt released his debut album ''[[True 2 Myself]]'' on October 11, 2019. It debuted and peaked at number 5 on the US [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]]. In December, he was featured on a remix of [[24kGoldn]]'s song "[[Valentino (24kGoldn song)|Valentino]]".<ref>{{Cite web|last=A.|first=Aron|date=January 3, 2020|title=24KGOLDN Taps Lil Tjay For "Valentino (Remix)"|url=https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/24kgoldn-taps-lil-tjay-for-valentino-remix-new-song.1985681.html|access-date=March 5, 2021|website=HotNewHipHop|language=en}}</ref>

===2020‚Äì2021: ''State of Emergency'' and ''Destined 2 Win''===
In early 2020, Merritt reached the ''Billboard'' Hot 100 again with his single "[[20/20 (song)|20/20]]", which peaked at number 94 on the chart. On May 8, 2020, Merritt released the EP ''[[State of Emergency (Lil Tjay EP)|State of Emergency]]'', which focused heavily on [[drill music]], a popular subgenre in [[New York City]].<ref name="SOE">{{Cite web|url=https://www.nme.com/reviews/album/lil-tjay-state-of-emergency-review-2661573|title=Lil Tjay ‚Äì 'State Of Emergency' EP review: trap'n'b star makes cynical grab for New York drill hype|work=[[NME]]|last=Williams|first=Kyann-Sian|date=May 8, 2020|access-date=May 8, 2020}}</ref> The seven-song EP included the track "Zoo York" (featuring [[Fivio Foreign]] and [[Pop Smoke]]), which peaked at number 65 on the Hot 100.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://twitter.com/i/events/1258614435922374661|title="Fans are loving Lil Tjay's song Zoo York"|via=Twitter|date=May 8, 2020|access-date=May 8, 2020}}</ref> In July, Merritt appeared on Pop Smoke's debut posthumous album, ''[[Shoot for the Stars, Aim for the Moon]]'', on the track "[[Mood Swings (Pop Smoke song)|Mood Swings]]", which reached number 17 on the ''Billboard'' Hot 100.<ref>{{cite magazine|url=https://www.billboard.com/artist/pop-smoke/chart-history/hsi/|title=Pop Smoke Chart History: Hot 100|magazine=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|access-date=July 20, 2020}}</ref> On August 11, Merritt was included on ''[[XXL (magazine)|XXL]]''<nowiki/>'s 2020 [[XXL (magazine)#Annual Freshman Class list|Freshman Class]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.xxlmag.com/xxl-2020-freshman-class-revealed/|title=XXL 2020 FRESHMAN CLASS REVEALED|work=[[XXL (magazine)|XXL]]|last=Cline|first=Georgette|date=August 11, 2020|access-date=August 11, 2020}}</ref> In October, Merritt hinted at a new project on [[Twitter]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://twitter.com/liltjay/status/1319838345271365639|title=Spent the last couple months pouring my heart out into this album. On February 12, 2021, Merritt released his single "[[Calling My Phone]]", a collaboration with [[Atlanta]] singer [[6lack]], which was accompanied by a music video directed by Cam Busby.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://pitchfork.com/news/lil-tjay-and-6lack-share-new-song-calling-my-phone-listen/|title=Lil Tjay and 6LACK Share New Song "Calling My Phone": Listen|website=[[Pitchfork (website)|Pitchfork]]|date=February 12, 2021}}</ref> The song debuted at number three on the ''Billboard'' Hot 100, becoming the highest-charting single of Merritt's career.<ref name=":0">{{Cite magazine|last=Trust|first=Gary|date=February 22, 2021|title=Olivia Rodrigo's 'Drivers License' Leads Hot 100 For 6th Week, Lil Tjay's 'Calling My Phone' Dials Up No. 3 Debut|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/business/chart-beat/9529511/olivia-rodrigo-drivers-license-number-one-sixth-week-hot-100/|access-date=February 22, 2021|magazine=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]}}</ref> On March 19, Merritt released "[[Headshot (song)|Headshot]]", a collaboration with Polo G and Fivio Foreign as the second single from his second studio album, ''[[Destined 2 Win]]'', which was released on April 2, 2021. The album consists of twenty-one tracks.<ref name="Destined">{{cite web|url=https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/lil-tjay-announces-new-album-destined-2-win-news.128977.html|title=Lil Tjay Announces New Album "Destined 2 Win"|work=HotNewHipHop|last=Findlay|first=Mitch|date=March 24, 2021|accessdate=March 24, 2021}}</ref> On October 22, Merritt released the lead single to his upcoming third studio album "[[Not in the Mood]]" (featuring Fivio Foreign and [[Kay Flock]]). The single was teased in snippets using [[TikTok]] from September prior to its release.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/lil-tjay-drops-new-drill-single-not-in-the-mood-with-fivio-foreign-and-kay-flock-new-song.1993039.html?_amp|title=Lil Tjay Drops New Drill Single "Not In The Mood" With Fivio Foreign & Kay Flock|website=HotNewHipHop|last=Zidel|first=Alex|date=October 22, 2021|accessdate=April 23, 2022}}</ref>

==2022 shooting==
On June 22, 2022, Merritt was shot seven times during an attempted robbery in [[Edgewater, New Jersey]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bcpo.net/three-men-charged-in-edgewater-nj-shooting/|title=THREE MEN CHARGED IN EDGEWATER, NJ SHOOTING|website=Bergen County Prosecutor's Office|date=June 22, 2022|access-date=August 26, 2022}}</ref> He was flown to [[Hackensack University Medical Center]] and underwent emergency surgery, while a second victim‚Äî22-year-old Antoine Boyd‚Äîwho is friends with Merritt, was also shot once and was in stable condition.<ref>{{Cite web |date=2022-06-22 |title=Rapper Critically Wounded, Second Victim Stable In Edgewater Shooting |url=https://dailyvoice.com/new-jersey/cliffsidepark/news/rapper-critically-wounded-second-victim-stable-in-edgewater-shooting/835818/ |access-date=2022-06-22 |website=Cliffside Park-Edgewater Daily Voice |language=en}}</ref> Meanwhile, the shooter was also hit by gunfire, and escaped with help from an unidentified co-conspirator.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Quinn |first=Liam |title=Court records detail what led up to the shooting of NY rapper Lil Tjay in Edgewater |url=https://www.northjersey.com/story/news/bergen/edgewater/2022/06/23/edgewater-nj-shooting-lil-tjay-suspect-also-shot/7712683001/ |access-date=2022-06-24 |website=North Jersey Media Group}}</ref> The Bergen County Prosecutor's Office reported that the shooting did not appear random, and that one victim was upgraded from critical to stable condition, while the other was in good condition.<ref>{{Cite tweet|user=bcponj|number=1539652258169638913|title=Update on #BCPONJ & @EdgewaterPolice investigation in Edgewater NJ shooting: per initial investigation, does not appear to be random act and one victim upgraded from critical to stable condition. Other admitted to hospital with non-life threatening injuries in good condition.}}</ref>

Later that day, police arrested 27-year-old Mohamed Konate, who was charged with three counts of first-degree [[attempted murder]], three counts of first-degree armed robbery, and multiple weapons offenses.<ref>{{Cite web |date=June 23, 2022 |title=Prosecutor: Man charged in Edgewater shooting that injured 2, including rapper Lil Tjay |url=https://newjersey.news12.com/police-2-shot-overnight-in-edgewater-tmz-reports-one-victim-is-rapper-lil-tjay |access-date=2022-06-23 |website=News 12 - New Jersey |archive-date=June 22, 2022 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20220622163057/https://newjersey.news12.com/police-2-shot-overnight-in-edgewater-tmz-reports-one-victim-is-rapper-lil-tjay |url-status=dead }}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |first1=Tariq |last1=Zehawi |first2=Liam |last2=Quinn |first3=Nicholas |last3=Katzban |title=New York rapper Lil Tjay shot in Edgewater shopping center in attempted robbery |url=https://www.northjersey.com/story/news/bergen/edgewater/2022/06/22/lil-tjay-shot-edgewater-nj-emergency-surgery/7698064001/ |access-date=2022-06-23 |website=North Jersey Media Group}}</ref> Jeffery Valdez, who was with Merritt but was not injured, and Antoine Boyd were both charged with second-degree unlawful possession of a weapon, but charges were later dropped. Merritt was not charged with any crime.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Bronx-born rapper Lil Tjay shot in N.J., Manhattan man charged |url=https://www.nydailynews.com/snyde/ny-rapper-lil-tjay-shot-surgery-new-jersey-20220622-kkttmsq4orgzfdps3w5cmiurja-story.html |access-date=2022-06-23 |website=New York Daily News|date=June 22, 2022 }}</ref>

On June 23, 2022, it was reported that Merritt was still unconscious following the shooting.<ref>{{Cite news |title=Lil Tjay Still Unconscious Following Shooting |url=https://www.tmz.com/2022/06/23/lil-tjay-shooting-new-jersey-unconscious/ |access-date=2022-06-23 |website=TMZ |language=en}}</ref> Days following a lack of any credible updates on Merritt's condition, rumors began to surface claiming that he was paralyzed and possibly brain dead. However, multiple sources later disproved these rumors, as news came out on July 1, 2022, stating that Merritt was still hospitalized but "alert and talking."<ref>{{Cite tweet|title=Lil Tjay Alert & Talking Following NJ Shooting... |user=liltjay|number=1562473894123950082}}</ref>

On August 24, 2022, Merrit uploaded an update video on his social media accounts. In the video, he states how he's doing better and is "going to come back stronger than ever" while wearing a neck brace.<ref>{{cite tweet|user=liltjay|number=1562473894123950082|title=Thanks for the love and support ya I appreciate everything}}</ref>

On August 26, 2022, Merritt released his single "Beat the Odds", which details his near-fatal experience and recovery.<ref name="hypebeast.com"/>

== Legal issues ==
On January 16, 2023, Merritt was arrested in the Bronx and was placed in police custody, following a traffic stop.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Lil Tjay Arrested For Gun Possession En Route To Ice Spice Video Shoot |url=https://www.tmz.com/2023/01/16/lil-tjay-arrested-gun-possession-bronx-ice-spice-music-video/ |access-date=2023-01-17 |website=TMZ |language=en}}</ref> Merritt was on his way to film with fellow Bronx rapper [[Ice Spice]] at the time of the arrest. The police found a handgun in the vehicle, before arresting him. He was charged with weapon possession and a court date was set for February 14.<ref name="hiphopdx.com">{{Cite web  |date=2023-01-28 |title=Lil Tjay Released Again After Judge Blunder Sent Him Back To Jail |url=https://hiphopdx.com/news/lil-tjay-out-on-bail-again-gun-charges |access-date=2023-02-05 |website=HipHopDX |language=en}}</ref> It is unknown whether Merritt attended the trial, and what the results of the trial were.{{citation needed|date=August 2023}}

Merritt's attorney, Dawn Florio, claims the gun found by police does not belong to Merritt.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Lil Tjay's Attorney Thinks Rapper Was Illegally Searched During Gun Arrest |url=https://www.tmz.com/2023/01/17/lil-tjay-attorney-illegal-search-gun-arrest-bronx-ice-spice-music-video/ |access-date=2023-01-21 |website=TMZ |language=en}}</ref> She also claims that the search conducted by officers was conducted illegally, as the car was allegedly parked legally on the street and Merritt was allegedly a passenger in the car.<ref name="hiphopdx.com"/>

Lil Tjay and director Fakedell‚Äôs film crew were arrested in June of 2023 for a firearm used during shooting of a music video. The incident was caught on video by a crowd of kids first pleading with the cops to not arrest him, and that the gun was just a prop.{{citation needed|date=August 2023}}

==Artistry==

Influenced by his upbringing in [[the Bronx]], Merritt is a melodic rapper who frequently incorporates [[Auto-Tune]] in his songs.<ref name="Rolling Stone Feel Old">{{Cite magazine|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-features/lil-tjay-profile-artist-you-need-to-know-773450/|title=Rising Bronx Rapper Lil Tjay Will Make You Feel Old|last=Holmes|first=Charles|date=January 3, 2019|magazine=Rolling Stone|access-date=February 10, 2019}}</ref> He has drawn comparisons to fellow [[New York (state)|New York]] rapper [[A Boogie wit da Hoodie]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://grittyvibes.com/2018/06/lil-tjay-is-new-yorks-next-big-star/|title=Lil Tjay is New York's next big STAR|last=Hunt|first=Relly|date=June 21, 2018|website=Gritty Vibes Magazine|access-date=April 3, 2019}}</ref> In an interview with ''[[Rolling Stone]]'', he revealed that his biggest musical influences are [[Drake (musician)|Drake]], [[Meek Mill]], and [[Usher (musician)|Usher]].<ref name="Rolling Stone Feel Old" /> In the same interview, Merritt described his music as having a melodic sound when singing or rapping about his past struggles. Merritt also credits his success to his year spent in prison, where he claims that his ability to write songs peaked.<ref name="AllMusic Bio">{{Cite web|url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/lil-tjay-mn0003807005/biography|title=Lil Tjay {{!}} Biography & History|website=AllMusic|access-date=April 3, 2019}}</ref> In [[Pitchfork (website)|''Pitchfork'']]'s featured article on Merritt, he is referred to as a "hip-hop heartthrob".<ref name="pitchfork.com" /> Later in the interview, Merritt explained that he is often called the "Bronx Justin Bieber" because of his sampling of [[Justin Bieber]]'s "[[Baby (Justin Bieber song)|Baby]]" on his song, "None of Your Love".<ref name="pitchfork.com" />

== Live performances ==
Merritt accompanied [[Seattle]] rapper [[Lil Mosey]] on his nationwide tour throughout 2019.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.jambase.com/band/lil-tjay|title=Lil Tjay ‚Äì Upcoming Shows, Tickets, Reviews, More|date=August 26, 2018|website=JamBase|access-date=April 5, 2019}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.chicagoreader.com/chicago/lil-mosey-lil-tjay-c-glizzy-bandkids/Event?oid=63378992|title=Lil Mosey, Lil TJay, C Glizzy, Bandkids|website=Chicago Reader|date=September 23, 2009|access-date=April 5, 2019}}</ref> He also has performed at the three-day [[Rolling Loud]] music festival along with [[Migos]], [[Travis Scott]], and [[Kid Cudi]] in [[Miami Gardens, Florida]], in May 2019<ref name="Pitchfork Rolling Loud">{{Cite web|url=https://pitchfork.com/news/rolling-loud-miami-2019-travis-scott-kid-cudi-cardi-b-more/|title=Rolling Loud Miami 2019: Travis Scott, Kid Cudi, Cardi B, amogh <3 Kiran, and More|author=<!--Staff writer(s); no by-line.-->|website=Pitchfork|date=January 28, 2019|access-date=April 5, 2019}}</ref> and at the [[Los Angeles]] festival in December. He frequently appears and performs on concerts with close friend Jay Gwuapo.{{citation needed|date=June 2021}}

In September 2022, a few months after the shooting, Merritt performed his first concert since being shot. He returned to the stage for his 2022 Rolling Loud performance on September 23 in New York.
