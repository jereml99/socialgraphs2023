'''Scott Monroe Sterling''' (March 2, 1962 – August 27, 1987), known by the stage name '''DJ Scott La Rock''', was an American hip-hop disc jockey and music producer from [[the Bronx]], New York. He was a founding member of the [[East Coast hip hop]] group [[Boogie Down Productions]]. He was also known for his association with the rapper KRS-One who was a member of Boogie Down Productions.

Sterling's death in August 1987 is said to be the first murder of a major hip hop artist.{{citation needed|date=December 2021}}

== Early life ==

Scott Sterling was born on March 2, 1962, in the Bronx, New York City. His parents separated when he was four years old, so he was raised by his mother, Carolyn Morant, a municipal employee.<ref name="xxl" /> They moved from [[Queens]], New York City, to the Bronx: first [[Morrisania, Bronx|Morrisania]] and then [[Morris Heights, Bronx|Morris Heights]].

He excelled in both academics and sports at [[Our Saviour Lutheran High School]], graduating in 1980. He attended [[Castleton State College]] in [[Vermont]] and earned a varsity letter in basketball there.<ref name="xxl" />

== Career ==
La Rock returned to New York City in hopes of finding work and making inroads to the music industry. Through a connection of his mother's, La Rock landed a job as a social worker at Franklin Armory Men's Shelter on 166th Street in the Bronx. At night, though, he spun records at the [[hip hop]] hot spot the Broadway Repertoire Theatre.<ref name="xxl">{{Cite news |last=Callahan-Bever |first=Noah |date=August 27, 2010 |title=R.I.P. Scott La Rock – Remembering the BDP Legend 23 Years Later |work=[[XXL Magazine]] |url=http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2010/08/rip-scott-la-rock-remembering-the-bdp-legend-23-years-later/ |url-status=live |access-date=July 17, 2011 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170113124906/http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2010/08/rip-scott-la-rock-remembering-the-bdp-legend-23-years-later/ |archive-date=January 13, 2017}}</ref>

During his time as a social worker, La Rock met [[rapper]] [[KRS-One]] in 1986 at Franklin Men's Shelter where KRS resided. The pair formed [[Boogie Down Productions]] (BDP) with DJ [[D-Nice|Derrick "D-Nice" Jones]], a cousin of the shelter's security guard, Floyd Payne.<ref name="xxl" /> The group's 1987 debut album, ''[[Criminal Minded]]'', is considered a [[Hip hop music|hip hop]] classic.<ref>{{Cite web |title=500 CDs You Must Own: Hip-Hop |url=http://www.blender.com/guide/articles.aspx?id=126 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080809133340/http://www.blender.com/guide/articles.aspx?id=126 |archive-date=August 9, 2008 |access-date=September 11, 2007 |website=[[Blender (magazine)|Blender]]}}</ref><ref>{{Cite magazine |title=500 Greatest Albums of All Time |url=https://www.rollingstone.com/news/story/5938174/the_rs_500_greatest_albums_of_all_time/5 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100407053104/http://www.rollingstone.com/news/story/5938174/the_rs_500_greatest_albums_of_all_time/5 |archive-date=April 7, 2010 |access-date=September 11, 2007 |magazine=[[Rolling Stone]]}}</ref>

== Shooting ==
On August 27, 1987, D-Nice was assaulted by men upset that he had been talking to one of their ex-girlfriends. D-Nice asked La Rock to help defuse the situation. Later that day, La Rock, Scotty "Manager Moe" Morris, DJ McBooo, D-Nice and BDP bodyguard Darrell rode a [[Jeep CJ-7]] to the [[Highbridge, Bronx|Highbridge Homes Projects]]<ref name="xxl" /><ref name="ambrosia">{{Cite web |last=Paine |first=Jake |date=November 15, 2016 |title=Just-Ice Reveals Vivid Details About The Day Scott La Rock Was Killed (Video) |url=https://ambrosiaforheads.com/2016/11/scott-larock-killed-just-ice-bdp-video/ |url-status=live |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180429102142/http://ambrosiaforheads.com/2016/11/scott-larock-killed-just-ice-bdp-video/ |archive-date=April 29, 2018 |access-date=January 17, 2020 |website=AmbrosiaForHeads.com}}</ref> building on University Avenue in the [[South Bronx]].<ref name="xxl" /> As they were leaving, bullets were fired through the side and top of the Jeep. La Rock struck his head on the dashboard, not realizing at the time he had been struck by a bullet in the back of his head.<ref name="ambrosia" /> He was driven to [[Lincoln Hospital (Bronx, New York)|Lincoln Hospital]]. He was conscious as he was wheeled into the emergency department, telling doctors he was feeling cold and tired. He died in the operating room.

Two men were arrested and charged with La Rock's murder, but they were acquitted at the trial.<ref name="xxl" />
