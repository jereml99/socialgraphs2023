'''David James Parker''' (born October 26, 1962), known by the stage name '''Busy Bee''',<ref>''[[Spin (magazine)|Spin Magazine]]'', October 1985, Vol. 1, No. 6, p.39</ref> is an American [[old school hip hop|old-school hip hop]] musician from [[New York City|New York, NY]]. First coming on the New York City music scene in 1977, Busy Bee worked with many of hip-hop's founding fathers, including [[Melle Mel]], [[Afrika Bambaataa]], and Kool DJ AJ.

==Career==
Known for his comedic rhymes, Busy Bee originally gained a large following through MC rap battles in [[Staten Island]], [[Brooklyn]], and [[New Jersey]]. He was famously roasted by [[Kool Moe Dee]] at Harlem World in [[Manhattan|Manhattan, NY]], December 1981, in one of the earliest documented rap battles.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.dallasobserver.com/2007-04-26/news/blow-average/|title=Blow Average|publisher=[[Dallas Observer]]|accessdate=2007-04-26}}</ref> In 1985, he won the [[New Music Seminar|New Music Seminar's]] MC World Supremacy Belt.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Ettelson |first1=Robbie |title=The New Music Seminar Battle For World Supremacy: An Oral History |url=http://daily.redbullmusicacademy.com/2015/07/new-music-seminar-oral-history |website=Red Bull Music Academy Daily |publisher=Red Bull |accessdate=15 July 2018}}</ref> In the early 1980s Afrika Bambaataa asked Busy to join his [[Universal Zulu Nation|Zulu Nation]] where the young MC would DJ for Bambaataa's Zulu Nation parties.<ref>{{Cite web|url={{AllMusic|class=artist|id=p23286|pure_url=yes}}|title=Busy Bee Biography|publisher=[[AllMusic]]|accessdate=2007-04-27}}</ref> Busy Bee continues to rhyme today, most recently appearing on [[KRS-One]] and [[Marley Marl]]'s collaborative 2007 album ''[[Hip-Hop Lives]]''.

Busy Bee was featured in the 1983 film ''[[Wild Style]]'', billed as the first hip-hop motion picture, directed by [[Charlie Ahearn (director)|Charlie Ahearn]]. More recently, he played himself in the 2002 inner-city drama ''[[Paid in Full (2002 film)|Paid in Full]]''. In 2007, he was included in the video documentary ''Hip Hop Legends''.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.imdb.com/title/tt0961203/|title=Hip Hop Legends|publisher=[[IMDb]]|accessdate=2007-04-28}}
</ref>

Thirty years after the song's initial release, in 2018 [[Robert Rippberger]] directed the official music video for “Suicide” by Busy Bee, the single off his gold album ''Running Thangs''. The video features a cameo by artist [[Ice-T]].<ref>{{cite web |title=Busy Bee - Suicide - Official Music Video |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DrLPVsHeu4Q  |archive-url=https://ghostarchive.org/varchive/youtube/20211213/DrLPVsHeu4Q |archive-date=2021-12-13 |url-status=live|website=YouTube |accessdate=18 October 2019}}{{cbignore}}</ref>

==Personal life==
He currently resides in Las Vegas Nevada, with Michelle, his wife of over 20 years. He has two daughters.
