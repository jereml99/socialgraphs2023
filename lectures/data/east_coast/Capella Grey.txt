'''Curtis Anthony Jackson II''' (born August 2, 1995), known professionally as '''Capella Grey''', is an American singer, songwriter, rapper and record producer from the [[Bronx, New York]]. He is the founder of Allepac the Family and signed a joint venture with [[Capitol Records|Capitol]]. He released two mixtapes in 2020 and received widespread recognition in 2021 with his breakout single "[[Gyalis]]".<ref>{{cite magazine |last=Dailey |first=Hannah |title=How Capella Grey's 'No Rules' Mantra Led to Breakthrough Hit 'Gyalis' |url=https://www.billboard.com/pro/capella-grey-gyalis-hot-100-interview/ |magazine=Billboard}}</ref>

==Early life==
Curtis Jackson II was born in [[New Rochelle, New York]]. He grew up in a Jamaican family and started playing instruments at a young age, learning how to play music from church.<ref>{{cite web |title=Capella Grey Is No Longer Taking A Back Seat To The Music Industry |url=https://bossip.com/2044129/capella-grey/ |website=Bossip|date=October 5, 2021 }}</ref> He was raised in [[the Bronx]] where he went to [[Harry S. Truman High School (Bronx)|Harry S. Truman High School]].<ref>{{cite web |title=914 The Fast Hour (Capella Grey) |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=JsaLCTCTd8M |website=YouTube}}</ref> He developed his musical career in [[Mount Vernon, New York]] after graduating high school.<ref>{{cite web |last1=F. |first1=Arden |title=Capella Grey's Energy Is Perfect For Our Movement! |url=https://www.foursideny.com/articles/capella-greys-energy-is-perfect-for-our-movement-by-arden-f |website=Four Side NY}}</ref>

==Musical career==
===2016–2020: Career beginnings and early mixtapes===
Capella Grey started his musical career writing and producing for other artists, including "Love You Better" by King Combs featuring [[Chris Brown]]. He signed an R&B girl group called Rèign to Allepac Records, and wrote and produced their debut album released in 2019.<ref>{{cite web |title=Allepac Records Presents Girl Group Reign |date=August 28, 2018 |url=https://lilmisschitchat.wixsite.com/lil-miss-chit-chat/post/2018/08/28/allpec-records-presents-girl-group-reign}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |last1=OTV Studios |title=OTV LIVE Episode #048 - Allepac Records |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=xxsW8fkxVGg |via=YouTube}}</ref> On January 8, 2020, he released his debut mixtape ''Yea Nah I'm Out'', followed by his second mixtape ''The QuaranTape Vibe 1.''<ref>{{cite web |last=Benoit |first=Kevin |title=The Story of Capella Grey – An Undeniable Force Rises |url=https://parlemag.com/2021/12/capella-grey-story-undeniable-rise/ |website=Parlé Mag|date=December 10, 2021 }}</ref> 

===2021–present: ''Vibe Responsibly Vol. 1''===
On January 4, 2021, he released the single "[[Gyalis]]", which samples Juvenile's "[[Back That Azz Up]]". The song went viral in New York and on social media platforms, spawning unofficial remixes from [[A Boogie wit da Hoodie]], [[Kranium]], [[Tory Lanez]], [[Angel (British musician)|Angel]] and [[WSTRN|Haile]], among others. The song reached number 10 on the ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' [[Hot R&B/Hip-Hop Songs]] chart, and was called the "song of the summer" by multiple publications.<ref>{{cite magazine |last=Lamarre |first=Carl |title=Capella Grey Wants to Rewrite Hip-Hop History: 'I Feel Like We're Cheating Right Now' |url=https://www.billboard.com/music/rb-hip-hop/capella-grey-gyalis-inteview-9652179/ |magazine=Billboard}}</ref><ref>{{cite magazine|url=https://www.billboard.com/pro/capella-grey-gyalis-rb-hip-hop-songs-chart/|title=Capella Grey's 'Gyalis' Hits Top 10 on Hot R&B/Hip-Hop Songs Chart|magazine=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|last=Andeson|first=Trevor|date=November 12, 2021|access-date=November 23, 2021}}</ref> The official remix was released on October 1, 2021, featuring [[Popcaan]] and [[Chris Brown]].<ref name="dancehallmag">{{cite web|url=https://www.dancehallmag.com/2021/09/28/news/capella-grey-announces-gyalis-remix-with-popcaan-chris-brown.html|title=Capella Grey Announces 'Gyalis' Remix With Popcaan, Chris Brown|work=Dancehallmag|last=Baugh|first=Claudine|date=September 28, 2021|access-date=November 23, 2021}}</ref> He signed with [[Capitol Records|Capitol]] in 2021, saying they are "not trying to change me as an artist to meet the standards or expectations of music now, which is why I went with them."<ref>{{cite web |title=Capella Grey's Music Sounds Like a New York Summer |url=https://www.vice.com/en/article/4avqd3/capella-greys-gyalis-music-sounds-like-a-new-york-summer |website=Vice|date=July 2, 2021 }}</ref> 

On December 17, 2021, Capella Grey released the single "Talk Nice".<ref>{{cite web |title=Capella Grey says they should "Talk Nice" in new video |url=https://www.revolt.tv/article/2021-12-17/144886/capella-grey-talk-nice-video/ |website=Revolt}}</ref> On March 18, 2022, he released the single "Confujawn" with [[Nija Charles|Nija]].<ref>{{cite web |title=Capella Grey And Nija Want No 'Confujawn' Through Romance In Their Flirtatious New Video |url=https://uproxx.com/music/capella-grey-nija-confujawn-video/ |website=Uproxx|date=March 19, 2022 }}</ref> On May 6, he released the music video for "Sas Crise" featuring Alayzha Sky and Ghost, and directed by Mikey Scho.<ref>{{cite web |title=Capella Grey Releases New Visual, "SAS CRISE" ft. Alayzha Sky and Ghost |url=https://thisis50.com/2022/05/06/capella-grey-releases-new-visual-sas-crise-ft-alayzha-sky-and-ghost/ |website=This Is 50|date=May 6, 2022 }}</ref> On June 10, Capella Grey released the single "OT" featuring [[Ty Dolla Sign]].<ref>{{cite web |title=Capella Grey Announces His Upcoming Single 'OT' Featuring Ty Dolla Sign |url=https://uproxx.com/music/capella-grey-ty-dolla-sign-ot-announce/ |website=Uproxx|date=May 26, 2022 }}</ref> 

He appeared on Chris Brown's album ''[[Breezy (album)|Breezy]]'', on the song "Till The Wheels Fall Off" featuring [[Lil Durk]].<ref>{{cite web |title=Chris Brown Reveals "Breezy" Tracklist featuring Lil Durk, Fivio Foreign, Lil Wayne & More |url=https://allhiphop.com/news/chris-brown-reveals-breezy-tracklist/ |website=All Hip Hop|date=June 23, 2022 }}</ref> On July 1, he was featured on [[DJ Drama]]'s single "Forever" featuring [[Fabolous]], [[Benny the Butcher]], and [[Jim Jones (rapper)|Jim Jones]].<ref>{{cite web |title=DJ Drama Drops "Forever" Ft. Jim Jones, Benny The Butcher, Fabolous, & Capella Grey |url=https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/dj-drama-drops-forever-ft-jim-jones-benny-the-butcher-fabolous-and-capella-grey-new-song.1994894.html |website=Hotnewhiphop|date=July 2022 }}</ref> Capella Grey was the opening act for [[Joey Badass]] on a 20-date summer concert tour, the 1999-2000 Tour.<ref>{{cite web |title=Joey Bada$$ announces new album and North American summer tour |url=https://www.thefader.com/2022/05/12/joey-badass-new-album-release-date-north-american-summer-tour |website=The Fader}}</ref> On July 22, he also featured on the song "Welcome Back" with Joey Badass and Chris Brown, on the album ''[[2000 (Joey Badass album)|2000]]''.<ref>{{cite web |title=JOEY BADA$$ TAPS DIDDY, CHRIS BROWN, WESTSIDE GUNN + MORE FOR '2000' TRACKLIST |url=https://hiphopdx.com/news/id.71881/title.joey-badass-2000-features-cover-art-diddy-westside-gunn-chris-brown |website=HipHopDX|date=July 20, 2022 }}</ref>

==Artistry==
Capella Grey described his musical style as "singing at the tempo of a rapper", and cited [[Kranium]], [[Ty Dolla Sign]], [[Charlie Wilson (singer)|Charlie Wilson]], [[Jodeci]], [[Sean Combs|Diddy]], [[50 Cent]], and [[The Diplomats|Dipset]] as his musical influences.<ref name="The Break">{{cite web |title=THE BREAK PRESENTS – CAPELLA GREY |url=https://www.xxlmag.com/capella-grey-interview-the-break/ |website=XXL|date=August 25, 2021 }}</ref><ref>{{cite web |last1=Hot 97 |title=Capella Grey on A.Boogie Remixing Gyalis, Andre 3000 and Relationship Insecurities |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=fsPu0PSG6JI |via=YouTube}}</ref> He calls the genre of his music "RNBOP", which was coined by his frequent collaborator BBearded.<ref>{{cite web |last1=OTV Studios |title=Who is BBEARDED? BBeareded speaks on working with Capella Grey , Justin Timberlake and his childhood |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=siBix_rFk6Y |via=YouTube}}</ref>

'''Allepac the Family''' ("Capella" spelled backwards) is an American record label based in New York City founded by Capella Grey.<ref name="The Break"/> The label signed a joint venture deal with [[Capitol Records|Capitol]]. Capella Grey talked about the deal saying "they are a great team of record breakers and I am excited to work with them."<ref>{{cite web |title=Capella Grey, born Gyalis |url=https://www.jamaicaobserver.com/splash/capella-grey-born-gyalis_231432 |website=Jamaica Observer|date=September 17, 2021 }}</ref>
