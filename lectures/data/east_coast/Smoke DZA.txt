'''Sean Joseph Pompey''' (born February 8, 1984), better known by his stage name '''Smoke DZA''', is an American rapper and songwriter.

== Biography ==

=== 1984–2007: Early life and career beginnings ===
Smoke DZA was born February 8, 1984, at Harlem Hospital in New York City and has lived in New York all his life. The son of two Guyanese immigrants, Smoke was exposed to music at home by his father and fell in love with hip hop at a young age.<ref name=Digiwaxx>{{cite web|author=KingVanGogh|title=Digiwaxx TV and @PhotosbyRome Present: A Conversation with @SmokeDZA|url=http://www.theblastbydigiwaxx.com/2012/06/18/digiwaxxtv-photosbyrome-present-a-conversation-with-smoke-dza-video/|work=Video|publisher=The Blast By DIGWAXX|access-date=June 19, 2012}}</ref> He grew up listening to and emulating fellow New Yorkers [[Jay-Z]] and [[Notorious BIG]].<ref>{{cite web|last=Pomellis|first=Ayarra|title=Smoke DZA Interview: "Rolling Stoned"|url=http://rawroots.com/2011/08/smoke-dza-interview-rolling-stoned/|publisher=Raw Roots|access-date=25 June 2012}}</ref>  After dropping out of high school to pursue his music career, DZA began [[battle rap]]ping and has battled fellow Harlem-based rapper [[J.R. Writer]].<ref name=AngelaYee>{{cite web|title=Video: Smoke DZA Interview with Angela Yee|url=http://nahright.com/news/2010/03/05/video-smoke-dza-interview-w-angela-yee-cinematictv-ep-11/|publisher=Nah Right|access-date=25 June 2012}}</ref> Smoke DZA decided on his moniker after being influenced by Chris Tucker's character Smokey from the movie ''[[Friday (1995 film)|Friday]]'' and the acronym DZA stands for "Dream Zone Achieve".<ref>{{cite web|author=Thisis50Tv|title=TI50: Smoke DZA Says His Name Came From The Movie "Friday" & LA Has The Best Weed|url=http://www.thisis50.com/profiles/blogs/ti50-smoke-dza-says-his-name-came-from-the-movie-friday-la-has|publisher=Thisis50.com|access-date=25 June 2012}}</ref> He was discovered by [[Jonny Shipes]] of Cinematic Music Group in 2002 and went on to [[Ghostwriter|ghostwrite]] for numerous rappers, including  [[Hi-Tek]].<ref>{{cite web|last=Marrack|first=Peter|title=Interview: Smoke DZA|url=http://themarrack.com/interview-smoke-dza/|publisher=The Marrack|access-date=19 June 2012}}</ref> In 2002, he partnered to become one half of the group Smoke & Numbers, until going solo in 2008.<ref name=Respect>{{cite web|last=Marrack|first=Peter|title=The Interview with Smoke DZA|url=http://respect-mag.com/the-interview-w-smoke-dza-talks-sweet-baby-kushed-god-xxl-freshman-cover-christmas-in-the-trap-smoking-at-shows/|publisher=Respect Magazine|access-date=19 June 2012}}</ref>

=== 2008–2016: ''Rugby Thompson'', ''Dream.Zone.Achieve'' and ''Don't Smoke Rock'' ===
Since splitting from Smoke & Numbers to pursue a solo career,<ref name="Respect"/> DZA has released a string of mixtapes including Substance Abuse, Substance Abuse 1.5, George Kush The Button, Rolling Stoned, Sweet Baby Kushed God, Cuz I Felt Like It and K.O.N.Y. He has worked with [[Big K.R.I.T.]], [[Wiz Khalifa]], and [[ASAP Rocky]] among others and is a founding member of the Smokers Club.<ref>{{cite web|last=Markman|first=Rob|title=Fire Starter: Smoke DZA|url=http://www.mtv.com/news/articles/1662464/smoke-dza.jhtml|publisher=MTV|access-date=19 June 2012}}</ref>  His album, Rolling Stoned was awarded the High Times Doobie Award for Hip Hop album of the year for 2011.<ref>{{cite web|last=Black|first=Bobby|title=2011 Doobie Awards Round Up|url=http://hightimes.com/lounge/bobbyblack/7029|publisher=High Times|access-date=19 June 2012}}</ref>  DZA latest album Rugby Thompson was released in June 2012 by High Times and Cinematic Music Group. The album was produced entirely by Harry Fraud.<ref>{{cite web|last=Diep|first=Eric|title=Album Stream:Smoke DZA "Rugby Thompson"|url=http://www.complex.com/music/2012/06/album-stream-smoke-dza-rugby-thompson|publisher=Complex Magazine|access-date=19 June 2012}}</ref> The album features numerous artists including [[Action Bronson]], [[Currensy]], [[Domo Genesis]], [[Schoolboy Q]] and [[ASAP Twelvyy]].<ref>{{cite web|last=Badami|first=Anthony|title=Discovery Smoke DZA|date=18 June 2012 |url=http://www.interviewmagazine.com/music/discovery-smoke-dza#_|publisher=Interview Magazine|access-date=19 June 2012}}</ref>

On April 1, 2014, Smoke DZA released ''Dream.Zone.Achieve'', his third studio album. The album consisted of 21 songs broken down into three sections, Dream, Zone and Achieve, each with 7 tracks. Features included fellow Harlem native [[Cam'ron]], Joey Bada$$, Ab-Soul, [[Wiz Khalifa]] and [[Curren$y]].<ref>{{cite web|last=Hardy |first=Jasmine |url=http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/news/id.27482/title.smoke-dza-dream-zone-achieve-release-date-cover-art-tracklist |title=Smoke DZA "Dream.ZONE.Achieve" Release Date, Cover Art & Tracklist |work=[[HipHopDX]] |date=4 March 2014 |publisher=Cheri Media Group|access-date=2014-03-06}}</ref>

On April 6, 2014, in the midst of [[WrestleMania XXX]], Smoke DZA released a mixtape titled ''Ringside 2'', the second installment in his wrestling-inspired ''Ringside'' EP series. Production was handled his by fellow R.F.C. member 183rd, with features from Peter Rosenberg, then [[WWE Intercontinental Championship|WWE Intercontinental Champion]] [[Big E Langston]], Aston Matthews and [[Flatbush Zombies]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.hotnewhiphop.com/smoke-dza-ringside-2-ep-prod-by-183rd-new-mixtape.110906.html|title=Smoke DZA - Ringside 2 EP (Prod. By 183rd)|first=Nicolas |last=James|work=HotNewHipHop|date=6 April 2014 |access-date=9 March 2015}}</ref>

On December 2, 2016, he and Pete Rock released "Don't Smoke Rock", featuring 13 songs with guest appearances from [[Rick Ross]], [[Dave East]], [[Cam'ron]], [[Big K.R.I.T.]] and [[Mac Miller]].<ref>[http://www.hotnewhiphop.com/smoke-dza-and-pete-rock-limitless-feat-dave-east-new-song.1971791.html Don't Smoke Rock]</ref>

Smoke DZA released his eight album, ''Homegrown'', on October 2, 2020. It marks his fourth project of the year, following the acclaimed ''A Closed Mouth Don't Get Fed'', ''Ringside 8'' and ''The Smokers Club Presents: Smoke DZA's Worldwide Smoke Session'', the latter of which was released on April 20. ''Homegrown'' is an ode to his native New York City.<ref name="Homegrown">{{cite web|url=https://thesource.com/2020/09/03/smoke-dza-announces-homegrown-album-for-october-releases-hibachi-video/|title=Smoke DZA Announces 'Homegrown' Album for October|work=[[The Source]]|last=Grant|first=Shawn|date=September 3, 2020|access-date=October 1, 2020}}</ref>

==Musical style and influences==
Smoke cites fellow New York natives [[Jay-Z]] and [[Notorious B.I.G.]] as his main influences. As an adolescent he would use their songs and write his own lyrics, helping him learn rhyming patterns."I used to re-write Biggie. It started with '[[Mo Money, Mo Problems]].' I would switch up the words, make it my own."<ref>{{cite web|last=Badami|first=Anthony|title=Discovery: Smoke DZA|date=18 June 2012 |url=http://www.interviewmagazine.com/music/discovery-smoke-dza#_|publisher=Interview Magazine|access-date=25 June 2012}}</ref> He has also cited fellow Harlem rappers like Mase, Big L and Dipset as influences and inspiration.<ref>{{cite web|first=Cris |last=Copastetik|title=Smoke DZA Talks "Rolling Stoned" Reviews|url=http://dealwithnodeal.net/2012/01/smoke-dza-talks-rolling-stoned-reviews.html/|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120203130718/http://www.dealwithnodeal.net/2012/01/smoke-dza-talks-rolling-stoned-reviews.html|archive-date=3 February 2012|publisher=Deal with No Deal|access-date=5 February 2016}}</ref>  Though he is often mistakenly classified as a weed rapper, Smoke considers himself a lifestyle rapper and prefers not to be confined to one genre of rap.<ref>{{cite web|title=Smoke DZA Interview with Good*Fella Media|url=http://www.fresh-grind.com/2012/01/13/smoke-dza-interview-w-goodfella-media-video-2/|work=video|publisher=FreshGrind.com|access-date=25 June 2012}}</ref> DZA's albums and mixtapes often feature many collaborative tracks. DZA says he enjoys collaborating with fellow artists like Dom Kennedy and [[Kendrick Lamar]] as he feels it helps him "step his ball game up" in terms of his craft.<ref>{{cite web|last=Meara|first=Paul|title=Smoke DZA Interview|date=15 August 2011 |url=http://mearablogtv.wordpress.com/2011/08/15/smoke-dza-interview/|publisher=TheMearaBlog.com|access-date=25 June 2012}}</ref>

== Personal life ==
In 2016, Smoke endorsed [[Bernie Sanders]]' [[Bernie Sanders 2016 presidential campaign|presidential bid]].<ref>{{Cite web |last=Camp |first=Zoe |date=2016-04-19 |title=Smoke DZA Endorses Bernie Sanders on Flying-Lotus Produced Track: Listen |url=https://pitchfork.com/news/64927-smoke-dza-endorses-bernie-sanders-on-flying-lotus-produced-track-listen/ |access-date=2023-08-14 |website=[[Pitchfork (website)|Pitchfork]] |language=en-US}}</ref>
