'''Sandra Jacqueline Denton''' (born 9 November 1964<ref>{{Cite book|last=Hess|first=Mickey|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=XkCncJ7j744C&dq=sandra+denton+1964&pg=PA60|title=Hip Hop in America: A Regional Guide|publisher=ABC-CLIO|year=2009|isbn=978-0-313-34321-6}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|last=Hicks|first=Jonathan P.|date=November 9, 2013|title=This Day in Black History: Nov. 9, 1964|url=http://www.bet.com/news/national/2013/11/09/this-day-in-black-history-nov-9-1964.html|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140122222034/http://www.bet.com/news/national/2013/11/09/this-day-in-black-history-nov-9-1964.html|archive-date=January 22, 2014|website=bet.com}}</ref> or 1969<ref>[http://worldcat.org/identities/lccn-no2001068506/ Worldcat – Denton, Sandra 1969 -]</ref><ref>{{Cite book|last1=Price|first1=Emmett George|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=Q84TiHcqDqcC&dq=sandra+denton+1969&pg=PA185|title=Hip Hop Culture|last2=Iber|first2=Jorge|date=2006|publisher=ABC-CLIO|isbn=978-1-85109-867-5|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite book|last=Betts|first=Graham|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=FAo5AQAAIAAJ&q=sandra+denton+1969|title=Complete UK Hit Singles 1952-2006|date=2006|publisher=Collins|isbn=978-0-00-720077-1|language=en}}</ref>), better known by her stage name '''Pepa''' or '''Pep''', is a Jamaican-American rapper, best known for her work as a member of the female rap trio [[Salt-N-Pepa]]. Denton starred in ''[[The Salt-N-Pepa Show]]'', a reality TV series focusing on reforming the group which aired on the [[VH1]] network in 2008. Since January 2016, Denton has appeared as a supporting cast member on the music [[reality television]] show ''[[Growing Up Hip Hop]]'' which airs on [[We TV]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.etonline.com/tv/221929_exclusive_salt_n_pepa_makes_a_cameo_sneak_peek_of_growing_up_hip_hop_season_three_watch |title=Salt-N-Pepa Makes a Cameo in Sneak Peek of 'Growing Up Hip Hop' Season Three |format=video|author=<!--Not stated--> |date=19 July 2017 |website= Entertainment Tonight |publisher=<!--Not stated--> |access-date=17 December 2017 }}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.etonline.com/tv/222567_exclusive_master_p_gets_flirty_with_sandy_pepa_denton_on_growing_up_hip_hop |title=Master P Gets Flirty With Sandy 'Pepa' Denton on 'Growing Up Hip Hop' |author=<!--Not stated--> |date=27 July 2017 |website=Entertainment Tonight |publisher=<!--Not stated--> |access-date=17 December 2017 }}</ref>

== Early life ==
Sandra Jacqueline Denton<ref>{{cite book |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=ioKfAAAAMAAJ&q=sandra+jacqueline+denton |title=Encyclopedia of Rap and Hip-hop Culture |last=Bynoe|first=Yvonne|date=2006 |publisher=Greenwood Press |access-date=16 October 2017 |isbn=978-0-313-33058-2 }}</ref><ref>{{cite book |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=Fmyx52UuMjAC&q=sandra+jacqueline+denton |title=Hip-Hop Divas |last=Magazine |first=Vibe |date=2001 |publisher=Three Rivers Press|access-date=16 October 2017 |isbn=978-0-609-80836-8 }}</ref> was born in [[Kingston, Jamaica]] to Charles and Enid Denton (née Hyacinth),<ref>{{cite book |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=Fmyx52UuMjAC&q=enid+denton |title=Hip-Hop Divas |last=Magazine |first=Vibe |date=2001 |publisher=Three Rivers Press|access-date=22 November 2017 |isbn=978-0-609-80836-8 }}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=DpntcE26NMgC&q=enid+denton&pg=PA114 |title=Wedding Of The Decade (Pepa & Treach Wed In Star Studded Ceremony) |work=Ebony Magazine |date= October 1999 |publisher= Johnson Publishing Company |via= Google Books}}</ref> the youngest of eight children. Denton lived on a farm in Jamaica with her grandmother until she was six years old.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.biography.com/people/sandra-pepa-denton-21262655|title=Sandra "Pepa" Denton|website=Biography|date=25 January 2021 }}</ref> Denton's family moved to [[Queens]], New York, when she was three; and she later joined them. Denton has been a performer since childhood. Denton was molested as a child. Denton released a book about her life in 2008 entitled ''Let's Talk About Pep'' in which she talked about being molested, her many abusive relationships, and how Salt-N-Pepa formed.{{citation needed|date=May 2021}}

In 1979, 15-year-old<ref name="VH1145"/> Denton was sent to live with her elder sister Patsy in [[Logan, Utah]], when her family home in Queens caught fire and so she could have a better life.<ref>{{cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=_EmoPe7YXMwC&q=Utah|title=Let's Talk About Pep|isbn=978-1-4391-2660-8|last1=Denton|first1=Sandy|date=26 August 2008|publisher=Simon and Schuster }}</ref><ref name="VH1145">{{cite web|url=http://www.vh1.com/episodes/vsyrsw/behind-the-music-salt-n-pepa-behind-the-music-145-season-1-ep-145|title=Behind The Music – Salt 'n' Pepa: Behind the Music #145 – VH1|website=VH1}}</ref> According to Denton, she was the only Black student attending [[Logan High School (Utah)|Logan High School]] during that time. Denton became inspired by [[rock music]]; citing [[AC/DC]] and [[Led Zeppelin]] as her inspirations during her time in Utah. After a year in Utah, Denton returned to New York, later graduating from [[Springfield Gardens High School]]. After high school, Denton enrolled at [[Queensborough Community College]] to study [[nursing]]. While attending college, Denton met fellow nursing student [[Salt (rapper)|Cheryl James]].{{citation needed|date=May 2021}}

== Career ==
With the production by [[Hurby Azor|Hurby "Luv Bug" Azor]] whom she and James met while working as customer service representatives at [[Sears]], James and Denton released a single called "The Showstopper" which became a moderate [[R&B]] hit in late 1985.<ref>{{cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=Z2Z7DRO5BygC&q=The+showstopper+salt+n+pepa+1985&pg=PT21|title=Hip-Hop Fashion|first=Sue Vander|last=Hook|date=6 February 2018|publisher=Capstone|via=Google Books|isbn=978-1-4296-4017-6}}</ref><ref>{{cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=LldOLnIQ66cC&q=The+showstopper+salt+n+pepa+1985&pg=PA198|title=Icons of Hip Hop: An Encyclopedia of the Movement, Music, and Culture|first=Mickey|last=Hess|publisher=ABC-CLIO|via=Google Books|isbn=978-0-313-33902-8|year=2007}}</ref> They were joined with [[Latoya Hanson]] who was the original DJ of the group. Shortly after in 1986, [[DJ Spinderella|Deidra "Spinderella" Roper]] joined as the group's DJ as a full-length debut album, ''[[Hot, Cool & Vicious]]'', was being released. The trio released a total of five studio albums: ''Hot, Cool, and Vicious'' (1986), ''[[A Salt with a Deadly Pepa]]'' (1988), ''[[Blacks' Magic]]'' (1990), ''[[Very Necessary]]'' (1993), and ''[[Brand New (Salt-n-Pepa album)|Brand New]]'' (1997), plus several greatest hits albums. Salt-N-Pepa disbanded in 2002, several months after their ''Brand New'' album was released on Red Ant Records. Denton's co-group member Cheryl James had stated she was ready to leave the music industry. The trio reunited for a performance on [[VH1]]'s ''[[Hip Hop Honors]]'' program on 22 September 2005. In 2005, Denton was a cast member of VH1's ''[[The Surreal Life]]'' (season 5).{{citation needed|date=May 2021}}

Denton's acting credits also include the motion picture ''[[Joe's Apartment]]'', an appearance in the [[HBO]] movie ''First Time Felon'', and a stint as Officer [[Andrea Phelan]] on the HBO drama, ''[[Oz (TV series)|Oz]]''. She starred on ''[[The Surreal Life: Fame Games]]''. Salt-N-Pepa reformed in 2008, and are still{{when|date=February 2021}} in the process of releasing an album since reforming as they work out past issues.{{citation needed|date=February 2021}} Denton teamed up with James for VH1's ''[[The Salt-N-Pepa Show]]''. She starred in her own reality show on the network entitled ''[[Let's Talk About Pep]]'', word-play on the group's hit song "[[Let's Talk About Sex]]". She can be heard speaking Jamaican Patois in the song "[[Need U Bad]]" by [[Jazmine Sullivan]]. In August 2008, Denton released her autobiography, which was also entitled ''Let's Talk About Pep''. It was co-written by [[Karen Hunter]], and it offered a look behind the fame, family, failures, and successes of her life in one of hip-hop's most successful groups. It features an introduction by [[Queen Latifah]], and an epilogue by [[Missy Elliott]]. To accommodate the book, Pepa launched her own social network for her fans. On 23 October 2008, Salt-N-Pepa performed at the BET Hip Hop Awards. In January 2011, Denton appeared in an episode of the [[TBS (U.S. TV channel)|TBS]] sitcom ''[[Are We There Yet? (TV series)|Are We There Yet?]]'' as Tammy, a woman who falls for the [[Terry Crews]] character of Nick. Denton joined the reality television show ''[[Growing Up Hip Hop]]'' as a supporting cast member in January 2016 along with her daughter Egypt (a main cast member) and niece Tahira Francis.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.wetv.com/shows/growing-up-hip-hop/meet-the-cast |title= Growing Up Hip-Hop: Meet The Cast |author=<!--Not stated--> |date=<!--Not stated--> |website=WE tv |publisher=<!--Not stated--> |access-date=17 December 2017 }}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.wetv.com/shows/growing-up-hip-hop/videos/season-03/bad-and-boogie/is-pepas-new-house-haunted |title=IS PEPA'S NEW HOUSE HAUNTED? SEASON 3, EPISODE 2 |author=<!--Not stated--> |date=27 July 2017 |website=WE tv |publisher=<!--Not stated--> |access-date=17 December 2017}}</ref>

== Personal life ==
Denton once dated and was engaged to rapper [[Prince Markie Dee]] sometime during the mid-1980s.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://people.com/archive/salt-n-pepa-shake-it-up-laying-a-cold-rap-on-men-vol-29-no-15/|title=Salt 'n Pepa Shake It Up, Laying a Cold Rap on Men}}</ref> On 6 July 1990, Denton gave birth to her first child, a son she had with New York rapper Tyran "Tah-Tah" Moore<ref name=BN>{{cite web|url=https://www.instagram.com/p/Bk6UEVcjnJf/ |archive-url=https://ghostarchive.org/iarchive/s/instagram/Bk6UEVcjnJf |archive-date=2021-12-24 |url-access=limited|title=Wishing my son, my first born a HAPPY BIRTHDAY! I can't wait until your back back in LA and we celebrate! As Mama Says All the Best! Love you Ty ! @tysensei07|last=Pepa D|first=Sandra|website=Instagram.com|access-date=9 July 2018}}{{cbignore}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.nydailynews.com/entertainment/gossip/rapper-life-story-pepa-violence-article-1.319765|title=Rapper's life story, Pepa'd with violence|website=[[New York Daily News]]}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://nypost.com/2003/08/29/hostage-drama-thug-holds-trio-in-2-hour-standoff/|title=HOSTAGE DRAMA: THUG HOLDS TRIO IN 2-HOUR STANDOFF|date=29 August 2003}}</ref> whom she named Tyran Denton Jr. She made a guest appearance on ''[[Ricki Lake (TV series)|Ricki Lake]]'' in 1994, teaching teenage girls the responsibilities of being a mother. Denton was the owner of HollyHood, a retail clothing store in the Buckhead section of [[Atlanta]], [[Georgia (U.S. state)|Georgia]], which opened in November 1995.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=jCwEAAAAMBAJ&q=Hollyhood+atlanta+pepa&pg=PA116 |title=Pepa's Hollyhood |work=Vibe Magazine |date= 1 March 1996 |publisher=<!--Not stated--> |access-date=17 December 2017 |via=Google Books}}</ref><ref>{{cite magazine |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=7SsEAAAAMBAJ&q=Hollyhood+atlanta+pepa&pg=PA46 |title= Strictly Business: Rappers Get Entrepreneurial Itch |magazine=Vibe Magazine |date= 1 November 1996 |publisher=<!--Not stated--> |access-date=17 December 2017 |via=Google Books}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=MTsDAAAAMBAJ&q=Hollyhood+atlanta+pepa&pg=PA45 |title= Signing In |work=JET Magazine|date=18 December 1995 |publisher=Johnson Publishing Company |access-date=17 December 2017 |via=Google Books}}</ref> On 2 April 1999, Denton married [[Treach]] (whose real name is Anthony Criss) of the rap group [[Naughty by Nature]] at a Kansas City tattoo parlor after dating on and off for seven years.<ref>{{cite book |url=https://archive.org/details/bub_gb_QSwEAAAAMBAJ |page=[https://archive.org/details/bub_gb_QSwEAAAAMBAJ/page/n38 115] |quote=Pepa and treach. |title= Lovers |author=<!--Not stated--> |date=December 1996 – January 1997 |work= Vibe Magazine |publisher=Vibe Media |access-date=28 November 2017 |via= Internet Archive}}</ref><ref>{{cite book |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=LldOLnIQ66cC&q=Pepa+and+treach&pg=PA212 |title=Icons of Hip Hop: 1 |author=<!--Not stated--> |date=2007 |publisher= ABC-CLIO |access-date=28 November 2017 |isbn=978-0-313-33902-8}}</ref> Their wedding ceremony was held at their home in [[Morristown, New Jersey]], on 24 July 1999.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Brady|first1=Lois|title=VOWS; Sandra Denton and Anthony Criss|url=https://www.nytimes.com/1999/08/01/style/vows-sandra-denton-and-anthony-criss.html|access-date=28 April 2016|work=The New York Times|date=1 August 1999}}</ref> Denton and Treach had one daughter, Egypt Jahnari Criss (born 2 September 1998). After allegations of physical abuse inflicted by Treach, they divorced in 2001.<ref>{{cite news|title=Sandy "Pepa" Denton: Pep Talk|url=http://www.essence.com/2008/08/27/sandy-pepa-denton-pep-talk|access-date=28 April 2016|publisher=Essence}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://celebritybabies.people.com/2008/08/29/sandy-pepa-dent/|title=Sandy 'Pepa' Denton Keeps an Open Dialogue with&nbsp;Kids}}</ref>
