'''Dainene Alexia Baldwin'''<ref>{{Cite web|title=Dai Burger on Instagram|url=https://www.instagram.com/p/BwMf8RmBP8u/|website=[[Instagram]]|access-date = July 23, 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.powderzine.com/node/409|title=the dai burger interview|website=powderzine.com|access-date=July 23, 2019}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=Dainene Alexia Baldwin|url=https://www.deezer.com/en/artist/9182320|website=[[Deezer]]|access-date = July 23, 2019}}</ref> (born February 13),<ref>{{cite web|url=https://ladygunn.com/dai-burger/|title=DAI BURGER|date=February 14, 2014|website=Ladygunn|access-date=July 23, 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190723195756/https://ladygunn.com/dai-burger/|archive-date=July 23, 2019|url-status=dead}}</ref> best known by her stage name, '''Dai Burger''' [[Pronunciation respelling for English|(/deɪ/)]]<ref>{{Cite web|title = DAY|url = http://dictionary.cambridge.org/us/pronunciation/english/day|website = dictionary.cambridge.org|access-date = 2015-12-06}}</ref> is an American rapper. She is from [[Queens]] in [[New York City]]. She has released two studio albums to date.

==Early life==
Baldwin was born and raised in Queens, but has also lived in [[Brooklyn]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.highsnobiety.com/2017/10/02/dai-burger-soft-serve-interview/|title=Meet Dai Burger, the Foodie-Rapper You Need To Stop Sleeping On|date=October 2, 2017 |website=Highsnobiety|access-date=July 23, 2019}}</ref>

==Career==
Baldwin's performing career began while working as a [[backup dancer]] on tour with [[Lil Mama]].<ref name="Shorey2">{{cite web |url=http://www.mtviggy.com/lists/the-next-wave-of-queer-hip-hop-lgbt-rap/ |title=The Next Wave of Queer Hip-Hop: 12 Upcoming Legendary Children |last=Shorey |first=Eric |date=September 1, 2013 |website=www.queerty.com |publisher=[[Queerty]] |access-date=July 20, 2015 |quote=She has previously collaborated with Cakes Da Killa and Jonte (another notable queer artist) and sometimes outshines her partners.}}</ref> Since that time she has appeared in several music videos, including [[Beyoncé]]'s "[[Party (Beyoncé song)|Party]]",<ref name="Ladygunn">{{cite web |url = http://ladygunn.com/music/dai-burger|title = Dai Burger|author = <!--Staff writer(s); no by-line.-->|date = February 13, 2014|website = ladygunn.com/|publisher = LADYGUNN|access-date = July 20, 2015|quote = If you don’t know,..}}</ref> and has worked as a stylist for designer [[Patricia Field]]'s clothing boutique.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Rosenbloom|first1=Stephanie|title=Embracing the Pop Star in Your Closet|url=http://runway.blogs.nytimes.com/2011/10/25/embracing-the-pop-star-in-your-closet/|work=The New York Times|date=October 25, 2011}}</ref> She had also toured with [[Chris Brown]] and [[Soulja Boy]] as a backup dancer.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Sanders|first=Wren|title=Dai Burger Wants You to Take a Bite of Her Latest Album|url=https://www.them.us/story/out-loud-dai-burger-latest-album|access-date=2021-05-28|website=them.|date=December 3, 2019|language=en-US}}</ref>

Baldwin launched her music career with her 2010 project ''MyMixxxytape'', hosted by rapper [[Junglepussy]]. She explains that her [[stage name]] came "out of nowhere, but I like to think that I'm stacked, and juicy."<ref>{{Cite news|url = http://bedfordandbowery.com/2013/07/bushwick-is-burning-underground-trap-raves-keep-it-weird/|title = Bushwick is Burning: Underground trap Raves Keep it Weird|last = Maharry|first = Lindsay|date = 9 July 2013|work = Bedford + Bowery|access-date = 22 July 2015}}</ref> In 2011, Burger released her second compilation ''Raw Burger'' featuring Junglepussy on the track "Titty Attack".<ref name="RS">{{cite magazine|last1=Shepherd|first1=Julianne Escobedo|title=Artist to Watch: Dai Burger|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/videos/artist-to-watch-dai-burger-20140428|magazine=Rolling Stone|date=April 28, 2014}}</ref>

"In Ya' Mouf" was released via ''[[Complex Networks|Complex]]'' Magazine in January 2014. Featuring production by Shane Augustus and Mighty Mark, the mixtape was well received because of Baldwin's clever lyricism and confident representation of girl power.<ref name=Ortiz>{{Cite web|title = Stream Dai Burger's New Project "In Ya Mouf"|url = http://www.complex.com/music/2014/01/dai-burger-in-ya-mouf-stream|last=Ortiz |first=Edwin |website = Complex|access-date = 2015-12-06}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |title=In Ya Mouf! : @DaiBurger Drops New Mixtape #EXCLUSIVE - #THRILLERGANG |url=http://jackthriller.com/in-ya-mouf-daiburger-drops-new-mixtape-exclusive/ |website=#THRILLERGANG |access-date=2015-12-06 |language=en-US |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140307175031/http://jackthriller.com/in-ya-mouf-daiburger-drops-new-mixtape-exclusive/ |archive-date=March 7, 2014 |df=mdy }}</ref> The video for the single "Soufflé" premiered in ''[[Interview (magazine)|Interview]]'' magazine<ref>{{Cite web|title = Exclusive Video Premiere: 'Soufflé,' Dai Burger|last=Chapman |first=Alex| url = http://www.interviewmagazine.com/music/exclusive-video-premiere-souffl-dai-burger/|website = Interview Magazine|date=November 19, 2013 |access-date = 2015-12-06}}</ref> and was picked as one of the best [[Feminism|feminist]] music videos of 2013.<ref name="BF">{{cite web |url=http://feministing.com/2013/12/12/best-feminist-music-videos-of-2013/ |title=Best Feminist Music Videos of 2013 |last=Bayetti Flores |first=Verónica |date=2013 |website=feministing.com |publisher=[[Feministing]] |access-date=July 20, 2015}}</ref> The release was featured on [[Solange Knowles]]' Saint-Heron<ref>{{Cite web|title = Video: Dai Burger, "Soufflé" - Saint Heron|url = http://saintheron.com/music/video-dai-burger-souffle/|website = Saint Heron|access-date = 2015-12-06|language = en-US}}</ref> and contributed to Burger being named "Artist To Watch" by ''Rolling Stone'' magazine in April 2014.<ref name="RS" />

Gearing up for her next project, Baldwin collaborated with London-based producer [[Darq E Freaker]] on the track "Choppin Necks".<ref>{{cite news|last1=Trammell|first1=Matthew|title=Darq E Freaker & Dai Burger's "Choppin Necks" Is Wicked|url=http://www.thefader.com/2015/03/03/darq-e-freaker-dai-burgers-choppin-necks-is-wicked|work=The FADER|date=March 3, 2015}}</ref> "Choppin Necks" was hailed by critics as a futuristic dark song, in all the right ways. Burger delivered "jaw-dropping bars", according to James Keith from ''Complex''.<ref>{{Cite news|url = http://uk.complex.com/music/2015/03/darq-e-freaker-choppin-necks-feat-dai-burger|title = Darq E Freaker & Dai Burger's 'Choppin Necks' Is All Space-Age Grime|last = Keith|first = James|date = 4 March 2015|work = Complex|access-date = 22 July 2015}}</ref>

On December 4, 2015, Dai Burger premiered her 5-track self-titled project "Dai Burger" exclusively with ''[[Dazed]]'' magazine.<ref name="dazeddigital.com">{{Cite web|title = Premiere: Dai Burger is back with her attitude-packed new EP|url = http://www.dazeddigital.com/music/article/28706/1/premiere-dai-burger-is-back-with-her-attitude-packed-new-ep|website = Dazed|date = December 3, 2015|access-date = 2015-12-06}}</ref>

Baldwin performed at the first ever "Sound & Style" event in New York, hosted by [[Boss Lady]] and [[Reebok]]<ref>{{Cite news|url = http://www.vibe.com/2012/02/boss-lady-brings-sound-style-nyc-brianna-snow-da-product-jasmine-solano-and-more/|title = Boss Lady Brings 'Sound & Style' to NYC|last = DamnAnthony|date = 9 February 2012|work = Vibe|access-date = 22 July 2015|archive-date = July 22, 2015|archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20150722190132/http://www.vibe.com/2012/02/boss-lady-brings-sound-style-nyc-brianna-snow-da-product-jasmine-solano-and-more/|url-status = dead}}</ref> and was as an opening act for [[Lil Wayne]] and [[2 Chainz|2 chainz]] in Long Beach, NY in the summer of 2014. To date she has collaborated with artists including [[Kool Keith]], [[Cakes da Killa]], [[Cazwell]] and [[Jonte' Moaning]].<ref name="Shorey1">{{cite web |url=http://www.queerty.com/5-gay-lady-rappers-who-are-also-better-than-everybody-20130901/3 |title=5 Gay Lady Rappers Who Are (Also) "Better Than Everybody" |last=Shorey |first=Eric |date=January 21, 2015 |website=www.mtviggy.com/ |publisher=[[MTV]] |access-date=July 20, 2015}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Kool Keith Announces Condom-equipped 'Total Orgasm' Box Set|url=http://exclaim.ca/music/article/kool_keith_announces_total_orgasm_box_set_with_condom|publisher=Exclaim!|access-date=26 May 2016|date=April 13, 2015}}</ref>

Her debut studio album, titled ''Soft Serve'', was released on August 24, 2017.<ref>{{Cite magazine|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/7941825/dai-burger-interview-new-album-soft-serve|title=Rising Star Dai Burger Talks New Album 'Soft Serve,' Spice Girls & Sexuality|magazine=Billboard|access-date=2017-08-25}}</ref>

Baldwin released her second studio album, ''Bite the Burger'', in 2019. The album released a score of 6.7 from ''Pitchfork,'' with the reviewer commenting, "An artist as bold and honest as Dai should never sound so boring."<ref>{{Cite web|title=Dai Burger: Bite the Burger|url=https://pitchfork.com/reviews/albums/dai-burger-bite-the-burger/|access-date=2021-06-24|website=Pitchfork|language=en}}</ref>

Dai Burger is openly gay.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.queerty.com/5-gay-lady-rappers-who-are-also-better-than-everybody-20130901|title = 5 Gay Lady Rappers Who Are (Also) "Better Than Everybody"| date=September 2013 }}</ref> Talking in an interview about her sexuality she stated, "I like [[queer]] because it’s an umbrella, more than it identifies one thing. I don’t have to, you know, choose a letter."<ref>{{Cite web|title=Here comes Dai Burger - a fabulous antidote to misery|url=https://www.loudandquiet.com/interview/here-comes-dai-burger-a-fabulous-antidote-to-misery/|access-date=2021-06-24|website=Loud And Quiet|language=en-US}}</ref>
