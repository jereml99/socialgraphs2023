'''Junius Donald Rogers''', professionally known as '''ZillaKami''', is an American rapper, singer, and songwriter. He is a member of the [[hip hop]] duo [[City Morgue]] and a former songwriter for [[6ix9ine]].<ref name="Darkweb Insider">{{cite web |last1=King |first1=Seth |title=Why you love Zillakami, not 6IX9INE |url=https://darkwebinsider.com/lifestyle-culture/2018/9/3/34wqj9axyhwjgupao0ai87uqfwvbvg |access-date=December 12, 2018}}</ref>

He has been noted by publications such as ''[[HotNewHipHop]]'' as a pioneer of [[trap metal]].<ref>{{Cite web|date=September 17, 2021|title=ZillaKami Delivers Experimental New Album "Dog Boy"|url=https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/zillakami-delivers-experimental-new-album-dog-boy-new-mixtape.121956.html|access-date=November 17, 2021|website=HotNewHipHop|language=en}}</ref> Rogers is the CEO of Dog Years Skate Club, a [[Skateboarding|skate]] apparel and equipment brand.

== Musical style and influence ==
ZillaKami's music has been described as merging elements of [[hardcore punk]] and [[heavy metal music|heavy metal]] with [[trap music]],<ref name="Rolling Stone" /> by incorporating electric guitars and guttural shouted vocals and aggressive lyrical themes.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Guzmán |first1=José |title=ZillaKami: A creator of nightmares and responsible for 6ix9ine |date=November 27, 2018 |url=https://heabbi.com/zillakami-6ix9ine |access-date=December 13, 2018}}</ref><ref name="undergroundunderdogs">{{cite web |last1=SHARMA |first1=SAMYAK |title=How NYC's Zillakami is Redefining Rap |date=February 14, 2018 |url=https://undergroundunderdogs.com/articles/zillakami/ |access-date=December 13, 2018}}</ref> His lyrics often depict themes of extreme violence, death, [[Sadomasochism|masochism]] and drug-use.<ref name="undergroundunderdogs" /><ref name="Complex">{{cite web |last1=MOORE |first1=JACOB |title=New York Rapper Zillakami is Taking Hip-Hop to Brutal New Extremes |url=https://www.complex.com/pigeons-and-planes/2017/12/zillakami |website=[[Complex (magazine)|Complex]] |access-date=December 15, 2018}}</ref> In an article by [[Complex (magazine)|Complex magazine]], writer Jacob Moore described his music as "the most merciless rap music since [[Necro (rapper)|Necro]]".<ref name="Complex" />

He cites his biggest influences as [[Title Fight]]'s albums ''[[Floral Green]]'' and ''[[Hyperview (album)|Hyperview]]'', [[DMX (rapper)|DMX]], as well as [[Radiohead]]'s album ''[[In Rainbows]]'' and [[Onyx (hip hop group)|Onyx]].<ref name="No Jumper" />
