'''Demond Price''' (born February 16, 1982), known professionally as '''Conway the Machine''' (or simply '''Conway'''),<ref name=":0"/> is an American rapper. Alongside his paternal half-brother [[Westside Gunn]] and cousin [[Benny the Butcher]], Conway was a member of Griselda Records. On February 21, 2022, Conway announced his departure from Griselda Records.<ref>{{Cite news |last=Moorwood |first=Victoria |date=February 21, 2022 |title=Conway the Machine announces split from Griselda and Shady Records |work=Revolt |url=https://www.revolt.tv/article/2022-02-21/153601/conway-the-machine-leaves-griselda-shady-records/ |access-date=February 25, 2022}}</ref>

== History ==
===2012–2019: Early mixtapes and ''G.O.A.T.''===
At age 23, Conway spent some time in jail, which he has cited as a motivation for weaning from a gangster lifestyle to focus on his career in music.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|url=https://consequenceofsound.net/2017/11/artist-of-the-month-conway-on-repping-buffalo-second-chances-and-being-the-grimiest-of-all-time/|title=Artist of the Month Conway on Repping Buffalo, Second Chances, and Being the Grimiest of All Time|date=November 16, 2017|website=Consequence of Sound|access-date=June 1, 2019}}</ref>

In 2012, Conway was shot in the head, neck and shoulder;<ref>{{Cite web|date=March 18, 2012|title=4 wounded in 3 separate shootings|url=https://buffalonews.com/news/4-wounded-in-3-separate-shootings/article_9c9044ab-9a4e-5500-95f8-86997c2b6314.html|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210609155109/https://buffalonews.com/news/4-wounded-in-3-separate-shootings/article_9c9044ab-9a4e-5500-95f8-86997c2b6314.html |archive-date=June 9, 2021 |access-date=|website=[[The Buffalo News]]}}</ref> his face was partially paralyzed, giving him a signature slur.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://pitchfork.com/reviews/albums/conway-goat/|title=Conway: G.O.A.T.|website=Pitchfork|access-date=June 1, 2019}}</ref>

In 2014, Westside Gunn founded [[Griselda Records]], through which Conway and Westside Gunn would self-release their own projects and the works of artists such as Benny the Butcher and Mach-Hommy.<ref>{{Cite web |title=A Guide to Griselda |url=https://www.complex.com/music/griselda-records-guide-everything-you-need-to-know |access-date=2022-11-26 |website=Complex |language=en}}</ref> In 2015, Conway released his first two official mixtape projects through Griselda Records, ''The Devil's Reject'' and ''Reject 2''.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Conway the Machine - The Devil's Reject |url=https://www.albumoftheyear.org/album/135924-conway-the-devils-reject.php |access-date=2022-11-26 |website=Album of The Year |language=en-US}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |title=Reject 2, by Conway |url=https://daupe.bandcamp.com/album/reject-2 |access-date=2022-11-26 |website=Daupe |language=en}}</ref> Conway and Westside Gunn also released two collaborative EPs through London-based record label Daupe!,<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.discogs.com/label/489399-Daupe!|title=Daupe!|website=[[Discogs]]|access-date=June 1, 2019}}</ref> the first of which was 2015's ''Hall & Nash'', followed by 2016's ''Griselda Ghost''. In April 2016, during [[Mobb Deep]] and [[Smif-N-Wessun]]'s "Hell On Earth" tour, Conway was introduced to [[Prodigy (rapper)|Prodigy]] of Mobb Deep, who would become one of his earliest notable collaborators and supporters.<ref name=":0" />

On March 3, 2017, Griselda Records signed a deal with [[Eminem]]'s [[Shady Records]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://shadyrecords.com/2017/03/03/shady-x-griselda-ink-deal-for-westside-gunn-and-conway/|title=Shady X Griselda Ink Deal For Westside Gunn and Conway|website=Shady Records|access-date=June 1, 2019}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.npr.org/transcripts/789690521|title = Griselda Set Out to be Your Favorite Rapper's Favorite Rappers. It's Paying off| website=[[NPR]] }}</ref> On October 10, 2017, Conway contributed an a cappella rap verse to the 2017 [[BET Hip Hop Awards]] Shady cypher.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.xxlmag.com/video/2017/10/conway-boogie-shady-2017-bet-hip-hop-awards-cypher/|title=Conway, Boogie and More Spit in Shady 2017 BET Hip Hop Awards Cypher – XXL|last=Mojica|first=Nick|website=XXL Mag|date=October 11, 2017 |access-date=June 1, 2019}}</ref> The same year, [[Complex (magazine)|Complex]] included Westside Gunn and Conway in their list of "15 Best Unofficial Rap Duos in the Game",<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.complex.com/music/2017/07/when-these-rappers-pair-up-something-special-happens/|title=The 15 Best Unofficial Rap Duos in the Game Right Now|website=Complex|access-date=June 1, 2019}}</ref> as well as two of the "17 Artists to Watch in 2017".<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.complex.com/music/2017/01/artists-to-watch-in-2017|title=17 Artists to Watch in 2017|website=Complex|access-date=June 1, 2019}}</ref>

Following Griselda's deal with Shady Records, Conway's first official release was ''G.O.A.T.'' on December 21, 2017, which included production from Griselda's in-house producer Daringer and [[The Alchemist (musician)|The Alchemist]], and featured [[Royce Da 5'9"]], [[Raekwon]], [[Prodigy (rapper)|Prodigy]], [[Styles P]] and [[Lloyd Banks]].<ref>{{Cite web |title=Conway the Machine: G.O.A.T. |url=https://pitchfork.com/reviews/albums/conway-goat/ |access-date=2022-11-26 |website=Pitchfork |language=en-US}}</ref> Earlier in 2017, he released a collaborative mixtape with [[DJ Green Lantern]] titled ''More Steroids''.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Melis |first=Matt |date=2017-10-30 |title=Album Review: Conway - More Steroids |url=https://consequence.net/2017/10/album-review-conway-more-steroids/ |access-date=2022-11-26 |website=Consequence |language=en-US}}</ref>

===2019–present: Studio albums===
On May 16, 2019, Conway, Benny The Butcher and Westside Gunn released their first single with [[DJ Premier]] titled "Headlines".<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://djbooth.net/features/2019-05-17-dj-premier-griselda-gang-headlines-interviews|title=DJ Premier & Griselda Interview: Making "Headlines"|last=Chesman|first=Donna-Claire|website=DJBooth|date=May 17, 2019 |access-date=2019-06-01}}</ref>

Conway released "[[Bang (Conway The Machine single)|Bang]]", at the time expected to be the first single from his album ''God Don't Make Mistakes'', alongside [[Eminem]] on July 19, 2019.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.stereogum.com/2051790/conway-the-machine-bang-feat-eminem/music/|title=Conway The Machine – "Bang" (Feat. Eminem)|date=July 19, 2019|website=Stereogum|access-date=July 30, 2019}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.spin.com/2019/07/conway-the-machine-eminem-bang/|title=Listen to Eminem's Guest Verse on Conway the Machine's "Bang"|date=July 19, 2019|website=Spin|access-date=July 30, 2019}}</ref> ''God Don't Make Mistakes'' had been intended to be Conway's debut studio album, and Westside Gunn had stated it was "90 percent complete" in 2018;<ref>{{Cite web|first=Riley|last=Wallace|title=Westside Gunn Takes One Last Independent Swing on 'Supreme Blientele' Before Heading to the Big Leagues|url=https://exclaim.ca/music/article/westside_gunn_takes_one_last_independent_swing_on_supreme_blientele_before_heading_to_the_big_leagues|date=2018-06-26|access-date=2022-10-10}}</ref> however, Conway's first studio album would instead be the project ''[[From King to a God]]'', which he released on September 11, 2020.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Critic Reviews for From King to a God - Metacritic |url=https://www.metacritic.com/music/from-king-to-a-god/conway-the-machine/critic-reviews |url-status=live|access-date=2022-10-10|website=[[Metacritic]]|language=en}}</ref> During this period, Conway continued releasing mixtapes and collaborative albums as well.

''[[God Don't Make Mistakes]]'' was ultimately released on February 25, 2022, as Conway's second studio album.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.metacritic.com/music/god-dont-make-mistakes/conway-the-machine|title=God Don't Make Mistakes by Conway the Machine|website=[[Metacritic]]|access-date=2022-10-10}}</ref>
