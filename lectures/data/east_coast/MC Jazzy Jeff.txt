'''Jeffrey Miree''' (born July 7, 1962), also known as '''MC Jazzy Jeff''', is an American rapper and member of the [[Funky 4 + 1]] who was active from 1979 to 2002.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.discogs.com/artist/840896-Jeff-Miree|title=Jeff Miree|website=Discogs.com|accessdate=March 6, 2020}}</ref>

He is sometimes referred to as Original M.C. Jazzy Jeff, in order to indicate that he preceded<ref name="LarkinDM">{{cite book|title=[[Encyclopedia of Popular Music|The Virgin Encyclopedia of Dance Music]]|editor=[[Colin Larkin (writer)|Colin Larkin]]|publisher=[[Virgin Books]]|date=1998|edition=First|isbn=0-7535-0252-6|page=170}}</ref> [[DJ Jazzy Jeff]] from [[Jazzy Jeff and the Fresh Prince]]. Miree began his career in 1976 with hip hop group Funky 4 + 1.<ref name="LarkinDM" /> They were the first hip hop group to perform on national television (''[[Saturday Night Live]]'') performing their hit "That's the Joint" in 1981. After splitting up in 1983, MC Jazzy Jeff signed a solo deal with [[Jive Records]] and recorded the album ''On Fire'' (1985).<ref name="LarkinDM" /> The track "King Heroin" became a moderate success.<ref name="LarkinDM" /> His second album, which was completed in 1987, was never released by the label. That same year Jive Records signed [[DJ Jazzy Jeff|Jeffrey Townes]] and [[Will Smith]], better known then as [[DJ Jazzy Jeff & the Fresh Prince]].<ref name="LarkinDM" /> A year later, ''I'm the DJ, He's the Rapper'' – DJ Jazzy Jeff & the Fresh Prince's debut album – was on sale.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://apnews.com/1fa8816bcfedf69152251821c28ed2f1|title=He's The DJ – I'm The Guy With the Same Name|website=Apnews.com|accessdate=March 6, 2020}}</ref>

In 1994, MC Jazzy Jeff sued Jive Records and he won a lawsuit over the rights over the name Jazzy Jeff.{{citation needed|date=March 2020}}
