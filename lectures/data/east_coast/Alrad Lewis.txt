'''Alrad Lewis''' (born May 10, 1977), also known as '''Boola''', is an American hip-hop producer, composer and artist. Growing up in [[Hempstead, Long Island]], Boola started his own rap group with kids in the neighborhood.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.breakthruradio.com/#/post/?dj=djmsdrama&post=2150&blog=40&autoplay=1 |title=Breakthru Radio |access-date=December 1, 2012 |archive-date=December 13, 2012 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20121213154139/http://www.breakthruradio.com/#/post/?dj=djmsdrama&post=2150&blog=40&autoplay=1 |url-status=dead }}</ref> After taking on production responsibilities for the group, Boola decided to concentrate on the production aspect of the music business and soon began working with artists signed to [[Warner Records|Warner]] and [[MCA Records|MCA]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.rapindustry.com/production-34.htm |title=Hip Hop Production Block &#124; Rap Producers – Beats |publisher=Rapindustry.Com |date= |access-date=November 1, 2012}}</ref>

== Music career ==
In 2002, after producing for [[Warner Records|Warner]] and [[MCA Records]], Boola signed a deal with [[Roc-A-Fella]] records.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=s4FnNJb6Wfc |title=Roc A Fella Producer Boola Talks Jayz And Dame Before Break Up |publisher=YouTube |date=April 7, 2011 |accessdate=November 1, 2012}}</ref> For the next seven years, Boola produced for and worked with artists such as [[Beanie Sigel]], [[Cam'ron]], [[Kanye West]], [[Nicole Wray]], [[Ne-Yo]], [[Nas]] and [[Jaheim]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Don't Call It A Comeback: Boola |publisher=Blogs.bet.com|url=http://blogs.bet.com/music/sound-off/tag/boola/|accessdate=November 1, 2012}}</ref> After the Roc-A-Fella split, Boola continued to produce expanding into TV and film placements including VH1's Basketball Wives and MTV's Making With The Band.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://vimeo.com/12766818|title = Mitsue/Basketball Wives}}</ref><ref>{{Cite the "Produced By Boola" mixtape series which featured original material from Nas, Ne-Yo, Bun B and others.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.vibe.com/|title=VIBE.com|website=VIBE.com|accessdate=June 7, appeared on BET's ''[[106 & Park]]'',<ref>{{cite web|title=Boola live on BET |url=http://thatsenuff.com/index.php/2012/10/boola-live-on-bet/|publisher=Thatsenuff.com|accessdate=November 1,
