'''Justin Harrell''' (born December 29, 1984), better known by his stage name '''38 Spesh''', is an American rapper and producer.

==Biography==

===Early life and career beginnings===
38 Spesh was born on December 29, 1984 in Rochester, New York and began rapping in the early 2000s. He began making appearances on [[Sirius XM]] show Invasion Radio hosted by fellow Rochester native DJ Green Lantern in 2008 and was featured in [[XXL Magazine]] for his music in 2009.<ref name="XXL.com">{{cite web|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/rap-music/reviews/2009/05/dj-green-lantern-presents-38-special-out-on-bail/|title=DJ GREEN LANTERN PRESENTS 38 SPECIAL: OUT ON BAIL|publisher=XXL Staff|access-date=July 14, 2018}}</ref>

Shortly after his initial breakthrough, 38 Spesh spent two years incarcerated and was paroled to [[Atlanta, Georgia]] where he began to pursue a full-time career in music.

Following his release, Spesh began releasing several notable mixtapes and albums with other artists affiliated with his Trust Comes First Music group. Spesh's musical style is highlighted by one of the most unique rapping voices in the game and his strong punchlines. Notable rappers who have worked with 38 Spesh include [[Styles P]], [[Jadakiss]], [[Fred the Godson]], [[AZ (rapper)|AZ]], [[N.O.R.E.|Noreaga]] and [[Kool G Rap]]. He has worked with several high-profile hip hop record producers among the likes of [[DJ Premier]], [[Pete Rock]] and [[The Alchemist (musician)|The Alchemist]].<ref>[https://hiphopdx.com/news/id.47074/title.kool-g-rap-38-spesh-share-son-of-g-rap-cover-art-tracklist Kool G Rap & 38 Spesh Deliver "Son Of G Rap" Album] HipHop</ref>

On September 8th, 2023, 38 Spesh released an album "Speshal Machinery" in collaboration with [[Conway the Machine|Conway The Machine.]]<ref>{{Cite web |last=Zamora |first=José |date=2023-09-13 |title=Speshal Machinery: A Duo to Watch Out For |url=https://allrapnews.com/album-review/speshal-machinery-a-duo-to-watch-out-for/ |access-date=2023-09-15 |website=All Rap News |language=en-US}}</ref>
