'''Stephen Goss''' (born March 15, 1992), known professionally as '''Cousin Stizz''', is an American rapper and songwriter. He is from [[Dorchester, Massachusetts]] and signed to [[RCA Records]].

==Early life==
Stephen Goss was born on March 15, 1992, in [[Boston]], Massachusetts. He grew up in the [[Boston]], Massachusetts neighborhood of [[Dorchester, Boston|Dorchester]]. A childhood friend gave him the nickname "Cousin Stizz" when he was 12. After a close friend was shot to death,<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.vice.com/en/article/rgpj3w/cousin-stizz-interview-profile|title=Cousin Stizz Is the Hypnotic Rapper Giving Boston a Shout Out|website=Vice.com|accessdate=January 19, 2021}}</ref> he began to behave recklessly. He attended [[Reading Memorial High School]], graduating in 2010.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_BJavgcR1N8 |title=Cousin Stizz Talks Music, Racism in Boston & Getting Nods from Drake - YouTube |website=[[YouTube]] |access-date=May 1, 2018 |archive-date=June 28, 2018 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180628104127/https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_BJavgcR1N8 |url-status=dead }}</ref>

==Career==
In his late teens, he began freestyling with friends in a local cypher series and released music as part of the group Pilot Nation later that year. A boost to his drive to turn rapping into a full-time career came in 2013 with the early success of friend and fellow Boston rapper [[Michael Christmas]]. In 2014 after months of recording, Stizz released his first official single as a solo artist, "Shoutout."<ref>Tara Mahadevan, [https://noisey.vice.com/en_us/article/rgpj3w/cousin-stizz-interview-profile, "Cousin Stizz Is the Hypnotic Rapper Giving Boston a Shout Out"], Noisey, August 21, 2015, retrieved February 26, 2017</ref><ref name="auto">Nina Corcoran, [https://digboston.com/cousin-stizz-how-a-dorchester-rapper-built-boston-back-up/, "Cousin Stizz: How a Dorchester Rapper Built Boston Back Up"], DigBoston, July 13, 2017, retrieved February 26, 2017</ref>

His debut mixtape as a solo artist, ''Suffolk County'', released June 1, 2015, received over 12 million listens on [[SoundCloud]]. Wide notice also came with the release on the internet of a video of [[Drake (musician)|Drake]] playing Stizz's single "Shoutout" at the star's birthday party.<ref>Martín Caballero, [https://www.bostonglobe.com/arts/music/2015/09/04/dorchester-cousin-stizz-takes-spotlight-quietly/qM2VWaZpKfMbKvGpifHLNO/story.html, "Dorchester's Cousin Stizz takes the spotlight, quietly"], ''Boston Globe'', September 4, 2015, retrieved February 26, 2017</ref><ref>Alex Frandsen, [https://www.bostonglobe.com/arts/music/2017/05/18/can-cousin-stizz-put-his-hometown-hip-hop-map/ZbV8ycY7zwHwm8lH9pX48K/story.html, "Can Cousin Stizz put his hometown on the hip-hop map?"], Boston Globe, May 19, 2017, retrieved February 26, 2017</ref> His next mixtape, ''MONDA'', released in the summer of 2016, was named one of [[DigBoston]]'s Best Local Albums of 2016.<ref name="auto"/>

He signed a record deal with [[RCA Records]] in late 2016, and his third release and first on RCA, ''One Night Only'', came out on July 12, 2017. It includes features from [[Offset (rapper)|Offset]] (of [[Migos]]), [[G-Eazy]], Big Leano, and [[Buddy (rapper)|Buddy]], and production credits from Tee-WaTT, Tedd Boyd, [[Vinylz]], FrancisGotHeat, [[Smash David]], [[WondaGurl]], and Dave Sava6e.<ref>Lindsey India, [http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2017/07/cousin-stizz-one-night-only-project/, "Listen to Cousin Stizz's New Project 'One Night Only'"], XXL, July 12, 2017, retrieved February 26, 2017</ref> Three weeks after the album's release, the single "Headlock" (feat. [[Offset (rapper)|Offset]]) had been streamed over 11.3&nbsp;million times on [[Spotify]], reaching No. 12 on Billboard's Next Big Sound chart, while viewers on YouTube had watched Stizz's videos (combined) over 8 million times. Author [[Shea Serrano]] of ''[[The Ringer (website)|The Ringer]]'' is an active fan of the rapper and also his No. 1 fan in the entire world, calling his music inspiring.<ref>Shea Serrano, [https://www.theringer.com/2017/11/28/16707332/cousin-stizz-one-night-only-inspirational-rap, "The Inspirational Rap of Cousin Stizz'], ''The Ringer'', November 28, 2017, retrieved April 8, 2018</ref>

In December 2018, he was crowned "Artist of the Year" at the [[Boston Music Awards]]. With his victory, Stizz became the first hip-hop musician to receive the awards ceremony's highest honor.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.bostonglobe.com/arts/2018/12/12/cousin-stizz-named-artist-year-boston-music-awards/gsEvISAUhu9gyz0G2sUMrJ/story.html|title=Cousin Stizz named artist of the year at Boston Music Awards – The Boston Globe|last=Feldberg|first=Isaac|website=BostonGlobe.com|access-date=December 28, 2018}}</ref>
