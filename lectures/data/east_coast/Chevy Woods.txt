'''Kevin Lee''' "'''Chevy'''" '''Woods''' (born May 13, 1981) is an American rapper and songwriter from [[Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania]]. Throughout his career he has been affiliated with rapper [[Wiz Khalifa]] and his respective [[Taylor Gang Records|Taylor Gang record label]], also serving as Khalifa's hypeman on tour. His first major appearance was on Wiz Khalifa's song "[[Taylor Gang (song)|Taylor Gang]]". Woods has been on more than 20 tours and has released several mixtapes including ''The Cookout'' with Khalifa and the ''Gangland'' series.<ref name=paper>{{cite web| url=http://www.pghcitypaper.com/pittsburgh/after-building-a-career-on-touring-and-mixtapes-taylor-gang-rapper-chevy-woods-releases-his-first-official-ep/Content?oid=1845261| author = Trevor Leard| title= After building a career on touring and mixtapes, Taylor Gang rapper Chevy Woods releases his first official EP| date= August 5, 2015| publisher=Pittsburgh City Paper| access-date=April 21, 2016}}</ref>

== Career ==

=== 2003–2010: Early career, Taylor Gang formation and mixtapes ===
Woods started rapping as a child with friends using instrumentals for background music.<ref name="xxlmag1">{{cite web|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/rap-music/come-up/2011/04/act-like-u-know-chevy-woods/ |title=Chevy Woods &ndash; The Come Up - XXL |publisher=Xxlmag.com |date=2011-04-01 |access-date=2013-09-25}}</ref> After graduating high school, he attended [[Robert Morris University]] as an athlete and grew his passion for rapping.<ref name="autogenerated1">{{cite web|url=http://www.mtv.com/news/articles/1668103/wiz-khalifa-chevy-woods-red-cup-music.jhtml |title=Wiz Khalifa Shows Chevy Woods 'A Different Way' - Music, Celebrity, Artist News |publisher=MTV.com |date=2011-07-28 |access-date=2013-09-25}}</ref><ref name=post>{{cite web|url = http://www.post-gazette.com/ae/celebrities/2015/08/06/Chevy-Woods-Wiz-khalifa-hunnid-project-hazelwood/stories/201508040130| author = Jewell Porter| title= Rapper Chevy Woods drops 'The 48 Hunnid Project'|date= August 6, 2015| publisher=The Pittsburgh Post-Gazette| access-date= April 21, 2016}}</ref> Woods became serious about rap when he met another musician who created and distributed his own mixtapes. He wanted to pursue music and began writing and rapping under the name Kev Tha Hustla.<ref name="xxlmag1"/>

In 2004, he met fellow [[Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania]] rapper [[Wiz Khalifa]], through Pennsylvania producers [[I.D. Labs]], who collaborated with Khalifa.<ref name="autogenerated1"/><ref>{{cite web |url=http://blogordiepgh.blogspot.com/2009/04/shine-monthly-kev-tha-hustla-heavy.html |title=Shine Monthly: Kev Tha Hustla (Heavy Hustle Records) |publisher=Blogordiepgh.blogspot.com |date=2009-04-16 |access-date=2013-09-25}}</ref> Woods and Khalifa formed a partnership, which led to the formation of the group [[Taylor Gang Records|Taylor Gang]] in 2008.<ref name="autogenerated1"/><ref name = post/> Woods released two mixtapes, ''The Corner's Correspondent'' in 2008 and ''Animal'' in 2009.<ref name="xxlmag1"/> His first two mixtapes featured collaborations with Wiz Khalifa, [[Rostrum Records|Boaz]] and members of Heavy Hustle Records. [[Record production|Production]] was primarily handled by I.D. Labs and [[Sledgren]].<ref name="datpiff1">{{cite web |url=http://www.datpiff.com/Kev-Tha-Hustla-Tha-Corners-Correspondent-mixtape.17580.html |title=Kev Tha Hustla - Tha Corner's Correspondent Hosted by DJ Shef // Free Mixtape @ |publisher=Datpiff.com |date=2008-07-23 |access-date=2013-09-25 |archive-date=2013-10-02 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131002014825/http://www.datpiff.com/Kev-Tha-Hustla-Tha-Corners-Correspondent-mixtape.17580.html |url-status=dead }}</ref> On August 3, 2010, Woods was featured on the track “Paper Route” from Mac Miller's mixtape ''[[K.I.D.S.]]'' In September 2010, Woods released his third official mixtape ''Pilot Shit''.<ref>{{cite web |author=Crys.C |url=http://defpenradio.com/mixtape-chevy-woods-pilot-sht |title=MixTape: Chevy Woods - Pilot Sh*t |publisher=Def Pen Radio |date=2010-09-13 |access-date=2013-09-25 |archive-date=2010-11-25 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20101125005424/http://defpenradio.com/mixtape-chevy-woods-pilot-sht/ |url-status=dead }}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.hotnewhiphop.com/chevy-woods-pilot-shit-hosted-by-dj-burn-one-mixtape.3438.html |title=Chevy Woods - Pilot Shit (Hosted By DJ Burn One) |date=14 September 2010 |publisher=Hotnewhiphop.com |access-date=2013-09-25}}</ref>

=== 2011: ''Red Cup Music'' and ''The Cookout'' ===
In February 2011, Wiz Khalifa released his ninth mixtape, ''[[Cabin Fever (mixtape)|Cabin Fever]]'', which featured Woods on three tracks, including "[[Taylor Gang (song)|Taylor Gang]]", "Middle of You" and "Homicide".<ref>{{cite web|url=http://rapradar.com/2011/02/17/new-mixtape-wiz-khalifa-cabin-fever/ |title=New Mixtape: Wiz Khalifa Cabin Fever |publisher=Rap Radar |date=2011-02-17 |access-date=2013-09-25}}</ref>  Woods released his fourth mixtape ''Red Cup Music'', which featured guest appearances by [[Juicy J]], [[French Montana]], and production by ID Labs and [[Mac Miller]] in March 2011.<ref>{{cite web |last=Stark |first=Tony |url=http://defpenradio.com/chevy-woods-red-cup-music-mixtape |title=Chevy Woods - Red Cup Music |publisher=Def Pen Radio |date=2011-03-17 |access-date=2013-09-25 |archive-date=2011-07-02 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110702061902/http://defpenradio.com/chevy-woods-red-cup-music-mixtape/ |url-status=dead }}</ref> Woods told [[MTV]], the ''Red Cup Music'' was "really when I found a style for myself, some laid-back, cool music. Drink a little bit, smoke a little bit."<ref name="autogenerated1"/> Khalifa released the music video for the [[Lex Luger (record producer)|Lex Luger]]-produced track "Taylor Gang" in April 2011, which became an [[iTunes]] bonus track on Khalifa's debut studio album ''[[Rolling Papers (Wiz Khalifa album)|Rolling Papers]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.complex.com/music/2011/04/video-wiz-khalifa-taylor-gang |title=Video: Wiz Khalifa "Taylor Gang" |publisher=Complex |date=2011-04-07 |access-date=2013-09-25}}</ref> The song peaked at number 22 on the ''Billboard'' [[Bubbling Under Hot 100 Singles]] chart.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/artist/431494/Wiz%2BKhalifa/chart?page=1&f=344 |title=Wiz Khalifa - Chart history |publisher=Billboard |access-date=2013-09-25}}</ref> In April 2013, Khalifa went on tour in support of ''Rolling Papers'' with Woods and [[Big Sean]].<ref name="autogenerated1"/><ref>{{cite web|last=Kujundzic |first=Petar |url=http://hypetrak.com/2011/04/wiz-khalifa-big-sean-announce-tour/ |title=Wiz Khalifa & Big Sean Announce Tour |publisher=Hypetrak |access-date=2013-09-25}}</ref> This tour introduced Woods to Khalifa's mainstream audience.<ref name="hiphopdx1">{{cite web|last=Ortiz |first=Edwin |url=http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/dxnext/id.207/title.dxnext-chevy-woods |title=DXnext: Chevy Woods &#124; Check Out New Hip Hop Artists & Upcoming Rappers |publisher=HipHop DX |date=2011-06-16 |access-date=2013-09-25}}</ref> In August 2011, Woods released a mixtape, titled ''The Cookout'', which featured Wiz Khalifa.<ref name="autogenerated1"/><ref name="hiphopdx1"/>

After Khalifa officially founded [[Taylor Gang Records]], Woods was immediately added to the roster. ''The Cookout'' mixtape, produced by ID Labs, Sledgren, [[Cardo (record producer)|Cardo]], [[Key Wane]] and Big Jerm, was released in September 2011 and featured Wiz Khalifa on eight of the eleven tracks.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.complex.com/music/2011/09/mixtape-chevy-woods-f-wiz-khalifa-the-cookout |title=Mixtape: Chevy Woods f/ Wiz Khalifa "The Cookout" |publisher=Complex |date=2011-09-24 |access-date=2013-09-25}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.vibe.com/article/new-mixtape-chevy-woods-cookout-featuring-wiz-khalifa |title=New Mixtape: Chevy Woods 'The Cookout' Featuring Wiz Khalifa |publisher=Vibe |date=2011-09-25 |access-date=2013-09-25}}</ref> The mixtape received positive reviews from ''[[XXL (magazine)|XXL]]'', who praised the production, but mentioned Woods was overshadowed by Khalifa throughout the tape.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/rap-music/reviews/2011/10/chevy-woods-the-cookout/ |title=Chevy Woods, The Cookout - XXL |publisher=Xxlmag.com |date=2011-10-03 |access-date=2013-09-25}}</ref> However, [[MTV]] disagreed calling Woods the mixtape's star and praised his laid back flow.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.mtv.com/news/articles/1671994/wiz-khalifa-chevy-woods-the-cookout.jhtml |title=Wiz Khalifa On Chevy Woods: 'It's His Time' - Music, Celebrity, Artist News |publisher=MTV.com |date=2011-10-04 |access-date=2013-09-25}}</ref>

=== 2012-2015: ''Gangland'' mixtape series and The 48 Hunnid Project ===
[[File:ChevyWoodsDecember2013 2.jpg|thumb|left|150px|Chevy Woods performing on The Smoker's Club Tour in December 2013.]]
In 2012, Woods released a new mixtape, titled ''Gangland'', which consisted of dark material influenced by Woods early life on the streets, and the television show ''[[Gangland (TV series)|Gangland]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.mtv.com/news/articles/1685980/chevy-woods-gangland.jhtml?xrs=share_twitter |title=Chevy Woods Gives 'Voice' To Voiceless On Gangland - Music, Celebrity, Artist News |publisher=MTV.com |date=2012-05-29 |access-date=2013-09-25}}</ref> Woods along with the rest of Taylor Gang Records, [[Lola Monroe]], Juicy J, and Wiz Khalifa appeared on the May 2012 cover of ''[[The Source (magazine)|The Source]]''.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://rapfix.mtv.com/2012/04/25/wiz-khalifa-taylor-gang-cover-the-source/ |title=Wiz Khalifa And Taylor Gang Cover The Source |publisher=Rapfix.mtv.com |date=2012-04-25 |access-date=2013-09-25 |archive-date=2013-09-28 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130928023410/http://rapfix.mtv.com/2012/04/25/wiz-khalifa-taylor-gang-cover-the-source/ |url-status=dead }}</ref> ''Gangland'', produced by Lex Luger, I.D. Labs, Cardo, [[Sonny Digital]], [[Key Wane]], [[Sledgren]] and [[Harry Fraud]], was released in June 2012 and featured Taylor Gang's Wiz Khalifa, Lola Monroe, Juicy J, Tuki Carter, as well as artists [[Soulja Boy]], [[Trae Tha Truth]], and Young Jerz.<ref>{{cite web|author=JP DelaCuesta |url=http://allhiphop.com/2012/06/07/mixtape-chevy-woods-bga-gangland/ |title=MIXTAPE: Chevy Woods [BGA&#93; – "Gangland" |publisher=AllHipHop.com |date=2012-06-07 |access-date=2013-09-25}}</ref> In a ''XXL'' review of ''Gangland'', the magazine stated, "Over the past two years, Taylor Gang's Chevy Woods has gradually built up his name with mixtapes like ''Red Cup Music'' and ''The Cookout''. Progressing with each release, Wiz's budding sidekick has shown he can hang high without resting on the back of his co-pilot. On his latest offering, ''Gang Land'', Chevy flies high and gives the world an in depth look into his life as a Taylor Gang aviator."<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/rap-music/reviews/2012/06/chevy-woods-gang-land/ |title=Chevy Woods, Gang Land - XXL |publisher=Xxlmag.com |date=2012-06-16 |access-date=2013-09-25}}</ref>
Following the release of ''Gangland'', Woods worked on his next mixtape and his debut studio album.<ref name="hotnewhiphopii">{{cite web|url=http://www.hotnewhiphop.com/chevy-woods-talks-gangland-ii-and-coming-up-with-wiz-khalifa-news.6889.html |title=Chevy Woods Talks "Gangland II" & Coming Up With Wiz Khalifa |date=20 August 2013 |publisher=Hotnewhiphop.com |access-date=2013-09-26}}</ref> Woods went on the first Under the Influence of Music [[concert tour]] with Khalifa, [[Mac Miller]], [[Schoolboy Q]] and [[Kendrick Lamar]] in support of ''Gangland'' in July and August 2012, .<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.mtv.com/news/articles/1683313/wiz-khalifa-mac-miller-tour.jhtml |title=Wiz Khalifa, Mac Miller Unite For Joint Tour - Music, Celebrity, Artist News |publisher=MTV.com |date=2012-04-17 |access-date=2013-09-26}}</ref> He toured with the rest of Taylor Gang Records on the 2050 Tour from October through December 2012.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.missinfo.tv/index.php/wiz-khalifa-announces-taylor-gang-the-2050-tour-delays-o-n-i-f-c-album/ |title=Wiz Khalifa Announces Taylor Gang 'The 2050 Tour', Delays 'O.N.I.F.C.' Album |publisher=MissInfo.tv |date=2012-09-05 |access-date=2013-09-26 |archive-date=2013-09-29 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130929041651/http://www.missinfo.tv/index.php/wiz-khalifa-announces-taylor-gang-the-2050-tour-delays-o-n-i-f-c-album/ |url-status=dead }}</ref> During the tour, Woods announced that his next mixtape would be titled ''Gangland 2''.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.freeonsmash.com/news/2012/11/04/chevy-woods-announces-gang-land-2-2/ |title=Chevy Woods Announces "Gang Land 2" &#124; OnSMASH |publisher=Freeonsmash.com |date=2012-11-04 |access-date=2013-09-26 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130927055342/http://www.freeonsmash.com/news/2012/11/04/chevy-woods-announces-gang-land-2-2/ |archive-date=2013-09-27 }}</ref> In 2013, he toured on the second Under the Influence tour with Khalifa, [[ASAP Mob]], [[B.o.B]], [[Joey Badass]], [[Ty Dolla Sign]], Pro Era, Berner, and [[Trinidad James]].<ref>{{cite web|author=Mikey T The Movie Star |url=http://allhiphop.com/2013/09/19/chevy-woods-talks-blacc-hollywood-ty-dolla-signs-and-upcoming-projects-video/ |title=Chevy Woods talks Blacc Hollywood, Ty Dolla Signs and Upcoming Projects [VIDEO |publisher=AllHipHop.com |date=2013-09-19 |access-date=2013-09-26}}</ref>

In August 2013, Woods told ''HotNewHipHop'', his debut album would contain production by Sledgren and C4, the track list for ''Gangland 2'' was finalized, and it would be hosted by [[DJ Drama]].<ref name="hotnewhiphopii"/> Woods released the cover artwork for ''Gangland 2'' in September 2013.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.hotnewhiphop.com/chevy-woods-reveals-cover-art-for-gangland-2-mixtape-news.7359.html |title=Chevy Woods Reveals Cover Art For "Gangland 2" Mixtape |publisher=Hotnewhiphop.com |access-date=2013-09-26}}</ref> He toured North America with Joey Badass, [[Ab-Soul]], [[The Underachievers]], and [[Pro Era]] on the fourth annual The Smoker's Club tour in October and November 2013.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://consequenceofsound.net/2013/08/joey-bada-ab-soul-and-the-underachievers-team-for-the-smokers-club-tour/ |title=Joey Bada$$, Ab-Soul, and The Underachievers team for The Smokers Club tour |publisher=Consequence of Sound |date=2013-08-26 |access-date=2013-09-26}}</ref> Woods released ''Gangland 2'', a mixtape featuring [[guest appearance]]s from Wiz Khalifa, Juicy J, Trinidad James, [[Young Scooter]], [[King Los]] and Compton Menace, via Taylor Gang Records in October 2013.<ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2013/10/chevy-woods-releases-gangland-2-mixtape/ | title=Chevy Woods Releases 'Gangland 2′ Mixtape - XXL | date=24 October 2013 }}</ref> Woods released his first retail single "30 Deep" via Taylor Gang in April 2014 and announced in September 2014 that he would release a retail EP.<ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2014/07/chevy-woods-taught-wiz-khalifa-streets/ | title=Chevy Woods Taught Wiz Khalifa About the Streets - XXL | date=9 July 2014 }}</ref> He made two appearances on Wiz Khalifa's third studio album ''[[Blacc Hollywood]]'', on "Still Down" and spoke on the intro to the album.<ref>{{cite web | url=http://freeonsmash.com/music/wiz-khalifa-blacc-hollywood-album-stream/ | title=Wiz Khalifa – Blacc Hollywood &#91;Album Stream&#93; | date=12 August 2014 }}</ref> Woods toured with rapper [[Kevin Gates]] in July and August 2014.<ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.complex.com/music/2014/07/listen-to-chevy-woods-and-kevin-gates-boat | title=Premiere: Listen to Chevy Woods and Kevin Gates' "Boat" | website=[[Complex Networks]] }}</ref>

In January 2015, Woods released the third installment of his ''Gangland'' series featuring production from Ricky P, Zaytoven, Sonny Digital, Ty Dolla Sign and more.<ref>{{cite web| url=http://www.hotnewhiphop.com/chevy-woods-sticc-to-the-plan-new-video.33417.html| author= Patrick Lyons| title= Chevy Woods "Sticc To the Plan" video| date=April 9, 2015| publisher=Hot New Hip Hop| access-date=April 21, 2016}}</ref> In August 2015, Woods produced and released ''The 48 Hunnid Project'', his first retail release.<ref>{{cite web|url = http://theboombox.com/chevy-woods-the-4800-hunnid-project-interview/| author = Preezy| title= Chevy Woods talks 'The 4800 Hunnid Project'|date= August 31, 2015| publisher=The Boombox| access-date=April 21, 2016}}</ref> The project named for the neighborhood where Woods was raised, took 12 years to create and encompassed his experiences from being a child through adulthood.<ref>{{cite web|url = http://thesource.com/2015/08/06/chevy-woods-preps-the-48-hunnid-project-gives-update-from-the-boys-of-zummer-tour/| author = Pologod| title= Chevy Woods preps 'The 48 Hunnid Project'; Gives update from The Boys of Zummer Tour| date= August 6, 2015| publisher=The Source| access-date=April 21, 2016}}</ref> He also toured with Wiz Khalifa on the Boys of Zummer Tour with [[Fall Out Boy]].<ref>{{cite web|url = http://www.post-gazette.com/ae/music-reviews/2015/07/02/Concert-review-Wiz-Khalifa-Fall-Out-Boy-rock-a-packed-Pavilion-with-Boys-of-Zummer-Tour/stories/201507020221| author= Scott Mervis| title= Concert review: Wiz Khalifa, Fall Out Boy rock a packed Pavilion with Boys of Zummer Tour|date=July 2, 2015| publisher=Pittsburgh Post-Gazette| access-date=April 21, 2016}}</ref>

=== 2016-2017: TGOD Volume 1, Gang Sh*t Only, and Thanks For Everything ===
On February 26, 2016, Chevy woods released a 4 track extended play called And The Story Goes. On October 11, 2016 [[Taylor Gang Records]] released a compilation mixtape titled TGOD Volume 1 with all the members including [[Wiz Khalifa]], [[Berner (rapper)|Berner]], [[Juicy J]], [[Ty Dolla $ign]] among others. On November 11, 2016, Gang Sh*t Only was released with features from Wiz Khalifa, Hardo, Stevie B, FTR Drama and [[Rich The Kid]] with production [[Honorable C.N.O.T.E.]], [[808 Mafia]], [[K.E. on the Track]] among others. On May 13, 2017, Chevy Woods released a 14 track mixtape titled Freemix 1.5 Reloaded. Exactly What They Want was released on September 28, 2017, with features from Wiz Khalifa, Bigga Rankin and [[K Camp]] with production from K.E. on the Track, Yung Lan, [[Stevie B]] among others. Thanks For Everything was released on December 23, 2017, with features from Wiz Khalifa on the tracks All Love and Joint & A Lighter with production from K.E. on the Track, Yung Lan, [[OG Parker]] among others.

===2018-present: ''81'', ''Lewis Park Legend'' and ''New 90's''===
On June 15, 2018, Chevy Woods released the mixtape titled ''81'' with a feature from [[Wiz Khalifa]] on the track Bitch You Lyin and production from [[K.E. on the Track]], [[Tay Keith]], Beat Mechanics among others. On December 19, 2018, Chevy Woods dropped a new mixtape titled ''Lewis Park Legend''. On May 13, 2019, Chevy Woods released a new mixtape titled ''New 90's''. On July 3, 2020, Chevy Woods dropped a debut album titled ''Since Birth''.
