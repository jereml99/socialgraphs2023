'''Frederick Thomas''' (February 22, 1985 – April 23, 2020),<ref name="Times Obit">{{cite news|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2020/04/24/arts/music/fred-the-godson-dead-coronavirus.html|work=New York Times|title=Fred the Godson, Nimble New York Rapper, Dies at 41|first=Jon |last=Caramanica|date=April 24, 2020|accessdate=April 24, 2020}}</ref> known as '''Fred the Godson''', was an American DJ and rapper from [[Bronx|The Bronx]], [[New York (state)|New York]].<ref name="Billboard Obit">{{cite magazine |url= https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/obituary/9364643/fred-the-godson-dies-35-coronavirus-obituary|first=Gil|last=Kaufman|magazine=Billboard|title=Fred the Godson Dies at 35 From Coronavirus|date=April 23, 2020|accessdate=April 24, 2020}}</ref><ref name="NPR Obit">{{cite news |url= https://www.npr.org/sections/coronavirus-live-updates/2020/04/24/843381098/fred-the-godson-new-york-hip-hop-fixture-dead-at-35-from-covid-19-complications |work= National Public Radio|title=Fred The Godson, New York Hip-Hop Fixture, Dead At 35 From COVID-19 Complication|first=Jon |last=Lewis|date=April 24, 2020 |accessdate=April 24, 2020}}</ref> At his death in April 2020, he became one of the most notable New Yorkers to have died of COVID-19 complications early in the pandemic.

==Early life==
Frederick Thomas was born on February 22, 1985, and grew up in [[The Bronx, New York City]]. He gained an early nickname of "Big Bronx".<ref name="Times Obit" />

He started freestyling in the early 2000s.<ref name="Times Obit" />

==Career==
Thomas's music was characterized by his husky voice, humorous [[wordplay]], and creative rhymes.<ref name="Times Obit" /> In the early-2010s, he released two mixtapes; his debut was ''Armageddon'' in 2010, featuring samples of [[The Notorious B.I.G.]], [[Busta Rhymes]] and [[Cam'ron]].<ref name="NPR Obit" /> His second release, ''City of God'', was one of a series for DJ Drama's Gangsta Grillz,<ref name="Times Obit" /> and featuring [[Pusha T]] and [[Raekwon]].<ref name="Billboard Obit" />

In 2011, ''[[XXL (magazine)|XXL]]'' included him in its annual Freshman class of up-and-comers.<ref name="Billboard Obit" />

Between 2011 and 2020, Thomas released music and performed regularly, guest DJ-ing on New York radio station based in Hudson [[Hot 97]],<ref name="NPR Obit" /> and collaborated with artists including Pusha T, [[Jadakiss]], Cam'ron, and Raekwon, as well as releasing the [[answer song]], "Monique's Room".<ref name="Times Obit" /> He released two additional mixtapes in 2020, ''Training Day'' in January with [[Jay Pharoah]], and ''Payback'', released on March 20, 2020, which would be his last release before his death from COVID-19 one month later.<ref name="NPR Obit" />

==Personal life==
Thomas was married to LeeAnn Jemmott; they had two daughters.<ref name="Times Obit" /><ref name="Bronx12">{{cite news|url=http://bronx.news12.com/story/41988922/rapper-fred-the-godson-diagnosed-with-covid19-fighting-for-his-life|title=Rapper Fred The Godson diagnosed with COVID-19, fighting for his life|date=April 8, 2020|work=Bronx News 12|accessdate=April 24, 2020}}</ref>

Thomas was reported to be suffering from acute asthma and kidney problems, after contracting [[COVID-19]] during the [[COVID-19 pandemic in New York (state)]], and by April 6, 2020, his fever had abated in hospital, according to his publicist and his [[Instagram]] account.<ref name="Billboard Obit" /><ref name="Source">{{cite news|last=White|first=Roman|url=https://thesource.com/2020/04/22/source-fred-the-godsons-kidneys-not-working-but-fever-improvement-is-big/|title=Source: Fred The Godson's Kidneys 'Not Working' But Fever Improvement is 'Big'|date=April 22, 2020|work=Source (magazine)|accessdate=April 24, 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.instagram.com/p/B-puaempK0f/ |archive-url=https://ghostarchive.org/iarchive/instagram/fredthegodsonmusic/2281558815946353951 |archive-date=December 25, 2021 |url-access=registration|title=Fred the Godson's Instagram|author=Fred the Godson|date=April 6, 2020|work=Instagram|accessdate=April 24, 2020}}{{cbignore}}</ref> His wife told media on April 9 that she was concerned he was going to die,<ref name="Bronx12" /> but the next day, said he was "going to make it", and was being weaned from a [[ventilator]].<ref name="Source" /><ref name="XXL Update">{{cite news|last=Woods|first=Aleia|url=https://www.xxlmag.com/news/2020/04/fred-the-godson-wife-coronavirus-diagnosis-update/|title=Fred the Godson's Wife Says He's Fighting for His Life After Coronavirus Diagnosis|date=April 9, 2020|work=XXL (magazine)|accessdate=April 24, 2020}}</ref>

Thomas died at [[Montefiore Medical Center]] on April 23, 2020, due to complications of COVID-19.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Fred The Godson, New York Hip-Hop Fixture, Dead At 35 From COVID-19 Complications|url=https://www.npr.org/sections/coronavirus-live-updates/2020/04/24/843381098/fred-the-godson-new-york-hip-hop-fixture-dead-at-35-from-covid-19-complications|access-date=2021-02-06|website=NPR.org|date=April 24, 2020 |language=en|last1=Lewis |first1=Jon }}</ref> He was 35.<ref name="Times Obit" /><ref name="Billboard Obit" /><ref name="NPR Obit" />

=== Legacy ===
On February 22, 2021, what would've been his 36th birthday, [[Bronx Borough President]] [[Rubén Díaz Jr.]] honored Fred by giving him his own street co-name on the block of his childhood home that he grew up in.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Thomas|first=Datwon|date=2021-02-22|title=Fat Joe, Jim Jones and Bronx Community Celebrate Fred The Godson's Street Naming On His Birthday|url=https://www.vibe.com/news/entertainment/fat-joe-jim-jones-celebrate-fred-the-godson-687174/|url-status=live|access-date=2021-02-22|website=VIBE.com|language=en-US}}</ref> Special appearances were made by [[Sway Calloway]], [[Fat Joe]], [[Jim Jones (rapper)|Jim Jones]], [[Jaquae]], Mysonne and [[Justina Valentine]].
