'''Barrington DeVaughn Hendricks''' (born October 22, 1989),<ref name=":2">{{cite web|url=http://www.papermag.com/jpegmafia-1957629741.html|title=Radical Contrarian Rapper JPEGMafia On Gun Ownership, Trump And Flipping Rhetoric On Its Head|last=Gillespie|first=Blake|date=August 3, 2016|work=[[Paper (magazine)|Paper]]|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200105132347/http://www.papermag.com/jpegmafia-1957629741.html|archive-date=January 5, 2020|access-date=April 21, 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.complex.com/pigeons-and-planes/2018/03/who-is-jpegmafia|title=Who Is JPEGMAFIA?|last=Corrigan|first=Graham|date=March 29, 2018|work=[[Complex (magazine)|Pigeons & Planes]]|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180421234330/https://pigeonsandplanes.com/in-depth/2018/03/who-is-jpegmafia|archive-date=April 21, 2018|access-date=April 21, 2018}}</ref> known professionally as '''JPEGMafia''' (stylized in [[all caps]]), is an American rapper, singer, and record producer born in [[New York City]] and based in [[Baltimore, Maryland]]. His 2018 album ''[[Veteran (JPEGMafia album)|Veteran]]'', released through [[Deathbomb Arc]], received widespread critical acclaim and was featured on many year-end lists. It was followed by 2019's ''[[All My Heroes Are Cornballs]]'' and 2021's ''[[LP!]]'', released to further critical acclaim. His most recent release, a collaborative studio album with [[Danny Brown]] titled ''[[Scaring the Hoes]]'', was released in 2023.

==Career==
Hendricks developed an interest in music production at the age of 15, and he began producing after he learned how to [[Sampling (music)|sample]].<ref name="tg1">{{cite web|url=https://www.theguardian.com/music/2019/oct/04/jpegmafia-i-want-to-create-a-space-for-invisible-black-people|title=Radical rapper Jpegmafia: 'Black People have things to be mad about'|last=Pearce|first=Sheldon|date=October 4, 2019|work=The Guardian|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200301212518/https://www.theguardian.com/music/2019/oct/04/jpegmafia-i-want-to-create-a-space-for-invisible-black-people|archive-date=March 1, 2020|access-date=November 17, 2019}}</ref> Describing his production, Hendricks says "When I was first making beats, no one liked the beats. To this day I give people beats and they're just confused." He says "I started rapping because no one liked my beats." Noting that he started producing before he started rapping, Hendricks said "I'm a producer first kind of, and then a rapper second but I take both of them seriously."<ref>{{cite AV media |date=March 1, 2020 |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=jsv8XwyA6cI |title=JPEGMAFIA {{!}} Q&A {{!}} Cambridge Union (2/2) |publisher=[[Cambridge Union]] |via=[[YouTube]] |access-date=February 14, 2023 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20220829222402/https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=jsv8XwyA6cI |archive-date=August 29, 2022 |url-status=live}}</ref>

During his military stay in Japan, he was producing and writing music under the name Devon Hendryx.<ref name="tg1"/> In 2015, he moved to [[Baltimore]], where he began to make music under his moniker JPEGMafia,<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.papermag.com/jpegmafia-all-my-heroes-are-cornballs-2640309333.html|title=JPEGMAFIA Is Doing It All Wrong|author=Michael|date=September 13, 2019|work=[[Paper (magazine)|Paper]]|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190915205015/https://www.papermag.com/jpegmafia-all-my-heroes-are-cornballs-2640309333.html|archive-date=September 15, 2019|access-date=September 24, 2019}}</ref>  under which he released his [[mixtape]] ''Communist Slow Jams'' in April 2015. Only a month later, he released his mixtape ''Darkskin Manson'' which was inspired by the [[2015 Baltimore protests|Freddie Gray protests]] in Baltimore that were happening right as he had moved there.<ref name="tl"/>
Following a number of mixtapes, Hendricks released his debut [[studio album]], ''[[Black Ben Carson]]'', in February 2016 via [[Deathbomb Arc]] featuring a much harsher and more distorted sound than his other projects.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.vice.com/en_us/article/6wqy8w/jpegmafia-black-ben-carson-premiere|title=JPEGMAFIA's New Album 'Black Ben Carson' Is Going to Heal, Inspire, and Revive America|last=Kramer|first=Kyle|date=February 15, 2016|website=Vice|language=en-US|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190610015832/https://www.vice.com/en_us/article/6wqy8w/jpegmafia-black-ben-carson-premiere|archive-date=June 10, 2019|access-date=June 8, 2019}}</ref> Four months later, he released a collaborative [[Extended play|EP]] with fellow Baltimore-based rapper Freaky titled ''The 2nd Amendment''.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.tinymixtapes.com/music-review/jpegmafia-x-freaky-2nd-amendment|title=JPEGMAFIA x Freaky â€“ The 2nd Amendment|website=[[Tiny Mix Tapes]]|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190608023241/https://www.tinymixtapes.com/music-review/jpegmafia-x-freaky-2nd-amendment|archive-date=June 8, 2019|access-date=June 7, 2019}}</ref>
After less than 3 years living there, Hendricks moved from Baltimore to Los Angeles for his next studio album.<ref name="fader2018-a">{{cite web|url=https://www.thefader.com/2018/04/18/jpegmafia-veteran-interview|title=JPEGMAFIA is the out-of-pocket rap rebel the world needs right now|last1=Younger|first1=Briana|website=The Fader|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190910095130/https://www.thefader.com/2018/04/18/jpegmafia-veteran-interview|archive-date=September 10, 2019|access-date=April 18, 2018}}</ref> In January 2018, he released his second studio album [[Veteran (JPEGMafia album)|''Veteran'']].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.thefader.com/2018/11/12/jpegmafia-veterans-day-merch|title=JPEGMAFIA dropped dog tags and PS2-themed merch for Veterans Day|last=Renshaw|first=David|date=November 12, 2018|website=[[The Fader]]|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190715154520/https://www.thefader.com/2018/11/12/jpegmafia-veterans-day-merch|archive-date=July 15, 2019|access-date=June 7, 2019}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.jpegmafia.net/albums|title=JPEGMAFIA â€“ Albums|website=jpegmafia.net|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200307162537/http://www.jpegmafia.net/albums|archive-date=March 7, 2020|access-date=June 7, 2019}}</ref> In an article on Bandcamp,<ref name="bndarticle">{{cite web|url=https://daily.bandcamp.com/2018/01/31/jpegmafia-feature/|title=On "Veteran," Rapper JPEGMAFIA Revels in Creative Freedom|last1=Gillespie|first1=Blake|website=Bandcamp Daily|date=January 31, 2018|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190526171954/https://daily.bandcamp.com/2018/01/31/jpegmafia-feature/|archive-date=May 26, 2019|access-date=January 31, 2018}}</ref> he said "I wanted to show I'm not just a one-trick pony. I always do weird shit. I usually just keep it to myself. This time, I just let the filter go." At the time of its release, ''Veteran'' was considered JPEGMAFIA's most experimental album to date, receiving widespread critical acclaim.<ref name="Breiham"/><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.orlandoweekly.com/orlando/rapper-jpegmafia-laughs-while-the-world-burns/Content?oid=10778234|title=Rapper JPEGMAFIA laughs while the world burns|last=Moyer|first=Matthew|date=February 14, 2018|website=[[Orlando Weekly]]|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180410030134/https://www.orlandoweekly.com/orlando/rapper-jpegmafia-laughs-while-the-world-burns/Content?oid=10778234|archive-date=April 10, 2018|access-date=June 7, 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://pitchfork.com/reviews/albums/jpegmafia-veteran/|title=JPEGMAFIA: Veteran Album Review|last=Thompson|first=Paul A.|date=January 31, 2018|website=Pitchfork|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180131080014/https://pitchfork.com/reviews/albums/jpegmafia-veteran/|archive-date=January 31, 2018|access-date=March 13, 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.thefader.com/2018/02/22/jpegmafia-rules-and-heres-proof|title=JPEGMAFIA rules, and here's proof|last=Darville|first=Jordan|date=February 22, 2018|website=The Fader|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180226041109/http://www.thefader.com/2018/02/22/jpegmafia-rules-and-heres-proof|archive-date=February 26, 2018|access-date=March 13, 2018}}</ref>
