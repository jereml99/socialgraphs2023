'''Greg Hogan''' (born May 15, 1985), better known by his stage name '''Frenchie''', is an American rapper. He was in [[Gucci Mane]]'s [[1017 Records|1017 Brick Squad]] and [[1017 Records|So Icey Boyz]] and [[Waka Flocka Flame]]'s [[1017 Records|Brick Squad Monopoly]]. Frenchie is credited for introducing [[Nicki Minaj]] and [[French Montana]] to Debra's Mizay Entertainment before their mainstream breakouts.<ref name="Power105"/>  He is currently serving a twelve year prison sentence after being convicted of burglary charges in [[Suffolk County, New York|Suffolk County]].<ref name="patch article">{{cite news |title=Rapper Sentenced After Terrorizing Family In LI Home Invasion: DA |url=https://patch.com/new-york/threevillage/s/in10h/rapper-sentenced-after-terrorizing-family-in-li-home-invasion-da |work=Patch}}</ref>

== Career ==
Frenchie hails from a musical family background with several of his relatives in the industry. His cousin Bimmy was an [[Artists and repertoire|A&R]] for [[Def Jam Recordings]] while his uncle from his father's side [[DJ Hurricane]] worked with [[Beastie Boys]].<ref name="Jus24x7">{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=xH6wsiStdEk |title=Band Stand: Frenchie Interview |publisher=Jus 24x7 |date=August 7, 2015 |accessdate=January 16, 2016}}</ref> His cousin/aunt{{clarify|date=December 2016}} Debra Antney was manager to Gucci Mane and [[OJ da Juiceman|OJ Da Juiceman]]. Frenchie was rapping when he was 19 and would go on to record his first professionally produced track Shirt Off in late 2008.<ref name="AHH2014">{{cite web|url=http://allhiphop.com/2014/11/26/exclusive-frenchie-talks-gucci-mane-nicki-minaj-the-power-of-prayer-making-universal-music/ |title=Frenchie Talks Gucci Mane, Nicki Minaj, The Power Of Prayer & Making Universal Music |publisher=[[AllHipHop]] |date=November 26, 2014 |accessdate=January 16, 2016}}</ref> Prior to the record appeared in various street DVDs performing freestyles and cyphers.

== 2008–2011: So Icey Entertainment, So Icey Boyz and 1017 Brick Squad ==
During a trip to Atlanta to attend a family reunion Frenchie's aunt Debra Antney had introduced him to Gucci Mane who signed him to his So Icey Entertainment.<ref name="DJBoogz">{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_K1lBGO2sCs |title=Frenchie Interview: Brick Squad is Still Together |publisher=DJ Boogz |date=December 9, 2014 |accessdate=January 16, 2015}}</ref> The song "Shirt Off" from Gucci Mane's The Movie mixtape released in September 2008 which featured Frenchie, Wooh Da Kid and OJ da Juiceman as [[1017 Records|So Icey Boyz]] – would be Frenchie's breakout track becoming a hit on Atlanta radio.<ref name="BBA"/> Since then he had been managed by Debra Antney's Mizay Entertainment.

Frenchie released his debut mixtape French Connection on February 22, 2009, hosted by DJ Big Mike and Trap-A-Holics.<ref name="Mixtape01"/> Later in the year his camp would go on to form 1017 Brick Squad. His second mixtape ''Chicken Room'', released on September 15,<ref name="Mixtape02"/> would be his first under the new imprint. Frenchie would be featured on "Candy Lady" from Gucci Mane's ''[[The Burrprint]]'' released in October 2009 alongside OJ da Juiceman, Wooh Da Kid and Waka Flocka Flame which would be the second and last time 1017 Brick Squad collaborated on one song since Shirt Off the year prior.<ref name="DJBoogz"/>

According to interviews Frenchie has been the one to introduce Nicki Minaj to Debra Antney who then became her manager,prior to the latter's mainstream breakout.<ref name="Power105">{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8EgLpQPUDwc |title=Frenchie at Breakfast Club Power 105.1 |publisher=[[The Breakfast Club (radio show)|The Breakfast Club]] |date=July 19, 2013 |accessdate=January 17, 2016 }}</ref> In an interview with DJ Lazy K in December 2009 Frenchie announced plans for an upcoming collaborative tape with French Montana.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XD5LjwZm5hM |title=Frenchie Exclusive Radio Interview DJ Lazy K | publisher=PMS Radio |date=December 21, 2009 |accessdate=January 16, 2016}}</ref> Although the tape never materialized, Montana would go on to collaborate with Frenchie and others from Brick Squad the following year. Frenchie had introduced Montana to Debra who would sign him later in the year.<ref name="Power105"/><ref name="EME">{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=EkgE5v8TctM |title=Frenchie of Brick Squad Monopoly Exclusive ''French-Elo Anthony'' Interview |publisher=EME |date=March 9, 2012 |accessdate=January 17, 2012}}</ref> In March 13, 2010, Frenchie's friend and frequent collaborator G-Baby was shot and killed in [[Jamaica, Queens]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.mtv.com/news/1633916/rapper-g-baby-killed/ |title=Rapper G Baby Killed |publisher=[[MTV News]] |date=March 15, 2010 |accessdate=January 18, 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=KazN_iwIbZ8 |title=Frenchie Speaks About G Baby (RIP Gregory Brown) |publisher=D.Hun |date=March 24, 2011 |accessdate=January 18, 2016}}</ref> The two had collaborated on several tracks off of Frenchie's fifth mixtape Trap To The Future released on March 29,<ref name="Mixtape05"/> and his sixth mixtape French Connection Part 2 released on July 18.<ref name="Mixtape06"/>

== 2011–2014: Brick Squad Monopoly ==
Starting in 2011 Frenchie would spend most of his time in [[Atlanta]] and touring with [[1017 Records|1017 Brick Squad]] and Mizay Entertainment. That year Waka Flocka Flame would go on to form [[1017 Records|Brick Squad Monopoly]] with Frenchie as one of the original members. He released his eighth mixtape Bringing Gangsta Back on June 25<ref name="Mixtape08"/> and his ninth mixtape ''Concrete Jungle'' on October 17.<ref name="Mixtape09"/> In December 2011, fellow BSM member Slim Dunkin was shot to death during an altercation.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/news/slim-dunkin-killed-in-atlanta-music-studio-20111217 |title=Slim Dunkin Killed In Atlanta Music Studio |publisher=[[Rolling Stone (magazine)|Rolling Stone]] |date=December 17, 2011 |accessdate=January 18, 2016}}</ref> In 2012 Frenchie released his tenth mixtape French-Elo Anthony on March 10,<ref name="Mixtape11"/> and his eleventh mixtape Concrete Jungle 2 on December 17.<ref name="Mixtape10"/>

On May 21, 2013, he released his twelfth mixtape Long Over Due<ref name="Mixtape13"/> aptly titled so having felt the need to showcase a solo effort with fewer guest features compared to his previous records.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.mtv.com/news/1707880/frenchie-long-overdue-mixtape-waka-flocka-flame/ |title=Waka Flocka Associate Frenchie Gets Impatient On ''Long Over Due'' Mixtape | publisher=[[MTV News]] |date=May 22, 2013 |accessdate=January 16, 2016}}</ref> Later that year, Gucci Mane and Waka Flocka Flame would become embroiled in a rather public falling-out, with Frenchie noticeably on the side of his cousin Waka Flocka.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://hiphopdx.com/news/id.25367/title.brick-squad-possibly-over-as-gucci-mane-waka-flocka-flame-more-exchange-fire-on-twitter |title=Brick Squad Possibly Over As Gucci Mane, Waka Flocka Flame & More Exchange Fire On Twitter |publisher=[[HipHopDX]] |date=September 8, 2013 |accessdate=January 17, 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cIPz1MB6UcM |title=Frenchie: Gucci Takes Meds And He Hasn't Been Taking Them! |publisher=FreakGeniusTV |date=October 7, 2013 |accessdate=January 16, 2016}}</ref> However, when asked about the incident a year later, Frenchie affirmed that the event was blown out of proportion by the media and they were all currently on good terms.<ref name="AHH2014"/><ref name="DJBoogz"/> In 2013 Frenchie released singles Power Move and Don't Know Em which peaked at [[Mediabase]] urban charts at #44 and #89, respectively. He appeared on [[MTV]]'s RapFix Live on March 27,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.mtv.com/news/2500756/schoolboy-q-frenchie-uno-freestyle-on-rapfix-live/ |title=ScHoolBoy Q, Frenchie & Uno Freestyle On 'RapFix Live' |publisher=MTV RapFix Live|date=March 27, 2015 |accessdate=January 19, 2016}}</ref> and on the [[VH1]] show [[Black Ink Crew]] which aired on October 7.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.vh1.com/shows/black_ink_crew/rapper-frenchie-stops-by-the-shop/961412/video/ |title=Rapper Frenchie Stops By The Shop |publisher=[[Black Ink Crew]] |date=October 7, 2013 |accessdate=January 19, 2016}}</ref> On December 29, his cousin and fellow BSM member KayO Redd committed suicide.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.sohh.com/breaking-waka-flocka-flames-brother-passes-frenchie-debra-antney-more-react/ |title=Breaking: Waka Flocka Flame's Brother Passes, Frenchie, Debra Antney & More React |publisher=[[SOHH|SOHH.com]] |date=December 30, 2013 |accessdate=January 19, 2016}}</ref> KayO who is Debra's youngest son died from a self-inflicted gunshot.

In 2014 Frenchie released a collaborative mixtape entitled Underrated with D-Dash on January 14,<ref name="Mixtape14"/> and released his fourteenth mixtape ''Long Over Due 2'' on March 3.<ref name="Mixtape15"/> It would be his last releases under Brick Squad Monopoly and manager Debra Antney with Frenchie venturing off under his own label later in the year. Having accompanied Waka Flocka Flame, [[Steve Aoki]] and [[Borgore]] on tour the year before, he also showed a new-found interest in [[Electronic dance music|EDM]] which he expressed plans to pursue in the future.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.vice.com/en_ca/read/brick-squad-affiliate-frenchie-is-trying-to-make-electric-dance-trapping-a-thing |title=Brick Squad Affiliate Frenchie Is Trying to Make 'Electric Dance Trapping' a Thing |publisher=[[Vice (magazine)|VICE.com]] |date=May 16, 2014 |accessdate=January 17, 2016}}</ref>

== 2014–present: Protekted Records ==

On September 9, 2014, Frenchie announced he was joining Protekted Records an independent label he helped found with Karen Fraiberg and Jon Gornbein.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.prweb.com/releases/2014/09/prweb12155390.htm |title=1017 Brick Squad Founding Member Leave Brick Squad Launches New Imprint |publisher=[[PRWeb]] |date=September 9, 2014 |accessdate=January 17, 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2014/09/frenchie-launches-new-imprint-protekted-records/ |title=Brick Squad Monopoly Frenchie Launches New Imprint Protekted Records |publisher=[[XXL (magazine)|XXL]] |date=September 14, 2014 |accessdate=January 17, 2016 }}</ref> Under the new management, he would go on to release his fifteenth mixtape on September 16 entitled ''Fukk Fame''.<ref name="Mixtape16"/> That year, he released the single Ain't Goin Nowhere featuring [[B.o.B]] and [[Chanel West Coast]], which peaked at #29 on [[Mediabase]] urban charts.<ref name="Jus24x7"/> In 2015, Frenchie released a collaborative mixtape entitled ''Phil Jackson'' with his Artist rapper/producer Jusjoose on November 8.<ref name="Mixtape17"/> His seventeenth mixtape Chicken Room 2 came out in 2015.

==Criminal conviction==
On June 22, 2022, Frenchie was convicted in [[Suffolk County, New York|Suffolk County]] of burglary for an armed home invasion which occurred in 2021.<ref>{{cite news |title=RAPPER CONVICTED OF THREE COUNTS OF FIRST DEGREE BURGLARY |url=https://suffolkcountyny.gov/da/Newsroom/Press-Releases/rapper-convicted-of-three-counts-of-first-degree-burglary |work=Suffolk County District Attorney}}</ref> Frenchie was determined to have been one of seven men, some armed with handguns, who entered the home of a sleeping family because they incorrectly believed there was $100,000 worth of marijuana inside.<ref name="fox5 article">{{cite news |title=Rapper Gregg 'Frenchie' Hogan convicted after Long Island home invasion |url=https://www.fox5ny.com/news/long-island-rapper-gregg-frenchie-hogan-convicted-burglary |work=Fox 5 New York}}</ref> On April 6, 2023, Frenchie was sentenced to twelve years in prison for this crime.<ref name="patch article" />
