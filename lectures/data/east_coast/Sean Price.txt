'''Sean Duval Price''' (March 17, 1972{{snd}}August 8, 2015)<ref>https://www.ascap.com/repertory#ace/work/880799375 {{Bare URL inline|date=August 2022}}</ref> was an American rapper and member of the [[Hip hop music|hip hop]] collective [[Boot Camp Clik]].<ref name="allmusic bio">{{cite web|title=Sean Price biography|url=http://www.allmusic.com/artist/sean-price-p544517/biography|publisher=allmusic|access-date=June 28, 2011}}</ref> He was one half of the duo [[Heltah Skeltah]], performing under the name '''Ruck''', along with partner Rock.

==Early life and education==
Growing up in the borough of [[Brooklyn]], Price was a member of the [[Decepticons (gang)|Decepticons]] in high school.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.vibe.com/2012/10/memory-lane-sean-price-takes-us-back-school/|title=Memory Lane: Sean Price Takes Us Back to School|date=22 October 2012}}</ref>

==Career==
A member of the underground hip hop collective [[Boot Camp Clik]], Price first appeared on [[Smif-N-Wessun]]'s 1995 album ''[[Dah Shinin']]''. He would soon resurface in 1996 as one half of the critically acclaimed Brooklyn duo [[Heltah Skeltah]], who released their debut album ''[[Nocturnal (Heltah Skeltah album)|Nocturnal]]'' on [[Duck Down Records]] that year. Heltah Skeltah would go on to release two more albums: 1998's ''[[Magnum Force (album)|Magnum Force]]'' and 2008's ''[[D.I.R.T. (Heltah Skeltah album)|D.I.R.T.]]''.

In the 2000s, Price established himself as a strong force in the underground hip hop community. He was featured on Grand Theft Auto 3's GAME FM radio station in 2001. In 2005, Price released his first solo album ''[[Monkey Barz]]'', to critical acclaim. He followed this in 2007 with ''[[Jesus Price Supastar]]'' and ''[[Mic Tyson]]'' in 2012.

Price was interviewed on the online series ''Grand Angel TV'' in 2008<ref>{{cite AV media|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=lgNQ5mJf1Ig |archive-url=https://ghostarchive.org/varchive/youtube/20211222/lgNQ5mJf1Ig |archive-date=2021-12-22 |url-status=live|title=GRAND ANGEL TV 3 – Paid Dues 2008/ Sean Price Interview|date=3 April 2008|work=YouTube}}{{cbignore}}</ref> and 2010,<ref>{{cite AV media|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=rFdPWtSWJX8 |archive-url=https://ghostarchive.org/varchive/youtube/20211222/rFdPWtSWJX8 |archive-date=2021-12-22 |url-status=live|title=Grand Angel TV #13: Sean Price Interview & Performances|date=3 October 2010|work=YouTube}}{{cbignore}}</ref> with [[Paid Dues]] performance footage alongside Boot Camp Clik and [[Drew "Dru Ha" Friedman|Dru Ha]], respectively.

In 2011, Price was a judge on the ''Ultimate MC'' TV show alongside [[Royce da 5'9"]], [[Organik (musician)|Organik]], [[Planet Asia]], and [[Pharoahe Monch]].<ref>"{{cite web |url=http://ultimatemc.tv/ |title=Ultimate MC |access-date=2012-05-21 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120426072525/http://ultimatemc.tv/ |archive-date=2012-04-26 }}</ref> Also in 2011, he formed the group [[Random Axe]] with [[Guilty Simpson]] and producer [[Black Milk]].

He had a planned collaborative album with rapper [[Ill Bill]] in the works, called ''The Pill''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/news/id.15750/title.sean-price-talks-random-axe-and-new-album-with-ill-bill|title=Sean Price Talks Random Axe And New Album With Ill Bill|author=HipHopDX|date=28 June 2011|work=HipHopDX}}</ref> ''Songs in the Key of Price'', a mixtape recorded before Price's death, was released posthumously on August 21, 2015. The posthumous release of his fourth studio album, ''[[Imperius Rex]]'', followed in August 2017.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.vibe.com/music/music-news/sean-prices-imperius-rex-album-530991/|title=Stream Sean Price's Posthumous 'Imperius Rex' Album|author=Fresh, Mikey|date=August 9, 2017|work=Vibe}}</ref>

==Death==
On August 8, 2015, Price died at the age of 43.<ref>[https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-news/sean-price-veteran-rapper-and-boot-camp-clik-member-dead-at-43-33569/ Sean Price, Veteran Rapper and Boot Camp Clik Member, Dead at 43]</ref> A statement from his reps confirmed his death, while adding that he died in his sleep.

"It is with beyond a heavy heart that Duck Down Music is sadly confirming that Sean Price passed away early this morning in his Brooklyn apartment, Saturday, August 8th, 2015," read the statement. "The cause of death is currently unknown, but it was reported that he died in his sleep. He's survived by his three children."<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ballerstatus.com/2015/08/08/sean-price-of-heltah-skeltah-dead-at-43/ |title=Sean Price, Of Heltah Skeltah, Dead At 43 |publisher=BallerStatus.com |date=August 8, 2015}}</ref>
