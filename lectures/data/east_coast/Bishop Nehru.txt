'''Markel Ni'Jee Scott''' (born August 26, 1996), better known by his stage name '''Bishop Nehru''', is an American rapper and record producer from [[Nanuet, New York]]. He also directs and edits [[music video]]s.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://allhiphop.com/news/bishop-nehru-the-teenage-phenom-that-s-ready-to-be-a-renaissance-man-sG1TBS2E9kmXf6FGOShTkA|title=Bishop Nehru: The Teenage Phenom That's Ready To Be A Renaissance Man|website=AllHipHop.com}}</ref>

== Career ==

===2009–13: Early career===
At age 13, Nehru began making jazz tracks and hip hop instrumentals under the name "Kelz Scott" soon being changed to the name Kile Kanvas at age 14.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|url=http://www.kanyetothe.com/forum/index.php?topic=1611009.0|title=The Official Bishop Nehru Thread « Kanye West Forum|website=www.kanyetothe.com|access-date=2016-02-21}}</ref> Nehru released his earlier works on the [[Odd Future]] forum and other forums like Hypebeast, [[Lookbook.nu]], and many more.{{citation needed|date=January 2015}}

In 2012, [[WorldStarHipHop]] named Bishop Nehru the Youth Rap Talent of the Week for his 8-bar freestyle over [[Mos Def]]'s "[[Mathematics (Mos Def song)|Mathematics]]."<ref>{{Cite web|title = Bishop Nehru is making dranks {{!}} Crack Magazine|url = http://crackmagazine.net/article/music/bishop-nehru-interview/|website = Crack Magazine|access-date = 2016-02-21|language = en-US}}</ref> Nehru was featured on [[WQHT|Hot97.com]] as part of their ''Who's Next'' section, which showcases rappers who are "next to blow up." He also had the song make its way to [[Power 105|Power 105.1's]] ''New NY'' with Jovonn "The Don" in February 2013,<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.sing365.com/music/lyric.nsf/Bishop-Nehru-Biography/A8D15E10B7FA7D7048257BAB003F82FC |title=Bishop Nehru Biography<!-- Bot generated title --> |access-date=2013-11-05 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131105234656/http://www.sing365.com/music/lyric.nsf/Bishop-Nehru-Biography/A8D15E10B7FA7D7048257BAB003F82FC |archive-date=2013-11-05 |url-status=dead }}</ref> and opened for [[Wu-Tang Clan]] on their 20th Anniversary European tour.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://theboombox.com/bishop-nehru-interview/|title=The Futurist: Bishop Nehru|first=Kidd|last=Future|website=The Boombox}}</ref>

When he was 16 years old, Bishop Nehru released his debut mixtape, ''Nehruvia'', a 13-song project that included production from [[DJ Premier]], [[Madlib]], MF DOOM and more.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://djbooth.net/artists/bishop-nehru|title=Bishop Nehru|website=DJBooth}}</ref> He followed that up with ''strictlyFLOWz'' which was presented by New York radio host [[Peter Rosenberg]] and the UK's [[DJ Semtex]].<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.djbooth.net/index/albums/review/bishop-nehru-strictlyflowz |title=Bishop Nehru - strictlyFLOWZ - Download & Stream<!-- Bot generated title --> |access-date=2013-11-05 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131106001843/http://www.djbooth.net/index/albums/review/bishop-nehru-strictlyflowz |archive-date=2013-11-06 |url-status=dead }}</ref>

In August 2013, it was announced that Bishop Nehru and [[MF Doom|MF DOOM]] would collaborate on a project set to be released via [[Lex Records]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://pitchfork.com/news/51845-doom-working-on-project-with-bishop-nehru/|title=DOOM Working on Project With Bishop Nehru|website=Pitchfork|date=9 August 2013}}</ref>

=== 2014–present: Mass Appeal Records, ''NehruvianDOOM'' ===
On May 22, 2014, it was announced that Bishop Nehru, [[Boldy James]] and [[Fashawn]] were the first signees to [[Nas]]' [[Mass Appeal Records]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.xxlmag.com/news/2014/05/boldy-james-fashawn-nas-mass-appeal/|title=Boldy James And Fashawn On Signing To Nas' Mass Appeal Records - XXL|first=X. X. L.|last=Staff|website=XXL Mag}}</ref> On June 5, 2014,  [[Dizzy Wright]] and Bishop Nehru released a free collaborative EP titled ''BrILLiant Youth EP''.

''[[NehruvianDOOM]]'' was released on October 7, 2014. Nas announced via [[Twitter]] that he will be the [[executive producer]] on Nehru's forthcoming album, which has yet to be released.<ref>{{Cite magazine|title = Bishop Nehru's 'Nehruvia: The Nehruvian' EP Premiere: Rapper Talks Nas' Advice {{!}} Billboard|url = http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/the-juice/6561195/bishop-nehru-nehruvia-the-nehruvian-ep-premiere-interview|magazine = Billboard|access-date = 2016-02-21}}</ref>

In 2015, Nehru's single, "You Stressin", was featured on [[EA Sports]]'s ''[[NBA Live 15]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://massappeal.com/nas-to-executive-produce-bishop-nehrus-forthcoming-solo-album/|title=Nas to Executive Produce Bishop Nehru's Forthcoming Solo Album|author=Mass Appeal staff|publisher=MassAppeal.com|date=October 6, 2014|accessdate=October 7, 2014|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141010081004/http://massappeal.com/nas-to-executive-produce-bishop-nehrus-forthcoming-solo-album/|archive-date=October 10, 2014|url-status=dead}}</ref>

== Influences ==
Bishop Nehru's influences include [[Michael Jackson]], Nas, [[2Pac]], [[MF Doom|MF DOOM]], [[Kanye West]] and Wu-Tang Clan.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/news/id.27993/title.bishop-nehru-sure-music-will-come-out-of-meeting-with-nas|title = Bishop Nehru "Sure Music Will Come Out Of" Meeting with Nas|date = 21 March 2014}}</ref> Other influences include [[Herbie Hancock]], [[50 Cent]], [[Eminem]], [[Pharrell Williams]], [[ASAP Rocky]], [[Tyler, The Creator]], [[Red Hot Chili Peppers]], and [[Incubus (band)|Incubus]]<ref>[http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/the-juice/6561195/bishop-nehru-nehruvia-the-nehruvian-ep-premiere-interview Bishop Nehru Premieres 'Nehruvia: The Nehruvian' EP, Talks Stephen Curry & Advice from Nas] ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' May 11, 2015. Retrieved May 23, 2015.</ref>

Nehru chose his stage name from a combination of [[Tupac Shakur|Tupac's]] character in the movie ''[[Juice (1992 film)|Juice]]'', noting that the character of Bishop inspires him "to go out and get mine, you've got to earn respect" and Nehru is taken from the first prime minister of [[India]], [[Jawaharlal Nehru]], who worked closely with [[Mahatma Gandhi]].<ref>{{Cite web|title = New York phenom Bishop Nehru is the new Golden Child of hip-hop|url = http://waxpoetics.com/features/articles/new-york-phenom-bishop-nehru-is-the-new-golden-child-of-hip-hop/|website = Wax Poetics|access-date = 2016-02-21|language = en-US}}</ref>
