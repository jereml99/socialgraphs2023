Herring''' (born April 13, 1984),<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.soundbysight.com/future-islands-the-observatory-041514/|title=Future Islands – The Observatory – 04/15/14|website=Sound by Sight|first=Natasha|last=Vi|date=April 20, 2014|access-date=November 22, 2015|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20151208111937/http://www.soundbysight.com/future-islands-the-observatory-041514/|archive-date=December 8, 2015|url-status=dead}}</ref> also known as '''Hemlock Ernst''',<ref>{{cite web|url=http://pitchfork.com/news/54448-future-islands-frontman-samuel-t-herring-is-also-a-rapper-watch-him-in-action/|title=Future Islands Frontman Samuel T. ==Early life==
Born in [[Carteret County]], [[North Carolina]], Herring grew up in [[Newport, North Carolina|Newport]]<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.indyweek.com/indyweek/future-islands-international-star-is-rising-but-their-roots-run-back-to-north-carolina/Content?oid=3982010|title=Future Islands' international star is rising, but their roots run back to North Carolina|website=[[Indy Week]]|first=Corbie|last=Hill|date=March 26, 2014|access-date=November 22, 2015}}</ref> and [[Morehead City, North Carolina|Morehead City]].<ref name="consequenceofsound2011"/> He began [[rapping]] at age 14 at [[freestyle battles]] and cyphers.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.sfweekly.com/sanfrancisco/future-islands-sam-herring/Content?oid=4087269|title=Sam Herring: The Late-Night Big Bad Wolf|website=[[SF Weekly]]|first=Matt|last=Saincome|date=September 23, 2015|access-date=22 November 2015}}</ref> In 2002, he enrolled at [[East Carolina University]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-features/future-islands-the-unlikely-rise-of-baltimores-heartache-kings-123082/|title=Future Islands: The Unlikely Rise of Baltimore's Heartache Kings|website=[[Rolling Stone]]|first=Sarah|last=Grant|date=April 10, 2017|access-date=April 15, 2020}}</ref> Herring is a keen [[Association football|soccer]] fan and an avid supporter of [[Everton F.C.]]<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/53431020|title=Premier League predictions: Lawro v Future Islands frontman and Everton fan Samuel T Herring
|website=[[BBC Sport]]|first=Mark|last=Lawrenson|date=July 20, 2020|accessdate=January 22, 2021}}</ref>

==Career==
In 2003, while attending East Carolina University, Herring and other colleagues started the performance art band [[Art Lord & the Self-Portraits]], which lasted until 2005.<ref name="thedailybeast2014"/> Some of its former members started [[Future Islands]] in 2006, and the band relocated to [[Baltimore]], [[Maryland]] in 2008.<ref name="thedailybeast2014"/> In 2013,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.baltimoremagazine.net/2015/4/22/listen-to-future-islands-samuel-t-herring-rap-on-a-new-track|title=Listen to Future Islands' Sam Herring Rap on a New Track|website=[[Baltimore (magazine)|Baltimore]]|first=Lydia|last=Woolever|date=April 22, 2015|access-date=November 22, 2015}}</ref> Herring took a break from the band's touring in order to spend more time on his solo rap project, writing more, and doing more shows.<ref name="thedailybeast2014">{{cite web|url=http://www.thedailybeast.com/articles/2014/04/03/future-islands-frontman-samuel-t-herring-on-his-11-year-journey-to-letterman-and-viral-stardom.html|title=Future Islands Frontman Samuel T. Herring on Their 11-Year Journey to Letterman and Viral Stardom|website=[[The Daily Beast]]|first=Marlow|last=Stern|date=April 3, 2014|access-date=November 22, 2015}}</ref>

Parallel to [[Future Islands]], Herring continued rapping either solo, under the moniker Hemlock Ernst, or with his brother as Flesh Epic.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.newraleigh.com/articles/archive/flesh-epic-brings-the-beat-to-berkeley/|title=Flesh Epic Brings the Beat to Berkeley|website=New Raleigh|first=Stacey|last=Stacey|date=August 27, 2010|accessdate=November 22, 2015}}</ref> Hemlock was his original writing name on an on-line music board when he was in the 9th grade, from a poem he wrote about [[Socrates]] taking the [[Conium maculatum|hemlock]] poison.<ref name="aux2014"/> Ernst comes from his character in Art Lord & the Self-Portraits who was named Locke Ernst-Frost: a reference to [[John Locke (poet)|John Locke]] the religious poet, [[Max Ernst]], the artist and [[Robert Frost]], the American poet.<ref name="aux2014">{{cite web|url=http://www.aux.tv/2014/07/future-islands-talk-alter-egos-the-smashing-pumpkins-and-the-upside-to-being-sad/|title=Future Islands talk alter egos, the Smashing Pumpkins, and the upside to being sad|website=[[A.Side TV]]|first=Jabbari|last=Weekes|date=July 7, 2014|access-date=November 22, 2015|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20151208192902/http://www.aux.tv/2014/07/future-islands-talk-alter-egos-the-smashing-pumpkins-and-the-upside-to-being-sad/|archive-date=December 8, 2015|url-status=dead}}</ref>
