'''Naeem Juwan Hanks''',<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.ascap.com/repertory#/ace/search/workID/887204228 |title=ASSASSIN |website=ASCAP |publisher=American Society of Composers, Authors and Publishers |access-date=April 29, 2023}}</ref> better known by his stage name '''Spank Rock''', is an American rapper and songwriter from [[Baltimore]]. He rose to fame with his 2006 album ''[[YoYoYoYoYo]]'', which was produced by former group member Alex Epton ([[XXXChange]]). A harbinger of post-millennial alternative rap,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.thefader.com/2011/08/01/video-spank-rock-dtf-dadt/|title=Spank Rock: DTF DADT|work=[[The Fader]]|first=Duncan|last=Cooper}}</ref> the duo became known for its mixing of disparate [[Hip hop music|hip hop]] and [[Electronic Dance Music|club genres]], including [[Baltimore club]],<ref name="allmusic">{{cite web|url=http://www.allmusic.com/album/everything-is-boring-everyone-is-a-f-ing-liar-mw0002169836|title=Everything Is Boring & Everyone Is a F---ing Liar Album Review|work=[[AllMusic]]|first=John|last=Bush}}</ref> [[Miami bass]],<ref name="miaminewtimes">{{cite web|url=http://blogs.miaminewtimes.com/crossfade/2010/10/spank_rock_feeds_his_serious_n.php|title=Spank Rock Feeds his Serious Need for Speed|work=[[Miami New Times]]|first=S.|last=Pajot}}</ref> [[electro music]] and rock.

In 2007, Epton left the group to pursue his own production<ref>{{cite web|url=http://newsflash.bigshotmag.com/big-shot-magazine/1458/|title=Exclusive: DJ XXXChange Is No Longer In Spank Rock|work=Big Shot Mag|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140513120802/http://newsflash.bigshotmag.com/big-shot-magazine/1458/|archive-date=2014-05-13|url-status=dead}}</ref> while Juwan went on to release the ''[[Bangers & Cash]]'' EP (2007) with pop producer [[Benny Blanco]]. After a five-year contract struggle with his label [[Downtown Records]],<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.allmusic.com/artist/spank-rock-mn0000279593|title=Spank Rock - Biography|work=[[AllMusic]]}}</ref> Juwan released his second album, ''[[Everything Is Boring and Everyone Is a Fucking Liar]]'', with a range of producers including [[Boys Noize]], [[Le1f]],<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.urb.com/2011/08/31/spank-rock-ft-big-freedia-nasty/|title=Spank Rock feat. Big Freedia: Nasty|work=[[URB (magazine)|URB]]|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20111113175645/http://www.urb.com/2011/08/31/spank-rock-ft-big-freedia-nasty/|archive-date=November 13, 2011|url-status=dead}}</ref> [[XXXChange]], and [[Squeak E. Clean]].<ref name="discogs">{{cite web|url=http://www.discogs.com/Spank-Rock-Everything-Is-Boring-Everyone-Is-A-F-Liar/release/3167181|title=Liner Notes for Everything Is Boring and Everyone Is a Fucking Liar|work=[[Discogs]]}}</ref>

==Biography==
Juwan grew up in West Baltimore in a row home alongside five sisters and two brothers.<ref name="ResidentAdvisor">{{cite web|url=http://www.residentadvisor.net/dj/spankrock/biography|title=Spank Rock Biography|work=[[Resident Advisor]]}}</ref> As a teenager, Juwan frequented clubs such as Paradox, where DJs played [[Baltimore club]] music. In high school, his older sister introduced him to Brooklyn-based producer [[J.Period|J. Period]], known for producing records by underground rappers [[Mos Def]] and [[Artifacts (group)|Artifacts]]. J. Period mentored Juwan and helped him improve his skills as a rapper for some time. After moving to Philadelphia and dropping out of college, Juwan met fellow Baltimore native Alex Epton, who had studied composition at the [[New England Conservatory|New England Conservatory of Music]] in Boston and learned studio production as an intern at the [[DFA Records]] studio in New York.<ref name="ResidentAdvisor" />

Juwan and Epton started performing under the name Spank Rock together in [[Philadelphia]] and [[Phoenixville]], where their reputation quickly increased. They later toured with Hollertronix and [[M.I.A. (rapper)|M.I.A.]] They also toured with [[Beck]] in 2006.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/wp-dyn/content/article/2006/10/29/AR2006102900771.html|title=Baltimore's Spank Rock: Unhinged but Hot|newspaper=[[The Washington Post]]|first=Christopher|last=Porter|date=October 30, 2006}}</ref> Spank Rock's debut studio album, ''[[YoYoYoYoYo]]'', was released in 2006 on the [[Big Dada]] record label.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://music.ign.com/articles/702/702646p1.html|title=Spank Rock - YoYoYoYoYo|work=[[IGN]]|first=Todd|last=Gilchrist|date=April 21, 2006|access-date=July 21, 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120205192042/http://music.ign.com/articles/702/702646p1.html|archive-date=February 5, 2012|url-status=dead}}</ref> It was nominated for the 2006 [[Shortlist Music Prize]].<ref name=nme>{{cite web|url=https://www.nme.com/news/music/cat-power-40-1349320|title=Shortlist finalists announced|work=[[NME]]|date=May 1, 2007|access-date=July 21, 2020}}</ref>

In 2007, Alex Epton ([[XXXChange]]) left Spank Rock to pursue his own production while Naeem Juwan signed a deal with [[Downtown Records]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.thedailyswarm.com/swarm/spank-rock-goes-downtown-records-will-they-be-new-gnarls-barkley/|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20070630020628/http://www.thedailyswarm.com/swarm/spank-rock-goes-downtown-records-will-they-be-new-gnarls-barkley/|url-status=dead|archive-date=June 30, 2007|title=Spank Rock Goes Downtown: Will They Be the New Gnarls Barkley?|work=The Daily Swarm|date=June 20, 2007}}</ref> Spank Rock's first Downtown Records release came in the form of fall 2007's ''[[Bangers & Cash]]'' EP, a [[2 Live Crew]]-inspired collaborative EP with [[Benny Blanco]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.pitchforkmedia.com/article/news/45350-spank-rocks-2-live-crew-inspired-ep-revealed|title=Spank Rock's 2 Live Crew Inspired EP Revealed|work=[[Pitchfork (website)|Pitchfork]]|first=Paul|last=Thompson|date=September 4, 2007}}</ref>

In 2010, Spank Rock was featured, along with [[The View (band)|The View]]'s [[Kyle Falconer]], on "[[The Bike Song]]" by [[Mark Ronson]], which appeared on Ronson's 2010 album [[Record Collection (album)|Record Collection]]. Spank Rock toured with Ronson for the album's 2010 UK tour.

The following year, Spank Rock released his second studio album, ''[[Everything Is Boring and Everyone Is a Fucking Liar]]'', on his own label, Bad Blood Records.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://stereogum.com/721571/spank-rock-energy/mp3s/|title=Spank Rock – 'Energy'|work=[[Stereogum]]|date=May 5, 2012|access-date=June 7, 2012}}</ref> ''[[Pitchfork (website)|Pitchfork]]'' premiered the first single from the album, titled "Energy," produced by [[Boys Noize]],<ref name="pitchforkpremiere" /><ref>{{cite web|url=http://spankrock.com/archives/384|title=Energy|work=Spank Rock}}</ref> and later selected the single "Nasty" as a "Best New Track."<ref>{{cite web|url=http://pitchfork.com/reviews/tracks/12514-nasty-ft-big-freedia/|title=Spank Rock "Nasty"|work=[[Pitchfork (website)|Pitchfork]]|first=Carrie|last=Battan}}</ref> ''[[Rolling Stone]]'' premiered the album in September 2011 on their website.<ref name="rollingstone">{{cite magazine|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/news/exclusive-stream-spank-rocks-rowdy-new-album-20110922 |title=Exclusive Stream: Spank Rock's Rowdy New Album|magazine=[[Rolling Stone]]|date=September 22, 2011}}</ref> The album featured production from Boys Noize, XXXChange, [[Sam Spiegel]], and Le1f, as well as appearances by [[Santigold]] and [[Big Freedia]].<ref name="discogs" /><ref name="pitchforkpremiere">{{cite web|url=http://pitchfork.com/news/42719-spank-rock-returns-with-new-album/ |title=Spank Rock Returns With New Album|work=[[Pitchfork (website)|Pitchfork]]|date=June 7, 2012|access-date=June 7, 2012}}</ref> After the release of his album, Spank Rock was selected to be one of the faces of [[Alexander Wang (designer)|Alexander Wang]]'s 2011 T collection, along with Santigold.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.style.com/stylefile/2011/07/exclusive-santigold-and-spankrock-for-t-by-alexander-wang/|title=Exclusive: Santigold And Spankrock For T By Alexander Wang|work=Style}}</ref>

In 2012, Spank Rock released a second EP, ''E. I. B. A. E. I. A. F. L. Remixes'', a collection of various producers' remixes of tracks from ''Everything Is Boring and Everyone Is a Fucking Liar''. He also co-wrote the Boys Noize-produced track "Look at These Hoes" from [[Santigold]]'s album, [[Master of My Make-Believe]].<ref name="discogs" /><ref name="Look at These Hoes">{{cite web|url=http://www.allmusic.com/song/look-at-these-hoes-mt0043980755/|title='Look at These Hoes' liner notes|work=[[AllMusic]]}}</ref>

Spank Rock released his third EP, ''The Upside'', in 2014 on Bad Blood Records and Boysnoize Records, with production contributions from Kid Kamillion, Boys Noize, and Damian Taylor. Lead single "Gully" was remixed by Brodinski and [[Switch (house DJ)|Switch]], and received extensive play on [[BBC Radio 1]].<ref name="bbc">{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/music/artists/6b55530c-efc2-46e1-9703-d9c08cacf6af|title=Spank Rock profile on BBC One|website=[[BBC]]}}</ref>

Spank Rock began appearing in concerts in 2016 as an [[Rapping|MC]] with [[Australia]]n electronic music group [[The Avalanches]]. He subsequently appeared in performances at [[Coachella Valley Music and Arts Festival|Coachella]]<ref>{{cite web | url=https://music.mxdwn.com/2017/04/14/news/spank-rock-joins-the-avalanches-live-band-at-coachella-2017/ | title=Spank Rock Joins the Avalanches' Live Band at Coachella 2017 - | date=April 14, 2017 }}</ref> and [[Vivid Sydney]]<ref>{{cite web | url=https://www.theguardian.com/music/2017/may/28/vivid-sydney-the-avalanches-block-party-dj-shadow-briggs-jonti-dj-jnett | title=Vivid Sydney: The Avalanches' block party with DJ Shadow shuts down just as it's revving up | website=[[TheGuardian.com]] | date=May 28, 2017 }}</ref> in 2017.

Under the Naeem moniker, Juwan released a solo studio album, ''[[Startisha]]'', in 2020.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.metacritic.com/music/startisha/naeem|title=Startisha by Naeem|work=[[Metacritic]]|publisher=[[CBS Interactive]]|access-date=July 21, 2020}}</ref> It features contributions from [[Justin Vernon]], [[Francis and the Lights]], Ryan Olson, [[Swamp Dogg]], Velvet Negroni, [[Amanda Blank]], and Micah James.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://pitchfork.com/news/naeem-announces-new-album-startisha-shares-new-song-with-justin-vernon-listen/|title=Naeem Announces New Album Startisha, Shares New Song With Justin Vernon: Listen|work=[[Pitchfork (website)|Pitchfork]]|first=Matthew|last=Strauss|date=April 16, 2020|access-date=July 21, 2020}}</ref>
