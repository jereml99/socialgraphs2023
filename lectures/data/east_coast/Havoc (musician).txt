'''Kejuan Waliek Muchita''' (born May 21, 1974), better known by his stage name '''Havoc''', is an American rapper and record producer. He was one half of the [[Hip hop music|hip hop]] duo [[Mobb Deep]] with [[Prodigy (rapper)|Prodigy]].

== Biography ==
Havoc was born in Brooklyn and raised in the [[Queensbridge Houses]]. At a later age, Havoc found out his paternal grandfather was jazz musician [[Bill Dixon]]. In his teenage years, he shot a Def Jam employee in the belly by accident.<ref>{{cite web|first=Talib |last=Kweli |title=Mobb Deep's Havoc Goes Deep On Prodigy, "Shook Ones Pt. II," His Production, & More |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Tf1RAk8w7zc |publisher=People's Party with Talib Kweli, on UPROXX Video |date=August 3, 2023 |access-date=August 17, 2023}}</ref> He graduated from the [[High School of Art and Design]] in [[Manhattan, New York]] in 1992, where he met [[Prodigy (rapper)|Prodigy]]. He is one half of the iconic hip hop duo Mobb Deep, and in addition to producing nearly the entire Mobb Deep catalogue, has also produced songs for [[Master of Ceremonies|MCs]] such as [[The Notorious B.I.G.]], [[Eminem]], [[Nas]], [[Raekwon]], [[50 Cent]], [[112 (band)|112]], [[Method Man]], [[Mariah Carey]], [[Sean Combs|Puff Daddy]], [[LL Cool J]], [[Rohff]], [[Big Noyd]], [[Lil Kim]], [[Capone-N-Noreaga]], [[Foxy Brown (rapper)|Foxy Brown]], [[Onyx (band)|Onyx]], and his partner [[Prodigy (rapper)|Prodigy]] on his solo work. In 2005, Mobb Deep recorded for fellow Queens rapper [[50 Cent]]'s record label [[G-Unit Records]] and left the label in 2009, in a mutually agreed-to release.<ref>{{cite web |last=Langhorne |first=Cyrus |url=http://www.sohh.com/2009/11/50_cent_released_mobb_deep_from_g-unit_r.html |title=50 Cent Released Mobb Deep From G-Unit Records, Says Game Is Still Signed To His Label |publisher=Sohh.Com |date=November 10, 2009 |access-date=March 30, 2012 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://archive.today/20120529181605/http://www.sohh.com/2009/11/50_cent_released_mobb_deep_from_g-unit_r.html |archive-date=May 29, 2012 }}</ref>

Havoc is renowned as one of the most iconic figures in New York hip hop. Popular music magazine ''Complex'' ranked Havoc among the top hip hop producers of 1995 after ''The Infamous''.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.complex.com/music/2018/10/best-hip-hop-producers|title=Best Hip Hop Producer Alive Every Year Since 1979|last=(Multiple)|first=(Multiple)|date=December 17, 2018|website=Complex|access-date=December 17, 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20181218012556/https://www.complex.com/music/2018/10/best-hip-hop-producers|archive-date=December 18, 2018|url-status=live}}</ref> Beattips.com ranked him as the 24th most influential beatmaker of all time<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://beattips.com/2014/09/07/beattips-top-30-beatmakers-of-all-time-havoc-of-mobb-deep/|title=BeatTips Top 30 Beatmakers of All Time: Havoc (of Mobb Deep)|date=September 7, 2014|website=BeatTips|language=en-US|access-date=December 17, 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20181218012153/http://beattips.com/2014/09/07/beattips-top-30-beatmakers-of-all-time-havoc-of-mobb-deep/|archive-date=December 18, 2018|url-status=live}}</ref> citing his dual abilities of rapping and producing. Following ''[[The Infamous]]'', he tweaked his skills and transitioned to a more atmospheric production style that incorporated samples from classical music, most notably visible on the 1996 album ''[[Hell on Earth (Mobb Deep album)|Hell on Earth]]''. As a rapper, he is known for his hardcore lyricism and consistent flow. He also contributed background vocals and a verse on [[Black Moon (band)|Black Moon]]'s debut album ''[[Enta da Stage]]'' in 1993.

In July 2009, Havoc was featured alongside  in [[J Dilla]]'s music video for "24K Rap" off of the ''[[Jay Stay Paid]]'' album, the video was directed by [[Derek Pike]]. In 2010, Havoc produced a beat for [[Eminem]] that resulted in a song called "Untitled" which is a hidden track on Eminem's album ''[[Recovery (Eminem album)|Recovery]]'' and contributed to the [[iTunes]] bonus track to Raekwon's album [[Shaolin vs. Wu-Tang|''Shaolin vs. Wu-Tang'']] & [[Bad Meets Evil]]'s ''[[Hell: The Sequel]] EP''. He revealed that he plans to record a new Mobb Deep album with [[Nas]].<ref>{{cite web |last=Vasquez |first=Andres |url=http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/news/id.15279/title.mobb-deep-nas-to-collaborate-on-full-length-lp-says-havoc |title=Mobb Deep & Nas To Collaborate On Full-Length LP, Says Havoc &#124; Get The Latest Hip Hop News, Rap News & Hip Hop Album Sales |publisher=HipHop DX |date=May 25, 2011 |access-date=March 30, 2012 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150112014025/http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/news/id.15279/title.mobb-deep-nas-to-collaborate-on-full-length-lp-says-havoc |archive-date=January 12, 2015 |url-status=dead }}</ref> His third studio album ''[[13 (Havoc album)|13]]'' was released on May 7, 2013.

Havoc worked with [[Kavinsky]] on his 2013 album [[OutRun (album)|OutRun]]. He wrote the lyrics and sang the vocals for "Suburbia", the sixth track on the album.<ref>{{cite AV media notes |title=OutRun |type=CD liner notes |others=[[Kavinsky]] |publisher=Record Makers |year=2013 |id=REC86}}</ref> In 2016, Havoc helped produce "[[Real Friends (Kanye West song)|Real Friends]]" and "Famous", two tracks on [[Kanye West]]'s album ''[[The Life of Pablo]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2016/02/havoc-produced-on-kanye-west-the-life-of-pablo-album-interview/|title=Havoc Breaks Down His Production Work on Kanye West's 'The Life of Pablo' Album – Exclusive|website=[[XXL (magazine)|XXL]]|date=February 19, 2016|access-date=February 19, 2016|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160220094011/http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2016/02/havoc-produced-on-kanye-west-the-life-of-pablo-album-interview/|archive-date=February 20, 2016|url-status=live}}</ref>

His original productions have been featured in numerous commercially successful films, well known television series and popular video games, and he is a character in [[Def Jam: Fight For NY]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://pro.imdb.com/name/nm0369895/about|title=Havoc - Filmography &#124; IMDbPro}}</ref>
Havoc produced the [[New York Yankees]] official 2020 anthem, "Squad Up", that features verses from both Havoc and [[Method Man]].
