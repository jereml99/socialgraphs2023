'''Spencer George Bellamy'''<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.ascap.com/repertory#/ace/search/workID/904276541 |title=DAY IN A LIFE |website=ASCAP |publisher=American Society of Composers, Authors and Publishers |access-date=April 28, 2023}}</ref> (born October 30, 1969), better known as '''East Flatbush Project''' (also known as '''Spencer 4 Hire'''), is an American [[hip hop music|hip hop]] [[record producer]] from [[Brooklyn]], [[New York City]], [[New York (state)|New York]]. He has released six singles to date, the most famous of which is titled "Tried by 12".<ref name="Spencer Bellamy">[http://www.discogs.com/artist/Spencer+Bellamy Spencer Bellamy] . Retrieved on 2008-03-24</ref> Each single was released on his record label, 10/30 Uproar Records.<ref name="East Flatbush Project">[http://www.discogs.com/artist/East+Flatbush+Project East Flatbush Project] . Retrieved on 2008-03-24</ref> The East Flatbush Project has released an [[Extended play|EP]] in July 2008 titled "First Born" that is available online.

In 2011, [[DJ Premier]] used the "Tried by 12" instrumental as the beat for the Shady 2.0 [[Freestyle battle|cypher]] at the [[BET Hip Hop Awards#2011|2011 BET Hip Hop Awards]]. The cypher consisted of [[Eminem]], [[Shady Records]] artists [[Slaughterhouse (group)|Slaughterhouse]] (a hip hop collective of rappers [[Royce da 5'9"]], [[Joe Budden]], [[Joell Ortiz]] and [[Crooked I]]) and [[Yelawolf]] all [[Freestyle rap|freestyling]] over the beat. This cypher was critically acclaimed and became one of the most discussed events to take place at the awards.
