'''David Alex Villegas''' (born December 19, 1995),<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2019/01/skinnyfromthe9-fetty-wap-joint-project-on-the-way/|title=Skinnyfromthe9 and Fetty Wap Have a Joint Mixtape on the Way|first=Robby Seabrook|last=III|website=XXL Mag|access-date=February 21, 2019}}</ref> known by his stage name '''Skinnyfromthe9''' (formerly stylized '''$kinnyFromThe9'''), is an American [[Rapping|rapper]] and singer from [[Somerville, New Jersey|Somerville]], New Jersey. He first gained widespread attention after the release of his song "Love Blast" in late 2017. He then signed to [[L.A. Reid]]'s record label, HITCO, in 2018. He released his debut mixtape, ''It's An Evil World'', in November 2018.

==Early life==
David Alex Villegas<ref name="Complex">{{cite magazine | last=Espinoza| first=Joshua| title =Skinnyfromthe9 Arrested on Assault and Kidnapping Charges| magazine =Complex| date =August 11, 2018 | url =https://www.complex.com/music/2018/08/skinnyfromthe9-arrested-for-kidnapping-aggravated-assault| access-date =December 22, 2018}}</ref> was born in [[Hialeah, Florida]]. He is of mixed Puerto Rican, Colombian, and Dominican descent. He grew up largely in [[Somerville, New Jersey]], although he and his family moved around frequently. As a child, his father was imprisoned, and his mother became involved with another man. During Villegas' adolescence, his mother and stepfather engaged in scams and identity fraud until his stepfather was eventually caught and spent time in prison. Villegas also spent time living in homeless shelters and hotels as a youth. He began focusing on [[Hip hop music|hip hop]] as a teenager after his father purchased a mic for him.<ref name="XXLBreak">{{cite magazine | last=Coe| first=Kairi| title =The Break Presents: SkinnyFromthe9| magazine =XXL| date =March 23, 2018 | url =http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2018/03/skinnyfromthe9-interview-the-break/| access-date =December 22, 2018}}</ref><ref name="Noisey">{{cite news | last=Burney| first=Lawrence| title =Skinnyfromthe9's "Back When I Was Broke" Is an Ode to Perseverance| publisher =Noisey| date =May 4, 2018 | url =https://noisey.vice.com/en_us/article/zmgjva/skinnyfromthe9s-back-when-i-was-broke-is-an-ode-to-perseverance| access-date =December 22, 2018}}</ref> He adopted the stage name, Skinnyfromthe9, because his nickname was "Skinny" and he was from [[area code 908]] (colloquially, "the 9").<ref name="RespectInt">{{cite news | last=Powell| first=Jon| title =RESPECT. Interview: Skinnyfromthe9| publisher =RESPECT| date =June 13, 2018 | url =https://respect-mag.com/2018/06/respect-interview-skinnyfromthe9/| access-date =December 22, 2018}}</ref>

==Career==
Skinnyfromthe9 began putting his music on [[SoundCloud]] in 2016. In 2017, he released the songs, "Family Rich" featuring [[Jay Critch]]<ref name="XXLBreak"/> and "Count It" featuring [[Stitches (rapper)|Stitches]].<ref name="Woke">{{cite news | last=Dicello| first=Josh| title =Skinnyfromthe9 & Stitches drop visuals for their latest track ‘Count It’| publisher =Woke Online| date =November 4, 2017 | url =http://wokeonline.net/skinnyfromthe9-stitches-drop-visuals-for-their-latest-track-count-it/| access-date =December 22, 2018}}</ref> In late 2017, he released the single, "Love Blast,"<ref name="RespectLove">{{cite news | last=White| first=Omari| title =On The Radar: Skinnyfromthe9 – "Love Blast"| publisher =RESPECT| date =December 1, 2017 | url =https://respect-mag.com/2017/12/radar-skinnyfromthe9-love-blast/| access-date =December 22, 2018}}</ref> which would go on to accumulate more than 3.5&nbsp;million streams on SoundCloud.<ref name="NJAug">{{cite news | last=Gross| first=Paige| title =Rapper who toured with Fetty Wap arrested, facing kidnapping, assault charges| work =The Star-Ledger| date =August 20, 2018 | url =https://www.nj.com/somerset/index.ssf/2018/08/somerset_rapper_arrested_facing_kidnapping_and_ass.html| access-date =December 22, 2018}}</ref> In January 2018, Skinny joined [[Fetty Wap]] on his "FMF Tour."<ref name="XXLBreak"/> Later that year, it was announced that he had signed to L.A. Reid's new record label, HITCO.<ref name="Vlad">{{cite news | last=Todd| first=Jessica| title =EXCLUSIVE: Skinnyfromthe9 on $5M Deal with L.A. Reid, Pulling Up to Old School in Lambo| publisher =VladTV| date =July 12, 2018 | url =https://www.vladtv.com/article/247020/skinnyfromthe9-on-5m-deal-with-la-reid-pulling-up-to-old-school-in| access-date =December 22, 2018}}</ref><ref name="XXLJump">{{cite magazine | last=Seabrook III| first=Robby| title =Skinnyfromthe9 'Jump Out That' Video Featuring PnB Rock: Watch Them Hit the Open Road| magazine =XXL| date =August 3, 2018 | url =http://www.xxlmag.com/video/2018/08/skinnyfromthe9-jump-out-that-video-pnb-rock/| access-date =December 22, 2018}}</ref>

In April 2018, he released a music video for "Love Blast"<ref name="XXLApr">{{cite magazine | last=Mojica| first=Nick| title =Skinnyfromthe9 Lets 'Love Blast' in New Video| magazine =XXL| date =April 2, 2018 | url =http://www.xxlmag.com/video/2018/04/skinnyfromthe9-love-blast-video/| access-date =December 22, 2018}}</ref> and also released the single, "Back When I Was Broke."<ref name="Hype">{{cite news | last=Maduakolam| first=Emmanuel| title =SkinnyFromthe9 Drops 'Back When I Was Broke'| publisher =HypeBeast| date =April 17, 2018 | url =https://hypebeast.com/2018/4/skinnyfromthe9-back-when-i-was-broke| access-date =December 22, 2018}}</ref> The following month, he published music videos for "Back When I Was Broke"<ref name="Noisey"/> and "Pink Choppas."<ref name="XXLPink">{{cite magazine | last=Seabrook III| first=Robby| title =Skinnyfromthe9 Shows Off a New Droptop in 'Pink Choppas' Video| magazine =XXL| date =May 3, 2018 | url =http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2018/05/skinnyfromthe9-pink-choppas-video/| access-date =December 22, 2018}}</ref> Rappers [[Wiz Khalifa]] and [[Russ (rapper)|Russ]] made cameos in the former video.<ref name="XXLBack">{{cite magazine | last=India| first=Lindsey| title =Skinnyfromthe9 Surrounds Himself With Models and Mansions in 'Back When I Was Broke' Video| magazine =XXL| date =May 8, 2018 | url =http://www.xxlmag.com/video/2018/05/skinnyfromthe9-back-when-i-was-broke-video/| access-date =December 22, 2018}}</ref> Later that month, Skinny also released the songs, "Space" and "I Drip."<ref name="XXLSpace">{{cite magazine | last=Miller| first=Kai| title =Skinnyfromthe9 Drops New Videos for "Space" and "I Drip"| magazine =XXL| date =June 4, 2018 | url =http://www.xxlmag.com/video/2018/06/skinnyfromthe9-space-video-i-drip-video/| access-date =December 22, 2018}}</ref>

Beginning in June 2018, Skinny was supposed to join rapper, 24hrs, on his "3200 Lenox Rd." tour, but 24hrs was hospitalized in June, and the tour was canceled.<ref name="XXLSpace"/><ref name="HNHH24">{{cite news | last=A.| first=Aron| title =24Hrs Announces Social Media Hiatus After Being Admitted to the Hospital | publisher =HotNewHipHop| date =June 15, 2018 | url =https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/24hrs-announces-social-media-hiatus-following-hospitalization-news.52781.html| access-date =December 22, 2018}}</ref> In July 2018, Skinny made a cameo appearance in the video for [[PnB Rock]]'s "London."<ref name="XXLJul">{{cite magazine | last=Seabrook III| first=Robby| title =PnB Rock Throws a Wild Pool Party in New "London" Video| magazine =XXL| date =July 16, 2018 | url =http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2018/07/pnb-rock-london-video/| access-date =December 22, 2018}}</ref> The following month, he released the single, "Jump Out That," featuring PnB Rock.<ref name="XXLJump"/> Later in August, Skinny was arrested on suspicion of simple assault and theft.<ref name="MCJ01">{{cite news | last=Russell| first=Suzanne| title =Somerville rap artist 'skinnyfromthe9,' 3 others charged in kidnapping| newspaper =Bridgewater Courier News| date =August 10, 2018 | url =https://www.mycentraljersey.com/story/news/crime/2018/08/10/somerville-kidnapping-villegas-warren/961207002/| access-date =December 22, 2018}}</ref><ref name="MCJ03">{{cite news | last=Muscavage| first=Nick| title =Skinnyfromthe9, Somerville rapper, released from jail| newspaper =Bridgewater Courier News| date =September 27, 2018 | url =https://www.mycentraljersey.com/story/news/crime/2018/09/27/somerville-rap-artist-skinnyfromthe-9-accepts-plea-deal-kidnapping-case/1442750002/| access-date =December 22, 2018}}</ref>

In October 2018, while serving time in jail, he released a freestyle rap along with two more songs, "Alone" and "Songs About You."<ref name="XXLOct">{{cite magazine | last=Orcutt| first=KC| title =Skinnyfromthe9 Shares New Freestyle From Jail| magazine =XXL| date =October 12, 2018 | url =http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2018/10/skinnyfromthe9-freestyle-from-jail/| access-date =December 22, 2018}}</ref> He was released from jail in November 2018.<ref name="XXLRelease">{{cite magazine | last=Coe| first=Kairi| title =Skinnyfromthe9 Released From Jail, Thinks Overcoming Sentence Will Make Him an All-Time Great| magazine =XXL| date =November 5, 2018 | url =http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2018/11/skinnyfromthe9-jail-interview-all-time-great/| access-date =December 22, 2018}}</ref> Soon after his release, he published the music video for his song, "Just Left Jail."<ref name="Wave">{{cite news | last=Marsh| first=Ryan| title =Skinnyfromthe9 – Just Left Jail (Official Music Video)| publisher =Rap Wave| date =November 19, 2018 | url =https://www.rapwave.net/2018/11/19/skinnyfromthe9-just-left-jail-official-music-video/| access-date =December 22, 2018}}</ref> His debut mixtape, ''It's An Evil World'', was released on November 30, 2018, through HITCO. It featured guest appearances from Fetty Wap, PnB Rock, and [[Kap G]].<ref name="XXLEvil">{{cite magazine | last=Haffenden| first=Dayna| title =Skinnyfromthe9 ‘It's an Evil World’ Project Tracklist Features Fetty Wap, PnB Rock and More| magazine =XXL| date = November 14, 2018| url =http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2018/11/skinnyfromthe9-its-an-evil-world-project-tracklist/| access-date =December 22, 2018}}</ref><ref name="HNHHDec">{{cite news | last=A.| first=Aron| title =Skinnyfromthe9 Drops "It's A Evil World" Ft. PnB Rock, Fetty Wap & Kap G| publisher =HotNewHipHop| date =December 4, 2018 | url =https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/skinnyfromthe9-drops-its-a-evil-world-ft-pnb-rock-fetty-wap-and-kap-g-new-mixtape.118703.html| access-date =December 22, 2018}}</ref> going into 2020 skinnyfromthe9 did a song with [[Hitta Castro]] that blow up called "In My Feelings2.{{citation needed|date=October 2021}}

==Legal issues==
Since 2014, Skinnyfromthe9 has been charged with several minor offenses such as [[shoplifting]] and [[reckless driving]]. Many of those charges were later dropped.<ref name="NJJun">{{cite news | last=Tiamoyo Harris| first=Taylor| title =Rapper labeled a criminal by some, but millions are listening to his music| work =The Star-Ledger| date =June 19, 2018 | url =https://www.nj.com/somerset/index.ssf/2018/06/some_say_nj_rapper_is_a_terrorizer_but_skinnyfromt.html| access-date =December 22, 2018}}</ref>

In August 2018, Skinny, his father, his brother, and a fourth defendant were arrested and charged with [[kidnapping]] and aggravated assault in relation to an incident in [[Somerset County, New Jersey]] in which the four men were accused of luring another man into a vehicle and striking him.<ref name="MCJ01"/> In September, the charges for Skinny were downgraded to luring an adult, simple assault, and theft after he took a [[plea deal]] that also allowed him to avoid prison time in exchange for a [[probation]] sentence. Skinny has maintained his innocence, claiming that the person who brought charges against him stole a chain worth $8,000.<ref name="MCJ03"/><ref name="MCJ04">{{cite news | last=Muscavage| first=Nick| title =Skinnyfromthe9, Somerville rap artist, talks new album, Manville show| newspaper =Bridgewater Courier News| date =November 8, 2018 | url =https://www.mycentraljersey.com/story/news/local/people/2018/11/08/skinnyfromthe-9-new-album/1918454002/| access-date =December 22, 2018}}</ref>
