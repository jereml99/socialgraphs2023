'''Joell Christopher Ortiz''' (born July 6, 1980) is an American [[Rapping|rapper]] and a former member of the group [[Slaughterhouse (group)|Slaughterhouse]]. Ortiz grew up in the [[NYCHA|Cooper Park Houses]] in the [[East Williamsburg, Brooklyn|East Williamsburg]] section of Brooklyn, New York, formerly signed to [[Dr. Dre]]'s [[Aftermath Entertainment]] record label. He was featured in the Unsigned Hype column of the March 2004 issue of ''The Source'' Magazine and was also selected as Chairman's Choice in ''XXL'' Magazine. 

During the same time Joell also went on to win the 2004 EA Sports Battle which earned his song "Mean Business" a spot on the NBA Live 2005 soundtrack. The same year he was offered a contract to [[Jermaine Dupri]]'s [[So So Def]] label. He released his debut album ''[[The Brick: Bodega Chronicles]]'' in 2007. Since then, he has released ''[[Free Agent (album)|Free Agent]]'' (2011), ''[[House Slippers]]'' (2014) and ''Monday'' (2019).<ref>{{cite web |last1=Montes |first1=Patrick |title=Joell Ortiz Taps Big K.R.I.T. for "Learn You" & Announces New Album 'Monday' |url=https://hypebeast.com/2019/6/joell-ortiz-big-krit-learn-you-song-stream-monday-new-album |website=hypebeast}}</ref> He is currently one half of the rap duo Crook and Joell with his former Slaughterhouse bandmate, [[Crooked I]]. Together, they released three studio albums.<ref>{{cite web | url=https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/kxng-crooked-and-joell-ortiz-are-fielding-suggestions-for-the-name-of-their-duo-news.157503.html | title=KXNG Crooked & Joell Ortiz Are Fielding Suggestions for the Name of Their Duo | website=HNHH | date=September 7, 2022 }}</ref>

==Biography==
Joell Christopher Ortiz<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.ascap.com/repertory#ace/performer/JOELL%20ORTIZ|title=ACE Repertory: JOELL CHRISTOPHER ORTIZ|publisher=[[ASCAP]]|access-date=December 25, 2017}}</ref> was born to Puerto Rican parents in [[Brooklyn, New York]] on July 6, 1980, where he grew up in [[East Williamsburg, Brooklyn|East Williamsburg]]. He was formerly signed to [[Dr. Dre]]'s [[Aftermath Entertainment]] record label.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.latinpost.com/articles/21712/20140917/puerto-rican-slaughterhouse-rapper-joell-ortiz-incorporates-healthy-sober-lifestyle.htm |title=Puerto Rican Slaughterhouse Rapper Joell Ortiz Incorporates Healthy, Sober Lifestyle Into 'House Slippers' Album : Entertainment |publisher=Latin Post|date=September 17, 2014|access-date=July 14, 2015}}</ref><ref name="hiphopdx1">{{cite web|author=ahale|url=http://hiphopdx.com/news/id.6304/title.joell-ortiz-no-longer-with-aftermath |title=Joell Ortiz No Longer With Aftermath &#124; Get The Latest Hip Hop News, Rap News & Hip Hop Album Sales|publisher=HipHopDX|date=January 30, 2008|access-date=July 14, 2015}}</ref> 

He was featured in the Unsigned Hype column of the March 2004 issue of ''The Source'' Magazine and was also selected as Chairman's Choice in ''XXL'' Magazine.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://theboombox.com/happy-birthday-joell-ortiz|title=Happy Birthday, Joell Ortiz!|publisher=Theboombox.com|access-date=July 14, 2015}}</ref> During the same time Joell also went on to win the 2004 EA Sports Battle which earned his song "Mean Business" a spot on the NBA Live 2005 soundtrack.<ref>{{cite web|last=Chalifoux|first=Jordan|url=http://formatmag.com/features/joell-ortiz|title=Joell Ortiz|publisher=Formatmag.com|date=January 29, 2007|access-date=July 14, 2015}}</ref> 

The same year he was offered a contract to [[Jermaine Dupri]]'s [[So So Def]] label. The deal quickly went sour which caused Joell to start beef with Jermaine. Joell has since collaborated with [[KRS-One]] and [[Kool G. Rap]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.thestateofhiphop.com/joell-ortiz-interview.html|title=Joell Ortiz on Signing with Violator, Writing and More|publisher=Thestateofhiphop.com|access-date=July 14, 2015|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131211200855/http://www.thestateofhiphop.com/joell-ortiz-interview.html|archive-date=December 11, 2013|url-status=dead}}</ref>

Joell released his street album called ''[[The Brick: Bodega Chronicles]]'' April 24, 2007, on [[E1 Entertainment|Koch Records]]. The album was recorded while Joell was trying to get signed and going through some tough times personally.<ref>{{cite web|author=Staff|url=http://www.allmusic.com/album/the-brick-bodega-chronicles-mw0000776661|title=The Brick: Bodega Chronicles - Joell Ortiz|publisher=AllMusic|date=February 27, 2007|access-date=July 14, 2015}}</ref> 

Although he was signed to Aftermath Entertainment, Dr. Dre allowed him to release the street album on Koch Records.<ref name="hiphopdx1"/> ''The Brick'' features production by Showbiz, [[The Alchemist (producer)|The Alchemist]], [[Domingo (producer)|Domingo]], Ho Chi from Killahertz Productions, Lil' Fame of [[M.O.P.]], [[Novel (rapper)|Novel]], and Moss, among others.<ref name="hiphopdx1"/> Guest spots include [[Big Daddy Kane]], [[Styles P]], [[Big Noyd]], [[M.O.P]], [[Akon]], [[Immortal Technique]], [[Grafh]], [[Ras Kass]], Stimuli, and Novel.<ref name="hiphopdx1"/>

Ortiz is one quarter of the [[Supergroup (music)|supergroup]] Slaughterhouse who released their highly anticipated ''[[Slaughterhouse EP]]'' on February 8, 2011.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/slaughterhouse-ep/id414201733|title=Slaughterhouse - EP by Slaughterhouse|publisher=Itunes.apple.com|date=February 8, 2011|access-date=July 14, 2015}}</ref> The group released their second studio album, ''[[Welcome to: Our House]]'', on August 28, 2012.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/welcome-to-our-house-deluxe/id553933777|title=iTunes - Music - Welcome To: Our House (Deluxe Version) by Slaughterhouse|publisher=Itunes.apple.com|date=August 28, 2012|access-date=July 14, 2015}}</ref>

==Music career==
He was on Kool G Rap's 2002 album "The Giancana Story" on the song "It's Nothing"
===2007–2010: ''The Brick: Bodega Chronicles'' and joining Slaughterhouse===
Though signed to Aftermath, Ortiz released an album titled ''[[The Brick: Bodega Chronicles]]'' April 24, 2007 on [[Koch Records]].<ref name=autogenerated1>XXL Magazine. Joell Ortiz: A Hip-Hop Happy Ending by Brandon Edwards. November 2007</ref> ''The Brick'' features production by [[Showbiz and A.G.|Showbiz]], Street Radio, and [[The Alchemist (producer)|The Alchemist]], among others. Guest artists include rappers [[Big Daddy Kane]], [[Styles P]], [[Big Noyd]], [[Akon]], [[Immortal Technique]], [[Grafh]], and [[Ras Kass]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.allmusic.com/album/the-brick-bodega-chronicles-mw0000776661|title=The Brick: Bodega Chronicles|publisher=AllMusic|date=February 27, 2007|access-date=April 4, 2016}}</ref> Ortiz parted ways with [[Aftermath Entertainment]] on April 15, 2008.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.defsounds.com/news/Joell_ortiz_speaks_on_leaving_aftermath|title=Joell Ortiz Speaks On Leaving Aftermath|publisher=Defsounds.com|date=January 31, 2008|access-date=November 11, 2010|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090509162939/http://www.defsounds.com/news/Joell_ortiz_speaks_on_leaving_aftermath|archive-date=May 9, 2009|url-status=dead}}</ref> 

2009 was a busy year for Ortiz, as he released a number of freestyles and remixes leading up to the ''Road Kill'' mixtape.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://nahright.com/2009/12/15/joell-ortiz-frequency-road-kill-mixtape/ |title=Joell Ortiz & Frequency – Road Kill (Mixtape) |publisher=Nah Right |date=December 15, 2009 |access-date=April 4, 2016}}</ref> Among these was "Stressful" a song that remixed [[Drake (entertainer)|Drake's]] "Successful."<ref>{{cite web|last=Leps|first=Jeanne|url=http://uproxx.com/smokingsection/joell-ortiz-novel-stressful|title=Joell Ortiz & Novel – "Stressful"|publisher=Uproxx.com|access-date=April 4, 2016}}</ref>

[[Joe Budden]] reached out to [[Crooked I]], [[Royce da 5'9"]], Joell Ortiz, and Nino Bless for a track titled "Slaughterhouse" on his digital release, ''[[Halfway House (album)|Halfway House]]''.<ref name="xxlmag.com">{{cite web|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/online/?p=53509|title=FEATURE: Crooked I, The Freestyler[Definitive Dozen|publisher=Xxlmag.com|date=August 14, 2009|access-date=March 30, 2012|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20091223013748/http://www.xxlmag.com/online/?p=53509|archive-date=December 23, 2009}}</ref> 

Based on the reception of the track, they decided to form a super-group, minus Nino Bless, and named it after the first song they made together.<ref name="xxlmag.com"/> They released numerous songs throughout early 2009, building a buzz for their [[slaughterhouse (Slaughterhouse album)|self-titled album]] which was released through [[E1 Entertainment|E1]] on August 11, 2009.<ref>{{cite web|last=Jeffries|first=David|url={{Allmusic|class=album|id=r1596722|pure_url=yes}}|title=Slaughterhouse|publisher=AllMusic|date=August 11, 2009|access-date=November 11, 2010}}</ref> The album features production from [[Alchemist]], [[DJ Khalil]] and [[Mr. Porter]], plus guest appearances from [[Pharoahe Monch]], K-Young, and The New Royales.<ref>{{cite web|last=Kuperstein|first=Slava|url=http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/reviews/id.1237/title.slaughterhouse-self-titled|title=Read Hip Hop Reviews, Rap Reviews & Hip Hop Album Reviews|publisher=HipHopDX|date=August 10, 2009|access-date=November 11, 2010|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100723074102/http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/reviews/id.1237/title.slaughterhouse-self-titled|archive-date=July 23, 2010|url-status=dead}}</ref> In January 2011, the group signed to [[Shady Records]] and left [[E1 Entertainment]].<ref>{{cite web|author=mtv |url=http://www.mtv.com/news/1655811/eminem-signs-slaughterhouse-yelawolf-to-shady-records/ |title=Eminem Signs Slaughterhouse, Yelawolf To Shady Records |publisher=MTV |date=January 12, 2011 |access-date=July 14, 2015}}</ref>

===2010–2014: ''Free Agent'' and ''House Slippers''===
Ortiz started to have problems with [[E1 Entertainment]] and after almost a year, Ortiz left the label on November 5, 2010.<ref>{{cite web|last=Langhorne|first=Cyrus|url=http://www.sohh.com/2010/03/joell_ortiz_disses_slaughterhouses_recor.html|title=Joell Ortiz Disses Slaughterhouse's Record Label, "E1 Is F*cking C*ckblocking Us From Doing Something Monumental"|publisher=Sohh.Com|date=March 23, 2010|access-date=November 11, 2010|archive-date=April 17, 2014|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140417171042/http://www.sohh.com/2010/03/joell_ortiz_disses_slaughterhouses_recor.html|url-status=dead}}</ref> In August 2010, Ortiz was in talks, about signing a deal with [[Steve Rifkind]]'s label [[SRC Records]]. However the deal was never finished.<ref>{{cite web|author=ajacobs|url=http://hiphopdx.com/news/id.12093/title.steve-rifkind-hints-at-joell-ortiz-signing-to-src-records|title=Steve Rifkind Hints At Joell Ortiz Signing To SRC Records|publisher=HipHopDX|date=August 31, 2010|access-date=July 14, 2015}}</ref> 

In an October 31, 2010, interview on Conspiracy Worldwide Radio, Ortiz discussed his relationship with [[Eminem]] and the flood of record labels that have flocked to sign him after his ''Free Agent'' album was released. He also spoke of [[Eminem]]'s excitement at working with him.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://conspiracyworldwide.podomatic.com/entry/index/2010-10-30T08_58_34-07_00|title=Joell Ortiz discusses Eminem Oct 31st Uncensored Radio Interview|publisher=Conspiracyworldwide.podomatic.com|date=October 30, 2010|access-date=November 11, 2010}}</ref>

During [[Slaughterhouse (group)|Slaughterhouse]] 2012 tour, the group stopped in [[Las Vegas, Nevada]] at Bootleg Kev's radio show, where they talked about their album ''[[Welcome to: Our House]]'', and their work with [[Eminem]]. It was revealed that Ortiz signed with [[Shady Records]] as a solo act.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://rapradar.com/2012/04/18/bootleg-kev-interviews-slaughterhouse|title=Bootleg Kev Interviews Slaughterhouse - Rap Radar|work=Rap Radar|access-date=March 11, 2015}}</ref> In 2012, Joell as part of the group Slaughterhouse released their second album ''Welcome To Our House'' on August 28, 2012.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/news/id.20282/title.eminem-reveals-slaughterhouse-album-release-date-talks-beastie-boys/|title=Eminem Reveals Slaughterhouse Album Release Date & Talks Beastie Boys|author=HipHopDX|date=July 1, 2012|work=HipHopDX|access-date=March 11, 2015}}</ref> 

On September 16, 2014, Ortiz released his third studio album ''[[House Slippers]]''. The album features [[guest appearance]]s from, among others,  [[B.o.B]], [[Royce da 5'9"]], [[Joe Budden]], [[Crooked I]], and [[Maino (rapper)|Maino]]. The album was supported by the singles "House Slippers" and "Music Saved My Life".<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/album-reviews/id.2344/title.joell-ortiz-house-slippers|title=Joell Ortiz - House Slippers|work=HipHopDX|access-date=September 20, 2014|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140920194614/http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/album-reviews/id.2344/title.joell-ortiz-house-slippers|archive-date=September 20, 2014|url-status=dead}}</ref>

In early 2019, before the release of his ''Monday'' album, Ortiz was featured in the song "Revenge" (along with fellow [[Slaughterhouse (group)|Slaughterhouse]] member [[Crooked I]]) on Cryptik Soul’s album ''Killer's Blood''. Among the other artists on whose songs he has appeared as a featured guest are [[Brother Ali]], [[Mega Ran]], [[Mr. Capone-E]], Playboy Tre and Chan Hays.

==Controversy==
Following the release of his 2011 song "[[Big Pun]] Back", Ortiz received criticism from Liza Rios (Pun's widow), along with former [[Terror Squad (group)|Terror Squad]] members [[Cuban Link]] and [[Tony Sunshine]], claiming that the song was "disrespectful" to the late rapper's memory. Ortiz claims the song was meant to be a tribute, and later, rapper and friend of Big Pun [[Fat Joe]] spoke up in his defense.<ref>{{cite web|last=Horowitz|first=Stephen|title=Joell Ortiz Addresses "Big Pun's Back" Controversy|url=http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/news/id.15550/title.joell-ortiz-addresses-big-puns-back-controversy|work=Blog Article|publisher=Hip Hop Dx|access-date=October 19, 2011|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110902035600/http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/news/id.15550/title.joell-ortiz-addresses-big-puns-back-controversy|archive-date=September 2, 2011|url-status=dead}}</ref>
