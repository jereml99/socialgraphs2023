'''Cordae Amari Dunston''' (born August 26, 1997), known mononymously as '''Cordae''' (formerly '''YBN Cordae''' and '''Entendre'''), is an American rapper, singer, and songwriter. He gained popularity by releasing remixes of popular songs, such as "[[My Name Is]]" by [[Eminem]], and "Old Niggas", which was a response to the song "[[1985 (J. Cole song)|1985]]" by [[J. Cole]]. Videos to both remixes were uploaded to [[WorldStarHipHop]]'s [[YouTube]] channel, which led to a positive response from both mainstream media and the online rap community.<ref name="ComplexEverything">{{cite web|last1=McKinney|first1=Jessica|url=https://www.complex.com/music/2019/07/ybn-cordae-everything-you-need-to-know/|title=Everything You Need to Know About Cordae|work=[[Complex (magazine)|Complex]]|date=July 24, 2019|access-date=July 25, 2019}}</ref>

His debut studio album, ''[[The Lost Boy (album)|The Lost Boy]]'', was released in 2019 to wide acclaim; it received two nominations for [[Grammy Award for Best Rap Album|Best Rap Album]] and [[Grammy Award for Best Rap Song|Best Rap Song]] for the single "[[Bad Idea (YBN Cordae song)|Bad Idea]]" at the [[62nd Annual Grammy Awards|62nd Grammy Awards]].<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|url=https://www.grammy.com/grammys/artists/ybn-cordae|title=YBN Cordae Grammy Awards History|date=November 19, 2019|website=[[The Recording Academy]]|language=en|access-date=November 25, 2019}}</ref> Cordae was a member of the [[YBN (collective)|YBN]] collective from 2018 until he left the group in 2020 and dropped the "YBN" from his stage name.

==Early life==
Cordae was born in [[North Carolina]], then moved to [[Charles County, Maryland]], where he was mostly raised.<ref name="ComplexEverything"/><ref name="BillTables">{{cite magazine | last=Lamarre| first=Carl| title =How YBN Cordae Went From Waiting Tables To Hitting the Charts| magazine=Billboard | date =August 9, 2019 | url =https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/hip-hop/8526639/ybn-cordae-up-next-debut-album-interview| access-date =December 2, 2019}}</ref> As a young child, he took an interest in music after his father played [[classic hip hop]] artists such as [[Rakim]], [[Nas]], [[Big L]], and [[Talib Kweli]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://pigeonsandplanes.com/interviews/2018/05/ybn-cordae-interview|title=Bridging Rap's Generational Divide: An Interview With YBN Cordae|via=Pigeons & Planes|access-date=July 30, 2018}}</ref> He began writing raps around the age of fifteen. As he got older, he would do research on artists through YouTube, attempting to find music like his father played.<ref name="Open Space: YBN Cordae">{{cite web|url=https://youtube.com/AsNTQkKabpo?t=68|title=Open Space: YBN Cordae|via=Mass Appeal|access-date=July 30, 2018}}{{Dead link|date=June 2023 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}</ref> As Dunston grew older, he became more interested in pursuing a rap career. He prioritized his studies, but often became distracted by writing lyrics. He released three mixtapes as a teenager: "Entendre" ‚Äì Anxiety (2014), I'm So Anxious (2016), and I'm So Anonymous (2017).<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ok-tho.com/2014/06/entendre-anxiety-mixtape.html|title=Entendre ‚Äì Anxiety [Mixtape]|date=June 24, 2014 |via="OK-THO" Blog|access-date=July 30, 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://musiconthedot.com/entendre-im-anxious-mixtape/|title=Entendre ‚Äì I'm So Anxious (Mixtape)|date=August 8, 2016|via=Music On The Dot|access-date=July 30, 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/im-so-anonymous/1271420541|title=I'm so Anonymous|date=August 9, 2017|via=iTunes|access-date=July 30, 2018}}</ref> He graduated from high school in 2015 and decided to go to college at [[Towson University]]. He dropped out in 2018, explaining that college was "bigger than him" as a first-generation student, and he was mainly doing it for his mother. He moved to Los Angeles shortly afterward.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.elevatormag.com/ybn-cordae-is-fully-prepared-for-his-bright-future/|title=YBN CORDAE IS FULLY PREPARED FOR HIS BRIGHT FUTURE|via=Elevator Mag|access-date=July 30, 2018}}</ref>

==Career==
===2018‚Äì2019: Early beginnings, record deal, and ''The Lost Boy''===
Cordae began taking his rap career seriously in early 2018. When visiting Los Angeles, he hung around [[YBN Nahmir]] and YBN Almighty Jay , whom he'd met through social media. Nahmir had reached out to Cordae after hearing a song Dunston made with his brother Simba.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.elevatormag.com/ybn-cordae-met-nahmir-and-almighty-jay-through-xbox-live/|title=YBN CORDAE MET NAHMIR AND ALMIGHTY JAY THROUGH XBOX LIVE|via=Elevator Mag|access-date=July 30, 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.kanyetothe.com/forum/index.php?topic=7780865.18|title=YBN cordae is easily the best out of the group|date=May 13, 2018 |via=kanyetothe.com|access-date=July 30, 2018}}</ref> Dunston is the only member of YBN who hadn't been introduced through video games. He said he already felt like a part of YBN, and so changed from "Entendre" to "YBN Cordae". He also said that Entendre had been a name that he'd wanted to change for while: "I was just always around Nahmir and shit, those were just like my brothers... And, I was thinking, yo, I need to change my shit to like, Cordae, and shit, my actual first name. I was already "YBN" without the social tag".<ref name="Open Space: YBN Cordae"/>

In May 2018, Cordae released his first single, which he called his "introduction to the world": a remix of the 1999 song "[[My Name Is]]" by [[Eminem]]. He released the song along with a music video via [[WorldStarHipHop]] on YouTube. He then released "Old Niggas" as a response to "1985" by J. Cole, followed by the tracks "Fighting Temptations" and "[[Kung Fu (YBN Cordae song)|Kung Fu]]".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.z-pov.com/music/2018/5/19/ybn-cordae-the-outspoken-artist-declaring-the-new-generation-of-rap-has-promise|title=YBN Cordae, The Outspoken Artist Declaring The New Generation of Rap Has Promise|via=z-pov.com|access-date=July 30, 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.thefader.com/2018/05/17/the-10-best-new-rap-songs-right-now|title=The 10 best new rap songs right now|via=The Fader|access-date=July 30, 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://revolt.tv/stories/2018/07/11/ybn-cordae-releases-track-video-kung-fu-070080e9ff|title=YBN Cordae releases new track and video "Kung Fu"|via=Revolt TV|access-date=July 30, 2018|archive-date=August 14, 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180814002111/https://revolt.tv/stories/2018/07/11/ybn-cordae-releases-track-video-kung-fu-070080e9ff|url-status=dead}}</ref>

==Musical style and influences==
Cordae lists [[Nas]], [[Jay-Z]], [[Kid Cudi]], [[Kanye West]], [[Eminem]], [[Capital Steez]], [[Lil Wayne]], [[J. Cole]], [[Big L]], [[Travis Scott]], and [[Kendrick Lamar]] as some of his biggest influences.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://youtube.com/z6dbnZo47l8?t=1338|title=The YBN Cordae Interview|via=YouTube|access-date=July 30, 2018}}{{Dead link|date=June 2023 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}</ref> Shortly after the release of ''The Lost Boy'', he said his five favorite rappers were [[Jay-Z]], [[Nas]], [[2Pac]], [[The Notorious B.I.G.]], and [[Big L]]. He has called Nas' album ''[[It Was Written]]'' a big influence.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.complex.com/music/2019/07/ybn-cordae-lists-top-5-rappers|title=YBN Cordae Lists His Top 5 Rappers|website=Complex|language=en|access-date=August 29, 2019}}</ref> He has repeatedly said that, given his musical background and understanding of old school hip-hop and what hip-hop is evolving into, he can be the key in bridging the generational gap in hip-hop.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.elevatormag.com/ybn-cordae-is-bridging-the-generational-gap-in-hip-hop/|title= CORDAE IS BRIDGING HIP-HOP'S GENERATIONAL GAP|via=Elevator Mag|access-date=July 30, 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.vibe.com/2018/05/ybn-cordae-j-cole-1985-response-old-niggas/|title=YBN Cordae Attempts To Bridge Generational Gap In J. Cole Response Track, "Old Ni**as"|date=May 18, 2018|via=Vibe|access-date=July 30, 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://genius.com/a/ybn-cordae-breaks-down-old-n-ggas-on-genius-series-verified|title=YBN Cordae Breaks Down "Old N*ggas" On Genius' Series 'Verified'|via=Genius|access-date=July 30, 2018}}</ref>

''Respect Magazine'' opined in 2018 that Dunston was ahead of his time as a rapper. "He is a mix of the old and new school, and the deliverance and perception of his music sets himself apart from the new generation of rappers these days. We get a clear glimpse of this in his music videos for "Old N*ggas," 'My Name Is,' and 'Kung Fu,' which all have over 5 million views on YouTube. He is building a solid buzz for himself day by day, and letting the world know his bars and rhymes are ahead of his time. He has yet to drop a project proving such."<ref>{{cite web|url=https://respect-mag.com/2018/08/ahead-of-the-game-ybn-cordae/|title=Ahead Of The Game: Cordae|via=Respect Magazine|access-date=July 30, 2018}}</ref>

Revolt TV said in 2018, "There's always going to be artists who go against the grain and manage to generate conversation without being mundane or routine.  Cordae is the result of perfect timing, a need for change and managing to bridge the gap between aesthetic and talent. He's the Gohan of the YBN collective, appearing docile and juvenile but displaying a 'mastery' over words that seems at odds with old-school "hip-hoppers" belief about his age group. It is yet to be seen if his talent warrants the praise he's receiving."<ref>{{cite web|url=https://revolt.tv/stories/2018/05/31/industry-plant-conversation-longer-place-rap-music-070040ea8f|title=The 'industry plant' conversation no longer has a place in rap music|via=Revolt TV|access-date=July 30, 2018|archive-date=August 14, 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180814002107/https://revolt.tv/stories/2018/05/31/industry-plant-conversation-longer-place-rap-music-070040ea8f|url-status=dead}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Before leaving college, Cordae worked at a local [[T.G.I. Friday's]] in Maryland. He told [[Adam22]] of No Jumper that he "hated" it and that he always knew he was destined for more.<ref>{{cite web|title=Who is YBN Cordae? The rapper bridging 'old heads' with the new generation|url=https://kulturehub.com/who-is-ybn-cordae/|access-date=July 30, 2018|via=Kulture Hub}}</ref>

Cordae participated in a [[Black Lives Matter]] protest in 2016, rapping among the crowd about the struggles he faced, and the issues he'd been noticing in his community.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.reddit.com/r/hiphopheads/comments/8u7jli/ybn_cordae_at_dmv_protests_2_years_ago/|title=YBN Cordae at DMV Protests 2 years ago|date=June 27, 2018|via=Reddit|access-date=July 30, 2018}}</ref> In July 2020, Dunston was arrested at a [[Shooting of Breonna Taylor|Breonna Taylor]] protest in Louisville, Kentucky, along with American professional football player [[Kenny Stills]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=YBN Cordae and Kenny Stills Among Those Arrested at Breonna Taylor Protest in Louisville|url=https://www.complex.com/music/2020/07/ybn-cordae-kenny-stills-arrested-breonna-taylor-protest|access-date=July 15, 2020|website=Complex}}</ref> He wore a [[defund the police]] t-shirt while watching the [[2020 US Open ‚Äì Women's singles#Finals|2020 US Open women's singles final]].

He began dating tennis star [[Naomi Osaka]] in 2019, whom he met at a [[Los Angeles Clippers]] game.<ref>{{cite web| title=YBN Cordae Discusses Girlfriend Naomi Osaka's U.S. Open Success |url=https://www.complex.com/sports/2019/09/ybn-cordae-discusses-girlfriend-naomi-osakas-us-open-success| website= Complex|access-date=September 2, 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |title=Top 5 Photos 8/26: Osaka wishes Cordae a happy birthday |url=http://baseline.tennis.com/article/90278/top-5-photos-wta-atp |website=Baseline Tennis |access-date=September 2, 2020 | date = August 26, 2020}}</ref> On January 11, 2023, they announced that they are expecting their first child.<ref>{{Cite magazine |last=Dailey |first=Hannah |date=2023-01-11 |title=Cordae Is Expecting a Baby With Naomi Osaka |url=https://www.billboard.com/music/music-news/naomi-osaka-is-pregnant-expecting-child-with-cordae-1235197328/ |access-date=2023-01-11 |magazine=Billboard |language=en-US}}</ref>

On July 7, 2023, they welcomed their first child, a girl.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://people.com/naomi-osaka-boyfriend-cordae-welcome-first-baby-daughter-exclusive-7504607|title=Naomi Osaka Welcomes First Baby, a Girl, with Boyfriend Cordae (Exclusive)|publisher=People.com|language=EN|date=July 7, 2023|access-date=July 7, 2023}}</ref> On July 11, 2023, Cordae revealed the baby‚Äôs name, Shai, at a concert.
