'''Ackquille Jean Pollard''' (born August 4, 1994),<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2014/12/official-charges-against-bobby-shmurda-and-gs9/|title=Here Are The Official Charges Against Jamie Do And GS9|author=dan-rys|date=December 18, 2014|work=XXL Mag|access-date=January 30, 2016}}</ref> known professionally as '''Bobby Shmurda''', is an American rapper. Along with [[Rowdy Rebel]], Shmurda is considered a pioneer of [[Brooklyn drill]] music.<ref>{{Cite web|title=You're Not Paying Attention to New York's Most Exciting New Rap Scene|url=https://www.vice.com/en/article/zmxb93/youre-not-paying-attention-to-new-yorks-most-exciting-new-rap-scene|access-date=2020-10-21|website=www.vice.com|date=August 3, 2017 |language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|date=April 15, 2020|title=There's a New Hip-Hop Movement Brewing in New York, and Everybody Knows It|url=https://www.xxlmag.com/new-york-hip-hop-movement/|access-date=2020-10-22|website=XXL Mag|language=en}}</ref> He rose to international fame in 2014 when his song "[[Hot Nigga]]" peaked at number six on the [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]].<ref>{{cite magazine|title=Tomorrow's Hits: Bobby Shmurda, Priory & Anzel|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/6186195/tomorrows-hits-bobby-shmurda-priory-anzel|magazine=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|access-date=August 9, 2014|date=July 25, 2014}}</ref><ref name="US-100" />  The success of the single led to Shmurda signing a deal with [[Epic Records]]. His debut [[extended play|EP]], ''[[Shmurda She Wrote]]'', was released in November 2014.

In December 2014, [[New York City Police]] arrested Shmurda and charged him, along with several other members of [[GS9]], with [[conspiracy to murder]], weapons possession, and reckless endangerment.<ref name=WaPo /> In 2016, he pleaded guilty and was sentenced to seven years in prison, which was reduced to five years after receiving credit for the two years he already served awaiting trial.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.nydailynews.com/new-york/bobby-shmurda-cops-seven-year-plea-deal-gang-conspiracy-case-article-1.2784861|title=Bobby Shmurda cops seven-year plea deal in gang conspiracy, case|website=[[New York Daily News]] |date=September 9, 2016 |access-date=September 12, 2016}}</ref> After over six years in prison, Shmurda was released from [[Clinton Correctional Facility]] in February 2021.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Yoo|first=Noah|title=Bobby Shmurda Released From Prison|url=https://pitchfork.com/news/bobby-shmurda-released-from-prison/|access-date=2021-02-23|website=Pitchfork|date=February 23, 2021|language=en-us}}</ref> His release was widely celebrated across the hip-hop community.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Why 'Free Bobby Shmurda' Meant So Much to So Many People|url=https://www.complex.com/music/free-bobby-shmurda-campaign-essay|access-date=2021-02-23|website=Complex|language=en}}</ref>

== Early life ==
Ackquille Pollard was born in [[Miami]], [[Florida]].<ref name="dailybeast1">{{Cite news|url=https://www.thedailybeast.com/articles/2014/12/17/rapper-bobby-shmurda-arrested-at-new-york-s-notorious-quad-studios|title=Rapper Bobby Shmurda Arrested at New York's Notorious Quad Studios|last=Nestel|first=M. L.|date=2014-12-17|access-date=2020-01-01|language=en}}</ref> His mother is [[African-American]] and his father is [[Jamaica]]n.<ref>{{cite magazine |title=Premiere: Brooklyn's Last Badmon Bobby Shmurda Talks "Hot Nigga" Reggae Remix |url=https://www.thefader.com/2014/08/28/premiere-brooklyns-last-badmon-bobby-shmurda-talks-hot-nigga-reggae-remix |magazine=The Fader |location=United States |access-date=January 10, 2020 }}</ref>  His mother moved to [[East Flatbush, Brooklyn|East Flatbush]] from Florida after his father's incarceration.<ref name="complex1">{{cite web|last1=Ahmed|first1=Insanul|title=Meet Bobby Shmurda, the Brooklyn Rapper Everyone Is Talking About|url=http://www.complex.com/music/2014/07/interview-bobby-shmurda-brooklyn-rapper|website=[[Complex (magazine)|Complex]]|access-date=August 9, 2014|date=July 14, 2014}}</ref>
Pollard had run-ins with the law while living in Brooklyn, including fifteen months spent in detention for probation violation and being arrested on [[gun charges]] that were later dropped. According to his 2014 indictment, Pollard was the ringleader of a criminal enterprise called "GS9" that regularly entered into disputes with criminal gangs, was responsible for murders and non-fatal shootings, and engaged in [[Illegal drug trade|drug trafficking]]<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.nyc.gov/html/snp/downloads/pdf/gs-9-pr.pdf|title=15 members of GS 9 gang indicted and arrested: 210 firearms seized|date=December 18, 2014|website=nyc.gov}}</ref> along [[Kings Highway (Brooklyn)|Kings Highway]] to [[East Flatbush, Brooklyn|East Flatbush]].<ref>{{cite news | access-date=August 18, 2017 | url=https://www.theguardian.com/music/2015/dec/16/bobby-shmurda-brooklyn-rapper-jail-conspiracy-gun-epic-records | title=Why is Bobby Shmurda still in jail? | work=The Guardian | date=December 16, 2015 | last=Pearce | first=Sheldon}}</ref>

== Criminal conviction ==
On June 3, 2014, Shmurda was arrested and charged with felony criminal possession of a weapon. Police say they saw him flashing the gun in an apartment, and when they went to investigate he tried to hide it in a couch. He was set free on [[US$]]10,000 bail.<ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.contactmusic.com/story/rapper-bobby-shmurda-facing-gun-charge_4346237 | title=Bobby Shmurda – Rapper Bobby Shmurda Facing Gun Charge | date=August 29, 2014 | access-date=August 29, 2014 | website=Contactmusic.com}}</ref> On December 17, 2014, police arrested Shmurda and charged him with conspiracy, [[reckless endangerment]], and drug and gun possession; charges against the others included murder, attempted murder, assault, and drug dealing.<ref name=WaPo />  Shmurda pleaded not guilty and was held on $2 million [[bail]].<ref>{{Cite magazine|url=http://www.billboard.com/bobby-shmurda-jail-rikers-interview|title=Bobby Shmurda Speaks From Jail: 'People Are Trying to Take My Life Away for Being Blessed' (Exclusive)|magazine=Billboard|access-date=April 22, 2016|date=February 8, 2015}}</ref> Police said they had been investigating the gang for murder and shooting indiscriminately at crowds in public places long before Shmurda rose to fame. Police said Shmurda was "the driving force" in a gang also known as GS9 (standing for "Grimey Shooters," "Gun Squad" or "G Stone Crips."),<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.elitedaily.com/music/bobby-shmurda-schmoney-dance-gs9/706897|title=A 20-Year-Old Kid From Brooklyn Just Owned Hip-Hop This Summer, Here's How|website=Elite Daily|date=August 13, 2014 }}</ref> the name of his label, as they dealt [[crack cocaine]] and waged deadly battles with rival [[gangs in the United States|gangs]] for territory. He faced a maximum sentence of 8–25 years.<ref name="Yahoo News">{{cite news|url=https://news.yahoo.com/ny-rapper-bobby-shmurda-faces-gun-drug-charges-065210216.html|title=NYC Rapper pleads not guilty to gun, drug charges|date=December 18, 2014|publisher=Yahoo News|agency=Associated Press|last1=Hays|first1=Tom|access-date=December 19, 2014}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|date=2021-01-07|title=Bobby Shmurda will be eligible for prison release next month|url=https://happymag.tv/bobby-shmurda-denied-parole/|access-date=2021-01-07|website=Happy Mag|language=en-US}}</ref>

James Essig, head of the [[NYPD]] unit that made the arrests, said Shmurda's songs and videos were "almost like a real-life document of what they were doing on the street."<ref name="Yahoo News" /> In his song "[[Hot Nigga]]", Shmurda rapped that "I been selling crack since like the fifth grade" because "Jaja taught me"; Shmurda also rapped about his crew's past and future murders.<ref>{{cite web|title=Hot Nigga Lyrics|url=https://genius.com/3451538/Bobby-shmurda-hot-nigga/And-tell-my-niggas-shmurda-teaming-ho-mitch-caught-a-body-bout-a-week-ago-week-ago|website=Genius Lyrics|access-date=December 19, 2014}}</ref> Shmurda has asserted that the lyrics represent his real life in several interviews, though the [[Supreme Court of New Jersey]] recently ruled that lyrics cannot be read at trial as evidence unless they have a "strong nexus" to a specific crime.<ref name=WaPo>{{cite news|last1=Sullivan|first1=Gail|title=How rapper Bobby Shmurda, charged in murder, drug trafficking sting, became an alleged gangster|url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/news/morning-mix/wp/2014/12/19/hip-hops-bobby-shmurda-charged-in-murder-drug-trafficking-sting/|access-date=December 19, 2014|newspaper=[[The Washington Post]]|date=December 19, 2014}}</ref> During his jail time, Shmurda was involved in a fight suspected to be related to the [[Bloods]] vs. [[Crips]] rivalry.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.tmz.com/2015/05/16/bobby-shmurda-rapper-prison-gang-fight-guards/|title=Bobby Shmurda -- Knuckles Up for Crips vs. Bloods ... Jailhouse Edition|date=May 16, 2015|work=tmz.com|access-date=January 30, 2016}}</ref>

Between late June and early July 2015, the rapper and his ex-girlfriend were caught by officials after smuggling a knife into Rikers Island jail.<ref name="Vice">{{cite news|last1=Hill|first1=John|title=Bobby Shmurda Faces More Jail Time After Smuggling a Knife into Jail – Noisey|url=http://noisey.vice.com/blog/bobby-shmurda-knife-smuggle-charges-1|access-date=June 23, 2015|publisher=[[Vice (magazine)|Vice]]}}</ref> Both were charged with two counts of promoting contraband, in addition to one count of criminal possession of a weapon in the fourth degree, which could result in an additional seven-year sentence. Shmurda appeared in the Bronx Supreme Court to face his charges of prison contraband plus 25 years for his previous charge in late 2014. He and his ex-girlfriend pleaded not guilty to the contraband charge, but Shmurda was still facing a maximum sentence of 25 years for the drug and gun charges.<ref>{{cite news|title=Bobby Shmurda & Girlfriend Plead Not Guilty to Her Smuggling Knife Into Prison: Report – Billboard|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/6642034/bobby-shmurda-girlfriend-smuggling-knife-prison|date=July 24, 2015|access-date=November 12, 2015|magazine=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|publisher=Prometheus Global Media}}</ref><ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.hotnewhiphop.com/bobby-shmurda-denied-bail-yet-again-trial-set-for-2016-news.18027.html | title=Bobby Shmurda Denied Bail Yet Again, Trial Set For 2016 | work=HotNewHipHop | date=October 5, 2015 | access-date=January 30, 2016 | author=Schwartz, Danny}}</ref>

=== Conviction ===
On September 9, 2016, as part of a [[plea deal]], Shmurda pleaded guilty to one count of third-degree conspiracy and one count of weapons possession, and was sentenced to seven years in prison. Under the deal, Shmurda could not appeal the sentence, but was to be given credit for two years served, leaving five years left on his sentence, followed by five years of probation.<ref>{{Cite magazine|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/7503516/bobby-shmurda-accepts-plea-deal-serve-seven-years|title=Bobby Shmurda Accepts Plea Deal, Will Serve Seven Years|magazine=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] |access-date=September 20, 2016}}</ref> His lawyer, [[Alex Spiro]], expected that with good behavior Shmurda would only serve approximately three and a half years.<ref>{{cite news | access-date=September 20, 2016 | url=https://www.nytimes.com/2016/09/10/nyregion/rapper-bobby-shmurda-takes-7-year-plea-deal-in-gang-case.html | title=Rapper Bobby Shmurda Takes 7-Year Plea Deal in Gang Case | date=September 9, 2016 | last=Mckinley Jr.|first=James C.| newspaper=The New York Times}}</ref> In early 2017, Shmurda was given a four-year sentence for sneaking a makeshift knife ([[Shiv (weapon)|shiv]]) into his cell. The sentence was to run concurrently to his ongoing seven-year sentence, so that he would not face any additional prison time.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.tmz.com/2017/04/07/bobby-shmurda-sentencing-shank-prison/|title=Bobby Shmurda Sentenced For Prison Shank|date=April 7, 2017|website=tmz.com}}</ref>

=== Incarceration ===
Shmurda was first imprisoned in [[Rikers Island]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://hiphopdx.com/news/id.42243/title.bobby-shmurda-moves-prisons-after-someone-tries-to-stab-him|title=Bobby Shmurda Moves Prisons After Someone Tries To Stab Him|date=February 3, 2017|website=HipHopDX}}</ref> He was involved in numerous incidents while incarcerated. In May 2015, Shmurda took part in a brawl that included members from the Bloods and Crips street gangs.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.complex.com/music/2015/05/bobby-shmurda-gang-related-jail-fight|title=Bobby Shmurda Was Reportedly Involved in a Gang-Related Jail Fight|website=Complex|language=en|access-date=August 18, 2017}}</ref> In November 2016, Shmurda was involved in a brawl between several inmates that led to him losing phone privileges for an unnamed period of time and being subject to [[solitary confinement]] for a month.<ref name=":0">{{Cite news|url=http://www.bet.com/music/2017/04/03/bobby-shmurda-prison-job.html|title=Here's How Much Money Bobby Shmurda Is Reportedly Making With New Prison Job|work=BET.com|access-date=August 18, 2017}}</ref> In 2017, he was moved to [[Clinton Correctional Facility]] in upstate New York, to finish his sentence in [[protective custody]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://urbanislandz.com/2018/10/06/bobby-shmurda-looks-happy-prison-new-photo-family/|title=Bobby Shmurda Looks Happy In Prison In New Photo With Family|date=October 6, 2018|website=Urban Islandz}}</ref>

Images of Shmurda while incarcerated surfaced online after three years served of the five-year sentence.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.bet.com/music/2017/08/10/bobby-shmurda-prison-photos.html|title=Photos Released of Bobby Shmurda as He Approaches 3 Years Behind Bars|work=BET.com|access-date=August 18, 2017}}</ref>

=== Release ===

A hearing in the week of August 18, 2020 denied Shmurda parole.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Despite online rumors, Bobby Shmurda's parole hearing isn't until later this month|url=https://www.ajc.com/news/despite-online-rumors-bobby-shmurdas-parole-hearing-isnt-until-later-this-month/QXO3XEQR2BAZDGPQ25QSAMZRU4/|access-date=2020-08-12|website=ajc|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|last=|first=|date=|title=Bobby Shmurda wont get out of prison until 2021 He was Denied Parole|work=CelebrityInsider|url=https://celebrityinsider.org/boddy-shmurda-wont-get-out-of-prison-until-2021-he-was-denied-parole-438920/|access-date=September 21, 2020}}</ref>

Shmurda's conditional release date was set to December 11, 2020, after serving his full sentence, by his parole board.<ref>{{Cite news|last=|first=|date=|title=Bobby Shmurda jail photo surfaces ahead of 2020 release date|work=Monsters and Critics|url=https://www.monstersandcritics.com/celebrity/bobby-shmurda-jail-photo-surfaces-ahead-of-2020-release-date/#:~:text=When%20does%20Bobby%20Shmurda%20get%20out%20of%20prison%3F,new%20music%20before%20the%20end%20of%20the%20year|access-date=January 10, 2020}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|last=|first=|date=|title=Bobby Shmurda wont get out of prison until 2021 He was Denied Parole|work=CelebrityInsider|url=https://celebrityinsider.org/boddy-shmurda-wont-get-out-of-prison-until-2021-he-was-denied-parole-438920/|access-date=September 21, 2020}}</ref> However, after a review by the Department of Corrections, Shmurda's credit for good institutional behavior was restored, qualifying him for conditional release on February 23, 2021, with the rest of his sentence to be served on parole.<ref name="bobbyrelease1">{{Cite news|title=Bobby Shmurda Is Coming Home. What Happens Next?|url=https://www.npr.org/2021/02/22/969404434/bobby-shmurda-is-coming-home-what-happens-next|access-date=2021-02-23|newspaper=NPR.org}}</ref><ref name="bobbyrelease2">{{Cite news|last=|first=|date=January 4, 2021|title=Bobby Shmurda Eligible for Release From Prison in February|work=The New York Times|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2021/01/04/arts/music/bobby-shmurda-parole.html|access-date=February 1, 2021|last1=Coscarelli|first1=Joe}}</ref>

Shmurda was released on February 23, 2021 under supervision by fellow community members in [[Brooklyn|Kings County, New York]].<ref>{{Cite web|last=Trepany|first=Charles|title=Bobby Shmurda, viral 'Hot Boy,' 'Shmoney Dance' rapper, released from prison|url=https://www.usatoday.com/story/entertainment/celebrities/2021/02/22/bobby-shmurda-picked-up-prison-migos-rapper-quavo/4549877001/|access-date=2021-02-23|website=USA TODAY|language=en-US}}</ref> He will be under parole until the end of his full sentence on February 23, 2026.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Bobby Shmurda Will Enjoy Family Time, Back to Work After Prison Release|url=https://www.tmz.com/2021/02/22/bobby-shmurda-prison-release-plans-family-time/|access-date=2021-02-23|website=TMZ|language=en}}</ref> Rapper [[Quavo]] picked up Shmurda from prison on February 23, 2021, after stating in an interview with ''Billboard'' that he was "...personally (gonna go) pick up Bobby Shmurda."<ref>{{Cite magazine|title=Quavo Says He Will Pick Up Bobby Shmurda From Prison When Released: Exclusive|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/hip-hop/9529222/quavo-pick-up-bobby-shmurda-from-prison|access-date=2021-02-23|magazine=Billboard|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=Quavo Picked Up Bobby Shmurda With a Private Jet|url=https://www.yahoo.com/entertainment/quavo-picked-bobby-shmurda-private-165232766.html|access-date=2021-02-24|website=www.yahoo.com|date=February 23, 2021 |language=en-US}}</ref>
