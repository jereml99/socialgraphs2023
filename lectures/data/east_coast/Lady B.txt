'''Wendy Clark''' (born c. 1962), better known by her [[stage name]] '''Lady B''', is an American female [[Rapping|rapper]] and [[Radio personality|radio DJ]] from [[Philadelphia]], [[Pennsylvania]]. She is one of the earliest female rappers in [[hip hop]], and one of the first hip hop artists to record a [[Single (music)|single]], "To the Beat, Y'all", in 1979.<ref name="anthology">{{cite == Career ==
Clark recorded her first single, "To the Beat, Y'all," on the Sugar Hill record label in 1979.<ref name="Coard">{{Cite web|url=https://www.phillytrib.com/commentary/coard-treat-lady-b-like-the-philly-radio-icon-she/article_dbefe3ac-e3af-552b-bca5-0247a18ed4c0.html|title=Coard: Treat Lady B like the Philly radio icon she is|last=Coard|first=Michael|website=The Philadelphia Tribune|language=en|access-date=2019-05-02}}</ref>

Clark's rise and longevity (3 decades) in hip-hop earned her the title as Godmother of hip-hop. She is one of the first DJs to play rap records on the radio outside [[New York City|New York]],<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.philly.com/philly/entertainment/20110821_WRNB_s_Lady_B_celebrates_30_years_as_hip-hop_jump-starter.html|title=WRNB's Lady B celebrates 30 years as hip-hop jump-starter|last=Wellington|first=Elizabeth|date=2011-08-21|newspaper=[[The Philadelphia Inquirer]]|accessdate=2018-01-01}}</ref> playing artists such as [[Will Smith]] and [[Soulsonic Force]] at the start of their careers.<ref name="anthology" /><ref name="metro">{{cite web|url=https://www.metro.us/news/local-news/philadelphia/lady-bs-defenders-want-her-returned-to-the-airwaves|title=Lady B's defenders want her returned to the airwaves|last=Newhouse|first=Sam|date=December 20, 2017|publisher=Metro Media|accessdate=2018-01-01}}</ref> She had encountered the New York City rap scene while traveling with World B.Free, ex-player of the Philadelphia 76ers basketball team.<ref>{{Cite journal|last1=Phillips|first1=Layli|last2=Reddick-Morgan|first2=Kerri|last3=Stephens|first3=Dionne Patricia|date=July 2005|title=Oppositional Consciousness within an Oppositional Realm: The Case of Feminism and Womanism in Rap and Hip Hop, 1976-2004|journal=The Journal of African American History|volume=90|issue=3|pages=253–277|doi=10.1086/jaahv90n3p253|s2cid=140943451 |issn=1548-1867}}</ref>

In 1979, Mary Mason on WRNB 100.3 gave Lady B her own weekend show, which transformed into a success and brought hip hop to the radio in Philly.<ref name="Coard"/> and was a large success.

In 1984, Lady B moved to Philadelphia's [[WUSL|Power 99 FM]] and started the program ''The Street Beat,'' which blew the radio station's ratings through the roof.<ref name="Coard"/> She ran this program until 1989.<ref name="hiphop">{{cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=XkCncJ7j744C&pg=PA153|title=Hip Hop in America: A Regional Guide|publisher=Greenwood|year=2009|isbn=9780313343216|editor-last=Hess|editor-first=Mickey|volume=1|location=Santa Barbara, California|page=153}}</ref> She later broadcast for [[Sirius Satellite Radio]] in New York City.<ref name="hiphop" /> She also worked for [[WRNB|WRNB 100.3]] in Philadelphia until she was dismissed in December 2017.<ref name="metro" /><ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.phillytrib.com/news/listeners-protest-ouster-of-lady-b-from-wrnb/article_ba17cd8c-e654-11e7-b3e9-3b31bd9a94c5.html|title=Listeners protest ouster of Lady B from WRNB|last=Mitchell|first=John N.|date=December 22, 2017|newspaper=The Philadelphia Tribune}}</ref>

== Awards and recognitions ==
Lady B has received numerous awards throughout her career. In 2002, she received the "Philly Urban Legend Award",<ref name="anthology" /> which acknowledges pioneers in rap music. The World Renowned Entertainment,” Role Model of Excellence Award, two Lifetime Achievement Awards (including the Douglass”Jocko” Henderson award) and is also listed in VIBE magazine's History of Hip-Hop as “maybe the most influential female in hip- hop radio history”.<ref name=":0" />

On August 13, 2022 the city of Philadelphia renamed the 5700 block of Wyndale Avenue “Lady B Way” in recognition to her accomplishments.
