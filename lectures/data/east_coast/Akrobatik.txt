'''Jared K. Bridgeman''',<ref>{{cite web|last=Kergan|first=Wade|title=Akrobatik – Biography & History|url={{AllMusic|id=mn0000933487|tab=biography|pure_url=yes}}|access-date=July 9, 2017|work=[[AllMusic]]}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.ascap.com/repertory#/ace/search/workID/620534529 |title=BALANCE |website=ASCAP |publisher=American Society of Composers, Authors and Publishers |access-date=April 28, 2023}}</ref> (born May 3, 1974) better known by his stage name '''Akrobatik''', is an American rapper from the [[Dorchester, Boston|Dorchester]] neighborhood of [[Boston]], [[Massachusetts]].<ref name=rausch>{{cite book|first=Andrew J.|last=Rausch|title=I Am Hip-Hop: Conversations on the Music and Culture|publisher=[[Scarecrow Press]]|year=2011|pages=11–18}}</ref> He is also a part of the hip hop collective named [[The Perceptionists]] with [[Mr. Lif]] and [[DJ Fakts One]], which released ''[[Black Dialogue]]'' in 2005.<ref name=rausch/><ref>{{cite web|url=http://hiphopdx.com/news/id.43613/title.is-new-music-from-mr-lif-akrobatik-of-the-perceptionists-on-the-way|title=Is New Music From Mr. Lif & Akrobatik Of The Perceptionists On The Way?|work=[[HipHopDX]]|first=Kyle|last=Eustice|date=May 30, 2017|access-date=July 9, 2017}}</ref>

== Career ==
In 1998, Akrobatik released his first single, "Ruff Enough", on Boston's Detonator Records label. It was followed by "Internet MCs" and "Say Yes Say Word".<ref name=rausch/>

His music has appeared on television series such as HBO's ''[[The Wire]]'' and ESPN's ''[[Playmakers]]'', in films such as ''[[Date Movie]]'' and ''[[Wholetrain]]'', and in video games such as ''[[NBA Live 06|NBA Live '06]]'', ''[[Amplitude (2003 video game)|Amplitude]]'', ''[[Frequency (video game)|Frequency]]'', [[EA Sports UFC]], and ''[[Need for Speed: Most Wanted (2005 video game)|Need for Speed: Most Wanted]]''.<ref>{{cite web|date=13 February 2008|title=Akrobatik – Biography|url=http://www.britishhiphop.co.uk/features/articles/akrobatik_-_biography.html|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160304050110/http://www.britishhiphop.co.uk/features/articles/akrobatik_-_biography.html|archive-date=March 4, 2016|work=British Hip Hop.co.uk}}</ref>

In 2003, Akrobatik released an album, ''[[Balance (Akrobatik album)|Balance]]'', on Coup D'état.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://pitchfork.com/reviews/albums/90-balance/|title=Akrobatik: Balance|work=[[Pitchfork (website)|Pitchfork]]|first=Julianne|last=Shepherd|date=May 15, 2003|access-date=July 9, 2017}}</ref> It ranked at number 4 on [[CMJ]]'s "Hip-Hop 2003" chart.<ref>{{cite journal|title=Hip-Hop 2003 (Covering 1/7/2003 to 12/9/2003)|journal=[[CMJ New Music Report]]|date=December 29, 2003|page=20}}</ref>

In December 2005, Akrobatik signed to Fat Beats Records.<ref>{{cite web|last=Strock|first=Owen|date=2005-12-23|title=CMJ News Story|url=http://prod1.cmj.com/articles/display_article.php?id=7047258|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20070927221540/http://prod1.cmj.com/articles/display_article.php?id=7047258|archive-date=2007-09-27|access-date=2007-06-29|work=[[CMJ]]}}</ref> The Brooklyn-based label released his second studio album, ''[[Absolute Value (album)|Absolute Value]]'', in 2008.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://thephoenix.com/boston/music/121899-akrobatik-down-but-not-out/|title=Akrobatik: Down But Not Out|work=[[The Phoenix (newspaper)|The Phoenix]]|first=Chris|last=Faraone|date=June 8, 2011}}</ref> It received positive reviews from [[AllMusic]],<ref>{{cite web|last=Brown|first=Marisa|title=Absolute Value – Akrobatik|url={{AllMusic|class=album|tab=mw0000500327|pure_url=yes}}|access-date=July 9, 2017|work=[[AllMusic]]}}</ref> ''[[HipHopDX]]'',<ref>{{cite web|url=http://hiphopdx.com/reviews/id.915/title.akrobatik-absolute-value|title=Absolute Value – Akrobatik|work=[[HipHopDX]]|author=J-23|date=February 20, 2008|access-date=July 9, 2017}}</ref> [[Okayplayer]],<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.okayplayer.com/reviews/akrobatik-200802275516.html|title=Akrobatik|work=[[Okayplayer]]|first=Ginger|last=Lynn|date=February 27, 2008|access-date=July 9, 2017}}</ref> and ''[[PopMatters]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.popmatters.com/review/akrobatik-absolute-value/|title=Akrobatik: Absolute Value|work=[[PopMatters]]|first=Andrew|last=Martin|date=March 2, 2008|access-date=July 9, 2017}}</ref>

In 2011, Akrobatik suffered a ruptured heart valve and was rushed to [[Massachusetts General Hospital]], where he underwent an emergency valve replacement surgery.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://archive.boston.com/ae/music/articles/2011/06/10/local_rap_community_rallies_around_akrobatik_after_his_heart_scare/?page=full|title=Feeling the beat: Local rap community rallies around Akrobatik after his heart scare|work=[[The Boston Globe]]|first=Martín|last=Caballero|date=June 10, 2011|access-date=July 29, 2017}}</ref> In 2014, he released an album, ''Built to Last'', which was listed by ''[[Spin (magazine)|Spin]]'' as one of the 40 best hip-hop albums of the year.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.spin.com/2014/12/40-best-hip-hop-albums-2014/akrobatik-built-to-last/|title=Akrobatik, Built To Last (Self-Released)|work=[[Spin (magazine)|Spin]]|first=Jason|last=Gubbels|date=December 12, 2014|access-date=July 9, 2017}}</ref>
