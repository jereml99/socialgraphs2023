'''Tyler-Justin Anthony Sharpe''' (born August 26, 2002), known professionally as '''Lil Tecca''', is an American rapper and singer.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|url=https://pitchfork.com/levels/lil-tecca-ransom-new-song-listen/|title=The Ones: Lil Tecca's "Ransom"|website=Pitchfork|date=May 23, 2019|access-date=July 5, 2019}}</ref> He rose to mainstream fame with the release of his 2019 single "[[Ransom (Lil Tecca song)|Ransom]]", which peaked at number 4 on the [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://genius.com/videos/Who-is-lil-tecca|title=Behind The Rise Of Lil Tecca|website=Genius|access-date=July 5, 2019}}</ref><ref>[https://www.billboard.com/charts/hot-100 Billboard Hot 100 Charts]</ref> The single was included on his debut mixtape ''[[We Love You Tecca]]'' (2019) which peaked at number 4 on the US [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]].<ref name="bb200">{{cite magazine|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/business/chart-beat/8529417/tools-fear-inoculum-debuts-at-no-1-on-billboard-200-chart-rock-album|title=Tool's 'Fear Inoculum' Debuts at No. 1 on Billboard 200 Chart With Biggest Week For a Rock Album in Over 1 Year|magazine=Billboard|last=Caulfield|first=Keith|date=September 8, 2019|access-date=September 9, 2019}}</ref> The mixtape also included the singles "[[Love Me (Lil Tecca song)|Love Me]]" and "[[Did It Again (Lil Tecca song)|Did It Again]]".<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.complex.com/music/2019/06/lil-tecca-interview-ransom|title=Lil Tecca Was a 16-Year-Old Rapper Making Hits on the Weekends|website=Complex|access-date=July 5, 2019}}</ref> His debut studio album, ''[[Virgo World]]'' (2020), debuted at number 10 on the ''Billboard'' 200 and included the ''Billboard'' Hot 100-charting songs "[[Dolly (song)|Dolly]]" and "When You Down". His second mixtape, ''[[We Love You Tecca 2]]'', was released in August 2021 which included "[[Repeat It]]" and "Lot of Me".

== Early life ==
Tyler-Justin Anthony Sharpe was born on August 26, 2002, in the borough of [[Queens]] New York City to [[Jamaican Americans|Jamaican immigrants]]. He was raised in the [[Springfield Gardens]] neighborhood of Queens,<ref>Skelton, Eric. [https://www.complex.com/music/2019/06/lil-tecca-interview-ransom "Lil Tecca Is a 16-Year-Old Rapper Making Hits on the Weekends"], [[Complex (website)]], June 6, 2019. Accessed September 26, 2020. "[Q] You grew up in Queens, right? [A] Yeah, I grew up in Springfield Gardens. Then in seventh grade I moved to Nassau County, Long Island."</ref> but later relocated to [[Cedarhurst, New York|Cedarhurst]] on [[Long Island]].<ref name=Medium>Koscik, Danielle. [https://medium.com/@dkoscik1/rapper-lil-teccas-transition-from-high-school-student-to-chart-topping-artist-16483cf94d90 "Rapper Lil Tecca's Transition from High school Student to Chart Topping Artist"], [[Medium (website)]], November 21, 2019. Accessed September 26, 2020. "Tyler-Justin Anthony Sharpe grew up in Cedarhurst, Long Island right around the corner from the high school he would attend for 3 years before making it big. While at Lawrence High school he would tend to stay in the background before people worldwide knew his name."</ref> From a young age, Sharpe had aspirations of being in the [[NBA]], but around the age of 12, he turned his attention towards pursuing a career in music.<ref>[https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Hw8EuoYLzTM Lil Tecca Interview with "Power 105.1"]</ref> He attended [[Lawrence High School (Cedarhurst, New York)|Lawrence High School]].<ref name=Medium />

== Career ==
When Tecca was nine years old, he catered to his interest in music by rapping with his friends over his mic on his [[Xbox]]. They would make diss tracks directed toward each other, two of which were uploaded to [[SoundCloud]] years ago, but has since been taken down.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://power1051.iheart.com/content/2019-03-31-lil-tecca-talks-balancing-musicschool-whos-repping-the-youth-in-hip-hop/|title=Lil Tecca Talks Balancing Music/School, Who's Repping The Youth In Hip-Hop|website=[[WWPR-FM|Power 105.1]]|date=March 31, 2019|access-date=November 8, 2019}}</ref> At first, he did not have intentions to start a musical career and instead had dreams of being an [[National Basketball Association|NBA]] star. However, in his middle school years, he lost interest in basketball and considered taking music more seriously.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://power1051.iheart.com/content/2019-03-31-lil-tecca-talks-balancing-musicschool-whos-repping-the-youth-in-hip-hop/|title=Lil Tecca Talks Balancing Music/School, Who's Repping The Youth In Hip-Hop|website=Power 105.1 FM|access-date=July 5, 2019}}</ref>

His first track to gain some popularity, "Tectri", was made in collaboration with his friend, Lil Gummybear, and was released in early 2017. Other preliminary tracks of his include "Callin".<ref>[https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ANbr2thjjvw Lil Tecca – Callin (Original Song)]</ref> Just a year later, Tecca's career began to take off with his hit single, "[[Ransom (Lil Tecca song)|Ransom]]", which was originally uploaded on [[Cole Bennett]]'s Lyrical Lemonade [[YouTube]] channel on May 22, 2019. The song peaked at number 4 on the [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]] chart{{Citation needed|date=February 2023}} and has received over 1 billion plays on [[Spotify]]<ref>{{Cite web|title=Ransom |date=August 30, 2019|url=https://open.spotify.com/track/1lOe9qE0vR9zwWQAOk6CoO |website=Spotify |language=en|access-date=March 26, 2021}}</ref>{{Failed verification|date=February 2023}} and over 400 million views on YouTube.<ref>{{cite web |title=Lil Tecca - Ransom (Directed by Cole Bennett) |author=Lyrical Lemonade |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1XzY2ij_vL4 |website=YouTube}}</ref>

On August 30, 2019, Tecca released his debut mixtape, titled ''[[We Love You Tecca]]''.<ref name="No Jumper">{{cite web |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4-WqxeuE2d8 |title=The Lil Tecca "No Jumper" Interview |website=YouTube}}</ref> The mixtape peaked at number 4 on the US [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]].<ref name="bb200" />

After the release of multiple singles in 2020, Tecca released his debut album, ''[[Virgo World]]'', on September 18, 2020.<ref name="Complex-VWrelease">{{cite web|url=https://amp.www.complex.com/music/2020/09/lil-tecca-virgo-world-album |title=Stream Lil Tecca's Album 'Virgo World' f/ Lil Uzi Vert, Lil Durk, Nav, and More |work=[[Complex (magazine)|Complex]] |last=Mahadevan |first=Tara C. |date=September 18, 2020|access-date=September 20, 2020}}</ref>

On April 6, 2021, Tecca released the single "[[Show Me Up]]". On May 6, 2021, he released the single "[[Never Left (song)|Never Left]]", which peaked at number 54 on the US ''Billboard'' Hot 100.<ref name="Hot 100">{{cite magazine|url=https://www.billboard.com/artist/lil-tecca/chart-history/hsi/|title=Lil Tecca Chart History: Hot 100|magazine=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|access-date=March 26, 2020}}</ref>

On August 6, 2021, Tecca released the single "[[Repeat It]]" with American rapper [[Gunna (rapper)|Gunna]]. On August 27, 2021, Tecca released his second studio album, ''[[We Love You Tecca 2]]'', the sequel to his debut mixtape, ''[[We Love You Tecca]]''. He later went on the Tecca Loves You Tour with [[tana (rapper)|tana]], [[Bktherula]], and [[yvngxchris]].{{Citation needed|date=February 2023}}

In 2022, Tecca released a collaboration "[[Let Love Go]]" with English singer Mabel as part of Mabel's second studio album ''[[About Last Night... (album)|About Last Night]]''.{{Citation needed|date=February 2023}}

Tecca is a co-owner of Galactic Records. Galactic's roster of artists consists of Tecca himself, [[Baby Santana|tana]], Tokin, and WAV.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Galactic |url=https://galacticrecords.com/ |access-date=May 26, 2022 |website=galacticrecords.com}}</ref>
