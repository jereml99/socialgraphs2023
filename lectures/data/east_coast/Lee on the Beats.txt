'''Anthony Leedel Norris''' (born February 11, 1990) is an American [[hip hop music|hip hop]] [[record producer]] and [[songwriter]], professionally known as '''Lee on the Beats'''. Lee has produced for artists such as [[Lil Wayne]], [[DJ Khaled]], [[Kid Ink]], [[Ace Hood]], [[French Montana]], [[Rick Ross]], [[Meek Mill]] and [[Freeway (rapper)|Freeway]], among others.<ref name="complex1">{{cite web|url=http://www.complex.com/music/2012/10/25-new-producers-to-watch-out-for/anthony-lee |title=Anthony Lee — 25 New Producers To Watch Out For |publisher=Complex |date= |accessdate=2013-11-07}}</ref> In 2013, Lee was signed to [[DJ Khaled]]'s [[We the Best Music Group]]. He is best known for producing French Montana’s “[[Pop That]]” and co-producing Khaled’s “[[Do You Mind (DJ Khaled song)|Do You Mind]]”.

== Career ==
As a teenager Lee on the Beats started [[rapping]] and begun [[Hip hop production|producing]] for local acts in [[Queens, New York]].<ref name="bestofbothoffices1"/> His family was more involved in the business and executive side of things. For example, His uncle, Skane Dolla was [[DJ Clue]]'s manager as well as one of the owners of [[Desert Storm Records]]. He helped A&R projects for artists like [[Ludacris]], [[Method Man]], [[DMX (rapper)|DMX]], [[Fabolous]], and [[Joe Budden]] for [[Def Jam Recordings|Def Jam]]. Lee was around for all of it, so he learned a lot growing up. Lee started a team called, The Amazinz with his cousin Roc Da Producer. From there they started looking for producers they could build the team. The current members are Roc, Dane, Jabarrie, Murrille, Mae N. Maejor, Robbin, and LDB.{{cn|date=June 2023}}

Lee kn the Beats early major production credits included [[Kid Ink]]'s "Is It You" and co-production on [[Freeway (rapper)|Freeway]]'s "Superstar" featuring [[Meek Mill]].<ref name="complex1"/> In 2012, it only took Lee On the Beats three hours to create the beat for "[[Pop That]]", which he then gave to New York rapper [[Chinx Drugz]], who then passed it on to [[French Montana]].<ref name="rapradar1">{{cite web|author=B.Dot |url=http://rapradar.com/2012/07/07/lee-beats-on-french-montana-pop-that/ |title=Lee Beats On French Montana "Pop That" |publisher=Rap Radar |date=2012-07-07 |accessdate=2013-11-07}}</ref> The [[2 Live Crew]] sampling song, that would feature rappers [[Lil Wayne]], [[Drake (rapper)|Drake]] and [[Rick Ross]], became the [[lead single]] from Montana's debut studio album ''[[Excuse My French (album)|Excuse My French]]''.<ref name="complex1"/> The song would end up peaking at number 36 on the [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]], and would be shortly certified [[RIAA certification|Platinum]] by the [[RIAA]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.riaa.com/goldandplatinumdata.php?artist=%22Pop+That%22 |title=Gold & Platinum Searchable Database - November 07, 2013 |publisher=RIAA |date= |accessdate=2013-11-07}}</ref>

In March 2013, [[DJ Khaled]] announced that he had signed Lee On the Beats, to his [[record label]] [[We the Best Music Group]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2013/03/dj-khaled-speaks-on-signing-pop-that-producer-lee-on-the-beats/ |title=DJ Khaled Speaks On Signing "Pop That" Producer Lee on the Beats - XXL |publisher=Xxlmag.com |date=2013-03-15 |accessdate=2013-11-07}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|last=Horowitz |first=Steven J. |url=http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/news/id.23203/title.dj-khaled-signs-lee-beats-to-we-the-best-imprint |title=DJ Khaled Signs Lee Beats To We The Best Imprint &#124; Get The Latest Hip Hop News, Rap News & Hip Hop Album Sales |publisher=HipHop DX |date=2013-03-14 |accessdate=2013-11-07}}</ref> Following his signing, he produced two song's on [[Ace Hood]]'s fourth studio album ''[[Trials & Tribulations (Ace Hood album)|Trials & Tribulations]]'' including the single "[[We Outchea]]". In July 2013, Lee On the Beats was named one of the "top ten young producers on the rise", by [[Black Entertainment Television|BET]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.bet.com/music/photos/2013/07/10-young-producers-on-the-rise.html#!073113-music-producer-anthony-lee-lee-on-the-beats |title=10 Young Producers On the Rise &#124; Music |publisher=BET |date= |accessdate=2013-11-07}}</ref> On August 2, 2013, DJ Khaled released "[[I Wanna Be With You (DJ Khaled song)|I Wanna Be With You]]", the second single from his album ''[[Suffering from Success]]'', which was produced by Lee On the Beats and featured Rick Ross, [[Future (rapper)|Future]] and [[Nicki Minaj]].<ref name="rapradar2">{{cite web|author=B.Dot |url=http://rapradar.com/2013/10/10/lee-beats-on-dj-khaled-i-wanna-be-with-you/ |title=Lee Beats On DJ Khaled "I Wanna Be With You" |publisher=Rap Radar |date=2013-10-10 |accessdate=2013-11-07}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.rap-up.com/2013/07/29/new-music-dj-khaled-f-nicki-minaj-future-rick-ross-i-wanna-be-with-you/ |title=New Music: DJ Khaled f/ Nicki Minaj, Future, & Rick Ross – 'I Wanna Be With You' |publisher=Rap-Up.com |date=2013-07-29 |accessdate=2013-11-07}}</ref> He also co-produced two more songs on the album, "You Don't Want These Problems" and "I'm Still".<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.rap-up.com/2013/10/15/new-music-dj-khaled-f-chris-brown-wiz-khalifa-ace-hood-wale-im-still/ |title=New Music: DJ Khaled f/ Chris Brown, Wiz Khalifa, Ace Hood, & Wale – 'I'm Still' |publisher=Rap-Up.com |date=2013-10-15 |accessdate=2013-11-07}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://hiphopwired.com/2013/10/16/dj-khaled-ft-many-mcs-dont-want-problems-listen/ |title=DJ Khaled ft. Too Many MCs – "You Don't Want These Problems" [LISTEN&#93; |publisher=Hiphopwired.com |date=2013-10-16 |accessdate=2013-11-07}}</ref> In October 2013, Lee revealed that he would release an instrumental project in 2014.<ref name="rapradar2"/> The instrumental mixtape titled ''Catch the Vibe'' was released on February 11, 2014.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.datpiff.com/Lee-On-The-Beats-Catch-The-Vibe-Instrumentals-mixtape.566089.html|title = Catch the Vibe (Instrumentals) Mixtape by Lee on the Beats Hosted by Got Instrumentals & We the Best Music Group}}</ref>

== Production style ==
Lee on the Beats has said that his production style is influenced by [[IC3T3K 2K]], [[Pharrell Williams]], [[Ryan Leslie]], and [[Swiss Beatz]].<ref name="rapradar1"/>
