'''Richard Anthony Simpson''' (born May 28, 1968), also known as '''Chubb Rock''', is an American [[rapper]] who released several successful [[hip hop music|hip hop]] albums in the late 1980s and early 1990s.<ref name="Larkin90"/> A [[National Merit Scholar]], Chubb Rock dropped out of [[Brown University]] to pursue his musical career.<ref>[https://web.archive.org/web/20071017175728/http://blackamericaweb.com/site.aspx/entertainment/blackmusicmonth/updates605 Where Are They Now? An Update on Ex-Music Stars Chubb Rock, Ray Parker Jr. and Others], archived copy of Blackamericaweb.com</ref>

== 1990s and earlier ==
Discovered and produced by his first cousin DJ-Producer [[Howie Tee]], Chubb Rock first appeared on the national scene with his 1988 self-titled debut ''Chubb Rock'' and 1989's ''[[And the Winner Is... (Chubb Rock album)|And the Winner is...]]''<ref name="Larkin90">{{cite book|title=[[Encyclopedia of Popular Music|The Virgin Encyclopedia of Nineties Music]]|editor=Colin Larkin|editor-link=Colin Larkin (writer)|publisher=[[Virgin Books]]|date=2000|edition=First|isbn=0-7535-0427-8|page=88}}</ref> The latter produced the minor hit "Ya Bad Chubbs" which garnered air play on [[Yo! MTV Raps]] during that time.

His 1990 release entitled ''[[The One (Chubb Rock album)|The One]]'' reached No. 13 on Billboard's Top Hip-Hop/R&B chart for that year. Three singles from that release, "[[Treat 'Em Right]]", "Just The Two of Us" and "The Chubbster", made it to No. 1 on [[Billboard (magazine)|''Billboard'']]'s Top Rap Singles chart list for the same year.<ref name="Larkin90"/>

The following year saw the release of ''I Gotta Get Mine, Yo'', a release that featured guest performances from [[Grand Puba Maxwell]] and [[Trackmasters|Poke]]. This release also helped fledgling music producers [[Trackmasters]], on their rise to prominence, as the group handled production duties on the recording.<ref name="Larkin90"/> Chubb Rock also made an appearance on [[MC Serch]]'s 1992 song "Back to the Grill."

Chubb Rock was a member of the 1995 incarnation of the [[Crooklyn Dodgers]], a hip hop act that also featured [[O.C. (rapper)|OC]] and [[Jeru the Damaja]]. His [[backup dancer]]s started another group, A.T.E.E.M, which released its debut, ''A Hero Ain't Nothin' But A Sandwich'', in 1992 on [[Select Records]].

In 1996, he appeared on the [[Red Hot Organization]]'s compilation album ''[[America is Dying Slowly]]'', alongside [[Wu-Tang Clan]], [[Coolio]], and [[Fat Joe]], among others.  The album, meant to raise awareness of the [[AIDS]] epidemic among African American men, was heralded as a masterpiece by ''[[The Source (magazine)|The Source]]'' magazine.

==2000s and on==
Chubb Rock's work in the 2000s has been limited to only a few songs. He appears on a song for Raptivism Records' "No More Prisons" project with Lil' Dap of [[Group Home]] and [[Ed O.G.]], and also worked with [[Mr. Len]] on the song "Dummy Smacks", where he says, ''"Some people thought I was gone... never that!"'' He made an appearance alongside [[Vast Aire]] on the 2007 [[Zimbabwe Legit]] album ''[[House of Stone]]'' with the song "Wake Up". In 2001 he appeared in the soundtrack for [[Wet Hot American Summer]] on the song Summer in America.

In 2008, his single "Treat 'Em Right" was ranked number 82 on VH1's 100 Greatest Songs of Hip Hop. Chubb Rock also released "[[Obama We Believe]]" in support of President Obama.  His most recent appearance was on the [[K'naan]] single "ABC's". He also collaborated with Polish rapper {{proper name|eMCeeM}} on a track for his album ''To dopiero poczatek''.

In February 2009, the single "Back In" was released, from the album ''Bridging The Gap'', a collaboration with [[Anthony Parker (musician)|Wordsmith]], released on July 14, 2009.

Chubb Rock is featured in the single "Summertime Anthem" by Eric Roberson in which they filmed the music video on the streets of [[Bedford-Stuyvesant|Bed-Stuy]], [[Brooklyn]], New York City where he credited his wife and manager, KeKe "Diamond" Simpson, for putting the duo together.{{When|date=October 2019}}

Chubb Rock also released an EP in September 2011. That year, he also appeared with his childhood sweetheart and wife of 24 years, KeKe Simpson on the [[NBC]] [[game show]] ''[[The Marriage Ref (U.S. TV series)|The Marriage Ref]]''.

Chubb Rock moved to Atlanta and joined a radio show hosted by Silas "SiMan Baby" Alexander on Atlanta's [[WAMJ|Majic 107.5/97.5]]. On October 26, 2018 <ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.ajc.com/blog/radiotvtalk/silas-siman-alexander-leaving-majic/b4g1uCavYHaKOAVx9R1RsL/|title=Silas 'SiMan' Alexander and Chubb Rock leaving Majic|last=Ho|first=Rodney|website=AJC|language=english|access-date=2019-08-28}}</ref> they left the station and later started a syndicated afternoon radio show.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.ajc.com/blog/radiotvtalk/siman-chubb-rock-start-syndicated-afternoon-radio-show/DQpcdAkGnnUvsFXtZDYcwJ/|title=SiMan, Chubb Rock start syndicated afternoon radio show|last=Ho|first=Rodney|website=AJC|language=english|access-date=2019-08-28}}</ref>
