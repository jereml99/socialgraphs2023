'''Michael Anthony Deering'''<ref name="auto">{{cite web|url=http://www.oldschoolhiphop.com/artists/emcees/mikeyd.htm |title=Mikey D Biography |publisher=40-K |date=January 7, 2010 |accessdate=2021-09-06}}</ref> (born November 25,<ref name="twitter.com">{{cite web|url=https://twitter.com/DoomerH/status/934514445795168257|title=This guy right here helped me break in the industry...Happy Birthday my brother @TheReal_MikeyD|first=Doomdawiz|last=DoomerH|date=25 Nov 2017|publisher=}}</ref> 1967), known by his stage name '''Mikey D''', is an American [[rapper]]. He is best known for being a lead member of the groups '''Mikey D & The L.A. Posse''' and [[Main Source]]. As a member of Main Source, he replaced [[Large Professor]],<ref name="allmusic">{{cite web | url=http://www.allmusic.com/artist/main-source-p15099 | title=Main Source - All music.com | access-date=September 16, 2021 | author=Kellman, Andy| website=[[AllMusic]] }}</ref> who parted ways with the group due to business differences. In 1994, he appeared on Main Source's second album, ''[[Fuck What You Think]]'', as the lead MC.

== Career ==
Mikey first started rapping at a young age at his junior high school, after hearing music by the [[Cold Crush Brothers]] and [[Clientele Brothers]], which inspired him to become an MC. He was the youngest member to join the [[Clientele Brothers]], a group of emcees consisting of Eddie O'Jay and Will Seville<ref>{{cite web|url=https://thesource.com/2021/09/05/the-real-mikey-d-shares-incredible-journey-in-upcoming-documentary-series/ |title=THE REAL MIKEY D SHARES INCREDIBLE JOURNEY IN UPCOMING DOCUMENTARY SERIES
|publisher=Sha Be Allah |date=September 5, 2021 |accessdate=2021-09-06}}</ref> He would be writing rhymes and freestyling in parks and areas around his neighborhood,<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.promote.hiphop/artist/mikey-dstruction/ |title=MIKEY D'STRUCTION |publisher=Promote Hip-Hop|date= |accessdate=2021-09-26}}</ref> getting training help by O'Jay and Seville, trying to develop his skills and rhymes flows in his adolescence.<ref name=youtube.com>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OY9z4tphKCU&t=1506s |title=Mikey D Speaks On DJ Vlad's Lack Of Hip-Hop Knowledge And Replacing Large Professor In Main Source |publisher=Youtube |date= |accessdate=2021-09-25}}</ref>

While in high school, he met future emcee/actor [[LL Cool J]]. Although, him and Mikey didn't go to the same school together. They went to their own separate schools. James went to [[Andrew Jackson High School (Queens)|Andrew Jackson High School]]. They would come over to each of their own schools they were attending and freestyling rhymes with each other. He was known as J-Ski, but Mikey would suggest a new name for his moniker. He would give him a stage name "Ladies Love", but didn't want it in his stage name. He shortened it to just "LL", put it next to the word "Cool" and added "J", which stood for his first name, James.<ref name=bombhiphop>{{cite web|url=http://www.bombhiphop.com/newbomb/bombpages/mikeyd.html |title=MIKEY D |publisher=Mikey D |date= |accessdate=2021-09-06}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.ozy.com/true-and-stories/mikey-d-the-best-rapper-you-never-heard-of/6439/ |title=MIKEY D: THE BEST RAPPER YOU NEVER HEARD OF |publisher=Ozy |date= 5 February 2016|accessdate=2021-09-06}}</ref>

In 1985, Mikey and his group called the Symbolic Three, got signed to Reality Records.<ref name="bombhiphop" /> Around that year, he and his friend Johnnie Quest met inspiring upcoming producer named Paul "[[Paul C]]" McKasty, through Cliente Brothers members Eddie O'Jay and Will Seville.<ref name=":18">{{Cite web|url=https://medium.com/micro-chop/paul-c-mckasty-the-legend-the-tragedy-the-story-of-an-era-aea20515d282|title=Paul C. McKasty: the Legend, the Tragedy, the Story of an Era|last=Sorcinelli|first=Gino|date=2017-09-20|website=Medium|access-date=2017-09-25}}</ref>

He formed a group with the late [[Paul C]] and childhood friend DJ Johnnie Quest. According to Deering, he stated that his group originally wanted to be called "Boom Bash", but one of his managers didn't think it was a good idea.<ref name="youtube.com" /> The group was called Mikey D & the L.A. Posse. They were signed to Public Records. They had a single which was released in 1987, song called "My Telephone", along with tracks like "Dawn" and "Bust a Rhyme". He followed up with two other singles "I Get Rough" & "Go For It" in the same year.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.bombhiphop.com/newbomb/bombpages/mikeyd.html |title=Mikey D |work=Bomb Hip Hop |date=2012 |access-date=6 September 2021}}</ref>

In 1988, Mikey D was the winner of the New Music Seminar Battle for World Supremacy where he battled [[Melle Mel]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://mikeydstruction.weebly.com/micography.html|title = Micography}}</ref>

While recording an album with his group, he learned that [[Paul C]] was murdered in his sleep at his home.<ref name="youtube.com" /> There were disputes between another group who were also named the [[L.A. Posse]], a group from [[Los Angeles]] and [[Sleeping Bag Records]], a label that Mikey was signed to.<ref name="youtube.com" /> Sleeping Bag Records was forced to drop the group's name after being sued for name duplication and Mikey  took a three-year break from the music business after the death of producer Paul "[[Paul C]]" McKasty.<ref name="youtube.com" /> While spending time in Miami, Florida, his manager was Mike Beasley at the time.<ref name="youtube.com" /> He had a cousin who own a studio in [[Manhattan, New York City]], [[New York (state)|New York]]. He would try to find a label that was gonna give Mikey a record deal but he caught the attention of [[R&B]] singer [[Jeff Redd]] who heard his freestyling raps.<ref name="youtube.com" /> He told him there was a group that needed a lead rapper for the group [[Main Source]]. He contacted them and freestyled raps to the group and liked what they heard.<ref name="youtube.com" />

In 1993, he become an official member of the critically acclaimed Toronto/Queens-based hip hop group [[Main Source]] after [[Large Professor]] left to pursue a solo career.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cPoClzuLU_M |title=Outlaw Radio Live's Interview With The Real Mikey D |work=Youtube |date=2020 |access-date=25 September 2021}}</ref> He appeared on ''[[Fuck What You Think]]'' on [[Wild Pitch Records]] in 1994. They released a single called "What You Need", which did well in [[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'s [[Hot Rap Singles]], only peaking at #48.

He is featured on a song called "Pump Ya Fist Like This" on [[Large Professor]]'s 2008's third album, ''[[Main Source (album)|Main Source]]''.

In 2020, Mikey released a distribution company called Pass the Torch, where he is the founder and currently operates his own company.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://pass-the-torch.com/about/|title=About|date=25 May 2020}}</ref>

Mikey currently hosts his own show on [[YouTube]] called ''The Real Mikey D History (His-story)'',<ref>The Real Mikey D History (His-story).</ref> a documentary series released in 2021.
