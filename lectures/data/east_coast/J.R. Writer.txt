'''Rusty Brito''' (born May 28, 1985) better known by his stage name '''J.R. Writer''', is a Dominican American [[Rapping|rapper]] and [[Freestyle rap|freestyle]] champion. He was also an affiliate of [[East Coast hip hop]] group [[The Diplomats]].

==Career==
Writer is from [[Harlem]], New York. He has released one studio album and 15 mixtapes since 2003, having collaborated with Cassidy, Fred Money, Lloyd Banks, Tito Green, Ransom, Drag-On, Sen City, Paul Wall, T.W.O., Duke Da God, A-Money, Vado, Jae Millz, AraabMuzik and more.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.hotnewhiphop.com/JRWriter/profile/ |title=JR Writer |publisher=Hotnewhiphop.com |date=May 28, 1984 |access-date=February 27, 2014}}</ref> He released his debut album ''[[History in the Making (J.R. Writer album)|History in the Making]]'' in 2006, through [[E1 Music|Koch]] and [[Diplomat Records]]. The album peaked at number 25 on the [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]] and at number 2 on both the [[Top R&B/Hip-Hop Albums]] and [[Top Rap Albums]], held from the top spot of both charts by [[Pimp C]]'s ''[[Pimpalation]]''.<ref name="BB">{{cite web | url = http://www.billboard.com/#/album/the-diplomats-presents-jr-writer/history-in-the-making/774146| title = ''Histort in the Making'' chart history at Billboard.com| publisher = Billboard.com | date = July 10, 2011}}</ref>

On February 23, 2014, Writer released a 13-track mixtape titled ''The Return of Greatness'', which includes guest appearances from Styles P, Fat Trel, Vado, Hell Rell and Fred Money as well as production from Track Officialz, SpecX2, Butter Beats DBK, Razah, Myes William, Stat Five Ave, Stoopid On The Beat and Automatik Beatz.<ref>{{cite web|last=Baker |first=Soren |url=http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/news/id.27651/title.j-r-writer-the-return-of-greatness-release-date-cover-art-tracklist-download-mixtape-stream |title=J.R. Writer "The Return Of Greatness" Release Date, Cover Art, Tracklist, Download & Mixtape Stream |publisher=HipHop DX |date=February 23, 2014 |access-date=February 27, 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|last=Augustin |first=Camille |url=http://www.vibe.com/article/hot-97-interviews-jr-writer-he-turns-himself-jail |title=Hot 97 Interviews JR Writer Before He Turns Himself Into Jail |publisher=Vibe.com |date=March 18, 2014 |access-date=May 31, 2015}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|last=Cole |first=Sally |url=http://thesource.com/2014/03/19/exclusive-jr-writers-last-interview-before-going-to-prison/ |title=Exclusive: JR Writer's Last Interview Before Going To Prison |publisher=Thesource.com |access-date=May 31, 2015}}</ref>

On March 18, 2014, Writer began a prison sentence at Ulster Correctional Facility in New York State.{{Citation needed|date=March 2021}}
    
On January 26, 2019, Writer released a [[diss track]] aimed at [[Tory Lanez]] called "Head Shot".{{Citation needed|date=March 2021}} One month later, he released another titled "Run Tory Run".{{Citation needed|date=March 2021}}
