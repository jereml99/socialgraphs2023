'''Michael Gene Dionne''' (born September 4, 1985), better known by his [[stage name]] '''Eyenine''', is an American [[hip hop music|hip hop]] recording artist from [[Dover, New Hampshire]].

Eyenine began writing and recording music at the age of 16, being heavily inspired by acts including [[Eyedea]], [[Sage Francis]], [[Adeem (hip hop artist)|Adeem]] and [[Atmosphere (music group)|Atmosphere]]. In his twenties, Eyenine began touring the country with such acts as PT Burnem, Paulie Think, [[Ecid]], Prhym8 and Farout. He helped found an upstart recording label called Flyrock Records which released multiple albums from various New England artists including The If In Life, Rio Bravo, The Nasty and others. After years of freestyling at the weekly Rap Night in [[Portland, Maine]] he entered and won two separate Ruckus Cup Classic battle tournaments.<ref name=Ruckus1>{{cite web |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=LWuwZnNBvOA |title=Eyenine winning the 4th Annual Ruckus Cup Classic Battle Tournament |author=Eyenine |publisher=Eyenine |access-date=February 8, 2011}}</ref><ref name=Ruckus2>{{cite web |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=SFt6LaQMZ3A |title=Eyenine winning the 6th Annual Ruckus Cup Classic Battle Tournament |author=King Crooked |publisher=King Crooked |access-date=February 2, 2013}}</ref> He also went on to win two separate Church of Providence battle tournaments hosted by [[B. Dolan]] and refereed by [[Sage Francis]]<ref name=ChurchofProv>{{cite web |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Zykr2NBSHZQ |title=Eyenine winning the Church of Providence Battle Tournament |author=eyenine |publisher=eyenine |access-date=August 24, 2013}}</ref> and later came out of battle rap retirement to win the first ever Overcast battle championship in Cincinnati, OH, the birthplace of the Scribble Jam, in 2018.<ref>{{cite web |title=Eyenine winning Overcast 2018 |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=fYxjMxN_gtc |website=YouTube}}</ref> Eyenine is currently under Real Hood Music LLC, a brand of Wu Tang Management and has shared stages with artists such as [[RZA]] and [[GZA]].<ref name=RealHood>{{cite web |url=http://realhoodmusicllc.com/portfolio/eyenine/ |title=Real Hood Music, LLC |author=Real Hood Music, LLC |publisher=Real Hood Music, LLC}}</ref>

==Career==
Eyenine began his career in New Hampshire doing local shows around the area. He was one of the founding members of the label Flyrock Records, which released multiple albums and hosted shows in the New England area. Eyenine and Flyrock Records were featured in an episode of [[Chronicle (U.S. TV series)|''Chronicle'']].<ref name=Chronicle>{{cite web |url=http://www.wmur.com/article/thursday-march-22nd-fish-tales-taxidermy/5172682 |title=Eyenine & Flyrock Records on NH Chronicle |author=WMUR|publisher=WMUR |access-date=March 22, 2012}}</ref> He was able to work with his biggest influence, [[Eyedea]] before his untimely passing on his album ''Afraid to Dream''.<ref name=Eyedea1>{{cite web |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=TDn2Wcm0pjY |title=Eyenine - Cataract Vision (ft. Eyedea) |author=MichealLarsenDotCom |publisher=MichealLarsenDotCom |access-date=June 14, 2012}}</ref> After two full length releases, he began touring the nation and building up a fanbase which eventually caught the attention of the legendary [[RZA]] of the [[Wu Tang Clan]]. RZA invited Eyenine to join him on the Major Motion Picture Soundtrack tour for ''[[The Man with the Iron Fists (soundtrack)|The Man with the Iron Fists]]'' across the country from [[Brooklyn]], [[New York City]] to [[Los Angeles]], [[California]].<ref name=IronFists1>{{cite web |url=http://www.appletonradio.com/?p=859 |title=Eyenine on tour with RZA |author=Appleton Radio |publisher=Appleton Radio |access-date=October 22, 2012}}</ref> Since then, Eyenine released his third studio album, ''Dissembler'' in 2013,<ref name=Dissembler>{{cite web |url=http://mainetoday.com/blog/newz-by-the-nunz/eyenine-celebrates-the-release-of-dissembler-with-a-proper-party-at-the-big-easy-tonight/ |title=Eyenine Celebrates the Release of Dissembler with a Proper Party at the Big Easy Tonight |author=Holly Nunan |publisher=MaineToday.com |access-date=July 24, 2013}}</ref> toured with [[GZA]],<ref name="Eyenine performing with GZA">{{cite web|title=Mic check: Wu-Tang Clan's GZA draws motley crew at The Westcott|url=http://www.thenewshouse.com/story/mic-check-wu-tang-clans-gza-draws-motley-crew-westcott|website=The News House|accessdate=25 September 2017}}</ref> and has done multiple shows with [[Ghostface Killah]], [[U-God]], [[Raekwon]], [[Killah Priest]] and many others.

In May 2019, after a five year hiatus, Eyenine teamed up with The Lonely Ghosts to release a full length titled ''A Reason for Departure'' which features a marked difference in sound and style from his earlier solo ventures. Eyenine is in the process of recording his fourth solo studio album, titled ''A Little Above Low Key'', being produced by El Shupacabra and features such artists as [[Ceschi]], Witness, [[Adeem (hip hop artist)|Adeem]] and more. The album is set to be released, after major delays due to COVID-19, in the first quarter of 2021.<ref name=Eyenine>{{cite web |url=https://www.eyeninemusic.com/ |title=Eyenine - Official Site |author=Live Free Media, LLC |publisher=Live Free Media, LLC |access-date=January 16, 2017 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20161122224256/http://www.eyeninemusic.com/ |archive-date=November 22, 2016 |url-status=dead }}</ref>{{Update inline|date=March 2022}}

==Touring==
Eyenine has done many tours over the course of his career, ranging from self funded DIY tours to Major Motion Picture Soundtrack tours such as “The Man With the Iron Fists” alongside [[Rza]], [[U-God]], [[Supernatural (rapper)|Supernatural]] and more. He has performed at [[SXSW]] multiple years and continues to reach new places each tour. Recently, he has toured the country with fellow acts [[Ecid|ECID]]<ref name=EcidTour>{{cite web |url=http://realhoodmusicllc.com/eyenine-is-back-on-tour/ |title=Eyenine is Back on Tour |author=Real Hood Music, LLC |publisher=Real Hood Music, LLC |access-date=January 15, 2013}}</ref> and Farout multiple times and plans to continue the trend into 2019 in support of his fourth studio album, ''A Little Above Low Key''.
[[File:Eyenine3.jpg|thumb|Eyenine performing at the House of Blues in Las Vegas]]

==Personal life==

Eyenine is an avid fan of anime, video games, Brazilian Jiu Jitsu and playing music of various genres in multiple collaboration projects and bands. He also hosts multiple events around New Hampshire, including various shows of multiple genres and also open mic comedy events.
