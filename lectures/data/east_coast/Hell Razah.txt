'''Chron Smith''' (born October 1, 1976), better known by his [[stage name]] '''Hell Razah''', is an American [[rapping|rapper]]. He is best known as a member of [[Sunz of Man]], an early [[List of Wu-Tang Clan affiliates|affiliate group]] of the [[Wu-Tang Clan]].

Razah is rumored to have suffered a [[brain aneurysm]] in April 2010.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://kevinnottingham.com/2010/04/07/rumor-hell-razah-suffers-brain-aneurysm-in-a-coma/ |title=Rumor: Hell Razah Suffers Brain Aneurysm, In A Coma |publisher=KevinNottingham.com |date=April 7, 2010 |access-date=October 18, 2011 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20111004054225/http://kevinnottingham.com/2010/04/07/rumor-hell-razah-suffers-brain-aneurysm-in-a-coma/ |archive-date=October 4, 2011 |url-status=dead }}</ref> He was quick to recover and continue recording. He came to adopt the moniker '''Heaven Razah'''.

== Recording history ==
=== Da Last Future & Sunz of Man ===
Razah made his rap debut in the early 90s as a member of the group Da Last Future, an early incarnation of the Sunz of Man, which included [[Killah Priest]], [[Shabazz the Disciple]], [[Prodigal Sunn]], [[Supreme (producer)|Supreme]] and 7th Ambassador.<ref name="SoM">{{cite web |url=http://wu-international.com/Killabeez/wukillabeezN_Z.htm |title=Wu-Tang Clan Family Tree - &#91;Wu -Ta |website=wu-international.com |access-date=2 February 2022 |archive-url=https://archive.today/20120723161029/http://wu-international.com/Killabeez/wukillabeezN_Z.htm |archive-date=23 July 2012 |url-status=dead}}</ref> Razah and 7th Ambassador self-released the single "Livin' in Hell" b/w "Mental Combat" independently before the group adopted the Sunz of Man moniker.<ref name="AB">{{cite web |url=http://lyrics.chambermusik.com/hell_razah/razahs_ladder/06-audiobiography.txt |format=TXT |title=Artist: Blue Sky Black Death & Hell Razah f/ Shabazz the Disciple : Album: Razah's Ladder : Song: Audiobiography |website=Lyrics.chambermusik.com |access-date=2016-09-26 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160303195342/http://lyrics.chambermusik.com/hell_razah/razahs_ladder/06-audiobiography.txt |archive-date=March 3, 2016 |df=mdy-all }}</ref> Razah recorded a number of tracks with the group before their induction into the Wu-Tang family, including "Deep in the Water", "Writing Rhymes With a Liquid Pen" (aka "Elements"), "Lulla Bye" and "Psychic Hotline".<ref name="WL">{{cite web |url=http://lyrics.chambermusik.com/sunz_of_man.html |title=Sunz of Man |website=Lyrics.chambermusik.com |access-date=2016-09-26 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160303185746/http://lyrics.chambermusik.com/sunz_of_man.html |archive-date=March 3, 2016 |df=mdy-all }}</ref>

In 1995, the group adopted the moniker Sunz of Man, and signed a record deal with [[Priority Records|Wu-Tang/Priority Records]].<ref name="PR">{{cite web|url=http://www.discogs.com/release/1153550 |title=Sunz Of Man - Bloody Choices (Cassette) |website=Discogs.com |access-date=2016-09-26}}</ref> SoM released a number of singles on Wu-Tang Records, including "Soldiers of Darkness", "No Love Without Hate" and "Bloody Choices",<ref name="NL">{{cite web|url=http://www.discogs.com/artist/Sunz+Of+Man |title=Sunz Of Man Discography |website=Discogs.com |access-date=2016-09-26}}</ref> in anticipation of their 1996 debut album ''Nothing New Under the Sun''. Due to internal conflicts and label complications, the album was shelved.<ref name="BS">{{cite web|url=http://bringyaskillz.blogspot.com/2008/01/nothing-new-under-sun-sunz-of-man.html |title=Bring Ya Skillz: Nothing New Under the Sun - Sunz of Man |access-date=2008-01-19 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080323234858/http://bringyaskillz.blogspot.com/2008/01/nothing-new-under-sun-sunz-of-man.html |archive-date=March 23, 2008 |df=mdy }}</ref> Sunz of Man later signed a record deal with Red Ant Entertainment for the release of their official 1998 debut ''[[The Last Shall Be First (Sunz of Man album)|The Last Shall Be First]]'', with the group's roster shaved down to just Razah, Killah Priest, Prodigal Sunn and [[60 Second Assassin (emcee)|60 Second Assassin]].<ref name="RY">{{cite web|url=http://rateyourmusic.com/artist/sunz_of_man |title=Sunz of Man Albums: songs, discography, biography, and listening guide |publisher=Rate Your Music |access-date=2016-09-26}}</ref> The album was one of the most successful Wu-Tang affiliate releases, debuting at No.&nbsp;20 on the [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]] album chart, and No.&nbsp;7 on the R&B/Hip-Hop chart.<ref name="CP">{{cite web | url = {{AllMusic|class=album|id=r367842/charts-awards|pure_url=yes}} | title = The Last Shall Be First chart positions at AllMusicGuide }}</ref>

After the departure of Killah Priest in 1998, the group took a hiatus. 1999 saw the release of ''[[The First Testament]]'', which featured previously recorded material from the group, including the early tracks from Da Last Future.<ref name="FT">{{cite web|url=http://rateyourmusic.com/release/album/sunz_of_man/the_first_testament/ |title=The First Testament by Sunz of Man (Album, East Coast Hip Hop): Reviews, Ratings, Credits, Song list |publisher=Rate Your Music |access-date=2016-09-26}}</ref> Razah, Prodigal Sunn and 60 Second Assassin returned in 2002 with the album ''[[Saviorz Day]]'', the second official Sunz of Man studio album.<ref name="SD">{{cite web|url=http://rateyourmusic.com/release/album/sunz_of_man/saviorz_day/ |title=Saviorz Day by Sunz of Man (Album, East Coast Hip Hop): Reviews, Ratings, Credits, Song list |publisher=Rate Your Music |access-date=2016-09-26}}</ref> The group has yet to release a third studio album, but it has been reported that the group has reconciled with Shabazz the Disciple, Killah Priest and Supreme.<ref name="SoM"/>

=== Solo career ===
Razah made his solo debut in 2001, though quietly, with the independent release ''When All Hell Breaks Loose''. The album featured guest appearances from 7th Ambassador, Killah Priest, Prodigal Sunn and [[Timbo King]] of [[Royal Fam]], and production from Supreme.<ref name="BL">{{cite web|url=http://www.discogs.com/release/1007192 |title=Hell Razah - When All Hell Breaks Loose (CD, Album) |website=Discogs.com |access-date=2016-09-26}}</ref> The album spawned the single "Champaigne Room" b/w "Ghetto Love".<ref name="CR">{{cite web|url=http://www.discogs.com/release/720338 |title=Hell Razah - Champaigne Room / Ghetto Love (Vinyl) |website=Discogs.com |access-date=2016-09-26}}</ref> The tracks "Must B tha Music", "Oh! Now You Bangin'", "Ghetto Government", "Champaigne Room", "Like It or Not", "What We Came to Do", "B.B.P. (Business Before Pleasure)" and "Rowdy, Rowdy" made up roughly half of the 2004 Sunz of Man compilation ''Elements'', with the other half made up of tracks from ''The First Testament''.<ref name="EM">{{cite web|url=http://www.discogs.com/release/354312 |title=Sunz Of Man - Elements (CD, Album) |website=Discogs.com |access-date=2016-09-26}}</ref>

Razah's next solo album, ''[[Renaissance Child]]'', was released in early 2007 on [[Nature Sounds|Nature Sounds Records]]. The album featured appearances from [[Timbo King]], [[Tragedy Khadafi]], [[R.A. the Rugged Man]], [[Talib Kweli]], [[Daniel Dumile|Viktor Vaughn]], [[Bronze Nazareth]], [[Killah Priest]] and [[Ras Kass]], and production from [[List of Wu-Tang Clan affiliates#Godz Wrath|Godz Wrath]], [[Daniel Dumile|MF DOOM]], [[Bronze Nazareth]], [[Krohme]] and [[4th Disciple]].<ref name="RC">{{cite web|url=http://www.discogs.com/release/927622 |title=Hell Razah - Renaissance Child (CD, Album) |website=Discogs.com |date=2007-02-20 |access-date=2016-09-26}}</ref> The album was a well-received, critical success and featured the singles "The Renaissance"<ref name="TR">{{cite web|url=http://www.discogs.com/release/700135 |title=Black Market Militia - Gem Stars (DJ Absolut Remix) / Mayday / The Renaissance (Vinyl) |website=Discogs.com |access-date=2016-09-26}}</ref> and "Buried Alive" b/w "Project Jazz".<ref name="BA">{{cite web|url=http://www.discogs.com/release/935353 |title=Hell Razah - Buried Alive / Project Jazz (Vinyl) |website=Discogs.com |access-date=2016-09-26}}</ref>
In October 2007, Razah released his next album, ''[[Razah's Ladder]]'', entirely produced by the production duo [[Blue Sky Black Death]].<ref name="RL">{{cite web|url=http://www.discogs.com/release/1161653 |title=Blue Sky Black Death & Hell Razah - Razah's Ladder (CD, Album) |website=Discogs.com |access-date=2016-09-26}}</ref> The album also was acclaimed,<ref name="AC">{{cite web|url=http://rapreviews.com/archive/2007_11_razahsladder.html |title=Hell Razah :: Blue Sky Black Death presents Hell Razah: Razah's Ladder :: Babygrande Records |website=Rapreviews.com |date=2007-11-13 |access-date=2016-09-26}}</ref> due to BSBD's soulful production work, and also to Razah's new spiritual lyrical direction, inspired by the recent death of his father.<ref name="ITV">{{cite web|url=http://www.hiphopcrack.com/viewVideo.hhc?videoId%3D269 |title=CrackSpace - the Place for Hip-Hop > Featured Video > "Hell Razah Interview" |access-date=2007-10-15 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20071011083708/http://www.hiphopcrack.com/viewVideo.hhc?videoId=269 |archive-date=October 11, 2007 |df=mdy }}</ref> The album featured guest appearances from Prodigal Sunn, Shabazz the Disciple, [[Crooked I]], [[Ill Bill]] and [[Sabac Red]], and featured no singles.<ref name="WIN">{{cite web|url=http://www.discogs.com/artist/Hell+Razah |title=Hell Razah Discography |website=Discogs.com |access-date=2016-09-26}}</ref> Self-funded videos were shot for the tracks "Razah's Ladder"<ref name="LD">{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=uDelRzvwxaQ |title=Hell Razah (Blue Sky Black Death) -"Razah's Ladder"-Prequel |publisher=[[YouTube]] |date=2007-10-16 |access-date=2016-09-26}}{{cbignore}}{{Dead Youtube links|date=February 2022}}</ref> and "Audiobiography", the latter directed by Sunz of Man producer Supreme,<ref name="ADB">{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=fWXPVr838Yg  |archive-url=https://ghostarchive.org/varchive/youtube/20211221/fWXPVr838Yg |archive-date=2021-12-21 |url-status=live|title=HELL RAZAH-Razah's Ladder feat SHABAZZ the Dis |publisher=[[YouTube]] |date=2007-10-22 |access-date=2016-09-26}}{{cbignore}}</ref> and "Razah's Ladder",<ref name="LD"/>

=== Collaborations ===
In 2004, Hell Razah and [[4th Disciple]] released a full-length collaboration album, ''[[Freedom of Speech (Hell Razah & 4th Disciple album)|Freedom of Speech]]'', under the title "Sunz of Man presents". The album featured fifteen newly recorded tracks with 4th Disciple, and five other previously released tracks from ''When All Hell Breaks Loose''.<ref name="FS">{{cite web|url=http://www.discogs.com/release/354440 |title=Sunz Of Man Presents Hell Razah & 4th Disciple - Freedom Of Speech (CD, Album) |website=Discogs.com |access-date=2016-09-26}}</ref> The album featured the single "Article One" b/w "Project Love".<ref name="AO">{{cite web|url=http://www.discogs.com/release/715881 |title=Hell Razah - Article One (Vinyl) |website=Discogs.com |date=2006-06-14 |access-date=2016-09-26}}</ref>

In 2005, Razah, Killah Priest, Royal Fam's Timbo King, Tragedy Khadafi and William Cooper formed the underground [[Supergroup (music)|supergroup]] [[Black Market Militia]]. The group released their self-titled debut album ''[[Black Market Militia (album)|Black Market Militia]]'' that same year, with production from Godz Wrath and Bronze Nazareth, and guest appearances from [[dead prez]] and [[Abiodun Oyewole]] of [[The Last Poets]].<ref name="BMM">{{cite web|url=http://www.discogs.com/release/569586 |title=Black Market Militia - The Black Market Militia (CD, Album) |website=Discogs.com |access-date=2016-09-26}}</ref> The album spawned the singles "Audobon Ballroom" b/w "Thug Nation"/"Hood Lullabye" and "Gem Stars" b/w "Mayday!".<ref name="BM">{{cite web|url=http://www.discogs.com/artist/Black+Market+Militia |title=Black Market Militia Discography |website=Discogs.com |access-date=2016-09-26}}</ref> By the end of 2005, Hell Razah had named Saul Abraham the latest addition to the line-up. Razah's next album collaboration album was with Shabazz the Disciple and producer [[Ayatollah (producer)|Ayatollah]] as the group T.H.U.G. Angelz, titled ''[[Welcome to Red Hook Houses]]'', to be released on July 8, 2008 on [[Babygrande Records]]. Other releases came in 2008 such as solo album titled ''Heaven Razah'', and ''Article 2: Right to Bare Arms'', a second full-length collaboration with [[4th Disciple]].<ref name="UC">{{cite web|url=http://www.myspace.com/heavenrazah |title=HEAVEN-RAZAH &#124; Listen and Stream Free Music, Albums, New Releases, Photos, Videos |website=Myspace.com |access-date=2016-09-26}}</ref> In an October 2007 interview, Razah also announced plans for numerous other releases for the year. He announced that the Sunz of Man were reunited and planned on releasing an album sometime soon. He also stated that [[The Maccabeez]], a group consisting of himself, Killah Priest and Timbo King, were nearly finished recording their debut album. Black Market Militia are also planning for another album. Another possible release is a collaboration between Razah, Shabazz and Blue Sky Black Death, and a project from G.G.O. (Ghetto Government Officialz), which consists of Razah, 7th Ambassador, Lazarus, Face God and other Red Hook locals.<ref name="IV">{{cite web |url=http://wu-international.com/misc_albums/Interviews/HellRazahBSBD%20Interview.htm |title=Hell Razah / Blue Sky Black Death - &#91;Razah's Ladder Interview&#93; - Wu-i… |website=wu-international.com |access-date=2 February 2022 |archive-url=https://archive.today/20130209191246/http://wu-international.com/misc_albums/Interviews/HellRazahBSBD%20Interview.htm |archive-date=9 February 2013 |url-status=dead}}</ref>

Hell Razah suffered a [[Brain Aneurysm]] in 2010. After surviving that near death experience, he began to go under the name ''Heaven Razah''.

In 2011, Hell Razah's health was improving and he said on Twitter several albums will come out in the future, including Heaven Razah 2,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.wutang-corp.com/forum/showthread.php?t=105287 |title=Heaven Razah Pt 2 – Wu-Tang Corp. – Official Site of the Wu-Tang Clan |publisher=Wu-Tang Corp. |date=February 14, 2011 |access-date=October 18, 2011}}</ref> the second album with [[Shabazz the Disciple]] as [[Welcome to Red Hook Houses|T.H.U.G. Angelz]] (produced by [[Ayatollah (producer)|Ayatollah]] and [[4th Disciple]]),<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.twitlonger.com/show/9cet6q |title=TwitLonger – When you talk too much for Twitter |publisher=Twitlonger.com |date=March 20, 2011 |access-date=October 18, 2011}}</ref> Razah's Ladder 2 produced by [[Blue Sky Black Death]],<ref>{{cite web|url=https://twitter.com/HEAVENRAZAH/status/74554603131248640|title=#GGO PRESIDENT on Twitter|work=twitter.com|access-date=September 26, 2016}}</ref> a gospel/rock album produced by Krohme called Krohme Razahs,<ref>{{cite web|url=https://twitter.com/HEAVENRAZAH/status/73572470396952577|title=#GGO PRESIDENT on Twitter|work=twitter.com|access-date=September 26, 2016}}</ref> an album with [[Shyheim]] called The Verrazano<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.facebook.com/shyheimfranklin/posts/10150099368667554  |archive-url=https://ghostarchive.org/iarchive/facebook/19922647553/10150099368667554 |archive-date=2022-02-26 |url-access=limited|title=Confirmed: Finally... |publisher=Facebook |access-date=October 18, 2011}}{{cbignore}}</ref> and the sequel to ''[[Freedom of Speech (Hell Razah & 4th Disciple album)|Freedom of Speech]]'' called Write to Bare Arms produced by 4th Disciple.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://twitter.com/HEAVENRAZAH/status/95185407561572352|title=#GGO PRESIDENT on Twitter|work=twitter.com|access-date=September 26, 2016}}</ref> An album with [[Killah Priest]] as Thug Vaticans called The Lamb’s Supper will come out in 2013.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://kevinnottingham.com/2011/12/12/thug-vaticans-hell-razah-killah-priest-new-millennium-child-exclusive/ |title=Thug Vaticans (Hell Razah & Killah Priest): New Millennium Child [Exclusive&#93; &#124; The Underground Hip Hop Authority &#124; Hip Hop Music, Videos & Reviews |publisher=KevinNottingham.com |date=2011-12-12 |access-date=2012-07-23 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140820200316/http://www.kevinnottingham.com/2011/12/12/thug-vaticans-hell-razah-killah-priest-new-millennium-child-exclusive/ |archive-date=August 20, 2014 |url-status=dead }}</ref> Another collaboration album, with producer Tokyo Cigar under the name Heavenly Cartel, is planned for 2013 and is called Spiritual Kung Fu.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://kevinnottingham.com/2012/01/02/heaven-razah-boombox-saints-prod-by-tokyo-cigar/ |title=Heaven Razah: Boombox Saints [prod by Tokyo Cigar&#93; ''Heaven Razah Part 2'' became ''Living After Death'' and was released on [[iTunes]] on March 31, 2013.<ref name="itunes.apple.com">{{cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/living-after-death/id613238650 |title=ITunes - Music - Living After Death by Hellrazah |website=[[iTunes]] |access-date=2013-10-31 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131111003821/https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/living-after-death/id613238650 |archive-date=November 11, 2013 |df=mdy }}</ref> In May, Hell Razah announced the formal roster of his new label, Hell Razah Music Inc. to include Razah's oldest son "Heaven Sun" aka Yung Razah, Queen the Prophet, Scripture, and a few more to formally represent Razah's long time "Ghetto Govt" global movement also known as "GGO" (Ghetto Govt Officialz). The Ghetto Govt Movement has global chapters that are dedicated to making a positive impact in their communities as well as in the underground hip-hop community.

'''Risen: The Story of Chron "Hell Razah" Smith - Documentary'''

Risen: The Story of Chron “Hell Razah” Smith which is a documentary directed by Frank Meyer and stars Wu Tang’s RZA, Cappadonna and Popa Wu, Sunz of Man members Killah Priest, Prodigal Sunn, 60-Second Assassin and Shabazz the Disciple, producers A. Supreme Wilder and Tom "Shroom" Johnson, rappers RA The Rugged Man and Ras Kass, industry heavyweight Randy Philips (AEG, Global Ent.), Hell Razah’s family and friends, and many more. Risen is executive produced by Digital Reacharound CEO Robert Juster, produced by film maker Paul Harb (The Expendables, Rambo, Rocky Balboa), and Esquire Network’s Michael D’Alonzo, with Cinematography by Jason Valdez. Risen: The Story of Chron “Hell Razah” Smith is available on Amazon Prime Video, Tubi, and You Tube. https://www.amazon.com/Risen-Story-Chron-Razah-Smith/dp/B085RC21RB

In 2019, Razah was featured in the song "The Grandmaster" on [[Cryptik Soul]]'s album ''[[Killer's Blood]]''.
