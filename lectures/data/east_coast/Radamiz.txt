'''Radhames Rodriguez''' (born October 6, 1992), better known by his stage name '''Radamiz''', is an American [[rapper]] from [[Brooklyn]], [[New York (state)|New York]]. He is of [[Dominican Republic|Dominican]] descent. Radamiz is a part of the Brooklyn-based collective, Mogul Club. In 2013, he was declared as [[WQHT|HOT 97]]'s East Coast "Who's Next" artist and performed at [[Summer Jam (festival)|Summer Jam]] on the festival stage later that year.

== Career ==

On April 5, 2016, Radamiz was announced as one of the performers at the 2016 [[The Brooklyn Hip-Hop Festival|Brooklyn Hip-Hop Festival]] opening up for [[Nas]].<ref>{{cite web|author=Stephanie Smith-Strickland|title=Here's What Went Down at the 12th Annual Brooklyn Hip-Hop Festival|url=https://www.highsnobiety.com/2016/07/18/brooklyn-hip-hop-festival-2016/|publisher=highsnobiety.com |date=July 18, 2016 |accessdate=17 November 2018}}</ref> Two weeks later he released his debut album, Writeous, which took four years to complete.<ref>{{cite web|author=Cassidy Kakin |url=https://djbooth.net/features/2017-03-23-radamiz-investing-interview |title=This Is What Investing in an Album as an Indie Artist Really Looks Like |publisher=djbooth.com |date=March 23, 2017 |accessdate=17 November 2018}}</ref> In October 2016, Radamiz was invited on [[Statik Selektah]]'s [[Shade 45]] [[satellite radio]] show "Showoff Radio" to promote his album.<ref>{{cite web|author=Niyah Nel |url=http://hiphopsince1987.com/2016/videos/radamiz-showoff-radio-freestyle-with-statik-selektah-shade-45-ep-45-video/ |title=Radamiz Showoff Radio Freestyle With Statik Selektah Shade 45 (Ep. 45) (Video) |publisher=hiphopsince1987.com |date=November 9, 2018 |accessdate=17 November 2018}}</ref>

In May 2018, Radamiz announced his signing to the relaunched [[Payday Records]].<ref>{{cite web|author=Riley Wallace |url=https://hiphopdx.com/interviews/id.3104/title.radamiz-is-helping-lead-payday-records-second-wave |title=Radamiz Is Helping Lead Payday Records' Second Wave|publisher=hiphopdx.com |date=May 29, 2017 |accessdate=17 November 2018}}</ref> That same month he released "V.I.M" produced by V'Don. Radamiz was one of the performers at the 2018 Brooklyn Hip-Hop Festival opening up for [[Black Star (rap duo)|Black Star]].<ref>{{cite web|author=Andrew Sacher |url=http://www.brooklynvegan.com/brooklyn-hip-hop-festival-2018-underway-black-star-headlining-the-final-concert/ |title=Brooklyn Hip Hop Festival 2018 underway (Black Star headlining the final concert) |publisher=brooklynvegan.com |date=July 11, 2018 |accessdate=17 November 2018}}</ref> On September 13, 2018, Radamiz was featured in a billboard and [[New York City Subway]] ad campaign in New York City for [[Depop]]. Later that month he released a follow-up single titled "NYNYNYNY".<ref>{{cite web|author=Emmanuel Maduakolam |url=https://hypebeast.com/2018/9/radamiz-nynynyny |title=Radamiz Drops New Anthem Single "NYNYNYNY" |publisher=hypebeast.com |date=September 21, 2018 |accessdate=17 November 2018}}</ref> In January 2019, Radamiz was featured on a FifthGod song titled "Transgression" that included [[Ab-Soul]] and [[Dash (rapper)|Da$H]].<ref>{{cite web|author=Ashton Howard |url=https://www.elevatormag.com/long-island-creative-fifth-god-taps-dah-radamiz-ab-soul-for-his-scathing-new-record/ |title=Long Island Creative Fifth God Taps Da$h, Radamiz & Ab-Soul For His Scathing New Record|publisher=elevatormag.com |date=January 6, 2019 |accessdate=7 January 2019}}</ref> The following month he released his third single titled "Save The Youth" featuring History & Tedy Andreas.<ref>{{cite web|author=Trixx |url=https://www.fashionably-early.com/2019/02/11/radamiz-save-the-youth-f-tedy-andreas-history/ |title=Radamiz – Save The Youth f. Tedy Andreas & History |publisher=fashionably-early.com |date=February 11, 2019 |accessdate=6 March 2019}}</ref>
