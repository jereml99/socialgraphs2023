'''William Alfred''', better known as '''Aone''', is a Nigerian-born American [[record producer]] and [[songwriter]], signed to [[Rick Ross (rapper)|Rick Ross]]' label [[Maybach Music Group]].<ref name="auto">{{cite web|url=http://www.mtv.com/news/articles/1671834/rick-ross-aone-mixtape.jhtml |title=Rick Ross Producer Aone Advises: 'Get Ready' - Music, Celebrity, Artist News |publisher=MTV.com |date=2011-09-30 |accessdate=2012-06-09}}</ref> He became active in the music industry in 2008 and is known for his work with urban music artists such as<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.magcloud.com/browse/issue/226735 |title=Hips On Deck Magazin... &#124; Hips On Deck Magazine issue 3 &#124; HP MagCloud |publisher=Magcloud.com |date=2011-10-08 |accessdate=2012-06-09}}</ref> [[Juicy J]], [[Birdman (entertainer)|Birdman]], [[Meek Mill]], [[Young Buck]], [[Rick Ross (rapper)|Rick Ross]], [[Wale (rapper)|Wale]], [[Young Jeezy]], [[The Lox|D Block]],<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/reviews/2011/08/meek-mill-dreamchasers/ |title=Meek Mill, Dreamchasers |date=16 August 2011 |publisher=Xxlmag.Com |accessdate=2012-06-09}}</ref>  [[The Outlawz]], and others. His major influences in producing come from [[Just Blaze]], [[Drumma Boy]] and [[Dr. Dre]]. His work is marked with a "You are now rocking with Aonebeats" tag in most of his recent music.

AOne has also done production for high-profile artists such as [[French Montana]], [[Rick Ross (rapper)|Rick Ross]], [[Young Buck]], Juicy J, [[Birdman (entertainer)|Birdman]], [[Wizkid (musician)|Wizkid]], [[Prodigy (rapper)|Prodigy]] and [[Yo Gotti]], among many others. In the summer of 2013, AOne produced the bass thumping record "Gallardo" for Maybach Music Group and [[Atlantic Records]]'s "Self Made Vol.3" Compilation Album, which debuted at No. 1 on the US [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]] chart with first-week sales of 50,000 copies.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/charts/r-b-hip-hop-albums/2013-10-05 |title=TOP R&B/HIP-HOP ALBUMS |publisher=billboard.com |accessdate=2013-10-05}}</ref> On March 23, 2012 AOne released his first solo full-length project, a [[mixtape]] titled ''Aone Perico'', which featured guest appearances from several rappers including [[Uncle Murda]], [[French Montana]], [[Trae tha Truth]], [[Mistah F.A.B.]] and more. in May 2015, AOne teamed up with Livemixtapes to release his second [[solo project]], an [[extended play]] (EP) titled ''Cocaine, Steak & Wine''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.livemixtapes.com/mixtapes/34227/aone-cocaine-steak-wine.html |title=AOne – Cocaine, Steak & Wine (@AOneBeats) |publisher=Livemixtapes.Com |accessdate=2015-05-26}}</ref>

== Music career ==
Aone gained mainstream attention through his collaboration with [[Rick Ross (rapper)|Rick Ross]], on the song "10 Bricks", which featured [[Birdman (rapper)|Birdman]], off of Ross's mixtape [[Ashes to Ashes (mixtape)|Ashes To Ashes]] released on December 24, 2010.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.hotnewhiphop.com/rick-ross-ashes-to-ashes-mixtape.6752.html |title=Rick Ross - Ashes To Ashes [New Mixtape] |date=24 December 2010 |publisher=hotnewhiphop.com |accessdate=2010-12-24}}</ref> Aone has also worked extensively with American rapper [[Young Buck]], producing records like "Brand New," which featured [[Juicy J]],<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.hotnewhiphop.com/young-buck-brand-new-feat-juicy-j-new-song.647754.html |title=Young Buck - Brand New Feat. Juicy J [New Song] |publisher=hotnewhiphop.com |accessdate=2012-01-09}}</ref> as well as records like "In The Air," "Home Run," and "Drug Related," from Young Buck's [[Live Loyal Die Rich]] mixtape. In May 2010, Aone produced ‘Pay Off,’ for American hip-hop group [[Outlawz]], the record featured Rapper [[Young Buck]], and was included on The [[Outlawz]]’s sixth and final studio album, “[[Perfect Timing (Outlawz album)|Perfect Timing]].”

===2012–present: Aone Perico, Cocaine, Steak & Wine===
On Mar 23, 2012 AOne released his debut mixtape titled  Aone Perico, The mixtape was released in conjunction with popular mixtape website [[DatPiff]]. The mixtape features guest appearances from  Juicy J, [[Rick Ross (rapper)|Rick Ross]], Uncle Murda, French Montana, [[Trae tha Truth]], Mistah F.A.B. and more. The mixtape is entirely self-produced and does not include any vocals of his.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.datpiff.com/Aone-Various-Artists-Aone-Perico-mixtape.328337.html |title=Aone & Various Artists - Aone Perico Hosted by Evil Empire |publisher=datpiff.com |date=2012-03-23 |accessdate=2012-03-23}}</ref>

On May 26, 2015, Aone partnered with popular music site Livemixtapes to announce the instalment of his second mixtape, [[Cocaine, Steak & Wine]], and a release of the mixtape's first single, "Listen", which features Bay Area MC [[Mistah F.A.B.]] and UK singer Katie London.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/rap-music/new-music/2015/04/listen-aone-feat-mistah-f-b-katie-london-listen/ |title=Listen to AOne Feat. Mistah F.A.B. and Katie London, "Listen" |publisher=xxlmag.com |accessdate=2015-04-26}}</ref> The project Cocaine, Steak & Wine features songs with Mistah Fab, [[Warren G]], Creative Gold, [[AZ (rapper)|A.Z]], [[Young Breed]], JaeO Daftpick, and others.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.livemixtapes.com/mixtapes/34227/aone-cocaine-steak-wine.html |title=AOne - Cocaine, Steak & Wine |publisher=livemixtapes.com |accessdate=2015-04-26}}</ref>

== Style and equipment ==
Aone has called himself a fan of producers such as [[Havoc (musician)]] and [[DJ Toomp]], but writes and produces a variety of genres. About his style, he has stated "It's a mix of the South, and it's a mix of New York. It's a mix of everything. It's something different."<ref name="auto"/> Aone uses [[FL Studio]] and custom plug-ins to create his beats.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://issuu.com/defiance29/docs/the_resource_magazine_vol._4|title=The Resource Magazine Vol. 4 |publisher=Resource Magazine |date=2013-01-27}}</ref> He is known for sampling jazz, funk, and soul artists,<ref name="auto"/> Like many other producers Aone uses a musical signature tag on many of the songs he has produced. His main tag is a Female pitched-up voice saying "You are now rocking with Aonebeats"

== AOne Music Group ==
In March 2013, AOne Beats started his own record label called AOne Music Group. The label's first artist signed up was Grammy Nominated Singer and Songwriter Torica.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://kaboom-magazine.com/2013/03/03/top-13-new-rb-songstress-to-watch-in-2013/ |title=TOP 13 NEW R&B SONGSTRESS TO WATCH IN 2013 |publisher=kaboom magazine |accessdate=2013-03-03}}</ref> The record label's first release was Torica's studio album titled A Beautiful Mess: and featured Miami Rapper [[Young Breed]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.mp3panda.me/video/two-hearts-torica-featuring-young-breed-of-triple-cs?v=hF526WUEkgg |title=Two Hearts - Torica featuring Young Breed of Triple C's Download |publisher=mp3panda.me |accessdate=2013-01-01 }}{{Dead link|date=May 2019 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}</ref>
