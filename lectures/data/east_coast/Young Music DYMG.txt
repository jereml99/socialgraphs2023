'''Young Music DYMG''' is an American DJ, songwriter and record producer.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Biography|url=https://www.youngmusicdymg.com/bio|access-date=October 31, 2020|website=Young Music DYMG|language=en-US}}</ref> He initially gained recognition as the official DJ for Washington, D.C.-based singer [[Ginuwine]] at the age of 16.<ref>{{cite web|title = DJ Young Music: You Can't Win In The DMV|url = http://www.bet.com/news/music/2015/03/03/dj-young-music-you-can-t-win-in-the-dmv.html|publisher = [[BET]]|access-date = January 23, 2017}}</ref> He is the youngest artist to sign a worldwide distribution deal with [[E1 Music]].<ref>{{cite web|title = The Youngest in Charge: DJ Young Music|url = http://soultrain.com/2015/03/17/the-youngest-in-charge-dj-young-music/|publisher = [[Soul Train]]|access-date = January 23, 2017}}</ref>

==Career==
===Disc Jockey===
Young Music began deejaying at the age of 12. In 2011, his uncle, Ginuwine, hired Young Music to open for him at shows in 40 cities across the United States.<ref>{{cite web|title = DJ Young Music Speaks On New Position At WPGC| date=March 7, 2015 |url = http://hiphopdx.com/news/id.32853/title.dj-young-music-explains-donating-salary-from-wpgc-to-charity#|publisher = [[Hip Hop DX]]|access-date = January 23, 2017}}</ref> In 2015, he became the youngest DJ to be added to the [[CBS Radio]] owned [[WPGC-FM]] roster.<ref>{{cite web|title = DJ Young Music to Guest Spin Wednesdays at 10 p.m. on WPGC|url =http://wpgc.cbslocal.com/2015/02/18/dj-young-music-to-spin-wednesdays-at-10-p-m-on-wpgc/|publisher = [[WPGC-FM]]|access-date = January 23, 2017}}</ref> In August of that same year, he toured with [[Chris Brown]] on the North American leg of the [[One Hell of a Nite Tour]].{{Cn|date=August 2022}} Later that year, he joined Duane Myko and Tony Redz on the 30 city, "Should Could Dream Tour" which was co-sponsored by [[Revolt (TV network)|Revolt TV]] and WPGC.<ref>{{cite web|title =5 & Done: DJ Young Music – Hello Young World|date =March 17, 2015|url =http://allhiphop.com/2015/03/17/5-done-dj-young-music-hello-young-world/|publisher = [[AllHipHop]]|access-date = January 23, 2017}}</ref> Following the conclusion of the tour, Young Music performed guest DJ sets on Sway in the Morning as well as Revolt TV. As of November 2016, Young Music had mix shows on WPGC 95.5 FM in Washington, DC, E93 WEAS in Savannah, GA, WDKX 103.9 FM in Savannah, GA, WDAI 98.5 KISS in Myrtle Beach, SC and Coast 97.3 in Wilmington, NC.<ref>{{cite web|title =DJ Young Music on his rise to hip-hop stardom|date =May 13, 2015|url =http://rollingout.com/2015/05/13/dj-young-music-rise-hip-hop-stardom/|publisher = Rolling Out|access-date = January 23, 2017}}</ref> In 2018 he signed an exclusive international radio syndication deal with "Radio Express International" for all territories outside of the United States. The deal is for his radio shows, "Streetz Slow Lane" and "Streetz Fast Lane." In September 2018 he formed his own national radio syndication company, "DJ Young Music LLC." The company serves his combined 30 weekly markets with his two formatted self-produced programs.<ref>{{cite web|title =Young Music DYMG: Hip-Hop's Newest Renaissance Man |date =November 16, 2018|url =https://respect-mag.com/2018/11/young-music-dymg-hip-hops-newest-renaissance-man/|publisher = [[Respect. (magazine)|Respect.]]|access-date = November 25, 2018}}</ref>

===DYMG===
In 2015 Young Music signed a development deal with N.S.U.C. Entertainment Group, distributed by E1 Distribution. The deal gave Young Music full development rights to release music, films and soundtracks under his label, DYMG. The first song that he released was "Pull Up On The Block" which featured [[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] charting artist, [[iLoveMemphis]].<ref>{{cite web|title =DJ YOUNG MUSIC & IHEART MEMPHIS' "PULL UP ON THE BLOCK": "WE JUST GOT INTO THE STUDIO & MADE THAT SONG"|date =November 26, 2015|url =http://www.sohh.com/dj-young-music-iheart-memphis-pull-up-on-the-block-we-just-got-into-the-studio-made-that-song-audio/|publisher = [[SOHH]]|access-date = January 23, 2017}}</ref> By the end of that year, Young Music signed [[Washington metropolitan area|DMV]] based artists, Ant Glizzy and SHADE.<ref>{{cite web|title =DJ YOUNG MUSIC ON HIS DYMG MOVEMENT: "IT'S BIGGER THAN BUSINESS, IT'S LOYALTY"|date =December 3, 2015|url =http://www.sohh.com/dj-young-music-on-his-dymg-movement-its-bigger-than-business-its-loyalty-video/|publisher = [[SOHH]]|access-date = January 23, 2017}}</ref> In October 2018, Young Music released a single entitled HeHe which featured [[Rich The Kid]].<ref>{{cite web|title =Rich The Kid & Young Music Hit the Beach for HeHe|url =https://vergecampus.com/2018/11/rich-the-kid-young-music-hit-the-beach-for-he-he-watch/|publisher =Verge Campus|access-date =November 25, 2018|archive-url =https://web.archive.org/web/20181125204445/https://vergecampus.com/2018/11/rich-the-kid-young-music-hit-the-beach-for-he-he-watch/|archive-date =November 25, 2018|url-status =dead}}</ref>

===SUPER RADIO===
In 2016 Young Music signed with Super Radio making him the youngest DJ to join their network. This deal expanded his weekly audience to an estimated 30 million listeners.<ref>{{cite web|title =ON THE RISE: FOR DJ YOUNG MUSIC, FAILURE SIMPLY ISN'T AN OPTION|date =November 10, 2016|url =http://thesource.com/2016/11/10/on-the-rise-for-dj-young-music-failure-simply-isnt-an-option/|publisher = [[The Source]]|access-date = November 25, 2018}}</ref>
