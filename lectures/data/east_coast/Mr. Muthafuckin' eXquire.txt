'''Hugh Anthony Allison''' (born 1986), better known by his stage name '''Mr. Muthafuckin' eXquire''' (often censored as '''Mr. MFN eXquire''' or simply '''eXquire'''), is an American rapper. Born and raised in [[Brooklyn]], he debuted with the release of his first [[mixtape]], ''The Big Fat Kill'', in 2008. Allison would follow-up with what is considered to be his breakout project, the mixtape ''[[Lost in Translation (mixtape)|Lost in Translation]]'', released by [[Mishka NYC]] in 2011. He signed his first major-label record deal with [[Universal Republic Records]], in 2012, which he subsequently left. In 2017, with the release of Allison's fourth EP ''Brainiac'', he launched Chocolate Rabbit, his own record label imprint. His [[Mr. Muthafuckin' eXquire (album)|self-titled debut studio album]] was independently released in 2019.

==Early life==
Hugh Anthony Allison<ref name=vice2013/> was born in the [[Brooklyn]] borough of [[New York City]] in 1986.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/mr-muthafuckin-exquire-mn0002789537/biography|title=Mr. Muthafuckin' eXquire: Biography|work=[[AllMusic]]|first=Neil Z.|last=Yeung|accessdate=October 26, 2019}}</ref> He grew up in Brooklyn's [[Crown Heights, Brooklyn|Crown Heights]] neighborhood.<ref name=vice2013>{{cite web|url=https://www.vice.com/en_us/article/rybk56/the-cosmic-adventures-of-mr-muthafuckin-exquire|title=The Cosmic Adventures of Mr. Muthafuckin' eXquire|work=[[Vice (magazine)|Vice]]|first=Drew|last=Millard|date=May 20, 2013|accessdate=October 26, 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.mtv.com/news/1712604/mr-mfn-exquire-brooklyn-vma/|title=Mr. MFN eXquire Flavors Brooklyn's 'Melting Pot' With Dope Rhymes|work=[[MTV]]|first=Rob|last=Markman|date=August 19, 2013|accessdate=}}</ref> Since his parents separated at an early age, he spent his weekdays with his mother and weekends with his father.<ref name=vice2013/> He dropped out of high school at the age of 15.<ref name=vice2013/>

==Career==
In 2008, eXquire released his first full-length project, a mixtape titled ''The Big Fat Kill''.<ref name=thefader2011>{{cite web|url=http://www.thefader.com/2011/12/06/gen-f-mr-muthafuckin-exquire|title=GEN F: Mr. Muthafuckin eXquire|work=[[The Fader]]|first=Brendan|last=Frederick|date=December 6, 2011|accessdate=}}</ref> He first began gaining major recognition in June 2011, when he released the music video for a song titled "Huzzah".<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.thefader.com/2011/06/17/video-mr-muthafukin-exquire-huzzah|title=Video: Mr. Muthafukin Exquire, "Huzzah"|work=[[The Fader]]|first=Andrew|last=Noz|date=June 17, 2011|accessdate=}}</ref> On September 11, 2011, eXquire released his second mixtape, titled ''[[Lost in Translation (mixtape)|Lost in Translation]]'', which features "Huzzah".<ref>{{cite web|url=http://pitchfork.com/reviews/albums/15864-lost-in-translation/|title=Mr. Muthafuckin' eXquire: Lost in Translation|work=[[Pitchfork (website)|Pitchfork]]|first=Ian|last=Cohen|date=September 29, 2011|accessdate=}}</ref> The mixtape, which was released on [[Mishka NYC]], includes 18-tracks, one of them being the remix to "Huzzah", titled "The Last Huzzah". The remix, which features guest verses from fellow American rappers [[Despot (rapper)|Despot]], [[Heems]], [[Kool A.D.]], [[Danny Brown]] and [[El-P]], was called one of the best songs of 2011 by ''[[Rolling Stone]]''.<ref>{{cite magazine|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/lists/50-best-singles-of-2011-20111207/mr-muthafuckin-exquire-feat-despot-das-racist-danny-brown-and-el-p-the-last-huzzah-20111209|title=46 - Mr. Muthafuckin' eXquire feat. Despot, Das Racist, Danny Brown and El-P, 'The Last Huzzah'|magazine=[[Rolling Stone]]|date=December 7, 2011|accessdate=}}</ref> eXquire's third mixtape, titled ''Merry eXmas & Suck My Dick'', was released on December 25, 2011.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://pitchfork.com/reviews/albums/16141-merry-exmas-suck-my-dick/|title=Mr. Muthafuckin' eXquire: Merry eXmas & Suck My Dick|work=[[Pitchfork (website)|Pitchfork]]|first=Ian|last=Cohen|date=January 5, 2012|accessdate=}}</ref>

On January 7, 2015, eXquire released ''Merry eXmas & SMD 2'', the sequel to his 2011 Christmas mixtape.<ref name="smd2">{{cite web|url=http://www.hotnewhiphop.com/mr-muthafuckin-exquire-merry-ex-mas-and-smd-2-new-mixtape.115663.html|title=Mr. Muthafuckin eXquire - Merry eX-Mas And SMD 2|work=HotNewHipHop|first=Patrick|last=Lyons|date=January 8, 2015|accessdate=}}</ref> On January 8, 2015, eXquire released another mixtape, this one titled ''Live from the Danger Room'', which is compiled of unreleased tracks.<ref name="LDR">{{cite web|url=http://www.hotnewhiphop.com/mr-muthafuckin-exquire-live-from-the-danger-room-new-mixtape.115665.html|title=Mr. Muthafuckin eXquire - Live From The Danger Room|work=HotNewHipHop|first=Kevin|last=Goddard|date=January 8, 2015|accessdate=}}</ref> On October 8, 2015, eXquire released his second EP, titled ''Live Forever''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://nahright.com/news/2015/10/22/mr-muthafuckin-exquire-live-forever-ep-stream/|title=Nah Right|accessdate=}}</ref> On July 12, 2019, eXquire released his [[Mr. Muthafuckin' eXquire (album)|self-titled debut studio album]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://pitchfork.com/news/7-albums-out-today-you-should-listen-to-now-purple-mountains-blood-orange-and-more/|title=7 Albums Out Today You Should Listen to Now: Purple Mountains, Blood Orange, and More|work=[[Pitchfork (website)|Pitchfork]]|first=Matthew|last=Strauss|date=July 12, 2019|accessdate=October 27, 2019}}</ref>

==Style and influences==
eXquire has stated that the capital X in his stage name derives from [[DMX (rapper)|DMX]].<ref name=thefader2011/>
