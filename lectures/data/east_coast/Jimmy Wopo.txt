'''Travon DaShawn Frank Smart''' (January 13, 1997 – June 18, 2018), better known by his stage name '''Jimmy Wopo''', was an American rapper from [[Pittsburgh]], [[Pennsylvania]].

Wopo was an affiliate of fellow Pittsburgh-based rapper [[Wiz Khalifa]] and his [[Taylor Gang Entertainment|Taylor Gang]] imprint. He made his first high-profile appearance with his 2016 breakout single, "Elm Street", produced by Stvii B, which ''[[Complex (magazine)|Complex]]'' included in the "'Bout to Blow: 10 Dope New Songs You Should Be Hearing Everywhere Soon" list.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.complex.com/music/2016/07/bout-to-blow-july/|title=Bout to Blow: 10 Dope New Songs You Should Be Hearing Everywhere Soon|work=Complex|accessdate=November 27, 2016}}</ref> Following that success, he collaborated with several prominent rappers including Wiz Khalifa, [[Sonny Digital]], [[21 Savage]] and more.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.hotnewhiphop.com/hardo-todays-a-good-day-feat-wiz-khalifa-and-jimmy-wopo-new-song.1972012.html|title=Hardo-Todays A Good Day Featuring Wiz Khalifa and Jimmy Wopo|work=Hot New Hip Hop|accessdate=November 27, 2016}}</ref> After receiving a co-sign from hip hop producer [[Mike Will Made It]], rap duo [[Rae Sremmurd]] brought Wopo out to perform during their set at the Pittsburgh stop of their SremmLife 2 Tour.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://twitter.com/MikeWiLLMadeIt/status/776463134160629760|title=Mike Will Made It Co-Signs Jimmy Wopo|work=Twitter|accessdate=November 27, 2016}}</ref> Wopo was murdered in a [[drive-by shooting]] on June 18, 2018.

== Career ==
In January 2015, Wopo began uploading original tracks to his SoundCloud account. Taylor Maglin, the owner of the Pittsburgh-based blog ''Daily Loud'', partnered with him and began to publicize his music. This additional push helped him to accumulate millions of views on his music videos on his YouTube channel.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://hiphopdx.com/videos/id.24514/title.bangeralert-jimmy-wopo-says-oh-my|title=#BangerAlert: Jimmy Wopo Says "Oh My"|work=Hip Hop DX|date=August 25, 2016|accessdate=November 27, 2016}}</ref> Joining the wave of Wopo's viral success, urban hip hop blog [[WorldStarHipHop]] exclusively debuted five of his music videos in 2016.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.worldstarhiphop.com/videos/search.php?s=jimmy+wopo|title=Jimmy Wopo videos|work=WorldStarHipHop|accessdate=November 27, 2016}}</ref> On July 24, 2016, he premiered his debut project, ''Woponese'', on Daily Loud.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://thedailyloud.com/jimmy-wopo-woponese-mixtape/|title=Jimmy Wopo Woponese mixtape|work=Daily Loud|accessdate=November 27, 2016|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190125170422/http://thedailyloud.com/jimmy-wopo-woponese-mixtape/|archive-date=January 25, 2019|url-status=dead}}</ref> This 8-track mixtape included the single "Back Door", which features [[Sonny Digital]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://indy.livemixtapes.com/mixtapes/40418/jimmy-wopo-woponese.html|title=Jimmy Wopo "Woponese" Mixtape|work=Live Mixtapes|accessdate=November 27, 2016}}</ref> In October 2016, [[Riff Raff (rapper)|Riff Raff]] tapped Wopo for a feature on the track "Stay Away from You" on his ''Balloween'' mixtape.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://hypebeast.com/2016/10/riff-raff-balloween-mixtape|title=Stream Riff Raff's New 'Balloween' Mixtape|work=Hypebeast|date=October 25, 2016|accessdate=November 27, 2016}}</ref> On November 25, 2016, he released a joint album with his fellow Pittsburgh-based rapper Hardo. This nine-track project featured ShadyHigler, [[21 Savage]] and [[Wiz Khalifa]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/trapnese/id1171293962|title=Trapnese by Hardo and Jimmy Wopo|work=iTunes|accessdate=November 27, 2016}}</ref>

As many as fifty completed, unreleased songs will be released posthumously, according to Wopo's manager.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.post-gazette.com/ae/music/2018/06/27/Jimmy-Wopo-unreleased-songs-muney-lane-muzik-2-jimmy-digital-taylor-gang-atlantic-records/stories/201806270157|title=Slain Pittsburgh rapper Jimmy Wopo left behind more than 50 unreleased songs|work=Pittsburgh Post-Gazette|access-date=June 29, 2018|language=en}}</ref>

== Legal issues ==
On February 7, 2016, Wopo was arrested during a traffic stop in [[Washington County, Pennsylvania]]. Police arrested Wopo and two others after finding two stamp bags of suspected heroin and a small amount of marijuana. He was arraigned on drug charges and drinking charges and placed in jail with a $25,000 bond. Later, while out, Wopo, landed in jail because of probation violation. Wopo failed to inform officials of his trip to New York State, where he was caught and imprisoned on such charges. While in jail, he wrote an album, ''Back Against the Wall'', which he recorded the first day he got out of jail.<ref name="Arrest">{{cite web|url=http://www.observer-reporter.com/20160208/eight_arrested_on_drug_charges_in_washington_county_over_weekend|title=Eight arrested on drug charges in Washington County over weekend|website=Observer-Reporter|first=Kathie|last=Warco|date=February 8, 2016}}</ref>

== Death ==
At 4:22 PM EDT on June 18, 2018, Smart and a male passenger were shot in Pittsburgh's [[Hill District (Pittsburgh)|Hill District]] neighborhood.<ref name="fader">{{Cite news|url=http://www.thefader.com/2018/06/19/rip-jimmy-wopo-pittsburgh-taylor-gang|title=R.I.P. Jimmy Wopo|work=The Fader|access-date=June 21, 2018|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |date=2018-06-18 |title=Rapper Jimmy Wopo shot and killed in Pittsburgh's Hill District |url=https://www.post-gazette.com/news/crime-courts/2018/06/18/rapper-Jimmy-Wopo-shot-Pittsburgh-Hill-District/stories/201806180180 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200811165510/https://www.post-gazette.com/news/crime-courts/2018/06/18/rapper-Jimmy-Wopo-shot-Pittsburgh-Hill-District/stories/201806180180 |archive-date=2020-08-11 |access-date=2022-07-11 |website=Pittsburgh Post-Gazette |language=en}}</ref> The passenger survived, but Smart, who was shot in the head, died after being rushed to the [[UPMC Presbyterian]] at 5:56 PM.<ref>{{Cite web|date=June 19, 2018|title=Rapper Jimmy Wopo shot and killed in Pittsburgh's Hill District|url=https://www.post-gazette.com/news/crime-courts/2018/06/18/rapper-Jimmy-Wopo-shot-Pittsburgh-Hill-District/stories/201806180180|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180726194125/https://www.post-gazette.com/news/crime-courts/2018/06/18/rapper-Jimmy-Wopo-shot-Pittsburgh-Hill-District/stories/201806180180|archive-date=July 26, 2018|access-date=June 18, 2021|website=Pittsburgh Post-Gazette|language=en}}</ref> He was 21 years old, and left behind three children.<ref name=":0">{{cite web|url=https://www.cnn.com/2018/06/19/entertainment/jimmy-wopo-dead/index.html|title=Rapper Jimmy Wopo gunned down in Pittsburgh|last=Respers France|first=Lisa|date=June 19, 2018|work=[[CNN]]|accessdate=June 19, 2018}}</ref> As of 2023, no one has been charged for the murder.

Smart has been posthumously implicated in numerous gang related crimes. Multiple members of his gang were sentenced to prison in 2021.<ref>{{cite web|title=The Darkside Of Pittsburgh: Jimmy Wopo and His Notorious 11 Hunnit Gang|url=https://raptology.com/documentary/the-darkside-of-pittsburgh-jimmy-wopo-and-his-notorious-11-hunnit-gang/}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=https://allhiphop.com/news/jimmy-wopo-gang-sentenced-to-prison/|url=https://allhiphop.com/news/jimmy-wopo-gang-sentenced-to-prison/}}</ref>
