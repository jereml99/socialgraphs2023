'''Stephen Michael Feigenbaum''',<ref name="BostonGlobe">{{cite web|last=Sullivan|first=James|url=https://www.bostonglobe.com/2022/05/11/arts/johan-lenoxs-adult-angst-with-beats-strings/|title=Johan Lenox’s adult angst, with beats and strings|work=[[The Boston Globe]]|date=May 11, 2022|access-date=June 1, 2022}}</ref> known professionally as '''Johan Lenox''', is an American singer, composer and producer. His musical style combines elements of [[trap music]], [[hip hop music|hip hop]], [[Pop music|pop]] and [[classical music]]. He began gaining attention for his production for high-profile artists like [[Travis Scott]] and [[Kanye West]], among others.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/johan-lenox-mn0003871169/credits|title=Johan Lenox, Credits|work=[[AllMusic]]|access-date=June 1, 2022}}</ref> He released his first project in 2019, and has released three more since.

==Early life==
Feigenbaum was raised in [[Massachusetts]], where he spent summers studying classical music at [[Tanglewood]] in [[Lenox, Massachusetts]].<ref name="BostonGlobe" /> He spent six years at [[Yale University]] studying music. He was inspired upon hearing [[Kanye West]]'s 2010 album, ''[[My Beautiful Dark Twisted Fantasy]]'', realizing the potential of combining classical music with modern pop and hip hop.<ref name="WBUR">{{cite web|last=Ruddell|first=Charley|url=https://www.wbur.org/news/2022/05/12/johan-lenox-debut-album-wdywtbwygu|title=Musician Johan Lenox debuts symphonically inspired pop album 'WDYWTBWYGU'|work=[[WBUR-FM]]|access-date=June 1, 2022}}</ref>

==Career==
===2016-2018: "Yeethoven"===
Feigenbaum developed his stage name combining the names of composer [[Johannes Brahms]] and the town of Lenox, Massachusetts, where he studied classical music.<ref name="BostonGlobe" /> Feigenbaum began receiving attention in 2016, co-creating and arranging music for a live orchestra event called "Yeethoven", a project that combines and compares the works of [[Kanye West]] and [[Beethoven]].<ref name="Yeethoven">{{cite web|last=Bruner|first=Raisa|url=https://time.com/5051044/yeethoven-kanye-west-beethoven-concert/|title=How a Live Orchestra Is Mashing Up Kanye West and Beethoven|work=Time (magazine)|date=December 14, 2017|access-date=June 1, 2022}}</ref> This project captured the attention of [[Time (magazine)|''Time'']], [[Pitchfork (website)|''Pitchfork'']],<ref>{{cite web|last=Yoo|first=Noah|url=https://pitchfork.com/news/64868-watch-yeethoven-orchestra-perform-kanye-wests-blood-on-the-leaves-and-new-slaves/|title=Watch "Yeethoven" Orchestra Perform Kanye West's "Blood on the Leaves" and "New Slaves"|date=April 17, 2016|access-date=June 1, 2022}}</ref> ''[[The Atlantic]]''<ref>{{cite web|last=Kornhaber|first=Spencer|url=https://www.theatlantic.com/entertainment/archive/2018/07/yeethoven/564206/|title=What Classical Music Can Learn From Kanye West|date=July 2, 2018|access-date=June 1, 2022}}</ref> and artists like [[Vic Mensa]] and producer [[Mike Dean (record producer)|Mike Dean]]. Impressed by the work, Dean invited Feigenbaum to help produce for several Kanye West projects in 2018, including [[Nas]]' ''[[Nasir (album)|Nasir]]'' and [[Teyana Taylor]]'s ''[[The Album (Teyana Taylor album)|The Album]]''.<ref>{{cite web|last=Hellerbach|first=Miki|url=https://www.okayplayer.com/music/johan-lenox-interview.html|title=Meet Johan Lenox: The Composer Adding Classical Music to Your Favorite Rap Songs|work=[[Okayplayer]]|access-date=June 1, 2022}}</ref>

===2019-2022: Solo work, ''WDYWTBWYGU'', and isomonstrosity===
In the subsequent years, Feigenbaum released a series of three projects under [[Island Records]]; ''Everybody's Cool but Me'' in 2019, ''Cancel the Party'' in 2020, and ''World on Fire'' in 2021. The song "Deep Reverence" by Big Sean received a nomination for [[Grammy Award for Best Rap Performance|Best Rap Performance]] at the [[63rd Annual Grammy Awards]], bringing Feigenbaum his first [[Grammy Awards|Grammy]] nomination.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2020-12-15|title=Big Sean|url=https://www.grammy.com/grammys/artists/big-sean/355|access-date=2021-03-17|website=GRAMMY.com}}</ref> His fourth project, which he dubbed his "debut album", was released independently in 2022 and is titled ''WDYWTBWYGU'' (an acronym of ''What Do You Want to Be When You Grow Up''), and features [[KayCyy]], [[Ant Clemons]], [[Mr. Hudson]], [[070 Shake]], among others. In mid-2022, he joined 070 Shake as the opener for her nationwide tour for her second studio album, ''You Can't Kill Me''. At the end of the year he released the debut album from isomonstrosity, a collaborative group with [[Ellen Reid]] and Yuga Cohler, performed by [[International Contemporary Ensemble]] then chopped and rearranged with vocals added by artists including [[Danny Brown]], [[Kacy Hill]], and [[Empress Of]]. Released on the [[Brassland]] label, The FADER said it was "quite possibly the platonic ideal of a pandemic album."<ref>{{Cite web|date=2022-09-20|title=The FADER|url=https://www.thefader.com/2022/09/20/song-you-need-a-pandemic-symphony-pulls-danny-brown-and-645ar-out-of-their-comfort-zones|website=thefader.com}}</ref>
