'''Charles Edward Hugo''' (born February 24, 1974) is an American record producer and multi-instrumentalist. With close friend [[Pharrell Williams]], he formed the production duo [[the Neptunes]] in the early 1990s, with whom he has produced songs for many recording artists. He is also a member of the band [[N.E.R.D.]], alongside Williams and [[Shay Haley]].

==Early Life==
Charles Edward Hugo was born February 24, 1979, in [[Portsmouth, Virginia]] to a Filipino-American family. His mother was a lab technician and his father served in the United States Navy.<ref>{{cite web |author1=Diep, Eric |title=20 Years Later, Neptunes Producer Chad Hugo Is Still Searching For More |url=https://www.billboard.com/music/rb-hip-hop/chad-hugo-neptunes-interview-1235079287/ |website=Billboard |access-date=9 September 2023}}</ref> His early musical experiences included recording [[mixtapes]] in his home using a boombox. He later joined his school band, playing the saxophone, through which he met [[Pharrell Williams]], with whom he began producing music. They formed [[The Neptunes]], and in 1992, and upon entering a local talent contest, they were discovered by Teddy Riley, whose studio was close to Williams' school.<ref name="TheAge">{{cite web |title=Like the song? These guys wrote it |url=https://www.theage.com.au/entertainment/music/like-the-song-these-guys-wrote-it-20040515-gdxu7b.html |website=The Age |access-date=10 September 2023}}</ref> Williams also introduced Hugo to Hayley,<ref>{{cite web |title=Pharrell Williams on meeting his Neptunes partner Chad Hugo |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=72pQQl7dSE4 |publisher=CBS Sunday Morning |access-date=17 September 2023}}</ref> with whom they formed the group [[N.E.R.D]] in 1999.

==Career==
{{Main|The Neptunes|N.E.R.D}}
Through working with Riley, the Neptunes wrote [[Wreckx-n-Effect]]'s 1992 song "[[Rump Shaker (song)|Rump Shaker]]" while still in school.<ref name="TheAge"/> They also worked with Riley's group [[Blackstreet]], co-writing the single "[[Tonight's the Night (Blackstreet song)|Tonight's the Night]]" from [[Blackstreet (album)|their self-titled debut album]].<ref name="TheAge"/>

The Neptunes continued their production work, contributing to projects by [[New York City]]-based rapper [[N.O.R.E.|Noreaga]], [[Ol' Dirty Bastard]], and [[Kelis]]<ref name="TheAge"/> for whom they would entirely produce her debut studio album, ''[[Kaleidoscope (Kelis album)|Kaleidoscope]]'' (1999), and her 2001 album ''[[Wanderland]]''.<ref name="Kaleidoscope">{{cite web |last1=Bierut |first1=Patrick |title='Kaleidoscope': How Kelis Rewrote The R&B Rulebook |url=https://www.udiscovermusic.com/stories/kelis-kaleidoscope-album/ |publisher=Udiscovermusic.com |access-date=June 14, 2020 |date=March 7, 2020}}</ref> The Neptunes also produced hip hop duo [[Clipse]]'s album, ''[[Exclusive Audio Footage]]'' (1999), which went unreleased until 2022.<ref>{{cite web |title=On 'Exclusive Audio Footage,' The Long-Lost Clipse Album |url=https://magazine.vinylmeplease.com/magazine/exclusive-audio-footage/ |website=Vinyl Me, Please |access-date=June 14, 2020 |date=April 19, 2018}}</ref> Hugo met [[Jay-Z]] during a studio session for his second studio album, [[In My Lifetime, Vol. 1]], contributing on saxophone to [[The City Is Mine]],<ref>{{cite web |last1=Jenkins |first1=Craig |title=The Humble Hitmaker |url=https://www.vulture.com/2022/05/producer-chad-hugo-neptunes-interview-pharrell.html |website=Vulture |access-date=17 September 2023}}</ref> with the Neptunes going on to produce a number of tracks with Jay-Z.

In 2001, [[N.E.R.D.]] released their debut studio album, ''[[In Search of... (N.E.R.D. album)|In Search of...]]'', featuring production from the Neptunes.<ref name="InSearchOfPitchfork">{{cite web |last1=Bernstein |first1=Michael |title=N.E.R.D.: In Search of... Album Review |url=https://pitchfork.com/reviews/albums/5748-in-search-of/ |website=[[Pitchfork (website)|Pitchfork]] |access-date=June 15, 2020 |date=March 25, 2002}}</ref> In the same year, the Neptunes gained their first worldwide hit with [[Britney Spears]]' single, "[[I'm a Slave 4 U]]", for her eponymous third studio album, ''[[Britney (album)|Britney]]''.<ref>{{cite web |title=The Neptunes produced Britney Spears 'Slave 4 U' |url=https://www.capitalxtra.com/artists/pharrell-williams/lists/facts/britney-spears/ |website=[[Capital Xtra]] |access-date=June 14, 2020}}</ref> The Neptunes wrote and produced [[NSYNC]]'s final single, "[[Girlfriend (NSYNC song)|Girlfriend]]", then wrote and produced most of co-lead singer [[Justin Timberlake]]'s debut solo album, ''[[Justified (album)|Justified]]'' (2002), including lead singles "[[Like I Love You]]", "[[Rock Your Body]]", and "[[Señorita (Justin Timberlake song)|Señorita]]".<ref>{{cite web |last1=Kaufman |first1=Gil |title=Preview Of Justin Timberlake Album From Neptunes' Hugo |url=http://www.mtv.com/news/1457175/preview-of-justin-timberlake-album-fromneptunes-hugo/ |publisher=[[MTV]] |access-date=June 14, 2020 |date=August 23, 2002}}</ref> In 2003, the Neptunes released a compilation album, ''[[Clones (album)|Clones]]'', featuring songs and remixes from various [[Star Trak Entertainment|Star Trak]] artists. The Neptunes won "Producer of the Year" at the [[46th Annual Grammy Awards|2004 Grammy Awards]].<ref name="2004Grammys">{{cite web |title=The Neptunes earn long-overdue Grammy |url=https://www.today.com/popculture/neptunes-earn-long-overdue-grammy-wbna4217864 |work=Today |access-date=June 14, 2020 |date=February 9, 2004}}</ref>

Hugo and [[Pharrell Williams]] were announced to be inducted into the [[Songwriters Hall of Fame]] as a part of the 2020 class.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.hollywoodreporter.com/amp/news/mariah-carey-neptunes-inducted-songwriters-hall-fame-1270360|title = Mariah Carey, Neptunes Inducted into Songwriters Hall of Fame|website = [[The Hollywood Reporter]]|date = January 16, 2020}}</ref>
