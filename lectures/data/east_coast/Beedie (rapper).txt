'''Brian Benjamin Green''' (born January 19, 1988), better known by his [[stage name]] '''Beedie''', is an American [[rapper]] based out of [[Pittsburgh|Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania]]. Born in [[New York City]] to a family of working entertainers, he began his music career in 2007 with fellow Pittsburgh emcee [[Mac Miller]] as the duo [[The Ill Spoken]].

==Early life and education==
Beedie was born in New York City and lived in Washington, D.C. and Oklahoma, before moving to Pittsburgh in 2001 with his mother, Maria Posvar, and younger brother.<ref name="http://pittnews.com/newsstory/beedie-seeks-to-establish-rap-culture-in-steel-city/">{{Cite web|url=http://pittnews.com/newsstory/beedie-seeks-to-establish-rap-culture-in-steel-city/|title = Beedie seeks to establish rap culture in Steel City|date = January 11, 2010}}</ref> Posvar graduated from Pittsburgh's [[Carnegie Mellon University]] and had done some acting, including a role in ''[[Days of Our Lives]]''. His father was a sports announcer. His maternal grandparents are the late [[Wesley Posvar]], who was the [[Chancellor (education)|Chancellor]] of the [[University of Pittsburgh]] from 1967 to 1991, and his wife [[Mildred Miller]], an opera singer who performed at the [[Metropolitan Opera]] from the 1950s to 1970s and then founded the [[Opera Theater of Pittsburgh]].<ref name=":1">{{Cite web|url = http://www.pghcitypaper.com/pittsburgh/being-beedie/Content?oid=1569774|title = Being Beedie|last = Webb|first = Rory D.|website = Pittsburgh City Paper|access-date = 2016-04-07}}</ref>

Beedie started writing rhymes starting at age 12. He attended [[Taylor Allderdice High School]], but dropped out after his parents kicked him out of the house. He took classes at [[Community College of Allegheny County]] to earn his [[General Educational Development|GED]].<ref name=":1"/>

==Music career==

===The Ill Spoken===
In 2007, when Beedie was 19, he and 15-year-old [[Mac Miller]] (then known as EZ Mac), founded the rap duo The Ill Spoken.<ref name=":1"/> After performing at shows around Pittsburgh and releasing ''How High: The Mixtape'' with Miller on October 27, 2008,<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|url = http://www.mixtapetorrent.com/dj-diggz-dj-rated-r-ill-spoken-how-high|title = DJ Diggz , DJ Rated R & The Ill Spoken - How High (The Mixtape) {{!}} MixtapeTorrent.com|website = www.mixtapetorrent.com|access-date = 2016-04-07}}</ref> the two emcees began focusing on solo careers.<ref name=":1"/> Beedie has cited [[J. Dilla]] and [[Kanye West]] as musical influences, and describes his sound as "classic hip-hop with a new-school twist".<ref name="http://pittnews.com/newsstory/beedie-seeks-to-establish-rap-culture-in-steel-city/"/>

===''The Beat Bully LP'' & Varsity Squad===
On June 30, 2011, Beedie released his first album unofficially, titled ''The Beat Bully LP''.<ref name="http://www.brainofbmw.com/2011/06/beatbullylp/">{{Cite web |url=http://www.brainofbmw.com/2011/06/beatbullylp/ |title=Beedie "The Beat Bully" LP |access-date=September 27, 2011 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20111005165652/http://www.brainofbmw.com/2011/06/beatbullylp/ |archive-date=October 5, 2011 |url-status=dead |df=mdy-all }}</ref> The album was well received by music critics.<ref name="http://www.pittsburghcitypaper.ws/pittsburgh/local-mc-beedie-is-ready-for-the-boom-bap-big-time/Content?oid=1397701">{{Cite web|url=http://www.pittsburghcitypaper.ws/pittsburgh/local-mc-beedie-is-ready-for-the-boom-bap-big-time/Content?oid=1397701|title = Pittsburghcitypaper.ws}}</ref> According to Beedie, this album shows a lot of growth as an artist, and he considers it his official debut.<ref name="http://hot16sand1.com/?p=419">{{Cite web |url=http://hot16sand1.com/?p=419 |title=NBA, Basketball, Rap |access-date=September 27, 2011 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120330194144/http://hot16sand1.com/?p=419 |archive-date=March 30, 2012 |url-status=dead }}</ref> Varsity Squad is a group/crew consisting of emcees Beedie and Jon Quest, who announced the forming of the group while performing alongside [[Method Man]] & [[Redman (rapper)|Redman]] in Pittsburgh in August 2010.<ref name="http://www.reppittsburghhiphop.com/2010/08/19/varsity-squad-debuts-at-meth-red-show/">http://www.reppittsburghhiphop.com/2010/08/19/varsity-squad-debuts-at-meth-red-show/ {{Dead link|date=February 2022}}</ref> The two emcees released a collaborative album titled ''New School Boom Bap'' on February 7, 2012. Their work garnered the attention of many influential underground acts such as [[One Be Lo]] of [[Binary Star (hip hop group)|Binary Star]], who tapped Varsity Squad for a feature on his 2011 ''Laborhood Part 2'' mixtape in the song "U Minor, We Major",<ref>{{Cite web|url = https://onebelo.bandcamp.com/album/laborhood-part-2|title = Laborhood Part 2, by One Be Lo|website = One Be Lo|access-date = 2016-04-08}}</ref> as well as producer [[Buckwild (music producer)|Buckwild]] of [[Diggin' in the Crates Crew|DITC]].<ref name=":2">{{Citation|last = TreeJTV2|title = Beedie - Robert Marley (prod. Buckwild)|date = 2012-08-06|url = https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=r7P1Wx5yVHs|accessdate = 2016-04-08}}</ref><ref name=":3">{{Citation|last = TreeJTV2|title = Beedie - Slow Ride (prod. Buckwild & Nick Brongers)|date = 2013-12-05|url = https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=V8LOKScRQAA|accessdate = 2016-04-08}}</ref>

=== ''Above The Weather'' ===
After releasing a stream of viral music videos on [[YouTube]],<ref>{{Cite web|url = https://www.youtube.com/treejtv2|title = TreeJTV2|website = YouTube|access-date = 2016-04-08}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url = https://www.youtube.com/beedieland|title = BeedieLand|website = YouTube|access-date = 2016-04-08}}</ref> Beedie released his next solo effort titled ''Above The Weather'' on August 13, 2012.<ref name=":1" /><ref name=":4">{{Cite web|url = http://www.datpiff.com/Beedie-Above-The-Weather-mixtape.383845.html|title = Beedie - Above The Weather|website = DatPiff|access-date = 2016-04-07}}</ref> The project featured production from the likes of [[Statik Selektah]],<ref>{{Cite web|url = http://breal.tv/2013/04/beedie-home-prod-statik-selektah/|title = Beedie - "Home" {{!}} Prod. Statik Selektah {{!}} BREAL.TV|website = BREAL.TV|date = April 9, 2013|language = en-US|access-date = 2016-04-08}}</ref><ref>{{Citation|last = TreeJTV2|title = Beedie (Prod. The release garnered the attention of many publications including the ''[[Pittsburgh Post-Gazette]]''<ref>{{Cite web|url = http://www.post-gazette.com/ae/music/2012/09/13/Local-Scene-Beedie-back-with-new-album/stories/201209130244|title = Local Scene: Beedie back with new album|website = Pittsburgh Post-Gazette|access-date = 2016-04-08}}</ref> and ''[[Pittsburgh City Paper]]'', who featured Beedie for a cover story on September 26, 2012.<ref name=":1" /> Soon after, Beedie and Mac Miller reunited to release another track as The Ill Spoken on October 23, 2012.<ref>{{Cite web|url = http://www.complex.com/music/2012/10/listen-the-ill-spoken-mac-miller-beedie-paid-dues-the-reunion|title = Listen: The Ill Spoken (Mac Miller & Beedie) "Paid Dues (The Reunion)"|website = Complex|access-date = 2016-04-08}}</ref> On January 5, 2013, producer Git released a single titled "Magic Spaceship" featuring [[Sean Price]], Beedie and fellow Pittsburgh rapper B. === ''Counter Culture'' & work with Wiz Khalifa ===
Beedie appeared on [[Sirius Satellite Radio|Sirius Radio]]'s [[Shade 45]] station with Statik Selektah on May 30, 2013, where he announced his next release would be titled ''Counter Culture''.<ref>{{Cite web|url = https://twitter.com/805Adub/status/340265597269057536|title = Adub on Twitter|website = Twitter|access-date = 2016-04-08}}</ref> After many delays, the album was finally released digitally on December 22, 2015,<ref name=":5">{{Cite web|url = https://twitter.com/Beedie412/status/679471961202913280|title = Beedie on Twitter|website = Twitter|access-date = 2016-04-07}}</ref> followed shortly after with a mixtape titled ''B-$ide$ Vol. 1: Lo$t Dream$'' released on Beedie's 28th birthday, January 19, 2016.<ref name=":6">{{Cite web|url = https://twitter.com/Beedie412/status/689559618557575170|title = Beedie on Twitter|website = Twitter|access-date = 2016-04-07}}</ref> As suggested by the title, the mixtape featured lost and scrapped songs during the long recording process between ''Above The Weather'' and ''Counter Culture''.<ref name=":6" /> Around this time Beedie formed a friendship with fellow Pittsburgh artist [[Wiz Khalifa]],<ref>{{Cite web|url = http://www.complex.com/music/2014/05/the-25-best-hip-hop-instagram-pictures-of-the-week-may-17/beedie-and-wiz-khalifa|title = The 25 Best Hip-Hop Instagram Pictures Of The Week - Beedie and Wiz Khalifa|website = Complex|access-date = 2016-04-08}}</ref> who brought out Beedie to perform on various tour dates<ref>{{Citation|last = Wiz Khalifa|title = DayToday: Showtime|date = 2014-06-25|url = https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=G7-Y8t6x_fU&t=61|accessdate = 2016-04-08}}</ref> and eventually featuring on the song "Make A Wish" on ''Counter Culture''.<ref>{{Cite web|url = http://www.complex.com/music/2015/03/beedie-new-song-premiere-feat-wiz-khalifa|title = Premiere: Listen to Beedie's "Make a Wish" f/ Wiz Khalifa|website = Complex|access-date = 2016-04-08}}</ref>
