'''Joseph Williams Jr.''' (born June 22, 1965), better known by the stage name '''Just-Ice''', is an American [[rapper]] from [[New York City]].

== Career ==
In 1986 he was charged with the murder of drug dealer Ludlaw DeSouza, but later proven innocent.<ref name="Bradley">Bradley, John Ed (1986) "[https://www.washingtonpost.com/archive/lifestyle/magazine/1986/09/07/survival-of-the-hippest/a1fca33c-0da0-4169-aeb0-406749343ad9/ Survival of the Hippest]", ''[[Washington Post]]'', September 7, 1986. Retrieved April 29, 2017</ref><ref name="Lazell">Lazell, Barry (1997) ''Indie Hits 1980â€“1989'', Cherry Red Books, {{ISBN|0-9517206-9-4}}, p. 125</ref> His third album, ''[[The Desolate One]]'' (1989), had minor success in the United Kingdom, reaching no. 16 on the [[UK Independent Chart]].<ref name="Lazell" />

Williams relocated from the Ft. Greene area in Brooklyn to the Castle Hill section of the Bronx in his early adolescent years. He currently resides in the Bronx which he considers his hometown.

[[Bradley Nowell]] ([[Sublime (band)|Sublime]]) brought Just-Ice's vinyl record ''[[The Desolate One]]'' to KROQ 106.7 FM Radio Station in California in the 1990s at the height of the band's success and proclaimed "You can drop the needle anywhere on this record and I guarantee you, GOLD!"<ref>{{ cite web |url=http://sublimearchive.com/links.html |archive-url=https://archive.today/20020607172320/http://sublimearchive.com/links.html |url-status=dead |archive-date=2002-06-07 |title=Sublime Archive }}</ref>  Of which, the DJ allowed and played "NA TOUCH DA JUST".
