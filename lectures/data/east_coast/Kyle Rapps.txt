'''Kyle Sutton''' (born 1980),<ref name="hotnewhiphop">[http://www.hotnewhiphop.com/KyleRapps/profile/ Kyle Rapps profile], hotnewhiphop.com. Accessed October 17, 2014.</ref> better known by his stage name '''Kyle Rapps''', is an American hip hop artist from [[New York City]].

==Early life and education==
Sutton was born in [[Grand Rapids, Michigan]],<ref name="hotnewhiphop"/> and raised in [[Princeton, New Jersey]],<ref>Richard Spadine, [http://www.djbooth.net/index/tracks/review/kyle-rapps-architecture “Kyle Rapps – Architecture,”] DJ Booth, December 20, 2013.</ref> primarily by his mother. His father was a minister.<ref name="mhidalgo">Marcel Hidalgo, [http://www.prefixmag.com/features/aaron-cohen-action-bronson-james-taylor-krs-one-kyle-rapps-michael-jackson-murs/kyle-rapps-discusses-breaking-out-of-the-cocoon/74115/ “Kyle Rapps Discusses Breaking Out Of The Cocoon,”] ''Prefix'', July 1, 2013.</ref> Sutton attended [[Rutgers University]], earning a BA in Spanish.<ref name="cstetler">Carrie Stetler, [http://news.rutgers.edu/issue.2012-01-03.4307075525/article.2012-01-27.6613347299#.VCv8vCldXT2 “Mayhem Poets Tour the World, but Their Legacy Stays on Campus,”] Rutgers Today, January 27, 2012.</ref> In 2015, he relocated from [[Harlem, New York]] to [[Mexico City, Mexico]].<ref name="nvukorepa">Nick Vukorepa, [http://www.earmilk.com/2013/12/24/kyle-rapps-architecture-feat-murs-video/ “Kyle Rapps – ‘Architecture’ (Feat. Murs),”] Earmilk, December 24, 2013.</ref><ref>[http://insomniacmagazine.com/2015/10/kyle-rapps-drops-df/ "Kyle Rapps Drops 'DF',"] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20151222090503/http://insomniacmagazine.com/2015/10/kyle-rapps-drops-df/ |date=2015-12-22 }} ''Insomniac'' magazine, October 9, 2015.</ref>

==Career==
===As Skeptik===
While at Rutgers, under the pseudonym Skeptik, he formed the underground hip hop group Thought Breakers. Their debut EP, ''Episode 1'', was released in 2004 and independently sold over 10,000 copies.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|last=Video|first=Rive|date=2011-10-21|title=Kyle Rapps|url=https://rivevideo.com/kyle-rapps/|access-date=2020-09-08|website=Rive Video Promotion|language=en-US}}</ref> The group opened for [[Wyclef Jean]], [[Fat Joe]], [[Talib Kweli]] and [[Dead Prez]].<ref name="hotnewhiphop"/><ref name="mhidalgo"/><ref name="respect072111">[http://respect-mag.com/new-music-kyle-rapps-x-talib-kweli-universe-traveler/ “New Music: Kyle Rapps X Talib Kweli ‘Universe Traveler’,”] ''[[Respect. (magazine)|Respect.]]'', July 21, 2011.</ref> Around that time, he formed a spoken-word poetry collective, Mayhem Poets.<ref name="mhidalgo"/><ref name="cstetler"/> In 2007, the collective won a [[Microsoft]] small business competition, enabling them to establish the open mic venue Slam Chops in Manhattan, which stayed open for two years.<ref name="cstetler"/> Also in 2007, the collective had a two-month [[off-Broadway]] run at the [[New Victory Theater]].<ref>Laurel Graeber, [https://query.nytimes.com/gst/fullpage.html?res=9A00E5D61230F931A35752C1A9619C8B63 “Mayhem Poets,”] ''[[New York Times]]'', November 2, 2007.</ref> After a 2009 trip to [[Liberia]] with Mayhem Poets, Sutton began to go by the name Black Skeptik, and released titles "Rent" and "Frankenstein Saves Hip Hop".<ref name="mhidalgo"/><ref name=":0" />

===As Kyle Rapps===
In 2010, now going by Kyle Rapps, he released the single "Love, Love," featuring [[KRS-One]] and [[Homeboy Sandman]].<ref name="respect072111"/> In 2011, his ''RE-Edutainment'' mixtape came out, with the title in homage to the 1990 [[Boogie Down Productions]] album ''[[Edutainment (album)|Edutainment]]''. KRS-One, [[Joell Ortiz]] and [[U-N-I]] appear on the mixtape.<ref>Christopher Weingarten, [http://blogs.villagevoice.com/music/2012/03/kyle_rapps_bully_mp3.php “Kyle Rapps’ BDP-Flipping ‘Bully’,”] ''[[Village Voice]]'', March 9, 2012.</ref><ref>Jake Paine, [http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/news/id.14610/title.kyle-rapps-talks-boogie-down-productions-influence-on-re-edutainment “Kyle Rapps Talks Boogie Down Productions Influence on RE-Edutainment,”] Hip Hop DX, April 1, 2011.</ref> Later in 2011, Rapps released ''On Air'', featuring appearances from Talib Kweli, [[C-Rayz Walz]], KRS-One and Homeboy Sandman.<ref name="respect072111"/> The eight-track mixtape is built around samples from French electronic music duo [[Air (French band)|Air]].<ref>[http://www.brooklynvegan.com/archives/2012/02/kyle_rapps_rele.html “Kyle Rapps releases new video, opening for Action Bronson; Air scored a film from 1902,”] [[BrooklynVegan]], February 8, 2012.</ref><ref>[http://www.huffingtonpost.com/2011/08/05/kyle-rapps-on-air_n_919176.html “Kyle Rapps, ‘Streets Move On’,”] ''[[Huffington Post]]'', August 5, 2011.</ref>

In June 2013, Mishka NYC Records released ''SUB'', Rapps' first full-length album, produced by Belief and featuring collaborations with [[Murs (rapper)|Murs]], [[Action Bronson]], [[Mr. Muthafuckin' eXquire]], [[Aaron Cohen (rapper)|Aaron Cohen]] and Spaceman.<ref>Justin Hunte, [http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/news/id.24361/title.kyle-rapps-sub-cover-art-download-mixtape-stream “Kyle Rapps ‘Sub’ Cover Art, Download & Mixtape Stream,”] Hip Hop DX, June 18, 2013.</ref><ref>[http://noisey.vice.com/music-video-premieres/kyle-rapps-feat-mr-muthafuckin-exquire-super-glue-official-video “Kyle Rapps Feat. Mr. Muthafuckin’ eXquire – ‘Super Glue’,”] ''[[Vice (magazine)|Vice]]'', June 3, 2013.</ref><ref>[http://thesource.com/2013/06/26/kyle-rapps-drops-sub-mixtape-with-action-bronson-murs-mr-mfn-exquire-more/ “Kyle Rapps Drops ‘SUB’ Mixtape with Action Bronson, Murs, Mr. MFN eXquire & More,”] ''[[The Source (magazine)|The Source]]'', June 26, 2013.</ref> ''[[Earmilk]]'' called it "a mesmerizing selection of songs."<ref name="nvukorepa"/>

In 2014, Rapps put out the single "The Sky's on Fire", produced by [[Jesse Shatkin|Belief]] and featuring vocals by Adrienne Mack-Davis.<ref>Richard Spadine, [http://www.djbooth.net/index/tracks/review/kyle-rapps-the-skys-on-fire “Kyle Rapps – The Sky’s On Fire,”] DJ Booth, April 3, 2014.</ref>

In 2017, Rapps released "Latrell", its title and sampled intro referencing [[Latrell Sprewell]], a former professional basketball player who was suspended for attacking his Golden State Warriors coach P.J. Carlesimo in 1997.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Staff|date=2017-06-14|title=PREMIERE: Kyle Rapps Delivers New Single 'Latrell'|url=https://www.hypefresh.co/premiere-kyle-rapps-delivers-new-single-latrell/|access-date=2020-09-08|website=Hypefresh Inc|language=en-US}}</ref>

In 2020, Rapps performed virtually over Zoom at Soul Vey, a Black and Jewish community event led by Adam Swig and [[Kosha Dillz]].<ref>{{Cite web|last=Swig|first=Adam|date=2020-07-20|title='All you gotta do is listen': Connecting at a Black-Jewish Shabbat marathon|url=https://www.jweekly.com/2020/07/20/all-you-gotta-do-is-listen-connecting-at-a-black-jewish-shabbat-marathon/|access-date=2020-09-08|website=J.|language=en-US}}</ref>

Rapps has collaborated with a large variety of other music artists, including [[KRS-One]], [[Homeboy Sandman]], [[Diwon]], [[Action Bronson]], [[Mr. Muthafuckin' eXquire]], [[Murs (rapper)|Murs]], Adrienne Mack-Davis, [[Jesse Shatkin|Belief]], Hefna Gwap, [[Talib Kweli]], [[Chuuwee]], Faruz Feet, [[Dirt E. Dutch]], [[Joell Ortiz]], Sly5thAve, Aaron Cohen, Brody, [[Y-Love]], Nathan Sela, Vulkan the Krusader, Sam Siegel, S'natra, DJ JS-1, Breez Brewin, Dru the Monster, Nelson, Little Freckles, Ali Salah Rasé, and Ray Contour.
