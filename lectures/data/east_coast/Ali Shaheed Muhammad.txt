'''Ali Shaheed Muhammad''' (born August 11, 1970) is an American hip hop DJ, record producer, and rapper, best known as a member of [[A Tribe Called Quest]].<ref name="linearlabs" /> With [[Q-Tip (musician)|Q-Tip]] and [[Phife Dawg]] (and sometimes [[Jarobi White]]), the group released five studio albums from 1990 to 1998 before disbanding; [[We Got It from Here... Thank You 4 Your Service|their final album]] was released in 2016. A native of Brooklyn, New York, Muhammad currently lives in Los Angeles.<ref name="linearlabs" /><ref>{{cite web|title=The Midnight Hour is giving a jazzy nod to soul and hip-hop in upcoming Charlotte performance|url=https://qcitymetro.com/2019/09/13/the-midnight-hour-is-giving-a-jazzy-nod-to-soul-and-hip-hop-in-upcoming-charlotte-performance/|access-date=August 14, 2020|website=QCityMetro|date=September 13, 2019 }}</ref>

== Career ==
[[File:Ali shaheed muhammad.jpg|thumb|Muhammad speaking at [[Apple Fifth Avenue]] in New York City in 2006]]
Muhammad is a [[Muslim]].<ref>[http://www.muslimhiphop.com/forum/index.php?showtopic=2342]{{dead link|date=December 2017|bot=InternetArchiveBot|fix-attempted=yes}}</ref> Together with [[J Dilla|Jay Dee]] and Q-Tip, he formed the music-production collective [[the Ummah]].<ref name="linearlabs" />

After A Tribe Called Quest disbanded, Muhammad formed the [[Contemporary R&B|R&B]] supergroup [[Lucy Pearl]] with [[Dawn Robinson]], formerly of [[En Vogue]] and [[Raphael Saadiq]], formerly of [[Tony! Toni! Toné!]], releasing one album in 2000.<ref name="linearlabs" /> On October 12, 2004, he released his debut solo album, ''[[Shaheedullah and Stereotypes]]''.<ref name="linearlabs" /> Muhammad co-hosted the ''Microphone Check'' radio show, which ran on NPR until 2016,<ref>{{Cite web |title=Reshuffling of NPR Music includes farewell to ‘Microphone Check’ team |url=https://current.org/2016/09/reshuffling-of-npr-music-includes-farewell-to-microphone-check-team/}}</ref> and on Spotify until 2019<ref>{{Cite web |title=Wrap Up Episode |url=https://www.frannieandali.com/home/2019/3/27/wrap-up-episode}}</ref>.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.npr.org/blogs/microphonecheck/2013/08/29/216840663/about-microphone-check|title=About Microphone Check|website=Npr.org|date=June 2013 |access-date=July 2, 2018}}</ref>

In 2013, Muhammad worked with producer [[Adrian Younge]] on the [[Souls of Mischief]] album ''[[There Is Only Now]]'', as the album's narrator.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Rys|first=Dan|title=Adrian Younge Talks New Concept Album With Souls Of Mischief, Narrated By Ali Shaheed Muhammad – XXL|url=https://www.xxlmag.com/adrian-younge-talks-new-concept-album-with-souls-of-mischief-narrated-by-ali-shaheed-muhammad/|access-date=June 14, 2020|website=XXL Mag|language=en}}</ref> Since then Muhammad and Younge have collaborated to co-produce [[Luke Cage (soundtrack)|the soundtrack]] for both seasons of the ''[[Luke Cage (TV series)|Luke Cage]]'' TV series,<ref>{{cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/luke-cage-original-soundtrack/id1160979391|title=Luke Cage (Original Soundtrack Album) by Various Artists|date=October 7, 2016|website=Itunes.apple.com|access-date=July 2, 2018}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=Luke Cage season 2 soundtrack revealed: who are the musical acts in Netflix's Marvel series?|url=https://www.radiotimes.com/news/on-demand/2019-03-15/luke-cage-season-2-soundtrack-music-acts-artists-netflix/|access-date=June 14, 2020|website=Radio Times|language=en}}</ref> and they have toured together since 2018 as the Midnight Hour, releasing an album by the same name.<ref>{{Cite web|title=The Midnight Hour: Tiny Desk Concert|url=https://www.npr.org/2018/07/18/629744046/the-midnight-hour-tiny-desk-concert|access-date=June 14, 2020|website=NPR.org|date=July 18, 2018 |language=en|last1=Madden |first1=Sidney }}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=Ali Shaheed Muhammad / Adrian Younge: The Midnight Hour|url=https://pitchfork.com/reviews/albums/ali-shaheed-muhammad-adrian-younge-the-midnight-hour/|access-date=June 14, 2020|website=Pitchfork|language=en}}</ref>

In 2019, Muhammad contributed to Saadiq's fifth album, ''[[Jimmy Lee (album)|Jimmy Lee]]''.<ref>{{cite magazine|last=Strauss|first=Matthew|date=June 7, 2019|url=https://pitchfork.com/news/raphael-saadiq-announces-jimmy-lee-first-new-album-in-eight-years/|title=Raphael Saadiq Announces Jimmy Lee, First New Album in Eight Years|magazine=[[Pitchfork (website)|Pitchfork]]|access-date=September 15, 2020}}</ref>

In 2020, Muhammad and Younge launched the [[Jazz Is Dead (record label)|Jazz Is Dead]] album series, collaborating with jazz musicians [[Roy Ayers]], [[Marcos Valle]], [[Doug Carn]], [[Gary Bartz]], [[João Donato]], and the trio [[Azymuth]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.allmusic.com/album/jazz-is-dead-001-mw0003371342|title=Ali Shaheed Muhammad / Adrian Younge – Jazz Is Dead 001|website=[[AllMusic]]|access-date=September 7, 2021}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/ali-shaheed-muhammad-mn0000389128/discography|title=Ali Shaheed Muhammad – Discography|website=[[AllMusic]]|access-date=September 7, 2021}}</ref>
