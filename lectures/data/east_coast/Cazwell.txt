'''Luke Caswell''', known mononymously as '''Cazwell''', is an American [[Rapping|rapper]], record producer and songwriter.<ref name="gaywired">{{cite web |title=Ten Minutes with Cazwell |date=December 26, 2006 |url=http://gaydarnation.com/UserPortal/Article/Detail.aspx?ID=16592&sid=26&gnbox_cn=0&gnbox_ca=1 |publisher=GayWired.com |access-date=2010-08-08 |first=Bryan |last=Ochalla |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110711080258/http://gaydarnation.com/UserPortal/Article/Detail.aspx?ID=16592&sid=26&gnbox_cn=0&gnbox_ca=1 |archive-date=July 11, 2011 |url-status=dead }}</ref> He has released the three studio albums ''[[Get Into It (Cazwell album)|Get into It]]'' in 2006, ''[[Watch My Mouth]]'' in 2009 and ''Hard 2 B Fresh'' in 2014, along with videos and singles.

==Career==

===1999–05: Career beginnings===
Starting in the late 1990s, under the name Caswell, he was part of the [[hip-hop]] duo Morplay, which also included female MC Crasta Yo.<ref name=Advocate>{{google books|2mQEAAAAMBAJ|The Advocate 13 May 2003|page=46}}</ref> Morplay started in Boston and moved to New York in 1999.<ref>{{cite news
|last=Kiritsy|first=Laura|title=White and gay – and trying to make it in the highly homophobic world of rap |newspaper=Bay Windows |location=Boston |date=June 7, 2001 |url=http://www.baywindows.com/white-and-gay-and-trying-to-make-it-in-the-highly-homophobic-world-of-rap-70268}}</ref> In 2003, Cazwell released his first single ''The Sex That I Need'' with Avenue D.

===2006–08: ''Get Into It''===
In 2006, Cazwell released his debut studio album ''[[Get Into It (Cazwell album)|Get into It]]'' featuring lead single "All Over Your Face" and the four follow-up singles "Do You Wanna Break Up", "I Buy My Socks On 14th Street" and "Watch My Mouth".

His debut single "All Over Your Face" was banned from [[Logo (TV channel)|Logo]] due to explicit lyrics and sexual imagery portrayed in the music video.<ref name="xtra2">{{cite web|last1=Turcotte|first1=Bradley|date=6 March 2013|title=Cazwell unzipped|url=http://www.dailyxtra.com/ottawa/arts-and-entertainment/cazwell-unzipped-3106|access-date=19 June 2017|publisher=dailyxtra.com}}</ref>

Cazwell was featured as a guest rapper on Colton Ford's "That's Me" along with Stephen Reed and Peppermint's "Servin' it Up". During the summer of 2007, Cazwell was a part of the multi-artist [[True Colors Tour 2007|True Colors Tour]] which traveled through 15 cities in North America. The tour, sponsored by [[logo (TV channel)|Logo]], was hosted by comedian [[Margaret Cho]] and headlined by [[Cyndi Lauper]] and included [[Deborah Harry]], [[Rufus Wainwright]], [[The Dresden Dolls]], [[Rosie O'Donnell]], [[Indigo Girls]], Stephen Reed and other special guests. Profits from the tour helped to benefit [[LGBT]] organizations [[Human Rights Campaign]], [[PFLAG]] and The [[Matthew Shepard]] Foundation.<ref name="montreal">{{cite news |title=Cazwell's queer and winding road brings NYC hip hop sensation to Montreal |url=https://montrealgazette.com/entertainment/music/cazwells-queer-and-winding-road-brings-nyc-hip-hop-sensation-to-montreal |access-date=17 June 2017 |work=Montreal Gazette |date=4 October 2012}}</ref>

===2009–12: ''Watch My Mouth''===
His second album, ''[[Watch My Mouth]]'' was released on September 1, 2009, containing all of his previous singles, except "Do You Wanna Break Up". The album was preceded by the single "I Seen [[Beyoncé Knowles|Beyoncé]]" featuring [[Jonny Makeup]], released back in September 2008. The video was directed by Francis Legge and [[Bec Stupak]]. In 2009, he released "Tonight", which sampled "[[Give Me Tonight]]" by [[Shannon (American singer)|Shannon]]. In 2010, he released the single, "[[Ice Cream Truck (Cazwell song)|Ice Cream Truck]]",<ref name="montreal"/> which was featured in the film [[Spork (film)|Spork]] as well has been included on a ''Watch My Mouth'' deluxe edition re-release. The video of the song was directed by Marco Ovando, premiered on YouTube on July 30, 2010 and received over 1 million views in its first week.<ref name="montreal"/> The singles "Get Into It" featuring guest vocals by [[Amanda Lepore]] and "Get My Money Back" featuring Lost Daze followed in 2010 and 2011 respectively. Cazwell frequently hosts and DJs parties such as BoysRoom in New York City featuring guests such as [[Ladyfag]], [[Amanda Lepore]], Dj Adam, Raquel Reed and Stephen Reed. In 2012, Cazwell appeared on the variety show ''[[She's Living for This]]'', hosted by the drag queen [[Sherry Vine]]. On the show, he performed his single "Get My Money Back".<ref>{{cite web |title="She's Living for This" The Cazwell Episode (TV Episode 2012) |url=https://www.imdb.com/title/tt2286932/ |publisher=[[Internet Movie Database|IMDb]] |access-date=19 October 2013}}</ref> In 2012, he appeared on "Something For Everybody", a song by DAB & [[Fargetta|Get Far]] along with Jonathan Mendelsohn.

===2013–present: ''Hard 2 B Fresh''===
Cazwell collaborated with Peaches on the single "Unzip Me", released back on November 8, 2011. On June 19, 2012, "Rice and Beans" was released. The songs appear on Cazwell's third studio album ''Hard 2 B Fresh''. In 2013, the single "Guess What?" was released, featuring the singer [[Luciana (singer)|Luciana]].<ref name="xtra2"/> "No Selfie Control" was released in the late of 2013. The single and music video for "Helen Keller" featuring ''Drag Race'' superstar [[Manila Luzon]], Roxy (known for underground House classics "Get Huh" and "Accident") and Richie Beretta was also released.<ref>{{cite web |title=CAZWELL & MANILA LUZON featuring ROXY and RICHIE BERETTA "Helen Keller" |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=FKKDQKJXBlA  |archive-url=https://ghostarchive.org/varchive/youtube/20211221/FKKDQKJXBlA |archive-date=2021-12-21 |url-status=live|publisher=youtube.com |access-date=19 May 2020 |date=30 Jun 2015}}{{cbignore}}</ref> On August 22, Cazwell announced that his third studio album ''Hard 2 B Fresh'' would be released on September 30 and posted the track listing and the cover art on his official site. In summer of 2015, Cazwell released another single from ''Hard 2 B Fresh'' entitled "The Biscuit". Its video featured Middle-Eastern imagery including pyramids, camels, a snake charmer, tan-skinned models wearing keffiyehs, and original music from South African DJ Naaldekoker's track "Ek Smaak Jou".<ref>{{Cite web |title=VIDEO: Cazwell Mixes Middle-Eastern Studs And Stereotypes In "The Biscuit" |url=https://unicornbooty.com/cazwell-mixes-middle-eastern-studs-and-stereotypes-in-the-biscuit/ |publisher=Unicorn Booty |access-date=2015-12-11 |date=August 13, 2015 |first=Matthew |last=Lawrence |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20151217192243/https://unicornbooty.com/cazwell-mixes-middle-eastern-studs-and-stereotypes-in-the-biscuit/ |archive-date=December 17, 2015 |url-status=dead }}</ref>

In 2015, Cazwell collaborated with fashion designer [[Geoffrey Mac]] on a range of form-fitting underwear, based on the colours used in the 'Ice Cream Truck' video.<ref name="Musto">{{cite web |last1=Musto |first1=Michael |title=Rapping Stud Cazwell On Hot Guys, Groupies, and His New Underwear Line |url=https://www.out.com/michael-musto/2015/3/02/rapping-stud-cazwell-hot-guys-groupies-and-his-new-underwear-line |date=2 March 2015 |publisher=out.com |access-date=19 June 2017}}</ref>

In 2017, he released ''Loose Wrists'', his first single produced by his new music label, Snow Cone.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Salandra |first1=Adam |title=Cazwell Wants To Make America Femme Again With New Anthem "Loose Wrists" |url=http://www.newnownext.com/cazwell-loose-wrists-lace-rompers/06/2017/ |date=13 June 2017 |publisher=newnownext.com |access-date=16 June 2017}}</ref>

==Artistry==
Cazwell is openly gay and his work focuses on gay and [[Bisexuality|bisexual]] urban themes and content, and his music and videos are frequently played on [[Logo (TV channel)|Logo]], a US cable channel geared towards the [[gay, lesbian, bisexual, and transgender]] (LGBT) community. His music comprises the genres [[hip hop]], dance, [[electronica]], [[house music|house]] and [[homo hop]].
