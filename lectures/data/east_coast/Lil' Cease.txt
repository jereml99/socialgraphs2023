'''James Lloyd''', better known by his stage name '''Lil' Cease''',<ref name=MTV>{{cite web |last1= Oh |first1= Minya |title=Rapper Lil' Cease Sentenced For Gun Possession|url=http://www.mtv.com/news/1459406/rapper-lil-cease-sentenced-for-gun-possession/|website=MTV|publisher=Viacom International Inc.|access-date=November 10, 2015|ref=MTV}}</ref> is an American rapper and former member of hip hop group [[Junior M.A.F.I.A.]]<ref name=Shaheem>{{cite web|last1=Reid|first1=Shaheem|title=Lil' Cease Says He's No Snitch For Testifying Against Kim|url=http://www.mtv.com/news/1498345/lil-cease-says-hes-no-snitch-for-testifying-against-kim/|website=MTV.com|access-date=November 10, 2015|ref=Shaheem}}</ref>

==Early life, family and education==
{{expand section| date= July 2022}}
James Lloyd was born in [[New York City]].

== Career ==
As part of Junior M.A.F.I.A., Cease's first album ''[[Conspiracy (Junior M.A.F.I.A. album)|Conspiracy]]''  was released in 1995. He gained mainstream attention with a memorable verse on "[[Player's Anthem]]", the album's highest charting single. Frequent [[wikt:name-drop#English|name dropping]] from mentor [[The Notorious B.I.G.]] throughout Cease's career helped keep the young rapper in the spotlight.

Cease featured on [[Def Jam Recordings|Def Jam]]'s soundtrack for the 1997 comedy film ''[[How to Be a Player]]'' alongside [[LeVert]], [[Cam'ron]] and [[Mase]]. He is also known for being a featured artist on [[Lil' Kim]]'s record [[Crush on You (Lil' Kim song)|Crush on You]] from her debut album [[Hard Core (Lil' Kim album)|Hard Core]], which was a number 1 hit on the [[Hot Rap Songs]] chart.  Rapper Cam'ron revealed that he wrote the original rendition of the song ''Crush on You''.<ref name=XXL>{{cite news| last1= Horowitz|first1=Steven J.|title=Cam'ron Says He Wrote Lil' Cease Verses on 'Crush on You' Cam'ron Says He Wrote Lil' Cease Verses on 'Crush on You'|url=http://theboombox.com/camron-says-he-wrote-lil-cease-verses-on-crush-on-you/|access-date=November 10, 2015| work= The Boom Box|publisher=The XXL Network |date=April 28, 2011|ref=April 28, 2011, 2:00 PM}}</ref>

After making guest appearances on other artists' albums, Cease released his debut and only studio album ''[[The Wonderful World of Cease A Leo]]'' in 1999. It peaked at #26 on the [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]] and #3 on the [[Top R&B/Hip-Hop Albums]], and features [[Jay Z]], Lil' Kim and Bristal. In 2005, Lil' Cease, Banger and MC Klepto reunited Junior M.A.F.I.A. and released the group's second album, ''[[Riot Musik]]''. In January 2009, Lil' Cease released "Letter to B.I.G.", which sampled "Letter to B.I.G." by [[Jadakiss]] from the [[Notorious (soundtrack)|soundtrack]] to the feature film ''[[Notorious (2009 film)|Notorious]]''.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://smokingsection.uproxx.com/TSS/2009/01/lil-cease-letter-to-big |title=Smokingsection |access-date=December 14, 2009 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130725123249/http://smokingsection.uproxx.com/TSS/2009/01/lil-cease-letter-to-big |archive-date=July 25, 2013 |url-status=dead }}</ref>
