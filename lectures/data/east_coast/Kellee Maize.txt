'''Kellee Maize''' is an American [[rapper]], singer, and [[songwriter]].<ref name=TribLive/> Her first album, ''[[Age of Feminine]]'', was released in 2007. Maize has recorded and released six full albums and several singles.<ref name="Kademlia">{{cite web|last1=Kademlia|title=Kellee Maize:Aligned Archetype|url=http://www.frostclick.com/wp/index.php/2010/01/29/kellee-maize-aligned-archetype-hottest-new-female-mc-with-an-original-dancehip-hop-flavor/?dsq=32162901#comment-32162901|website=FrostClick|date=29 January 2010|accessdate=15 June 2010}}</ref>

==Early life==
Maize was born in [[Reading, Pennsylvania|Reading]], [[Pennsylvania]], and was adopted by Terry and Christine Maize, who lived in [[New Berlin, Pennsylvania|New Berlin]], a small town in Central Pennsylvania.<ref name=Oprah/><ref name="Gifford"/> At 9 years old, she started her first rap group, Thunder and Lightning.<ref name="Rouvalis">{{cite news |last1=Rouvalis |first1=Cristina |title=Idol Find: Pittsburgh Rapper Teams Up with Dad Jimmy McNichol for New Show |url=https://www.pittsburghmagazine.com/idol-find-pittsburgh-rapper-teams-up-with-dad-jimmy-mcnichol-for-new-show/ |accessdate=21 April 2020 |publisher=Pittsburgh Magazine |date=June 22, 2016}}</ref> Maize graduated from [[Mifflinburg Area High School]].<ref name=McKinney/> In her teens, she moved to Pittsburgh and in 2000 began rapping and singing on stage, but financial needs led her to the workforce. At 18 years old, Maize began studying at the [[University of Pittsburgh]] as a drama major and later switched her major to communications.<ref name="Rouvalis"/><ref name="Jamendo">{{cite web|title=Kellee Maize|url=https://www.jamendo.com/artist/357359/kellee-maize?language=en|website=Jamendo|accessdate=18 October 2016}}</ref><ref name="Post-Gazette">[http://www.post-gazette.com/stories/ae/music/hip-hop-to-britpop-new-releases-from-kellee-maize-mace-ballard-summer-winter-and-black-crash-323306/  ''Pittsburgh Post-Gazette'' (30 March 2012)]</ref><ref name="Mervis">{{cite web|last1=Mervis|first1=Scott|title=Hip-Hop to Britpop: New releases from Kellee Maize, Mace Ballard, Summer-Winter and Black Crash|url=http://www.post-gazette.com/stories/ae/music/hip-hop-to-britpop-new-releases-from-kellee-maize-mace-ballard-summer-winter-and-black-crash-323306/|website=Post-Gazette|publisher=Pittsburgh Post-Gazette|accessdate=30 March 2012}}</ref>

Maize worked at the ''[[Pittsburgh City Paper]]'' and began promoting events around the city. She was soon promoted to the position of events and promotions director. After experiencing her mother going through cancer and the sudden passing of her father, Maize again turned to music as a way to cope.<ref name="Jamendo"/><ref name="Rouvalis"/>

==Career==

===Early career===
In 2006 Maize founded Näkturnal, a Pittsburgh-based guerrilla marketing firm.<ref name="Iron City Sound"/> Maize remains the owner and CEO of the company and uses the company to promote her own music.<ref name=McKinney/><ref name="Rouvalis"/>

Maize released her first rap album, ''[[Age of Feminine]]'', independently in June 2007. The album features traditional [[hip hop]] beats. The 11-song album received positive reviews with over two million listens and over one hundred thousand downloads.<ref name="SonicBids Bio">[http://www.sonicbids.com/2/EPK/?epk_id=302721#bio SonicBids Bio]</ref><ref name=Kademlia/> Her second album, ''[[Aligned Archetype]]'', was released in January 2010. The album features the sounds of [[dubstep]], hip hop, and [[Baltimore club]].<ref name="TribLive">{{cite web|last1=Staff|first1=Tribune Review|title=Personality Test: Kellee Maize|url=http://triblive.com/x/pittsburghtrib/ae/music/s_676435.html|website=TribLive|publisher=Pittsburgh Tribune Review|accessdate=15 April 2010}}</ref> The album became the featured album for [[FrostWire]], where she became the Most Downloaded Artist on FrostWire, with over 135,000 full album downloads in one month.<ref name="Post-Gazette"/>

===''Integration'' to present===
Maize's third album, ''[[Integration (Kellee Maize album)|Integration]]'', was released on November 11, 2011. The album received immediate attention as it was downloaded over 100,000 times within its first month of being released on [[FrostWire]].<ref name="Iron City Sound">[http://www.ironcitysound.com/artists/kellee-maize IronCitySound (November 2011)] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150203153926/http://www.ironcitysound.com/artists/kellee-maize |date=2015-02-03 }}</ref> At the time, the success of this album helped her become the most-downloaded artist on [[Amazon (company)|Amazon]]. In June 2011, she performed at [[Bonnaroo]] in [[Tennessee]].<ref name="Cy Magazine">{{Cite web |url=http://www.cy-magazine.com/2011/06/google-me-kellee-maize/ |title=Cy-Magazine (11 June 2011) |access-date=29 May 2012 |archive-date=16 August 2011 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110816025140/http://www.cy-magazine.com/2011/06/google-me-kellee-maize/ |url-status=dead }}</ref><ref name="Not Quite Millionaires">{{Cite web |url=http://www.thenqm.com/2011/05/07/nqm-submission-kellee-maize-joins-eminem-lil-wayne-at-bonnaroo/ |title=The NQM (11 May 2011) |access-date=29 May 2012 |archive-date=13 May 2011 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110513040628/http://www.thenqm.com/2011/05/07/nqm-submission-kellee-maize-joins-eminem-lil-wayne-at-bonnaroo/ |url-status=dead }}</ref> She performed a 45-minute set list along with long-time collaborator DJ Huggy.<ref name="Blogs sites-Pittsburgh Post-Gazette">{{Cite web |url=http://blogs.sites.post-gazette.com/index.php/arts-a-entertainment/pop-noise/27867-kellee-maize-talks-bonnaroo-wiz-yoga |title=Blogs sites Post-Gazette (13 June 2011) |access-date=29 May 2012 |archive-date=17 June 2011 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110617140636/http://blogs.sites.post-gazette.com/index.php/arts-a-entertainment/pop-noise/27867-kellee-maize-talks-bonnaroo-wiz-yoga |url-status=dead }}</ref> Maize then began work on her fourth studio album and released the single "[[Google Female Rapper]]", a freestyle over [[Twista]]’s popular song, "Overnight Celebrity".<ref name="Leroy">{{cite web|last1=Leroy|title=Video: Kellee Maize - Google Female Rapper|url=http://www.spotlighttv.nl/spotlight/video-kellee-maize-google-female-rapper/|website=SpotlightTV|accessdate=19 October 2016}}</ref>

During an interview in 2012 with ''[[XXL (magazine)|XXL]]'', Maize said she would like to change hip hop by "rapping and singing about things that aren’t found in mainstream hip hop like yoga, environmentalism, oneness, spirituality, beings from other dimensions, and indigenous wisdom."<ref name="XXL">{{cite web|author=XXL Staff|title=The Break Presents: Kellee Maize|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/the-break/2012/03/the-break-presents-kellee-maize/|website=XXL|date=28 March 2012 |accessdate=28 March 2012}}</ref>

Maize's fourth album, ''[[Owl Time]]'', was released December 12, 2012.

Her fifth album, ''[[The 5th Element (Kellee Maize album)|The 5th Element]]'', was officially released on Valentine's Day 2014. The album had been available exclusively to the [[Jamendo]] community 10 days prior. By the 15th of February, ''The 5th Element'' had gathered over 90,000 plays and over 6,000 downloads on Jamendo. The main theme of ''The 5th Element'' has been cited as love.<ref name="Mervis2">{{cite web|last1=Mervis|first1=Scott|title=Kellee Maize spreads the love on 'The 5th Element'|url=http://www.post-gazette.com/ae/music/2014/02/13/Rapper-singer-Kellee-Maize-spreads-the-love-on-The-Fifth-Element/stories/201402130152|website=The Pittsburgh Post-Gazette|accessdate=19 October 2016}}</ref> The album design features over 1,000 fans that sent pictures of themselves displaying love in whatever way they saw fit.<ref name="The 5th Element">[http://www.jamendo.com/en/list/a131319/the-5th-element  The 5th Element by Kellee Maize]</ref>

Her sixth album, ''The Remixes'', was released in 2015 and released nine remixes of Maize's songs.<ref name="Remixes">{{cite web|last1=Kademlia|title=Kellee Maize: The Remixes|url=http://freemusicarchive.org/curator/frostclick/blog/kellee_maize_the_remixes_1874|website=Free Music Archive|accessdate=19 October 2016}}{{Dead link|date=May 2023 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}</ref>

She launched her acting career in June 2017, appearing as the character Popp in the Adult Swim show ''[[Decker (TV series)|Decker]]''.

==Personal life==
Maize reconnected with her biological family at 22 years old.<ref name="Gifford"/> Her biological father is actor [[Jimmy McNichol]], who fathered her at age 18, and her biological aunt is former actress [[Kristy McNichol]].<ref name="Okura">{{cite news |last1=Okura |first1=Lynn |title=Meet Kellee Maize, The Daughter '70s Heartthrob Jimmy McNichol Never Knew He Had (VIDEO) |url=https://www.huffpost.com/entry/jimmy-mcnichol-daughter-kellee-maize_n_5960802?guccounter=1&guce_referrer=aHR0cHM6Ly9lbi53aWtpcGVkaWEub3JnLw&guce_referrer_sig=AQAAAMp8DUItSlwceZLGdE6pTBtdMKebz0tv3Vz7OjwhN0cix64skP5dW8MeEjIKU0AOaQsYN9VnzGx9Kro2ho1fEazXD5E6ozsB_o0DmoNjhtGD-SQsRO4pdKthkizeus0C0ofoajQhKgxCOpr79REZtGtzUOCckinx8G4OlXnxvVXe |accessdate=21 April 2020 |work=The Huffington Post |date=December 10, 2017}}</ref><ref name=Oprah>{{cite web|last1=Maize|first1=Kellee|title=Creating Reality on Oprah: Finding My Famous Birth Dad and Aunt and Sharing the First Piece of Our Story|url=http://www.huffingtonpost.com/kellee-maize/creating-reality-on-oprah_b_5969912.html|website=Huffington Post|accessdate=20 October 2014|date=11 Oct 2014|quote=My birth dad Jimmy McNichol and my aunt Kristy McNichol, both were huge stars in the 70s.}}</ref> Maize and McNichol unsuccessfully pitched a television special, which they hoped could tell their story of reconnecting.<ref name="Rouvalis"/> Through McNichol, Maize has two siblings. Maize is married to Joey Rahimi and has three children, one son and two daughters.<ref name="Gifford">{{cite web|last1=Gifford|first1=Michelle|title=Kellee Maize: Rapper, Singer, Activist, Entrepreneur, and Adoptee|url=https://adoption.com/kellee-maize-rapper-singer-activist-entrepreneur-adoptee|website=Adoption.com|accessdate=18 October 2016}}</ref><ref name="Rouvalis"/>

{{As of|2016}}, Maize lives in the [[Shadyside (Pittsburgh)|Shadyside]] neighborhood of [[Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania]].<ref name="Rouvalis"/>

===Activism and spirituality===
Maize is an activist on women's rights, environmentalism, and issues relating to poverty and racism. Her spiritual side is reflected in her music. She studies world and environmental issues, the power of intention, meditation, quantum physics, astrology, Tarot and indigenous wisdom. In 2012, of her meditation practice, Maize said, "I learned [[Transcendental Meditation technique|Transcendental Meditation]] many years ago and found it to be life changing."<ref>{{cite web|title=Kellee Maize|url=https://it-it.facebook.com/kelleemaizemusic/posts/371545839557970|accessdate=22 September 2014|archiveurl=https://archive.today/20141005205059/https://www.facebook.com/kelleemaizemusic/posts/371545839557970%23|archivedate=2014-10-05|date=29 April 2012|quote=I learned Transcendental Meditation many years ago and found it to be life changing.}}</ref>

In 2013 Maize performed at Zeitgeist Media festivals in Los Angeles and New York City supporting [[The Zeitgeist Movement]]. Maize also appeared in TZM founder [[Peter Joseph]]'s short documentary ''Culture in Decline 6: Take of Two Worlds''.<ref name="Culture in Decline">{{Cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6vNCpGaxWno|title=Culture in Decline &#124; Episode #6 "Tale of Two Worlds" by Peter Joseph|website=[[YouTube]]}}</ref> In 2020 she appeared in [[Peter Joseph]]'s movie InterReflections singing "Welcome to the Freak Show" song.

Maize has said that she "has issues with politics" but supported [[Hillary Clinton]] in the [[2016 United States presidential election|2016 Presidential Election]]. She released a single, "#MakeLoveNotTrump" online on October 19, 2016, in opposition to candidate [[Donald Trump]].<ref name=Trump/>

==Awards and nominations==
In polls taken by the ''[[Pittsburgh City Paper]]'', Maize was voted in the "Top 3 Best Hip Hop Performers" three years in a row (2008–2010).<ref name="PittsburghCityPaper">{{cite web|title=Culture + Nightlife Readers' Poll Results|url=http://www.pittsburghcitypaper.ws/pittsburgh/culture-nightlife-readers-poll-results/Content?oid=1342969|website=Pittsburgh City Paper|accessdate=19 October 2016}}</ref> In 2010, Maize was nominated as "Best Female Artist at the Pittsburgh Hip Hop Awards".<ref name="Post-Gazette award">[http://www.post-gazette.com/stories/ae/music/x-clan-reunion-highlights-hip-hop-awards-232119/?p=0  Pittsburgh Post-Gazette (28 March 2012)]</ref>

Maize's single, "City of Champions", which was on her album, ''[[Aligned Archetype]]'', competed against Wiz Khalifa's "[[Black and Yellow]]" as the [[Pittsburgh Steelers]]’ favorite "Road to the Super Bowl" song.<ref name="Spagnolo">{{cite web|last1=Spagnolo|first1=Don|title=Best of the Web: The Top 20 Links of the Week|url=http://pittsburgh.cbslocal.com/2011/01/26/best-of-the-web-the-top-20-links-of-the-week-12/|website=CBS Pittsburgh|date=26 January 2011|publisher=CBS|accessdate=26 January 2011}}</ref>

In 2011, Maize was nominated as one of ''[[Pittsburgh Magazine]]''<nowiki>'</nowiki>s and PUMP's 40 Under 40. The award recognizes individuals that are committed to shaping [[Western Pennsylvania]].<ref name="McKinney">{{cite web|last1=McKinney|first1=Tara D.|title=Kellee Maize 24|url=http://www.dailyitem.com/kellee-maize/article_67a8b518-c071-5caf-82d0-8e5fef1d4063.html|website=The Daily Item|date=24 February 2011 |accessdate=24 February 2011}}</ref>
