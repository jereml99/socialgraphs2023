'''Jasun Wardlaw'''<ref name="Allmusic">{{cite web|last=Birchmeier|first=Jason|title=Half-A-Mill > Biography|url={{AllMusic|class=artist|id=p372304/biography|pure_url=yes}}|work=Allmusic|date=May 20, 2008|accessdate=March 7, 2010}}</ref> (April 6, 1973 &ndash; October 22, 2003) better known by his stage name '''Half a Mill''', was a [[Brooklyn]]-based American [[rapper]].

==Career==
Half a Mill was considered an underground rapper in the early 1990s. His mainstream breakthrough came in 1997 when he scored a guest spot on supergroup [[The Firm (hip hop group)|the Firm]]'s only release, ''[[The Album (The Firm album)|The Album]]''.

==Personal life and death==
Wardlaw committed suicide by shooting himself in the head in Brooklyn's Albany Projects on October 22, 2003. His body was found by police inside his apartment.<ref name="NYPD Daily Blotter">{{cite web|url=https://nypost.com/2003/10/24/nypd-daily-blotter-397/ |title=New York Post - NYPD Daily Blotter |date=24 October 2003 |publisher=nypost.com |accessdate=November 11, 2021}}</ref><ref name="VannDigit">{{cite web|url=https://www.vanndigital.com/bloodsport-claims-half-committed-suicide-firm-came-funeral/ |title=VannDigital - Bloodsport Claims Half A Mill Committed Suicide & No One From The Firm Came To His Funeral|publisher=vanndigital.com |accessdate=July 18, 2017}}</ref> He left behind a son, Jasun Jabbar Wardlaw Jr., who is a rapper and actor.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.protejayatl.com/bio|title = BIO}}</ref>

Half a Mill was the subject of the 2010 full-length documentary ''[[Player Hating: A Love Story]]'' by filmmaker [[Maggie Hadleigh-West]].<ref name="Player Hating">{{cite web|url=http://www.yomaggie.com/player-hating-a-love-story |title=Player Hating: A Love Story |publisher=YoMaggie.com |accessdate=May 6, 2017}}</ref>
