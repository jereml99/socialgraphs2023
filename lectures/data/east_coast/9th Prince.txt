'''Terrance Hamlin''' (born February 10, 1977), better known as '''9th Prince''', is an American [[rapper]] and one of the founding members of rap group [[Killarmy]].<ref name="interview">{{cite web|last=Mlynar |first=Phillip|url=https://www.villagevoice.com/2010/10/04/qa-rapper-9th-prince-on-his-older-brother-rza-and-the-early-days-of-the-wu-tang-clan/|title=Q&A: Rapper 9th Prince On His Older Brother RZA And The Early Days Of The Wu-Tang Clan |website=Villagevoice.com |date=October 4, 2010 |access-date=March 24, 2013}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |title=9th prince - Dot Celebrity |url=https://dotcelebrity.com/networth/9th-prince/ |access-date=2022-12-12 |language=en-US}}</ref> He was initially responsible for bringing the various members together, under the guide of his brother. He released his solo debut album ''[[Granddaddy Flow]]'' in 2003. He is the younger brother of [[RZA]].<ref name="interview" /> He went by the alias Madman & Iron Fingers, especially in his early recordings. 9th Prince gets his name from the kung-fu movie ''[[Shaolin Prince]]''. He has pushed hard for a reunion album with his group and helped to release a 'greatest hits' album.

== Legal issues ==
On March 15, 2005, 9th Prince was sentenced to three years of prison after second-degree robbery. He was released on June 27, 2008.
