{{Short description|American rapper (1968–2020)}}
'''Stephen Jerome Williams'''<ref name="obi">{{cite web |url=https://www.kelseyfh.com/obituary/Stephen-WilliamsSr|title=Stephen Jerome "Stezo" Williams, Sr.Stephen Jerome "Stezo" Williams, Sr. March 10, 1968 ~ April 29, 2020 (age 52)|date=|accessdate=July 19, 2022|website=kelseyfh.com}}</ref><ref name="whoz">{{cite book |last=Stancell |first=Steven |date=1996 |title=Rap Whoz Who: The World of Rap Music, Performers, Producers & Promoters |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=UWyfAAAAMAAJ |location=New York |publisher=Schrimer Books |page=277 |isbn=0028645200 }}</ref> (March 10, 1968 – April 29, 2020), known by his stage name '''Stezo''', was an American [[rapper]] and [[music producer|producer]].

==Career==
Stezo was born Stephen Williams in 1968 in [[New Haven, Connecticut]].<ref name="obi"/> He first appeared on the hip-hop scene as a dancer for the group [[EPMD]].<ref name="whoz"/> He appeared in the video for EPMD's single "You Gots to Chill" from their 1988 album ''[[Strictly Business (EPMD album)|Strictly Business]]''. After this experience Stezo sought a career as a hip-hop artist on his own.

In 1989, Stezo signed a record deal with [[Sleeping Bag Records]] and released his 12-inch single "To the Max" and eventually a full album ''[[Crazy Noise (Stezo album)|Crazy Noise]]''.<ref name="whoz"/> Stezo handled all rapping and production on the album. The single received some attention around the time it was released.<ref name="whoz"/> The album charting at #37 on the [[Billboard magazine|''Billboard'']] R&B albums chart,<ref>{{AllMusic|class=artist|id=stezo-p214977/charts-awards/billboard-albums|pure_url=yes}}</ref> and received praise for its very funky production and on-point rapping.{{Citation needed|reason=Need source|date=February 2020}} The album's two singles both charted on [[Billboard (magazine)|''Billboard'']] Hot R&B/Hip-Hop Chart.

Despite the success of his debut album, Stezo didn't release more music for five years. In 1994, he released the single "Bop Ya Headz"/"Shining Star". Then in 1997 he released his follow-up album ''Where's the Funk At''. The album received little attention and did not chart. In 2005, Stezo released a third album, ''C.T. (The Lost State)'', paying homage to his home state of [[Connecticut]].

Stezo died in his sleep on April 29, 2020, at age 52, from heart complications.<ref name="obi"/><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://heavy.com/entertainment/2020/04/stezo-dead/|title=Old School Rap Icon & Dancer Stezo Dies at 51|first=Paul|last=Farrell|date=April 30, 2020}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.monstersandcritics.com/celebrity/stezo-dead-tributes-pour-in-after-legendary-rapper-dies-age-51/|title=Stezo dead: Tributes pour in after legendary rapper dies age 51|first=John Thomas|last=Didymus|date=April 30, 2020|website=Monsters and Critics}}</ref><ref>{{cite web| url = https://hiphopdx.com/news/id.55682/title.dj-premier-pete-rock-questlove-more-mourn-epmd-affiliate-stezos-death| title = DJ Premier, Pete Rock, Questlove & More Mourn EPMD Affiliate Stezo's Death {{!}} HipHopDX}}</ref>
