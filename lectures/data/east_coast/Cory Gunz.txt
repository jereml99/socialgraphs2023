'''Peter Cory Pankey, Jr.''' (born June 22, 1987), better known by his stage name '''Cory Gunz''', is an American [[Rapping|rapper]] from [[The Bronx]], New York City.<ref name="Dx">Jessica Koslow, "Interview: Cory Gunz: Youngest In Charge," February 1, 2005, found at [http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/interviews/id.372 HipHopDx website]. Accessed January 23, 2010.</ref> Gunz is currently signed to After Platinum Records, [[Young Money Entertainment]], [[Cash Money Records]], and [[Universal Republic Records]].<ref name=Cannon>"Nick Cannon and Gunz bringing elegance in '09 and oranges ," [http://www.nickcannonarchives.com/2009/07/20/nick-cannon-and-cory-gunz-bringing-elegance-in-09-and-beyond/ Nick Cannon's website]. Accessed January 23, 2010.</ref>

== Career ==
At age 14, Gunz was first signed by [[Tommy Mottola]] to [[Casablanca Records]] and [[Def Jam Recordings]].<ref name=Dx /><ref>Shaheem Reid, "Cory Gunz Talks Reality Show With Nick Cannon, Mixtape With DJ Drama: Bronx MC tells Mixtape Daily his upcoming show is 'about bringing hip-hop back and taking it all the way to the top.'"  Found at [http://www.mtv.com/news/articles/1616509/20090717/cory_gunz_1.jhtml MTV news website]. Accessed January 23, 2010.</ref> [[Jay-Z]] shepherded him into a joint venture through [[The Island Def Jam Music Group]]. He was featured on a [[remix]] of [[Rihanna]]'s single "[[If It's Lovin' That You Want]]".<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.rapartists.com/artists/corygunz/biography |title=Cory Gunz Biography |website=Rapartists.com |access-date=March 21, 2020}}</ref> Gunz mixtape ''The Apprentice 3 – Season Finale'' has amassed over 85,000 listens on the [[mixtape]] website [[DatPiff]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.datpiff.com/Cory_Gunz_The_Apprentice_3_-_Season_Finale.m171.html |title=The Apprentice 3 – Season Finale Mixtape by Cory Gunz |website=Datpiff.com |date=January 30, 2006 |access-date=March 21, 2020}}</ref> He also contributed a verse to the original version of [[Lil Wayne]]'s "[[A Milli]]" before it was replaced with new verses by Wayne for the album version.<ref>{{cite web |author=HoodHollywood.com |url=http://vodpod.com/watch/924794-corey-gunz-speaks-about-lil-waynes-a-millie-and-how-he-got-on-the-first-version-audio-youtube-link |title=Corey Gunz Speaks about Lil Wayne's "A Millie" and how he got on the first version (AUDIO YOUTUBE LINK) Video |publisher=Vodpod.com |access-date=March 28, 2012 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100506033737/http://vodpod.com/watch/924794-corey-gunz-speaks-about-lil-waynes-a-millie-and-how-he-got-on-the-first-version-audio-youtube-link |archive-date=May 6, 2010 |url-status=dead }}</ref>

In 2009, Gunz signed a contract with After Platinum Records. In early 2010, Gunz signed with [[Young Money Entertainment]] and [[Universal Motown]]. Gunz has worked with actor and musician [[Nick Cannon]].<ref name=Cannon /> In 2011, he was featured on Lil Wayne's single "[[6 Foot 7 Foot]]" from ''[[Tha Carter IV]]''. Also in 2011, Gunz starred on [[MTV]]'s "Son of A Gun" a reality show that follows Gunz after and before he got signed by Young Money. The show features appearances from close friend [[Nick Cannon]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.mtv.com/shows/son_of_a_gun/series.jhtml |title=Son Of A Gun &#124; Full Episodes, Photos, Episode Synopsis and Recaps |publisher=MTV |access-date=March 28, 2012}}</ref> It was announced that [[Universal Republic Records]] was going defunct, all of the artists on the roster moved from the label including (Cory Gunz) was being moved to [[Republic Records]] making the label itself revived.<ref>{{cite web |url= http://www.republicrecords.com/artists/artist_single.aspx?aid=412 |title= Birdman now with Republic Records |access-date= November 24, 2012 |url-status= dead |archive-url= https://web.archive.org/web/20121030002859/http://www.republicrecords.com/artists/artist_single.aspx?aid=412 |archive-date= October 30, 2012 }}</ref> Gunz was a featured artist on Dirti Diana's 2013 mixtape ''R.I.P. To The Competition''.<ref>{{cite web |title=Breeding Ground Spotlight: Dirty Diana (@WhosDIRTYDIANA) |url=https://allhiphop.com/news/breeding-ground-spotlight-dirty-diana-whosdirtydiana-cI5B782MfkmVyW40euvT-A/ |website=Allhiphop.com|date=November 7, 2013 |access-date=August 21, 2019 }}</ref> On July 16, 2013, he released his first mixtape in two years with ''Datz WTF I'm Talkin Bout'', it featured guest appearances by [[Busta Rhymes]], Charlie Rock, [[Mack Maine]], [[Wiz Khalifa]], and [[Juicy J]] among others.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2013/07/cory-gunz-releases-new-mixtape-datz-wtf-im-talkin-bout/ |title=Cory Gunz Releases New Mixtape, 'Datz WTF I'm Talkin Bout' – XXL |website=Xxlmag.com |access-date=March 21, 2020}}</ref> In March 2014, Gunz revealed he was working on an EP titled ''Kriminal Mind'' and following its release he would release his debut studio album.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2014/03/cory-gunz/ |title=Cory Gunz Is Working On A New EP – XXL |website=Xxlmag.com |access-date=March 21, 2020}}</ref>

== Controversies ==

=== Legal issues ===
On January 28, 2012, Gunz was arrested for possession of a gun and faced three and a half years in prison, which was lowered to a {{frac|2|1|2}}-year sentence.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Stephanie |first1=Shanice |url=http://thesource.com/2018/01/05/cory-gunz-might-have-new-music-on-the-way/ |title=CORY GUNZ MIGHT HAVE NEW MUSIC ON THE WAY! |publisher=Thesource.com |date=January 5, 2018 |access-date=August 22, 2019}}</ref> Cory Gunz' father,  [[Lord Tariq and Peter Gunz|Peter Gunz]] admitted that he had encouraged his son to keep a low profile and stop releasing music for a while in the aftermath of his arrest. In an interview from December 17, 2013, Peter Gunz stated that Cory Gunz was trying to work on new records in Miami while on probation.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Brown |first1=Lakeia |url=https://hiphopdx.com/interviews/id.2239/title.peter-gunz-says-he-kept-cory-gunz-quiet-to-avoid-jail-time |title=Peter Gunz Says He Kept Cory Gunz Quiet To Avoid Jail Time |publisher=Thesource.com |date=December 16, 2013 |access-date=August 22, 2019}}</ref>

== Personal life ==
Pankey is the father of twin daughters.<ref>{{cite web | url=https://glamourpath.com/cory-gunz | title=Facts About Cory Gunz - Peter Gunz's Son and Rapper | date=November 15, 2019 }}</ref>
