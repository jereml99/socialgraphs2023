'''Tyler James Williams''' (born October 9, 1992) is an American actor. He began his career as a child actor, making several appearances on ''[[Saturday Night Live]]'', ''[[Little Bill]]'', and ''[[Sesame Street]]''.<ref>{{Cite web |date=February 17, 2022 |title=Tyler James Williams: From Child Actor to Hollywood Hunk + Nina Parker on 'Tamron Hall' {{!}} WATCH |url=https://eurweb.com/2022/02/16/tyler-james-williams-from-child-actor-to-hollywood-hunk-nina-parker-on-tamron-hall-watch/ |access-date=July 12, 2022 |website=EURweb}}</ref> Williams later rose to prominence for playing the role of [[Chris Rock]] on the [[UPN]]/[[The CW|CW]] [[sitcom]] ''[[Everybody Hates Chris]]'' (2005{{ndash}}09). Following this, he starred as songwriter Cyrus DeBarge in the [[Disney Channel]] film ''[[Let It Shine (film)|Let It Shine]]'' (2012), and Noah on the [[AMC (TV channel)|AMC]] horror television series ''[[The Walking Dead (TV series)|The Walking Dead]]'' (2014{{ndash}}15)''.''<ref name=":0" />

He currently stars as Gregory Eddie on the [[American Broadcasting Company|ABC]] sitcom ''[[Abbott Elementary]]'', for which he received critical acclaim as well as a [[Golden Globe Awards|Golden Globe Award]], a [[Screen Actors Guild Awards|Screen Actors Guild Award]] and two nominations for the [[Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Supporting Actor in a Comedy Series]].

Additionally, Williams has also appeared in films such as [[Detroit (film)|''Detroit'']] (2017) and ''[[The United States vs. Billie Holiday]]'' (2021).

==Early life==
Williams was born in [[Westchester County, New York]], and grew up in [[Yonkers, New York]].<ref name=filmr/> His mother, Angela Williams, is a counselor, and his father, Le'Roy Williams, is a teacher and retired police sergeant.<ref name=filmr>[http://www.filmreference.com/film/94/Tyler-James-Williams.html Tyler James Williams] filmreference.com</ref><ref>[https://www.tvguide.com/celebrities/tyler-james-williams/bio/193951 Tyler James Williams Biography]. Tvguide.com. Retrieved on June 18, 2012.</ref> Williams has two younger siblings; [[Tyrel Jackson Williams]] (b. 1997) and [[Tylen Jacob Williams]] (b. 2001), both are actors.

==Career==
Williams began his acting career at age 4.<ref name=ebony>{{cite web |url=https://www.questia.com/read/1G1-140092999 | title=Tyler James Williams: star of Everybody Hates Chris. | work=Ebony | date=January 1, 2006 | access-date=}}{{dead link|date=July 2021}}</ref> He later starred in ''[[Little Bill]]'' as the voice of Bobby, briefly replacing Devon Malik Beckford in 2000, and played himself (or an eponymous character, "Tyler") on the [[PBS]] series ''[[Sesame Street]]'' from 2000 to 2005. He rose to fame in September 2005 by playing the title character in the hit series ''[[Everybody Hates Chris]]'',<ref>{{cite web|title=Everybody Hates Chris|url=http://teacher.scholastic.com/scholasticnews/indepth/everybodyhateschris.asp|website=teacher.scholastic.com|access-date=September 21, 2016}}</ref> which finished in May 2009. He won a [[Young Artist Award]] in 2007 for his work on the show. He was also a guest star in ''[[Two for the Money (2005 film)|Two for the Money]]'', ''[[Law & Order: Special Victims Unit]]'', and the show ''[[Hi-Jinks]]''.{{citation needed|date=March 2021}} In late 2009, Williams appeared in the second season of ''[[True Jackson, VP]]'' as True's love interest Justin "Lil' Shakespeare" Weber in the two-part episode "Flirting with Fame."<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.j-14.com/posts/natasha-bedingfield-and-tyler-james-williams-guest-star-in-new-true-jackson-vp-movie-1508|title=Natasha Bedingfield and Tyler James Williams Guest Star in New True Jackson, VP Movie!|author=J-14 Magazine|website=J-14|date=May 6, 2013|access-date=November 29, 2017}}</ref>

In 2012, Williams got the role of Owen in the series [[Go On (TV series)|''Go On'']].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://deadline.com/2015/01/tyler-james-williams-cast-criminal-minds-spinoff-cbs-1201361056/|title=Tyler James Williams Cast in 'Criminal Minds' Spinoff|date=January 28, 2015}}</ref> He also played the lead role of Cyrus DeBarge in the [[Disney Channel]] film ''[[Let It Shine (film)|Let It Shine]]''<ref>{{cite web|title=Tyler James Williams Reveals His Musical Talents In Disney's 'Let It Shine'|url=https://www.huffpost.com/entry/tyler-james-williams-musical-talents-disney-let-it-shine_n_1601141|website=HuffPost|date=June 15, 2012|access-date=June 20, 2020}}</ref> alongside [[Coco Jones]], [[Trevor Jackson (actor)|Trevor Jackson]], and [[Brandon Mychal Smith]]. Williams was also featured on nine songs on the film's soundtrack, showing off his rapping skills. The film premiered on June 15, 2012, and his songs "Don't Run Away," "Me and You," "Guardian Angel," "Let It Shine" and "Moment of Truth" all received airplay on [[Radio Disney]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/walt-disney-records-let-it-shine-soundtrack-boasts-songs-by-coco-jones-tyler-james-williams-and-other-stars-from-the-disney-channel-original-movie-152490215.html|title=Walt Disney Records' Let It Shine Soundtrack Boasts Songs By Coco Jones, Tyler James Williams And Other Stars From The Disney Channel Original Movie|author=Walt Disney Records|website=PR News Wire|date=May 22, 2012|access-date=November 29, 2017}}</ref> The videos were also played frequently on the Disney Channel. Williams also guest starred in the [[Disney XD]] series ''[[Lab Rats (American TV series)|Lab Rats]]'' as a future version of the character played by his younger brother [[Tyrel Jackson Williams]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://ontheblacklist.net/5-quick-facts-actor-hip-hop-recording-artist-tyler-williams/|title=6 Fantastic Facts About Actor and Hip Hop Recording Artist, Tyler Williams|website=On the Black List|author=jaej|date=September 6, 2017|access-date=November 29, 2017}}</ref>

In 2014, Williams starred in [[Justin Simien]]'s independent film ''[[Dear White People]]'' as Lionel Higgins,<ref>{{cite web|title="Dear White People" Actor Tyler James Williams Talks To BOSSIP About Race & Sexuality|url=http://bossip.com/1049783/bossip-exclusive-tyler-james-williams-talks-gay-kissing-scenes-in-dear-white-people-and-interracial-dating-in-2014/|website=bossip.com|date=October 26, 2014|access-date=September 21, 2016}}</ref> and was cast as the recurring character Noah on the television series ''[[The Walking Dead (TV series)|The Walking Dead]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.hollywoodreporter.com/live-feed/walking-dead-tyler-james-williams-735504|title='Walking Dead' Adds 'Go On' Alum in New Mystery Role|author=Lesley Goldberg|work=The Hollywood Reporter|date=September 24, 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title='Walking Dead's' Latest Fatality: Savage Death Will "Rock" Glenn|url=https://www.hollywoodreporter.com/live-feed/walking-dead-noah-dies-tyler-781577|website=www.hollywoodreporter.com|date=March 15, 2015|access-date=September 21, 2016}}</ref> In 2016, Williams played Russ "Monty" Montgomery in the ''[[Criminal Minds]]'' spin-off ''[[Criminal Minds: Beyond Borders]]''.<ref>{{Cite web|title = 'Criminal Minds' Episode 10.19 Photos: Meet the FBI's International Unit|url=http://www.buddytv.com/slideshows/criminal-minds/criminal-minds-episode-1019-photos-meet-the-fbis-international-unit-98755.aspx|website = www.buddytv.com|date=March 27, 2015 |access-date = January 30, 2016}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title = Tyler James Williams Cast In 'Criminal Minds' Spinoff|url=https://deadline.com/2015/01/tyler-james-williams-cast-criminal-minds-spinoff-cbs-1201361056/|website = Deadline|access-date = January 30, 2016|language = en-US|first = Nellie|last = Andreeva|date = January 28, 2015}}</ref> In 2017, Williams was diagnosed with [[Crohn's disease]] after experiencing symptoms for three years. Since his diagnosis, he has undergone treatment, including multiple surgeries.<ref name="Crohns">{{cite web |last1=Crohn's and Colitis Foundation |title=#myIBD: Tyler James Williams' Story |url=https://www.facebook.com/ccfafb/videos/549028692226145 |website=Facebook |access-date=November 16, 2020 |date=December 1, 2018}}</ref> In 2019, Williams was cast as Jake in ''[[The Wedding Year]]'', a teen [[romantic comedy]] film directed by [[Robert Luketic]] and starring [[Sarah Hyland]], [[Jenna Dewan]], [[Matt Shively]], and [[Anna Camp]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.rottentomatoes.com/m/the_wedding_year|title = The Wedding Year| website=[[Rotten Tomatoes]] }}</ref>

In 2020, Williams played the role of Paul in ''[[The Argument (film)|The Argument]]''.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.hollywoodreporter.com/news/the-argument-film-review|title='The Argument': Film Review|website=[[The Hollywood Reporter]] |date=September 4, 2020}}</ref> In 2021, he began playing the role of Gregory Eddie in the [[American Broadcasting Company|ABC]] [[Mockumentary]] sitcom Abbott Elementary.<ref>{{Cite web |date=February 16, 2022 |title='Abbott Elementary': Tyler James Williams Explains Moment He Knew Show Was a Hit |url=https://www.indiewire.com/2022/02/abbott-elementary-tyler-james-williams-1234699622/ |access-date=July 12, 2022 |website=IndieWire}}</ref> At the [[74th Primetime Emmy Awards]], Williams earned a nomination for [[Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Supporting Actor in a Comedy Series|Outstanding Supporting Actor in a Comedy Series]] for the role.<ref>{{Cite web |last1=Schneider |first1=Jordan Moreau,Michael |last2=Moreau |first2=Jordan |last3=Schneider |first3=Michael |date=July 12, 2022 |title=Emmys 2022: The Complete Nominations List |url=https://variety.com/2022/tv/awards/emmys-nominations-list-2022-1235313788/ |access-date=July 12, 2022 |website=Variety |language=en-US}}</ref>
