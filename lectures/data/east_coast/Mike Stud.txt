'''Michael Francis Seander''' (born October 30, 1988), known professionally as '''Mike''' (stylized as '''mike.'''; formerly '''Mike Stud'''), is an American [[hip hop music|hip hop]] recording artist. His first musical recognition came with the release of his viral single "College Humor", which he recorded on [[GarageBand]] ironically<ref>{{cite web |url=http://noisey.vice.com/blog/meet-mike-stud-college-baseball-player-turned-professional-bro-rapper |title= Meet Mike Stud, College Baseball Player Turned Professional Rapper |work= Vice |date=2013-04-10}}</ref> while he was a relief pitcher at [[Duke University]].<ref name=":0">{{Cite news|url = http://www.mstarz.com/articles/33249/20140707/former-college-baseball-prospect-mike-stud-who-suffered-tommy-john-surgery-releases-album-closer.htm|title = Former College Baseball Prospect Mike Stud Who Suffered Tommy John surgery Releases Album 'Closer'|last = Brown|first = Kenneth|date = July 7, 2014|work = Mstarz|access-date = August 6, 2015|archive-date = November 18, 2015|archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20151118192210/http://www.mstarz.com/articles/33249/20140707/former-college-baseball-prospect-mike-stud-who-suffered-tommy-john-surgery-releases-album-closer.htm|url-status = dead}}</ref>

== Early life ==
In 2006, Seander graduated from [[St. Raphael Academy]] in [[Pawtucket, Rhode Island]].  At {{convert|6|ft|2|in|abbr=on}}, he [[varsity letter|lettered]] in both baseball and basketball and was named Rhode Island's 2006 Gatorade Player of the Year, earning him an athletic scholarship to Duke University.{{citation needed|date=May 2019}} Seander earned 2nd team all-state honors in basketball after averaging 21 points and 7 rebounds as a senior. He earned an 8-2 record with a 0.72 ERA and 88 strikeouts for the Saints as a junior. In his senior season, he posted a 9-2 record, an ERA of 0.91, and struck out 107 en route to being named both the Gatorade Player of the Year<ref>{{cite web|url=http://playeroftheyear.gatorade.com/archive.aspx |title=Gatorade POY archive |website=Gatorade.com |access-date=2013-02-15 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20121227083536/http://playeroftheyear.gatorade.com/archive.aspx |archive-date=2012-12-27 }}</ref> and Louisville Slugger Player of the Year in Rhode Island.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.goduke.com/ViewArticle.dbml?SPSID=22847&SPID=1850&DB_LANG=C&DB_OEM_ID=4200&ATCLID=273422 |title= Duke Baseball Announces 2006 Signing Class |website= goduke.com |publisher= Duke University Athletics |date= 2006-05-03}}</ref>

== Baseball career ==
After graduating, Seander attended [[Duke University]] in [[Durham, North Carolina]], to play baseball. While at [[Duke University]], he was teammates with current [[Chicago Cubs]] pitcher [[Marcus Stroman]], who is now generally noted as Seander's best friend. He was named the team's closer as a true freshman. Seander saved 9 games in 28 appearances and earned a 1.61 ERA (lowest in Duke baseball history).<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.goduke.com/fls/4200/web-docs/2012%20Duke%20Baseball%20Media%20Guide.pdf?DB_OEM_ID=4200 |title= Duke Baseball Media Guide |format=PDF|website=Goduke.com}}</ref> Those 9 saves were the fifth highest total in school history and was good for fourth best in the [[Atlantic Coast Conference|ACC]] that season. At season's end, Seander was named to the Louisville Slugger and Rivals.com Freshman All-American teams. The Rhode Island native also spent a summer in 2007 as the closer for the [[Newport Gulls]] in the [[NECBL]].

Seander suffered from arm issues following his sophomore season that would eventually require [[Tommy John surgery]]. After missing the entire 2009 season recovering from the surgery, Seander graduated from Duke on the All-ACC Honor Roll with a 3.4 GPA. He decided to use his final year of college eligibility at [[Georgetown University]] in Washington, D.C. He appeared in 9 games as a senior for the Hoyas, striking out 9 in 8.2 innings pitched, and earned a graduate degree in sports management.<ref>{{cite news |url=http://voices.washingtonpost.com/dcsportsbog/2010/10/charlie_hustle_n_flow_hoyas_ba.html |title= Charlie Hustle 'n' Flow? Hoyas ballplayer records hip-hop tunes |newspaper=The Washington Post |date=2010-10-28}}</ref>

== Music career==
While recovering from surgery, Stud turned to music to pass the time.<ref name=":0" /><ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.mtv.com/news/1857068/mike-stud-closer-video/ |title=Mike Stud Gets One Step 'Closer' To His Dreams In New Video|website=Mtv.com|access-date=2015-08-05}}</ref>

In December 2010, Stud released a music video for "College Humor".<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.startribune.com/big-gigs-jackson-browne-gov-t-mule-black-flag-keyshia-cole-warped-tour-more/267539631/ |title=Big Gigs: Jackson Browne, Gov't Mule, Black Flag, Keyshia Cole, Warped Tour, more |website=StarTribune.com |access-date=2015-08-05}}</ref> Stud stated that he originally made the track as a joke for his baseball teammates. Since its release, the video has been viewed over 2.5 million times (as of April 2021). In March, Stud followed up his hit with "In This Life", which featured West Coast rapper [[Alex Lagemann]].

All three hits were featured on Stud's first mixtape—''A Toast to Tommy''—which he released in October 2011.{{citation needed|date=August 2015}} In August, Stud released another mixtape, ''Click'', as a collaboration with fellow hip-hop artist Huey Mack.{{citation needed|date=August 2015}}

On May 13, 2013, Stud released his debut studio album ''[[Relief (album)|Relief]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://mike.space/|title=4 The Homies Album Announcement and Member Signup|website=Mike.space|date=August 2015}}</ref>

On July 7, 2014, he released his second album ''[[Closer (Mike Stud album)|Closer]]''.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/6187756/mike-stud-number-one-album-closer-for-300-entertainment|title=Baseballer-Turned-Rapper Mike Stud Hits a Home Run With His No. 1 Album 'Closer'|website=Billboard.com |date=25 July 2014|access-date=2015-08-05}}</ref>

On October 30, 2015, he released an 8-track mini-album, ''This Isn't the Album'',<ref>{{cite web|url=http://fistintheair.com/2015/10/30/mike-stud-celebrates-birthday-with-release-of-this-isnt-the-album/|title=Mike Stud Celebrates Birthday with Release of 'This Isn't The Album'|website=Fistintheair.com|access-date=2015-10-30|archive-date=2015-11-01|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20151101134350/http://fistintheair.com/2015/10/30/mike-stud-celebrates-birthday-with-release-of-this-isnt-the-album/|url-status=dead}}</ref> a mix of previously released singles and new tracks.

On January 12, 2016, Stud released his third studio album, ''These Days'', which includes a feature from [[New York Mets]] pitcher and former Duke teammate, [[Marcus Stroman]].<ref>[http://goodmusicallday.com/2016/01/mike-stud-tops-charts-new-album-thesedays-review] {{dead link|date=April 2020}}</ref>

Mike most recently starred in a TV show on the Esquire Network called ''This Is Mike Stud''. The show follows him and his crew around on his most recent Back2YouTour. The show premiered Tuesday, June 21, 2016.

In 2017, Stud was featured on "To the Grave" by American singer-songwriter [[Bea Miller]], which is included on her EP, ''[[Chapter Three: Yellow]]'' and her second studio album, ''Aurora''.

On November 12, 2018, Stud released his fourth album, ''4TheHomies'', which included 23 songs and features from [[Vory (musician)|Vory]] and [[Goody Grace]]. The album is very special to Stud, as the pinned tweet reads, "Hey guys. I released a new project last night. 23 songs that are basically the last 2 years of my life in music form. I made every song in my house. this shit couldn't be more me. I hope u guys have as much fun listening as we did making it."

In 2018, Stud began his Final Mike Stud Tour, leading many fans to believe it was his last tour ever. However, he clarified this by saying he was merely changing his stage name to "Mike" after the tour concluded.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://us.blastingnews.com/showbiz-tv/2018/11/mike-stud-drops-new-album-4thehomies-002774477.html#:~:text=He%20has%20named%20his%20tour,his%20upcoming%20tour%20is%20done.|title = Mike Stud drops new album 4Thehomies|date = 15 November 2018}}</ref>

In 2021, the highs., was mike.'s first album released under his new stage name.
