'''Vinson Jamel Johnson''', better known by his stage name '''Wordsworth''', is an American rapper from [[Brooklyn, New York]].

==Biography==
Wordsworth grew up in [[Brooklyn, New York]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.mvremix.com/urban/interviews/wordsworth.shtml|title=Grown Man Talk|work=MVRemix|first=Bill|last=Heinzelman|date=2004|access-date=May 22, 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080517015047/http://www.mvremix.com/urban/interviews/wordsworth.shtml|archive-date=May 17, 2008|url-status=live}}</ref> He started rapping in fifth grade.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.mvremix.com/urban/interviews/wordsworth2.shtml|title=Wordsworth: An Emcee Wealthy In Words|work=MVRemix|first=Todd E.|last=Jones|date=2004|access-date=May 28, 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090604181923/http://www.mvremix.com/urban/interviews/wordsworth2.shtml|archive-date=June 4, 2009|url-status=live}}</ref> He graduated from the [[State University of New York at Old Westbury]] and the [[University of Miami]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.browardpalmbeach.com/music/interview-rapper-wordsworth-loving-day-job-as-middle-school-teacher-10485794|title=Rapper Wordsworth Loving Day Job as Middle School Teacher|work=[[New Times Broward-Palm Beach]]|first=Hank|last=McCoy|date=January 21, 2020|access-date=May 28, 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200422022617/https://www.browardpalmbeach.com/music/interview-rapper-wordsworth-loving-day-job-as-middle-school-teacher-10485794|archive-date=April 22, 2020|url-status=live}}</ref>

Wordsworth recorded with his partner Punchline on [[A Tribe Called Quest]]'s ''[[The Love Movement]]'' and on [[Black Star (rap duo)|Black Star]]'s ''[[Mos Def & Talib Kweli Are Black Star]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.miaminewtimes.com/2004-09-23/music/bush-bash/|title=Bush Bash (page 1 of 2)|work=[[Miami New Times]]|first=Mosi|last=Reeves|date=September 23, 2004|access-date=May 22, 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120222051547/http://www.miaminewtimes.com/2004-09-23/music/bush-bash/|archive-date=February 22, 2012|url-status=live}}</ref> He was involved in the [[MTV]] comedy sketch series ''[[The Lyricist Lounge Show]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2000/07/09/tv/spotlight-rappers-go-for-laughs-on-mtv.html|title=SPOTLIGHT; Rappers Go For Laughs On MTV|work=[[The New York Times]]|first=Ben|last=Sisario|date=July 9, 2000|access-date=May 28, 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150527133115/http://www.nytimes.com/2000/07/09/tv/spotlight-rappers-go-for-laughs-on-mtv.html|archive-date=May 27, 2015|url-status=live}}</ref> He also appeared in the Kevin Fitzgerald film ''[[Freestyle: The Art of Rhyme]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.rapreviews.com/interview/wordsworth.html|title=Wordsworth Interview|work=RapReviews.com|first=William|last=Ketchum III|date=August 3, 2004|access-date=May 22, 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20060521140527/http://www.rapreviews.com/interview/wordsworth.html|archive-date=May 21, 2006|url-status=live}}</ref> In 2004, he released his debut solo studio album, ''[[Mirror Music]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://nowtoronto.com/music/brooklyn-mc-wordsworths-higher-learning---word-wizard/|title=Brooklyn MC Wordsworth's higher learning Word wizard|work=[[Now (newspaper)|Now]]|first=Tim|last=Perlich|date=June 10, 2004|access-date=May 28, 2020}}</ref> In 2012, he released another solo studio album, ''[[The Photo Album (Wordsworth album)|The Photo Album]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.platform8470.com/interviews/interview.php?intid=209|title=Wordsworth: Words Worth A Thousand Photos|work=Platform8470.com|author=CPF|date=June 14, 2012|access-date=May 28, 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160304023749/http://www.platform8470.com/interviews/interview.php?intid=209|archive-date=March 4, 2016|url-status=live}}</ref>
