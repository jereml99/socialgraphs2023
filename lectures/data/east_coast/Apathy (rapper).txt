'''Chad Aaron Bromley''' (born March 8, 1979), better known by his [[stage name]] '''Apathy''' (formerly '''The Alien Tongue'''), is a former rapper and producer from [[Willimantic, Connecticut]].<ref>{{Cite web|title = Apathy - Biography|url = http://www.billboard.com/artist/279774/apathy/biography|website = www.billboard.com|access-date = 2015-11-24|last = Brown|first = Marisa|publisher = Rovi}}</ref>

His first major release was his debut album; ''[[Eastern Philosophy (album)|Eastern Philosophy]]'' in March 2006 with guest appearances from [[Celph Titled]], [[Ryu (Rapper)|Ryu]], and [[Blue Raspberry (singer)|Blue Raspberry]]. His second album ''[[Wanna Snuggle?]]'' was released in 2009 and his third studio album ''[[Honkey Kong (Apathy album)|Honkey Kong]]'' was released in 2011, both to critical acclaim. His fourth studio album, ''[[Connecticut Casual]]'' was released on June 3, 2014 and reached #41 in the R&B/Hip Hop Albums charts.<ref>{{Cite web|title = Apathy - Chart history {{!}} Billboard|url = http://www.billboard.com/artist/279774/apathy/chart?f=333|website = www.billboard.com|access-date = 2015-11-24}}</ref>

Apathy is known for his frequent collaborations with other MCs and groups such as Demigodz, [[Celph Titled]], Doe Rakers, [[Jedi Mind Tricks]], [[Army of the Pharaohs]], [[Styles of Beyond]], and the [[Get Busy Committee]].

==Biography==

===Early career===

In 2001 Bromley joined Demigodz, a local rap collective that included [[Celph Titled]], [[7L & Esoteric]], EL Fudge, [[Louis Logic]] and others.<ref>{{Cite web|title = Apathy {{!}} Music Videos, News, Photos, Tour Dates |url = http://www.mtv.com/artists/apathy/|website = MTV Artists|access-date = 2015-11-24}}</ref> In the period 2000 to 2002 he released a number of solo singles that included “Compatible”, “Just Begun”, “School”, and “Ain’t Nuttin’ Nice" released through Bronx Science Records.<ref>{{Cite web|title = Apathy (single and EP releases)|url = http://www.discogs.com/artist/26936-Apathy|website = Discogs|access-date = 2015-11-24}}</ref> Bromley made his album debut when he appeared on the [[Jedi Mind Tricks]] album ''[[The Psycho-Social, Chemical, Biological & Electro-Magnetic Manipulation of Human Consciousness|The Psycho Social]]'' in 1997. He featured on the tracks "The Three Immortals", "Omnicron" and "The Apostle's Creed".<ref>{{Cite web|title = Jedi Mind Tricks - The Psycho-Social, Chemical, Biological, And Electro-Magnetic Manipulation Of Human Consciousness|url = http://www.discogs.com/Jedi-Mind-Tricks-The-Psycho-Social-Chemical-Biological-And-Electro-Magnetic-Manipulation-Of-Human-Co/release/2504596|website = Discogs|access-date = 2015-11-24}}</ref> By mid-1997, the Demigodz collective fractured and Apathy together with producer Open Mic self-released the ''Sureshot Affair'' EP in 1998''.''<ref>{{Cite web|title = The Demigodz - Sureshot Affair|url = http://www.discogs.com/Demigodz-Sureshot-Affair/release/4247373|website = Discogs|access-date = 2015-11-24}}</ref> He continued to collaborate under the Demigodz moniker and released ''The Godz Must Be Crazy'' EP in 2002 on Ill Boogie Records.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.allmusic.com/artist/the-demigodz-mn0000147931|title=DemiGodz on AllMusic.com|website=[[AllMusic]]|access-date=2012-10-13}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title = The Demigodz - The Godz Must Be Crazy|url = http://www.discogs.com/Demigodz-The-Godz-Must-Be-Crazy/master/69474|website = Discogs|access-date = 2015-11-24}}</ref> The two-disc ''It's the Bootleg, Muthafuckas! Vol. 1'' was released in 2003 and collected the earlier Demigodz material,<ref>{{Cite web|title = Apathy - It's The Bootleg, Muthaf*ckas! Volume One|url = http://www.discogs.com/Apathy-Its-The-Bootleg-Muthafckas-Volume-One/release/355352|website = Discogs|access-date = 2015-11-25}}</ref> while in 2004 ''Where's Your Album?!!'' brought together some of the previously released solo releases.<ref>{{Cite web|title = Apathy - Where's Your Album?!!|url = http://www.discogs.com/Apathy-Wheres-Your-Album/release/655877|website = Discogs|access-date = 2015-11-25}}</ref> Both appeared on the Demigodz label. After this second EP, Bromley signed on to [[Atlantic Records]], but left the label in 2009 due to creative disagreements.<ref>{{Cite web|title = Remixing The Industry, Part 2 11.04.09: Apathy|url = http://411mania.com/music/remixing-the-industry-part-2-11-04-09-apathy/|website = 411mania.com|access-date = 2015-11-24|date = November 4, 2009|first = Michael|last = Melchor|archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20151125041234/http://411mania.com/music/remixing-the-industry-part-2-11-04-09-apathy/|archive-date = November 25, 2015|url-status = dead}}</ref>

===2006–2011===

As a solo artist now, Bromley released the album, ''[[Eastern Philosophy (album)|Eastern Philosophy]]'', in March 2006 through [[Babygrande Records]].

Apathy also became a member of [[Army of the Pharaohs]], which was originally formed by [[Vinnie Paz]] alongside other underground rap artists. He was featured on their debut album; [[The Torture Papers (album)|The Torture Papers]] released in 2006.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.undergroundhiphop.com/store/detail.asp?UPC=BBG0324CD|title=Army Of The Pharaohs - Ritual Of Battle - cover art, buy, producer|author=Muhammet the Slav|date=30 May 2014|work=UGHH|access-date=26 November 2012|archive-date=13 September 2007|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20070913222632/http://www.undergroundhiphop.com/store/detail.asp?UPC=BBG0324CD|url-status=dead}}</ref> In January 2007, Apathy released another collection of his most recent works, ''Baptism by Fire''.

In 2009, [[Get Busy Committee]] was formed in Los Angeles, California, by longtime friends Ryu (of [[Styles of Beyond]]), Apathy and Scoop DeVille. All production was done by Scoop DeVille and Apathy, while vocals are given by everyone in the group. Their debut album, ''[[Uzi Does It]]'', was released for pre-sale download on October 27, 2009 at www.getbusycommittee.com, and available on [[iTunes]] and other outlets on November 10.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/news/id.9959/title.apathy-ryu-scoop-deville-form-get-busy-committee |title=Apathy, Ryu & Scoop Deville Form Get Busy Committee |date=October 13, 2009 |last=Paine |first=Jake |work=Hiphopdx.com |access-date=May 24, 2011}}</ref> As of 2012, Get Busy Committee is on hiatus, or "dead" as they refer to it; the group members are focused on their solo careers.

He released his second studio album, ''[[Wanna Snuggle?]]'' in 2009. It was released on Demigodz Records. The album features guest appearances from [[Phonte]] of [[Little Brother (band)|Little Brother]], [[B-Real]] of [[Cypress Hill]], [[Celph Titled]], and other members of the Demigodz.

His third solo album, ''[[Honkey Kong (Apathy album)|Honkey Kong]]'' was released on August 23, 2011 on Dirty Version Records, along with the ''Primate Mindstate'' EP as a bonus disk.  The album features guest contributions by [[Celph Titled]], [[Vinnie Paz]], [[Slaine (rapper)|Slaine]], [[Ill Bill]], [[Xzibit]], Blacastan, [[Mad Lion]], [[Steele (rapper)|General Steele]], [[7L & Esoteric|Esoteric]], Crypt the Warchild, Motive, Planetary, and [[Reef the Lost Cauze]]. Producers for ''Honkey Kong'' such as [[DJ Premier]] and [[Vanderslice]] contribute to a limited number of beats on this album, though the majority of the production is handled by Apathy himself.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/reviews/2011/08/apathy-honkey-kong/|title=Apathy, Honkey Kong|work=XXL Mag}}</ref>

===2011–2015===
During 2011, Apathy and Celph Titled started producing a second duo album, called ''Will Sing for Vengeance''. They also worked on their solo careers.<ref>{{cite tweet|number=208287651344818177|user=DirtyVersionRec|title=New @ApathyDGZ &amp; @CelphTitled EP title announced: "Will Sing For Vengeance" coming soon<!-- full text of tweet that Twitter returned to the bot (excluding links) added by TweetCiteBot. This may be better truncated or may need expanding (TW limits responses to 140 characters) or case changes. --> |date=31 May 2012}}</ref> In November 2011, Apathy uploaded on to YouTube a new song called "Demigodz Is Back", this song featured Apathy, Ryu and Celph Titled and was produced by Apathy.<ref>{{cite web|url= https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9qfFHgLyUXc |archive-url=https://ghostarchive.org/varchive/youtube/20211222/9qfFHgLyUXc |archive-date=2021-12-22 |url-status=live|title=The Demigodz - Demigodz Is Back (Prod. by Apathy)|website=[[YouTube]] |access-date=2012-10-20}}{{cbignore}}</ref> This song marked the return of Demigodz.

On May 10, 2012, the pair also announced in an interview with Hiphopauthority.com that they were working on a new Demigodz album, titled ''Killmatic''. Apathy stated, "We got a gang of shit in the works, so we always work on multiple, multiple things but we don’t announce things until we’re absolutely positive and definite that they’re coming out.  So, the Demigodz album should be coming in the early-2013."<ref>{{cite web|url=http://hiphopauthority.com/interview-building-with-apathy-celph-titled/|title=[Interview] Building With Apathy and Celph Titled|access-date=2012-09-08|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130303015957/http://hiphopauthority.com/interview-building-with-apathy-celph-titled/|archive-date=2013-03-03|url-status=dead}}</ref> Celph said that the album would contain all six members, "Which is: Apathy, Celph Titled, Ryu, Esoteric, Motive, and Blacastan."

In 2012, Apathy released the double CD ''[[It's the Bootleg, Muthafuckas! Volume 3: Fire Walk With Me]]'' as the third installment to the ''[[It's the Bootleg, Muthafuckas!]]'' series, as well as "[[The Alien Tongue]]," a collection of his earliest demos and recordings.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://newalbumreleases.net/48850/apathy-its-the-bootleg-muthafuckas-vol-3-fire-walk-with-me-2012/|title=Apathy – It's The Bootleg, Muthafuckas! On January 14, 2013, Apathy confirmed that ''Killmatic'' would be released on March 5. November, 2013</ref> It is due to be released April, 2014.<ref>[https://twitter.com/ApathyDGZ/status/422151118656118785 Connecticut Casual] Due to be released: April, 2014 (via:[[Twitter]])</ref> On November 30, 2013, [[Vinnie Paz]] revealed that two new Army Of The Pharaohs albums would be released in 2014. The first album; ''[[In Death Reborn]]'' is slated for a release on 22 April and the second LP is expected to drop in November.<ref>[https://twitter.com/ApathyDGZ/status/428615558616334336 Apathy tweets:] ''AOTP "In Death Reborn". On May 28, 2014, Apathy released "Martha Moxley (Rest in Peace)" featuring [[Chris Webby]], Kappa Gamma, Annoyd and Hayze. The song subtly repeats references to the [[Murder of Martha Moxley]], naming Moxley and Michael Skakel, both then 15.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://genius.com/Apathy-martha-moxley-rip-lyrics|title=Martha Moxley (R.I.P.) - Lyrics|date=2019-07-26|access-date=2020-06-21|website=Genius Lyrics|quote=Around the time when Michael Skakel murdered Martha Moxley|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190726215615/https://genius.com/Apathy-martha-moxley-rip-lyrics|archive-date=July 26, 2019|url-status=live}}</ref>

On June 30, 2015 Apathy released two projects: an EP called ''Weekend At The Cape'' and the fourth installment of his popular "It's the Bootleg, Muthafuckas!" compilation series called ''The Black Lodge''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.undergroundhiphop.com/apathy-weekend-at-the-cape/DRV108CD/|title=Apathy - Weekend At The Cape - tracklisting, reviews, cover art|author=Jacob|date=24 September 2015|work=UGHH}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.undergroundhiphop.com/apathy-the-black-lodge/DRV109CD/|title=Apathy - The Black Lodge - producers, buy, album stream|author=f my name|date=25 September 2015|work=UGHH}}</ref>

===2016–2021===
Albums for 2016 include ''Handshakes With Snakes'' (which was originally a mixtape<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.facebook.com/ApathyDGZ/posts/1033504576708423|title=Apathy - Timeline - Facebook|website=[[Facebook]]}}</ref>), ''The Widow's Son'' and an album with [[O.C. (rapper)|O.C.]] as Perestroika.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.facebook.com/ApathyDGZ/posts/1043707939021420|title=Apathy - Timeline - Facebook|website=[[Facebook]]}}</ref> However, on February 17, 2016 he told on his [[Facebook]] page that it wasn't possible to release all three projects in 2016 and that ''The Widow's Son'' was delayed until early 2017. March 2017 Apathy said it would be released by the end of the year<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.facebook.com/ApathyDGZ/posts/1069690949756452|title=Apathy - Timeline - Facebook|website=[[Facebook]]}}</ref> On July 11, 2017 he announced the release date for Perestroika for September 22, 2017.

''[[The Widow's Son (album)|The Widow's Son]]'' was released on March 2, 2018, through Dirty Version records. It features guests appearances from [[Pharoahe Monch]], [[M.O.P.]], [[Celph Titled]], [[AG (rapper)|A.G.]], [[Locksmith (rapper)|Locksmith]], [[Ryu (Rapper)|Ryu]] and [[Diabolic]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://apathydgz.bandcamp.com/album/the-widows-son|title=The Widow's Son, by Apathy|website=Apathy}}</ref> The album contains production work from Apathy himself as well as from [[DJ Premier]], [[Pete Rock]], [[Buckwild (music producer)|Buckwild]], [[Nottz]], Stu Bangas, and Chumzilla.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://genius.com/Apathy-the-widows-son-lyrics|title=Apathy (Ft. Ryu) – The Widow's Son|via=genius.com}}</ref>

On September 26, 2020, Apathy posted his planned releases for 2021 to his Instagram page. The projects stated were "Where The River Meets The Sea", An untitled joint project with producer Stu Bangas, "Dive Medicine 2" (a sequel to Apathy's beat tape "Dive Medicine"), an untitled joint project with producer Vanderslice, and an untitled joint project with producer Evidence. Apathy is currently Chief Growth Officer of Greater Property Group.
