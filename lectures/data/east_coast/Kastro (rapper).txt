'''Katari Terrance Cox''' (born December 30, 1976)<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://2paclegacy.net/tupacs-family/ |title=TUPAC’S FAMILY|website=2paclegacy.net|access-date= August 14, 2018}}</ref> better known by his [[stage name]] '''Kastro''', is an American [[Rapping|rapper]]. A cousin of [[2Pac]],<ref name="capital">{{Cite web|url=https://www.capitalxtra.com/features/lists/tupac-family-tree-facts/katari-terrance-kastro-cox-cousin/|title=Katari Terrance 'Kastro' Cox is Tupac's cousin.|website=Capitalxtra.com|access-date= December 24, 2021}}</ref> Kastro would perform alongside him in the former's rap group, the [[Outlawz]],<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.complex.com/music/2015/07/where-are-the-outlawz-2pac-now|title=Where Are 2Pac's Outlawz Now?|website=Complex.com|access-date= September 18, 2015}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://uproxx.com/music/tupac-hit-em-up-best-diss-songs/|title=7 Things You Probably Didn’t Know About Tupac’s ‘Hit ‘Em Up’|website=Uproxx.com|access-date= June 4, 2015}}</ref> and is featured on many of the late rapper's songs such as "[[Made Niggaz]]",<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://youtube.com/watch?v=lg_c2NP4DF4|title=2Pac - Made Niggaz (Feat. Outlawz) (360°-Version) (Official Music Video) (HD)|via=[[YouTube]]}}</ref> and "[[Hail Mary (2Pac song)|Hail Mary]]". When he was in [[elementary school]], he introduced his friend, and future Outlawz rapper, Malcolm Greenridge, later named [[E.D.I. Mean]], to 2Pac.

==Career==
Kastro began rapping under the name K-Dog, in a rap group with Big Malcolm ([[E.D.I. Mean]]), and Young Hollywood ([[Yaki Kadafi]]). He later rapped on some of [[Tupac Shakur]]'s songs during the studio sessions for his album ''[[Strictly 4 My N.I.G.G.A.Z.]]''. Eventually, Mutah ([[Napoleon (rapper)|Napoleon]]) was added to the group and they became known as Dramacydal.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://tupacuncensored.com/tupacs-dramacydal-rap-group-recorded-this-song-first/|title=TUPAC’S DRAMACYDAL RAP GROUP RECORDED THIS SONG FIRST|website=Tupacuncensored.com|access-date=July 30, 2022}}</ref> K-Dog was featured on the song "Outlaw" from Tupac's [[RIAA certification|platinum]] album ''[[Me Against The World]]'', released on March 14, 1995. He was also featured on the songs "Tradin War Stories", "When We Ride", and "Thug Passion" from Tupac's [[RIAA certification|diamond]] certified double LP ''[[All Eyez On Me]]'', released on February 13, 1996.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/music/rb-hip-hop/tupac-shakur-remembered-friends-20-years-later-7503756/|title=Tupac Shakur Remembered by Closest Collaborators, 20 Years Later|website=Billboard.com|access-date= September 13, 2016}}</ref> During the recording sessions for this  album, Tupac gave K-Dog the name Kastro, naming him after Cuban revolutionary [[Fidel Castro]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://tupacuncensored.com/why-tupac-named-the-outlawz-after-enemies-of-the-united-states/|title=WHY TUPAC NAMED THE OUTLAWZ AFTER ENEMIES OF THE UNITED STATES|website=Tupacuncensored.com|access-date= November 23, 2022}}</ref>

On June 4, 1996, Kastro featured on Tupac's "[[Hit 'Em Up]]", appearing in the music video, alongside the rest of the [[Outlawz]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://tupacuncensored.com/tupac-cousin-kastro-talks-hit-em-up-impact-via-rare-footage/|title=TUPAC’S COUSIN KASTRO TALKS “HIT ‘EM UP” IMPACT VIA RARE FOOTAGE|website=Tupacuncensored.com|access-date= June 5, 2022}}</ref> The song was the B-side to Shakur's "[[How Do U Want It]]" single and was a vicious diss song aimed at [[Bad Boy Entertainment]], [[Mobb Deep]], and [[Chino XL]]. The song is widely considered one of the most aggressive hip-hop diss tracks ever recorded.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.thedailybeast.com/tupacs-hit-em-up-the-most-savage-diss-track-ever-turns-20|title=Tupac’s ‘Hit ‘Em Up’: The Most Savage Diss Track Ever Turns 20|website=Thedailybeast.com|access-date= July 12, 2019}}</ref> Kastro's other features include "Hail Mary" and "Life of An Outlaw" from Shakur's [[RIAA certification|multi-platinum]] album
''[[The Don Killuminati: The 7 Day Theory]],'' which was recorded over the course of seven days in August 1996.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.complex.com/music/2011/11/end-of-discussion-2pac-don-killuminati-the-7-day-theory-is-better-than-all-eyez-on-me|title=End of Discussion: Why 2Pac’s “The Don Killuminati: The 7 Day Theory” Is Better Than “All Eyez On Me”|website=Complex.com|access-date= July 12, 2019}}</ref>

On September 7, 1996, Tupac [[Murder of Tupac Shakur|was fatally wounded by gunshots]] on the [[Las Vegas Strip]] and was rushed to a hospital at the [[University of Las Vegas]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.ebony.com/tupac-shakur-dies-six-days-after-las-vegas-shooting/|title=ON THIS DAY: TUPAC SHAKUR DIES SIX DAYS AFTER LAS VEGAS SHOOTING|website=Ebony.com|access-date= September 13, 2018}}</ref> He died six days later on September 13, at the age of 25. Kastro was present when the shooting took place. He was in the car directly behind Tupac but could not identify the shooters.<ref name=capital />
