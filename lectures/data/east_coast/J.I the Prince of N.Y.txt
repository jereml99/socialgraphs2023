'''Justin Irvin Rivera''' (born September 23, 2001), better known by his stage name '''J.I the Prince of N.Y''' or more recently simply '''J.I.''', is an American rapper, singer, and songwriter. He is best known for his 2019 breakout single "Need Me", which has over 100 million streams on [[Spotify]]{{facts|date=January 2023}}

==Early life==
Rivera grew up in [[Crown Heights, Brooklyn]] and is of [[Puerto Rico|Puerto Rican]] descent.<ref name="love">{{cite magazine|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/hip-hop/9358626/ji-proud-of-me-interview|title=Meet J.I., the Melodic Romantic Who Believes in Real Love|magazine=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|first=Carl|last=Lamarre|date=April 15, 2020|accessdate=July 18, 2020}}</ref><ref name="nextlevel">{{cite news|url=http://thenextlevelmag.com/ji-the-prince-of-ny/|title=JI The Prince of New York – Claiming The Throne of the Streets of New York|work=The Next Level Magazine|first=Kisha|last=Chandler|date=November 29, 2016|accessdate=July 17, 2020}}</ref> At a young age he was introduced by his parents to music like [[old-school hip hop]] and [[reggaeton]], and would make rudimentary beats on a miniature keyboard.<ref name="proud">{{cite news|url=https://djbooth.net/features/2020-04-10-ji-proud-of-me-need-me-interview-new-music|title=Brooklyn's J.I. Hopes You're Proud of Him|work=DJBooth|first=Donna-Claire|last=Chesman|date=April 10, 2020|accessdate=July 18, 2020}}</ref> In school, English was his favorite subject and he would write short stories.<ref name="radar">{{cite news|url=https://newsroom.spotify.com/2020-07-15/spotifys-radar-program-launches-second-us-artist-j-i-the-prince-of-n-y-alongside-new-content/|title=Spotify's RADAR Program Launches Second US Artist, J.I The Prince of N.Y Alongside New Content|work=[[Spotify]]|date=July 15, 2020|accessdate=July 21, 2020}}</ref> He began writing raps and freestyling with his brothers in middle school, influenced by New York City rappers like [[The Notorious B.I.G.|Biggie]], [[Nas]], [[DMX (rapper)|DMX]], [[Jay-Z]] and [[Big Pun]].<ref name=nextlevel/><ref name="break">{{cite news|url=https://www.xxlmag.com/j-i-the-prince-of-ny-interview-the-break/|title=The Break Presents: J.I. the Prince of NY|work=[[XXL (magazine)|XXL]]|first=Robby|last=Seabrook III|date=January 29, 2020|accessdate=July 18, 2020}}</ref>

==Career==
{{quote box | width=30em | bgcolor=#F2F2F2 | align=right | quote=
I got to a point where everything went left, and I had a decision to make: Am I gonna just give up and do nothing for myself? I had already started my career; at the time, I developed a name for myself. Let me try to go hard. I can’t lose anything if I try one more time. So, I tried again.|source= — J.I. explaining his decision to return to music in 2019 after a period of inactivity.<ref name=proud/>}}

In 2016 he appeared on the second season of ''[[The Rap Game]]'', a reality show competition hosted by [[Jermaine Dupri]] on [[Lifetime (TV network)|Lifetime]].<ref name=break/> The then fifteen-year-old secured his place on the show after Dupri found his freestyles on [[Instagram]].<ref name=nextlevel/><ref name="haute">{{cite news|url=https://hauteliving.com/2020/01/rapper-ji/681045/|title=Rapper J.I. The Prince Of NY On Becoming A Social Media Sensation|work=[[Haute Living]]|first=Kellie|last=Speed|date=January 31, 2020|accessdate=July 18, 2020}}</ref> In one of the challenges, Rivera placed in first, earning a spot on the cover of ''Hip Hop Weekly'' magazine.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.empireboobookitty.com/2016/08/hip-hop-weekly-prince-of-new-york/|title=Hip Hop Weekly Prince Of New York Rapper, The Rap Game|work=Empire BBK|first=Jason|last=John|date=August 6, 2016|accessdate=July 18, 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=http://jpayeinbrief.com/2016/09/20/contracts-for-kids-the-legal-side-of-reality-music-tv-contracts/|title=Contracts for kids: The legal side of reality music TV contracts|work=JPaye in Brief|first=Johnetta|last=Paye|date=September 20, 2016|accessdate=July 18, 2020}}</ref> Although he did not win the overall competition, his impressive performance gained him a substantial following, and he released his debut single "No Static" soon thereafter.<ref name=haute/><ref>{{cite web|url=https://hitsdailydouble.com/the_bside&id=321243|title=The B-Side – J.I. the Prince of NY (Geffen/Interscope)|work=HITS Daily Double|date=April 28, 2020|accessdate=July 20, 2020}}</ref> By the end of the year he had received more than 500,000 views on his YouTube channel.<ref name="spotify">{{cite news|url=https://respect-mag.com/2020/07/18-year-old-rapper-j-i-the-prince-of-ny-announces-partnership-with-spotify-for-its-radar-emerging-artists-program-new-ep-out-this-friday/|title=18-Year-Old Rapper, J.I. the Prince of NY Announces Partnership With Spotify For Its Radar Emerging Artists Program; New EP Out This Friday|work=[[Respect (magazine)|Respect]]|first=Ayana|last=Rashed|date=July 15, 2020|accessdate=July 18, 2020}}</ref>

He released his first project in 2017, a three-track [[extended play]] called ''Barely Famous''.<ref name="newtrack">{{cite news|url=https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/ji-the-prince-of-ny-delivers-new-track-proud-of-me-new-song.1987005.html|title=J.I. The Prince Of N.Y. Delivers New Track, "Proud Of Me"|work=HotNewHipHop|first=Lynn|last=Sharpe|date=April 26, 2020|accessdate=July 18, 2020}}</ref> After his clout from the show died down, he went through a period where he admits he lost his motivation and stopped focusing on his music.<ref name=proud/> He blew all of his money from ''The Rap Game'' on [[marijuana]] and was considering a job in construction.<ref name="247HH">{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=P9VB8JIq0wQ|title=J.I. – Blowing Money On Weed After "The Rap Game" & First Expense After Interscope Deal (247HH EXCL)|work=YouTube|date=June 17, 2020|accessdate=July 19, 2020}}</ref> After getting kicked out of his mom's house he made the decision to give music one last try and connected with his future manager Gabi Acevedo to restart his career.<ref name=247HH/><ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.one37pm.com/culture/news/ji-prince-of-ny-rapper-interview-podcast|title=J.I Prince of N.Y Might Soon Be the King of Hip-Hop|work=ONE37pm|first=Charlie|last=Kolbrener|date=January 20, 2020|accessdate=July 20, 2020}}</ref>

In October 2019 he released his mixtape ''Hood Life Krisis, Vol. 1'', which included his breakout single "Need Me", a "melodic, punch-line laden" track sampling the 2000 song "[[Best of Me, Part 2]]" by [[Mýa]] featuring [[Jay-Z]].<ref name=love/><ref name=proud/> In November, with the song at over 3 million streams on [[Spotify]], it was announced he had signed a deal with [[Interscope Records]] via G*Starr Entertainment.<ref name="deal">{{cite magazine|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/hip-hop/8544774/ji-the-prince-of-ny-interscope-records|title=J.I. the Prince of NY Signs With GStarr Ent/Interscope Records, Drops 'Love Won't Change' Video: Watch|magazine=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|first=Michael|last=Saponara|date=November 25, 2019|accessdate=July 19, 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.hip-hopvibe.com/2019/11/22/j-i-the-prince-of-ny-has-signed-major-label-deal-with-interscope-records/|title=J.I. The Prince Of NY Has Signed Major Label Deal With Interscope Records|work=Hip-HopVibe.com|first=Jay|last=Rich|date=November 22, 2019|accessdate=July 19, 2020}}</ref> He also put out his fifth and final music video for the album, this time for "Love Won't Change".<ref name=deal/> As of 2022, "Need Me" remains his most-viewed with over 115 million YouTube views.

He released ''Hood Life Krisis, Vol. 2'' that December and embarked on his first headlining national tour the following month.<ref name=haute/> Holding the final shows in his hometown of New York, he became the first artist in history to sell out the legendary [[SOB's]] venue on three consecutive nights.<ref name=spotify/><ref>{{cite news|url=https://theknockturnal.com/j-i-talks-selling-out-sobs-family-reunion-and-upcoming-project/|title=J.I. Talks Selling Out SOBS, Family Reunion And Upcoming Project|work=The Knockturnal|first=Nishat|last=Baig|date=February 24, 2020|accessdate=July 23, 2020}}</ref> Notably, in April, [[Drake (musician)|Drake]] paid him homage by playing and singing along to his songs "Used To" and "On Me" during an [[Instagram Live]] session with OVO Mark.<ref name=love/><ref name="drake">{{cite news|url=https://hiphop-n-more.com/2020/04/ji-proud-of-me-new-song-drake-co-sign/|title=FOLLOWING DRAKE CO-SIGN, BROOKLYN RAPPER J.I. SHARES NEW SINGLE 'PROUD OF ME': WATCH|work=HipHop-N-More|first=Navjosh|last=Singh|date=April 10, 2020|accessdate=July 20, 2020}}</ref> A week later he released a new single, "Proud of Me", along with an accompanying music video.<ref name=newtrack/><ref name=drake/> After appearing on [[Lil Tjay]]'s ''[[State of Emergency (Lil Tjay EP)|State of Emergency]]'' EP, he further collaborated with him in June on the single "Hood Scars 2", which served as a followup to J.I.'s original song which had by then amassed 15 million streams across all platforms.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://respect-mag.com/2020/06/j-i-lil-tjay-team-up-for-the-highly-anticipated-hood-scars-2-visual-out-now/|title=J.I. & Lil Tjay Team Up For The Highly Anticipated "Hood Scars 2" Visual Out Now|work=[[Respect (magazine)|Respect]]|first=Ayana|last=Rashed|date=June 20, 2020|accessdate=July 20, 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/ji-the-prince-of-ny-and-lil-tjay-display-raw-emotion-on-hood-scars-2-new-song.1987643.html|title=J.I. The Prince Of N.Y & Lil Tjay Display Raw Emotion On "Hood Scars 2"|work=HotNewHipHop|first=Alexander|last=Cole|date=June 20, 2020|accessdate=July 20, 2020}}</ref>

In July 2020 he came out with the single "Spanglish" with Puerto Rican rapper [[Myke Towers]], with both artists mixing English and Spanish in their verses.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://respect-mag.com/2020/07/j-i-prince-of-ny-taps-into-his-latin-roots-with-his-new-single-visual-for-spanglish-featuring-myke-towers/|title=J.I. Prince of NY Taps Into His Latin Roots with His New Single/ Visual For "Spanglish" Featuring Myke Towers|work=[[Respect (magazine)|Respect]]|first=Ayana|last=Rashed|date=July 7, 2020|accessdate=July 21, 2020}}</ref> It served as the lead single to his next EP, ''Welcome to GStarr Vol. 1'', which he released on July 17 and also featured [[Lil Durk]].<ref name="complex">{{cite news|url=https://www.complex.com/music/2020/07/ji-the-prince-of-ny-welcome-to-gstarr-vol-1-ep|title=J.I the Prince of N.Y Drops 'Welcome to GStarr Vol. 1' EP|work=[[Complex Networks|Complex]]|first=Joe|last=Price|date=July 17, 2020|accessdate=July 21, 2020}}</ref> In the first 20 minutes, it garnered 1.3&nbsp;million streams on Spotify.<ref name=complex/> A few days prior, Spotify announced a partnership with J.I. as a part of its recently launched RADAR program meant to promote "world-class talent through [their] global marketing and editorial teams" with placement in their official playlists, a mini-documentary, third-party partnerships, a Spotify Singles recording and support on the EP release.<ref name=radar/>

On November 30, 2020, his single "Need Me" was certified gold by the [[Recording Industry Association of America|RIAA]], his first career plaque.<ref name="RIAA">{{cite web |url=https://www.riaa.com/gold-platinum/?tab_active=default-award&se=J.I+THE+PRINCE+OF+N.Y#search_section |title=Gold & Platinum: J.I the Prince of N.Y |publisher=[[Recording Industry Association of America]] |accessdate=December 7, 2020}}</ref>

On December 18, 2020, J.I released ''Hood Life Krisis, Vol. 3'', the third installment in his ''Hood Life Krisis'' series. The EP includes a sole guest appearance from [[A Boogie wit da Hoodie]] on the track "R&B Shit".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.complex.com/music/2020/12/ji-hood-life-krisis-vol-3-stream|title=J.I. Drops New Project 'Hood Life Krisis Vol. 3' f/ A Boogie Wit Da Hoodie|work=[[Complex (magazine)|Complex]]|last=Rose|first=Jordan|date=December 18, 2020|accessdate=December 25, 2020}}</ref>

==Artistry==
He has been described as having a "penchant for syrupy melodies."<ref name=love/> His melodic rap-sung style and distinctive [[New York accent]] have resulted in comparisons to [[A Boogie wit da Hoodie]] and [[Lil Tjay]].<ref name=love/><ref name=break/><ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/ji-the-prince-of-ny-is-ready-for-his-crown-from-drake-and-roddy-ricch-co-signs-to-welcome-to-the-g-s-news.114318.html|title=J.I The Prince Of N.Y Is Ready To Pop: From Drake & Roddy Ricch Co-Signs To "Welcome To GStarr Vol. 1"|work=HotNewHipHop|date=July 15, 2020|accessdate=July 21, 2020}}</ref>
