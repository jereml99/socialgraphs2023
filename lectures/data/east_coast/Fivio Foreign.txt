'''Maxie Lee Ryles III''' (born March 29, 1990), known by his stage name '''Fivio Foreign''', is an American rapper from New York. He rose to fame in 2019 with his single "[[Big Drip]]", which spawned a remix with [[Lil Baby]] and [[Quavo]]. He is signed to fellow New York rapper [[Mase]]'s RichFish Records and [[Columbia Records]].<ref name="RichFish">{{cite web|url=https://www.complex.com/music/2020/03/fivio-foreign-talks-mase-record-deal|title=Fivio Foreign Sets the Record Straight About His Deal With Mase|website=Complex|access-date=May 26, 2020|archive-date=April 27, 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200427173332/https://www.complex.com/music/2020/03/fivio-foreign-talks-mase-record-deal|url-status=live}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.okayplayer.com/culture/50-cent-mase-fivio-foreign.html|title=50 Cent Accuses Mase of Signing Fivio Foreign to a Predatory Deal|date=February 19, 2020|website=[[Okayplayer]]|access-date=May 26, 2020|archive-date=August 10, 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200810191056/https://www.okayplayer.com/culture/50-cent-mase-fivio-foreign.html|url-status=live}}</ref>

In May 2020, Ryles was featured on [[Drake (musician)|Drake]]'s song "Demons", from his mixtape ''[[Dark Lane Demo Tapes]]'', which charted at 34 on the [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]], earning him his first ''Billboard''-charting single. Later that month, he collaborated with [[Lil Tjay]] and [[Pop Smoke]] on the song "Zoo York", which peaked at 65 on the Hot 100. In August 2021, he reached further recognition through his feature alongside [[Playboi Carti]] on [[Kanye West]]'s single "[[Off the Grid (song)|Off the Grid]]", from his tenth studio album ''[[Donda]]'', which peaked at number 11 on the Hot 100. Fivio's debut album ''[[B.I.B.L.E.]]'' was released on April 8, 2022.

== Early life ==
Ryles was born and raised in the East Flatbush section of Brooklyn. In his youth, he was sent to Connecticut during the summer as part of the Fresh Air Fund.<ref name=":0">{{cite web|title=THE FRESH AIR FUND; Friendship Blooms in the Summer Sun|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2000/05/14/nyregion/the-fresh-air-fund-friendship-blooms-in-the-summer-sun.html|website=The New York Times|access-date=August 27, 2023|archive-date=January 8, 2022|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20220108211136/https://www.nytimes.com/2000/05/14/nyregion/the-fresh-air-fund-friendship-blooms-in-the-summer-sun.html|url-status=live}}</ref> He started rapping under the name "Lite Fivio" in 2011. In 2013, he changed his name to Fivio Foreign and formed a music collective with his friends under the name 800 Foreign Side.<ref name="Viral">{{cite web|title=Fivio Foreign Interview: Always Viral|url=https://www.complex.com/music/fivio-foreign-interview-brooklyn-drill|website=Complex|access-date=May 11, 2020|archive-date=May 1, 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200501050553/https://www.complex.com/music/fivio-foreign-interview-brooklyn-drill|url-status=live}}</ref>

After Ryles's mother died, Ryles lived on the streets or with some friends and family members.

== Career ==

Ryles started gaining traction after the release of his single "[[Big Drip]]". The song was featured on his [[Extended play|EPs]] ''Pain and Love'' (2019) and ''[[800 B.C. (EP)|800 B.C.]]'' (2020) In November, he signed a Seven Hundred Fifty Thousand Dollar record deal with [[Columbia Records]], in conjunction with American rapper [[Mase]]'s record label [[Mase|RichFish Records]].<ref name="RichFish" /><ref name=":0" />

In May 2020, Ryles earned his first two ''Billboard''-charting singles, with features on "Demons" by [[Drake (musician)|Drake]] and "Zoo York" by [[Lil Tjay]]. The same month, Ryles launched the non-profit organization Foreignside Foundation, "geared towards providing beneficial resources and programs for at-risk youth, the homeless, current and former gang-affiliated individuals, incarcerated individuals".<ref>{{cite web|title=FIVIO FOREIGN LAUNCHES NON-PROFIT ORGANIZATION, FOREIGNSIDE FOUNDATION|url=https://thesource.com/2020/05/20/fivio-foreign-foundation/|website=[[The Source]]|date=May 20, 2020|access-date=August 18, 2020|archive-date=September 3, 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200903055440/https://thesource.com/2020/05/20/fivio-foreign-foundation/|url-status=live}}</ref>

On August 11, 2020, Ryles was included on ''[[XXL (magazine)|XXL]]''{{'}}s 2020 [[XXL Freshmen Class|Freshman Class]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.xxlmag.com/xxl-2020-freshman-class-revealed/|title=XXL 2020 FRESHMAN CLASS REVEALED|work=[[XXL (magazine)|XXL]]|last=Cline|first=Georgette|date=August 11, 2020|access-date=August 11, 2020|archive-date=August 12, 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200812141625/https://www.xxlmag.com/xxl-2020-freshman-class-revealed/|url-status=live}}</ref> Throughout the rest 2020, he appeared on a number of songs by other artists, including [[Nas]]' "Spicy", [[Tory Lanez]]'s "K Lo K", [[French Montana]]'s "That's a Fact (Remix)", and&nbsp;[[King Von]]'s "I Am What I Am".<ref name="Baddie">{{cite web|url=https://www.revolt.tv/platform/amp/new-music/2020/12/3/22149992/fivio-foreign-baddie-on-my-wish-list-song|title=Fivio Foreign drops new "Baddie On My Wish List" song|work=[[Revolt (TV network)|Revolt]]|last=Cho|first=Regina|date=December 3, 2020|access-date=December 7, 2020|archive-date=April 16, 2021|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210416141825/https://www.revolt.tv/platform/amp/new-music/2020/12/3/22149992/fivio-foreign-baddie-on-my-wish-list-song|url-status=live}}</ref> In November 2020, he released the single "Trust".<ref name="Baddie" /> He also released the Christmas song "Baddie on My Wish List" on December 3, as part of [[Apple Music]]'s&nbsp;holiday project ''Carols Covered''.<ref name="Baddie" />

In 2021, He appeared on [[Kanye West]]'s ''[[Donda]]'' on the songs "Off the Grid" alongside [[Playboi Carti]] and "Ok Ok" alongside [[Lil Yachty]] and Rooga.<ref>{{Cite magazine|url = https://www.billboard.com/music/rb-hip-hop/soulja-boy-slams-kanye-west-left-off-donda-9621975/|title = Soulja Boy Tells Kanye West 'Don't Hit My Phone No More' After Apparently Being Left off 'Donda'|magazine = [[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|date = August 30, 2021|access-date = November 21, 2021|archive-date = February 4, 2022|archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20220204232609/https://www.billboard.com/music/rb-hip-hop/soulja-boy-slams-kanye-west-left-off-donda-9621975/|url-status = live}}</ref> On February 11, 2022, he released the lead single to his debut album ''B.I.B.L.E.'', "[[City of Gods]]" with Kanye West and [[Alicia Keys]], which was dedicated to his late friend T-Dott Woo.<ref name=":1" /> On the same day, the release date for his album ''B.I.B.L.E.'' was revealed to be March 25, 2022, but was eventually pushed back to April 8, 2022.<ref name=":1">{{cite web|title=First Impressions of Fivio Foreign, Kanye West, and Alicia Keys' "City of Gods"|url=https://www.complex.com/music/fivio-foreign-kanye-west-alicia-keys-city-of-god-first-impressions/|access-date=2022-02-12|website=Complex|archive-date=February 12, 2022|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20220212015113/https://www.complex.com/music/fivio-foreign-kanye-west-alicia-keys-city-of-god-first-impressions/|url-status=live}}</ref> On March 18, he released the second single for the album, "Magic City" featuring [[Quavo]]. On April 8, ''B.I.B.L.E.'' was released with features by [[ASAP Rocky|A$AP Rocky]], [[Polo G]], [[Lil Yachty]], Lil Tjay, and more. The album debuted at number 9 on the [[Billboard 200]], with 29,000 equivalent album units derived from 37.75&nbsp;million streams, but only 1,000 pure album sales. Ryles and fellow Brooklyn rapper [[6ix9ine]] traded barbs and boasts about their chart successes on social media, comparing Ryles's ''B.I.B.L.E.'' with 6ix9ine's ''[[TattleTales (album)|TattleTales]]'' album from 2020, which hit number 4 on the same chart but dropped to number 60 the next week.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.complex.com/music/fivio-foreign-hits-back-6ix9ine-blasts-first-week-numbers-claiming-king-of-new-york |title=Fivio Foreign Hits Back After 6ix9ine Blasts First-Week Numbers for Debut Album and Tries Claiming King of NY Title |last=Price |first=Joe |date=April 18, 2022 |website=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] |access-date=August 23, 2022 |archive-date=August 23, 2022 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20220823140919/https://www.complex.com/music/fivio-foreign-hits-back-6ix9ine-blasts-first-week-numbers-claiming-king-of-new-york |url-status=live}}</ref>
