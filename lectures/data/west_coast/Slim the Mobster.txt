== Early life ==
Slim The Mobster grew up all over from [[Loudonville, NY]] to [[Corpus Christi, Texas]].<ref name="Aftermath Interview" />{{Failed verification|date=October 2013}}

==Career==
In February 2008, he was featured on a [[Hussein Fatal]] mixtape.<ref name="Interview w HipHopDX May 2010" />  He delivered demos to [[Dr. Dre]] in 2008 and eventually got a record deal with [[Aftermath Entertainment]]. Dr Dre asked [[Eminem]] and [[50 Cent]] who agreed to sign Slim as a joint venture, officially signing him to Shady/Aftermath/G-Unit Records.<ref name="Interview w HipHopDX May 2010"/><ref name=autogenerated1>{{cite web|url=http://www.hiphopdx.com/interviews/id.1547/title.slim-da-mobster-warning-shots|title=Slim da Mobster: Warning Shots|last=Paine|first=Jake|date=May 24, 2010|access-date=23 November 2010|location=HipHopDX.com}}</ref>

=== 2009-2010: Working on Dr. Dre's ''Detox'' and debut album ===
Slim The Mobster started to work on Dr. Dre's [[Detox (Dr. Dre album)|''Detox'']] in 2009, including the single, "[[Kush (song)|Kush]]".

=== 2011: Slim The Mobster presents War Music ===
His first album was [[War Music (Slim the Mobster album)|''War Music'']], powered by Crooks & Castles. ''War Music'' features ten original tracks including "Whose House?", "What Goes Up", and "Back Against the Wall". In addition to rhymes from Slim, guest work came from [[Snoop Dogg]], [[Kendrick Lamar]], [[Prodigy (rapper)|Prodigy]], [[Sunday Service Choir|Nikki Grier]] and more. The street album features production from Dr. Dre, [[Jake One]], [[Nottz]], [[Bink (producer)|Bink]], Sid Roams, and Siege Monstracity. The album is executive produced by Dr. Dre, Snoop Dogg and [[Sha Money XL]] and was originally hosted by [[DJ Whoo Kid]].

=== 2012–present: ''S.O.O.N. (Something Out of Nothing)'' ===
His debut album under Aftermath Entertainment, entitled ''[[S.O.O.N. (Something Out Of Nothing)]]'', was due to be released on August 21, 2012. Among the many contributors are [[Xzibit]], [[King Tee]], Eminem, Kendrick Lamar, Snoop Dogg, [[Jay Rock]], [[Busta Rhymes]], and [[Big K.R.I.T.]]{{citation needed|date=August 2012}} In 2012 he left [[Aftermath Entertainment]] and planned to release his album ''S.O.O.N. (Something Out Of Nothing)'' on Gang Module.

== Personal life ==
He claimed to have been "stabbed, shot, robbed, kidnapped and was a member of the [[Crips]] street gang" during his life on the streets.<ref name="Interview w HipHopDX May 2010" /> His uncle is former drug kingpin [[Freeway Ricky Ross]].<ref>{{cite web|last=Jacobs |first=Allen |url=http://www.hiphopdx.com/news/id.13385/title.dr-dre-says-in-2011-hes-focusing-on-west-coast-hip-hop--kendrick-lamar-slim-da-mobster |title=Dr. Dre Says In 2011, He's Focusing On West Coast Hip Hop - Kendrick Lamar, Slim da Mobster &#124; Get The Latest Hip Hop News, Rap News & Hip Hop Album Sales |publisher=HipHop DX |date=2010-12-17 |access-date=2011-08-17}}</ref>
