'''Scott Andrew Caan''' (born August 23, 1976)<ref name="UPI"/><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.allmovie.com/artist/scott-caan-p195366|title=Scott Caan|work=[[AllMovie]]|access-date=April 1, 2019}}</ref> is an American actor, director, photographer, writer, and former rapper. He received his breakthrough role in ''[[Ocean’s Eleven]]'' as Turk Malloy who he played in the ''[[Ocean's (film series)|Ocean's trilogy]]'' and starred as Detective [[Danny "Danno" Williams]] in the [[CBS]] television series ''[[Hawaii Five-0 (2010 TV series)|Hawaii Five-0]]'' (2010–2020), for which he was nominated for a [[Golden Globe Award]]. Caan had a recurring role as manager Scott Lavin in the [[HBO]] television series ''[[Entourage (U.S. TV series)|Entourage]]'' (2009–2011). In the 1990s, he was a rapper and was a part of hip hop group [[The Whooliganz]] with [[The Alchemist (musician)|The Alchemist]], under the pseudonym '''Mad Skillz'''.

==Early life==
Caan was born on August 23, 1976, in [[Los Angeles]], [[California]], the son of actor [[James Caan]] and Sheila Marie Ryan, an actress and former model.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.suntimes.com/news/obituaries/15360391-418/dated-elvis-beatty-stallone-married-james-caan.html|title=Chicago - Chicago : News : Politics : Things To Do : Sports|work=Chicago|access-date=2 August 2015}}</ref> His paternal grandparents were [[Jews|Jewish]] immigrants from Germany.<ref name="Caan">{{cite web| last =Model| first =Betsy| title =The Ultimate Caan| work =Cigar Aficionado| url =http://www.cigaraficionado.com/Cigar/CA_Profiles/People_Profile/0,2540,187,00.html| access-date =2006-12-13| url-status =dead| archive-url =https://web.archive.org/web/20061206221723/http://www.cigaraficionado.com/Cigar/CA_Profiles/People_Profile/0,2540,187,00.html| archive-date =2006-12-06}}</ref> He has an older half-sister and three younger half-brothers.

==Career==

Caan was a [[roadie]] for the [[hip hop music|hip hop]] groups [[Cypress Hill]] and [[House of Pain]]. Caan was also a member of the [[hip-hop]] group [[The Whooliganz]] as Mad Skillz (with [[Record producer|producer]] and fellow [[MC]] [[The Alchemist (musician)|The Alchemist]] as Mudfoot).<ref>The Alchemist Speaks on Relationship with Scott Caan [Video]</ref> The Alchemist and he, when performing as the rap duo the Whooliganz in the early 1990s, signed a record deal with Tommy Boy/Warner Bros. records. They recorded the album ''Make Way for the W'', but after their first single "Put Your Handz Up",<ref name=VanityFair>"It's Evening in America". ''[[Vanity Fair (magazine)|Vanity Fair]]''. May 2012. Page 155.</ref> the album was shelved and the duo was dropped by Tommy Boy. In 1995, the Whooliganz' song "Whooliganz" was released as a single in the UK, but Caan and the Alchemist had already parted ways. Caan reunited with his former partner The Alchemist in 2014 on the Step Brothers project ''[[Lord Steppington]]'', performing on the song "Byron G" with musician [[Evidence (musician)|Evidence]].

After enrolling at the Playhouse West acting school in Los Angeles, Caan began acting in the late 1990s, appearing in a number of [[independent film]]s and low-budget films. His first role in a major motion picture was that of Charlie Tweeder, a reckless [[philandering]] [[Texas]] [[high school football]] [[wide receiver]] in the [[teen movie]] ''[[Varsity Blues (film)|Varsity Blues]]'' ([[1999 in film|1999]]), alongside [[James Van Der Beek]] and [[Paul Walker]].  In the same year, he played the role of Drew in the film ''Saturn'' (also known as ''Speed of Life''). Caan subsequently appeared in several studio films, including ''[[Ready to Rumble]]'' (2000) co-starring [[David Arquette]], ''[[Boiler Room (film)|Boiler Room]]'' (2000) co-starring [[Vin Diesel]], ''[[Gone in 60 Seconds (2000 film)|Gone in 60 Seconds]]'' (2000) as Tumbler, and ''[[American Outlaws (2001 film)|American Outlaws]]'' (2001) co-starring [[Colin Farrell]], in which Caan played 19th-century outlaw [[Cole Younger]]. In 2003, Caan made his directorial debut with the film ''[[Dallas 362]]'', which won a prize at the 2003 Las Vegas Film Festival.

Caan appeared in the feature film trilogy ''[[Ocean's Eleven]]'', ''[[Ocean's Twelve]]'', and ''[[Ocean's Thirteen]]''. In 2005, he co-starred with [[Paul Walker]] (with whom he had appeared in ''Varsity Blues'') in the action film ''[[Into the Blue (2005 film)|Into the Blue]]''. Caan wrote and directed the 2006 comedy ''[[The Dog Problem]],'' and appeared as a supporting character in the film as well. He appeared on the television series ''[[Entourage (U.S. TV series)|Entourage]]'' in a recurring role as talent manager Scott Lavin from seasons six to eight.<ref>{{cite web|url =http://www.tvguide.com/News/Entourage-Caan-Fichtner-1006707.aspx|title=Entourage Admits Caan, Fichtner, Letscher|publisher=TVGuide.com|access-date=2009-06-08}}</ref>

Caan played Detective [[Danny "Danno" Williams]] in ''[[Hawaii Five-0 (2010 TV series)|Hawaii Five-0]]'' (a re-imagining of the 1968 ''[[Hawaii Five-O (1968 TV series)|Hawaii Five-O]]'' television series). The new series premiered on September 20, 2010, and in 2011 he was nominated for a [[Golden Globe Award for Best Supporting Actor – Series, Miniseries or Television Film]] for his performance.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.hitfix.com/blogs/whats-alan-watching/posts/golden-globes-2011-analyzing-the-tv-nominations|title=Golden Globes 2011: Analyzing the TV nominations|work=HitFix|access-date=2 August 2015}}</ref> 

[[BuddyTV]] ranked him 95th on its list of "TV's Sexiest Men of 2011".<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.buddytv.com/slideshows/white-collar/tvs-100-sexiest-men-of-2011-29104.aspx|title=TV's 100 Sexiest Men of 2011|date=December 7, 2011 |publisher=[[BuddyTV]]|access-date=January 13, 2012}}</ref>

In addition to acting, Caan has also pursued a career in photography.<ref>{{cite web|last=Caan |first=Scott |title=Scott Caan Photography |url=http://www.scaanphoto.com |access-date=16 May 2011 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110507184808/http://www.scaanphoto.com/ |archive-date=May 7, 2011 }}</ref> Inspired and trained by cinematographer [[Phil Parmet]] while working together on the 2003 film ''Dallas 362'', Caan has been shooting ever since. "In preparing for the film," Caan said, "Phil inspired me to learn about lenses, lights, frames, and the operation of a 250 millimeter camera.  By the time the film was over, I wanted to shoot the next one."<ref>{{cite web|last=Caan|first=Scott|title=Scott Caan Photographs 2003-2009 - Biography|url=http://www.scaanphoto.com/index2.html|access-date=16 May 2011}}</ref> In 2009, he published his first collection of photographs in a 256-page book titled ''Scott Caan Photographs, Vol. 1''. The book was edited and designed by Howard Nourmand, and includes an introduction by Steve Olson.<ref>{{cite book|title=Amazon|isbn=978-0981805603|last1=Caan|first1=Scott|year=2009}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Caan has a black belt in Brazilian jiu-jitsu.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://ego.globo.com/famosos/noticia/2014/02/scott-caan-astro-da-serie-havai-cinco-0-se-rende-ao-jiu-jitsu-brasileiro.html|title=EGO - Scott Caan, astro da série 'Havaí Cinco-0', se rende ao jiu-jítsu brasileiro - notícias de Famosos|date=18 February 2014|work=Ego|access-date=2 August 2015}}</ref> In July 2014, Caan's girlfriend Kacy Byxbee gave birth to their daughter.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.usmagazine.com/celebrity-moms/news/scott-caans-girlfriend-pregnant-actor-kacy-byxbee-expecting-child--2014106|title=Scott Caan's Girlfriend Pregnant: Actor, Kacy Byxbee Expecting Child - Us Weekly|date=11 June 2014|work=usmagazine.com|access-date=2 August 2015}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |title='Hawaii Five-0' star Scott Caan welcomes a daughter {{!}} Globalnews.ca |url=https://globalnews.ca/news/1479558/hawaii-five-0-star-scott-caan-welcomes-a-daughter/ |access-date=2023-01-25 |website=Global News |language=en-US}}</ref>

As of 2012, Caan was an active volunteer with several organizations that introduce children with autism to surfing.<ref>{{cite web |title=''Ability Magazine: Scott Caan - Surf Therapy for Autism"'' (2009) |url=http://abilitymagazine.com/scott-caan.html |access-date=2012-04-03}}</ref>
