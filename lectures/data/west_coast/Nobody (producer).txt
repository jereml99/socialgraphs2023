'''Elvin Estela''' (born March 14, 1977<ref name=birthday/>), better known by his stage name '''Nobody''', is an American hip hop producer based in [[Los Angeles]], [[California]].<ref>{{cite web|first=Jeremy|last=Leeuwis|url=http://musicremedy.com/n/nobody/album/revisions-revisions-3355.html|title=Nobody to release Revisions Revisions|work=Music Remedy|date=November 10, 2006}}</ref> He is a resident DJ at [[Low End Theory]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.spin.com/2013/06/dj-nobody-cedric-bixler-zavala-our-last-dance-stream/|title=DJ Nobody Teams With Cedric Bixler-Zavala for Sunny Psych-Hop 'Our Last Dance'|work=[[Spin (magazine)|Spin]]|first=Chris|last=Martins|date=June 21, 2013}}</ref> He has collaborated with [[2Mex]], [[Abstract Rude]], [[Freestyle Fellowship]],<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.rapreviews.com/archive/2001_01_soulmates.html|title=Nobody&nbsp;— Soulmates&nbsp;— Ubiquity Records|work=RapReviews|first=John|last=Book|date=January 16, 2001}}</ref> [[Busdriver]], and [[Mystic Chords of Memory]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://blogs.laweekly.com/westcoastsound/2010/10/nocando_dj_nobody_bomb_zombies_fuck_what_you_heard.php|title=Nocando and DJ Nobody are 'Bomb Zombies'|work=[[LA Weekly]]|first=Chris|last=Martins|date=October 6, 2010}}</ref> He has also been a member of Blank Blue<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.urb.com/2008/05/06/blank-blue-nobody-presents-blank-blue-western-water-music-vol-ii/ |title=Blank Blue&nbsp;– Nobody Presents Blank Blue: Western Water Music Vol. II (Review) [[Bomb Zombies]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.xlr8r.com/news/2010/10/nocando-and-nobody-collaborate-b|title=Nocando and Nobody Collaborate on Bomb ==Life and career==
Nobody's solo debut album, ''Soulmates'', was released on [[Ubiquity Records]] in 2000.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://exclaim.ca/Reviews/HipHop/nobody-soulmates|title=Nobody&nbsp;— Soulmates|work=[[Exclaim!]]|date=November 2000|first=Thomas|last=Quinlan}}</ref> The second solo album, ''Pacific Drift: Western Water Music Vol. 1'', was released in 2003.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.avclub.com/articles/nobody-pacific-drift-western-water-music-vol-1,11803/|title=Nobody: Pacific Drift: Western Water Music Vol. 1|work=[[The A.V. Club]]|first=Andy|last=Battaglia|date=September 16, 2003}}</ref> He released the third solo album, ''[[And Everything Else...]]'', on [[Plug Research]] in 2005.<ref>{{cite web|first=Ryan|last=Dombal|url=http://pitchfork.com/reviews/albums/5914-and-everything-else/|title=Nobody: And Everything Else...|work=[[Pitchfork (website)|Pitchfork]]|date=June 7, 2005}}</ref> It features guest appearances from [[Mia Doi Todd]], [[Prefuse 73]] and Xololanxinxo, among others.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.dustedmagazine.com/reviews/2216|title=...And Everything Else|work=Dusted Magazine|date=June 9, 2005|first=Emerson|last=Dameron}}</ref>

Nobody wrote the article about 1960s psychedelic rock for ''[[XLR8R]]'' in 2006.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.xlr8r.com/features/2006/04/nobody-trip-fantastic|title=Nobody: Trip Fantastic|work=[[XLR8R]]|first=Elvin|last=Estela|date=May 8, 2006}}</ref>

In 2008, Estela released the album, ''Western Water Music Vol. II'',<ref>{{cite web|url=http://larecord.com/interviews/2007/06/14/blank-blue-how-we-listen-is-how-we-live|title=Blank Blue: How We Listen Is How We Live|work=[[L.A. Record]]|date=June 14, 2007}}</ref> on Ubiquity Records under the Blank Blue moniker, a collaboration with Niki Randa.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.prefixmag.com/reviews/blank-blue/western-water-music-vol-2/18375/|title=Album Review: Blank Blue - Western Water Music Vol. 2|work=Prefix|first=Dan|last=Nishimoto|date=April 27, 2008}}</ref> It is the official follow-up to his 2003 album, ''Pacific Draft: Western Water Music Vol. 1''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.urb.com/2008/05/06/blank-blue-nobody-presents-blank-blue-western-water-music-vol-ii/ |title=Blank Blue – Nobody Presents Blank Blue: Western Water Music Vol. II |work=[[URB (magazine)|URB]] |date=May 6, 2008 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20121023054148/http://www.urb.com/2008/05/06/blank-blue-nobody-presents-blank-blue-western-water-music-vol-ii/ |archive-date=October 23, 2012 }}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.allmusic.com/album/western-water-music-vol-ii-mw0000785827|title=Western Water Music, Vol. II - Blank Blue|work=[[AllMusic]]|first=Marisa|last=Brown|access-date=November 8, 2014}}</ref> The duo's sound has been compared to [[The Monkees]], [[DJ Shadow]], and [[J Dilla]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://thephoenix.com/boston/music/62565-nobody/|title=Blank Blue: Western Water Music Vol. II|work=[[The Phoenix (newspaper)|The Phoenix]]|first=Gustavo|last=Turner|date=June 3, 2008}}</ref> Live the pair have been assisted by drummer Andres Renteria and bassist Brian Akio Martinez.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://larecord.com/live-reviews/2010/09/24/live-review-blank-blue-the-airliner|title=Blank Blue @ The Airliner|work=[[L.A. Record]]|first=Daiana|last=Feuer|date=September 24, 2010}}</ref>

The fourth solo album, ''One for All Without Hesitation'', was released on [[Alpha Pup Records]] in 2010.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://blogs.laweekly.com/westcoastsound/2010/06/interview_dj_nobody_free_album_auto_tune.php|title=Q&A: DJ Nobody on Love, Auto-Tune and Low End Theory|work=[[LA Weekly]]|first=Chris|last=Martins|date=June 10, 2010}}</ref>

He returned with ''Vivid Green'', an album featuring [[Cedric Bixler-Zavala]], [[Nocando]] and [[Baths (musician)|Baths]], in 2013.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://pitchfork.com/reviews/albums/18302-dj-nobody-vivid-green/|title=Nobody: Vivid Green|work=[[Pitchfork (website)|Pitchfork]]|first=Nate|last=Patrin|date=July 25, 2013}}</ref>

In 2019, he released a studio album, ''All Too Familiar''.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.allmusic.com/album/all-too-familiar-mw0003262279|title=All Too Familiar - Nobody|work=[[AllMusic]]|first=Paul|last=Simpson|access-date=June 9, 2019}}</ref>
