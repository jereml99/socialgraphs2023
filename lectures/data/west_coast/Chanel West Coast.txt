'''Chelsea Chanel Dudley'''<ref name="OfficalBiography">{{cite web|title=Chanel West Coast – Biography|url=http://www.iamchanelwestcoast.com/bio/|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140515010307/http://www.iamchanelwestcoast.com/bio |archive-date=15 May 2014 |access-date=January 8, 2015}}</ref> (born September 1, 1988),<ref>{{cite web |title=Chelsea Chanel Dudley, Born 09/01/1988 in California |url=https://www.californiabirthindex.org/birth/chelsea_chanel_dudley_born_1988_18476616 |website=CaliforniaBirthIndex.org |access-date=11 September 2022}}</ref> better known by her stage name '''Chanel West Coast''',<ref name=OfficalBiography /> is an American television personality, rapper, and singer.

She came to prominence for her roles in [[MTV]]'s ''[[Rob Dyrdek's Fantasy Factory]]'' and ''[[Ridiculousness (TV series)|Ridiculousness]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.mtv.com/shows/ridiculousness/series.jhtml |title=Ridiculousness &#124; Season 2 Full Episodes, Photos, Episode Synopsis and Recaps |publisher=MTV |access-date=August 17, 2013}}</ref>

== Early life ==
Chanel was born in [[Los Angeles]]. While growing up, she lived part of the year in [[North Hollywood, Los Angeles|North Hollywood]] with her mother and in [[New York City]] with her father, <ref name="hollywoodmask-boyfriend-2018-gender">{{cite web |title=Chanel West Coast Is Trying to Find a Husband — Inside Her Personal Life |url=https://hollywoodmask.com/celebs/chanel-west-coast-boyfriend-2018-dating-gender.html |website=hollywoodmask |date=August 4, 2022 |access-date=11 September 2022 |language=en}}</ref><ref name="intouchweekly-dj-dad">{{cite web |last1=Silverman |first1=Sam |title=We Love Chanel, But Where is Mister and Missus West Coast? |url=https://www.intouchweekly.com/posts/chanel-west-coasts-family-from-her-dj-dad-to-her-l-a-mom/ |website=In Touch Weekly |access-date=11 September 2022 |date=19 December 2019}}</ref> a DJ in New York City. She began listening to rap music at eleven years old and began rapping at 14;<ref name="laweekly">{{cite web|url=https://www.laweekly.com/chanel-west-coast-the-other-white-female-rapper/|title=Chanel West Coast: The Other White Female Rapper|date=August 13, 2014|access-date=April 15, 2020 | work=[[LA Weekly]] | first=Amanda | last=Lewis }}</ref> she says "[[How Do U Want It]]" by [[Tupac Shakur]] is the song which inspired her to start rapping.<ref name="Soundtracked">{{cite video |people=Chanel West Coast (guest) |date=April 27, 2018 |title=Chanel West Coast Reveals the Tupac Song That Inspired Her To Pursue Rap <nowiki>| Soundtracked |</nowiki> TRL | url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=N24kY4RSV6Q |publisher=YouTube |access-date =May 5, 2018}}</ref> Chanel attended [[William Howard Taft Charter High School|Taft High School]] in [[Woodland Hills, Los Angeles|Woodland Hills]], Los Angeles for two years before homeschooling until graduation.<ref name="laweekly" />

== Television career ==
Chanel was introduced to television personality [[Rob Dyrdek]] through mutual friends in 2008.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://m.mtv.com/shows/cast_member.rbml?id=fantasy_factory&person=15297&weburl=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.mtv.com%2Fshows%2Ffantasy_factory%2Fseason_5%2Fcast_member.jhtml%3FpersonalityId%3D15297&alt=http%3A%2F%2Fm.mtv.com%2Fshows%2Findex.rbml&cid=300 |title=MTV Mobile Series: Cast Member: Chanel |website=[[MTV]] |access-date=August 16, 2013 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131023060519/http://m.mtv.com/shows/cast_member.rbml?id=fantasy_factory&person=15297&weburl=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.mtv.com%2Fshows%2Ffantasy_factory%2Fseason_5%2Fcast_member.jhtml%3FpersonalityId%3D15297&alt=http%3A%2F%2Fm.mtv.com%2Fshows%2Findex.rbml&cid=300 |archive-date=October 23, 2013 |url-status=dead }}</ref> Shortly afterwards, Dyrdek offered Chanel a position as his receptionist while he was appearing on the [[MTV]] reality series ''[[Rob Dyrdek's Fantasy Factory]]''.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://dose.clubzone.com/events/670102/los_angeles/Euphoria.html |archive-url=https://archive.today/2013.10.15-072814/http://dose.clubzone.com/events/670102/los_angeles/Euphoria.html |url-status=dead |archive-date=October 15, 2013 |title=Avalon Hollywood Los Angeles CA (Nightclub) |publisher=Dose.clubzone.com |date=October 4, 2013 |access-date=January 13, 2014 }}</ref> The series was renewed for a second season by MTV.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.mtv.com/shows/fantasy_factory/season_2/series.jhtml |title=Rob Dyrdek's Fantasy Factory &#124; Full Episodes, Photos, Episode Synopsis and Recaps |publisher=MTV |access-date=January 13, 2014}}</ref> The third season of the series premiered in 2011<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.mtv.com/shows/fantasy_factory/season_3/video.jhtml |title=Rob Dyrdek's Fantasy Factory Videos |publisher=MTV |access-date=January 13, 2014}}</ref> and the fourth season began airing later that year.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.mtv.com/shows/fantasy_factory/season_4/series.jhtml |title=Rob Dyrdek's Fantasy Factory &#124; Full Episodes, Photos, Episode Synopsis and Recaps |publisher=MTV |access-date=January 13, 2014}}</ref> In 2011 Chanel appeared as Sheila in an episode of the MTV series ''[[The Hard Times of RJ Berger]]'', making her acting debut.

== Music career ==
Chanel began recording music in 2009, and began collaborating with numerous artists. That year, she recorded the song "Melting Like Ice Cream" featuring [[Girlicious|Tiffanie Anderson]]. She later appeared in the [[Planet Hollywood]] song "PHAMOUS", collaborating with [[Midi Mafia]] on the project. In 2011, Chanel confirmed she had signed with [[Polow Da Don]] on his record label [[Zone 4 (record label)|Zone 4]], though she later left the label without releasing any material. During this time, Chanel began releasing her own material for free download online, later recording a music video for the song "I Love Money".<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.hotnewhiphop.com/chanel-i-love-money-song.1355726.html |title=Chanel West Coast – I Love Money [New Song&#93; |publisher=Hotnewhiphop.com |date=April 30, 2013 |access-date=January 13, 2014}}</ref>

[[Lil Wayne]] signed Chanel to his label [[Young Money Entertainment]] in 2012. She went on to release her mixtape ''Now You Know'' in 2013 featuring [[Snoop Dogg]], [[French Montana]], [[Ty Dolla $ign]], [[Robin Thicke]] and [[Honey Cocaine]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.datpiff.com/Chanel-West-Coast-Now-You-Know-mixtape.508351.html |title=Chanel West Coast – Now You Know // Free Mixtape @ |publisher=Datpiff.com |access-date=January 13, 2014}}</ref> Riley Sky of ''Dropkick Divas Media'' gave the mixtape a grade of B−, saying "Now You Know accomplishes [the] mission" of "[selling] Chanel as an independent woman, its hip-hop-inspired vibe makes "Now You Know" fiercely individual".<ref name=Dropkick>{{cite web|last=Sky|first=Riley|title='' Now You Know'' Musical review|url=http://dropkickdivasmedia.blogspot.com/2014/05/now-you-know-musical-review.html|publisher=Dropkick Divas Media|date=May 2014}}</ref> Chanel promoted her mixtape by going on two headlining tours; the Punch Drunk Love Tour and the Now You Know Tour. She also released a music video for "Been On", featuring French Montana.<ref>[http://www.youngmoneyhq.com/2014/05/06/chanel-west-coast-been-on-feat-french-montana-music-video/ "Chanel West Coast – Been On (Feat French Montana) &#91; Music Video&#93;"]. ''Young Money Fansite''. May 6, 2014,</ref> She released her second mixtape ''WAVES'', featuring [[YG (rapper)|YG]] and [[B-Real]], in August 2015.<ref name="waves">{{cite magazine|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/the-juice/6677573/chanel-west-coast-waves-mixtape-premiere|title=Listen to Chanel West Coast's New Mixtape 'Waves': Exclusive|magazine=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] |date=August 26, 2015|access-date=April 15, 2020}}</ref>

=== 2014–present: Debut studio album ===
In 2014, West Coast confirmed that she had begun recording her debut studio album set to be released in 2016, which was originally projected to drop in 2014.<ref name=Bankruptcy>{{cite web|last1=Mel's|first1=Chloe|title=Chanel West Coast Files for Bankruptcy|url=http://hollywoodlife.com/2014/03/12/chanel-west-coast-bankruptcy-documents-pics/|publisher=Hollywood Life|date=March 12, 2014}}</ref> The first single, called "Blueberry Chills" featuring Honey Cocaine was released on January 15, 2014.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.rap-up.com/2014/01/15/new-music-chanel-west-coast-f-honey-cocaine-blueberry-chills/ |title=NEW MUSIC: CHANEL WEST COAST F/ HONEY COCAINE – 'BLUEBERRY CHILLS' |publisher=rap-up.com |date=January 15, 2014}}</ref> The second single off the album, "New Feeling" was released on January 29.<ref>{{cite web|title=New Feeling – Single|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/new-feeling-single/id811357937|publisher=[[iTunes]]|access-date=January 1, 2015}}</ref> The third single off the album, "Miles and Miles" was released on October 29, which features West Coast providing vocals for the first time. In 2015, West Coast released a new song that was featured on Rob Dyrdek's Fantasy Factory called "Bass In The Trunk".<ref>{{cite web|title=Miles and Miles – Single|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/miles-and-miles-single/id935326019|publisher=[[iTunes]]|access-date=January 1, 2015}}</ref> In 2018, West Coast released a song titled "Nobody."<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.intouchweekly.com/posts/chanel-west-coast-nobody-151774|title=Chanel West Coast's New Song 'Nobody' Is the Girl Power Anthem the World Needs Right Now (EXCLUSIVE)|date=January 18, 2018|work=In Touch Weekly|access-date=September 15, 2018}}</ref>

On August 23, 2019, Chanel released her Single "I Be Like" Featuring Dax, after performing to the public 2 years before the releasing date.
Then The Song Is remixed by the Armenian Music Producer "Harout Zadikian" on November 23, 2020, in Bass House Genre.

On October 23, 2020, Chanel West Coast released her debut studio album ''America's Sweetheart'' after delaying it for 4 years as a "[[surprise album]]."<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.complex.com/music/2020/10/chanel-west-coast-americas-sweetheart-debut-album|title=Listen to Chanel West Coast's Debut Album 'America's Sweetheart'|date=October 23, 2020 |work=Complex|access-date=October 23, 2020}}</ref>

== Other ventures ==
In 2009, Chanel helped create a clothing line called Valleywood.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.seventeen.com/fashion/celeb-fashion/news/a5827/valleywood-clothing-chanel/|title=Check Out the New Clothing Line, Valleywood!|date=September 28, 2009|work=[[Seventeen (American magazine)|Seventeen]]|access-date=January 30, 2020}}</ref> As of 2019, she also released another clothing line named LOL CARTEL.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.fuse.tv/videos/2019/10/bust-it-open-chanel-west-coast|title=Chanel West Coast Unboxes Vintage Streetwear + Giveaway|publisher=[[Fuse (TV channel)|Fuse]]|date=October 9, 2019}}</ref> In 2022, Chanel launched a line of eco-friendly body positivity swimwear called Coasty Swim.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Chensvold |first1=Christian |title=Chanel West Coast Launches Eco-Friendly, Body-Positive Swim Collection |url=https://www.apparelnews.net/news/2023/jan/22/chanel-west-coast-launches-eco-friendly-body-posit/ |website=Apparel News |access-date=2 May 2023 |date=22 January 2023}}</ref>

In 2018, Chanel curated the Chanel West Coast collection of smoking supplies for the cannabis subscription box company Daily High Club.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://honeysucklemag.com/daily-high-club-chanel-west-coast-video/|title=She Love It: Unboxing the Daily High Club's Chanel West Coast Collection (Video)|publisher=Honeysuckle|date=December 8, 2018}}</ref>
