'''Tyrone Wrice''', better known by his [[stage name]] '''Hurt-M-Badd''', is an American singer and record producer. He is a former member of short-lived R&B duo B-Rezell with Marc "MGM" McWilliams, which appeared on [[Death Row Records]] 1994 compilations ''[[Murder Was the Case]]'' and ''[[Above the Rim (soundtrack)|Above the Rim – The Soundtrack]]'' before the break-up. He participated on [[Tupac Shakur|2Pac]]'s 1996 ''[[The Don Killuminati: The 7 Day Theory]]'' album, most notably produced the single "[[Hail Mary (2Pac song)|Hail Mary]]". In 1997 he co-produced one song each for [[The Lady of Rage]]'s [[Necessary Roughness (album)|debut album]] and ''[[Gang Related – The Soundtrack]]''. From 1998 to 2002, he continued to produce songs for the [[Outlawz]] and 2Pac's posthumous releases,<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.allmusic.com/artist/hurt-m-badd-mn0001753842|title = Hurt-M-Badd &#124; Credits|website = [[AllMusic]]}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.rapnews.net/0-202-257410-00.html|title = Rap News Network - RapNewsDirect Talks to 2Pac Producer Hurt M Badd}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.bomb1st.com/hurt-m-badd-interview-clip/ |title=bomb1st {{!}} HURT-M-BADD INTERVIEW CLIP |website=www.bomb1st.com |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140727051446/http://www.bomb1st.com/hurt-m-badd-interview-clip/ |archive-date=2014-07-27}} </ref> as well as worked with [[Willie D]] and [[Snypaz]]. In 2013 he produced a bonus track for [[Hussein Fatal|Fatal]]'s ''[[The Interview: It's Not a Gimmik 2 Me]]''.
