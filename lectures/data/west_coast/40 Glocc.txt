'''Lawrence Carl Demetrious White''',<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.mankatofreepress.com/news/local_news/rapper-sentenced-in-prostitution-sting/article_33c2f98a-bbd8-11ea-8768-9bca494dd997.html|title = Rapper sentenced in prostitution sting}}</ref>(born 16 December 1979) better known by his stage name '''40 Glocc''', is an American rapper.

==Early life==
White was born in [[Greenville, Texas]]. At the age of 10, he moved with his mother to various locations in [[California]], until finally settling in the [[Inland Empire (California)|Inland Empire]] city of [[Colton, California|Colton]]. He began rapping with friends and by 1997, his group, the "Zoo Crew", were recording and promoting their first album, ''Migrate, Adapt or Die'', produced by Tony G and [[Julio G]]. H<ref>
[http://www.artistdirect.com/nad/store/artist/album/0,,373783,00.html The Zoo Crew: Migrate Adapt or Die at ARTISTdirect]. ArtistDirect. Retrieved August 12, 2008.</ref>

==Career==
White negotiated a deal with Empire Musicwerks, with distribution by [[Bertelsmann Music Group|BMG]]. It was through this deal, he recorded and released his first solo effort, "The Jakal", which featured such guests as [[Bad Azz (rapper)|Bad Azz]], [[Ras Kass]], [[Kurupt]], [[Mac Minister]],  and several others; and such producers as [[Battlecat (producer)|Battlecat]] and [[Dr. Dre]] among several others. It was after this album that he decided to part ways with Empire Music.

His manager was also managing Mobb Deep, who were then signed to G-Unit Records.<ref name="dubcnninterview">Jay, Lil (June 2008). [http://www.dubcnn.com/interviews/lloydbanks_tonyyayo/ Lloyd Banks & Tony Yayo Interview]. DuBCNN. Retrieved July 28, 2008.</ref><ref name="tattoos">Reid, Shaheem  (January 25, 2006) [http://www.mtv.com/news/articles/1521579/20060125/mobb_deep.jhtml?headlines=true Mobb Deep, 50 Cent Get Tattoos To Prove Loyalty] MTV Accessed August 15, 2008.</ref> 40 Glocc joined their label, Infamous Records, under the [[G-Unit]] imprint.<ref name="tnnreview">Milne, Conan (May 2007) [http://www.dubcnn.com/features/reviews/thatnewnigga/ Review of "That New Nigga"]. DubCNN. Retrieved August 11, 2008.</ref><ref name="G4TV">[http://www.g4tv.com/thefeed/blog/post/678639/Freestyle_101_40_Glocc__Gail_Gotti_GUnit.html G4 – The Feed – Freestyle 101: 40 Glocc & Gail Gotti (G-Unit)] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080424010646/http://www.g4tv.com/thefeed/blog/post/678639/Freestyle_101_40_Glocc__Gail_Gotti_GUnit.html |date=April 24, 2008 }} G4TV Accessed August 14, 2008.</ref>

White has worked with artists such as [[Dr. Dre]], [[The Alchemist (musician)|The Alchemist]] and [[Havoc (musician)|Havoc]]. He has also been working with his group, the "Zoo Babies", while also promoting his label "ZooLife Ent.".<ref>[http://www.dubcnn.com/cgi-bin/newspro/viewnews.cgi?search+giantz+playa+hamm DubCNN News Archive.] (You may have to scroll down to find the particular section.) DubCNN.com Accessed August 12, 2008.</ref> He has also been on tour with such acts as G-Unit, Mobb Deep, Ray J, Snoop & Doggpound and many more. He released a song entitled "Where the Hammers At?",<ref>[http://www.dubcnm.com/audio/2006/april/40_glocc_sam_scarfo-where_them_hammers_at-(dubcnn).mp3 40 Glocc & Sam Scarfo "Where the Hammers At?" on DubCNN.com] DubCNN.com Accessed August 7, 2008.</ref> the beat for which was later used as the beat for G-Unit's song [[Rider Pt. 2]].<ref name="dubcnninterview"/> He later released a remix to "Hammers" which features G-Unit and their respective verses of Rider Pt. 2.<ref>[http://www.dubcnm.com/audio/2008/march/40_gocc_ft_50_cent_lloyd_banks_young_buck_tony_yayo-hammers_remix_gunit_rider-(dubcnn).mp3 40 Glocc ft. 50 Cent, Lloyd Banks, Young Buck, Tony Yayo "Hammers" G-Unit remix on DubCNN.com] DubCNN.com (March 2008).</ref> In late fall 2010, White signed affiliated rapper [[Ras Kass]] to his label Zoo Life. He released a digital street album that year called "COPS" with various features from Jayo Felony, Tip Toe, Tony Yayo and more. in 2011, White and Spider Loc released an album "Graveyard Shift". The following year, he released his first solo album.

==Legal issues==
In 1996, White was arrested following a shootout with police outside the Arbor Terrace apartment complex in Colton, and was charged with attempted murder of a police officer.<ref name="facebook.com">{{Cite web | url=https://www.facebook.com/notes/thisis50/in-the-news-again-colton-rapper-40-glocc-scoffs-at-gangster-i/24201188050 |title = In the News Again: Colton rapper 40 Glocc scoffs at gangster i... &#124; Facebook|website = [[Facebook]]}}</ref>{{better source needed|date=November 2017}}

In June 2008, a video was posted online of 40 Glocc holding two diamond [[Young Money]] necklaces, previously taken from Young Money recording artist [[Tyga]].<ref>{{Citation|title=40 glocc @40glocc LIL WAYNE BLOOD CHAIN SNATCHED BY ZOOLIFE G-UNIT VILLAGE BOO 3 AMIGOS VIDEO|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=M7N1ZBq62vc |archive-url=https://ghostarchive.org/varchive/youtube/20211220/M7N1ZBq62vc |archive-date=2021-12-20 |url-status=live|language=en|access-date=February 1, 2020}}{{cbignore}}</ref> 40 Glocc held the necklaces for ransom after a fellow member of his entourage took them from Tyga.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.ballerstatus.com/2008/06/28/tyga-admits-to-chain-snatching-explains-incident/|title=Tyga Admits To Chain Snatching, Explains Incident|date=June 28, 2008|website=BallerStatus.com|language=en-US|access-date=February 1, 2020}}</ref> Both necklaces were reportedly bought back by [[Lil Wayne]] in "Cash Money".<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.ballerstatus.com/2008/06/11/tyga-addresses-chain-snatching-rumors-40-glocc-says-chains-were-bought-back/|title=Tyga Addresses Chain Snatching Rumors, 40 Glocc Says Chains Were Bought Back|date=June 11, 2008|website=BallerStatus.com|language=en-US|access-date=February 1, 2020}}</ref>

In October 2009, a video was posted online showing White and members of the Colton City Crips Gang showing up to Florida rapper [[Plies (rapper)|Plies]]' video shoot to test his street credibility.  The video shoot, which also featured [[Jamie Foxx]], was being filmed in L.A. Foxx, who was standing next to Plies when he was about to be approached by White, confirms that Plies ran towards the bathrooms and locked himself inside. He claims Plies was afraid for his life and returned to the video shoot wearing no jewelry.<ref>[http://allhiphop.com/2009/11/24/40-glocc-lil-boo-reply-to-plies-diss-with-return-to-sender/ 40 Glocc, Lil Boo Reply To Plies' Diss With ‘Return to Sender']. AllHipHop.com (November 24, 2009). Retrieved on 2013-04-20.</ref>

In October 2010, a California judge ruled that White, who is a gang member with the Colton City [[Crips]], would be included in an injunction prohibiting gang members from congregating near the Arbor Terrace apartments, which was believed to be the gang's headquarters.<ref>Gamble, Ronnie. (September 8, 2010) [http://www.ballerstatus.com/2010/09/08/40-glocc-banned-from-california-apartment-complex-as-part-of-gang-injunction/ 40 Glocc Banned From California Apartment Complex As Part of Gang Injunction]. BallerStatus.com. Retrieved on 2013-04-20.</ref> White was arrested on January 24, 2011, in San Bernardino after a loaded 9mm handgun was found in his car, and was found loitering a block away from the Arbor Terrace at the time of his arrest. The charges were ultimately dropped due to lack of evidence.<ref>[http://latimesblogs.latimes.com/lanow/2011/01/rapper-40-glocc-arrested-in-san-bernardino.html Rapper 40 Glocc arrested in San Bernardino]. Latimesblogs.latimes.com (January 24, 2011). Retrieved on 2013-04-20.</ref>

On October 3, 2011, White was arrested in Las Vegas and charged with three counts of domestic battery by strangulation and coercion with force. White allegedly hit his girlfriend several times around the head, face, legs, and rib area and choking her to the point she could not speak or breathe. Once he fell asleep, she was able to sneak away and dial 911.Police arrested White as he was trying to flee his home. The woman claims that White had been assaulting her for over a year and she was afraid to go to the law, in fear she might be killed.<ref>Langhorne, Cyrus. (December 10, 2011) [http://www.sohh.com/2011/12/g-units_40_glocc_arrested_over_domestic.html G-Unit's 40 Glocc Arrested Over Domestic Dispute, "[I&#93; Could Not Speak Or Breathe"]. Sohh.Com. Retrieved on 2013-04-20.</ref>

White has additionally been involved in multiple altercations with fellow rap artists. In July 2012, he was assaulted and beaten up by rapper [[Game (rapper)|The Game]] outside of a mansion in Hollywood.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/40-glocc-recounts-tumbling-down-hill-with-the-game-in-hot-pursuit-news.102342.html|title=40 Glocc Recounts Tumbling Down Hill With The Game In Hot Pursuit|website=HotNewHipHop|date=January 30, 2020|access-date=February 1, 2020}}</ref> White sued Taylor for $4.5 million in damages for [[assault]] and [[battery (crime)|battery]], as well as damage to his reputation.<ref>Horowitz, Steven J.. (March 11, 2013) [http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/news/id.23154/title.40-glocc-suing-game-for-4-5-million-over-assault 40 Glocc Suing Game For $4.5 Million Over Assault | Get The Latest Hip Hop News, Rap News & Hip Hop Album Sales]. HipHop DX. Retrieved on 2013-04-20.</ref><ref>[http://www.thisis50.com/profiles/blogs/exclusive-40-glocc-alledgley-knocks-out-games-manager-taydoe?xg_source=activity Exclusive! 40 Glocc Allegedly Knocks Out Game's Manager Taydoe] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130104141750/http://www.thisis50.com/profiles/blogs/exclusive-40-glocc-alledgley-knocks-out-games-manager-taydoe?xg_source=activity |date=January 4, 2013 }}. Retrieved on 2013-04-20.</ref> White was awarded $3,000 in 2015, and an additional $200,000 in 2017.<ref>{{Cite web | url=http://thesource.com/2015/12/26/the-game-ordered-to-pay-40-glocc-3k-for-assault/ |title = The Game Ordered to Pay 40 Glocc $3K for Assault|date = December 26, 2015}}</ref>

In May 2011, White and [[The Black Wall Street Records|Black Wall Street]]'s Compton Menace got into a physical altercation during the Cage vs. Cons event at the L.A. Sports Arena in Los Angeles. The fight allegedly stemmed from G-Unit and Black Wall Street having a long lasting dispute.<ref>Langhorne, Cyrus. (June 3, 2011) [http://www.sohh.com/2011/06/40_glocc_says_dont_be_a_menace_he_wanna.html 40 Glocc Says Don't Be A Menace, "He Wanna B*tch Up Now?"]. Sohh.Com. Retrieved on 2013-04-20.</ref>

White was arrested on February 23, 2017, after law enforcement learned of him and three others' involvement in a prostitution ring. Reports claim he drove a woman to an undisclosed location so she could perform sexual acts on someone she'd met on Backpage.com. Shortly afterwards, the unidentified female was arrested by the undercover Minnesota cop who contacted them for her services, unbeknownst to White and his partner.<ref name="tmz.com">{{Cite web|url=https://www.tmz.com/2019/10/23/40-glocc-plea-deal-prostitution-case/|title=40 Glocc Cops Sweet Plea Deal in Minnesota Prostitution Case|website=TMZ|language=en|access-date=February 1, 2020}}</ref> Detectives noticed her Bluetooth was in the middle of call during the arraignment and tracked down White's vehicle some time later, finding his hands covered in blood after he attempted to destroy any evidence of his involvement. The rapper—charged with two counts of intentionally promoting the prostitution of an individual and one count of inducing an individual to practice prostitution. He later accepted a plea deal for one felony count of promoting prostitution of an individual.<ref name="tmz.com"/>

On June 15, 2017, while out on bail from his arrest. White was hospitalized after being shot twice while attending a funeral in San Bernardino, California. He was shot at 2:30 p.m local time. When the bullets were fired at the cemetery, White was hit in his arm and chest.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.xxlmag.com/news/2017/06/40-glocc-shot-twice-california-funeral/|title=40 Glocc Shot Twice at California Funeral - XXL|last=Berry|first=Peter A.|website=XXL Mag|language=en|access-date=February 1, 2020}}</ref> At around the same time, a woman in the vicinity of the shooting was run over by a car, though whether or not the incidents are connected is unclear.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.sbsun.com/general-news/20170616/rapper-40-glocc-shot-while-attending-funeral-in-san-bernardino-expected-to-survive|title=Rapper 40 Glocc shot while attending funeral in San Bernardino, expected to survive|date=June 16, 2017|website=San Bernardino Sun|language=en-US|access-date=February 1, 2020}}</ref>
