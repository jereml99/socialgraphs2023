'''Gilbert Anthony Milam Jr.''' (born October 27, 1983), better known by his stage name '''Berner''', is an American rapper and entrepreneur signed to [[Wiz Khalifa]]'s [[Taylor Gang Entertainment]]. He has released over 40 albums, several of which have charted on ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]''{{'}}s "Top R&B/Hip-Hop Albums" chart.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Chapman |first1=Wilson |title=Rapper Berner Joins Cast of Indie Thriller ‘The Low End Theory,’ Will Compose Soundtrack |url=https://variety.com/2022/film/news/berner-the-low-end-theory-weed-1235306290/ |website=[[Variety (magazine)|Variety]] |access-date=July 1, 2022 |language=en |date=June 29, 2022}}</ref> As an entrepreneur, he created the Cookies brand of [[Cannabis (drug)|cannabis]] products and clothing gear that formed out of San Francisco in the early 2010s.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Hasse |first1=Javier |title=Rapper Berner Talks Cannabis Business Ventures, Shares Advice For Entrepreneurs |url=https://finance.yahoo.com/news/rapper-berner-talks-cannabis-business-192842314.html |access-date=March 16, 2023 |work=Yahoo Finance |date=October 16, 2018}}</ref> The company has grown to include dozens of [[Cannabis dispensaries in the United States|dispensaries]] across the United States and abroad, along with multiple clothing stores.<ref name="ktvu">{{cite news |last1=Wong |first1=Claudine |title=Berner, Cookies founder, makes it big as Latino cannabis trailblazer |url=https://www.ktvu.com/news/cookies-founder-a-san-francisco-native-cannabis-trailblazer-lands-on-cover-of-forbes |access-date=March 16, 2023 |work=KTVU |date=September 14, 2022}}</ref>

== Early life ==
A [[Sunset District]] native, Berner attended [[Galileo Academy of Science and Technology]] before dropping out. His mother was an office worker and his father was a chef at a Mexican restaurant on [[Fillmore Street]].<ref name="Huffington Post interview">{{cite web|last1=Harmon|first1=Steve|title=How Do You Spell Hip Hop Entrepreneur? Berner|url=http://www.huffingtonpost.com/steve-harmon/how-do-you-spell-hip-hop-entrepreneur-berner_b_10474158.html|website=Huffington Post|date=June 17, 2016|access-date=August 12, 2016}}</ref> When Berner was 13, the family moved to Arizona, where his father planned to open a restaurant. During high school, Berner began [[battle rap]]ping and in 2006 he released his first mixtape, ''Dirty Sneakers...Plenty Ways to Get It''.<ref name="Huffington Post interview" />

== Musical career ==

After forming his own label, Bern One Entertainment in 2007, Berner  released his debut album, ''Track Money & Pack Money'', with Equipto. His first solo album, ''Weekend at Bernie's'' arrived in 2009, along with four other albums.<ref name="Allmusic rec">{{Allmusic|class=artist|id=mn0000736196|label=Berner|access-date=March 18, 2016}}</ref>

In March 2012, Berner signed to [[Wiz Khalifa]]'s [[Taylor Gang Records]]. ''Urban Farmer'', Berner's first collection to be released on Taylor Gang Records, was released on October 2, 2012. In 2013, Berner embarked performed on the "Under the Influence of Music Tour" alongside Khalifa, [[A$AP Rocky]], [[Trinidad Jame$]] and [[Joey Bada$$]]. In 2015, Berner released ''Contraband'', a collaborative album with [[Cam'ron]], as well as ''Drought Season 3'' with deceased rapper [[The Jacka]]. In 2016, Berner released the solo album ''[[Hempire]]'', also producing a 4/20 show at the [[Bill Graham Civic Auditorium]] with a lineup that included Cypress Hill, Juicy J, Dizzy Wright, J Boog, Kool John, and Berner.<ref name="Allmusic rec" /><ref name="LA Music Blog">{{cite web|last1=Quitzon|first1=Kimberly|title=Recap: Berner Presents Hippie Hill 2016|url=http://lamusicblog.com/2016/04/review/recap-berner-presents-hippie-hill-2016/|website=LA Music Blog|publisher=LA Music Blog|access-date=August 12, 2016}}</ref>

''Urban Farmer'', another of Berner's albums, features [[Iamsu!]] from [[The HBK Gang]] on two tracks; and the Jacka, [[Rydah J. Klyde]] and [[San Quinn]] on ''21 Gunz''.<ref name="SFWeekly1">{{cite web|last1=Meline|first1=Gabe|title=Berner, the Rapper King of Girl Scout Cookies, Plays Tonight|url=http://www.sfweekly.com/shookdown/2014/06/27/berner-the-rapper-king-of-girl-scout-cookies-plays-tonight|website=SFWeekly|publisher=SFWeekly|access-date=March 22, 2016}}</ref>

Berner's single, ''20 Lights'' reached number 95 on the [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]] chart. It was released on January 6, 2015. "Best Thang Smokin" is the lead single in Berner's album ''Hempire'', released April 1, 2016.<ref name="Complex">{{cite web|last1=Mench|first1=Chris|title=Berner, Snoop Dogg, and Wiz Khalifa Burn One in Their Video for "Best Thang Smokin"|url=http://www.complex.com/music/2016/03/berner-new-video-best-thang-smokin-featuring-snoop-dogg-wiz-khalifa-b-real|website=Complex|publisher=Complex|access-date=March 22, 2016}}</ref>

On May 4, 2019, he released the album ''El Chivo''.

==Cookies cannabis brand==
Berner began working in the [[cannabis industry]] at age 18 at a San Francisco [[Cannabis dispensaries in the United States|dispensary]] named The Hemp Center.<ref name="sfw">{{cite news |last1=Roberts |first1=Chris |title=Million Dollar Cookie: How Berner Built a Business Empire on Marijuana |url=https://www.sfweekly.com/archives/million-dollar-cookie-how-berner-built-a-business-empire-on-marijuana/article_f050e658-5707-5f46-80f7-1250eaa863cd.html |access-date=March 16, 2023 |work=SF Weekly |date=February 2, 2016}}</ref> In the early 2000s he befriended a cultivator named Jai Chang (a.k.a. "Jigga"), who developed a [[Cannabis strain|strain of cannabis]] that he named "Girl Scout Cookies".<ref name="forbes">{{cite news |last1=Yakowicz |first1=Will |title=Weed vs. Greed: How America Botched Legalizing Pot |url=https://www.forbes.com/sites/willyakowicz/2022/08/02/how-america-botched-legalizing-cannabis-berner-cookies-kim-rivers-trulieve-steve-deangelo/ |access-date=March 16, 2023 |work=Forbes |date=August 2, 2022 |archive-date=October 13, 2022}}</ref> Berner and Chang began selling various strains that Chang developed to dispensaries, including Girl Scout Cookies.<ref name="forbes" /> After the [[Girl Scouts of the USA]] sent a [[cease and desist]] letter, they named their brand simply "Cookies".<ref name="forbes" /> Berner was also selling the strains to friends, including rapper [[Wiz Khalifa]] who he met in 2010.<ref name="sfw" /><ref name="forbes" /> Khalifa soon began mentioning Cookies in his rap songs, while Berner wore clothing bearing a Cookies logo in music videos that he appeared in.<ref |access-date=March 16, 2023 |work=KNTV |date=May 18, 2015}}</ref> The first Cookies cannabis dispensary followed in 2018,<ref name="bi">{{cite news |last1=Gilbert |first1=Ben |title=The story of In August 2022, Berner was the first cannabis executive to be featured on the cover of [[Forbes|Forbes Magazine]] since it launched in 1917.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Eustice |first1=Kyle |title=Berner Makes History As First Cannabis CEO To Cover 'Forbes': 'The Definition Of Life Goal Shit!' |url=https://hiphopdx.com/news/id.72254/title.berner-makes-history-as-first-cannabis-ceo-to-cover-forbes-the-definition-of-life-goal-shit |access-date=March 12, 2023 |work=HipHopDX |date=August 3, 2022}}</ref> Forbes conservatively estimated the Cookies brand to be worth $150 million.<ref name="forbes" />

==Personal life==
Berner was diagnosed with stage 3 [[Colorectal cancer|colon cancer]] in 2021,<ref name="ktvu" /> but it went into remission after chemotherapy.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Kudialis |first1=Chris |title=Berner on Fire: Cookies & Dough |url=https://cannabisnow.com/berner-on-fire-cookies-dough/ |access-date=March 16, 2023 |work=Cannabis Now |date=December 24, 2022}}</ref>

He has a daughter.<ref name="ktvu" />
