'''Isaiah Philip Camacho''' (born 1981), known professionally as '''Isaiah Toothtaker''', is an American rapper, tattoo artist, and designer.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Millard |first=Drew |date=August 8, 2014 |url=https://www.vice.com/en/article/rqq77r/thank-you-based-god-there-are-now-lil-b-emojis |title=Thank You Based God, There Are Now Lil B Emojis |website=Vice |access-date=April 25, 2022 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20201112020152/https://www.vice.com/en/article/rqq77r/thank-you-based-god-there-are-now-lil-b-emojis |archive-date=November 12, 2020 |url-status=dead}}</ref> He is the co-founder of the hip hop collective Machina Muerte.<ref>{{Cite news |last=Martins |first=Chris |date=January 10, 2011 |url=http://blogs.laweekly.com/westcoastsound/2011/01/bed_intruder_isaiah_toothtaker_antoine_dodson_rap.php |title=Antoine Dodson's "Bed Intruder Song" Remixed as a Bizarre Gangsta Warning to Snitches |newspaper=LA Weekly |access-date=February 8, 2023 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20121103012846/http://blogs.laweekly.com/westcoastsound/2011/01/bed_intruder_isaiah_toothtaker_antoine_dodson_rap.php |archive-date=November 3, 2012 |url-status=dead}}</ref> 

==Biography==
Isaiah Toothtaker was born Isaiah Philip Camacho in 1981.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.toothtaker.com/ |title=Surviving Toothtaker |website=Toothtaker |access-date=February 8, 2023 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210307141732/http://www.toothtaker.com/ |archive-date=March 7, 2021 |url-status=dead}}</ref> He is of [[Mexicans|Mexican]] and [[Native Americans in the United States|Native American]] heritage.<ref name="TheBoombox">{{Cite web |last=Future |first=Kidd |date=September 6, 2013 |title=The Futurist: Isaiah Toothtaker Knuckles Up |url=https://theboombox.com/isaiah-toothtaker-interview/ |website=The Boombox |access-date=April 25, 2022 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131015091330/http://theboombox.com/isaiah-toothtaker-interview |archive-date=October 15, 2013 |url-status=dead}}</ref> He grew up in [[Tucson, Arizona]].<ref>{{Cite web |last=Muyskens |first=Luke |date=March 10, 2011 |url=http://ugsmag.com/2011/03/isaiah-toothtaker/ |title=Isaiah Toothtaker |website=UGSMAG |access-date=February 8, 2023 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130315040928/http://ugsmag.com/2011/03/isaiah-toothtaker/ |archive-date=March 15, 2013 |url-status=dead}}</ref> At the age of 11, he was kicked out of home.<ref name="TheBoombox"/> At age 15, he started rapping.<ref>{{Cite web |author=Zio |date=February 23, 2014 |url=https://thehundreds.com/blogs/content/isaiah-toothtaker-interview |title=Isaiah Toothtaker :: Deux Ex Machina Muerte |website=The Hundreds |access-date=February 8, 2023 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200930195430/https://thehundreds.com/blogs/content/isaiah-toothtaker-interview |archive-date=September 30, 2020 |url-status=dead}}</ref> He learned tattooing from the then-president of the local [[Hells Angels]].<ref>{{Cite web |last=Roos |first=Brandon E. |date=April 5, 2011 |url=https://hiphopdx.com/interviews/id.1685/title.rap-ink-hip-hops-first-national-emcee-with-a-tattoo-parlor |title=Rap, Ink: Hip Hop's First National Emcee With A Tattoo Parlor |website=HipHopDX |access-date=February 8, 2023 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170514032709/http://hiphopdx.com/interviews/id.1685/title.rap-ink-hip-hops-first-national-emcee-with-a-tattoo-parlor |archive-date=May 14, 2017 |url-status=dead}}</ref>

In 2011, he released a solo album, ''Illuminati Thug Mafia''.<ref>{{Cite web |date=February 2, 2011 |url=http://www.imposemagazine.com/bytes/new-music/best-music-of-january-2011 |title=The best music of January 2011 |website=Impose |access-date=February 8, 2023 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20151201155001/http://www.imposemagazine.com/bytes/new-music/best-music-of-january-2011 |archive-date=December 1, 2015 |url-status=dead}}</ref> In 2012, he released a [[the Hood Internet]]-produced collaborative EP with [[Max B]], titled ''Toothy Wavy'',<ref>{{Cite web |last=Woodbury |first=Jason P. |date=December 24, 2012 |url=https://www.phoenixnewtimes.com/music/10-best-things-i-heard-in-2012-6625785 |title=10 Best Things I Heard in 2012 |website=Phoenix New Times |access-date=February 8, 2023 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20181130125341/https://www.phoenixnewtimes.com/music/10-best-things-i-heard-in-2012-6625785 |archive-date=November 30, 2018 |url-status=dead}}</ref> a [[Harry Fraud]]-produced collaborative EP with Rapewolf, titled ''Rob Zombie'',<ref>{{Cite web |last=Future |first=Kidd |date=June 5, 2012 |url=https://thesource.com/2012/06/05/freeepisaiahtoothtakerrapewolf-robzombieprodbyharryfraud/ |title=Free EP: Isaiah Toothtaker & Rapewolf – Rob Zombie (Prod. By Harry Fraud) |website=The Source |access-date=February 8, 2023 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180817040609/http://thesource.com/2012/06/05/freeepisaiahtoothtakerrapewolf-robzombieprodbyharryfraud/ |archive-date=August 17, 2018 |url-status=dead}}</ref> and a [[Sixtoo]]-produced solo EP, titled ''Sea Punk Funk''.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Ollman |first=Jonah |date=July 27, 2012 |url=https://cmj.com/news/isaiah-toothtaker-ep-released-for-free/ |title=Isaiah Toothtaker EP Released For Free |website=CMJ |access-date=February 8, 2023 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120804043303/https://cmj.com/news/isaiah-toothtaker-ep-released-for-free/ |archive-date=August 4, 2012 |url-status=dead}}</ref> In 2013, he released a solo album, ''Illmatic 2''.<ref>{{Cite web |last1=Bottai |first1=Sean |last2=Levine |first2=Joshua |last3=Swedlund |first3=Eric |date=January 2, 2014 |url=https://www.tucsonweekly.com/tucson/top-releases-in-13/Content?oid=3937854 |title=Top Releases in '13 |website=Tucson Weekly |access-date=February 8, 2023 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140416025927/http://www.tucsonweekly.com/tucson/top-releases-in-13/Content?oid=3937854 |archive-date=April 16, 2014 |url-status=dead}}</ref>

In 2014, he published a book of emoji art, titled ''That's Not Relevant''.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Messina |first=Irene |date=August 7, 2014 |url=http://www.tucsonweekly.com/tucson/thats-not-relevant/Content?oid=4798341 |title=That's Not Relevant |website=Tucson Weekly |access-date=February 8, 2023 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150907022252/http://www.tucsonweekly.com/tucson/thats-not-relevant/Content?oid=4798341 |archive-date=September 7, 2015 |url-status=dead}}</ref>

In 2020, ''[[Tucson Weekly]]'' took down their 2013 interview with him in response to behavioral allegations.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Nintzel |first=Jim |date=July 3, 2020 |title=Tucson Weekly Removes Isaiah Toothtaker Profile from Website |url=https://www.tucsonweekly.com/TheRange/archives/2020/07/03/tucson-weekly-removes-isaiah-toothtaker-profile-from-website |website=Tucson Weekly |access-date=July 4, 2020}}</ref> ''[[The New York Times]]'' investigated the charges and claims of sexual assault against him in a 2022 article.<ref>{{Cite news |last=Watkins |first=Ali |date=May 4, 2022 |title='A Monster in Our Midst': How a Tattoo Industry #MeToo Case Collapsed |url=https://www.nytimes.com/2022/05/04/nyregion/toothtaker-isaiah-camacho-sexual-assault.html |work=The New York Times |access-date=May 19, 2022}}</ref>
