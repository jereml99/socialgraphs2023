'''Kyle Thomas Harvey''' (born May 18, 1993<ref>{{Cite web|title=Kyle Thomas Harvey, Born 05/18/1993 in California {{!}} CaliforniaBirthIndex.org|url=https://www.californiabirthindex.org/birth/kyle_thomas_harvey_born_1993_22201579|access-date=July 27, 2021|website=www.californiabirthindex.org}}</ref>), known [[Mononym|mononymously]] as '''Kyle''' and '''SuperDuperKyle''' (formerly known as '''K.i.D''', an acronym for '''Kyle is Determined'''), is an American rapper, singer, songwriter, and actor from [[Ventura, California]]. He is best known for his 2016 breakout single "[[iSpy (Kyle song)|iSpy]]" (featuring [[Lil Yachty]]), which peaked at number 4 on the [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]]. Signed to Indie-Pop and [[Atlantic Records]],<ref name="Atlantic">{{cite web|url=http://www.fashionably-early.com/2017/02/07/kyle-signs-to-atlantic-records/|title=KYLE Signs to Atlantic Records – Fashionably-Early|date=February 7, 2017|website=fashionably-early.com|access-date=June 2, 2017}}</ref> Kyle is also known for his association with the SuperDuperCrew which consists of Brick, Jesus, and Maxx.

Kyle has collaborated with artists such as Waywo, [[Kehlani]], Lil Yachty, [[G-Eazy]], [[Chance the Rapper]], [[Miguel (singer)|Miguel]], [[MadeinTYO]], [[Yuna (singer)|Yuna]], and [[Ty Dolla Sign]], among others. Some of his songs in addition to "iSpy" include "Keep It Real", "Doubt It", "King Wavy" (featuring G-Eazy), and his 2018 hit single "[[Playinwitme]]" (featuring Kehlani).<ref>{{cite web|url=http://artisticmanifesto.com/2016/12/02/kyle-links-lil-yachty-ispy/|title="ISpy" – KYLE ft. Lil Yachty|publisher=Artistic Manifesto|access-date=January 12, 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170503075549/http://artisticmanifesto.com/2016/12/02/kyle-links-lil-yachty-ispy/|archive-date=May 3, 2017|url-status=dead}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/rap-music/new-music/2016/12/kyle-and-lil-yachty-ignore-the-haters-in-ispy/|title=Kyle and Lil Yachty Ignore the Haters in "iSpy"|work=[[XXL (magazine)|XXL]]|date=December 2, 2016 |access-date=January 10, 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://hypebeast.com/2016/12/kyle-lil-yachty-ispy|title=Kyle & Lil Yachty – "iSpy"|date=December 2, 2016 |publisher=HYPEBEAST|access-date=January 10, 2017}}</ref> In 2015, ''[[Rolling Stone]]'' named him as "one of the ten artists you need to know".<ref>{{Cite magazine|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/lists/10-new-artists-you-need-to-know-may-2015-20150526/kyle-20150522|title=10 New Artists You Need to Know Now|magazine=Rolling Stone|access-date=February 16, 2017}}</ref>

His commercial mixtapes ''Beautiful Loser'' and ''[[Smyle (Kyle album)|Smyle]]'' were released in 2013 and 2015, respectively.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://music.apple.com/us/album/beautiful-loser/674638528|title=Get millions of songs. All ad-free.|website=Apple Music|access-date=June 7, 2019|archive-date=October 21, 2021|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20211021225817/https://music.apple.com/us/album/beautiful-loser/674638528|url-status=dead}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://music.apple.com/us/album/smyle-deluxe/1034020656|title=Get millions of songs. All ad-free.|website=Apple Music|access-date=June 7, 2019|archive-date=November 8, 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20201108100035/https://music.apple.com/us/album/smyle-deluxe/1034020656|url-status=dead}}</ref> Kyle's debut studio album ''[[Light of Mine]]'' was released in 2018 to positive reviews and reached within the top-30 of the [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]].

Kyle made his acting debut in the 2018 [[Netflix]] original film ''[[The After Party (film)|The After Party]]''. He also released a single from the film "Moment" (featuring [[Wiz Khalifa]]).<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://music.apple.com/us/album/moment-feat-wiz-khalifa-single/1433803763|title=Moment (feat. Wiz Khalifa) – Single by KYLE|website=Apple Music}}</ref> His second album ''[[See You When I Am Famous]]'' was released on July 17, 2020.

== Early life ==
Harvey was born in the [[Reseda, Los Angeles|Reseda]] neighborhood of [[Los Angeles]], [[California]], to a single mother, in a home of eight family members and two siblings.<ref name="RS">{{Cite magazine|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/features/meet-kyle-ispy-mc-wants-to-be-raps-john-lennon-w478375|title=Meet Kyle, the "iSpy" MC Who Wants to Be Rap's John Lennon|magazine=Rolling Stone|access-date=October 1, 2017}}</ref> He grew up one hour away in [[Ventura, California|Ventura]]. He stated that he has a [[Speech disorder|speech impediment]]<ref>{{Citation|title=Kyle, A Boogie Wit Da Hoodie and Aminé's 2017 XXL Freshman Cypher|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GQEtvtPGL9k|language=en|access-date=July 27, 2021}}</ref> and that during his youth he was bullied because of it.<ref name="RS" /> Harvey attended [[Ventura High School]]<ref name="Vice" /> and regularly took part in drama classes which helped his confidence largely.<ref name="RS" />

He started singing at the age of 6 and was writing and performing his own songs by elementary school. Harvey started rapping when he was about 13 years old<ref name="Vice">{{cite web | url = https://noisey.vice.com/en_ca/article/kyle-interview-california-dont-wanna-fall-in-love-2014-57a2079d45c9addffd2a11fb | title = Kyle Is Rapping and Playing It Safe | work = vice.com | date = December 3, 2014 | access-date = February 2, 2017}}</ref> and began recording music after hearing [[Kid Cudi]]'s ''[[Man on the Moon: The End of Day]]''. Harvey's first songs were recorded on [[PreSonus]]' [[Studio One (software)|Studio One]] recording software using his aunt's computer while in high school.<ref name="RS" />

== Career ==
=== 2009–2012: Early mixtapes ===
Kyle started releasing [[mixtape]]s such as ''Senior Year'', ''Second Semester'', ''FxL'', ''Super Duper'', and ''K.i.D'' under the monikers KiddKash and K.i.D starting in 2009 to 2012.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://theekid.bandcamp.com/|title=K.i.D|website=K.i.D|access-date=June 2, 2017}}</ref>

=== 2013–2015: ''Beautiful Loser'' and ''Smyle'' ===
In 2013, he released his commercial debut [[mixtape]] ''Beautiful Loser''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.complex.com/music/2017/01/bout-to-blow-january-2017-edition/kyle-lil-yachty-ispy|title=Bout to Blow: 10 Dope New Songs You Should Be Hearing Everywhere Soon|publisher=[[Complex (magazine)|Complex]]|date=January 3, 2017|access-date=January 10, 2017}}</ref>

In May 2014, he was featured in [[G-Eazy]]'s music video for "[[I Mean It]]" as a technical director on the fictional news set.<ref>{{Citation|last=GEazyMusicVEVO|title=G-Eazy – I Mean It (Official Music Video) ft. Remo|date=May 28, 2014|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=CxnaPa8ohmM|access-date=September 12, 2018}}</ref>

In October 2015, he released his follow up commercial mixtape ''[[Smyle]]''. The album peaked on the [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]] at number 76. The album featured a guest spot by [[Chance the Rapper]] on the track "Remember Me?" and has garnered over 2 million views on [[YouTube]].

Kyle's first music video to reach one million views was "Keep It Real", which was accomplished later that year.

=== 2016–present: Breakthrough, ''Light of Mine'' and ''See You When I Am Famous'' ===
In December 2016, he released the single "[[iSpy (Kyle song)|iSpy]]", featuring [[Lil Yachty]]. The song gained widespread recognition for Kyle. The song peaked at number 4 on the US [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]] and number 14 on the [[Canadian Hot 100]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/artist/6723308/kyle/chart?f=793|title=Kyle – Chart history" Canadian Hot 100 for Kyle|publisher=Billboard|access-date=May 13, 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/artist/6723308/kyle/chart?f=379|title=Kyle – Chart history" Billboard Hot 100 for Kyle|publisher=Billboard|access-date=January 31, 2017}}</ref>

In February 2017, Kyle signed a deal with [[Atlantic Records]].<ref name="Atlantic" />

In June 2017, Kyle was named as one of the ten 2017 ''[[XXL (magazine)|XXL]]'' Freshmen along with [[A Boogie wit da Hoodie]], [[PnB Rock]], [[Playboi Carti]], [[Ugly God]], [[Aminé]], [[MadeinTYO]], [[Kamaiyah]], [[Kap G]], and [[XXXTentacion]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2017/06/2017-xxl-freshman-cover/|title=XXL Freshman 2017 Cover Revealed|publisher=XXL|date=June 13, 2017|access-date=June 17, 2017}}</ref>

In March 2018, alongside the release of his single "[[Playinwitme]]" featuring [[Kehlani]], Kyle announced that [[Khalid (singer)|Khalid]] would be featured on a track titled "iMissMe". Kyle's debut studio album ''[[Light of Mine]]'' was released on May 18, 2018. The album debuted at number 29 on the [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]].<ref>{{cite news|title=New Music: KYLE feat. Kehlani – 'Playinwitme'|url=http://www.rap-up.com/2018/03/20/new-music-kyle-kehlani-playinwitme/|work=Rap-Up|date=March 20, 2018|access-date=March 21, 2018}}</ref>

He appeared as the main character in the [[Netflix]] original film, ''[[The After Party (film)|The After Party]]'', which debuted on August 24, 2018.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://variety.com/2018/film/news/netflix-the-after-party-trailer-kyle-1202896379/|title=Rapper Kyle Vies for Stardom in Netflix's 'The After Party' Trailer|first1=Tara|last1=Bitran|date=August 6, 2018}}</ref>

His second album ''[[See You When I Am Famous]]'' was released on July 17, 2020, and was supported by the singles "Yes!", "What It Is" and "Bouncin".

On January 28, 2022, Kyle released the album ''It's Not So Bad'', his fifth project and first as a newly independent artist.<ref>{{Cite magazine|last=Lee|first=Cydney|title=Kyle Wants You to Know That 'It's Not So Bad' With New Independent Project|url=https://www.billboard.com/music/rb-hip-hop/kyle-its-not-so-bad-interview-1235024613/|date=January 28, 2022|access-date=January 28, 2022|magazine=Billboard}}</ref>
