'''Cymphonique Miller''' (born August 1, 1996),<ref name=bet /> known professionally by her [[mononym]] '''Cymphonique''', is an American singer and actress. She is the daughter of [[Master P|Percy Robert Miller]] (also known as Master P) and is the younger sister of [[Romeo Miller]] (a.k.a. Romeo). She is known in the acting world for her leading role as Kacey Simon on the [[Nickelodeon]] sitcom ''[[How to Rock]]'', as well as her voice role as Krystal on Nickelodeon's ''[[Winx Club]]''.

==Career==

===Early career and guest appearances===
Miller has performed on several national tours and in theme parks across the United States. She has also toured with [[Raven-Symoné]], [[Ashley Tisdale]], [[Demi Lovato]], [[JoJo (singer)|JoJo]], [[Wonder Girls]],  and [[College Boyys]].

She was a finalist in [[Radio Disney]]'s ''Next Big Thing'' but came in second place to [[Jasmine Sagginario|Jasmine]]. Her songs "Butterflies", "Lil Miss Swagger" and "Daddy I'm A Rockstar" received airplay on [[Radio Disney]]. The music video for "Lil Miss Swagger," released when she was 12 years old, received more than 11 million YouTube views.  Miller has guest-starred on ''[[Big Time Rush]]'', ''[[True Jackson, VP]]'', ''[[The Troop]]'' and ''[[Just Jordan]]''.<ref name="bet"/>

===Music and acting careers (2011–present)===
[[File:CYMPH - DOPE Magazine Cover PRINCESS - Fall 2k12 AMBITION.jpg|thumb|Miller on a cover of [[Pure DOPE]] magazine  in 2012]]
In 2012, Miller starred the lead and contributed to the music for the [[Nickelodeon]] series ''[[How to Rock]]'' along with [[Lulu Antariksa]].<ref name="bet">{{cite web|url=http://www.bet.com/news/celebrities/2011/05/24/cymphonique-miller-scores-new-nickelodeon-series.html|title=Cymphonique Miller Scores New Nickelodeon Series|publisher=[[Black Entertainment Television|BET]]|date=May 23, 2011|access-date=June 20, 2011|author=Arceneaux, Michael}}</ref><ref name="deadline">{{cite news|url=https://www.deadline.com/2011/05/nickelodeon-picks-up-how-to-rock-series-starring-cymphonique-with-20-ep-order|title=Nickelodeon Picks Up 'How To Rock' Series Starring Cymphonique With 20-Ep. Order|work=[[Deadline Hollywood]]|date=May 23, 2011|access-date=June 20, 2011|author=Andreeva, Nellie}}</ref>

The show lasted one season. She sings the theme song of Nickelodeon's ''[[Winx Club]]'' [[List of Winx Club episodes#Revived series 2|revival series]], which premiered on Nickelodeon in June 2011. She was also the voice of Princess Krystal on the series.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://tvbythenumbers.zap2it.com/2011/06/09/global-hit-animated-series-winx-club-comes-to-nickelodeon-starting-june-27/95196|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110611073320/http://tvbythenumbers.zap2it.com/2011/06/09/global-hit-animated-series-winx-club-comes-to-nickelodeon-starting-june-27/95196/|url-status=dead|archive-date=June 11, 2011|title=Global Hit Animated Series 'Winx Club' Comes To Nickelodeon, Starting June 27 |work=TV by the Numbers|date=June 9, 2011}}</ref> She is featured on the [[Big Time Rush (band)|Big Time Rush]] song, "I Know You Know".{{citation needed|date=August 2017}}

In 2011, Miller was nominated for "[[BET Award for Best Hip-Hop Artists|Best Female Hip-Hop Artist]]" at the [[BET Awards]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://cymphonique.com/763/cymphonique-nominated-for-best-hip-hop-artist-at-the-2011-bet-awards|title=Cymphonique nominated for "Best Female Hip-Hop Artist" at the 2011 BET Awards|website=Cymphonique.com|date=May 18, 2011|access-date=July 4, 2011}}</ref>

On July 7, 2012, Miller performed the halftime show at the [[Los Angeles Sparks]]' [[Girl Scouts of the USA|Girl Scouts]] night at [[The Staples Center]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.wnba.com/sparks/promotional_calendar.html|title=Los Angeles Sparks promotional calendar|website=WNBA.com|date=July 7, 2012|access-date=July 8, 2012|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120622235350/http://www.wnba.com/sparks/promotional_calendar.html|archive-date=June 22, 2012}}</ref>

Miller appeared on ''[[Mad TV]]'', in numerous national commercials, and in [[public service announcement]]s.<ref name=OfficalSite>{{cite web |last=Miller |first=Cymphonique |title=Official Site - Cymphonique Miller - Me |url=http://cymphonique.com/me |website=Cymphonique.com |access-date=September 23, 2012 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180407182658/http://cymphonique.com/me |archive-date=April 7, 2018 |url-status=dead }}</ref> In 2013, she released a mixtape entitled ''Passion''.<ref>{{cite web|author=Bennett, Rachel|url=http://www.j-14.com/2012/07/j-14-exclusive-interview-cymphonique-talks-how-to-rock-and-summer-tour.html|title=Exclusive Interview: Cymphonique Talks 'How to Rock' and Summer Tour|work=J-14|date=July 17, 2012|access-date=April 19, 2014}}</ref><ref>{{YouTube|odR7eQi1dvA}}</ref>

The same year, she appeared in the film ''The Demsey Sisters'' along with [[Lynn Whitfield]] and Denyce Lawton.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://blogs.indiewire.com/shadowandact/trailer-the-dempsey-sisters-stars-cymphonique-miller-denyce-lawton-lynn-whitfield-more|title=Trailer: 'The Dempsey Sisters' Stars Cymphonique Miller, Denyce Lawton, Lynn Whitfield|website=blogs.indiewire.com|access-date=August 15, 2017}}</ref>
