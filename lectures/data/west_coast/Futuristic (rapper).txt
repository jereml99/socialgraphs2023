'''Zachary Lewis Beck''' (born June 2, 1991), known professionally as '''Futuristic''', is an American rapper, singer, and songwriter.<ref name="LAHWF">{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gdWHR9iTpSU|title=Chatting with Futuristic|work=LAHWF|publisher=YouTube|date=May 31, 2017|access-date=March 15, 2019}}</ref> He was born in [[Bloomington, Illinois]] and currently resides in [[Tempe, Arizona]].<ref>{{cite web|last1=Payne|first1=Ogden|url=https://www.forbes.com/sites/ogdenpayne/2016/02/01/rapper-futuristic-finds-success-with-a-d-i-y-approach-to-the-music-business/#15d2d791414c|title=Rapper Futuristic Finds Success With A D.I.Y. Approach To The Music Business|work=[[Forbes]]|date=February 1, 2016|access-date=January 4, 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/rap-music/the-break/2013/05/the-break-presents-futuristic/|title=The Break Presents: Futuristic|work=[[XXL (magazine)|XXL]]|date=May 31, 2013|access-date=January 4, 2020}}</ref> Futuristic gained recognition after the release of his album, ''[[The Rise (Futuristic album)|The Rise]]'', along with his single "The Greatest" in 2015.<ref name="Rise">{{cite web|url=https://music.apple.com/us/album/the-rise/981624271|title=The Rise by Futuristic|work=Apple Music|date=May 12, 2015|access-date=January 4, 2020}}</ref>

==Early life==
Zachary Lewis Beck was born in [[Bloomington, Illinois]] on June 2, 1991.<ref name="LAHWF"/> Being one of eight kids, Beck started rapping when he was in the first grade with his brothers in a talent show. When he was in fifth grade, his parents separated and he moved with his mom to [[Danville, Illinois]]. Later, he moved to [[Tempe, Arizona]] and went to [[McClintock High School]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.axs.com/news/futuristic-interviews-on-traveling-local-and-his-grind-to-the-top-13512|title=Futuristic interviews on 'Traveling Local' and his grind to the top|work=[[AXS TV]]|date=July 1, 2014|access-date=January 4, 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20161026114335/http://www.axs.com/news/futuristic-interviews-on-traveling-local-and-his-grind-to-the-top-13512|archive-date=October 26, 2016}}</ref> Beck struggled through depression when his grandmother died, and he used music in order to develop himself.

==Career==
=== 2010–2014: Career beginnings ===
In September 2010, Futuristic released his first music video which was a remix to [[J. Cole|J. Cole's]] song, "[[Who Dat (J. Cole song)|Who Dat]]".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=RSW_nVmcQIA|title=Who Dat (Official Music Video) Futuristic -Rebel t2i-|work=owensmhs1990|date=September 19, 2010|access-date=January 4, 2020}}</ref> In February 2012, Futuristic released his debut studio album, ''Dream Big'', which was followed up in February 2013, with his second studio album, ''Chasing Down a Dream''.<ref name="DB">{{cite web|url=https://onlyfuturistic.bandcamp.com/album/dream-big|title=Dream Big - Futuristic - Bandcamp|access-date=January 4, 2020}}</ref><ref name="CDAD">{{cite web|url=https://music.apple.com/us/album/chasing-down-a-dream/602875504|title=Chasing Down a Dream by Futuristic|work=Apple Music|date=February 19, 2013|access-date=January 4, 2020}}</ref> The album was supported by the single: "Don't Mind If I Do" featuring vocals from [[Dizzy Wright]] and [[Jarren Benton]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://music.apple.com/us/album/dont-mind-if-i-do-feat-dizzy-wright-jarren-benton-single/563963178|title=Don't Mind If I Do (feat. Dizzy Wright & Jarren Benton) - Single by FUTURISTIC|work=Apple Music|date=September 19, 2012|access-date=January 4, 2020}}</ref> He then followed that up with his debut extended play, ''T.G.I.F'', in September 2013.<ref name="TGIF">{{cite web|url=https://music.apple.com/us/album/t-g-i-f/696942233|title=T.G.I.F. by Futuristic|work=Apple Music|date=September 6, 2013|access-date=January 4, 2020}}</ref>

Futuristic later went on and released his third studio album, ''Traveling Local'' on June 9, 2014.<ref name="TL">{{cite web|url=https://music.apple.com/us/album/traveling-local/934550154|title=Traveling Local by Futuristic|work=Apple Music|date=June 9, 2014|access-date=January 4, 2020}}</ref> The album was supported by four singles: "I Guess I'll Smoke" featuring vocals from Dizzy Wright and [[Layzie Bone]], "Dirty Chucks", "Plan A" featuring Jarren Benton and [[Chris Webby]] and "In the Zone" featuring Collins, J Rob the Chief and Devvon Terrell.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://music.apple.com/us/album/i-guess-ill-smoke-feat-dizzy-wright-layzie-bone-single/815282106|title=I Guess I'll Smoke (feat. Dizzy Wright & Layzie Bone) - Single by FUTURISTIC|work=Apple Music|date=February 3, 2014|access-date=January 4, 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://music.apple.com/us/album/dirty-chucks-single/852332342|title=Dirty Chucks - Single by FUTURISTIC|work=Apple Music|date=March 27, 2014|access-date=January 4, 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://music.apple.com/us/album/plan-a-single/912442639|title=Plan A (feat. Jarren Benton & Chris Webby) - Single by FUTURISTIC|work=Apple Music|date=June 6, 2014|access-date=January 4, 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://music.apple.com/us/album/in-zone-feat-collins-j-rob-chief-devvon-terrell-single/919790842|title=In the Zone (feat. Collins, J Rob the Chief & Devvon Terrell) - Single by FUTURISTIC|work=Apple Music|date=September 9, 2014|access-date=January 4, 2020}}</ref> The single, "No Way", was released on September 20, 2014, and was released as the lead single to his fourth studio album.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://music.apple.com/us/album/no-way-single/922523439|title=No Way - Single by FUTURISTIC|work=Apple Music|date=September 20, 2014|access-date=January 4, 2020}}</ref>

=== 2015–2016: ''The Rise'', ''Coast 2 Coast'', and ''As Seen on the Internet'' ===
On March 1, 2015, Futuristic announced his fourth studio album, ''[[The Rise (Futuristic album)|The Rise]]'', on his website with the released date of May 12.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.onlyfuturistic.com/blog/therise|title=New Album "The Rise" Coming May 12|work=OnlyFuturistic.com|date=March 1, 2015|access-date=January 4, 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150510163300/http://www.onlyfuturistic.com/blog/therise|archive-date=May 10, 2015}}</ref> Futuristic released his second single, "The Greatest", on March 14.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://music.apple.com/us/album/the-greatest-single/974134543|title=The Greatest - Single by FUTURISTIC|work=Apple Music|date=March 14, 2015|access-date=January 4, 2020}}</ref> The music video was released four days later and shows Futuristic dressed as a [[nerd]] rapping for people in his old school, McClintock High School.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=py-6nu_douk|title=Futuristic - The Greatest (Official Music Video)|publisher=TheBuffNerds|date=March 18, 2015|access-date=January 4, 2020}}</ref> The song and music video gained recognition after it was [[Cross-promotion|cross-promoted]] by another video uploaded by BigDawsTv on the same day tilted "Nerd Raps Fast in [[Compton, California|Compton!]]".<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3RFbxKqgMGw|title=NERD RAPS FAST IN COMPTON!!|publisher=BigDawsTv|date=March 18, 2015|access-date=January 4, 2020}}</ref> The album was released on May 12, 2015, and debuted at number 139 on the US [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]] chart.<ref name="BB200">{{cite magazine|url=https://www.billboard.com/artist/futuristic/chart-history/tlp/|title=Futuristic - Chart History: Billboard 200|magazine=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|access-date=January 4, 2020}}</ref> The album also debuted on the [[Top R&B/Hip-Hop Albums]] and [[Rap Albums]] charts at number 13 and 10 respectively.<ref name="R&BRap">{{cite magazine|url=https://www.billboard.com/artist/futuristic/chart-history/blp/|title=Futuristic - Chart History: Top R&B/Hip-Hop Albums|magazine=Billboard|access-date=January 4, 2020}}</ref><ref name="RapAlbums">{{cite magazine|url=https://www.billboard.com/artist/futuristic/chart-history/rlp/|title=Futuristic - Chart History: Top Rap Albums|magazine=Billboard|access-date=January 4, 2020}}</ref>

Futuristic would later contribute vocals to the song, "[[Hold Each Other]]", with [[A Great Big World]] in July 2015 with the music video being released in September.<ref>{{Cite web|last1=Hicks|first1=Gregory|url=http://www.mtv.com/news/2272758/a-great-big-world-hold-hold-each-other/|title=A Great Big World's 'Hold Each Other' Video Douses The World With Color|website=[[MTV News]]|date=September 16, 2015|access-date=January 4, 2020}}</ref> The song would later chart on the US [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]] at number 99, becoming his first Hot 100 entry.<ref>{{cite magazine|url=https://www.billboard.com/artist/futuristic/chart-history/hsi/|title=Futuristic – Chart History: Billboard Hot 100|magazine=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|access-date=January 4, 2020}}</ref> The song would also chart on the [[Canadian Hot 100|''Canadian'' Hot 100]] at number 69.<ref>{{cite magazine|url=https://www.billboard.com/artist/futuristic/chart-history/can/|title=Futuristic – Chart History: Canadian Hot 100|magazine=Billboard|access-date=January 4, 2020}}</ref>

On December 13, 2015, Futuristic released a remix to the song, "[[Blasé (song)|Blasé]]", with New York singer and rapper [[Devvon Terrell]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vGwnvKItaac|title=DEVVON TERRELL & FUTURISTIC - BLASÉ (remix)|work=DevvonTerrell|access-date=January 4, 2020}}</ref> They later announced that they would be releasing a collaborative album titled, "Coast 2 Coast", and leading up to the release of the album they would release various remixes to popular songs and dubbed them as "12 Days of Christmas" including "Bodied", a remix of Omaha rapper Cameron Brown's "Give Me the Crown" .<ref>{{cite web|url=https://music.apple.com/us/album/coast-2-coast/1065212224|title=Coast 2 Coast by Futuristic & Devvon Terrell|work=Apple Music|date=December 25, 2015|access-date=January 4, 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://on.soundcloud.com/jRb2S}}</ref> The album was released on December 25, 2015, and debuted at number 82 on the US ''Billboard'' 200 chart.<ref name="BB200"/>

On August 26, 2016, Futuristic released his fifth studio album titled, ''[[As Seen on the Internet]]''.<ref name="ASOTI">{{cite web|url=https://music.apple.com/us/album/as-seen-on-the-internet/1140184779|title=As Seen on the Internet by Futuristic|work=Apple Music|date=August 26, 2016|access-date=January 4, 2020}}</ref> The album features [[guest appearance]]s from [[Hopsin]], Devvon Terrell, Karmin and Goody Grace and went on and charted at number 116 on the US ''Billboard'' 200 chart.<ref name="BB200"/> After the release of the album, Futuristic released eleven music videos all directed by Jakob Owens to promote the album.<ref name="Sway">{{Cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=uKh8ba6sK_c|title=Futuristic Talks "As Seen On The Internet" and Using Influencers to Push Content|publisher=Sway's Universe|date=August 22, 2016|access-date=January 4, 2020}}</ref>

=== 2017–2019: ''Blessings'', ''I Am...'', and ''Zachary Lewis'' ===
[[File:FuturisticNov2018.jpg|thumb|upright|right|Futuristic performing at the Independent Grind Tour in November 2018]]
In 2017, Futuristic later went on and released various singles from February through November of that year.<ref name="Wave">{{cite web|url=https://music.apple.com/us/album/wave/1203252027|title=Wave - Single by FUTURISTIC|website=Apple Music|date=February 14, 2017|access-date=January 4, 2020}}</ref><ref name="FTIO">{{cite web|url=https://music.apple.com/us/album/forget-that-its-over/1302172990|title=Forget That's It Over - Single by FUTURISTIC|website=Apple Music|date=November 6, 2017|access-date=January 4, 2020}}</ref> On November 24, 2017, Futuristic released his second extended play titled, ''What More Could You Ask for?''.<ref name="WMCYAF">{{cite web|url=https://music.apple.com/us/album/what-more-could-you-ask-for/1308338516|title=What More Could You Ask for? by FUTURISTIC|work=Apple Music|date=November 24, 2017|access-date=January 4, 2020}}</ref> On December 25, 2017, Futuristic released his sixth studio album titled, ''[[Blessings (Futuristic album)|Blessings]]''.<ref name="Blessings">{{cite web|url=https://music.apple.com/us/album/blessings/1323310470|title=Blessings by FUTURISTIC|work=Apple Music|date=December 25, 2017|access-date=January 4, 2020}}</ref> The album was supported by three singles: "Epiphany" featuring vocals from [[NF (rapper)|NF]], "Get It Together" and "Talk" featuring vocals from [[Tech N9ne]] and Devvon Terrell.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://music.apple.com/us/album/epiphany-feat-nf/1215602706|title=Epiphany (feat. NF) - Single by FUTURISTIC|website=Apple Music|date=March 24, 2017|access-date=January 4, 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://music.apple.com/us/album/get-it-together-single/1293354596|title=Get It Together - Single by FUTURISTIC|website=Apple Music|date=October 15, 2017|access-date=January 4, 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://music.apple.com/us/album/talk-feat-tech-n9ne-devvon-terrell-single/1323331716|title=Talk (feat. Tech N9ne & Devvon Terrell) - Single by FUTURISTIC|website=Apple Music|date=December 18, 2017|access-date=January 4, 2020}}</ref>

On February 14, 2018, Futuristic released his third extended play titled, ''Songs About Girls'', which included the singles "Wave" and "Forget That's It Over".<ref name="SAG">{{cite web|url=https://music.apple.com/us/album/songs-about-girls/1345030941|title=Songs About Girls by FUTURISTIC|work=Apple Music|date=February 14, 2018|access-date=January 4, 2020}}</ref><ref name="Wave"/><ref name="FTIO"/> He later went on to release various singles throughout the year including: "Nobody Else", "My Own Zone" with Tech N9ne and Dizzy Wright and many more.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/nobody-else-single/1381595058|title=Nobody Else - Single by FUTURISTIC|website=Apple Music|date=May 18, 2018|access-date=January 4, 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/my-own-zone-feat-futuristic-dizzy-wright-single/1437255528|title=My Own Zone (feat. FUTURISTIC & Dizzy Wright) - Single by Tech N9ne|work=Apple Music|date=September 28, 2018|access-date=January 4, 2020}}</ref>

On July 12, 2019, Futuristic released his seventh studio album titled, ''I Am''.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://music.apple.com/us/album/i-am/1466626234|title=I Am... by FUTURISTIC|work=Apple Music|date=July 12, 2019|access-date=January 4, 2020}}</ref> The album was supported by four singles: "That's That", "Top 10" featuring gianni & kyle, "48 Hours" and "Up".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://music.apple.com/us/album/thats-that-single/1446611162|title=That's That - Single by FUTURISTIC|website=Apple Music|date=January 1, 2019|access-date=January 4, 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://music.apple.com/us/album/top-ten-single/1463431596|title=Top 10 - Single by FUTURISTIC & gianni & kyle on Apple Music|website=iTunes|date=June 1, 2019|access-date=January 4, 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://open.spotify.com/album/2WUA13ys9J42uaj9eJdzir|title=Futuristic - 48 Hours|work=Spotify|access-date=January 17, 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://open.spotify.com/album/0bcqekcvoElcPPH90Jbw4o|title=Futuristic - Up|work=Spotify|access-date=January 17, 2020}}</ref> Futuristic later released his eighth studio album, ''Zachary Lewis'', on September 1, which included the single "Main Homie".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://music.apple.com/us/album/zachary-lewis/1477124044|title=Zachary Lewis by FUTURISTIC|work=Apple Music|date=September 1, 2019|access-date=January 4, 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://music.apple.com/us/album/main-homie-single/1448648529|title=Main Homie - Single by FUTURISTIC|website=Apple Music|date=January 15, 2019|access-date=January 4, 2020}}</ref> Following the release of ''Zachary Lewis'', He later went on and release music videos for every song on the album.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=OLAK5uy_kegBowVgrof5KTVd3GCnZIYplq4yIsAxQ|title=Zachary Lewis|publisher=onlyfuturistic|access-date=January 4, 2020}}</ref>

=== 2020–present: ''Still on the Rise'' and ''Featuristic'' ===
On January 1, 2020, Futuristic announced on his social media accounts that the single, "Sorry for Y'all", would be released on January 24.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://twitter.com/OnlyFuturistic/status/1212418421549174784|title=Futuristic™ on Twitter: "Coming 1/24"|publisher=Twitter|date=January 1, 2020|access-date=January 4, 2020}}</ref> Futuristic's ninth studio album titled, ''Still on the Rise'', was released on May 12, 2020, which is a follow-up to his 2015 album ''The Rise''.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://music.apple.com/us/album/still-on-the-rise/1506331238|title=Still on the Rise by FUTURISTIC|work=Apple Music|date=May 12, 2020|access-date=April 15, 2020}}</ref> The album was supported by seven singles: "Sorry for Y'all", "Wishawoods", "Motion", "Champion", "Pull Up" featuring Kap G, "Like This" with [[Rittz]] and "OG Now" with [[Chris Webby]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://music.apple.com/us/album/wishawoods-single/1497999818|title=Wishawoods - Single by FUTURISTIC|work=Apple Music|date=February 28, 2020|access-date=February 28, 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://music.apple.com/us/album/motion-single/1500355859|title=Motion - Single by FUTURISTIC|work=Apple Music|date=March 6, 2020|access-date=March 6, 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://music.apple.com/us/album/champion-single/1501882405|title=Champion - Single by FUTURISTIC|work=Apple Music|date=March 13, 2020|access-date=March 13, 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://music.apple.com/us/album/pull-up-feat-kap-g-single/1505223385|title=Pull Up (feat. Kap G) - Single by FUTURISTIC|work=Apple Music|access-date=April 24, 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://music.apple.com/us/album/like-this-single/1506316043|title=Like This - Single by FUTURISTIC & Rittz|work=Apple Music|access-date=April 24, 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://music.apple.com/us/album/og-now-single/1506319406|title=OG Now - Single by FUTURISTIC & Chris Webby|work=Apple Music|access-date=April 24, 2020}}</ref>

On September 24, 2020, Futuristic released his tenth studio album titled, ''Featuristic''.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://music.apple.com/us/album/featuristic/1529981747|title=FEATURISTIC by FUTURISTIC|work=Apple Music|date=September 23, 2020|access-date=September 24, 2020}}</ref> The album was supported by six singles: "Cool AF", with Jzac, "I.O.F.W.R.", with [[Justina Valentine]] and [[Mark Battles]], "Skid Row", with Ekoh and Jarren Benton, "Now or Never", with Crypt, "Michael Jordan", with [[GAWNE]] and "Come Home", with [[24hrs (rapper)|24hrs]] and [[YBN Almighty Jay]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://music.apple.com/us/album/cool-af-single/1517451244|title=Cool AF - Single by FUTURISTIC, Dropout Records & JZAC|work=Apple Music|access-date=September 24, 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://music.apple.com/us/album/i-o-f-w-r-single/1522176339|title=I.O.F.W.R. - Single by FUTURISTIC, Justina Valentine & Mark Battles|work=Apple Music|access-date=September 24, 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://music.apple.com/us/album/skid-row-single/1524519648|title=Skid Row - Single by FUTURISTIC, Ekoh & Jarren Benton|work=Apple Music|access-date=September 24, 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://music.apple.com/us/album/now-or-never-single/1526174480|title=Now or Never - Single by FUTURISTIC, Crypt & Dropout Records|work=Apple Music|access-date=September 24, 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://music.apple.com/us/album/michael-jordan-single/1526925768|title=Michael Jordan - Single by Gawne, Luke Gawne & FUTURISTIC|work=Apple Music|access-date=September 24, 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://music.apple.com/us/album/come-home-single/1527833914|title=Come Home - Single by FUTURISTIC, 24hrs & YBN Almighty Jay|work=Apple Music|access-date=September 24, 2020}}</ref>

== Personal life ==
On January 9, 2020, Futuristic announced his engagement to Paris Shantè Rodriguez by proposing to her on a cruise.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://twitter.com/OnlyFuturistic/status/1215293582292193286|title=Futuristic™ on Twitter: "💍 Faked an Ankle Injury and asked her to marry her💍"|publisher=Twitter|date=January 9, 2020|access-date=January 11, 2020}}</ref> The pair married on October 13, 2020.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://twitter.com/OnlyFuturistic/status/1316469635814813698|title=Futuristic™ on Twitter: "I love you forever"|publisher=Twitter|date=October 14, 2020|access-date=November 8, 2020}}</ref>
