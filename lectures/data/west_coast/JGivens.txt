'''Jeremiah Timothy Givens''' (born May 24, 1987), also known by the stage name '''JGivens''', is an American former [[Christian hip hop]] musician. He has released four studio albums: ''Run'' in 2011, ''El v. Envy'' in 2013, ''[[Fly Exam]]'' in 2015, and ''Domino'' in 2019.

==Early life==
Jeremiah Timothy Givens<ref name="ASCAP">{{cite web |author=[[American Society of Composers, Authors and Publishers]] |title=Better Days Work ID No. 888894558 |url=https://www.ascap.com/repertory#/ace/search/workID/888894558 |url-status=live |archive-url= |archive-date= |access-date=July 7, 2022 |publisher=[[American Society of Composers, Authors and Publishers]]}} Work ID No. 888894558</ref> was born on May 24, 1987, in [[Los Angeles]], [[California]],<ref name="Humble Beast">{{cite web|url=http://www.humblebeast.com/jgivens/|title=JGivens|author=Humble Beast Records|publisher=Humble Beast Records|access-date=September 25, 2015|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150925152125/http://www.humblebeast.com/jgivens/|archive-date=September 25, 2015}}</ref><ref name="CBI">{{cite web |author=California Birth Index |title=Jeremiah Timothy Givens, Born 05/24/1987 in California |url=http://www.californiabirthindex.org/birth/jeremiah_timothy_givens_born_1987_17930580 |publisher=California Birth Index |accessdate=September 25, 2015}}</ref> to Brenda Banks.<ref name="CBI"/> His mother moved to [[Las Vegas]], [[Nevada]] when Givens was five years old, to remove the family from the presence of gang life.<ref name="Humble Beast"/>

Givens obtained his [[bachelor's degree]] from the [[University of Southern California]] in [[mechanical engineering]] at the [[USC Viterbi School of Engineering|Viterbi School of Engineering]].<ref name="Humble Beast"/>

==Music career==
JGivens' music career started in 2008,<ref>{{cite web|title=JGivens Discography|url=http://www.jesusfreakhideout.com/artists/JGivens.asp|publisher=Jesus Freak Hideout|accessdate=September 25, 2015}}</ref> and his debut studio album, ''Run'', was released on July 22, 2011, independently.<ref>{{Cite web|title = Rapzilla.com's 13 Freshman of 2013|url = http://www.rapzilla.com/rz/features/story/5920-rapzillacoms-13-freshman-of-2013?start=6|accessdate = 2015-09-28|first = Chad|last = Horton}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title = JGIVENS "RUN" (ALBUM REVIEW)|url = http://www.sphereofhiphop.com/rap/2011/11/jgivens-run-album-review/|first = Josh|last = Niemyjski|publisher = Sphere of Hip-Hop|accessdate = September 28, 2015|date = November 1, 2011}}</ref> His subsequent studio album, ''El v. Envy'', was released on August 6, 2013, independently.<ref>{{cite web|title=Album Review: JGivens – El v. Envy|url=http://wadeoradio.com/album-review-jgivens-el-v-envy/|first=Aubrey|last=McKay|publisher=Wade-O Radio|accessdate=September 25, 2015|date=August 19, 2013|archive-date=September 25, 2015|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150925180647/http://wadeoradio.com/album-review-jgivens-el-v-envy/|url-status=dead}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=JGivens "El v. Envy" (album review)|url=http://www.sphereofhiphop.com/rap/2013/09/jgivens-el-v-envy-album-review/|first=Josh|last=Niemyjski|publisher=Sphere of Hip Hop|accessdate=September 25, 2015|date=September 25, 2013}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=JGivens – El V. Envy|url=http://jamthehype.com/jgivens-el-v-envy-album-review/|first=Jim|last=Clifford|publisher=Jam the Hype|accessdate=September 25, 2015|date=September 8, 2013}}</ref> His third and most recent studio album, ''[[Fly Exam]]'', was released on September 25, 2015, with Humble Beast Records.<ref>{{cite web|title=Album Review: JGivens – Fly Exam|url=http://wadeoradio.com/album-review-jgivens-fly-exam/|first=Daniel|last=Cody|publisher=Wade-O Radio|accessdate=September 25, 2015|date=September 23, 2015|archive-date=September 25, 2015|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150925180650/http://wadeoradio.com/album-review-jgivens-fly-exam/|url-status=dead}}</ref>

In 2016, JGivens was featured on [[Lecrae]]'s mixtape, ''[[Church Clothes 3]]'', performing the song "Misconceptions 3" alongside Lecrae, [[John Givez]] and [[Jackie Hill Perry]].

In 2019, Givens transitioned away from Christian hip-hop and revealed he was addicted to drugs, openly gay, and [[HIV]]-positive.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://sojo.net/articles/christian-hip-hop-s-first-openly-gay-rapper-done-evangelizing|title=Christian Hip-Hop's First Openly Gay Rapper is Done Evangelizing|date=30 June 2021}}</ref>
