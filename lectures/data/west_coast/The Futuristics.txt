'''The Futuristics''' are a Grammy nominated American songwriting and music production duo consisting of '''Alex Schwartz''' and '''Joe Khajadourian'''. The duo formed after they met interning at [[Atlantic Records]] during the summer of 2005 and are currently based out of Los Angeles.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://dagmagazine.com/?p=973|title=Dagmagazine.com}}</ref> The Futuristics are signed with [[Artist Partner Group|Artist Publishing Group]].<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.songwriteruniverse.com/futuristics-producers-2017.htm|title=The Futuristics Co-Write & Produce Hit "Bad Things" for Machine Gun Kelly & Camila Cabello, Plus Hits for Flo Rida and Other Artists|date=January 30, 2017|work=Songwriter Universe {{!}} Songwriting News, Articles & Song Contest|access-date=March 28, 2017|language=en-US}}</ref>

In 2011, The Futuristics produced the [[Emmy Award]] nominated "[[3-Way (The Golden Rule)]]" for ''[[SNL]]''. The digital short featured [[Justin Timberlake]], [[Lady Gaga]], and [[Andy Samberg]] {{citation needed span|date=March 2021|text=and was given nationwide acclaim.}} In that same year, the duo produced the [[DJ Felli Fel]] song [[Boomerang (DJ Felli Fel song)|Boomerang]], which features [[Akon]], [[Pitbull (entertainer)|Pitbull]], and [[Jermaine Dupri]] and "[[Pot of Gold (Game song)|Pot of Gold]]" for American rapper [[The Game (rapper)|The Game]].

The Futuristics' 2012 release, "[[I Cry (Flo Rida song)|I Cry]]," for [[Flo Rida]] remained on the [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]] for 20 weeks, reaching #1 status on Billboard's Rap Songs. "[[I Cry (Flo Rida song)|I Cry]]" reached Top 10 status in 18 countries worldwide launching the duo to worldwide acclaim.  The Futuristics then produced the official remix of the [[Bruno Mars]] song "[[Moonshine (Bruno Mars song)|Moonshine]]" that year for the deluxe version of Mars' album, ''[[Unorthodox Jukebox]]''.<ref name="brunomars.com">{{cite web|url=http://www.brunomars.com/news/unorthodox-jukebox-target-deluxe-edition-available-now-2116231|title='Unorthodox Jukebox' Target Deluxe Edition Available Now – Bruno Mars Latest News|website=Bruno Mars Official Website}}</ref> The remix was received very well among critics and Mars' fans alike.{{Citation needed|date=March 2021}}

The Futuristics have also been recurring producers for ''[[The Fast and the Furious]] movie'' franchise. The theme song "[[We Own It (Fast & Furious)|We Own It]]" for the Universal Motion Picture, ''[[Fast & Furious 6]]'', featuring [[2 Chainz]] and [[Wiz Khalifa]], was produced by the duo selling 3× platinum worldwide. They followed it up with "Payback" for [[Furious 7 (soundtrack)|''Furious 7'']], featuring a rap collaboration with, [[Juicy J]], [[Future (rapper)|Future]], [[Sage the Gemini]], and [[Kevin Gates]].

2015 releases include the hit song "[[Somebody (Natalie La Rose song)|Somebody]]" featuring [[Jeremih]], by Universal Republic artist [[Natalie La Rose]]; "Hold Me Down" for [[Halsey (singer)|Halsey]]'s critically acclaimed album ''[[Badlands (Halsey album)|Badlands]]''; [[Heart Blanche|"Music to my Soul]]", the second single from [[CeeLo Green]]'s fifth studio album ''[[Heart Blanche]]''; "Serve It Up" by [[Trey Songz]]; and "Blue Jeans" by [[Chris Brown]].

Their 2016 work includes platinum hit "[[Fetish (song)|Fetish]]" by [[Selena Gomez]] featuring [[Gucci Mane]], top 5 [[Billboard Hot 100|Billboard]] hit and 2x [[Platinum record|Platinum]] in the US "[[Bad Things (Machine Gun Kelly and Camila Cabello song)|Bad Things]]" by [[Machine Gun Kelly (rapper)|Machine Gun Kelly]]  and [[Camila Cabello]], and "One Shot" by [[Robin Thicke]] featuring rapper [[Juicy J]].

In 2017 The Futuristics worked with [[G-Eazy]] on [[Platinum record|Platinum]] hit song called "[[Him & I]]" featuring [[Halsey (singer)|Halsey]]. G-Eazy and the duo followed that song with another called "[[Sober (G-Eazy song)|Sober]]" featuring [[Charlie Puth]]. Later in the year, they were also responsible for producing the song "Tribe" by Kim Viera featured in the award-winning American musical comedy ''[[Pitch Perfect (film series)|Pitch Perfect]]''. The Futuristics would then move on to work with [[Lil Wayne]], [[Niykee Heaton]], [[Why Don't We]], [[Loren Gray]], and [[Kiiara]] in 2018 and 2019.

Recent production credits include two songs off the [[Blink-182]] album [[Nine (Blink-182 album)|''Nine'']], [[Bebe Rexha]]'s single "[[You Can't Stop the Girl]]", [[G-Eazy]]'s & Delacy's "Cruel Intentions", and Meghan Trainor's "You Don't Know Me".

The duo's work on Justin Bieber's latest album, "Justice", has garnered them a Grammy nomination for Album of The Year 2022.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.grammy.com/grammys/news/2022-grammys-complete-winners-nominees-nominations-list|title=Album Of The Year Nomination}}</ref>
