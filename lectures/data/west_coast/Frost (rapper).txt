'''Arturo R. Molina Jr.''' (born May 31, 1964), better known as '''Frost''' (originally '''Kid Frost'''), is an American rapper, songwriter and record producer from Los Angeles. He charted in the 1990s with his first four albums: ''[[Hispanic Causing Panic]]'', ''[[East Side Story (Kid Frost album)|East Side Story]]'', ''[[Smile Now, Die Later]]'' and ''[[When Hell.A. Freezes Over]]''. His most successful single is "[[La Raza (song)|La Raza]]" which hit number 6 on the rap songs chart in August 1990. Frost was an integral part of the Latin Alliance, releasing one album, 1991's ''[[Latin Alliance (album)|Latin Alliance]]''. 

Frost's music entered the [[Billboard 200]] again in 2002 with the album ''[[Still Up in This Shit!]]''. Frost is the father of record producer [[Scoop DeVille]].

== Early life ==
Molina was born and raised in [[Los Angeles, California]], and occasionally lived with his family in military bases in [[Guam]] and [[Germany]].<ref name="LarkinDM">{{cite book|title=[[Encyclopedia of Popular Music|The Virgin Encyclopedia of Dance Music]]|editor=[[Colin Larkin (writer)|Colin Larkin]]|publisher=[[Virgin Books]]|date=1998|edition=First|isbn=0-7535-0252-6|page=125}}</ref> He is of [[Mexicans|Mexican]] descent. He began his music career in 1982 as Kid Frost as a tribute to his rival [[Ice-T]], whom he often battled in the music industry. In an interview Frost stated that his first actual DJ was in fact [[Dr. Dre]] and [[DJ Yella]]. He soon became a [[breakdancing|breakdancer]] for [[Uncle Jamms Army]].<ref name="allmusic">{{cite web|url={{AllMusic|class=artist|id=p17735|pure_url=yes}}|title=Frost > Biography|last=Huey|first=Steve|website=[[AllMusic]]|access-date=August 25, 2018}}</ref>

== Career ==
In the mid-1980s, Frost released several pre-gangsta 12" singles on Los Angeles-based labels Electrobeat and Baja.<ref name="LarkinDM" /> In the late 1980s, Kid Frost moved to [[Virgin Records]].<ref name="LarkinDM" /> Virgin released his biggest hit, "[[La Raza (song)|La Raza]]".<ref name="LarkinDM" /> His debut album, ''[[Hispanic Causing Panic]]'' was released in 1990. He also established a Latin rap [[Supergroup (music)|supergroup]] called Latin Alliance,<ref name="LarkinDM" /> which released their only album, ''[[Latin Alliance (album)|Latin Alliance]]'', in 1991. His second album, ''[[East Side Story (Kid Frost album)|East Side Story]]'' was released in 1992,<ref name="LarkinDM" /> which featured appearances from [[MC Eiht]], [[A.L.T.]] and Ganxta Ridd from the [[Boo-Yaa T.R.I.B.E.]]

In 1995, Frost dropped the "Kid" from his nickname and signed with [[Ruthless Records]], [[Eazy-E]]'s label (distributed by [[Relativity Records|Relativity]]).<ref name="LarkinDM" /> ''[[Smile Now, Die Later]]'' was released that year.<ref name="LarkinDM" /> [[Above the Law (group)|Above The Law]] were featured as guest rappers, alongside [[A.L.T.]], O.G.Enius and [[Kokane]]. [[Rick James]] also appeared on Frost's version of "Mary Jane". His second album for Ruthless, ''[[When Hell.A. Freezes Over|When Hell.A.Freezes Over]]'', was released in 1997. [[Ice-T]], Scoop, O.G.Enius and [[Domino (rapper)|Domino]] also appeared as guest rappers. In 1998, Frost collaborated with [[South Park Mexican]] in "''El Jugador"'' music video along with Low-G released by [[Dope House Records]] in the [[Power Moves: The Table]] album. Frost was also featured in the songs: Cali-Tex Connect, and West Coast, Gulf Coast, East Coast also from the [[Power Moves: The Table]] album <ref>{{Citation|title=SPM – Power Moves The Table|year=1998 |url=https://www.discogs.com/release/3844286-SPM-Power-Moves-The-Table|language=en|access-date=February 21, 2022}}</ref>

In 1999, Frost moved to a small [[Independent record label|independent label]] called Celeb Entertainment Inc. His first album for Celeb Entertainment titled ''This Was Then This Is Now Vol. I'' was released in 1999. [[Kurupt]], [[King T]], [[Baby Bash]], [[Jay Tee]], [[Jayo Felony]], [[Xzibit]], [[B-Legit]] and [[Cameo (band)|Cameo]] were featured on the CD. ''That Was Then This Is Now Vol. II'' was released in 2000. Frank V., Clika One, Jay Tee, Baby Bash and other guest rappers were also featured on the CD.

2002's ''[[Still Up in This Shit!|Still Up In This Shit!]]'', released by Hit-A-Lick and [[Koch Records]], featured more Latin rap style and [[g-funk]] tracks as well as a hidden bonus [[rock music|rock]] track titled "Cannabis". [[Mellow Man Ace]], [[Daz Dillinger]], [[Baby Bash]], [[A.L.T.]], Nino Brown, [[Don Cisco]] and other guest rappers appeared, and one track featured the group [[Tierra (band)|Tierra]].

In 2004 ''Welcome to Frost Angeles'' was released on [[Thump Records]], which was produced almost entirely by Frost and his son Scoop DeVille. Only the Intro is produced by Binky Womack, and Philly Blunt co-produced one track. Guest rappers included Cameosis, Genovese and Jay Tee. Frost again signed to Low Profile Records and released his album ''Till The Wheels Fall Off'' in 2006. It had various guest appearances which included Baby Bash, Scoop DeVille and Mr. Sancho.

Frost also performed music for films including "Bite the Bullet (Theme from Gunmen)" in the 1993 film ''Gunmen'' and "Tears Of A Mother" in the film ''No Mothers Crying, No Babies Dying'', which featured Ice-T.

Frost is also an accomplished actor appearing in several films, as well as doing voice roles for fictional characters such as [[List of characters in Grand Theft Auto: San Andreas#T-Bone Mendez|T-Bone Mendez]] from ''[[Grand Theft Auto: San Andreas]]'' and contributing his song "[[La Raza (song)|La Raza]]".<ref>{{Cite web|title=GTA-SA|url=https://media-rockstargames-com.akamaized.net/rockstargames-newsite/img/manuals/en_au/GTASA_Xbox360_Manual_AU.PDF|website=Rockstar}}</ref>

He was named vice president of the Music Division of Goldmark Industries on August 30, 2006. Frost also appeared in a cameo role in [[Snoop Dogg]]'s "[[Vato (song)|Vato]]" music video, as well as [[B-Real]] that same year.

In 2016, Frost announced he has been diagnosed with cancer.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://thesource.com/2016/03/02/kid-frost-announces-he-has-been-diagnosed-with-cancer/|title=Kid Frost Announces He Has Been Diagnosed With Cancer {{!}} The Source|last=Allah|first=Sha Be|date=March 2, 2016|work=[[The Source]]|access-date=August 25, 2018}}</ref>
