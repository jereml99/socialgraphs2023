'''Rebbeca Marie Gomez'''{{r|latimes|"21under21"}} (born March 2, 1997),<ref name="21under21">{{cite magazine|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/6251181/becky-g-21-under-21-2014|title=Becky G: 21 Under 21 (2014)|magazine=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|access-date=January 4, 2015|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141229081509/http://www.billboard.com/articles/6251181/becky-g-21-under-21-2014|archive-date=December 29, 2014|url-status=live}}</ref> known professionally as '''Becky G''', is an American singer and actress. She first gained recognition in 2011 when she began posting videos of herself covering popular songs online. One of her videos caught the attention of record producer [[Dr. Luke]], who subsequently offered her a record deal with [[Kemosabe Records]] in a joint venture with [[RCA Records]]. While working on her debut effort, Gomez collaborated with artists [[will.i.am]], [[Cody Simpson]] and [[Cher Lloyd]]. In 2013, she released her debut commercial single, "[[Jenny from the Block #Cover versions|Becky from the Block]]", to positive reception. Later that year, she released her debut extended play, ''[[Play It Again (EP)|Play It Again]]''. Her second single and first in 2014, "[[Can't Get Enough (Becky G song)|Can't Get Enough]]", featured guest vocals from [[Pitbull (rapper)|Pitbull]] and went on to top the [[Latin Rhythm Airplay]] chart in the United States.

Gomez achieved mainstream success with the release of "[[Shower (song)|Shower]]", that same year, which went on to enter the top twenty of the [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]] chart. The single would go on to receive a multi platinum certification from the [[Recording Industry Association of America]] (RIAA), denoting two million units sold in the country. Following the success of "Shower", Gomez released "[[Can't Stop Dancin' (Becky G song)|Can't Stop Dancin']]", "Lovin' So Hard" and "[[Break a Sweat]]" during 2014 and 2015, as singles from her then-forthcoming album. She embarked on a co-headlining tour with [[J Balvin]] during September and October 2015, spanning throughout the United States. She portrayed Valentina Galindo in two episodes of the musical television series ''[[Empire (2015 TV series)|Empire]]'', contributing two new songs to the show's soundtrack. She released her first full-Spanish song "[[Sola (Becky G song)|Sola]]" in June 2016. In July 2018, Gomez released "Zooted" (featuring [[French Montana]] and [[Farruko]]), as her first English-language song in three years. On September 27, 2019, she was featured on the "[[Chicken Noodle Soup (J-Hope song)|Chicken Noodle Soup]]" cover by South Korean rapper and dancer [[J-Hope]] of [[BTS]]. Her success has led her to collaborate with artists such as [[Daddy Yankee]], [[Christina Aguilera]], and [[David Guetta]].

Gomez's debut studio album ''[[Mala Santa]]'', released in October 2019,<ref name="idolator">{{cite web|url=https://www.idolator.com/7899300/becky-g-announces-debut-album-mala-santa|title=Finally! Becky G Announces Her Debut Album, ''Mala Santa''|last=Wass|first=Mike|website=[[Idolator (website)|Idolator]]|date=October 8, 2019|accessdate=October 9, 2019|archive-date=September 14, 2023|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230914155210/https://www.idolator.com/7899300/becky-g-announces-debut-album-mala-santa|url-status=live}}</ref> debuted at number 85 in the US ''[[Billboard 200]]'', was certified 7× [[Music recording certification|platinum]] by the RIAA Latin, debuted at number three in the [[Top Latin Albums]], and spawned the two ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' [[Hot Latin Songs]] top-ten entries: "[[Mayores]]" and "[[Sin Pijama]]". Her May 2022 second studio album ''[[Esquemas]]'',<ref>{{cite web | url=https://variety.com/2022/music/news/becky-g-new-album-esquemas-1235265801/ | title=Becky G Drops Second Studio Album, 'Esquemas' | date=May 13, 2022 | access-date=May 14, 2022 | archive-date=May 14, 2022 | archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20220514165123/https://variety.com/2022/music/news/becky-g-new-album-esquemas-1235265801/ | url-status=live }}</ref> debuted at number 92 in the US, including number five on the Top Latin Albums, and debuted at number one in the [[Latin Pop Albums]]; its third single, "[[Mamiii]]", became her highest-charting single in the US.

[[List of awards and nominations received by Becky G|Her accolades]] include two [[American Music Awards]], four [[American Society of Composers, Authors and Publishers|ASCAP Latin Awards]], four [[Latin American Music Awards]], plus the honorable Extraordinary Evolution Award, six [[Premios Juventud]], and four nominations from the [[Latin Grammy Awards]].

==Artistry==
Gomez is described as a [[Hip hop music|hip hop]], [[Pop rap|pop rap]], [[Pop music|pop]], [[Latin music|latin]], and [[reggaeton]] artist.<ref>{{cite web|title=Becky G|url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/becky-g-mn0002999777|last=Collar|first=Matt|publisher=[[AllMusic]]. ==Achievements==
{{See also|List of awards and nominations received by Becky G}}
Gomez has won an [[iHeartRadio Music Awards|iHeartRadio Music Award]], nine [[Latin American Music Awards]], and three [[People's Choice Awards]]. She has been nominated for a [[Billboard Music Awards|Billboard Music Award]] and six [[Latin Grammy Awards|Latin Grammy Award]]. 

Gomez was honored as one of the [[Leading Ladies of Entertainment]] by [[The Latin Recording Academy]] in 2018.<ref name="latinacademy"/> In 2019, Gomez was awarded the [[Latin American Music Awards of 2019|Extraordinary Evolution Award]] for her evolving work in a short span of years.<ref name="extraordinaryevolutionaward"/> Gomez was named to ''[[Forbes]]''' [[30 Under 30]] list in the music category.<ref name="forbeslist"/> In 2023, she received the [[Billboard Women in Music#Impact Award|Impact Award]] at the [[Billboard Women in Music|''Billboard'' Women in Music]] event.<ref name="billboardimpact"/>
