'''Christopher Tafoya''' (born June 2, 1975), better known by his [[stage name]] '''Sleep''' or '''Sleep of Oldominion''', is an American [[Hip hop music|hip hop]] artist from [[Farmington, New Mexico]], who is currently based in [[Portland, Oregon]].<ref>[https://archive.today/20130202175559/http://www.spinner.com/2010/03/13/sleep-of-oldominion-interview-sxsw-2010/ ''Spinner'' - Sleep interview]</ref> He is a founding member of the [[Northwest hip hop|Pacific Northwest hip hop]] collective [[Oldominion]]<ref>[http://www.allmusic.com/artist/sleep-mn0000026582 ''All Music Guide'' - mini-biography]</ref> and hip hop duo [[The Chicharones]] alongside [[Josh Martinez]].<ref>[http://musicthatisntbad.org/2012/06/17/album-review-the-chicharones-swine-flew/ ''Music That Isn't Bad'' - ''Swine Flew'' album review] {{webarchive|url=https://archive.today/20130415102626/http://musicthatisntbad.org/2012/06/17/album-review-the-chicharones-swine-flew/ |date=2013-04-15 }}</ref><ref>[http://www.indymojo.com/the-chicharones-at-sxsw-interview/ ''Indy Mojo'' - interview] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131230232704/http://www.indymojo.com/the-chicharones-at-sxsw-interview/ |date=2013-12-30 }}</ref> He is signed to [[Strange Famous Records]].<ref>[http://www.strangefamousrecords.com/sleep/ Sleep at Strange Famous Records]</ref><ref>[http://www.wweek.com/portland/article-10693-sleep_hesitation_wounds_%28strange_famous%29.html ''Willamette Week'' - album review]</ref>

==Early life==
Tafoya grew up in the small town community of [[Farmington, New Mexico]]. He is of [[Mexico|Mexican]] descent.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.strangefamousrecords.com/artists/sleep/|title=Sleep of Oldominion - Strange Famous Records|newspaper=Strange Famous Records|language=en-US|access-date=2017-02-03}}</ref> At the age of five, Sleep was already rapping and [[b-boying]] with his friends and relatives in the small town, with himself naming [[Run–D.M.C.|Run DMC]] as an important influence when he first began.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.reocities.com/hiphopinterview/sleep.html|title=An Interview with SLEEP of Oldominion- Interview by Todd E. Jones|last=Poet|first=Todd Jones aka The New Jeru|website=www.reocities.com|access-date=2017-02-03}}</ref><ref>[http://english.fakeforreal.net/index.php/post/2005/10/03/138-sleep-interview-english-version ''Fake for Real'' - interview]</ref>

In addition to the hip hop he heard early on, his father was a former bass player for blues musician [[Clarence "Gatemouth" Brown|Gatemouth Brown]], and would play a wide array of music, from Mariachi, to blues, to rock. Sleep also grew up with seven other brothers who all played musical instruments and had a grandfather who was also a multi instrumentalist.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.brownpapertickets.com/event/15704|title=STIFF Licks Closing Party with Sleep and A Gun That Shoots Knives|website=www.brownpapertickets.com|access-date=2017-03-07}}</ref> Entering the world of rap, he felt that his talents as both an MC and a writer were overly slept on, hence the creation of his name Sleep.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://thehypemagazine.blogspot.com/2014/05/the-hype-magazine-interviews-sleep-of.html|title=The HYPE Magazine Interviews Sleep of Oldominion! {{!}} @SleepSFR|access-date=2017-02-03}}</ref><ref>[http://www.xlr8r.com/reviews/sleep-oldominion/riot-candlelight ''XLR8R'' - album review]</ref>

Growing up in New Mexico, Sleep went to the same school as [[Onry Ozzborn]], which led them to meet Pale Soul.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.rapstation.com/article/onry-ozzborn-rs|title=Onry Ozzborn: The RAPstation Interview|date=2015-12-12|newspaper=Rapstation|language=en-US|access-date=2017-02-03}}</ref> Together, they formed the group Oraclez Creed, which would later become [[Oldominion]] after combining with Frontline ([[Boom Bap Project|Destro]], Nyqwil, and Snafu).<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://english.fakeforreal.net/index.php/post/2005/10/03/138-sleep-interview-english-version|title=SLEEP - Interview - Fake For Real|last=codotusylv|website=english.fakeforreal.net|language=en|access-date=2017-02-03}}</ref> After the join, the massive [[Hip hop music in the Pacific Northwest|Pacific Northwest hip hop]] collective grew to over twenty members.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.allmusic.com/album/one-mw0000217832|title=One - Oldominion {{!}} Songs, Reviews, Credits {{!}} AllMusic|website=AllMusic|access-date=2017-02-03}}</ref>

== Musical career ==

=== 2001-2008: ''Riot by Candelight'', ''Christopher'' and the Chicharones ===
Sleep released his debut album, ''Riot by Candlelight'', in 2002.<ref>{{cite magazine|first=Thomas|last=Quinlan|url=http://exclaim.ca/Reviews/HipHop/sleep-riot_by_candlelight|title=Sleep - Riot by Candlelight|magazine=[[Exclaim!]]|date=October 2002}}</ref> Following the release, he was heavily involved in the multiple albums by Oldominion.<ref>[http://exclaim.ca/Reviews/HipHop/sleep-riot_by_candlelight ''Exclaim!'' - ''Riot by Candlelight'' album review]</ref> In 2001, Sleep met Canadian rapper [[Josh Martinez]] by chance encounter at the [[South by Southwest]] festival in [[Austin, Texas]].<ref>[http://www.urb.com/2009/07/07/sleep-hesitation-wounds/ ''URB'' - album review] {{webarchive|url=https://archive.today/20131229030651/http://www.urb.com/2009/07/07/sleep-hesitation-wounds/ |date=2013-12-29 }}</ref> Together, the two would create the hip hop duo ''[[the Chicharones]].''<ref>[https://archive.today/20130202193120/http://www.spinner.com/2010/02/09/sxsw-2010-the-chicharones/ ''Spinner'' - Chicharones interview]</ref><ref>[http://voices.yahoo.com/interview-chicharones-2012-warped-11388148.html?cat=40 ''Yahoo Voice'' - interview] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131231000430/http://voices.yahoo.com/interview-chicharones-2012-warped-11388148.html?cat=40 |date=2013-12-31 }}</ref> Sleep's second album, ''[[Christopher (Sleep album)|Christopher]]'', was released in 2005 (and then re-released with bonus tracks in 2009).<ref>{{cite web|url=http://sfrstore.myshopify.com/collections/cds/products/sleep-christopher-bonus-tracks-cd|title=Sleep - Christopher + Bonus Tracks CD - Strange Famous Records}}</ref>

=== 2009-2013: Strange Famous Records and ''Hesitation Wounds'' ===
Leading up to 2009, Sleep had contacted [[Sage Francis]] in an attempt to receive a feature for his upcoming album, which eventually led to his signing to Sage's independent label [[Strange Famous Records]].<ref>{{cite magazine|author=URB magazine|url=http://www.urb.com/2009/07/07/sleep-hesitation-wounds/|title=Sleep - Hesitation Wounds (Review)|magazine=[[URB (magazine)|URB]]|date=July 7, 2009|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://archive.today/20131229030651/http://www.urb.com/2009/07/07/sleep-hesitation-wounds/|archive-date=December 29, 2013}}</ref> In 2009, Sleep released his third solo album, ''[[Hesitation Wounds (album)|Hesitation Wounds]].'' It features verses from [[Grayskul]] and [[Del the Funky Homosapien]] as well as production from likes of [[Zavala (producer)|Zavala]] and [[Reanimator (producer)|Reanimator]], among others.<ref>{{cite news|first=Chris|last=Faraone|url=http://thephoenix.com/boston/music/84996-sleep-hesitation-wounds/|title=Sleep - Hesitation Wounds|newspaper=[[The Phoenix (newspaper)|The Phoenix]]|date=June 16, 2009}}</ref>

=== 2014-present: ''Oregon Failure'' ===
Sleep's fourth solo album, ''[[Oregon Failure]]'', was released on April 15, 2014 on Strange Famous Records as well.<ref>{{cite magazine|url=http://urb.com/2014/04/08/crosscheck-sage-francis-sleep/ |title=Crosscheck: Sage Francis & Sleep |magazine=[[URB (magazine)|URB]] |first=Jason |last=Chang |date=April 8, 2014 |accessdate=April 9, 2014}}</ref> The entire album is produced by Maulskull and has features from [[Onry Ozzborn]] and [[Ceschi]], among others.<ref name="oregonfailure">{{cite web|url=http://sleepofthechicharones.com/ |title=Sleep x Maulskull - Oregon Failure - Tues April 15th, 2014 |publisher=Sleep |first=Christopher |last=Tafoya |date=2014 |accessdate=2014-02-19}}</ref>

== Style ==
Sleep's style has been described as "having intricate rhyme schemes and rapid-fire delivery," with his lyrics being both witty and personal.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.strangefamousrecords.com/artists/sleep/|title=Sleep of Oldominion - Strange Famous Records|newspaper=Strange Famous Records|language=en-US|access-date=2017-02-03}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=http://hiphopdx.com/news/id.29206/title.sleep-reflects-on-his-intricate-rhyme-styles-touring-with-sage-francis|title=Sleep Reflects On His Intricate Rhyme Styles & Touring With Sage Francis|newspaper=HipHopDX|language=en-US|access-date=2017-02-03}}</ref>
