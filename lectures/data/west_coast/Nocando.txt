==Career==
Nocando began gaining notoriety in Leimert Park's [[open-mic]] workshops in [[South Los Angeles]]. In the battle circuit, it is  believed that he has won a hundred wins over many emcees.<ref name="law">{{cite web|url=http://www.laweekly.com/2010-01-28/music/locked-and-loaded-nocando/|title=Locked and Loaded: Nocando|work=[[LA Weekly]]|first=Jeff|last=Weiss|date=January 28, 2010|access-date=}}</ref> He has since focused on making and releasing music, only taking time out occasionally to battle.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://larecord.com/interviews/2010/01/25/nocando-interview-its-a-great-song-but-i-hate-you|title=Nocando: It's a Great Song, But I Hate You|work=[[L.A. Record]]|first=Chris|last=Ziegler|date=January 25, 2010|access-date=}}</ref>

In 2010, Nocando released his official debut album, ''[[Jimmy the Lock]]'', on [[Alpha Pup Records]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.urb.com/2010/01/28/nocando-%E2%80%93-jimmy-the-lock/|title=Nocando - Jimmy the Lock|work=[[URB (magazine)|URB]]|first=Dave|last=Madden|date=January 28, 2010|url-status=dead|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20111022015708/http://www.urb.com/2010/01/28/nocando-%E2%80%93-jimmy-the-lock/|archivedate=October 22, 2011}}</ref> It was produced by [[Thavius Beck]], [[Nosaj Thing]], [[Free the Robots]], [[Daedelus (musician)|Daedelus]], and [[Nicholas Thorburn]], among others.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.xlr8r.com/reviews/nocando/jimmy-lock|title=Jimmy the Lock|work=[[XLR8R]]|first=Zoneil|last=Maharaj|date=April 15, 2010|access-date=}}</ref> In that year, he also released ''[[Sincerely Yours (Bomb Zombies EP)|Sincerely Yours]]'', a collaborative EP with [[Nobody (producer)|Nobody]] as [[Bomb Zombies]], on [[Hellfyre Club]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.xlr8r.com/news/2010/10/nocando-and-nobody-collaborate-b|title=Nocando and Nobody Collaborate on Bomb Zombies|work=[[XLR8R]]|first=Patric|last=Fallon|date=October 20, 2010|url-status=dead|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20130819233816/http://www.xlr8r.com/news/2010/10/nocando-and-nobody-collaborate-b|archivedate=August 19, 2013}}</ref>

In 2011, he released ''[[10 Haters]]'', a collaborative album with [[Busdriver]] as [[Flash Bang Grenada]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://consequenceofsound.net/2011/07/busdriver-nocando-project-flash-bang-grenada-announces-debut/|title=Busdriver, Nocando project Flash Bang Grenada announces debut|work=[[Consequence of Sound]]|first=Harry|last=Painter|date=July 15, 2011|access-date=}}</ref> ''Alarm'' included it on the "50 Unheralded Albums from 2011" list.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://alarm-magazine.com/best_albums/50-unheralded-albums-from-2011/4/|title=50 Unheralded Albums from 2011|work=Alarm|first=Scott|last=Morrow|date=December 30, 2011|access-date=}}</ref>

In 2012, Nocando released a free EP, ''Zero Hour'',<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.imposemagazine.com/bytes/nocando-zero-hour-free-EP|title=Nocando's Zero Hour free EP|work=[[Impose (magazine)|Impose]]|first=Blake|last=Gillespie|date=January 12, 2012|access-date=}}</ref> as well as the singles "Where's the Money?"<ref>{{cite web|url=http://fashionably-early.com/2012/09/19/nocando-wheres-the-money-prod-low-limit/|title=Nocando – Where's The Money? (prod. Low Limit)|work=Fashionably Early|date=September 19, 2012|access-date=}}</ref> and "Up in the Air".<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.earmilk.com/2012/12/17/nocando-up-in-the-air/|title=Nocando - "Up In The Air"|work=Earmilk|first=Logan|last=Irish|date=December 17, 2012|access-date=}}</ref> In 2013, he released a single, "Little Green Monsters".<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.imposemagazine.com/bytes/nocando-little-green-monsters-feat-liphemra|title=Nocando, "Little Green Monsters" (feat. Liphemra)|work=[[Impose (magazine)|Impose]]|first=Blake|last=Gillespie|date=December 6, 2013|access-date=}}</ref> His second solo album, ''Jimmy the Burnout'', was released on Hellfyre Club in 2014.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://pitchfork.com/reviews/albums/19113-nocando-jimmy-the-burnout/|title=Nocando: Jimmy the Burnout|work=[[Pitchfork (website)|Pitchfork]]|first=Nate|last=Patrin|date=March 17, 2014|access-date=}}</ref>

In 2017, Nocando released ''Severed''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://uproxx.com/realtalk/nocando-new-album-severed/|title=Former Battle Rap Champion Nocando's New Album 'Severed' Is A Battle Between Nocando And Himself|work=[[Uproxx]]|first=Aaron|last=Williams|date=May 26, 2017|access-date=August 11, 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://daily.bandcamp.com/2017/06/09/nocando-interview/|title=No More Drama For Nocando|work=[[Bandcamp Daily]]|first=Will|last=Schube|date=June 9, 2017|access-date=June 12, 2018}}</ref> ''[[HipHopDX]]'' called it "one of Nocando's most powerful albums to date."<ref>{{cite web|url=http://hiphopdx.com/news/id.43620/title.nocando-drops-severed-project-featuring-slug-aceyalone|title=Nocando Drops "Severed" Project Featuring Slug, Aceyalone & Ghetts|work=[[HipHopDX]]|first=Kyle|last=Eustice|date=May 31, 2017|access-date=August 11, 2017}}</ref> ''[[Uproxx]]'' named it the 24th best rap album of 2017.<ref name=uproxx2017/> In that year, he also released ''King Snake''.<ref name=uproxx2017>{{cite web|url=http://uproxx.com/realtalk/best-rap-albums-2017-ranked-list/|title=All The Best Rap Albums Of 2017, Ranked|work=[[Uproxx]]|date=December 6, 2017|access-date=December 17, 2017}}</ref>

In 2019, Nocando changed his stage name to All City Jimmy,<ref name=theskinny2019>{{cite web|url=https://www.theskinny.co.uk/music/live-music/reviews/open-mike-eagle-the-art-school-glasgow-19-may|title=Open Mike Eagle @ The Art School, Glasgow, 19 May|work=[[The Skinny (magazine)|The Skinny]]|first=Lewis|last=Wade|date=May 21, 2019|access-date=October 31, 2019}}</ref> and later removed all Nocando titles from digital retailers.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.instagram.com/p/B4A1gXAA7aK/|title=This is just a heads up to my fans. ...|work=[[Instagram]]|author=All City Jimmy|date=October 24, 2019|access-date=October 30, 2019}}</ref>
