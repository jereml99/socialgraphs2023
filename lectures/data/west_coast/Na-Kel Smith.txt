'''Na-Kel Allah Smith'''  (born July 22, 1994) is an American skateboarder, rapper, songwriter and actor.<ref>{{cite web |title=Na-Kel Smith and Jonah Hill Interview |url=http://www.thrashermagazine.com/articles/na-kel-smith-and-jonah-hill-interview/ |website=www.thrashermagazine.com |language=en}}</ref> Originally a skateboarder from the [[Los Angeles]] area, he was known for his association with the [[hip hop collective]] [[Odd Future]], appearing in music videos, vlogs and their [[Adult Swim]] TV show ''[[Loiter Squad]]'', and performing live with the collective and its members.<ref name="pfork">{{cite web |last1=Strauss |first1=Matthew |title=mid90s Star Na-Kel Smith Releases New Album |url=https://pitchfork.com/news/mid90s-star-na-kel-smith-releases-new-album-listen/ |website=Pitchfork |date=January 14, 2019 |language=en}}</ref>

==Career==
Smith debuted his music career in 2013, marking his first recorded verse on [[Tyler, the Creator]]'s second studio album ''[[Wolf (Tyler, the Creator album)|Wolf]]'' on the [[posse cut]] "Trashwang". In 2014, after his appearance in the [[Supreme (clothing)|Supreme]] video "''Cherry''", Smith co-founded his own hardware/apparel company, Hardies, with teammate [[Tyshawn Jones]].<ref name="mc">{{cite web |last1=Monster |first1=Children |title=Tyshawn Jones on Hardies and His Collab With Adidas |url=https://www.monsterchildren.com/56461/tyshawn-jones-skate-hardies-collab-adidas/ |website=Monster Children |date=January 31, 2017}}</ref> In 2015, he formed Hog Slaughta Boyz, a duo composed of Smith and former Odd Future member [[Earl Sweatshirt]].<ref name="fader">{{cite web |title=Na-Kel Smith, interviewed by Jonah Hill |url=https://www.thefader.com/2018/10/26/na-kel-smith-jonah-hill-interview-mid90s |website=The FADER |date=October 2, 2018 |language=en}}</ref> In 2018, Smith starred in his first commercial film, ''[[Mid90s]]'', directed by [[Jonah Hill]].<ref name="nyt">{{cite web |last1=Scott |first1=A. O. |title=Review: "Mid90s" Skates Back to a Less Innocent Time |url=https://www.nytimes.com/2018/10/17/movies/mid90s-review-jonah-hill.html |website=The New York Times |date=October 17, 2018}}</ref> In 2019, Smith released his debut studio album ''Twothousand Nakteen'' through A Dream No Longer Deferred Records and Narcowave.<ref name=pfork/>

Smith is the nephew of professional skateboarder [[Kareem Campbell]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5GDVlbw1Hek&t=259s |archive-url=https://ghostarchive.org/varchive/youtube/20211213/5GDVlbw1Hek |archive-date=December 13, 2021 |url-status=live|title=Na-Kel Smith {{!}} The Nine Club With Chris Roberts - Episode 64|date=September 18, 2017|work=The Nine Club|access-date=November 25, 2020|language=en-US}}{{cbignore}}</ref>
