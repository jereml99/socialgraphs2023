'''Tim Chantarangsu''' (born March 6, 1986),<ref>{{cite web|title=Dear DeLaGhetto #21 (Birthday Edition!)|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=C5hY3cbkXx0known|website=Youtube|date=March 6, 2008|access-date=January 27, 2020}}</ref> formerly known as '''Timothy DeLaGhetto''' (and previously '''Traphik'''), is an American [[internet]] and [[television]] personality and rapper. He is best known from the [[Improvisational theatre|improv]] comedy show ''[[Wild 'N Out]]'' where he was a cast member from 2013 to 2018 and 2020 to 2021.

Chantarangsu is also known as the co-host of the food-based web shows ''Basic to Bougie,'' ''Deliciousness'' on [[MTV]],<ref>{{Cite web|last=Borge|first=Jonathan|date=November 23, 2020|title=MTV's Ridiculousness Spinoff Is Guaranteed To Make You Hungry—And Make You Laugh|url=https://www.eonline.com/ca/news/1211679/mtvs-ridiculousness-spinoff-is-guaranteed-to-make-you-hungry-and-make-you-laugh|access-date=2020-11-23|website=E! Online}}</ref> and ''Send Foodz'' on [[Thrillist]]. His career started on [[YouTube]] where he uploaded comedic videos and original music.

==Early life==
Chantarangsu was born in [[Billings, Montana]], to parents of [[Thai people|Thai]] heritage.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://twitter.com/TimothyDeLaG/status/8688829188 |title=Timothy DeLaGhetto on Twitter: fun fact of the day- I was born in Billings, Montana |date=February 5, 2010 |website=Twitter |access-date=September 13, 2016}}</ref><ref name=caam/> He was raised in [[Long Beach, California]], until the age of nine before moving to [[Paramount, California]], where his parents opened the Thai Smile restaurant.<ref name=fresh/><ref>{{Cite podcast|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Uw3EIf-MFug|title=Timothy DeLaGhetto on Long Beach, Wild 'N Out, and California Pizza Kitchen|website=The Steebee Weebee Show|last=Lee|first=Steven|date=September 18, 2019|access-date=January 29, 2020}}</ref> He attended [[Paramount High School]] and went on to attend [[California State University, Long Beach]] but dropped out to pursue an entertainment career.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Smith|first=Patricia|date=May 13, 2013|title=Facebook & free speech: what rights do workers and students have to speak their minds online? - Free Online Library|url=https://www.thefreelibrary.com/Facebook+&+free+speech:+what+rights+do+workers+and+students+have+to...-a0330678201|access-date=2017-01-05|website=The Free Library}}</ref>

In 2011, Chantarangsu was fired from his job at a [[California Pizza Kitchen]] in [[Long Beach, California|Long Beach]] for tweeting that their new uniforms were "lame".<ref name="caam">{{cite web |url=http://caamedia.org/blog/2010/03/09/new-event-changing-da-game-2/ |title=New Event: Changing Da Game |website=Center for Asian American Media |date=March 9, 2010 |access-date=September 13, 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=http://dailybruin.com/2015/01/22/qa-timothy-delaghetto-talks-becoming-youtube-sensation-mainstream-star/ |title=Q&A: Timothy DeLaGhetto talks becoming YouTube sensation, mainstream star |website=Daily Bruin |first=Kevin |last=Truong |date=January 22, 2015 |access-date=September 13, 2016}}</ref> As a child, he developed a business plan modeled after [[Will Smith]], which he called the "Fresh Prince Format"; the plan outlined three career objectives: get a record deal, a television show, and a movie deal.<ref name="fresh">{{cite news |first=Nalea |last=Ko |title=Now, This is A Story About Timothy DeLaGhetto |date=Nov 28, 2011 |url=http://pacificcitizen.org/news/entertainment/now-story-about-timothy-delaghetto?page=show |access-date=July 17, 2013 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141006092405/http://pacificcitizen.org/news/entertainment/now-story-about-timothy-delaghetto?page=show |archive-date=October 6, 2014}}</ref>

==YouTube==
His former stage name, Timothy DeLaGhetto, was inspired by an episode of ''[[The Fresh Prince of Bel-Air]]''.<ref name=":0">{{cite web |last1=Chantarangu |first1=Tim |title=Retiring "Timothy DeLaGhetto" (Not an April Fool's Joke lol) |website=[[YouTube]] |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=SVCyfOsS3_4 |access-date=3 April 2020}}</ref>

Chantarangsu's main YouTube channel had over 4 million subscribers and over 800 million video views {{as of|2019|06|07|df=US|lc=y}}.<ref name=TraphikYTAbout>[https://www.youtube.com/user/TimothyDeLaGhetto2/about Timothy DeLaGhetto About page - YouTube]</ref> It features original [[Sketch comedy|skits]], parodies, [[Monologue|rants]], and a series called ''Dear DeLaGhetto''.<ref>{{YouTube|DaN4LrieCjo|"Call Me Maybe" (DIRTY PARODY)}}</ref>

{{As of|2019|06|07|df=US}}, Chantarangsu had produced over 800 vlogs, and has garnered approximately 1.2 million subscribers and over 195 million video views on his vlog channel.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.youtube.com/theTimothyDeLaGhetto |title=Timothy DeLaGhetto's LIFE in VLOG form |publisher=[[YouTube]] |date=December 30, 2012}}</ref> The channel consists of collaborations with other musical artists, songs from his albums and [[mixtape]]s, and a series called Weekly 16's.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.youtube.com/TraphikMusik |title=Traphik Musik |publisher=[[YouTube]] |date=September 2, 2012}}</ref> In 2017, he retired his rap alias "Traphik".

His style channel, also known as The Bakery, has over 234,000 subscribers and over 16 million video views. The channel is composed of his fit-of-the-day videos, clothing collections and style videos and interviews with fellow [[Sneakers|sneaker]] fans called "Kickin' It".<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.youtube.com/user/TimsBakery |title=Timothy DeLaGhetto's Style Channel |publisher=[[YouTube]] |date=September 2, 2012}}</ref> One of his newest video ventures includes an animated web series named ''Powerhouse'' in which he plays himself.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.youtube.com/show/powerhouse?s=2 |title=Powerhouse |publisher=[[YouTube]] |date=April 25, 2012}}</ref>

In 2014, Chantarangsu's YouTube Channel was listed on [[NewMediaRockstars|New Media Rockstars]] Top 100 Channels, ranked at #64.<ref>{{cite web |title=The NMR Top 100 YouTube Channels: 75-51! |url=http://newmediarockstars.com/2014/12/the-nmr-top-100-youtube-channels-75-51/ |website=New Media Rockstars |access-date=6 January 2015}}</ref>

Chantarangsu has participated in two episodes of ''[[Epic Rap Battles of History]]'', as [[Kim Jong-il|Kim Jong-Il]] in Season 1 in 2011 and as [[Sun Tzu]] in Season 4 in 2015.

Chantarangsu began regularly uploading his dating and relationship podcast, ''No Chaser'', onto his main YouTube channel and other podcasting platforms in February 2019. His regular co-hosts include [[KWIN|97.7 KWIN]] host Nikki Blades and YouTuber Ricky Shucks.<ref>{{Cite podcast|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=SiBrmax0V3U|title=How to Friends w/ Benefits + Is Head Important? - Ep 1|website=No Chaser|last=DeLaGhetto|first=Timothy|date=February 27, 2019|access-date=January 25, 2020|last2=Shucks|first2=Ricky|last3=Blades|first3=Nikki}}<br /></ref>

He co-hosts the food show ''Send Foodz'' with fellow YouTuber [[David So]]. The show was picked up by [[Thrillist]] shortly after its initial debut on Chantarangsu's channel and airs every other Thursday.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://variety.com/2019/digital/news/thrillist-send-foodz-youtube-timothy-delaghetto-david-so-1203135876/|title=Thrillist Makes First Content Acquisition With Food Show From YouTube's Timothy DeLaGhetto, David So|last=Spangler|first=Todd|date=2019-02-11|website=Variety|language=en|access-date=2020-01-25}}</ref>

In April 2020, Chantarangsu announced that he was retiring the "Timothy DeLaGhetto" moniker and would begin using his birth name professionally.<ref name=":0" />
