'''Casey Joseph Jones''' (born July 18, 1993), better known by his stage name '''Casey Veggies''', is an American rapper and songwriter from [[Los Angeles]], [[California]]. Since 2007, he has released five [[Independent record label|independent]] mixtapes under LA clothing and management company Peas & Carrots Intl.<ref name="Respect Staff">{{cite web|url=http://respect-mag.com/casey-veggies-debuts-sleeping-in-class-for-east-coast/|title=Casey Veggies Debuts "Sleeping In Class" for East Coast|work=RESPECT.|access-date=March 9, 2015}}</ref> His debut studio album, ''[[Live & Grow]]'', was released on September 25, 2015. Aside from his solo career, Casey was a founding member of the [[Los Angeles]]-based hip hop collective [[Odd Future]].

==Career==

===2007–12: Career beginnings===
At the age of 14, Casey released his first mixtape, titled ''Customized Greatly Vol. 1''; while he was performing by his stage name at the time, Custom.{{cn|date=May 2022}} Casey was one of the founding members of the [[hip hop]] collective [[Odd Future]],<ref name="Upp, Jeff">{{cite web|url=http://respect-mag.com/opinion-casey-veggies-controls-his-own-odd-future/|title=Opinion: Casey Veggies Controls His Own (Odd) Future|work=RESPECT.|access-date=9 March 2015}}</ref> appearing on the collective's first effort [[The Odd Future Tape]], before he decided to branch out on his own, after his first mixtape release. In an interview with Respect Mag, Casey stated it as: "It’s hard to explain, really, but I just had more of a [different] vision for my own rap career. I wanted to do my own thing."<ref name="Upp, Jeff"/>

While working with a fellow rapper and [[Def Jam]]'s recording artist [[YG (rapper)|YG]] on his second mixtape, Veggies began to take rap much more seriously. In an interview with The Madbury Club, Veggies discussed how "strategic sponsorships, limited merchandise, organic partnerships, social presence and influence ability have proved fundamental in the success of Peas & Carats and thus, Casey Veggies".<ref name="Arman, Marcus">{{cite web|url=http://madburyclub.com/spread/fresh-produce/ |title=Fresh Produce: Casey Veggies and Peas & Carrots International - The Madbury Club - Making Things Since 2088. |access-date=9 March 2015 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130411030551/http://madburyclub.com/spread/fresh-produce/ |archive-date=11 April 2013 }}</ref>

After graduating high school, Veggies joined with [[Mac Miller]], as one of the opening acts for his [[Blue Slide Park Tour]].<ref name="LRG Staff">{{Cite web|url=http://l-r-g.com/blog/2011/09/26/casey-veggies-nyc/|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120308103659/http://l-r-g.com/blog/2011/09/26/casey-veggies-nyc/|title=''CASEY VEGGIES – NYC'', Lifted Research Group Inc|archive-date=March 8, 2012}}</ref> Since then, Casey performed with artists, such as [[Nipsey Hussle]], [[Dom Kennedy]] and [[Kendrick Lamar]], as well as friends in OFWGKTA; including [[Tyler, the Creator]], [[Hodgy Beats]] and [[Domo Genesis]].<ref name="Respect Staff"/> Casey has also featured on records with artists, such as Rich Hil, [[King Chip]] and [[Raheem DeVaughn]].

In April 2012, Veggies joined with [[Roc Nation]] artist [[Rita Ora]] for a sold-out performance at SOB's in NYC.<ref name="Lyons, Ryan">{{cite web|url=http://respect-mag.com/review-rita-ora-and-casey-veggies-roc-for-a-sold-out-crowd-at-sobs/|title=Review: Rita Ora And Casey Veggies "Roc" For A Sold Out Crowd At SOB's|work=RESPECT.|access-date=9 March 2015}}</ref> In the same month, he also appeared with American rappers Mac Miller and [[Wiz Khalifa]] on [[MTV]]'s RapFixLive.<ref name="Llamoca, Janice">{{cite web|url=http://losangelesleakers.com/2012/04/wiz-khalifa-mac-miller-casey-veggies-on-rapfix-live-live-stream/|title=Wiz Khalifa, Mac Miller & Casey Veggies On RapFix Live (Live Stream)|work=Los Angeles Leakers|access-date=9 March 2015}}</ref>

===2013–present: ''Life Changes'' and ''Live & Grow''===
On January 22, 2013, Veggies released a solo mixtape titled ''Life Changes''. In February 2013, Veggies signed a record deal with VIC and [[Epic Records]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.bet.com/news/music/2013/02/05/sylvia-rhone-signs-casey-veggies-to-new-vic-label.html|title=Sylvia Rhone Signs Casey Veggies to new VIC Label|date=5 February 2013|work=BET.com|access-date=9 March 2015}}</ref>

On the following day, the store opening of PNCINTL, Casey Veggies released a single, titled "Young Niggas" featuring [[Juicy J]]. On May 10, 2013, a new single, "Money Don't Buy Time" was released. Casey Veggies also appeared on the Earl Sweatshirt album ''[[Doris (Earl Sweatshirt album)|Doris]]'' on the single "[[Hive (song)|Hive]]"; along with [[Vince Staples]]. Casey Veggies released a mixtape with [[Rockie Fresh]], titled ''Fresh Veggies'' in December 2013. It was presented by [[Puma (brand)|PUMA]] and was co-released under [[Maybach Music Group]] and PNCINTL.{{fact|date=June 2019}} The mixtape features [[guest appearance]]s from Overdoz, [[Ty Dolla Sign]], Juicy J, [[Kirko Bangz]] and [[Hit-Boy]]; along with additional productions handled by [[Lunice]], [[Jahlil Beats]] and [[Hit-Boy]], among others. Casey Veggies has also appeared on [[The Neighbourhood]]'s 2014 mixtape ''000000 & #FFFFFF''.<ref name="The Neighbourhood">{{cite web |title=The Neighbourhood |url=http://thenbhd.com/gb/ |website=The NBHD official |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150507190643/http://thenbhd.com/gb/ |archive-date=2015-05-07 }}</ref>

Casey went on his first Canadian tour under PNCINTL management in May 2015.<ref name="hustleGRL">{{cite web|url=http://hustlegrl.com/blog/2012/03/casey-veggies-to-tour-in-canada/|title=Casey Veggies To Tour In Canada|access-date=9 March 2015}}</ref> Veggies' debut album, ''[[Live & Grow]]'' was released on September 25, 2015.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/casey-veggies-tied-up-feat-dej-loaf-new-song.1964185.html|title=Casey Veggies - Tied Up Feat. DeJ Loaf|website=HotNewHipHop}}</ref> 

In 2017, he was featured on the American rapper [[Wiz Khalifa]]'s mixtape ''[[Laugh Now, Fly Later]]'', as the only feature on the project.<ref>{{cite news |title=Wiz Khalifa Unveils New Mixtape ‘Laugh Now, Fly Later’ |url=https://www.vibe.com/music/music-news/wiz-khalifa-laugh-now-fly-later-548669/ |access-date=6 March 2023 |publisher=VIBE}}</ref>
