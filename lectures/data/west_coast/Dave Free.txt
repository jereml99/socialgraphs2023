'''David Isaac Friley'''<ref name="Birth name"> {{Cite web |title=David Isaac Friley, Born 11/13/1986 in California |url=https://www.californiabirthindex.org/birth/david_isaac_friley_born_1986_17348486 |access-date=2023-04-01 |website=California Birth Index}} </ref> (born November 13, 1986), known professionally as '''Dave Free''', is an American filmmaker and record executive.<ref>{{Cite news |last=Jackson |first=Mitchell S. |date=2022-12-27 |title=Kendrick Lamar’s New Chapter: Raw, Intimate and Unconstrained |language=en-US |work=The New York Times |url=https://www.nytimes.com/2022/12/27/magazine/kendrick-lamar-dave-free.html |access-date=2023-07-18 |issn=0362-4331}}</ref> Born and raised in [[Inglewood, California]], he is best known for his creative partnership with rapper [[Kendrick Lamar]]. Together, they have directed several music videos and films under the collective name the Little Homies, and founded the entertainment company [[PGLang]] in 2020. Through their partnership, Free has won a [[Grammy Awards|Grammy Award]] and three [[MTV Video Music Awards]].<ref>{{Cite web |last=Tardio |first=Andres |date=2015-08-21 |title=Here's How Kendrick Lamar and Dave Free Became Super Duo 'The Little Homies' |url=https://www.mtv.com/news/2246258/kendrick-lamar-dave-free-little-homies/ |access-date=2023-07-29 |website=[[MTV]]}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |last=Phillips |first=Yoh |date=2016-04-07 |title=The Little Homies Have the Best Creative Vision in Rap |url=https://djbooth.net/features/2016-04-07-the-little-homies-video-breakdown |access-date=2023-07-29 |website=DJBooth}}</ref>

== Early life ==
David Isaac Friley was born on November 13, 1986, in [[Inglewood, California]]; the son of a couple from the [[Community areas in Chicago|North]] and [[South Side, Chicago|South Sides]] of [[Chicago]].<ref name="NYT">{{Cite news |last=Jackson |first=Mitchell S. |date=2022-12-27 |title=Kendrick Lamar’s Unconstrained Next Chapter |language=en-US |work=The New York Times |url=https://www.nytimes.com/2022/12/27/magazine/kendrick-lamar-dave-free.html |access-date=2022-12-27 |issn=0362-4331}}</ref> He is the youngest of three children.<ref name="NYT" /> Friley is a childhood friend of rapper [[Kendrick Lamar]], having met while they were both in high school.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Yoo |first=Noah |date=2019-10-04 |title=Dave Free No Longer Co-President of Top Dawg Entertainment: Report |url=https://pitchfork.com/news/dave-free-no-longer-co-president-of-top-dawg-entertainment-report/ |access-date=2022-08-18 |website=Pitchfork |language=en-US}}</ref> After graduating, he worked as a computer technician while helping Lamar begin his rap career.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Greenburg |first=Zack O'Malley |title=Meet Dave Free, Kendrick Lamar's 30 Under 30 Manager |url=https://www.forbes.com/sites/zackomalleygreenburg/2016/01/04/meet-dave-free-kendrick-lamars-30-under-30-manager/ |access-date=2022-08-18 |website=Forbes |language=en}}</ref> He used his job to showcase Lamar's music to Anthony "Top Dawg" Tiffith, founder and CEO of independent record label [[Top Dawg Entertainment]] (TDE), which led to him signing a [[recording contract]] with the label in 2007.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Droke |first=Carolyn |date=2019-10-05 |title=Dave Free Reportedly Steps Down As Top Dawg Entertainment’s President |url=https://uproxx.com/music/dave-free-leaves-top-dawg-entertainment/ |access-date=2022-08-18 |website=UPROXX |language=en-US}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |date=2011-01-06 |title=Kendrick Lamar: The West Coast Got Somethin' To Say |url=https://hiphopdx.com/interviews/id.1641/title.kendrick-lamar-the-west-coast-got-somethin-to-say |access-date=2022-08-18 |website=HipHopDX}}</ref>

== Career ==
Shortly after Lamar signed with TDE, Free joined the label as an in-house producer. He became a member of the hip hop production team [[Digi+Phonics]] alongside [[Sounwave]], [[Willie B (producer)|Willie B]] and Tae Beast and worked significantly on projects for the hip hop supergroup [[Black Hippy]], composed of Lamar, [[Jay Rock]], [[Schoolboy Q]] and [[Ab-Soul]].<ref>{{Cite news |last=Ducker |first=Eric |date=2014-07-23 |title=A Rational Conversation: The Sound Of TDE's Success |language=en |work=NPR |url=https://www.npr.org/sections/therecord/2014/07/23/334492009/a-rational-conversation-the-sound-of-tdes-success |access-date=2022-11-12}}</ref> Their production work received critical praise; ''[[Complex Networks|Complex]]'' named Digi+Phonics as one of the top 25 new producers to watch out for in November 2012.<ref>{{Cite web |title=25 New Producers To Watch Out For |url=https://www.complex.com/music/2012/11/25-new-producers-to-watch-out-for/ |access-date=2022-11-12 |website=Complex |language=en}}</ref> [[BET]] named them as one of the top ten young producers on the rise in July 2013.<ref>{{Cite web |title=10 Young Producers On the Rise |url=https://www.bet.com/photo-gallery/nti147/10-young-producers-on-the-rise/5ykijb |access-date=2022-11-12 |website=BET |language=en}}</ref>

Free's business career began as the social media director for TDE.<ref>{{Cite web |title=More Levels, The Brains Behind Top Dawg Entertainment [September 2012 Story] - XXL |url=https://www.xxlmag.com/tde-sidebar-kendrick-coverage/ |access-date=2022-08-18 |website=XXL Mag |language=en}}</ref> In 2007, he was named the label's co-president alongside [[Punch (rapper)|Terrence "Punch" Henderson]].<ref>{{Cite web |title=Kendrick Lamar Addresses Macklemore Text, Will Reportedly Release His New Album In September |url=https://www.complex.com/tag/kendrick-lamar |access-date=2022-08-18 |website=Complex |language=en}}</ref> Free subsequently became Lamar's manager during his tenure and co-directed several of his studio albums and accompanying music videos under the collective name The Little Homies.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Phillips |first=Yoh |title=The Little Homies Have the Best Creative Vision in Rap |url=https://djbooth.net/features/2016-04-07-the-little-homies-video-breakdown |access-date=2022-08-18 |website=DJBooth |language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |title=Here’s How Kendrick Lamar And Dave Free Became Super Duo ‘The Little Homies’ |url=https://www.mtv.com/news/mpcmpj/kendrick-lamar-dave-free-little-homies |access-date=2022-08-18 |website=MTV |language=en}}</ref> Their filmmaking work earned praise and led to the duo winning the [[Grammy Award for Best Music Video]] and the [[MTV Video Music Awards]] for [[MTV Video Music Award for Best Hip Hop Video|Best Hip Hop Video]] and [[MTV Video Music Award for Video of the Year|Video of the Year]].<ref>{{Cite web |date=2018-01-28 |title=Grammy Awards Winners: The Complete List |url=https://variety.com/2018/music/news/2018-grammys-winners-list-music-awards-1202676802/ |access-date=2022-08-18 |website=Variety |language=en-US}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |last=Berry |first=Peter A. |title=Kendrick Lamar's 'Humble' Wins Best Hip-Hop at 2017 MTV Video Music Awards - XXL |url=https://www.xxlmag.com/kendrick-lamar-humble-wins-best-hip-hop-2017-mtv-video-music-awards/ |access-date=2022-08-18 |website=XXL Mag |language=en}}</ref>

On October 4, 2019, ''[[Rolling Stone]]'' reported that Free had left TDE and was working independently. He was also helping rapper [[Baby Keem]] as he started gaining recognition and expressed his intention of "getting heavy into the film game."<ref>{{Cite web |last=Leight |first=Elias |date=2019-10-04 |title=Dave Free Leaves Top Dawg Entertainment |url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-news/top-dawg-entertainment-dave-free-split-894781/ |access-date=2022-08-18 |website=Rolling Stone |language=en-US}}</ref> On March 5, 2020, Free and Lamar launched the "multi-lingual" entertainment company [[PGLang]].<ref>{{Cite web |last=Mamo |first=Heran |date=2020-03-05 |title=Kendrick Lamar Announces Mysterious ‘pgLang’ Project: What Does It All Mean? |url=https://www.billboard.com/music/rb-hip-hop/kendrick-lamar-pglang-company-announcement-9328964/ |access-date=2022-08-18 |website=Billboard |language=en-US}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |last=Holmes |first=Charles |date=2020-03-05 |title=Kendrick Lamar Launches… Something (And Says It's Not a Record Label) |url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-news/kendrick-lamar-dave-free-pglang-962892/ |access-date=2022-08-18 |website=Rolling Stone |language=en-US}}</ref> Through the company, Free has written and directed several music videos and advertising campaigns for [[Calvin Klein (fashion house)|Calvin Klein]], [[Converse (shoe company)|Converse]] and [[Cash App]].<ref>{{Cite web |last=Dunn |first=Frankie |date=2021-01-14 |title=Kendrick Lamar’s pgLang has collaborated with Calvin Klein |url=https://i-d.vice.com/en_uk/article/4ad7p3/kendrick-lamar-pglang-dave-free-calvin-klein-collaboration |access-date=2022-08-18 |website=i-D |language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |last=Inman |first=DeMicia |date=2022-05-04 |title=A Closer Look At The “pgLang For Converse” Collaboration |url=https://www.vibe.com/gallery/pglang-converse-sold-out-collaboration-1234661029/ |access-date=2022-08-18 |website=VIBE.com |language=en-US}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |title=Kendrick Lamar Links With Billionaire Ray Dalio for New pgLang-Produced Video Promoting Financial Literacy |url=https://www.complex.com/music/kendrick-lamar-ray-dalio-pglang-financial-literacy-video |access-date=2022-08-18 |website=Complex |language=en}}</ref> On January 13, 2022, Free and Lamar announced they were producing an untitled comedy film with ''[[South Park]]'' creators [[Trey Parker]] and [[Matt Stone]] for [[Paramount Pictures]].<ref>{{Cite web |last=Grobar |first=Matt |date=2022-01-13 |title=Kendrick Lamar, Dave Free & ‘South Park’ Duo Matt Stone And Trey Parker To Produce Comedy Penned By Vernon Chatman For Paramount
