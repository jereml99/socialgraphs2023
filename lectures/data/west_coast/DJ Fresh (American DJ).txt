'''Marqus Brown''', known as '''DJ.Fresh''', is an American [[Hip hop music|hip hop]] [[Disc jockey|DJ]] based in [[Oakland, California|Oakland]] and Los Angeles, California.<ref name="abouthome" /> Acclaim for his work includes being named to [[XXL (magazine)|''XXL'' magazine]]'s 30 Best Hip-Hop Producers of 2016.<ref name="XXL2016">{{Citation | last = Weinstein | first = Max | title = 30 of the Best Hip-Hop Producers of 2016| newspaper = [[XXL (magazine)|XXL]] | date = December 29, 2016 | url = http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2016/12/best-hip-hop-producers-2016/ | access-date = May 6, 2017}}</ref> In 2006 DJ.Fresh began releasing his ''The Tonite Show'' series of albums, which have featured artists such as [[Yukmouth]] and [[Raekwon]]. He has also released a number of mixtapes and has produced albums for other artists. His 2013 collaboration with [[J. Stalin]], ''[[Miracle & Nightmare on 10th Street]]'', peaked at #61 on the [[R&B/Hip-Hop Albums]] chart and at #28 on the [[Heatseekers Albums]] chart.<ref name="allmusicawards" />

== Early life==
Marqus Brown was born and raised in [[Baltimore, Maryland]].<ref name="abouthome"/> While an adolescent in Baltimore he became immersed in [[hip hop culture]], and he started mixing vinyl on [[turntables]] at age nine.<ref name="qalou"/> Among his early musical influences were [[Soul music|soul]], [[Hip hop music|hip hop]], and [[jazz]] artists popular in the 1970s and 1980s, including [[Anita Baker]], [[Whitney Houston]], and [[the Gap Band]].<ref name="qalou"/>

He moved with his mother and siblings to the [[San Francisco Bay Area]] in California<ref name="shospital"/> at nine years old.<ref name="abouthome"/> The crew battled and performed around the Bay Area.<ref name="qalou"/>

== Music career ==
=== Early years ===
[[File:The World's Freshest (DJ Fresh) - photo 2.jpg|thumb|right|250px|DJ.Fresh mixing live]]
DJ.Fresh's first major break was DJing at a show in [[San Francisco]] with [[Common (rapper)|Common]] at the age of 18, and shortly afterwards he Deejayed on two tours for the rapper [[Nas]].<ref name="qalou"/> He began producing his own music as well, creating beats with turntables and a [[digital audio workstation]].<ref name="abouthome"/> In 1999 he was named the 3rd best DJ in the US by ITF (International Turntablist Federation).<ref name="abouthome"/>

While he initially mixed with the moniker DJ Fresh, he adopted the name the Worlds Freshest (DJ.Fresh) to avoid confusion with [[DJ Fresh]], the English producer of the same name.<ref name="qalou"/>

=== ''The Tonite Show'' series ===
In 2005 DJ.Fresh started "The Tonite Show" album series, where he produces albums with guest artists that have since included [[Freddie Gibbs]], [[the Jacka]], [[The Grouch (rapper)|the Grouch]], [[J-Stalin]], [[Yukmouth]], [[Mitchy Slick]], [[E-40]],<ref name="abouthome"/> and [[Raekwon]].<ref name="qalou"/>

His 2014 ''Tonite Show'' release with [[Trae Tha Truth]] received a positive review from RapReviews.com, who stated the album "shows glimpses of a nice little partnership brewing".<ref name="rr"/>

=== Recent production ===
In 2006 DJ.Fresh began producing for a number of major artists, starting with the album ''The Real World West Oakland'' by [[J. Stalin]]. In the next two years he worked on albums by [[Shady Nate]] and [[Bicasso]] as well, and that later extended to artists such as [[Mitchy Slick]].<ref name="dubcnnetminan"/> He won Best West Coast Producer in 2009&ndash;10.<ref name="abouthome"/> As of 2010 he ran the Whole Shabang production team, with producers such as Jamon Dru, Mr. Tower, Sneaky Mike, [[Face the Music (music ensemble)|Face the Music]], Mike Rimzo, Scandal Beats & Young Gully.<ref name="shospital"/>

[[File:The World's Freshest (DJ Fresh) - photo 1.jpg|right|thumb|250px|DJ.Fresh]]
''[[Miracle & Nightmare On 10th Street]]'' is a double disc collaboration album between [[J. Stalin]] and DJ.Fresh, released on July 16, 2013. It peaked at #61 on the [[R&B/Hip-Hop Albums]] chart and at #28 on the Heatseekers Albums chart.<ref name="allmusicawards"/>

He also released the album ''Feet Match the Paint'' with [[Mitchy Slick]] in 2013,<ref name="siccnessinterview"/> and in 2014 he released his ''Boomin' System'' Mixtape, which has guest artists such as [[Scarface (rapper)|Scarface]] and [[Kendrick Lamar]]. The mixtape was received positively, and a review by ''Complex'' praised the variety and unpredictability of the production styles.<ref name="complexreview"/>

== Style, equipment ==
DJ.Fresh incorporates a variety of styles in his production, including [[Hip hop music|hip hop]], [[Electronic dance music|EDM]], [[House music|house]], [[jazz]], and [[Contemporary R&B|R&B]], though he self-identifies as more of an R&B than a hip hop producer.<ref name="qalou"/> He uses analog equipment including [[turntables]] and [[Gramophone record|vinyl]], and a [[MIDI controller]] [[Keyboard instrument|keyboard]]. He also uses [[Ableton Live]] and occasionally brings in live instrumentation.<ref name="qalou"/>
