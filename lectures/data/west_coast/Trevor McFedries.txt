'''Trevor McFedries'''<ref name="Food">{{cite news | last =Rodbard | first =Matt | title =Yung Skeeter Sends In His 5 Favorite Music Videos Featuring Food [NSFW] | publisher = Food Republic| date =4 December 2012 | url =http://www.foodrepublic.com/2012/12/04/yung-skeeter-sends-his-5-favorite-music-videos-fea | access-date =29 November 2014}}</ref><ref name="Rolling">{{cite news | title =10 New Artists You Need to Know: August 2014 | publisher = [[Rolling Stone]]| date =August 2014 | url =https://www.rollingstone.com/music/lists/10-new-artists-you-need-to-know-august-2014-20140823/alvvays-20140823 | access-date =29 November 2014}}</ref> (born November 27, 1985),  (formerly known as '''Yung Skeeter''') is a musician who worked as a [[DJ]], [[Record producer|producer]], and director for acts including [[Ke$ha]], [[Azealia Banks]], [[Katy Perry]], [[Chris Brown]], [[Steve Aoki]], and others.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.refinery29.com/en-us/30-under-30-los-angeles#page-22|title = 30 Under 30 - Rising Stars in Los Angeles|website=Refinery29.com}}</ref> He toured alongside Katy Perry on her 2011 [[California Dreams Tour]].<ref name="listenlookandlive.blogspot.com">{{Cite web|url=http://listenlookandlive.blogspot.com/2013/02/interview-yung-skeeter.html|title = INTERVIEW: Yung Skeeter|website=Listenlookandlive.blogspot.com}}</ref> McFedries has also performed at music festivals [[Lollapalooza]] and [[Coachella Valley Music and Arts Festival|Coachella]], served as an entrepreneur-in-residence at [[Bad Robot]], and was an early employee at [[Spotify]].<ref name="listenlookandlive.blogspot.com"/> He is the co-founder and CEO of technology startup Brud and the creator of virtual pop star [[Lil Miquela]].

He currently sits on the boards of the [[Southern California Institute of Architecture]] “SCI-Arc” and Rhizome, the Digital Artwork arm of [[The New Museum]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.sciarc.edu/news/2020/trevor-mcfedries-joins-sci-arc-board&sa=D&source=docs&ust=1643821269802839&usg=AOvVaw3_EgRYKKO_G0YFrAYI3yea|title=Transmedia Innovator and Cultural Worldbuilder Trevor McFedries Joins SCI-Arc Board - SCI-Arc|website=Sciarc.edu|access-date=3 February 2022}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://twitter.com/rhizome/status/1445758480552398856|title=We're also growing our Board of Directors! We're thrilled to have these four newly involved with Rhiz (though they are all longtime friends and colleagues)|website=Twitter.com|access-date=3 February 2022}}</ref>

==Early life==
McFedries grew up in [[Davenport, Iowa|Davenport]], [[Iowa]].<ref name="LLL">{{cite news | last =R | first =Leanne | title =INTERVIEW: Yung Skeeter | publisher = Listen, Look and Live| date =8 February 2013 | url =http://listenlookandlive.blogspot.com/2013/02/interview-yung-skeeter.html | access-date =29 November 2014}}</ref><ref name="MySpace">{{cite news | last =Culley | first =Liz | title =ARTIST OF THE DAY: Yung Skeeter Dreams Big | publisher = [[MySpace]]| date =18 September 2013 | url =https://myspace.com/article/2013/09/17/yung-skeeter-next-to-me-max-motif-dim-mak-rvca-banks| access-date =29 November 2014}}</ref> In [[high school]], he was a "football star"<ref name="Worst">{{cite book | last =Niccum | first =Jon | title =Worst Gig: From Psycho Fans to Stage Riots, Famous Musicians Tell All | publisher =[[Sourcebooks]] | date =1 October 2013 | url = https://books.google.com/books?id=seAUAgAAQBAJ&q=%22yung+skeeter%22+%22katy+perry%22&pg=PT79 | isbn =978-1402284953 }}</ref> and enjoyed playing basketball.<ref name="R29">{{cite news | last =Keyes | first =Sarah | title =30 Under 30: L.A.'s Bright Young Things | publisher = [[Refinery29]]| date =24 July 2013 | url =http://www.refinery29.com/30-under-30-los-angeles#page-22 | access-date =29 November 2014}}</ref> He moved with his family to [[Los Angeles]] when he was 16.<ref name="Nooz">{{cite news | last =Mann | first =Paul | title =Paul Mann: Hot New DJ Skeet Skeet Blazing a Trail | publisher = Noozhawk| date =26 August 2011 | url =http://www.noozhawk.com/article/082611_paul_mann_hot_new_dj_skeet_skeet_blazing_a_trail/| access-date =29 November 2014}}</ref> He earned a college scholarship to play football at [[San Jose State Spartans Football|San Jose State]] but dropped out of college and moved back to LA.<ref name="Beatport">{{cite web |url=http://www.beatport.com/artist/yung-skeeter/291914 |title=Yung Skeeter|author=<!--Staff writer(s); no by-line.--> |website=Beatport.com |publisher=[[Beatport]] |access-date=29 November 2014}}</ref> Los Angeles has been his "home base" since that time. He started DJing at the age of 19.<ref name="Nooz"/>

==Music career==
McFedries took the stage name DJ Skeet Skeet, and in 2007 he was introduced to [[Shwayze]] and [[Cisco Adler]].<ref name="Joon">{{cite news | last =Carletto | first =Tina | title =Inside The Mind of Yung Skeeter | publisher = Joonbug| date =21 November 2012 | url =http://joonbug.com/national/frequency/Inside-The-Mind-of-Yung-Skeeter/teZGp46mf5Z| access-date =29 November 2014}}</ref> The three created a partnership that would culminate in the 2008 release of [[Shwayze (album)|a self-titled album]]. The album made it to the top 10 on the [[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] charts with two songs ("[[Buzzin' (Shwayze song)|Buzzin']]" and "[[Corona and Lime (song)|Corona and Lime]]") that charted on the [[Billboard Hot 100]] list.<ref name="Bill"/> The popularity of the trio led to the creation of a short-lived [[reality television]] series on [[MTV]] called ''[[Buzzin' (TV series)|Buzzin']]''.<ref name="Paper02">{{cite news | title =It's the "Best Summer Ever!!!!!" For Cisco Adler and Skeet Skeet of Shwayze | publisher = [[Paper (magazine)|Paper Magazine]]| date =23 July 2008 | url =http://www.papermag.com/2008/07/catching_up_with_cisco_adler_a.php| access-date =29 November 2014}}</ref> Also in 2008, DJ Skeet Skeet was given the "People's Choice Award for Best DJ" by ''Paper Magazine''.<ref name="Paper01">{{cite news | last =Craig | first =Carolina Torem| author2 = Alexis Swerdloff | title =About Last Night... PAPER's Fourth Annual Nightlife Awards Presented by Diesel | publisher = [[Paper (magazine)|Paper Magazine]]| date =17 November 2008 | url =http://www.papermag.com/2008/11/about_last_night_papers_fourth.php| access-date =29 November 2014}}</ref> He also became the first DJ to perform at the entire slate of shows on the [[Warped Tour]] in 2008.<ref name="Worst"/>

By 2011, he had worked with acts like Chris Brown, [[Ke$ha]], [[3OH!3]], [[Bonde do Rolê]], and [[Sky Ferreira]], creating remixes of their tracks and producing various songs.<ref name="Nooz"/> In 2011, he also opened for Katy Perry on her California Dreams arena tour,<ref name="LLL"/> sharing a stage with both Perry and [[Robyn]].<ref name="TV">{{cite news | title =Go backstage on the California Dreams tour with DJ Skeet Skeet! | publisher = [[Teen Vogue]]| date =August 2011| url =http://www.teenvogue.com/entertainment/music/2011-08/dj-skeet-skeet-tour-diary/?slide=1| access-date =29 November 2014}}</ref> McFedries and Perry had been friends long before the tour.<ref name="Nooz"/> During this time, he worked and as a [[A&R]] at [[Photo Finish Records]].<ref name="Alt">{{cite news | last =Meyrick| first =Richard | title =DJ Skeet Skeet joins Photo Finish Records to help spearhead A&R operations!| publisher = [[AltSounds]]| date =11 August 2011 | url =http://hangout.altsounds.com/news/133017-dj-skeet-skeet-joins-photo-finish-records.html| access-date =29 November 2014}}</ref><ref name="Vegas">{{cite news | title =Yung Skeeter to Spin at Bazaar: Under The Eiffel Tower in Las Vegas June 30 | publisher = Vegas News| date =23 June 2012| url =http://www.vegasnews.com/76084/yung-skeeter-to-spin-at-bazaar-under-the-eiffel-tower-in-las-vegas-june-30.html| access-date =29 November 2014}}</ref>

In 2012, he decided to change his stage name to "Yung Skeeter," He produced and provided beats for various songs for other artists. McFedries made his directorial debut in 2012, directing the music videos for Steve Aoki's "Control Freak" and [[NERVO (duo)|NERVO's]] "[[We're All No One]]."<ref name="BangTidy">{{cite news | title =Yung Skeeter | publisher = Bang Tidy Music| date =30 November 2012| url =http://www.bangtidymusic.co.uk/2012/11/30/interview-yung-skeeter/ | access-date =29 November 2014}}</ref>

Starting in 2012, McFedries has been an Artist Advocate at Spotify alongside [[D.A. Wallach]] of [[Chester French]].<ref name="LLL"/><ref name="Guardian">{{cite news | last =Dredge | first =Stuart | title =How Spotify and its digital music rivals can win over artists: 'Just include us' | newspaper =[[The Guardian]]| date =29 October 2013 | url =https://www.theguardian.com/technology/2013/oct/29/spotify-amanda-palmer-songkick-vevo| access-date =29 November 2014}}</ref> His relationship with Steve Aoki (who runs Dim Mak Records) vaulted Skeeter into a position hosting a radio show on [[iHeartRadio]] for Dim Mak Records. In 2013, he became a spokesman for VitaminWater alongside acts like [[B.o.B.]], [[Matt and Kim]], and [[Santigold]]. He performed with these three acts in [[Boring, Oregon|Boring]], [[Oregon]] as part of VitaminWater's "Make Boring Brilliant" campaign. He has also appeared in ads for the company.<ref name="Fuse">{{cite news | title =Brilliance Accomplished: B.o.B, Matt & Kim, Santigold and Yung Skeeter Help Make Boring Brilliant | publisher =[[Fuse (TV channel)|Fuse]]| date =4 April 2013 | url =http://www.fuse.tv/2013/04/vitaminwater-brilliance-accomplished | access-date =29 November 2014}}</ref>

BANKS' debut album, ''[[Goddess (Banks album)|Goddess]]'', was executive produced by Yung Skeeter.<ref name="Guardian02">{{cite news | last =Hutchinson| first =Kate | title =Banks: 'I'm being as honest as I can'  | newspaper = [[The Guardian]]| date =30 August 2014 | url =https://www.theguardian.com/music/2014/aug/30/banks-goddess-lil-silver-jimmy-kimmel| access-date =29 November 2014}}</ref> He also produced "Wallace" on Azealia Banks' 2014 album, ''[[Broke with Expensive Taste]]''.<ref name="Muse">{{cite news | last =Stern| first =Bradley | title ='Broke With Expensive Taste': Azealia Banks Puts Her Money Where Her Mouth Is (Album Review) | website= MuuMuse.com| date =10 November 2014 | url =http://www.muumuse.com/2014/11/broke-with-expensive-taste-azealia-banks-puts-her-money-where-her-mouth-is-album-review.html/| access-date =29 November 2014}}</ref>

==Brud==
In addition to his musical career, McFedries worked for Bad Robot, the production company of J.J. Abrams.<ref name="Lil Miquela's Body Con Job">{{Cite web|url=https://www.thecut.com/2018/05/lil-miquela-digital-avatar-instagram-influencer.html|title = Lil Miquela's Body Con Job|website=Thecut.com|date = 11 May 2018}}</ref> In 2016, McFedries co-founded the tech company Brud<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.businessoffashion.com/community/people/trevor-mcfedries-sara-decou#:~:text=Trevor+McFedries+&+Sara+Decou+are,including+Chanel,+Burberry+and+Fendi.|title=Trevor McFedries & Sara Decou is part of the BoF 500|website=The Business of Fashion|access-date=3 February 2022}}</ref> which is as a “transmedia company” that produces digital personas utilized to create digital story-driven worlds. McFedries is the company’s CEO.<ref name="labusinessjournal.com">{{Cite web|url=https://labusinessjournal.com/news/2019/mar/08/virtual-influencer-maker-raises-nearly-20m/|title=Virtual Influencer Maker Raises Nearly $20M &#124; Los Angeles Business Journal|website=Labusinessjournal.com|access-date=3 February 2022}}</ref>

Of their creations has been Miquela, a CGI character and model that uses social media in order to detail her life, which includes a mixtures of fictional and non-fictional environments.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2019/06/17/business/media/miquela-virtual-influencer.html|title=These Influencers Aren't Flesh and Blood, Yet Millions Follow Them|newspaper=The New York Times|date=17 June 2019|last1=Hsu|first1=Tiffany}}</ref> They initially created Miquela anonymously,<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.vox.com/the-goods/2019/6/3/18647626/instagram-virtual-influencers-lil-miquela-ai-startups|title = Virtual influencers have got to be a fad — right?|website=Vox.com|date = 3 June 2019}}</ref> only later revealing themselves as her creator. They have also created other characters to interact with Miquela as well as other parts of social media,<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.refinery29.com/en-gb/bermuda-instagram-cgi-influencer|title = Meet Bermuda, the Most Controversial CGI Influencer on Instagram|website=Refinery29.com}}</ref>  including Bermuda—whom McFedries created under an in-story faux rival company before revealing Brud had also created her.

In 2018 McFedries and Brud received $6 million in funding from Sequoia Capital,<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://techcrunch.com/2018/04/23/the-makers-of-the-virtual-influencer-lil-miquela-snag-real-money-from-silicon-valley/|title = The makers of the virtual influencer, Lil Miquela, snag real money from Silicon Valley|website=Techcrunch.com}}</ref> and that year their creation became one of Time magazine’s “Most Influential People on the Internet”.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://time.com/5324130/most-influential-internet/|title=Meet This Year's 25 Most Influential People on the Internet|website=Time.com|access-date=3 February 2022}}</ref> In 2019, investments raised by his company had totaled $25.5 million, with a valuation of $144.5 million.<ref name="labusinessjournal.com"/> Through his fundraising, McFedries and his cofounders have stated that they would only accept funding from investment firms where either a woman or a person of color was in a position of authority.<ref name="Lil Miquela's Body Con Job"/>

On October 4, 2021 “Brud Inc”  was acquired by Dapper Labs for an undisclosed amount.<ref>[https://techcrunch.com/2021/10/04/nft-startup-dapper-labs-acquires-virtual-influencer-startup-brud/&sa=D&source=docs&ust=1643821269804660&usg=AOvVaw1G_iquNHo3mA8p6ff37rqK] {{dead link|date=February 2022}}</ref> The acquisition sees Trevor become the CEO of “Dapper Collectives” a new org that will be “Mainstreaming DAOs.” The first DAO that Dapper Collectives will bring to life is “Brud DAO” the decentralization of Brud Inc. that will allow for the community of fans and creators to own the project and guide it alongside the Brud team.  In the acquisition McFedries stated “Really, what we're going to be doing is building tools for DAOs, storytellers, and creators to come and build out the future in web3 with us on the Flow blockchain.” <ref>{{Cite web|url=https://avc.com/2021/10/dapper-collectives/|title=Dapper Collectives|website=Avc.com|date=4 October 2021|access-date=3 February 2022}}</ref>

==Friends with Benefits==
Friends With Benefits is an [[Ethereum]] based DAO created by McFedries in September 2020 and is considered to be the first Social DAO.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.theguardian.com/commentisfree/2021/nov/14/exclusive-clubs-called-daos-are-popping-up-online-whats-it-all-about|title=An exclusive type of club called 'Dao' is popping up online. What's it all about? &#124; Geoff Mak|date=14 November 2021|newspaper=[[The Guardian]]|access-date=3 February 2022}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://economicmotivation.blog/2021/11/18/businesses-i-shouldve-started/|title=Businesses I Should've Started|website=Economicmotivation.blog|date=19 November 2021|access-date=3 February 2022}}</ref>

==Art & A.I.==
McFedries was featured in Rhizome’s 7x7<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://rhizome.org/editorial/2019/mar/26/announcing-seven-on-seven-2019-participants-details/|title = Seven on Seven 2019|website=Rhizome.org}}</ref> program as a leader at the intersection of art and technology, as well as NYC Media Lab<ref>{{cite AV media |url-status = live |archive-url = https://ghostarchive.org/varchive/youtube/20211211/gw_ExoR-tHs |archive-date = 2021-12-11| url = https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gw_ExoR-tHs |title = Oct 9: Fireside Chat With Trevor McFedries and Aria Dean |website=[[YouTube]]}}{{cbignore}}</ref> where he discussed the future of storytelling, art, and A.I. with artist Aria Dean. McFedries spoke with Berkeley Art + Design<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://artsdesign.berkeley.edu/design/events/trevor-mcfedries-in-conversation-with-aria-dean-0|title = Trevor McFedries: In Conversation with Aria Dean |website=Artsdesign.berkeley.edu}}</ref> about how narrative and attitude make an avatar relatable and authentic.

==Politics==
McFedries was a campaign surrogate for Bernie Sanders 2020 presidential campaign and fundraised for George Gascón<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://consequence.net/2015/09/musicians-sign-their-support-for-bernie-sanders/|title=Thurston Moore, Jeff Tweedy, and 70 other musicians sign their support for Bernie Sanders|website=consequence.net|date=19 September 2015}}</ref> in his run to become District Attorney of Los Angeles County.
