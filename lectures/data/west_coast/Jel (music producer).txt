'''Jeffrey James Logan'''<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.sfweekly.com/sanfrancisco/unsettled-jel-and-the-anticon-label-celebrate-15-years-of-weirdo-hip-hop/Content?oid=2827310|title=Unsettled: Jel and the Anticon Label Celebrate 15 Years of Weirdo Hip-Hop |work=[[SF Weekly]]|first=Ian S.|last=Port|date=September 4, 2013|access-date=August 22, 2015}}</ref> (born May 7, 1978),<ref name=birthday>{{cite web|url=https://twitter.com/jelanticon/status/992806175124025344|title=Jel Day Blow Out!!!|work=[[Twitter]]|author=Jel|date=May 5, 2018|access-date=May 7, 2018}}</ref> better known by his stage name '''Jel''', is an American hip hop producer and rapper. He is co-founder of the record label [[Anticon]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://alarm-magazine.com/2012/review-jels-greenball-3-5/|title=Review: Jel's Greenball 3.5|work=Alarm|first=Meaghann|last=Korbel|date=April 26, 2012|access-date=August 22, 2015}}</ref> He has been a member of Presage,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.cityweekly.net/utah/themselves/Content?oid=2142387|title=Themselves - Two of a Kind: Doseone and Jel stay true to Themselves.|work=[[Salt Lake City Weekly]]|first=Reyan|last=Ali|date=December 9, 2009|access-date=August 22, 2015}}</ref> [[Themselves]], [[Subtle (band)|Subtle]], and [[13 & God]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.laweekly.com/music/jel-is-the-mr-miyagi-of-beat-machines-5436992|title=Jel Is the Mr. Miyagi of Beat Machines|work=[[LA Weekly]]|first=Peter|last=Holslin|date=March 18, 2015|access-date=August 22, 2015}}</ref>

==Biography==
Jel released his first solo album, ''[[10 Seconds (album)|10 Seconds]]'', on [[Mush Records]] in 2002. The album is titled after the limited sampling time of [[E-mu SP-1200]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://pitchfork.com/reviews/albums/4232-10-seconds/|title=Jel: 10 Seconds|work=[[Pitchfork (website)|Pitchfork]]|first=Brad|last=Haywood|date=May 16, 2002|access-date=August 22, 2015}}</ref> It features contributions from [[Dax Pierson]], [[Odd Nosdam]], [[Doseone]], and [[Alias (musician)|Alias]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://exclaim.ca/Reviews/HipHop/jel-10_seconds|title=Jel - 10 Seconds|work=[[Exclaim!]]|first=Thomas|last=Quinlan|date=May 2002|access-date=August 22, 2015}}</ref>

His second solo album, ''[[Soft Money (album)|Soft Money]]'', was released on [[Anticon]] in 2006. The album features contributions from [[Dosh (musician)|Dosh]], [[Wise Intelligent]] of [[Poor Righteous Teachers]], and Stefanie Böhm of [[Ms. John Soda]], among others.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://pitchfork.com/reviews/albums/4233-soft-money/|title=Jel: Soft Money|work=[[Pitchfork (website)|Pitchfork]]|first=Brian|last=Howe|date=May 9, 2006|access-date=August 22, 2015}}</ref>

He released his third solo album, ''[[Late Pass]]'', on Anticon in 2013.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.theskinny.co.uk/music/records/305848-jel_late_pass|title=Jel - Late Pass|work=[[The Skinny (magazine)|The Skinny]]|first=Bram E.|last=Gieben|date=September 5, 2013|access-date=August 22, 2015}}</ref>

==Style and influences==
Jel is primarily a producer. He is known for his use of [[E-mu SP-1200|SP-1200]] and [[Music Production Center#MPC2000XL|MPC2000XL]] to create drum beats with little or no sequencing like playing the drums live via the sampler pads.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://alarm-magazine.com/2012/qa-jel/|title=Q&A: Jel|first=Meaghann|last=Korbel|work=Alarm|date=April 20, 2012|access-date=August 22, 2015}}</ref> He has produced numerous tracks for underground hip hop artists such as [[Deep Puddle Dynamics]], [[Atmosphere (music group)|Atmosphere]], [[Sole (hip hop artist)|Sole]], [[Sage Francis]],<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.stylusmagazine.com/reviews/jel/10-seconds.htm|title=Jel - 10 Seconds|work=[[Stylus Magazine]]|first=Todd|last=Burns|date=September 1, 2003|access-date=August 22, 2015|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160304045848/https://www.stylusmagazine.com/reviews/jel/10-seconds.htm|archive-date=March 4, 2016}}</ref> and [[Serengeti (rapper)|Serengeti]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://pitchfork.com/reviews/albums/16870-car/|title=Serengeti: C.A.R.|work=[[Pitchfork (website)|Pitchfork]]|first=Jonah|last=Bromwich|date=July 31, 2012|access-date=August 22, 2015}}</ref>

Jel is also a rapper. He provided vocals on ''Soft Money''<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.allmusic.com/album/soft-money-mw0000351564|title=Soft Money - Jel|work=[[AllMusic]]|first=David|last=Jeffries|access-date=April 26, 2014}}</ref> and ''Late Pass''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.spin.com/2013/08/jel-late-pass-stream-anticon-odd-nosdam/|title=Jel's 'Late Pass' Is a Dubwise Boom-Bap Dream|work=[[Spin (magazine)|Spin]]|first=Chris|last=Martins|date=August 22, 2013|access-date=August 29, 2018}}</ref> In a 2006 interview with ''[[The Skinny (magazine)|The Skinny]]'', he said: "What inspires me lyrically is television conversations and dope MCs."<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.theskinny.co.uk/clubs/interviews/jel-anticon-founders-time-to-shine|title=Jel - Anticon Founder's Time to Shine|work=[[The Skinny (magazine)|The Skinny]]|first=Bram|last=Gieben|date=February 15, 2006|access-date=August 29, 2018}}</ref>
