'''Nappy Nina''' is an American rapper and producer. Raised in [[Oakland, California|Oakland]], she performed [[Poetry slam|slam poetry]] as an emcee in her adolescence. She moved to Brooklyn, New York in 2012 and has released the albums ''Naptime'', ''Extra Ordinary'', ''The Tree Act'', and ''Dumb Doubt''.<ref name="grammylookingglass" />

== Early life ==
Born in [[Oakland, California|Oakland]], California, she spent part of her childhood there before moving to nearby [[Union City, California|Union City]].<ref name="amadeus" /> Her father, Greg Bridges, is a popular local journalist and jazz radio host.<ref name="amadeus">{{Cite web|last=Vadi|first=Jose|date=2017-08-04|title=Nappy Nina: From Oakland Mainstay to Brooklyn Breakout|url=http://amadeusmag.com/blog/nappy-nina-oakland-roots-brooklyn-mainstay/|url-status=live|access-date=2021-03-03|website=amadeus|language=en-US}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|last=Byrne|first=Madeleine|date=March 2020|title=A Tree Grows in Brooklyn|url=https://reader.exacteditions.com/issues/86720/spread/1|url-status=live|access-date=2021-03-04|website=The Wire}}</ref> Because of her father's work in music she spent time around local musicians like Eesu Orundide and [[D'wayne Wiggins]] of [[Tony! Toni! Toné!]].<ref name="amadeus" /> Bridges played several different instruments growing up, which included piano and jazz clarinet.

As a teenager, she got involved in slam poetry through Youth Works and rapped in her slam performances.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|last=Crumpton|first=Taylor|date=2020-02-13|title=Rapper Nappy Nina Defies Uncertainty on "Dumb Doubt"|url=https://daily.bandcamp.com/features/nappy-nina-feature|url-status=live|access-date=2021-03-04|website=Bandcamp Daily}}</ref> In 2012, Nappy Nina moved to [[Brooklyn]] with $500 to pursue music.<ref name=":0" /> She aspired to become one of the few nationally-recognized rappers from Oakland.<ref name=":0" />

== Career ==
Nappy Nina released her debut EP ''Naptime'' in 2015.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2019-02-01|title=Brooklyn-Via-Oakland Rapper Nappy Nina Shares New LP "The Tree Act"|url=https://indiecurrent.com/nappy-nina-shares-the-tree-act/|access-date=2021-03-04|website=IndieCurrent|language=en-US}}</ref> Her next EP ''Extra Ordinary'' was released in 2017.<ref name="bitch2017">{{Cite web|last=Wakefield|first=Jamara Mychelle|date=2017-11-09|title="Extra Ordinary" is a Continuation of Black Radical Tradition|url=https://www.bitchmedia.org/article/extra-ordinary/nappy-nina|url-status=live|access-date=2021-03-03|website=Bitch Media|language=en}}</ref> In a positive review, Jamara Mychelle Wakefield wrote for ''[[Bitch (magazine)|Bitch]]'': "''Extra Ordinary'' is a quality manifesto from a new artist who wanted to prove herself as a “capable and worthy mc.” Nappy Nina’s debut EP floats in the air between Oakland and Brooklyn—refined like [[coconut oil]], but also rough and biting."<ref name="bitch2017" /> José Vadi of ''Amadeus Magazine'' referred to it as "a project as diverse in sonic structure and cadence as it is in content matter and lyrical depth."<ref name="amadeus" />

Nappy Nina's rhymes frequently cover political and social issues, as well as her perspectives as a Black queer woman.<ref name=":0" /> Her delivery was described as "smooth-sultry...that's almost jazz-like" by Pendarvis Harshaw of [[KQED (TV)|KQED]].<ref name=":1">{{Cite web|title=Musicians To Know: Nappy Nina on Becoming the Rap Bill Nye and Missing Oakland|url=https://www.kqed.org/arts/13891520/musicians-to-know-nappy-nina-has-bars-on-finance-therapy-and-toni-morison|access-date=2021-03-04|website=KQED|date=22 January 2021 |language=en-us}}</ref>

Her album ''The Tree Act'' (2019) was reviewed as combining a "righteous pen game with ace production" by [[Okayplayer]]'s Karas Lamb.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Lamb|first=Karas|date=2019-01-28|title=Mixtape Monday: friendkerrek, Nappy Nina, slenderbodies, Swindle + More|url=https://www.okayplayer.com/music/best-mixtapes-of-the-week-january-28th-download.html|url-status=live|access-date=2021-03-04|website=Okayplayer|language=en-US}}</ref>

In 2020 she released the album ''Dumb Doubt,'' which Taylor Crumpton of [[Bandcamp]] Daily said "marries airy, breathable beats with soulful jazz and Black radical tradition themes."<ref name=":0" /> That year she was the featured artist on the track "Money Can't Buy" on [[Yaeji]]'s debut mixtape ''What We Drew'' 우리가 그려왔던.<ref name="newyorker">{{Cite magazine|last=Hsu|first=Hua|date=2020-03-20|title=Yaeji's New Mixtape Is the Opposite of Social Distancing|url=https://www.newyorker.com/magazine/2020/04/06/yaejis-new-mixtape-is-the-opposite-of-social-distancing|url-status=live|access-date=2021-03-03|magazine=The New Yorker|language=en-us}}</ref> She also produced the track "Weight" with [[Mexican Summer]]'s Looking Glass digital music series for Brooklyn-based artists affected by the [[COVID-19 pandemic]].<ref name="grammylookingglass">{{Cite web|last=Velez|first=Jennifer|date=2020-10-29|title=Looking Glass Series Drops Nappy Nina's "Weight"|url=https://www.grammy.com/grammys/news/mexican-summer-continues-looking-glass-series-latest-nappy-nina-weight-release|url-status=live|access-date=2021-03-04|website=GRAMMY.com|language=en}}</ref>

On January 1, 2021, Nappy Nina released the track "Pandemy Stimmy" with Stas Thee Boss. Nina plans to release an EP titled ''Double Down'' in 2021.<ref name=":1" /> On March 5, 2021, she released the single "The Real Tea" featuring Stas Thee Boss, which Philippe Roberts of Pitchfork reviewed as "formed of equal parts fear and fury, “The Real Tea” finds balance in contradiction, airing grievances with an eye on the future."<ref name="pitchfork-therealtea">{{cite web |last1=Roberts |first1=Philippe |title=The Ones: Nappy Nina's "The Real Tea" [ft. Stas Thee Boss] |url=https://pitchfork.com/thepitch/the-ones-nappy-ninas-the-real-tea-ft-stas-thee-boss/ |website=Pitchfork |date=5 March 2021 |access-date=5 March 2021}}</ref>

== Personal life ==
Nappy Nina is queer.<ref name=":0" />
