'''Stefan Kendal Gordy''' (born September 3, 1975), better known by his [[stage name]] '''Redfoo''', is an American rapper, singer, songwriter, record producer, and DJ best known for being part of the musical duo [[LMFAO]], and for their hit songs "[[Party Rock Anthem]]" and "[[Sexy and I Know It]]". LMFAO released two studio albums, "[[Party Rock]]" in 2009, and "[[Sorry for Party Rocking]]" in 2011. Redfoo released his debut solo album ''[[Party Rock Mansion]]'' in 2016.

===2006–12: Breakthrough with LMFAO===
[[File:LMFAO Presented by Debit Mastercard (6430209423).jpg|thumb|140px|left|Redfoo during the [[LMFAO]]'s [[Sorry for Party Rocking Tour]], in 2011]]
He formed the electro-pop band LMFAO with his nephew [[Sky Blu (rapper)|Sky Blu]] in 2006. The duo started building a local buzz through their shows and radio play.<ref name="hitquarters">{{cite web |url=http://www.hitquarters.com/index.php3?page=intrview/opar/intrview_JacobsonN.html |title=Interview with Neil Jacobson (part 1) |publisher=[[HitQuarters]] |date=June 18, 2012 |access-date=August 26, 2012 |archive-date=November 18, 2015 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20151118234416/http://www.hitquarters.com/index.php3?page=intrview%2Fopar%2Fintrview_JacobsonN.html |url-status=live }}</ref> Once they had recorded some demos, Redfoo's friend [[will.i.am]] introduced them to [[Interscope]] head [[Jimmy Iovine]], who gave the green light for them to be signed to Interscope/will.i.am Music.<ref name="hitquarters"/> LMFAO released the debut album, ''[[Party Rock]]'' on July 7, 2009. The duo then recorded their second album, ''[[Sorry for Party Rocking]]'', late in 2010 and released it on June 17, 2011, in the U.S. The first single taken from the album, "[[Party Rock Anthem]]", was released on January 1, 2011. The song is so far the most successful of their career, peaking at number one in the United States,<ref>{{Cite magazine|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/469349/lmfao-brings-the-party-to-top-of-hot-100|title=LMFAO Brings the 'Party' to Top of Hot 100|magazine=Billboard|first=Gary|last=Trust|date=July 6, 2011|access-date=July 6, 2011|archive-date=November 30, 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20201130060550/https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/469349/lmfao-brings-the-party-to-top-of-hot-100|url-status=live}}</ref> Canada, the UK and over ten other countries, as well as being in the top ten in many others. The third single, "[[Sexy and I Know It]]", was released on October 3, 2011, and reached number one on the iTunes charts worldwide and number one on the Australian and Canadian Hot 100.

Also in 2011, LMFAO embarked on the world tour [[Sorry for Party Rocking Tour]].<ref name="asiantour">{{cite web |url=http://mistazam.blogspot.com/2011/01/lmfao-live-in-malaysia-philippines.html |title=LMFAO Live in Malaysia, Philippines & Singapore |publisher=mistazam.com |access-date=April 22, 2011 |archive-date=October 26, 2021 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20211026183847/http://blog.mistazam.com/2011/01/lmfao-live-in-malaysia-philippines.html |url-status=live }}</ref> On February 5, 2012, the group appeared with [[Madonna (entertainer)|Madonna]] at the [[Super Bowl XLVI]] during the [[List of Super Bowl halftime shows|Bridgestone Halftime Show]]. Also in 2012, Redfoo was sued by a previous management company for $7 million, claiming breach of contract.<ref>TMZ Staff, "LMFAO The $7 Million SURPRISE at American Idol", tmz.com, April 20, 2012</ref> On September 21, 2012, the duo released a statement announcing their hiatus. Redfoo said, "I feel like we've been doing this for so long, five or six years", and that he and Sky Blu would not be performing together anytime soon as they would be taking their careers in different directions.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://news.lalate.com/2012/09/21/lmfao-breakup-denied-split-will-be-temporary/|title=LMFAO's Redfoo, SkyBlu Split|date=September 21, 2012|publisher=B96 Chicago|access-date=August 18, 2013|archive-date=November 13, 2013|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131113160643/http://news.lalate.com/2012/09/21/lmfao-breakup-denied-split-will-be-temporary/|url-status=live}}</ref>

===2016–present: ''Party Rock Mansion''===
Redfoo announced his second album, ''[[Party Rock Mansion]]'', near the end of December 2015. He released a promotional single, "Lights Out", on January 15, 2016. The album was released on March 18, 2016, receiving mixed reviews from music critics, as well as being a commercial failure, with only 144 copies sold throughout Australia in its first week of release.

He has since released the singles "Brand New Day" and "Sock It to Ya" in 2017, and "Everything I Need" in 2018.

In 2020, his previously released single "New Thang" went viral on [[TikTok]], being used in many videos and gaining newfound traction.{{Citation needed|date=April 2022}}

In 2023, after being six years retired, came back with his new track "Long Live Party Rock" along with Dainjazone, which he released on his official YouTube channel on April 20.

He entered the USTA Northern California Sectional qualifying tournament in June and was knocked out 6–1, 6–2 in his first match.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://espn.go.com/tennis/story/_/id/9057523/stefan-redfoo-gordy-make-bid-play-us-open |title=Stefan 'Redfoo' Gordy to make bid to play in U.S. Open |publisher=ESPN |date=March 15, 2013 |access-date=July 29, 2014 |archive-date=October 27, 2014 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141027065517/http://espn.go.com/tennis/story/_/id/9057523/stefan-redfoo-gordy-make-bid-play-us-open |url-status=live }}</ref> Since 2018, he has been dating Jasmine Alkouri, a chef and road manager.

Redfoo, through his line of Party Rock clothing, sponsored the [[Red Rock Pro Open]], an [[ITF Women's Circuit]] tournament held in [[Las Vegas]], from 2012 to 2013. Tyler Weeks, a tennis pro at the [[Cosmopolitan of Las Vegas]], mentioned to Foo that his tournament needed a new sponsor after giving him a lesson, and Foo agreed for Party Rock clothing to be the new sponsor. The event is usually held at the end of September.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://tenniseastcoast.com/2012/08/28/musician-redfoo-backs-usta-pro-circuits-party-rock-open/|title=Musician Redfoo Backs USTA Pro Circuit's Party Rock Open|work=Tennis East Coast|access-date=November 9, 2012|archive-date=September 28, 2012|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120928171727/http://tenniseastcoast.com/2012/08/28/musician-redfoo-backs-usta-pro-circuits-party-rock-open/|url-status=live}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.usopen.org/en_US/news/articles/2012-08-30/201208301346362850695.html|title=LMFAO's Redfoo: Party rocking on the court|publisher=US Open|access-date=November 9, 2012|url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20121025111042/http://www.usopen.org/en_US/news/articles/2012-08-30/201208301346362850695.html|archive-date=October 25, 2012}}</ref>

Redfoo started coding in 2017 and has since hosted a three-day hackathon at his home.<ref name=code>{{cite web | url=https://afrotech.com/lmfao-redfoo-radix-coding | title=LMFAO's Redfoo Partners with Radix to Support Aspiring Coders — 'If I Can do It, Anyone Can do It' | date=July 14, 2022 }}</ref>  He is also vegan since 2017.{{cn|date=June 2022}}
