'''Kendrick Lamar Duckworth''' (born June 17, 1987) is an American rapper and songwriter.<!-- As per WP:Manual of Style/Lead section, the first sentence should only have occupations the subject is most notable for.--> Known for his [[Progressive rap|progressive]] musical styles and [[Social consciousness|socially conscious]] songwriting, he is considered one of the most influential hip hop artists of his generation and one of the greatest rappers of all time.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Gee |first=Andre |date=2019-12-30 |title=The 2010s: The Most Influential Rappers of the Decade |url=https://www.okayplayer.com/music/who-are-the-most-influential-rappers.html |access-date=2022-11-11 |website=[[Okayplayer]] |language=en-US |quote=Sonic experimentation, Afrocentric themes, and social commentary are in the fiber of popular music. And as “King Kendrick,” widely believed to be the best rapper of his generation, he played a huge part in that shift.}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |last=Weiss |first=Jeff |date=2019-11-18 |title=The Best Rappers of the 2010s |url=https://www.complex.com/music/best-rappers-of-the-2010s/ |access-date=2022-11-11 |website=[[Complex Networks|Complex]] |language=en |quote=A genius at writing dense literary missives that became massively popular anthems, he might be the closest analogue that rap has produced to Bob Dylan. Except there can only be one Kendrick Lamar, the one who made the times change.}}</ref><ref>Carl Lamarre, G. M., Lamarre, C., Mitchell, G., Murphy, K., Saponara, M., Thomas, D., Elibert, M., Diep, E., III, W. E. K., Mamo, H., & Rouhani, N. (2023, February 14). 50 greatest rappers of all time. Billboard. Retrieved February 14, 2023, from https://www.billboard.com/lists/best-rappers-all-time/</ref> Born and raised in [[Compton, California]], Lamar began his career as a teenager performing under the stage name K.Dot. He quickly garnered local attention which led to him signing a recording contract with [[Top Dawg Entertainment]] (TDE) in 2005.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Kendrick Lamar's Complete Discography – Tea & Weed |url=https://teaandweed.com/kendrick-lamar-discography/#YHNIC |access-date=March 23, 2022 |website=teaandweed.com|date=February 12, 2021}}</ref>

After becoming a founding member of the hip hop supergroup [[Black Hippy]], Lamar dropped his former stage name of '''K.Dot''' and started using his first and middle names professionally. In 2011, he released his debut studio album ''[[Section.80]]'', a [[Political hip hop#Conscious hip hop|conscious hip hop]] record. The album was met with positive reviews and included his debut single "[[HiiiPoWeR|HiiiiPower]]". In 2012, Lamar secured a record deal with [[Dr. Dre]]'s [[Aftermath Entertainment]], under the aegis of [[Interscope Records]], and released his second studio album ''[[Good Kid, M.A.A.D City]]''. The [[West Coast hip hop|West Coast]] and [[gangsta rap]] influenced album garnered widespread critical recognition and commercial success, while including the singles "[[Swimming Pools (Drank)]]", "[[Backseat Freestyle]]", and "[[Bitch, Don't Kill My Vibe]]".<ref>{{Cite web |date=December 8, 2012 |title=Kendrick Lamar: the rise of a good kid rapper in a mad city |url=http://www.theguardian.com/music/2012/dec/08/kendrick-lamar-good-kid-maaad-city |access-date=May 9, 2022 |newspaper=The Guardian |language=en}}</ref>

A visit to South Africa inspired Lamar's [[jazz]]-flavored third studio album ''[[To Pimp a Butterfly]]'' (2015).<ref>{{Cite web |title='To Pimp a Butterfly' remains an important display of the life and struggles of modern-day Black America |url=https://www.revolt.tv/article/2020-03-15/79685/to-pimp-a-butterfly-remains-an-important-display-of-the-life-and-struggles-of-modern-day-black-america/ |access-date=May 9, 2022 |website=REVOLT |language=en-US}}</ref> It received universal acclaim and became his first number-one album on the [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.metacritic.com/feature/best-albums-of-the-decade-2010s|title=Best Albums of the Decade|website=Metacritic|language=en|access-date=April 1, 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190406225640/https://www.metacritic.com/browse/albums/score/metascore/all/filtered|archive-date=April 6, 2019|url-status=live}}</ref> The same year, he topped the [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]] for the first time with the remix of "[[Bad Blood (Taylor Swift song)|Bad Blood]]" by [[Taylor Swift]].<ref>{{Cite magazine |last=Lipshutz |first=Jason |date=2015-05-27 |title=Kendrick Lamar Scores First Hot 100 No. 1 Thanks to Taylor Swift |url=https://www.billboard.com/pro/kendrick-lamar-bad-blood-first-no-1-single-taylor-swift/ |access-date=2022-11-09 |magazine=Billboard |language=en-US}}</ref> Lamar experimented with [[Contemporary R&B|R&B]], [[Pop music|pop]] and [[psychedelic soul]] in his fourth studio album ''[[Damn (Kendrick Lamar album)|Damn]]'' (2017). It spawned his first solo number-one single "[[Humble (song)|Humble]]" and became the first non-classical and non-jazz work to be awarded the [[Pulitzer Prize for Music]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.bbc.com/news/business-43789936|title=Kendrick Lamar wins Pulitzer music prize|last=Johnston|first=Chris|date=April 17, 2018|publisher=BBC News|access-date=May 31, 2018|language=en-GB|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180530000200/http://www.bbc.com/news/business-43789936|archive-date=May 30, 2018|url-status=live}}</ref> Following a four-year hiatus, Lamar released his fifth studio album ''[[Mr. Morale & the Big Steppers]]'' (2022), which served as his [[swan song]] from TDE.<ref>{{Cite news |last=Sisario |first=Ben |date=May 13, 2022 |title=Kendrick Lamar Returns With 'Mr. Morale & the Big Steppers' |language=en-US |work=The New York Times |url=https://www.nytimes.com/2022/05/13/arts/music/kendrick-lamar-new-album-mr-morale-and-the-big-steppers.html |access-date=May 13, 2022 |issn=0362-4331}}</ref> He has directed and produced several music videos and films with his creative partner [[Dave Free]], with whom he founded the creative collective [[PGLang]].

Having sold over 70 million records in the United States alone, all of Lamar's studio albums have been [[Music recording certification|certified platinum]] or higher by the [[Recording Industry Association of America]] (RIAA).<ref>{{Cite web |title=Gold & Platinum |url=https://www.riaa.com/gold-platinum/ |access-date=May 9, 2022 |website=RIAA |language=en-US}}</ref> He has received [[List of awards and nominations received by Kendrick Lamar|numerous accolades]] throughout his career, including 17 [[Grammy Awards]], a [[Primetime Emmy Awards|Primetime Emmy Award]], two [[American Music Awards]], six [[Billboard Music Awards|''Billboard'' Music Awards]], 11 [[MTV Video Music Awards]], a [[Brit Awards|Brit Award]], and a nomination for an [[Academy Awards|Academy Award]]. He was named [[MTV's Hottest MCs in the Game|MTV's Hottest MC in the Game]] in 2012.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.mtv.com/news/articles/1703255/hottest-mcs-2013-kendrick-lamar.jhtml |title=Kendrick Lamar Brings Crown To Compton As 'Hottest MC in the Game |publisher=MTV |date=March 7, 2013 |access-date=March 7, 2013 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140312012725/http://www.mtv.com/news/articles/1703255/hottest-mcs-2013-kendrick-lamar.jhtml |archive-date=March 12, 2014 |url-status=live }}</ref> In 2015, Lamar received the [[California State Senate]]'s Generational Icon Award. He has been featured in listicles such as the [[Time 100|''Time'' 100]] and [[Forbes 30 Under 30|''Forbes'' 30 Under 30]].<ref name="time 100" /><ref>{{Cite web |last=Greenburg |first=Zack O'Malley |title=Kendrick Lamar, Conscious Capitalist: The 30 Under 30 Cover Interview |url=https://www.forbes.com/sites/zackomalleygreenburg/2017/11/14/kendrick-lamar-conscious-capitalist-the-30-under-30-cover-interview/ |access-date=2022-10-05 |website=Forbes |language=en}}</ref> Three of his studio albums were included on ''[[Rolling Stone]]''{{'}}s 2020 ranking of the [[Rolling Stone's 500 Greatest Albums of All Time|500 Greatest Albums of All Time]].

== Early life ==
Kendrick Lamar Duckworth was born on June 17, 1987, in [[Compton, California]], to Paula Oliver, a [[McDonald's]] worker, and Kenneth "Kenny" Duckworth, a [[KFC]] worker.<ref>{{cite web |author=Kendrick Lamar |url=https://www.biography.com/people/kendrick-lamar-21349281 |title=Kendrick Lamar - Rapper, songwriter |access-date=June 23, 2017 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170519124500/http://www.biography.com/people/kendrick-lamar-21349281 |archive-date=May 19, 2017 |url-status=live }}</ref><ref>{{cite web |author=Biography by Andy Kellman |url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/kendrick-lamar-mn0002709646/biography |title=Kendrick Lamar {{pipe}} Biography & History |website=AllMusic |date=June 17, 1987 |access-date=June 23, 2017 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170707213649/http://www.allmusic.com/artist/kendrick-lamar-mn0002709646/biography |archive-date=July 7, 2017 |url-status=live }}</ref> His parents were from the [[South Side, Chicago|South Side of Chicago]] and relocated to Compton three years before his birth due to his father's affiliation with the [[Gangster Disciples]].<ref>{{Cite magazine |last=Edwards |first=Gavin |date=2015-01-09 |title=Billboard Cover: Kendrick Lamar on Ferguson, Leaving Iggy Azalea Alone and Why 'We're in the Last Days' |url=https://www.billboard.com/music/music-news/kendrick-lamar-billboard-cover-story-on-new-album-iggy-azalea-police-violence-the-rapture-6436268/ |url-status=live |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20211125023601/https://www.billboard.com/music/music-news/kendrick-lamar-billboard-cover-story-on-new-album-iggy-azalea-police-violence-the-rapture-6436268/ |archive-date=2021-11-25 |access-date=2023-04-27 |magazine=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] |language=en-US}}</ref> Lamar is the eldest of four children and was named after singer-songwriter [[Eddie Kendricks]] of [[the Temptations]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=Did You Know Kendrick Lamar Was Named After One Of The Temptations? ==Career==
===2003–2009: Career beginnings===
{{Main articles|C4 (mixtape)|Kendrick Lamar (EP)}}
In 2003, at the age of 16, Lamar released his first full-length project, a [[mixtape]] titled ''Youngest Head Nigga in Charge (Hub City Threat: Minor of the Year)'', under the pseudonym K.Dot.<ref name="hiphopdx1">{{Cite web |last= |first= |date=2011-01-06 |title=Kendrick Lamar: The West Coast Got Somethin' To Say |url=https://hiphopdx.com/interviews/id.1641/title.kendrick-lamar-the-west-coast-got-somethin-to-say |access-date=2022-10-17 |website=HipHopDX}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |date=2013-04-15 |title=Kendrick Lamar's first mixtape surfaces online; download it now |url=https://www.factmag.com/2013/04/15/kendrick-lamars-first-mixtape-surfaces-online-download-it-now/ |access-date=2022-05-12 |website=Fact Magazine |language=en-US}}</ref> The mixtape was released under Konkrete Jungle Muzik and garnered local recognition for Lamar.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.thatnewjam.com/kendrick-lamar-track-1-hova-intro-freestyle/ |title=Kendrick Lamar - Track 1 (Hova Intro Freestyle) |publisher=That New Jam |date=March 5, 2013 |access-date=October 14, 2015 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20151117014239/http://www.thatnewjam.com/kendrick-lamar-track-1-hova-intro-freestyle/ |archive-date=November 17, 2015 }}</ref> The mixtape led to Lamar securing a [[recording contract]] with [[Top Dawg Entertainment]] (TDE), a newly founded [[independent record label]] based in [[Carson, California]].<ref name="hiphopdx1"/> He began recording material with the label and subsequently released a 26-track mixtape two years later, titled ''Training Day'' (2005).<ref>[http://www.ozonemag.com/2010/06/23/issue-84-patiently-waiting-kendrick-lamar/ Ozone Magazine » Issue #84 Patiently Waiting » Issue #84 – Patiently Waiting: Kendrick Lamar] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110606070128/http://www.ozonemag.com/2010/06/23/issue-84-patiently-waiting-kendrick-lamar/|date=June 6, 2011}}. Ozonemag.com (June 23, 2010). Retrieved May 3, 2011.</ref>

Throughout 2006 and 2007, Lamar would appear alongside other up-and-coming West Coast rappers, such as [[Jay Rock]] and [[Ya Boy]], as [[opening act]]s for veteran West Coast rapper [[The Game (rapper)|The Game]]. Toward the end of last year he released the first single off the album titled "i" which also touches on Christian faith. |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150908020326/http://www.christianpost.com/news/were-in-the-last-days-i-truly-in-my-heart-believe-that-says-kendrick-lamar-132450/ |archive-date=September 8, 2015 |url-status=live }}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://noisey.vice.com/en_uk/article/jpzppp/kendrick-lamar-damn-spiritual-reawakening-religion|title=Kendrick's 'DAMN.' === Philanthropy and activism ===
[[File:Kendrick Lamar Obama Janelle Monáe 2016.jpg|thumb|right|Lamar (left) at the 2016 [[White House]] [[Independence Day (United States)|Independence Day]] celebration with President [[Barack Obama]] (center) and singer [[Janelle Monáe]] (right)]]In November 2011, Lamar headlined a [[Benefit concert|charity concert]] benefitting Los Angeles' Downtown Women's Center, which addresses the needs of women battling homelessness and poverty in [[Skid Row, Los Angeles|Skid Row]], and Project RISHI, a non-profit organization fighting poverty in villages across [[India]].<ref>{{Cite web |last=Weiss |first=Jeff |date=2011-11-18 |title=Kendrick Lamar to play charity concert for Downtown Women's Center |url=https://www.latimes.com/archives/blogs/pop-hiss/story/2011-11-18/kendrick-lamar-to-play-charity-concert-for-downtown-womens-center |access-date=2023-07-19 |website=Los Angeles Times |language=en-US}}</ref> He voiced his support for [[Frank Ocean]] after he [[Coming out|came out]] via an [[open letter]], and criticized rappers who negatively reacted to the letter yet wear luxury clothing created by [[LGBT|LGBT+]] designers.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Horowitz |first=Steven |date=2012-08-13 |title=Kendrick Lamar Explains Why He Signed To Aftermath & Interscope |url=https://hiphopdx.com/news/id.20777/title.kendrick-lamar-explains-why-he-signed-to-aftermath-interscope |access-date=2023-07-19 |website=HipHopDX |language=en}}</ref> He donated to the [[American Red Cross]] in November 2012 to support victims of [[Hurricane Sandy]].<ref>{{Cite web |date=2016-09-20 |title=Activism & Charity: The Many Ways Kendrick Lamar Gives Back |url=https://www.globalcitizen.org/en/content/kendrick-lamar-global-citizen-festival-2016/ |access-date=2023-07-19 |website=Global Citizen |language=en}}</ref>

In the midst of the [[2012 United States presidential election|2012 U.S. presidential election]], Lamar stated that he doesn’t vote because "everything has a contradiction, everything is higher ranking and way beyond us, way beyond people."<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.bet.com/news/music/2012/08/27/kendrick-lamar-explains-why-he-doesn-t-vote.html|title=Kendrick Lamar Explains Why He Doesn't Vote|work=BET.com|access-date=August 22, 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170822054332/http://www.bet.com/news/music/2012/08/27/kendrick-lamar-explains-why-he-doesn-t-vote.html|archive-date=August 22, 2017|url-status=live}}</ref> He continued by noting, "When I say the president can't even control the world, then you definitely know there's something else out there pushing the buttons. They could do whatever they want to, we['re] all puppets."<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.vibe.com/2012/08/kendrick-lamar-says-no-voting/|title=Kendrick Lamar Says No To Voting|first=Jazmine|last=Gray|date=August 27, 2012|work=Vibe|access-date=September 22, 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170822054301/https://www.vibe.com/2012/08/kendrick-lamar-says-no-voting/|archive-date=August 22, 2017|url-status=live}}</ref> Lamar retracted his statements several days before the election following swift backlash. He told [[MTV News]] in November, "I think we just really need to take matters into our own hands, uplift our community, put money back in our community and show these kids how there's something different". He also shared that he would be voting for President [[Barack Obama]] because he believes his opponent, [[Mitt Romney]], doesn't have "a good heart at all."<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://hiphopwired.com/193041/kendrick-lamar-will-be-voting-for-barack-obama-says-mitt-romney-doesnt-have-a-good-heart-video/|title=Kendrick Lamar Will Be Voting For Barack Obama, Says Mitt Romney Doesn't Have A "Good Heart" [VIDEO] {{!}} Hip-Hop Wired|website=hiphopwired.com|date=November 4, 2012|language=en-US|access-date=August 22, 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170822054402/http://hiphopwired.com/193041/kendrick-lamar-will-be-voting-for-barack-obama-says-mitt-romney-doesnt-have-a-good-heart-video/|archive-date=August 22, 2017|url-status=live}}</ref>

In December 2013, Lamar donated $50,000 to Centennial High School's music department.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Jones |first=Tracy |date=2017-05-16 |title=Kendrick Lamar and Dr. Dre's High School Has One of Best Music Programs in the Country |url=https://www.laweekly.com/kendrick-lamar-and-dr-dres-high-school-has-one-of-best-music-programs-in-the-country/ |access-date=2023-07-19 |website=www.laweekly.com |language=en-US}}</ref> He embarked on a five-stop tour in 2014, with all proceeds benefitting [[Habitat for Humanity]] and his hometown of Compton.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Barnes |first=Tom |date=2014-06-04 |title=The $524,000 Reason Kendrick Lamar is the Most Humble Man in Hip-Hop |url=https://www.mic.com/articles/90411/the-524-000-reason-kendrick-lamar-is-the-most-humble-man-in-hip-hop |access-date=2023-07-19 |website=Mic |language=en}}</ref> Lamar is a vocal advocate for the [[Black Lives Matter]] movement, and "[[Alright (Kendrick Lamar song)|Alright]]" serves as the movement's unofficial anthem.<ref>{{Cite news |last=Coscarelli |first=Joe |date=2015-12-29 |title=Kendrick Lamar on the Grammys, Black Lives Matter and His Big 2015 |language=en-US |work=The New York Times |url=https://www.nytimes.com/2016/01/03/arts/music/kendrick-lamar-on-a-year-of-knowing-what-matters.html |access-date=2023-07-19 |issn=0362-4331}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news |last=Harris |first=Aisha |date=2015-08-03 |title=Has Kendrick Lamar Recorded the New Black National Anthem?
