'''Davood Ali Asgari''', better known by his stage name '''Locksmith''', is an American rapper from [[Richmond, California]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.rapreviews.com/interview/locksmith09.html |title=Locksmith Interview|website=Rapreviews.com |date=June 9, 2009 |access-date=July 25, 2010}}</ref> He is half of the former underground duo [[The Frontline]], which pioneered the "New Bay" resurgence movement in the [[San Francisco Bay Area|Bay Area]] in the 2000s.<ref name="SFG"/><ref name="EastBay2005"/>

==Background==
Locksmith was born and raised in [[Richmond, California]] in the neighborhood of Crescent Park.<ref name="SFG"/> His father is [[Persians|Persian]],<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.rapreviews.com/interview/locksmith09.html|title = RapReview Feature for June 9, 2009 - Locksmith Interview|website=Rapreviews.com}}</ref> who had immigrated from [[Iran]], and his mother was [[African American]] from [[Chicago]],<ref name="Kickmag">{{Cite web|url=https://www.kickmag.net/2012/09/13/locksmiths-keywords-interview/|title = Locksmith's Keywords (Interview)|website=Kivkmag.net|date = 13 September 2012}}</ref> originally from [[Tennessee]].<ref name="tcus2017"/> For the greater part of his childhood, he was raised predominantly by his mother's side of his family as his father was the only one from his Iranian side, until he had gotten older when other paternal family-members also arrived.<ref name="Kickmag"/> He has four older [[half-sibling]]s from his mother's previous marriage.<ref name="EastBay2005"/><ref name="Kickmag"/> Between the 80s and 90s, his sister, who is a decade his senior,<ref name="Kickmag"/> rapped as MC Spice and had opened up for the likes of [[E-40]], [[Blackalicious]] and [[Digital Underground]].<ref name="EastBay2017">{{cite web|url=https://www.eastbayexpress.com/oakland/locksmiths-odyssey/Content?oid=6499267 |title=Locksmith's Odyssey|website=Eastbayexpress.com  |date=April 26, 2017 |access-date=December 9, 2018}}</ref> In 2010, his mother died.<ref name="tcus2017">{{Cite web|url=https://www.thecomeupshow.com/2017/10/25/locksmith-universe-wants-move-quickly-try-never-move-hurry-move-quickly-precisely/|title=Locksmith: If you are overthinking you're not moving|website=Thecomeupshow.com|date=25 October 2017}}</ref>

Locksmith went to [[De Anza High School]] in Richmond,<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://richmondconfidential.org/2009/12/03/richmond-rapper-treading-own-path/|title=Richmond rapper treading own path|website=Richmondconfidential.org|date=3 December 2009}}</ref> and attended [[University of California, Berkeley]], where he graduated in [[African American Studies]].<ref name="SFW">{{cite web|url=http://www.sfweekly.com/shookdown/2015/07/08/local-rapper-locksmith-talks-new-album-therapeutic-lyrics-and-headlining-in-australia-before-the-bay-area |title=Locksmith Talks New Album, Therapeutic Lyrics, and Headlining in Australia Before the Bay Area |publisher=SF Weekly |date=July 8, 2015}}</ref> He played basketball during his youth, through which he met his childhood friend Left.<ref name="SFG"/> After graduating from school, the pair worked as mentors at the [[Contra Costa County|West Contra Costa]] Youth Service Bureau where they mentored teenagers and adolescents.<ref name="SFG">{{cite news|url=https://www.sfgate.com/bayarea/article/RICHMOND-VIOLENCE-FUELS-HIP-HOP-DUO-2659746.php |title=Richmond Violence Fuels Hip-Hop Duo |newspaper=[[San Francisco Gate]] |date=June 26, 2006}}</ref> He worked there, part-time,<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://itsjulisa.com/locksmith/ |title=Locksmith – the Julisa Magazine |access-date=2018-12-08 |archive-date=2018-12-09 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20181209125542/http://itsjulisa.com/locksmith/ |url-status=dead }}</ref> for a decade before moving on to concentrate on his solo music career.<ref name="SFW"/>

==Career==
===Battle rap===
Locksmith first gained notoriety outside of the [[San Francisco Bay Area|Bay Area]] as a battle rapper. In 2003, after beating the likes of [[Mistah FAB]] and A-Wolf on radio station [[KMEL|106.1 KMEL]],<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.rapmusicguide.com/cd/9929/the-frontline-the-bootleg-ii |title=The Frontline – The Bootleg II |website=Rapmusicguide.com |access-date=December 8, 2018}}</ref><ref name="MTV2003"/> he qualified for and became a contender on [[List of programs broadcast by MTV|MTV's ''MC Battle'']] joining a pool of 32 contenders, judged by [[Just Blaze]] and [[Kanye West]]. Locksmith was the only contender from the [[West Coast hip hop|West Coast]].<ref name="EastBay2005">{{cite web|url=https://www.eastbayexpress.com/oakland/new-bay-incarnate/Content?oid=1076605&showFullText=true |title=New Bay Incarnate: Richmond's Frontline is spearheading a West Coast rap renaissance |website=Eastbayexpress.com|date=February 2, 2005 |access-date=December 9, 2018}}</ref> The event was the first-ever televised battle rap tournament.<ref>Archived at [https://ghostarchive.org/varchive/youtube/20211211/p8dwUGUyJkI Ghostarchive]{{cbignore}} and the [https://web.archive.org/web/20130401023826/http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=p8dwUGUyJkI&gl=US&hl=en Wayback Machine]{{cbignore}}: {{cite AV media| url = https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=p8dwUGUyJkI| title = Locksmith Names His Top 5 Rappers in the Game on Sway in the Morning {{!}} Sway's Universe | website=[[YouTube]]}}{{cbignore}}</ref> Locksmith then made it to the "elite eight" [[live television|live telecast]] on May 17, judged by [[Method Man]], [[Ludacris]] and [[Kevin Liles]].<ref name="MTV2003">{{cite web|url=http://www.mtv.com/news/1471956/reignman-crowned-champion-of-mtvdef-jam-mc-battle/ |title=Reignman Crowned Champion of MTV/Def Jam 'MC Battle' |publisher=[[MTV News]] |date=May 19, 2003 |access-date=December 9, 2018}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.gettyimages.com/detail/news-photo/the-two-finalists-locksmith-and-reign-man-perform-at-the-news-photo/2008738|title = The two finalists Locksmith and Reign Man perform at the "MTV MC|website=Gettyimages.com}}</ref>  Despite his strong performance, he finished as the [[runner-up]] in the championship final decided by votes by viewers at home. The polling numbers and percentages were never shown. Speaking back on the upset, he states "I was a little surprised, but actually I expected it. Everybody kinda felt like there was a bias, which there was."<ref name="EastBay2005"/> Between late 2008 and 2009, he did a series of battles in Grind Time battling the likes of Dizaster, Jonny Storm, Passwurdz and Daylyt.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PL5B5D50168496D594 |title=Locksmith Battles |via=[[YouTube]]|access-date=December 8, 2018}}</ref>

===The Frontline===
Locksmith met his childhood friend Left on the basketball court of their junior high school.<ref name="SFG"/> In 1996, the two formed [[The Frontline]]. In 2002, the duo appeared on the ''Watch Out Now'' mixtape, along with several other Bay Area rap prospects, one of which was [[Oakland, California|Oakland]] rapper Balance.<ref name="EastBay2005"/> In December 2002, Locksmith and Balance appeared for the entire duration of a Saturday night broadcast of ''The Wake Up Show'' on KMEL, hosted by [[Sway Calloway]], [[King Tech]] and DJ Revolution. Astonished by their performances, Sway commented, "These cats don't sound like regular Bay Area dudes." To which, Balance responded, "It's a New Bay."<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.dubcnn.com/features/onthecomeup/balance/|title=dubcnn.com // West Coast News Network //|website=Dubcnn.com|access-date=24 May 2023}}</ref> Led by The Frontline and Balance at the forefront, the New Bay movement included [[The Team (band)|The Team]], Mistah FAB, [[The Federation (group)|The Federation]], Certified Ryders, [[San Quinn]], F.E., and Esinchill.<ref name="Dub2004">{{Cite web |url=http://www.baycentrik.com/feature.php?subaction=showfull&id=1212681555&archive=&start_from=&ucat=3& |title=:: Baycentrik .::. Centered around the bay with a worldwide reach |access-date=2008-10-28 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20081028150358/http://www.baycentrik.com/feature.php?subaction=showfull&id=1212681555&archive=&start_from=&ucat=3& |archive-date=2008-10-28 |url-status=live }}</ref> It gained further momentum in 2003 following Locksmith's appearance on MTV's ''MC Battle'' in which he shouted out the "New Bay" in front of a nationwide audience.<ref name="EastBay2005"/><ref name="Dub2004"/>

In February 2004, The Frontline signed a deal with [[E-A-Ski]]'s label IMG. Between 2002 and 2004, the duo had released three mixtapes. They released their debut [[studio album]], ''Who R You'', on October 12, 2004, which peaked at #48 on the ''Billboard'' [[Independent Albums]] chart.<ref>{{cite magazine|url=https://www.billboard.com/music/the-frontline/chart-history/independent-albums/song/462999 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20181215094550/https://www.billboard.com/music/the-frontline/chart-history/independent-albums/song/462999 |url-status=dead |archive-date=December 15, 2018 |title=Who R You |magazine=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] |date=October 2004}}</ref> On May 10, 2005, The Frontline released their second studio album, ''Now You Know'', which peaked at #46 on the Independent Albums chart.<ref>{{cite magazine|url=https://www.billboard.com/music/the-frontline/chart-history/independent-albums/song/479388 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20181215072932/https://www.billboard.com/music/the-frontline/chart-history/independent-albums/song/479388 |url-status=dead |archive-date=December 15, 2018 |title=Now U Know |magazine=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] |date=May 2005}}</ref> In 2007, they released their third and final album, titled ''Left & Lock'', on September 25.

===Solo career===
In March 2009, it was announced that Locksmith was working on a solo album titled ''Frank the Rabbit''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.dubcnn.com/2009/03/23/locksmith-is-back-as-frank-the-rabbit/ |title=Locksmith Is Back As "Frank the Rabbit" |website=Dubcnn.com |date=March 23, 2009 |access-date=December 12, 2018}}</ref> It was slated to be entirely produced by E-A-Ski.<ref>{{cite magazine|url=https://hiphopdx.com/news/id.8876/title.e-a-ski-comments-on-protege-locksmith-album |title=E-A-Ski Comments On Protege Locksmith, Album |magazine=[[HipHopDX]] |date=April 1, 2009 |access-date=December 12, 2018}}</ref>

It wasn't until he began his solo career in 2010, he started to cultivate a loyal fan base. Locksmith has toured with [[Joe Budden]], [[Murs (rapper)|Murs]], [[R.A. the Rugged Man]], [[Vinnie Paz]] of [[Jedi Mind Tricks]] and collaborated with producers [[9th Wonder]] and [[Ski (record producer)|Ski Beatz]]. Locksmith has been featured on [[MTV]], [[BET]], [[Sirius XM]]'s [[Shade 45]], [[WQHT|Hot 97]]'s "Who's Next?" concert series and all major hip hop blogs.<ref>Schwartz, Danny [http://www.hotnewhiphop.com/locksmith-might-be-the-best-conscious-rapper-in-the-game-new-video.33612.html Locksmith Might Be The Best Conscious Rapper In The Game After This Freestyle] ''HotNewHipHop.com''. July 7, 2015</ref><ref>Fleischer, Adam [http://www.mtv.com/news/1725482/locksmith-hardest-song-ever-sexual-abuse/ Locksmith Opens Up About Sexual Abuse On ‘Hardest Song Ever’] ''MTV''. July 8, 2015</ref><ref>Meara, Paul [http://hiphopdx.com/news/id.34458/title.rap-release-dates-chris-brown-the-game-locksmith-miguel Rap Release Dates: Chris Brown, The Game, Locksmith, Miguel] ''HipHopDX''. July 8, 2015</ref>

Locksmith released a series of mixtapes from 2010 to 2013.  His first official album ''A Thousand Cuts'' was released April 15, 2014.<ref>Ivey, Justin [http://theboombox.com/locksmith-freestyle-shade-45-kay-slay/ Locksmith Freestyles Over Gang Starr Instrumentals on Shade 45 with DJ Kay Slay] ''The Boom Box''. July 8, 2015</ref> Previously, Locksmith has collaborated with [[Funk Volume]]'s [[Hopsin]] and [[Jarren Benton]], as well as, [[Futuristic (rapper)|Futuristic]] and Apathy. His second album, ''Lofty Goals'', was  released on June 30, 2015.<ref>[http://uproxx.com/smokingsection/2015/06/locksmith-lofty-goals-album-stream/ Guilt-Free Listening: Stream Locksmith's ‘Lofty Goals’ Album] ''Uproxx.com''. July 7, 2015</ref> The album features Jarren Benton, Futuristic, [[Chris Webby]] and others.<ref>Ward, Mark [http://rhymejunkie.com/2015/07/10/locksmith-lofty-goals-album-stream/ Locksmith – Lofty Goals (Album Stream)] ''Rhyme Junkie''. July 25, 2015</ref>

In March 2015, Locksmith performed at the [[South by Southwest]] music festival in [[Austin, Texas]].<ref>Harrington, Jim [http://www.mercurynews.com/music/ci_27699510/sxsw-music-2015-heres-complete-lineup SXSW music 2015: Here's the complete lineup] ''San Jose Mercury News''. July 8, 2015</ref>

Locksmith released his 3rd album "Olive Branch" on March 31, 2017.
