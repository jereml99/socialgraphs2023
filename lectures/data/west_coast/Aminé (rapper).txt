'''Adam Aminé Daniel''' (born April 18, 1994) is an American rapper, singer, and songwriter. He first gained notability for his commercial debut [[Single (music)|single]], "[[Caroline (Aminé song)|Caroline]]", which peaked at number 11 on the US [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]] chart.<ref name="hot100">{{cite magazine|url=https://www.billboard.com/artist/amine/chart-history/hsi/|title=Aminé – Chart History: Billboard Hot 100|magazine=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|access-date=January 19, 2018}}</ref> Aminé released his debut studio album ''[[Good for You (album)|Good for You]]'', on July 28, 2017,<ref name="GFYAM">{{cite web |url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/good-for-you/id1252787331|title=Good For You by Aminé on Apple Music|date=July 28, 2017 |publisher=[[Apple Music]]|access-date=July 29, 2017}}</ref> and his second studio album, ''[[Limbo (Aminé album)|Limbo]]'', on August 7, 2020.

==Early life and education==
Aminé is the son of an [[Ethiopian]] father and a [[Demographics of Eritrea|Eritrean]] mother.<ref>{{cite web | url=https://americansongwriter.com/limbo-amine-album-interview/#:~:text=A%20first%2Dgeneration%20American%20and,first%2Dgeneration%20Eritrean%2DEthiopian | title=Aminé Shares How His Mom, City Influenced the Music on 'Limbo' | date=August 31, 2020 }}</ref><ref name=":02">{{Cite news |last=Caramanica |first=Jon |date=July 27, 2017 |title=Meet Aminé, a Joyful Rapper With an Eye on Politics |language=en-US |work=The New York Times |url=https://www.nytimes.com/2017/07/27/arts/music/amine-good-for-you-interview.html |access-date=August 4, 2017 |issn=0362-4331}}</ref><ref name="Introducing">{{cite news|last1=Singer|first1=Matthew|title=Introducing: Aminé|url=http://www.wweek.com/music/2016/03/22/introducing-amine/|access-date=October 24, 2016|work=Willamette Week|publisher=Mark Zusman|date=March 22, 2016}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=Rapper Aminé on pushing boundaries, wearing whiteface in the Trump era and his Coachella show|url=https://www.latimes.com/entertainment/music/la-et-ms-coachella-amine-20180420-story.html|date=April 20, 2018|website=Los Angeles Times|language=en-US|access-date=May 14, 2020}}</ref> Aminé's mother worked at a post office and his father was a teacher and translator part-time.<ref name=":0">{{Cite news|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2017/07/27/arts/music/amine-good-for-you-interview.html|title=Meet Aminé, a Joyful Rapper With an Eye on Politics|last=Caramanica|first=Jon|date=July 27, 2017|work=The New York Times|access-date=August 4, 2017|language=en-US|issn=0362-4331}}</ref> Aminé was born and raised in the [[Northeast Portland]] neighborhood of [[Woodlawn, Portland, Oregon|Woodlawn]]. He grew up speaking [[Amharic]] at home.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Aminé's World of Fun Is Good for All of Us |url=https://www.vice.com/en/article/3kdgx8/amines-culture-hopping-world-of-fun-is-good-for-all-of-us |access-date=2023-03-20 |website=www.vice.com |language=en}}</ref>

Aminé graduated from [[Benson Polytechnic High School]], and attended [[Portland State University]] to study [[marketing]] before dropping out to focus on his music career full time.<ref>{{Cite web
|title=Introducing: Aminé|url=https://www.wweek.com/music/2016/03/22/introducing-amine/|access-date=2021-01-31|website=Willamette Week|language=en-US}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.mcmenamins.com/events/168117-3-days-in-portland-featuring-amine|title=Crystal Ballroom - 3 Days in Portland featuring AMINE
|work=McMenamins|language=en|access-date=August 4, 2017}}</ref> Aminé  worked as an [[intern]] at the hip-hop publication ''[[Complex (magazine)|Complex]]''.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.vice.com/en_us/article/zmmk7y/amine-remembers-the-time-mero-made-fun-of-him-at-complex|title=Aminé Remembers the Time Mero Made Fun of Him at Complex|date=August 31, 2017|work=Vice|access-date=September 29, 2017|language=en-us}}</ref>

==Career==
===2014–2015: Early career and career beginnings===
On January 17, 2014, Aminé began his music career after releasing his debut [[mixtape]], ''Odyssey to Me''. On September 4, 2014, Aminé also went on to release his debut [[extended play]], titled ''En Vogue''. On August 31, 2015, Aminé followed suit with his second mixtape titled, ''Calling Brío''.<ref name=Introducing/><ref>{{cite magazine|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/lists/10-new-artists-you-need-to-know-november-2016-w449519/amine-w449621|title=Aminé - 10 New Artists You Need to Know: November 2016|magazine=[[Rolling Stone]]|access-date=January 20, 2018}}</ref>

===2016–2017: Breakthrough with "Caroline" and ''Good for You''===
{{main|Caroline (Aminé song)|Good for You (album)}}
On March 9, 2016, Aminé released his debut [[Single (music)|single]], titled "[[Caroline (Aminé song)|Caroline]]". Aminé went on to release his self-directed music video for "Caroline" through his [[Vevo]] channel on [[YouTube]] on June 1, 2016.<ref>{{cite AV media |date=June 1, 2016 |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3j8ecF8Wt4E |title=Aminé - Caroline (Official Video) |publisher=AmineVEVO |via=[[YouTube]] |access-date=November 14, 2021 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20211031221237/https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3j8ecF8Wt4E |archive-date=October 31, 2021 |url-status=live}}</ref> The song debuted at number 96 on the US [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]] chart and would later rise to number 11.<ref name="hot100"/> In June 2017, the song was certified three-times platinum by the [[Recording Industry Association of America|RIAA]].<ref name="RIAA">{{cite web|url=https://www.riaa.com/gold-platinum/?tab_active=default-award&se=Amine#search_section|title=Gold & Platinum - RIAA|publisher=[[Recording Industry Association of America]]|access-date=April 6, 2017}}</ref>

In August 2016, Aminé partnered with [[Republic Records]]. Aminé went on to release his second single under Republic Records, titled "Baba" on November 4, 2016, following the success of "Caroline".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/baba-single/1172013103|title=Baba - Single by Aminé on Apple Music|date=November 4, 2016 |publisher=iTunes|access-date=January 20, 2018}}</ref> On November 15, 2016, Aminé performed  "Caroline" on ''[[The Tonight Show Starring Jimmy Fallon]]''.<ref>{{cite AV media |date=January 7, 2017 |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0eOxa39PSF4 |title=Aminé - Caroline (Live on The Tonight Show with Jimmy Fallon) |publisher=AmineVEVO |via=[[YouTube]] |access-date=November 14, 2021 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210924231423/https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0eOxa39PSF4 |archive-date=September 24, 2021 |url-status=live}}</ref>

On March 9, 2017, Aminé released a new single titled, "REDMERCEDES".<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/rap-music/new-music/2017/03/amine-redmercedes/|title=Amine Takes a Ride in His "RedMercedes" on New Song|website=XXL|access-date=August 5, 2017}}</ref> The music video for the song was released on April 7, 2017, through his Vevo channel on YouTube.<ref>{{cite AV media |date=April 7, 2017 |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=O8lFeVfYw_s |title=Aminé - REDMERCEDES (Official Video) |publisher=AmineVEVO |via=[[YouTube]] |access-date=November 14, 2021 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20211112131933/https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=O8lFeVfYw_s |archive-date=November 12, 2021 |url-status=live}}</ref> The song had been praised for being influenced by early 2000s rap, such as songs by [[Missy Elliott]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://prntscr.com/ev2t8i|title=Screenshot|website=Lightshot|language=en|access-date=April 11, 2017}}</ref> The official [[remix]] of the song features vocals from Missy Elliott and [[AJ Tracey]].<ref>{{cite web|last=Berry|first=Peter|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2017/05/missy-elliott-amine-redmercedes-remix/|title=Missy Elliott and AJ Tracey Jump on Amine's 'RedMercedes' Remix|work=[[XXL (magazine)|XXL]]|date=May 19, 2017|access-date=January 20, 2018}}</ref> The remix was released to [[iTunes]] on May 26, 2017.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/redmercedes-remix-feat-missy-elliott-aj-tracey/1238888409|title=REDMERCEDES (Remix) [feat. Missy Elliott & AJ Tracey - Single by Aminé on Apple Music|date=May 26, 2017 |publisher=iTunes|access-date=January 19, 2018}}</ref> Aminé went on to release another single on the same day titled, "Heebiejeebies" which features vocals from [[Kehlani]].<ref>{{cite web|last=Berry|first=Peter|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2017/05/amine-kehlani-collaborate-heebiejeebies/|title=Amine and Kehlani Collaborate on "Heebiejeebies"|work=XXL|date=May 26, 2017|access-date=January 20, 2018}}</ref>

Aminé was named as one of the ten members of ''[[XXL (magazine)|XXL's]]'' "2017 Freshman Class" on June 13, 2017, alongside rappers [[A Boogie wit da Hoodie]], [[PnB Rock]], [[Playboi Carti]], [[Ugly God]], [[Kyle (rapper)|Kyle]], [[MadeinTYO]], [[Kamaiyah]], [[Kap G]], and [[XXXTentacion]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2017/06/2017-xxl-freshman-cover/|title=XXL Freshman 2017 Cover Revealed|publisher=XXL|date=June 13, 2017|access-date=June 24, 2017}}</ref> Aminé went on to release the single "Turf" three days later.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://hypebeast.com/2017/6/amine-turf-charlie-wilson|title=Aminé Connects With Charlie Wilson for New Single "Turf"|publisher=HypeBeast|date=June 16, 2017|access-date=January 20, 2018}}</ref>

On June 22, 2017, Aminé announced and revealed the album's title and cover of his debut studio album titled ''[[Good for You (album)|Good for You]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2017/06/offset-nelly-features-amine-good-for-you-album-tracklist/|title=Offset, Nelly Featured on Amine's 'Good for You' Album Tracklist|website=XXL|access-date=July 17, 2017}}</ref> Leading up to the release of the album, Aminé released the singles "Blinds" and "Wedding Crashers" featuring vocals from rapper [[Offset (rapper)|Offset]].<ref>{{cite web|last=Dandridge-Lemco|first=Ben|url=http://www.thefader.com/2017/06/30/amine-blinds-single-good-for-you-album|title=Listen To Aminé's New Single "Blinds"|work=[[The Fader]]|date=June 30, 2017|access-date=January 20, 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|last=Craighead|first=Olivia|url=http://www.thefader.com/2017/07/21/amine-offset-wedding-crashers|title=Aminé Teams Up With Offset For "Wedding Crashers"|work=The Fader|date=July 21, 2017|access-date=January 20, 2018}}</ref> ''Good for You'' was released on July 28, 2017.<ref name="GFYAM"/> The album debuted at number 31 on the US ''[[Billboard 200]]'' chart with first-week sales of 13,000 copies first week.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://hitsdailydouble.com/sales_plus_streaming|title=HITS Daily Double|access-date=August 8, 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170807134919/http://hitsdailydouble.com/sales_plus_streaming|archive-date=August 7, 2017}}</ref> Following the release of ''Good for You'', Aminé released the single "Squeeze" on October 21, 2017.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/squeeze/1299372442|title=Squeeze - Single by Aminé on Apple Music|date=October 27, 2017 |publisher=iTunes|access-date=January 20, 2018}}</ref>

===2018–2020: ''OnePointFive'' and ''Limbo''===
{{main|OnePointFive|Limbo (Aminé album)}}

Injury Reserve) - Single by Aminé on Apple Music|publisher=iTunes|date=April 6, 2018|access-date=April 6, 2018}}</ref> After teasing a 2018 project release, the project, titled ''[[OnePointFive]]'', was announced on August 14, 2018, alongside the cover art, tracklist and its release date. The album was released to the public on August 15.<ref>{{cite tweet |author=Aminé |author-link=Aminé |user=heyamine |number=1029444007447818240 |date=August 14, 2018 |title=TONIGHT https://t.co/C2NmkqPNyH |language=en |access-date=November 14, 2021 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20201109020013/https://twitter.com/heyamine/status/1029444007447818240 |archive-date=November 9, 2020 |url-status=live}}</ref><ref>{{cite tweet |author=Aminé |author-link=Aminé |user=heyamine |number=1029515562898997251 |date=August 14, 2018 |title=COVER + TRACKLIST https://t.co/GY2qjJ32Dp |language=en |access-date=November 14, 2021 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210816164633/https://twitter.com/heyamine/status/1029515562898997251 |archive-date=August 16, 2021 |url-status=live}}</ref> He was later a part of the [[Spider-Man: Into the Spider-Verse (soundtrack)|soundtrack]] for the 2018 movie, ''[[Spider-Man: Into the Spider-Verse]]'', with the song, "Invincible" on December 14.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://music.apple.com/us/album/spider-man-into-spider-verse-soundtrack-from-inspired/1445949265|title=Spider-Man: Into the Spider-Verse (Soundtrack From & Inspired by the Motion Picture) by Various Artists|work=Apple Music|date=December 14, 2018|access-date=February 26, 2020}}</ref>

On February 26, 2020, Aminé released the single, "Shimmy".<ref>{{cite web|last1=Mahadevan|first1=Tara|url=https://www.complex.com/music/2020/02/amine-new-video-shimmy|title=Aminé Drops New Video "Shimmy|work=[[Complex (magazine)|Complex]]|date=February 26, 2020|access-date=February 26, 2020}}</ref> On May 29, 2020, Aminé released the single "Riri".<ref>{{cite AV media |date=June 24, 2020 |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=J6LUnNqYGsg |title=Aminé - Riri (Official Video) |publisher=Aminé |via=[[YouTube]] |access-date=November 14, 2021 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20211101173128/https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=J6LUnNqYGsg |archive-date=November 1, 2021 |url-status=live}}</ref> On July 6, 2020, Aminé released the single "Compensating" featuring [[Young Thug]], whilst announcing and revealing the cover art and release date for his second studio album, ''[[Limbo (Aminé album)|Limbo]]''.<ref>{{cite AV media |date=July 6, 2020 |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PccMeL8HWl4 |title=Aminé - Compensating feat. === Political views ===
Aminé is an open critic of former [[President of the United States|U.S. President]] [[Donald Trump]].<ref name=":0" /> During Aminé's television debut on ''[[The Tonight Show Starring Jimmy Fallon]]'' on November 15, 2016, Aminé performed a remixed version of the song "Caroline". At the end of the performance, Aminé performed a verse dedicated to the results of the [[2016 United States presidential election]] saying the lines:

<blockquote>[[September 11 attacks|9/11]], a day that we never forgetting, 11/9, a day that we all regrettin', If my president is Trump then it's relevant enough to talk 'bout it on TV and not give a... I'm black, and I'm proud my skin is brown and I'm loud, everybody love it when a rapper tells some lies well that ain't me, homie, I guess that's a surprise America wanna act all happy and holy but deep down inside they're like [[Brad Pitt|Brad]] and [[Angelina Jolie|Jolie]], Caroline divine and I won't get specific, Club Banana the illest and it's too terrific, You can never make America great again, all you ever did was make this country hate again.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Aminé Flips His "Caroline" Hit Into An Anti-Trump Anthem On 'The Tonight Show'|url=https://genius.com/a/amine-flips-his-caroline-hit-into-an-anti-trump-anthem-on-the-tonight-show|access-date=2017-08-04|website=Genius|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=Aminé Made A Political Statement With His Tonight Show Performance.|url=http://www.thefader.com/2016/11/16/amine-the-tonight-show|access-date=2017-08-04|website=The FADER}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|date=2016-11-16|title=Rapper Aminé Slams Donald Trump in Moving 'Tonight Show' Debut|language=en-US|work=EW.com|url=http://ew.com/article/2016/11/16/amine-donald-trump-tonight-show/|access-date=2017-08-04}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|date=2016-11-17|title=Aminé Debuts a Surprise Anti-Trump Anthem on the 'Jimmy Fallon'|language=en|work=ELLE|url=http://www.elle.com/culture/news/a40853/amine-tonight-show-jimmy-fallon-donald-trump/|access-date=2017-08-04}}</ref><ref>{{Cite magazine|title=A Young Rapper's Bold Anti-Trump Message on "The Tonight Show Starring Jimmy Fallon"|url=http://www.newyorker.com/culture/culture-desk/a-young-rappers-bold-anti-trump-message-on-the-tonight-show-starring-jimmy-fallon|access-date=2017-08-04|magazine=The New Yorker}}</ref></blockquote>

When speaking about Trump's immigration policy during the 2016 presidential campaign, Aminé said, "My parents are immigrants to this country, they came to this country for a better opportunity just like everyone else."<ref>{{Cite web|last=Magazine|first=Tadias|title=Rolling Stone: Ethiopian American Aminé Among 10 New Artists You Need to Know at Tadias Magazine|url=http://www.tadias.com/11/17/2016/ethiopian-american-hip-hop-artist-amine-slams-trump-on-tonight-show/|access-date=August 4, 2017}}</ref>
