'''Charles Kente Williams''' ([[Birth name|né]] '''Bowens'''; born October 21, 1977), better known by his stage name '''Keak da Sneak''', is an American rapper from [[Oakland, California|Oakland]], [[California]]. He is known for his gravelly voice,<ref name="Mercury2008">{{cite news |last1=Cabrera |first1=Marc |title=Keak Da Sneak is back, with several guests |url=https://www.mercurynews.com/2008/06/11/keak-da-sneak-is-back-with-several-guests/ |access-date=29 January 2023 |publisher=[[The Mercury News]] |date=11 June 2008 |quote="Keak Da Sneak has become, for many, the hip-hop voice of the Bay Area. It’s a gravel-toned, jagged-edged, smoked-too-much-growing-up-in-Oaktown growl that cuts through a track like a diamond slicing glass."}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |title=Raspy-Voiced Bay Area Legend Keak Da Sneak Announces ''{{as written|Withd|rawl}}'', Releasing November 3rd |url=https://audibletreats.com/keak-da-sneak_pr1/ |access-date=29 January 2023 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20221003042001/https://audibletreats.com/keak-da-sneak_pr1/ |archive-date=3 October 2022 |date=6 October 2017 |quote="Keak Da Sneak is renowned for his rubbery flow and raspy drawl."}}</ref> for coining the term ''[[hyphy]]'' in 1994,<ref>{{cite journal |last1=Horowitz |first1=Steven J. |title=An Oral History of Hyphy |journal=[[Complex Networks|Complex]] |date=13 June 2016 |url=https://www.complex.com/music/2016/06/hyphy-oral-history |access-date=29 January 2023}}</ref> and for his contributions to the hyphy movement.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Harshaw |first1=Pendarvis |title=A Hyphy History with Producer Trackademicks |url=https://www.kqed.org/arts/13900205/rightnowish-trackademicks |access-date=29 January 2023 |work=[[KQED Inc.|KQED]] |date=23 June 2021}}</ref>

He has collaborated with [[E-40]].

==Early life==
{{expand section| date= April 2017}} 
Keak da Sneak was born in [[Brewton, Alabama|Brewton]], [[Alabama]]. As a newborn he  moved to Oakland, CA. Keak found popularity while attending Allendale Elementary School, which he parlayed into later friendships and [[talent show]] performances at Oakland's [[Bret Harte]] Junior High.  Through theater Keak met his collaborator [[Agerman]].

==Career==
Keak and Agerman formed [[Dual Committee]] when Keak was 15 years old. The duo's performances were first recorded on "Murder Man" and "Stompin in My Steel Toes" on [[C-BO]]'s 1994 EP ''[[The Autopsy]]''. Citing the personal growth of all three artists, he later signed as a solo artist with [[Sacramento, California|Sacramento]]-based Moe Doe Records.<ref>Birchmeier, Jason. [{{AllMusic|class=artist|id=p372383|pure_url=yes}} Keak Da Sneak]. [[Allmusic]]</ref> Because of that, he began to receive more radio airplay, especially on [[San Francisco]] hip-hop station [[KMEL]].

===3X Krazy===

{{Main|3X Krazy}}

By the end of their junior year in high school, He and his collaborators had added rapper B.A. to form the group [[3X Krazy]]. Their first EP, ''[[Sick-O]]'', was released independently on August 5, 1995. In 1996 they signed to Virgin Records, releasing the album ''[[Stackin' Chips]]'' on March 8, 1997 (with help from the single Keep It on the Real; the album received national attention), the second album ''[[Immortalized (3X Krazy album)|Immortalized]]'', and then the release of ''[[Real Talk 2000]]'' on January 18, 2000.<ref>Birchmeier, Jason. [{{AllMusic|class=artist|id=p169594|pure_url=yes}} 3X Krazy]. [[Allmusic]]</ref> The last 3X Krazy album, a collection of previously unreleased material and remixed songs from ''[[Sick-O]]'', was ''[[flowamatic-9]]'', which was released in 2004. The group eventually disbanded.

===Solo career===
After years hustling in the underground scene, Keak began to see mainstream success in 2004 with the song ''[[That's My Word|Super Hyphy]]''. His 2006 collaboration with E-40, ''[[Tell Me When to Go]]'', received national attention.<ref name=Mercury2008/> In total, Keak da Sneak has released almost 20 albums and several mixtapes.

==Personal life==
Keak da Sneak has been married to his wife Dee Bowens, a former clothing designer, since February 14, 2004. He has four children.

After surviving a [[Gun violence|shooting]], Keak began carrying a [[firearm]] for protection, which led to a 2017 [[criminal charge|charge]] for firearm possession as a convicted felon. During the trial, Keak was targeted in another shooting; he was shot eight times, fell into a three-day coma, and was left paralyzed.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.nbcbayarea.com/news/local/Popular-Bay-Area-Rapper-Keak-Da-Sneak-Shot-in-Richmond-Police-441318173.html|title=Popular Rapper Keak Da Sneak Shot in Richmond: Police|last1=Noceda|first1=Kristofer|last2=Hern|first2=Jodi|website=[[NBC|NBC Bay Area]]|language=en|access-date=2019-01-31|last3=ez|date=21 August 2017 }}</ref> He now uses a wheelchair to get around.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Hardee |first1=Howard |title=da Sneak attack |url=https://www.newsreview.com/sacramento/content/da-sneak-attack/27927704/ |access-date=29 January 2023 |work=[[News & Review|Sacramento News & Review]] |date=28 May 2019 |quote="A Bay Area rapper living in Sacramento started carrying a gun after he was shot twice. Now Keak da Sneak faces prison in a wheelchair."}}</ref>

After a two-year legal battle, Keak was sentenced to 16 months in [[California Department of Corrections and Rehabilitation|state custody]]. He served 5 months of his sentence at [[California Health Care Facility]] before being released early due to [[2016 California Proposition 57|Proposition 57]]. Upon release, he returned home to his wife and children.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Voynovskaya |first1=Nastia |title='It Made Me Appreciate Life': Keak Da Sneak Celebrates Prison Release at Rolling Loud |url=https://www.kqed.org/arts/13867431/it-made-me-appreciate-life-keak-da-sneak-celebrates-prison-release-at-rolling-loud |access-date=29 January 2023 |work=[[KQED Inc.|KQED]] |date=2 October 2019}}</ref>
