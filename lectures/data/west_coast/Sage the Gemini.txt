No blogs, not IMDb, and not public records searches--> (born June 20, 1992),<ref name=allmusic>{{cite web|last1=Jeffries|first1=David|title=Sage the Gemini Biography|url=http://www.allmusic.com/artist/sage-the-gemini-mn0003036335/biography|publisher=[[AllMusic]] (All Media Network, LLC)|access-date=January 24, 2016}}</ref> better known by his stage name '''Sage the Gemini''' ({{IPAc-en|s|eɪ|dʒ}}), is an American rapper and record producer from [[Fairfield, California]]. He is perhaps best known for his 2013 singles "[[Gas Pedal]]" (featuring [[Iamsu!]]), which peaked at number 29 on the [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]], and "[[Red Nose]]", as well his feature on [[Flo Rida]]'s 2014 single "[[G.D.F.R. == Life and career ==
=== 1992–2012: Early life and career beginnings ===
Sage the Gemini was born on June 20, 1992, in [[San Francisco, California]].<ref name="Noisey">{{cite web|url=https://noisey.vice.com/en_us/article/r7qggr/sage-the-gemini-doesnt-listen-to-rap|title=Sage The Gemini Doesn't Listen to Rap|work=[[Vice (magazine)#Website|Noisey]]|date=March 25, 2014|access-date=June 15, 2018}}</ref> His family moved to [[Fairfield, California]], when he was 6–7 years old.<ref name="Noisey" /> He started recording at the age of eleven with his brother after he bought a microphone from Gordon's Music and Sound shop in Fairfield, California. The first track he recorded, at 14, was titled "Made In China".<ref>{{cite web|last=McIntire |first=George |url=http://www.eastbayexpress.com/oakland/sage-the-geminis-career-slams-the-gas-pedal/Content?oid=3750785 |title=Sage the Gemini's Career Slams the 'Gas Pedal' |date=October 30, 2013 |publisher=East Bay Express |access-date=March 28, 2014}}</ref> Woods took the name Sage the Gemini due to the color of his eyes and his [[zodiac]] sign.<ref name="republicrecords1">{{cite web|url=http://www.republicrecords.com/artists/sage-the-gemini/ |title=Artist Profile: Sage The Gemini|publisher=Republic Records |access-date=March 28, 2014| archive-date= July 9, 2015 | archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20150709062614/http://www.republicrecords.com/artists/sage-the-gemini/ | url-status=live}}</ref><ref name="hiphopdx1">{{cite web|last=Ju |first=Shirley
|url=http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/interviews/id.2336/title.sage-the-gemini-recalls-meeting-hbk-gang-his-early-influences |title=Sage The Gemini Recalls Meeting HBK Gang and His Early Influences &#124; Rappers Talk Hip Hop Beef and Old School Hip Hop |publisher=HipHop DX |date= March 27, 2014 |access-date=March 28, 2014| archive-date=January 15, 2015 | archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150115060226/http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/interviews/id.2336/title.sage-the-gemini-recalls-meeting-hbk-gang-his-early-influences | url-status=live}}</ref>

In 2008, Sage released his first single, "You Should Know" on MySpace. It became a viral hit, generating over three million views worldwide. He then joined Black Money Music Group. Being close friends with rapper [[Iamsu!]], he also joined [[The HBK Gang]], or the Heartbreak Gang.<ref name="republicrecords1" /><ref name="hiphopdx1" />

=== 2013–present: ''Remember Me'' ===
In March 2013, Sage released the singles "[[Red Nose]]" and "[[Gas Pedal]]" featuring [[Iamsu!]], the latter has reached number 29 on the US [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]], making it his first top 40 hit; it also reached number six on the [[Hot R&B/Hip-Hop Songs]].<ref name="Sage The Gemini – Chart history" /> The songs immediately became hits and gained substantial airplay, which persuaded [[Republic Records]] to sign a deal with him in August 2013.<ref>{{Cite web|date=August 22, 2013|title=Bay Area's Sage The Gemini Signs Deal with EMPIRE/Republic Records|url=http://www.dubcnn.com/2013/08/22/bay-areas-sage-the-gemini-signs-deal-with-empireuniversal-republic/|access-date=September 4, 2020|website=DUBCNN|language=en-US}}</ref> The [[Recording Industry Association of America]] certified "Red Nose" Gold and "Gas Pedal" Platinum.<ref>{{cite web|last=Baker|first=Soren|date=January 7, 2014|title=Sage the Gemini's "Gas Pedal" Goes Platinum and "Red Nose" Goes Gold|url=http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/news/id.26930/title.sage-the-gemini-s-gas-pedal-goes-platinum-red-nose-goes-gold|access-date=March 28, 2014|publisher=HipHop DX}}</ref> On March 25, 2014, Sage released his debut studio album, ''[[Remember Me (Sage the Gemini album)|Remember Me]]''.<ref name="xxlmag1">{{cite web|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2013/12/sage-gemini-kool-john-party-jumping-college-drop/ |title=Sage The Gemini and Kool John Have The Party Jumping In "College Drop" – XXL |publisher=Xxlmag.com |date=December 23, 2013 |access-date=January 16, 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=http://news.radio.com/2014/02/13/interview-sage-the-gemini-on-his-new-album-working-with-justin-bieber/ |title=Interview: Sage the Gemini On His New Album and Working With Justin Bieber « Radio.com News |publisher=News.radio.com |date=February 13, 2014 |access-date=March 28, 2014 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140222230016/http://news.radio.com/2014/02/13/interview-sage-the-gemini-on-his-new-album-working-with-justin-bieber/ |archive-date=February 22, 2014 }}</ref>

In October 2014, he was featured on [[Flo Rida]]'s single "[[G.D.F.R. (song)|G.D.F.R.]]", which peaked at number eight on the ''Billboard'' Hot 100.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.billboard.com/biz/articles/6509675/mark-ronson-bruno-mars-top-hot-100-for-12th-week-flo-rida-reaches-top-10|title=Mark Ronson, Bruno Mars Top Hot 100 for 12th Week; Flo Rida Reaches Top 10|date=March 25, 2015|access-date=June 15, 2018}}</ref> In July 2017, Sage released his debut mixtape, ''Morse Code'', which includes the Gold certified single "[[Now and Later (song)|Now and Later]]".<ref>{{cite web|url=http://hiphopdx.com/news/id.44103/title.sage-the-gemini-shares-new-single-cover-art-tracklist-for-morse-code-mixtape#|title=Sage The Gemini Drops "Morse Code" Mixtape|work=[[HipHopDX]]|date=July 23, 2017|access-date=June 15, 2018}}</ref> "Now and Later" peaked at the #1 spot on the urban charts in Australia and the UK.<ref>{{Cite web|date=January 27, 2017|title=Official Hip Hop and R&B Singles Chart Top 40 {{!}} Official Charts Company|url=https://www.officialcharts.com/charts/official-hip-hop-and-r-and-b-singles-chart/20170127/114/|access-date=September 4, 2020|website=www.officialcharts.com|language=en}}</ref>

== Personal life ==
