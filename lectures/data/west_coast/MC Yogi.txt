'''Nicholas Christian Giacomini''' (born August 26, 1979), better known by his stage name '''MC Yogi''', is an American [[raping|rapper]] and [[yogi]]. His music style characteristically contains themes promoting [[Hindu philosophy]] and many of his songs are [[bhajans]].

==Personal life==
MC Yogi was born in [[San Francisco]] on August 26, 1979, and grew up in [[San Francisco Bay Area]]. He started rapping when he was 13 and started practicing yoga when he was 17 while living in a group home for at-risk youth. He became attracted to yoga after his father started practicing it. His first teacher was [[Larry Schultz]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.seattleyoganews.com/mc-yogi-interview |title=Exclusive Interview with MC Yogi|first=|last=|work=Seattle Yoga News |date=July 2014 |accessdate=29 July 2014}}</ref> He is married to Amanda Giacomini and the pair divide their time between teaching at their [[Point Reyes]] yoga studio, Point Reyes Yoga, and traveling for music performance.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://mcyogi.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/09/YJ-2011-MC-amanda.jpg |title=Grounding Grooves |first=Karen |last=Macklin |work=[[Yoga Journal]] |date=September 2011 |accessdate=21 September 2011 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20110929032600/http://mcyogi.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/09/YJ-2011-MC-amanda.jpg |archivedate=29 September 2011 }}</ref>

==Reviews==
''[[Yoga Journal]]'' reviewed MC Yogi's work as "surprisingly educational" because "he makes rapping about Hindu deities little learning opportunities".<ref>{{cite web |url=http://blogs.yogajournal.com/cityblog/2008/09/nyc_ganesh_is_fresh.html |title=Samadhi & the City |first=Valerie |last=Reiss |work=[[Yoga Journal]] |date=3 September 2008 |accessdate=21 September 2011 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20120322165747/http://blogs.yogajournal.com/cityblog/2008/09/nyc_ganesh_is_fresh.html |archivedate=22 March 2012 }}</ref>

The ''[[New York Times]]'' said that MC Yogi may embody the ethos of the [[Wanderlust Festival]] more than any other performer.<ref>{{cite news |url= https://www.nytimes.com/2009/07/28/arts/music/28yoga.html?pagewanted=2&_r=3&ref=music |title=At a Lake Tahoe Festival, Party by Night and Downward Dog by Day |first=Melena |last=Ryzik |work=[[The New York Times]] |date=27 July 2009  |issn=0362-4331 |accessdate=21 September 2011}}</ref>

==In society==
In 2008 MC Yogi produced a video called "Obama '08 - Vote for Hope" promoting the presidential campaign of Barack Obama, and the video went viral on YouTube.<ref name="Starbucks">{{cite web |url= http://www.billboard.biz/bbbiz/content_display/industry/news/e3ie135f8f41b7a1814d9fefbe34e80fb1f |title=Starbucks Taps MC Yogi For New Campaign |first=Kamau |last=High |work=billboard.biz |date=21 January 2009 |accessdate=21 September 2011}}</ref><ref name="Fast">{{cite web |url= http://www.fastcompany.com/blog/linda-tischler/design-times/starbucks-and-mcyogi-offer-you-free-cuppa-joe-if-you-volunteer |title=Starbucks and M.C. Yogi Offer You a Free Cuppa Joe -- If You Volunteer |first=Linda |last=Tischler |work=[[Fast Company (magazine)|Fast Company]] |date=21 January 2009 |accessdate=21 September 2011}}</ref>

In 2009 Starbucks promoted an MC Yogi song as part of a campaign called "Are You In?"  The purpose of the campaign is to promote community service and volunteering.<ref name="Starbucks"/><ref name="Fast"/>

In 2012, MC Yogi produced a video in celebration of [[Gandhi Jayanti]] (October 2, the birth of [[Mohandas Karamchand Gandhi|Mahatma Gandhi]]). The video is called, " Be the Change (The Story of Mahatma Gandhi)."<ref name=prweb>{{cite web |url= http://www.prweb.com/releases/2012/10/prweb9972233.htm |title=MC Yogi Debuts "Be The Change" Music Video Celebrating {{sic|Ghan|di's|hide=y}} Life + Legacy |first= |last= |work=PRWeb |date=4 October 2012 |accessdate=11 October 2013}}</ref><ref name="hp">{{cite web |url= http://www.huffingtonpost.com/mc-yogi/happy-birthday-to-a-real-_b_1936152.html |title=Happy Birthday to A Real Super Hero: Mahatma Gandhi|first=MC |last=Yogi |work=Huffington Post |date=3 October 2012 |accessdate=11 October 2012}}</ref>
