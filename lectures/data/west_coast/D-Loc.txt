'''Dustin Gary Miller''' (born May 2, 1977), best known by his stage name '''D-Loc''', is an [[MC (hip hop)|MC]] in the [[rap rock]] group [[Kottonmouth Kings]] and rap group [[Kingspade]]. The group was signed to [[Suburban Noize Records|Suburban Noize]]. Miller has been a part of the Kottonmouth Kings since they were the P town Ballers (PTB) in 1994. Miller is also known by the stage names DJ Shakey Bonez,  and D-Double Dash.

Miller grew up in Placentia, California with current Kottonmouth Kings member and best friend [[Johnny Richter]] and deceased former Kottonmouth Kings member Steven Thronson (better known by his stage name [[Saint Dog]] or Saint Vicious). D-Loc and Johnny Richter, also make up the rap duo Kingspade.

== Career ==
D-Loc and the KMK have released 15 studio albums, as well as many other releases such as the hidden stash series, Joint Venture, Kottonmouth Xperience vol 1 and 2, classic hits live, Greatest Highs, and the Double Dose series. With Kottonmouth kings and Kingspade D-Loc has had over 22 album releases.

D-Loc's first solo album "Made For Kings" came out in 2010. In April 2011, D Loc started hosting his own segment "D-Loc Days" on a Seattle area radio show on 89.9 fm titled  The Hidden Stash along with The Faz and Spree The Producer. In January 2012, D-Loc started his own YouTube channel entitled D-LocTV.

In preparation of the release of his ''Weedman EP'', which dropped on March 20, 2012, the rapper released two remix music videos for previously released hit singles. On January 18, 2012, D-Loc dropped a remix/video of Chris Brown's hit single "Look At Me Now", his retitled "Rollin' Papers".<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ballerstatus.com/2012/01/18/d-loc-kottonmouth-kings-look-at-me-now-remix-aka-rollin-papers-music-video/ |title=D-Loc (Kottonmouth Kings): Look At Me Now (Remix) (aka Rollin Papers) (Music Video)  |publisher=BallerStatus.com |date=January 18, 2012 |access-date=January 18, 2012}}</ref> Over a month later, in late February, he followed with his own remix of Dr. Dre's Detox single, "Kush".<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ballerstatus.com/2012/03/01/d-loc-kottonmouth-kings-kush-remix-music-video/ |title=D-Loc (Kottonmouth Kings): Kush (Remix) (Music Video) |publisher=BallerStatus.com |date=March 1, 2012 |access-date=March 1, 2012}}</ref>

Most recently, D-Loc dropped the first visual off the Weedman EP, a music video for his single, "Smoke You Out".<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ballerstatus.com/2012/03/22/d-loc-smoke-youout-music-video/ |title=D-Loc: Smoke You Out (Music Video) |publisher=BallerStatus.com |date=March 22, 2012 |access-date=March 22, 2012}}</ref> On December 5, 2020, D-Loc announced that he had formed his very own record label called P.T.B. Records and that a new Kottonmouth Kings album is set to be released in April 2021.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://faygoluvers.net/v5/2020/12/kmk-launches-new-youtube-account-and-announces-new-kmk-album/|title=KMK Launches New Youtube Account and announces new KMK album! &#124; Faygoluvers}}</ref>
