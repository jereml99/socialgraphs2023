'''Amber Josephine Liu'''<ref name="Amber's name">{{Cite web | title = 엠버 본명 알고보니 유일운 "주먹 좀 쓰는 형 이름?" | author = Park Areum | work = Newsen | date = November 13, 2011 | access-date = April 12, 2016 | url = http://www.newsen.com/news_view.php?uid=201111131239471001 | language = ko  }}</ref> (born September 18, 1992), also known mononymously as '''Amber''', is an American<ref>{{cite web |last1=Zhao |first1=Benjamin |title=Amber Liu: The K-Pop star breaking the mould |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/programmes/p082l4df |website=BBC |date=February 7, 2020 |access-date=30 April 2021}}</ref> singer, rapper, and songwriter.<ref>{{cite news |title=Amber Liu goes it alone after 10 years with f(x) |url=https://www.scmp.com/lifestyle/entertainment/article/3052061/after-10-years-fx-k-pop-star-amber-liu-talks-about-her-solo |access-date=26 August 2020 |work=South China Morning Post |date=25 February 2020 |language=en}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |last1=CHEUNG |first1=RAINER |title=f(x)'s Amber Liu speaks out on dark side of K-pop and bandmate Sulli's death: 'I miss her a lot' |url=https://www.asiaone.com/entertainment/fxs-amber-liu-speaks-out-dark-side-k-pop-and-bandmate-sullis-death-i-miss-her-lot |access-date=26 August 2020 |work=AsiaOne |date=3 January 2020 |language=en}}</ref> She debuted as a member of the South Korean girl group [[f(x) (group)|f(x)]] in September 2009. In 2015, she became the first f(x) member to make a solo debut with the release of her first extended play ''[[Beautiful (Amber EP)|Beautiful]]'' and has since released solo singles in Korean, English, and Mandarin.

In July 2018, she joined Steel Wool Entertainment following her original label [[SM Entertainment]]’s joint partnership with Steel Wool to manage her American activities. In September 2019, she left SM and exclusively signed with Steel Wool prior to the release of her 2020 extended play ''X.'' In May 2021, she also signed with Ryce Entertainment for her activities in China following a successful stint as a mentor in survival show ''[[Produce Camp 2021]]''.

==Life and career==
===1992–2010: Early life and career beginnings===

Liu was born on September 18, 1992, in [[Los Angeles]], [[California]].<ref>{{Cite web | title = Amber's profile | publisher = Amber Official Website | access-date = April 12, 2016 | url = http://amber.smtown.com/ | language = ko | archive-date = June 19, 2015 | archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20150619034320/http://amber.smtown.com/ | url-status = dead }}</ref><ref>{{Cite web | title = New song from Amber coming out Friday | author = Jeong Byung-ki | work = [[Korea JoongAng Daily]] | date = March 24, 2016 | access-date = April 12, 2016 | url = http://koreajoongangdaily.joins.com/news/article/Article.aspx?aid=3016589 }}</ref> Her parents are from [[Taiwan]] and her father has [[Teochew people|Teochew]] ancestry.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dMTU3UL-Hu8&t=1m6s |archive-url=https://ghostarchive.org/varchive/youtube/20211221/dMTU3UL-Hu8 |archive-date=2021-12-21 |url-status=live|title=[ENG SUB] 110517 Henry and Zhou Mi talked about Amber and Victoria|last=chilloutb0x|date=July 12, 2011|via=YouTube}}{{cbignore}}</ref> She has one older sister named Jackie.<ref name="r2" /> She attended [[El Camino Real High School]] in Los Angeles. Liu exceled in [[SM Entertainment]]'s Los Angeles global audition in 2008 where she and one male were selected to be trainees for SM Entertainment. After a year and a half of training, she debuted as a member of the South Korean girl group [[F(x) (band)|f(x)]] in September 2009.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.allkpop.com/2009/08/sm_unmasks_fx_members |title=SM Unmasks f(x) Members |date=August 24, 2009 |publisher=allkpop.com |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110727040519/http://www.allkpop.com/2009/08/sm_unmasks_fx_members |archive-date=July 27, 2011 }}</ref> During an episode of the group's reality show, ''[[f(x) (band)#2013: South by Southwest, Pink Tape and the first concert|Go f(x)]]'', Liu revealed that her parents initially had mixed feelings regarding her music career.  She stated: "My dad was very cautious, at first ...but then my mom was always supportive from the beginning. I really thank her and she also convinced my dad".<ref name="r2">[https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=uHo36Ghx2bM ''Go f(x)'' on MnetAmerica's official YouTube page] Youtube.com (July 24, 2013). Retrieved on May 21, 2014.</ref>

[[File:Amber Liu in Seoul Plaza (2).jpg|left|thumb|Amber performing at Seoul Plaza. |251x251px]]

She has composed and written lyrics for some of the group's songs such as "Goodbye Summer" which was a single in their second studio album, ''[[Pink Tape (f(x) album)|Pink Tape]]'',<ref>{{cite magazine|title=f(x) Stay Quirky With Sophomore LP 'Pink Tape' |last=Oak |first=Jessica |magazine=Billboard |url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/k-town/5189658/fx-stay-quirky-with-sophomore-lp-pink-tape |access-date=February 11, 2015 |date=July 30, 2013}}</ref> and "Summer Lover" from their third studio album, ''[[Red Light (f(x) album)|Red Light]]''. Aside from featured songs, she also worked with fellow group mates [[Luna (South Korean singer)|Luna]] and [[Krystal Jung|Krystal]] for the [[Original soundtrack|soundtrack]] of [[Korea Broadcasting System|KBS]] drama ''[[Master of Study|God of Study]]'', in a song called "Spread its Wings".

She frequently collaborated with other SM Entertainment artists. Labelmate [[Kwon Yuri|Yuri]] of [[Girls' Generation]] performed a cover of [[Ciara]]'s song "[[1, 2 Step]]" at their first concert, [[Into the New World (concert)|Into the New World]], held on December 19 and 20, 2009 in [[Seoul]] and featured her as a rapper and dancer. The song was then included in their [[Into the New World (album)|first live album]]. In June 2010, she was featured in the lead single of Taiwanese singer [[Danson Tang]]'s third Mandarin-language studio album, ''[[The First Second]]''. She performed the Korean and English rap vocals and was also featured in the song's music video.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=v9i2poHjWmg |archive-url=https://ghostarchive.org/varchive/youtube/20211221/v9i2poHjWmg |archive-date=2021-12-21 |url-status=live|title=Danson I'm Back feat. f(x) Amber Music Video |website=[[YouTube]] |date=June 8, 2010}}{{cbignore}}</ref>

===2015–2019: Solo debut===
[[File:Amber Liu at Jeju K-Pop Festival, in October 2015 02.jpg|thumb|Amber Liu at the Jeju K-Pop Festival, in October 2015.]]

Liu officially debuted as a solo artist, releasing her debut EP, ''[[Beautiful (Amber EP)|Beautiful]]'', on February 13, 2015. The title track, "Shake That Brass", featured Girls' Generation's [[Kim Tae-yeon|Taeyeon]].<ref>{{cite news|author1=Lee Woo-young|title=Amber unveils first solo album|url=http://www.koreaherald.com/view.php?ud=20150213000592|access-date=February 25, 2015|work=The Korea Herald|date=February 13, 2015}}</ref> A few days prior to the release of the album, a lyric video for the B-side track, "Beautiful", was released. The music video contained childhood photos of the singer, as well as photos since her debut as a member of f(x). Its lyrics tells the story of the difficulties she faced as she chased after her dreams of being an entertainer and how she liked herself the way she is.<ref>{{cite news|author1=Yoon Sang-Guen|others=Translated by Cho Ah-Ra|title='f(x)''Amber' Begins Solo Activity with [Beautiful]…Releases Lyric Video|url=http://star.mt.co.kr/foreign/starViewEng.html?no=2015020909334381927|access-date=February 25, 2015|work=Star News|date=February 9, 2015}}</ref> ''Beautiful'' debuted at number two on ''Billboard'''s World Albums chart and number 19 on [[Top Heatseekers|Heatseekers Albums]]. According to Jeff Benjamin of ''[[Billboard K-Town]]'', this ranking shows Amber has a large international fanbase.<ref>{{cite magazine|last1=Benjamin|first1=Jeff|title=Amber's 'Beautiful' Solo Debut Swoops into World, Heatseekers Albums Charts|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/k-town/6479984/amber-beautiful-solo-debut-world-heatseekers-album|access-date=February 24, 2015|magazine=Billboard|date=February 19, 2015}}</ref>

As a part of the [[SM Station]] project, Liu's solo single, "Borders", was released in March 2016. She participated in composing, lyrics writing and arrangement of the song. Written entirely in English, the hip hop track relates her childhood experiences and gives a message not to give up on oneself against life difficulties.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://mwave.interest.me/enewsworld/en/article/103797/fxs-amber-to-release-solo-song-on-march-25|title=f(x)′s Amber to Release Solo Song on March 25|publisher=eNewsWorld|author=Woorim Ahn|date=March 23, 2016|access-date=March 23, 2016|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160404084708/http://mwave.interest.me/enewsworld/en/article/103797/fxs-amber-to-release-solo-song-on-march-25|archive-date=April 4, 2016|url-status=dead}}</ref>

Liu released the digital single, “On My Own" on May 18, 2016. On May 25, she released another digital single titled "Need to Feel Needed".<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.soompi.com/2016/05/22/fxs-amber-to-release-third-single-in-crossing-series/|title=f(x)'s Amber To Release Third Single In "Crossing" Series – Soompi|website=www.soompi.com|date=May 22, 2016 }}</ref> On September 9, 2016, SM Entertainment published the official music video for "Breathe Again". This was Liu's first entirely English song and her first collaboration with Japanese DJ, Ksuke.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://news.ameba.jp/20160909-29/|title=KSUKE×AMBER from f(x)コラボ作『BREATHE AGAIN』配信スタート！ ネオアジアMVも公開 – Ameba News [アメーバニュース]|access-date=September 8, 2016|archive-date=September 20, 2016|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160920221758/http://news.ameba.jp/20160909-29/|url-status=dead}}</ref> On April 15, 2018, Liu released her first English-language solo mixtape, ''Rogue Rouge'', with six songs, free on SoundCloud. She also released a music video for one track, "Closed Doors", on YouTube, with plans to release a total of six videos.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.allkpop.com/article/2018/04/fxs-ambers-mixtape-rogue-rouge-closed-doors-mv-dropped|title=f(x)'s Amber's mixtape 'Rogue Rouge' + 'Closed Doors' MV dropped}}</ref>

In July 2018, she signed with Steel Wool Entertainment and was also registered  by her real name. The agency represents and promotes her in the United States through singing, acting, as well as directing videos.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Baltin |first1=Steve |title=Exclusive: K-Pop Superstar Amber Liu On Making Her Big U.S. Push This Year |url=https://www.forbes.com/sites/stevebaltin/2018/07/09/exclusive-k-pop-superstar-amber-liu-on-making-her-big-u-s-push-this-year/#64d3314b3e08 |website=Forbes |access-date=September 20, 2019 |language=en |date=July 9, 2018}}</ref> Liu is also signed with [[United Talent Agency]] to represent her worldwide activities.<ref name="Amber UTA">{{cite web |url=https://music.utatouring.com/artist/amber-liu/ |title=Amber Liu – UTA Music |website=[[United Talent Agency]] |access-date=February 2, 2019}}</ref> In September 2018, Liu released a single album ''White Noise + Lost At Sea'', which includes two songs "White Noise" and "Lost At Sea".<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.munhwa.com/news/view.html?no=20180919MW151148619590|title=f(x) 엠버, 싱글 'WHITE NOISE + LOST AT SEA' 21일 발표|access-date=September 22, 2018}}</ref>

On September 1, 2019, Liu announced her departure from [[SM Entertainment]].<ref>{{cite web |title='에프엑스' 엠버, SM과 계약 종료…"미유, 영원히 사랑해"|url=https://www.dispatch.co.kr/2039482 |website=dispatch.co.kr |access-date=September 1, 2019 |language=ko-KR |date=September 1, 2019}}</ref> Five days later, she confirmed that she would continue forward with her Steel Wool Entertainment contract, and would release an album that she previously prepared with the label.<ref>{{cite web |title=엠버, SM 떠나 美 스틸울과 함께 새로운 도전 시작 |url=https://entertain.naver.com/read?oid=112&aid=0003209296 |website=entertain.naver.com |access-date=September 20, 2019 |date=September 6, 2019}}</ref> On September 12, Liu announced that she was releasing her second EP, ''X'', with six tracks. It was later confirmed that she would drop one track each month until the album's release on January 10, 2020.<ref name="sep212019">{{cite web |title=[공식입장] f(x) 출신 엠버, 美 엔터와 전속계약…오늘 새 앨범 |url=https://news.nate.com/view/20190920n15255 |website=news.nate.com/ |access-date=September 21, 2019 |language=ko |date=September 20, 2019}}</ref>

===2020–present: Post-SM career and activities in China===
On January 28, 2021, Liu was confirmed to be a mentor on the fourth season of the Chinese version of Produce 101, titled ''[[Produce Camp 2021]]'', which ran from February until April 2021 and formed the boy group [[INTO1]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.bandwagon.asia/articles/amber-liu-f(x)-joins-produce-101-china-as-mentor-chuang-2021-zhou-zhennan-ning-jing-zhou-shen-nene|title=Amber Liu joins latest season of Produce 101 China as mentor|website=bandwagon.asia/ |access-date=January 28, 2021}}</ref> On April 5, 2021, Liu announced her third extended play, ''y?''. Soon after on April 8, 2021, Liu released two versions of her song "Neon": a Mandarin version that featured Chinese hip-hop artist Blow Fever, and an English version featuring [[Peniel Shin|Peniel]] from [[BtoB (band)|BtoB]] with two accompanying music videos that she co-directed.<ref>{{Cite web|last= Ju| first=Shirley|url=https://flaunt.com/content/amber-liu|title=NEW MUSIC, NEW MEANING & NEW PERSPECTIVE|website=Flaunt}}</ref> On April 29, 2021, Liu released two more singles from her album ''y?'': the Mandarin version of "blue" featuring [[MaSiWei]] from [[Higher Brothers]], and an English song called "Vegas"; along with music videos for each.<ref>{{cite web |title=Amber刘逸云《blue》《vegas》双曲联发 新专辑《y？》呈现未来式惊喜|url=http://ent.ynet.com/2021/04/30/3259801t1254.html |website=ent.ynet.com}}</ref> On May 27, Liu and Steel Wool signed a partnership with Ryce Entertainment for her solo activities in China.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Lau|first=Elly|date=May 27, 2021|title=RYCE Entertainment teams up with Jackson Wang's Team Wang, announces Amber Liu as new artist|url=https://www.bandwagon.asia/articles/amber-liu-signs-with-got7-jackson-wang-team-wang-ryce-entertainment-album-chuang-produce-101-china-2021|url-status=live|access-date=December 18, 2021|website=Bandwagon Asia|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210527084514/https://www.bandwagon.asia/articles/amber-liu-signs-with-got7-jackson-wang-team-wang-ryce-entertainment-album-chuang-produce-101-china-2021 |archive-date=May 27, 2021 }}</ref>

On September 21, 2021, Liu announced her fourth extended play ''Z!'', with the first single "Paradise" being released on September 24, and the second single "Bad Decisions" being released on October 29.  It was released on February 25, 2022.

As of 2023, Liu is participating in Ride The Wind 2023.
