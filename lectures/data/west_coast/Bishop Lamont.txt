'''Philip Brandon Martin''' (born October 31, 1978), better known as '''Bishop Lamont''', is an American [[rapper]] from [[Carson, California]]. He was signed to [[Dr. Dre]]'s [[Aftermath Entertainment]] record label and released one project under the label, eventually leaving in 2010.<ref>[http://www.aftermathmusic.com/_artists/index.html Aftermath Entertainment Artists] {{webarchive |url=https://web.archive.org/web/20070507045358/http://www.aftermathmusic.com/_artists/index.html |date=May 7, 2007 }}. AftermathMusic. Accessed August 10, 2007.</ref>

==Biography==
Born in [[Inglewood, California]],<ref name=aftermath06>[http://www.aftermathmusic.com/_interviews/bishoplamont_january_2006.html Interview with Bishop Lamont January 2006] {{webarchive |url=https://web.archive.org/web/20070524063713/http://www.aftermathmusic.com/_interviews/bishoplamont_january_2006.html |date=May 24, 2007 }}. AftermathMusic (January 13, 2006). Accessed August 10, 2007.</ref> Martin started rapping at age 13. Dr. Dre met Martin while filming [[The Game (rapper)|The Game]]'s music video for "[[Dreams (The Game song)|Dreams]]".<ref name=allhiphop>Paine (February 7, 2007). [http://allhiphop.com/blogs/features/archive/2007/02/07/18133826.aspx Bishop Lamont: The Platinum Backpacker] {{webarchive |url=https://web.archive.org/web/20071013191425/http://allhiphop.com/blogs/features/archive/2007/02/07/18133826.aspx |date=October 13, 2007 }}. AllHipHop. Accessed August 12, 2007.</ref> After giving him praise on an L.A. radio station,<ref>Jason (February 21, 2007). [http://www.rapbasement.com/index.php?option=com_content&task=view&id=2455&Itemid=35 Dr. Dre Speaks, "Detox Will Definitely Be Out This Year"]. Rap Basement. Accessed August 12, 2007.</ref> Dr. Dre signed Martin in 2005. Martin was featured in ''[[The Source (magazine)|The Source]]''<nowiki>'</nowiki>s [[Unsigned Hype]] section, but he had already signed to Aftermath by the time the issue hit stands.

Martin appeared in the soundtrack for the video game ''[[True Crime: Streets of LA]],'' performing "True Crime" and "Let's Get It Poppin". He can be heard on three [[EA Sports]] video games. Tracks "The Best" and "We Got Next" are included in ''[[Madden NFL 07#Soundtrack|Madden 2007]]'' and ''[[NBA Live 06]]'' respectively. "I'm a Soldier" was included in ''[[NFL Street 2]]''. Martin has the title track "Welcome to Havoc", featured in ''[[Havoc (2005 film)|Havoc]]'', a film starring [[Anne Hathaway]].

Together with his War Doggz crew, Martin owns a record label called Diocese Records.<ref>Nima (October 2004). [http://www.dubcnn.com/interviews/bishoplamont Bishop Lamont Interview]. Dubcnn. Accessed August 12, 2007.</ref>

Martin is featured on Dr. Dre's album, ''[[Detox (Dr. Dre album)|Detox]].''<ref name=aftermath06/><ref>Shaheem Reid (February 26, 2007). [http://www.mtv.com/bands/m/mixtape_monday/022607/ Mixtape Monday] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20070404052425/http://www.mtv.com/bands/m/mixtape_monday/022607/|date=April 4, 2007}}. Accessed August 12, 2007.</ref> According to Martin, Dr. Dre stated that along with [[Eminem]], Martin is the only other rapper that made him uncomfortable, due to his [[Mentorship|protégé]]'s controversial and political lyrics.<ref>Andres Tardio (February 27, 2007). In 2005, Bishop Lamont was credited to seven songs on [[Warren G]]'s studio album [[In the Mid-Nite Hour]]. Production credits include [[Battlecat (producer)|Battlecat]], [[DJ Premier]], [[RZA|The RZA]], and [[Pete Rock]].<ref>[http://www.bishoplamont.com/main.html Bishop Lamont] {{webarchive |url=https://web.archive.org/web/20070602072151/http://www.bishoplamont.com/main.html |date=June 2, 2007 }}. Bishop Lamont (August 12, 2007). Accessed August 12, 2007.</ref> According to Martin, the album is about "rebellion, revolution, the positive and negative things in life and organized chaos".<ref name=aftermath06/> He also released ''Caltroit 2: Metropolis,'' containing music not included in the first release of the Caltroit mixtape.<ref>[http://www.hoodhype.com/blog/?p=907 Bishop Lamont Interview]. HoodHype.com (November 28, 2007). Accessed November 30, 2007.</ref> In 2008, Martin appeared in [[Busta Rhymes]]' music video for "[[We Made It (Busta Rhymes song)|We Made It]]", and [[Kardinal Offishall]]'s music video for "[[Set It Off (Kardinal Offishall song)|Set It Off]]". In 2009, Bishop was featured on Rob Dyrdek's track "Lights Out: Dirty Girl Part 2."

In January 2010, Bishop confirmed his amicable split from Aftermath/Interscope after five years on the label. Lamont, who walked away with over 700 songs he recorded there, said he still has a relationship with Dr. Dre. He said: "Dre is still my big bro, but after five years of just sitting there, it is kind of unfair to the fans and my family and myself that the release date has changed."<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/news/id.10403/title.bishop-lamont-a-free-agent-leaves-aftermath-records|title=Bishop Lamont A Free Agent, Leaves Aftermath Records|last=Cornish|first=Melanie|date=2010-01-10|publisher=HipHopDX.com}}</ref>
