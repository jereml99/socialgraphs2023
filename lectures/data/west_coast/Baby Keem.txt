'''Hykeem Jamaal Carter Jr.''' (born October 22, 2000), known professionally as '''Baby Keem''', is an American rapper and record producer.<ref>{{Cite web |date=2020-02-03 |title=Artist Spotlight: Who Is Las Vegas Rapper Baby Keem? |url=https://audiblwav.com/who-is-baby-keem/ |access-date=2022-03-07 |language=en-US}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |date=2021-02-04 |title=Baby Keem: The Full Profile |url=https://raptv.com/artist/baby-keem-the-full-profile/ |access-date=2022-03-07 |website=RapTV |language=en-US}}</ref> Raised in [[Las Vegas]], he gained recognition in the late 2010s with his contributions to ''[[Black Panther (soundtrack)|Black Panther: The Album]]'', ''[[Redemption (Jay Rock album)|Redemption]]'', ''[[Crash Talk]]'', and ''[[The Lion King: The Gift]]''.<ref>{{cite web |title=Meet Hykeem Carter, The Mysterious Artist Who Has Credits on Multiple TDE Projects |url=https://www.complex.com/pigeons-and-planes/2018/07/hykeem-carter |website=[[Complex (magazine)|Complex]]}}</ref> He rose to prominence in 2019 through his [[sleeper hit]] single "[[Orange Soda (song)|Orange Soda]]".

After signing with [[PGLang]] and [[Columbia Records]] in 2020, Keem released his debut studio album ''[[The Melodic Blue]]'' (2021), which was met with critical and commercial success. It was certified [[Music recording certification|platinum]] by the [[Recording Industry Association of America]] (RIAA), and its final single, "[[Family Ties (song)|Family Ties]]", won the [[Grammy Award for Best Rap Performance]]. His collaborations with [[Kanye West]] on ''[[Donda]]'' (2021) and his cousin [[Kendrick Lamar]] on ''[[Mr. Morale & the Big Steppers]]'' (2022) earned him two nominations for the [[Grammy Award for Album of the Year]].

== Early life ==
Carter was born in [[Carson, California]].<ref>{{cite web|url= https://acclaimmag.com/music/fyi-baby-keem/|title= FYI: BABY KEEM|website=Acclaim Magazine|date= March 25, 2021}}</ref> Carter spent his early life, as young as four years old, in music studios watching his aunts and uncles [[Burn CD|burn CDs]] using [[LimeWire]]. He later recalled memories of listening to American rapper [[Kanye West]]'s album "[[808s & Heartbreak]]" on the radio while living in [[Las Vegas]] during winter 2009. This sparked Carterâ€™s interest for music even more, and he began researching music in 2010. Carter looked up to artists such as West, [[Kid Cudi]], [[Lil Wayne]], [[Eminem]] and [[50 Cent]].<ref>{{cite web |last1=Pack |first1=The Rap |title=Baby Keem On His Journey, His Relationship W/ Kendrick Lamar, Working W/ Drake + More {{!}} The Rap Pack |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=N72BLEUiomE&t=1642s |website=YouTube |access-date=22 September 2022}}</ref> In 2014, Carter started pursuing his own musical career.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Pack |first1=The Rap |title=Baby Keem On His Journey, His Relationship W/ Kendrick Lamar, Working W/ Drake + More {{!}} The Rap Pack |website=[[YouTube]] |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=N72BLEUiomE&t=1642s}}</ref>

Carter made [[YouTube]] accounts "AllMightyModz" and "HykeezyHD", posting [[Minecraft (franchise)|Minecraft]] Survival minigames gameplay in the early 2010s from his Minecraft account "hykeemc".<ref>{{Citation |title=Survival Games {{!}} Ep. 2 {{!}} w/AllMightyModz |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OVx7TrQmm38 |language=en |access-date=2022-08-18}}</ref> Carter frequently [[Skype]]-called his fans, who played along with him.<ref>{{Citation |title=â˜… Minecraft Survival Games {{!}} Ep. 7 {{!}} The Wrong Hykeem! |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0GA8TYvRGgM |language=en |access-date=2022-08-18}}</ref> Today, Carter no longer posts, but his YouTube still links to his [[SoundCloud]] account.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Your Plug - YouTube |url=https://www.youtube.com/allmightymodz |access-date=2022-08-18 |website=www.youtube.com}}</ref>

==Musical career==
Keem worked with American record producer [[Cardo (record producer)|Cardo]], on his two solo mixtapes, ''The Sound of a Bad Habit'' (2018) and ''Die For My Bitch'' (2019).

==Artistry==
===Influences===
In a 2019 interview, Keem cited American musician [[Kid Cudi]], as a major influence: "I was inspired by Cudi's cadences and shit like that. Kid Cudi's one of my favorite artists."<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.complex.com/music/2019/07/baby-keem-interview-die-for-my-bitch |title=Baby Keem Has Arrived |first=Shawn |last=Setaro |date=July 31, 2019 |website=Complex}}</ref>

In a 2021 interview with ''Billboard'', Keem reportedly identified as part of the [[Mike Will Made It|Mike WiLL Made-It]] and [[Metro Boomin]] generation, saying he derives inspiration from the two producers, as well as from [[Kanye West]].<ref>{{Cite magazine |last=Rouhani |first=Neena |date=2021-09-20 |title=Baby Keem Gets Personal: 'My Mission Here Is Much Deeper' |url=https://www.billboard.com/music/rb-hip-hop/baby-keem-interview-album-melodic-blue-9629335/ |access-date=2022-03-11 |magazine=Billboard |language=en-US}}</ref>
