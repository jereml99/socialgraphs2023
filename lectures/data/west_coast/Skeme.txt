'''Lonnie Kimble''' (born January 3, 1990), better known by his stage name '''Skeme''', is an American rapper from [[Inglewood, California]]. Skeme has collaborated with various [[West Coast hip hop|West Coast]] rappers such as the group [[Black Hippy]], [[Bizzy Bone]], [[The Game (rapper)|The Game]], [[Dom Kennedy]], [[Nipsey Hussle]], [[E-40]], [[Casey Veggies]] and [[Problem (rapper)|Problem]] among others. In 2012, Skeme released his debut studio album ''Alive & Living'' under [[RBC Records]]. His second studio album ''Ingleworld'' was released in 2013 by MADE Headlines.

In July 2014, The Game announced that he had signed Skeme to [[Blood Money Entertainment]]. In 2016, Skeme told [[HotNewHipHop]] that he is not signed to [[Blood Money Entertainment]] anymore, but only affiliated with them. Later that year, Skeme signed a deal with [[DJ Drama]] and Don Cannon's [[DJ Drama|Generation Now]] label, alongside frequent collaborator [[Lil Uzi Vert]].

== Early life ==
Lonnie Kimble was born on January 3, 1990.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/skeme-mn0002568937/biography|title=Skeme Biography, Songs, & Albums|website=[[AllMusic]]}}</ref><ref name="laweekly1">{{cite web |last=Weiss |first=Jeff |url=http://www.laweekly.com/westcoastsound/2013/10/30/inglewood-rapper-skeme-attempts-to-bring-his-city-to-the-world |title=Inglewood Rapper Skeme Attempts to Bring His City to the World &#124; Los Angeles News and Events |publisher=LA Weekly |date=October 3, 2013 |accessdate=February 1, 2014 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140202113011/http://www.laweekly.com/westcoastsound/2013/10/30/inglewood-rapper-skeme-attempts-to-bring-his-city-to-the-world |archive-date=February 2, 2014 |url-status=dead }}</ref> His parent split when he was two years old and his father was in the military.<ref name="autogenerated1">{{cite web|url=http://noisey.vice.com/blog/skeme-welcome-to-ingleworld-1 |title=Skeme: Welcome to Ingleworld &#124; NOISEY |publisher=Noisey.vice.com |date=December 17, 2013 |accessdate=February 1, 2014}}</ref> As a result, he moved in with his grandmother in [[Inglewood, California]].<ref name="hiphopdx1">{{cite web|last=Smith |first=Bruce |url=http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/interviews/id.2214/title.skeme-reflects-on-west-coast-unity-being-influenced-by-dolla |title=Skeme Reflects On West Coast Unity & Being Influenced By Dolla &#124; Rappers Talk Hip Hop Beef & Old School Hip Hop |publisher=HipHop DX |date=November 5, 2013 |accessdate=February 1, 2014}}</ref> Once his father came into the picture at age 10, he started listening to hip hop and memorizing his favorite songs. His dad originally gave him the idea to come up with his own material and start rapping.<ref name="hiphopdx1"/>

Skeme acquired his stage name at 13, when a friend noticed his craftiness at generating money. He said most of these money making strategies, were of the illegal nature, but they allowed him to purchase a [[BMW 5-Series|BMW 525I]] while he was in the twelfth grade at [[Gardena, California|Gardena]]'s [[Junípero Serra High School (Gardena, California)|Junipero Serra High School]].<ref name="laweekly1"/> He grew up affiliated with the [[Bloods|Piru Blood]] gang and was a [[gang]]banger throughout his high school years.<ref name="autogenerated1"/><ref>{{cite web|author=Jake Crates (@JakeCrates) |url=http://allhiphop.com/2012/11/01/exclusive-skeme-on-the-prevalence-of-bloods-and-crips-in-l-a-and-the-industry/ |title=EXCLUSIVE: Skeme on the Prevalence of Bloods and Crips in L.A. and the Industry |publisher=AllHipHop.com |date=November 1, 2012 |accessdate=February 1, 2014}}</ref>

== Career ==

=== 2007–2010: Early mixtapes and ''Pistols and Palm Trees'' ===
Skeme first began rapping around age 12 and began writing around age 15. He recorded his first song at a studio in [[Long Beach, California]], at age 16.<ref name="autogenerated1"/> When he first began taking rapping seriously at age 17,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/rap-music/the-break/2011/06/the-break-presents-skeme/ | title=The Break Presents: Skeme – XXL |publisher=Xxlmag.com |date=June 3, 2011 |accessdate=February 1, 2014}}</ref> he was graduating high school. He shortly after met [[Dolla (rapper)|Dolla]], whom he began hanging out and frequently working with. He credits Dolla for significantly influencing him.<ref name="hiphopdx1"/> He has said, "Dolla kind of molded me when I was young. He shaped me into being somebody and trying to tell me what could happen if you took this shit seriously."<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/rap-music/2013/12/the-come-up-skeme/4/ |title=The Come Up: Skeme – Page 4 of 8 – XXL |publisher=Xxlmag.com |date=December 10, 2013 |accessdate=February 1, 2014}}</ref> In 2007 he released ''Skeme Of Things'', which was then followed by ''Skeme Of Things 2'' in June 2009.<ref name="xxlmag1">{{cite web|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/rap-music/2013/12/the-come-up-skeme/5/ |title=The Come Up: Skeme – Page 5 of 8 – XXL |publisher=Xxlmag.com |date=December 10, 2013 |accessdate=February 1, 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.2dopeboyz.com/2009/06/02/skeme-skeme-of-things-2-mixtape/ |title=Skeme – Skeme Of Things 2 (Mixtape) |publisher=2DOPEBOYZ |date=June 2, 2009 |accessdate=February 1, 2014}}</ref> On February 17, 2010, Skeme released his third official mixtape, ''All Rap'd Up'', hosted by DJ Ill Will.<ref name="xxlmag1"/><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.datpiff.com/Skeme-All-Rapd-Up-mixtape.99481.html |title=Skeme – All Rap'd Up Hosted by DJ Ill Will, DJ Rockstar // Free Mixtape @ |publisher=Datpiff.com |date=February 17, 2010 |accessdate=February 1, 2014}}</ref> During those early years, he forged friendships with Los Angeles-area rappers such as [[Kendrick Lamar]], [[Nipsey Hussle]] and [[Dom Kennedy]]. On October 19, 2010, Skeme released his fourth official mixtape ''Pistols and Palm Trees''. He recorded the entire mixtape at [[Top Dawg Entertainment]]'s studio in [[Carson, California]].<ref name="hiphopdx1"/><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/videos/id.15240/title.skeme-interview-with-snoop-dogg-on-ggn |title=Skeme – Interview with Snoop Dogg on GGN &#124; Watch Hip Hop Music Videos & New Rap Videos |publisher=HipHop DX |date=January 8, 2014 |accessdate=February 1, 2014}}</ref> It featured collaborations with [[Tyga]], [[Ty Dolla Sign]], Dom Kennedy and Kendrick Lamar.<ref name="allhiphop1"/> On the strength of that mixtape, Skeme begun building his buzz.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/rap-music/2013/12/the-come-up-skeme/ |title=The Come Up: Skeme – XXL |publisher=Xxlmag.com |date=December 10, 2013 |accessdate=February 1, 2014}}</ref>

=== 2011–2013: ''The Statement'' and ''Alive and Living'' ===
On May 19, 2011, Skeme released his fifth mixtape ''The Statement''.<ref>{{cite web|author=Randy Roper |url=http://hiphopwired.com/2011/05/19/skeme-talks-the-statement-mixtape-and-the-new-west-interview-mixtape-download/ |title=Skeme Talks 'The Statement' Mixtape and The New West [Interview&#93;, Mixtape Download |publisher=Hip-Hop Wired |date=May 19, 2011 |accessdate=February 1, 2014}}</ref> It featured collaborations with [[Casey Veggies]] and Dom Kennedy, with [[Record production|production]] by [[Boi-1da]], [[Bink!]] and [[The Futuristiks]], among others.<ref>{{cite web|author=TC |url=http://smokingsection.uproxx.com/TSS/2011/05/download-skeme-the-statement-mixtape |title=Skeme – The Statement Mixtape &#124; The Smoking Section |publisher=Smokingsection.uproxx.com |date=May 25, 2011 |accessdate=February 1, 2014}}</ref><ref name="datpiff1">{{cite web|url=http://www.datpiff.com/Skeme-The-Statement-mixtape.231982.html |title=Skeme – The Statement // Free Mixtape @ |publisher=Datpiff.com |date=May 19, 2011 |accessdate=February 1, 2014}}</ref> Later that year, on November 29, Skeme released his sixth mixtape ''Before My Next Statement''.<ref>{{cite web|author=TC |url=http://smokingsection.uproxx.com/TSS/2011/12/download-skeme-before-my-next-statement-mixtape |title=Skeme – Before My Next Statement Mixtape x "M.O.B." Video &#124; The Smoking Section |publisher=Smokingsection.uproxx.com |date=December 14, 2011 |accessdate=February 1, 2014}}</ref> With basketball player [[Baron Davis]], Skeme started a Los Angeles-based entertainment management company named L.A. UNFD. Then he signed a one-album deal with [[RBC Records]] to release his debut studio album.<ref name="autogenerated1"/> On October 10, 2012, Skeme revealed that track listing for his debut album, titled ''Alive & Living''. The album contained guest appearances by [[Iggy Azalea]], [[Schoolboy Q]], Kendrick Lamar, Dom Kennedy and [[Problem (rapper)|Problem]]. The album's production was handled by Boi-1da, The Futuristiks, [[DJ Mustard]], THC, and [[DJ Dahi]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/xxl-magazine/2012/10/skeme-releases-cover-and-tracklist-for-alive-living-album/ |title=Skeme Releases Cover and Tracklist for Alive & Living Album – XXL |publisher=Xxlmag.com |date=October 10, 2012 |accessdate=February 1, 2014}}</ref> The album was then released digitally on October 16, 2012. It was supported by the singles "Ain't Bout Dat Life" and "Kidz With Gunz", the latter which featured Schoolboy Q.<ref>{{cite web|author=Skeme |url=http://www.djbooth.net/index/albums/review/skeme-alive-and-living |title=Skeme – Alive and Living – Stream |publisher=Djbooth.net |date=n.d.|accessdate=February 1, 2014}}</ref>

In April 2013, Skeme told Bruce Smith of [[HipHopDX]] that he was in talks with Top Dawg Entertainment about signing to their record label.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2013/04/skeme-the-next-member-of-tde/ |title=Skeme: The Next Member Of TDE? – XXL |publisher=Xxlmag.com |date=April 24, 2013 |accessdate=February 1, 2014}}</ref> He commented on it saying, "It's yet to be seen where exactly I’m gonna go. I'm just trying to make the right move. The next move has to be the best one. [...] With that being said, I am very close to TDE, them is the homies, and we are in heavy talks. It could be something."<ref>{{cite web|last=Vasquez |first=Andres |url=http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/news/id.23687/title.skeme-reveals-he-is-in-heavy-talks-with-tde- |title=Skeme Reveals He Is 'In Heavy Talks' With TDE &#124; Get The Latest Hip Hop News, Rap News & Hip Hop Album Sales |publisher=HipHop DX |date=April 24, 2013 |accessdate=February 1, 2014}}</ref> He later stated that he chose not to sign to TDE, out of the concern he would get lost in the mix, or overshadowed, wanting instead to remain independent and build his brand more.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/rap-music/2013/12/the-come-up-skeme/7/ |title=The Come Up: Skeme – Page 7 of 8 – XXL |publisher=Xxlmag.com |date=December 10, 2013 |accessdate=February 1, 2014}}</ref><ref name="autogenerated2">{{cite web|url=http://www.mtv.com/news/articles/1719255/skeme-top-dawg-entertainment-kendrick-lamar-relationship.jhtml |title=Kendrick Lamar's 'Extended Family' Member Skeme Reps L.A. With Ingleworld – Music, Celebrity, Artist News |publisher=MTV |date=December 17, 2013 |accessdate=February 1, 2014}}</ref> Skeme also revealed that he had been in talks with [[Def Jam Recordings]], [[Universal Music Group|Universal]] and [[Roc Nation]] about record deals during 2013. However, he stated that he was in no rush to sign to a [[major record label]].<ref name="laweekly1"/> He was featured on an October 2013 commercial for [[Colt 45 (malt liquor)|Colt 45]] along with [[Snoop Dogg]], [[YG (rapper)|YG]], [[Snow Tha Product]], and [[Lil Debbie]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.complex.com/music/2013/10/snoop-dogg-colt-45-commercial |title=Snoop Dogg Colt 45 Commercial |publisher=Complex |date=October 2, 2013 |accessdate=February 1, 2014}}</ref>

=== 2013–2015: ''Ingleworld'' and first two sequels ===
On September 27, 2013, Skeme released his seventh mixtape ''Bare With Me''. It featured guest appearances by [[The Game (rapper)|The Game]], Nipsey Hussle, Problem and [[TeeFlii]].<ref>{{cite web|last=James |first=Jesse |url=http://stupiddope.com/2013/09/27/skeme-bare-with-me-mixtape-new-music/ |title=Skeme – Bare With Me (Mixtape) &#124; New Music – |publisher=Stupiddope.com |date=September 27, 2013 |accessdate=February 1, 2014}}</ref> It was met with a positive review by HipHopDX.<ref>{{cite web|last=Smith |first=Bruce |url=http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/album-reviews/id.2158/title.skeme-bare-with-me-mixtape-review- |title=Skeme – Bare With Me (Mixtape Review) &#124; Read Hip Hop Reviews, Rap Reviews & Hip Hop Album Reviews |publisher=HipHop DX |date=October 6, 2013 |accessdate=February 1, 2014}}</ref> ''Bare With Me'' consisted of left over songs from ''Ingleworld'', that either did not fit the theme of the album, or had sample clearance issues.<ref name="hiphopdx1"/> Following its release, Skeme appeared on [[E-40]]'s "Turn Up or Burn Up" from ''[[The Block Brochure: Welcome to the Soil 6]]'' and on The Game's "Welcome to California" from ''[[OKE (mixtape)|OKE]]''.<ref name="hiphopdx1"/> Skeme toured in promotion of his second studio album, in the United States, along with internationally in countries such as the United Kingdom and Sweden.<ref name="laweekly1"/>

On November 15, 2013, Skeme released the music video for "Millions" from ''Ingleworld''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2013/11/watch-skemes-new-video-millions/ |title=Watch Skeme's New Video "Millions" – XXL |publisher=Xxlmag.com |date=November 15, 2013 |accessdate=February 1, 2014}}</ref> On November 26, 2013, Skeme revealed the cover artwork and the track listing for his second studio album ''Ingleworld''. The track listing revealed that it would contain 15 songs, including guest appearances from [[Wale (rapper)|Wale]], Nipsey Hussle, Iggy Azalea and Dom Kennedy, with production handled by [[Boi-1da]], Wizzo and Sean Momberger, among others. He explained that the album had a serious tone, which is why he choose to release it at the end of the calendar year.<ref>{{cite web|last=Baker |first=Soren |url=http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/news/id.26418/title.-skeme-ingleworld-cover-art-tracklist-album-stream |title=Skeme "Ingleworld" Cover Art, Tracklist & Album Stream &#124; Get The Latest Hip Hop News, Rap News & Hip Hop Album Sales |publisher=HipHop DX |date=December 17, 2013 |accessdate=February 1, 2014}}</ref> The album's [[lead single]] "Different" was released on December 4, 2013.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.complex.com/music/2013/12/skeme-different |title=Skeme "Different" |publisher=Complex |date=December 4, 2013 |accessdate=February 1, 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/rap-music/new-music/2013/12/skeme-different/ |title=Skeme "Different" – XXL |publisher=Xxlmag.com |date=December 5, 2013 |accessdate=February 1, 2014}}</ref>

''Ingleworld'' was released digitally by MADE Headlines on December 17, 2013.<ref name="autogenerated2"/> The album was met with generally positive reviews. Bruce Smith of HipHopDX said, "On ''Ingleworld'', Skeme combines an array of styles and cadences and an unheralded but highly skilled production team to craft an album with charm and depth."<ref>{{cite web|last=Smith |first=Bruce |url=http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/album-reviews/id.2201/title.skeme-ingleworld |title=Skeme – Ingleworld &#124; Read Hip Hop Reviews, Rap Reviews & Hip Hop Album Reviews |publisher=HipHop DX |date=December 18, 2013 |accessdate=February 1, 2014}}</ref> Olivia Arezes of ''[[Exclaim!]]'' gave it a six out of ten saying, "''Ingleworld'' is a valiant effort from Skeme but mediocre overall. Still, with some growth and improvement, Skeme could easily become a leader of the new West Coast sound."<ref>{{cite web|last=Arezes |first=Olivia |url=http://exclaim.ca/Reviews/HipHop/skeme-ingleworld |title=Skeme – Ingleworld • Hip-Hop Reviews • |work=Exclaim! |date=December 17, 2013 |accessdate=February 1, 2014}}</ref> Following the release of the album, [[MTV]] called him one of "hip-hop's top prospects for 2014."<ref name="autogenerated2"/>

Starting January 23, 2014, Skeme begun touring with Dom Kennedy on the Get Home Safely tour, in promotion of Kennedy's album ''[[Get Home Safely]]'' that Skeme also made an appearance on. The tour ran through February 27, 2014.<ref>{{cite web|last=Tardio |first=Andres |url=http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/news/id.26799/title.dom-kennedy-get-home-safely-tour-dates |title=Dom Kennedy 'Get Home Safely Tour' Dates &#124; Get The Latest Hip Hop News, Rap News & Hip Hop Album Sales |publisher=HipHop DX |date=December 28, 2013 |accessdate=February 1, 2014}}</ref> Skeme was nominated for the 2014 ''[[XXL (magazine)|XXL]]'' freshman class, however he was not selected.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/10spot-artist/2014/03/skeme-2/|title=Skeme – XXL|accessdate=July 1, 2016}}</ref>

On July 14, 2014, The Game announced that Skeme had been signed to his [[Blood Money Entertainment]] Label. He released a collaboration with The Game titled "No Limit" after the announcement. He was featured heavily on the Blood Money Entertainment collaborative project released by The Game entitled ''[[Blood Moon: Year of the Wolf]]''.

In 2015, Skeme worked heavily with DJ Drama and [[Don Cannon]] as an artist in their generation.now label/company. He is still currently a part of this organization which consists of himself, DJ Drama, Don Cannon and [[Lil Uzi Vert]]. His first single of 2015 was "Red Carpet (Roll Out)". On June 9, 2015, Skeme released the mixtape ''Ingleworld 2 (hosted by DJ Drama)'', which featured artists such as The Game, T.I., RJ, Young Thug, BJ The Chicago Kid and Lil Uzi Vert. Ingleworld 2 was later released as an album as a no DJ version. Later he announced a third album in his "Ingleworld" series, which was to be released on November 13, 2015.

=== 2015–present ''Ingleworld4Eva''===
Skeme featured on The Game's second disc of ''The Documentary 2'' on the song "My Flag/Da Homies", which was also released as a single prior to the album's release. A video for this song was released on December 17, 2015. Skeme also featured on a track released by rapper Wale, "Know Me". Recently, Skeme announced another and possibly final addition to his Ingleworld album series, to be entitled ''Ingleworld4eva''. In preparation for this album, he has released a mixtape entitled "Before 4Eva" through datpiff.com and other music sites that stream free music.

On May 6, 2016, DJ Drama released a single off his "Quality Street Music 2" album, title "Wishing" featuring Chris Brown, Skeme and LyQuin. A music video has been shot for the single and features DJ Drama, Chris Brown, Skeme and LyQuin, with [[cameo appearances]] by [[50 Cent]] and [[The Game (rapper)|The Game]].

== Personal life ==
Skeme's great-uncle is the late [[Blues music|blues]] legend [[Howlin' Wolf]].<ref name="laweekly1"/> Skeme has one sibling. Skeme's first son Khalil Amari Kimble, nephew to [[Nipsey Hussle]], was born during September 2013. Skeme is an also a loyal member of the Inglewood Family Bloods aka the Westside Flame Gang.<ref name="autogenerated1"/>

== Musical style ==
Brooklyne Gipson of ''[[Complex (magazine)|Complex]]'' said that Skeme is a "testament to diversity in L.A.'s sound. With his raspy voice, marked by a slight southern drawl hidden under a distinctly West Coast dialect." She then stated that his raps have the "passion of [[2Pac]] and the wit of [[Lil Wayne]]" also complimenting his production choices.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.complex.com/music/2012/04/10-new-la-rappers-to-watch-out-for/skeme|title=10 New L.A. Rappers To Watch Out For – Skeme|website=[[Complex Networks]]|accessdate=July 1, 2016}}</ref>
