'''Robert Houston II''', also known as '''Black Pegasus''' or simply '''Black-P''', is an American [[rapping|rapper]]/South Western hip hop artist from [[Colorado Springs, Colorado|Colorado Springs]]. He is the current president and CEO of Brass Knuckle Entertainment.<ref name="Stanley">Stanley, J. Adrian (2006) "[http://www.gazette.com/entertainment/black-14499-album-radio.html Black P’s latest is bravely experimental] {{webarchive |url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120614031557/http://www.gazette.com/entertainment/black-14499-album-radio.html |date=June 14, 2012 }}", ''[[Colorado Springs Gazette]]'', September 29, 2006, retrieved 2010-07-19</ref>

==Biography==
Robert Houston II was born on March 24, 1980<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.theblackmexican.com/ |title=Home |access-date=2010-07-06 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100206161812/http://www.theblackmexican.com/ |archive-date=2010-02-06 |url-status=dead }}</ref> in [[Germany]] where his father was stationed with the [[US Air Force]], and lived in Arizona, Florida, and New Mexico before his family settled on the Southeast side of [[Colorado Springs, Colorado|Colorado Springs]] when he was age ten.<ref name="Reed">Reed, Bill (2003) "Colorado Springs hasn't heard the last of rapper Black Pegasus", ''[[Colorado Springs Gazette]]'', November 14, 2003</ref><ref name="Forman">Forman, Bill (2008) "[http://www.csindy.com/colorado/rapping-on-the-edge/Content?oid=1143930 Rapping on the edge: Black Pegasus mixes politics with punchlines] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110611060329/http://www.csindy.com/colorado/rapping-on-the-edge/Content?oid=1143930 |date=2011-06-11 }}", ''[[Colorado Springs Independent]]'', July 17, 2008, retrieved 2010-07-19</ref><ref name="Short">Short, Christopher (2007) "[http://www.gazette.com/entertainment/black-25368-music-says.html City earns hip-hop artist’s loyalty] {{webarchive |url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120614031606/http://www.gazette.com/entertainment/black-25368-music-says.html |date=June 14, 2012 }}", ''[[Colorado Springs Gazette]]'', July 28, 2007, retrieved 2010-07-19</ref> He is from the Woodside Townhomes section in Colorado Springs. He is of mixed African-American and Mexican descent, hence the title of his fourth album, ''The Black Mexican''.<ref name="Forman" /> He open enrolled for high school and attended Widefield and Mitchell High School, and began rapping when he was sixteen, soon performing and working in his early days under the name Yo Mamma's Pimp.<ref name="Forman" /> He worked with the group Fusion of Syllables (F.O.S.) for two years before going solo in 2001.<ref name="Forman" /><ref name="Navarro">Navarro, Linda (2004) "Black Pegasus ready to spread his wings, fly higher", ''[[Colorado Springs Gazette]]'', August 27, 2004</ref> Black-P has released five studio albums (''Black Pegasus'', ''Knuckle Up'', ''F**K YO RADIO'', ''The Black Mexican'', and ''Black By Popular Demand''), two mixtapes (''Black P The Mixtape'', and ''Shitting On the Industry Vol.1''), and one collaboration CD (''Banana Spitz'') with Liquid Assassin of the hip-hop group Grave Plott. His new album ''Brass Knuckle King'' was released in 2011, and featured guest appearances by [[Immortal Technique]], Liquid Assassin, and [[Kutt Calhoun]]. Black P is also currently working on a collaboration album entitled "The Council", which will consist of Black P, King Tef, Hypnautic, and Johnny Rocketz. Black P's seventh studio album, "Yo Mamma's Pimp", is slated for a 2012 release as well.

Black-P has taken part in several battle-rapping contests,<ref name="Jefferson">Jefferson, Elana Ashanti (2005) "THREE QUESTIONS: Myth conjures rapper's success", ''[[Denver Post]]'', July 17, 2005</ref> winning the Rock the Mic Battle in 2003 and taking second place in Scribble Jam in Cincinnati.<ref name="Reed" /><ref name="Forman" /> He has featured on the [[MTV]] shows ''[[Road Rules]]'' and ''[[The Real World (TV series)|The Real World]]'' and in the ''NBA Jam 2'' video game.<ref name="Navarro" />

==Fan Base==
Black Pegasus also has a very large following in [[Denver, Colorado|Denver]]. He has gained much notoriety throughout the [[Midwestern United States|Midwest]] and [[Southwestern United States]].<ref>http://www.BlackPegasusRaps.com/index.html{{dead link|date=November 2016 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}</ref>
