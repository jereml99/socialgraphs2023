'''Claudia Alexandra Madriz Meza''' (born June 24, 1987)<ref name="birthname">{{Cite magazine |date=July 8, 2020 |title=Snow Tha Product Turns Her Living Room into a One-Stop Music Shop for 'On My Sh-- Freestyle' Video |url=https://www.billboard.com/amp/articles/columns/hip-hop/9415345/snow-tha-product-on-my-sh-freestyle-video |magazine=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]}}</ref>''',''' known professionally as '''Snow Tha Product''', is a Mexican-American rapper, singer, actress, YouTuber, and podcaster. She was signed to [[Atlantic Records]] from 2012 until November 2018. Since then, she has been an independent artist. Her most well-known projects are her 2013 mixtape ''Good Nights & Bad Mornings 2: The Hangover''<ref>{{Citation|title=Snow Tha Product – Good Nights & Bad Mornings 2: The Hangover {{!}} Download & Listen [New Mixtape]|date=October 14, 2013|url=http://www.hotnewhiphop.com/snow-tha-product-good-nights-and-bad-mornings-2-the-hangover-mixtape.98286.html|access-date=February 22, 2017}}</ref> and the 2018 compilation album ''VIBEHIGHER''.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Vibe Higher Mixtape by Vibe Higher Hosted by DJ Pumba, Sikwitit, EC Martinez, Brown Royal, Chris Banks, Young Taylor, CXDY|url=https://www.datpiff.com/Vibe-Higher-Vibe-Higher-mixtape.931533.html|access-date=2021-06-19|website=www.datpiff.com}}</ref> On April 28, 2021, Snow collaborated with Bizarrap on "[[Snow Tha Product: Bzrp Music Sessions, Vol. 39|BZRP Music Sessions #39]]".<ref>{{cite AV media |date=April 28, 2021 |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=t490zXLrQDE |title=Snow Tha Product {{!}}{{!}} BZRP Music Sessions #39 |publisher=[[Bizarrap]] |via=[[YouTube]] |access-date=March 13, 2022 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20220304221546/https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=t490zXLrQDE |archive-date=March 4, 2022 |url-status=live}}</ref> Snow also appeared in several episodes of the USA network hit series, ''[[Queen of the South (TV series)|Queen of the South]]''.<ref>{{Cite web|author=Lisa Respers France|title=Snow Tha Product has proved her doubters wrong|url=https://www.cnn.com/2021/09/17/entertainment/snow-tha-product/index.html|access-date=2022-02-14|website=CNN|date=September 17, 2021 }}</ref>

== Early life ==
Claudia Alexandra Madriz Meza was born on June 24, 1987, in [[San Jose, California]],<ref name="AbqJArticle">{{cite news |last1=Gomez |first1=Adrian |title='No resting': Rapper Snow Tha Product pushes her socially conscious music |url=https://www.abqjournal.com/1089997/rapper-snow-tha-product-pushes-her-socially-conscious-music.html |work=Albuquerque Journal |date=November 10, 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite AV media |date=January 21, 2014 |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Q-S7WHNpbiM |title=Snow Tha Product Is Mexican AF (GGN) |publisher=SnoopDoggTV |via=[[YouTube]] |access-date=March 13, 2022 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210501024547/https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Q-S7WHNpbiM |archive-date=May 1, 2021 |url-status=live}}</ref><ref name=":2">{{Cite web|title=Snow tha Product {{!}} Biography & History|url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/snow-tha-product-mn0002640574/biography|access-date=December 17, 2020|website=AllMusic|language=en}}</ref> to Mexican parents. Her father was an undocumented immigrant from [[Michoacán]]<ref name="Weekly">{{Cite web|last=Weekly|first=Fort Worth|date=2011-02-16|title=Snow Tha Product: Immigrant Song|url=https://www.fwweekly.com/2011/02/16/snow-tha-product-immigrant-song/|access-date=2022-02-14|website=Fort Worth Weekly|language=en-US}}</ref> and her mother, who is from [[Zacatecas]], immigrated legally to the United States.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Snow Tha Product|date=October 8, 2016|title=Rapper Snow Tha Product: 'Immigrants Don't Feel Safe to Have a Voice, but Their Children Can Stand Up For Them'|url=https://www.popsugar.com/node/42236842|website=POPSUGAR Latina|language=en-US}}</ref> She has a younger brother named Miguel Ángel, whom she endearingly calls "Ito".<ref>{{cite tweet |author=SNOW THA PRODUCT |user=SnowThaProduct |number=506175154708578305 |date=August 31, 2014 |title=HAPPY BDAY TO MY LIL BROTHER @WOKEITO .. THE REASON I CAN GET AWAY WITH DOIN SO MUCH! MY RIGHT HAND... http://t.co/2fdSQj7FQk |language=en |access-date=March 13, 2022 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20220218073333/https://twitter.com/snowthaproduct/status/506175154708578305 |archive-date=February 18, 2022 |url-status=live}}</ref>

During her younger years, Madriz was exposed to [[mariachi]] music on her father's side of the family as both her father and grandfather were mariachi singers.<ref name="Weekly"/> At the age of 6, she appeared in school talent shows and began performing with her grandfather's mariachi band in [[Redwood City, California|Redwood City]]. She got her first exposure to [[Hip hop music|hip hop]] when she moved to San Diego and gradually became more involved with her own music. Claudia attended [[Mira Mesa Senior High School]].<ref>{{Cite web|last=Product|first=Snow Tha|title=Snow Tha Product {{!}} San Diego Reader|url=https://www.sandiegoreader.com/bands/snow-tha-product/|access-date=2022-02-15|website=www.sandiegoreader.com|language=en}}</ref> As a teenager, she started [[Freestyle rap|freestyling]] with her friends.<ref name="Latina">{{cite web|author=Samantha Leal|date=June 13, 2012|title=Introducing Music Artist Snow Tha Product|url=http://www.latina.com/entertainment/music/introducing-music-artist-rapper-snow-tha-product|access-date=December 22, 2012|publisher=Latina.com}}</ref>

== Career ==
=== 2007–2010: Beginnings and early success ===
After graduating high school, Madriz briefly attended [[San Diego Mesa College]] to begin studying to become a social worker. At the age of 19, she began taking music seriously and dropped out of her first semester of college to pursue a career as a rapper.<ref name="dfw">{{cite web|author=Cary Darling|date=August 8, 2012|title=Snow Tha Product creates a flurry of buzz|url=http://www.dfw.com/2012/08/07/662601/snow-tha-product-female-rapper.html|access-date=December 22, 2012|publisher=DFW.com}}</ref> She adopted her name from [[The Walt Disney Company|Disney]]'s character [[Snow White (Disney character)|Snow White]]. Initially she used the name "Snow White", and then added "the Product". Then she changed it into her new moniker as "Snow White Tha Product",<ref name="hiphopdx">{{cite web|author=Edwin Ortiz|date=December 12, 2012|title=Snow Tha Product Discusses 'Good Nights & Bad Mornings,' Confirms Tech N9ne Collaboration|url=http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/news/id.22185/title.snow-tha-product-discusses-good-nights-bad-mornings-confirms-tech-n9ne-collaboration|access-date=December 22, 2012|publisher=HipHopDX}}</ref><ref name="dfw" /><ref name="dope">{{cite web|date=November 12, 2012|title=SNOW Tha Product – DOPE MAG Cover Queen|url=http://dopemag.net/2012/11/snow-tha-product-dope-mag-cover-queen/|access-date=December 22, 2012|publisher=DOPE Magazine}}</ref> and ultimately "Snow Tha Product". After recording a number of songs in Spanish, she caught the attention of Mexican artist Jaime Kohen. They recorded a collaboration song, titled "Alguien", which became popular in Mexico and appeared on Kohen's album ''Fotosíntesis''.<ref name="dfw" /><ref name=":2" />

In 2007, Tha Product released her first compilation album ''Verbal Assault Vol.1'' with many local artists under her label Product Entertainment LLC. It was followed in 2008 with her first solo mixtape ''Raising Tha Bar: Tha Mixtape'', which included many remixes of popular songs of the era. In 2009, ''Verbal Assault Vol. 2'' was released, with the single "Whatever It Takes". In 2010, Tha Product released ''Wake Ya Game Up, Vol. 1'', along with the Wake Ya Game Up and Woke clothing brands and apparel.

=== 2010-2011: ''Unorthodox'' and breakthrough ===
In 2010, Tha Product released the mixtape ''Run Up or Shut Up'', which was hosted by DJ Ames from the UK and included the hit single "Drunk Love". On February 16, 2011, the official music video was featured in the popular Latino music television channel Mun2.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Weekly|first=Fort Worth|date=2011-02-16|title=Snow Tha Product: Immigrant Song|url=https://www.fwweekly.com/2011/02/16/snow-tha-product-immigrant-song/|access-date=2021-06-19|website=Fort Worth Weekly|language=en-US}}</ref> Tha Product then moved her and her family to [[Texas]] and performed for two consecutive years at the [[South by Southwest]] festival.<ref name="dfw" />

On October 26, 2011, Tha Product released her debut independent album on Street Science Ent. ''[[Unorthodox (Snow Tha Product album)|Unorthodox]]''.<ref name="itunes_unorthodox">{{cite web|title=Unorthodox|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/unorthodox/id477320740|publisher=iTunes Store|access-date=December 22, 2012}}</ref> "Drunk Love" was re-released as a single along with "Woke Wednesday".<ref name="drjays_bio">{{cite web|title=Summer Sensations // Snow Tha Product|url=http://www.drjays.com/content/summersensations/2012/snow-tha-product.html|publisher=Dr. Jays|access-date=December 22, 2012}}</ref> Her real breakthrough came with the song "Holy Shit". The 97-second-long song received acclaim and was featured on [[50 Cent]]'s site ThisIs50.com.<ref name="dfw" /> The success of her songs caught the attention of three major record labels, which got in contact with her – [[Sony Music Entertainment|Sony]], [[Universal Music Group|Universal]] and [[Atlantic Records|Atlantic]].<ref name="dfw" />  Adam Bernard of RapReviews.com, impressed by her work, went on to write "[Snow] may very well be one of the leaders of ... a bold new era of female emcees."<ref name="rapreviews">{{cite web|title=Snow Tha Product Interview|url=http://www.rapreviews.com/interview/snowthaproduct2011.html|publisher=RapReviews.com|access-date=December 22, 2012|author=Adam Bernard|date=September 27, 2011}}</ref>

=== 2017–2019: ''VIBEHIGHER'' and going independent  ===
On October 4, 2017, Snow released the unfinished mixtape of ''VIBEHIGHER'' with artists Castro Escobar, Lex the Great, Jandro, and AJ Hernz on [[YouTube]]. She followed this with a tour of the same name in 2017 and 2018.

In 2017, Tha Product featured as Lil' Traviesa, also known as Lil' T, on the television series ''[[Queen of the South (TV series)|Queen of the South]]''.<ref name=":1" />

In November 2018, Snow left Atlantic Records and went independent. She announced that she was releasing the full ''VIBEHIGHER'' mixtape on December 22, 2018, since it was finished and ready to be put out under her independent label Product Entertainment LLC.

On May 1, 2019, Snow went on the 30-day Going Off tour.<ref>{{cite web|title=Snow Tha Product|url=https://www.snowthaproduct.com/|access-date=March 26, 2019|website=Snowthaproduct.com}}</ref> On September 12, she went on tour again which she named Going Off: Part 2.

=== 2020–present: First nomination for Latin GRAMMY Awards, ''To Anywhere'' and Dale Gas tour ===
In 2021, Snow received her first nomination for [[Latin GRAMMY Awards]] in the category of Best Rap/Hip-Hop Song for her collaboration song with Argentine producer Bizarrap "[[Snow Tha Product: Bzrp Music Sessions, Vol. 39|BZRP Music Sessions #39]]".<ref>{{Cite web|last=Villa|first=Lucas|date=2021-10-20|title=Snow Tha Product Talks First Latin Grammy Nomination, Going Global With Bizarrap Collaboration|url=https://wearemitu.com/latidomusic/snow-tha-product-interview-said-what-i-said/|access-date=2022-02-14|website=we are Mitú. 100% American & Latino}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=Snow Tha Product gets first Latin Grammy nomination for viral Bizarrap collab|url=https://www.aldianews.com/culture/music/snows-time-shine|access-date=2022-02-14|website=Al Día News|language=en}}</ref> She announced that her album VALEMADRE would be released in mid-2022.

On February 11, 2022, Snow announced that she was going on a national tour throughout April and May 2022 which she named the Dale Gas tour.

On September 9, 2022, Tha Product released "Piña" with Cuban-American singer [[Lauren Jauregui]], with the videoclip releasing on December 9. On October 21, 2022, Snow tha Product released the studio album ''To Anywhere'' featuring collaborations with vf7, AJ Hernz, Santa Fe Klan, [[Juicy J]], and Rotimi. ''To Anywhere'' is her first solo project in five years and her first studio album in a decade.

== Musical style and influences ==
Snow is known for her [[Chopper (rap)|rapid fire style]] of rapping.<ref name="dope"/> She stated she admires and supports artists who have their own original style. She is influenced by artists such as [[Missy Elliott]], [[Da Brat]],<ref name="Latina" /> [[Big Pun]], [[Lauryn Hill]], [[Aaliyah]], [[Amy Winehouse]], [[André 3000]], [[Ludacris]], [[Johnny Cash]], [[Eminem]], [[Busta Rhymes]], [[Tupac Shakur|2Pac]],<ref name="rapreviews"/> [[Mac Dre]], [[Tech N9ne]], and [[Brotha Lynch Hung]],<ref name="hiphopdx"/> as well as Mexican actress [[María Félix]] and singers [[Selena Quintanilla]], [[Gloria Trevi]] and [[Lupita D'Alessio]].<ref name="hhdx2" />

Snow is bilingual, speaking fluent English and Spanish, and records in both languages.<ref name="drjays_bio"/>

== Personal life ==
In a June 2018 interview, Snow revealed that she got married when she was 19 which lasted for a decade from 2006 until 2016. From her marriage, she has a child by the name of Andrew Feliciano Jr. (born April 2010) with her former husband, Andrew Feliciano.<ref>{{cite AV media |date=June 19, 2018 |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=kIPBgIEQ1lE |title=Snow Tha Product on Myself, Coming Out and Immigration |publisher=[[HOT 97]] |via=[[YouTube]] |access-date=March 13, 2022 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210721201151/https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=kIPBgIEQ1lE |archive-date=July 21, 2021 |url-status=live}}</ref> During an interview with Power 106 Los Angeles radio show in July 2018 she spoke about her experience raising her son and said her son was eight years old during the time of the interview.<ref name="Snow Tha Product On Raising An 8-Year-Old & Representing The Mexican Community">{{cite AV media |date=July 13, 2018 |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=uI6fJQLUsnI |title=Snow Tha Product On Raising An 8-Year-Old & Representing The Mexican Community |publisher=Power 106 Los Angeles |via=[[YouTube]] |access-date=March 13, 2022 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210722093124/https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=uI6fJQLUsnI |archive-date=July 22, 2021 |url-status=live}}</ref>

In 2017, Snow started dating Julisa Aponte, also known as (Daddie) JuJu. On July 10, 2019, they announced their engagement when JuJu proposed to her.<ref>{{cite AV media |date=July 10, 2019 |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3p6Z4oBRbPY |title=SHE PROPOSED... In 2020, during the COVID-19 pandemic, Snow bought a ranch in California, wanting to embrace her Mexican roots of living on a ranch and centralizing all of her operations and businesses. She moved in September 2020.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Snow Tha Product Opens Up On Mental Health, 'QUE OSO,' & Representing For Her Mexican Community|url=https://flaunt.com/content/snow-tha-product-que-oso|access-date=2022-02-14|website=Flaunt Magazine}}</ref><ref>{{cite tweet |author=SNOW THA PRODUCT |user=SnowThaProduct |number=1302708392541057025 |date=September 6, 2020 |title=OFFICIALLY MOVING to the RANCH (OMG!!) https://t.co/N7hI4GWPX4 https://t.co/HMTrVAFbkd |language=en |access-date=March 13, 2022 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20220217134123/https://twitter.com/snowthaproduct/status/1302708392541057025 |archive-date=February 17, 2022 |url-status=live}}</ref>
