'''Quincy Adams Brooks''', known professionally as '''San Quinn''', is an American [[rapper]] from [[San Francisco, California]]. He is the cousin of [[Messy Marv]], [[Stevie Johnson]], and [[Ya Boy]].

==Early life==
Brooks was born in [[Oakland, California]], United States, and grew up in the [[Fillmore District]] of [[San Francisco]] since the age of three.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.riotsound.com/hip-hop/rap/interviews/san-quinn/index.php|title=San Quinn : Rap, Hip-Hop Interview|publisher=Riotsound.com|access-date=December 20, 2014|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150924091317/http://www.riotsound.com/hip-hop/rap/interviews/san-quinn/index.php|archive-date=September 24, 2015}}</ref> He attended [[George Washington High School (San Francisco)|George Washington High School]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.publicschoolreview.com/school_ov/school_id/11742|title=Washington (George) High School|publisher=Publicschoolreview.com|access-date=December 20, 2014}}</ref>

==Career==
Brooks made his first rap appearance as an [[opening act]] for [[2Pac]] and [[Digital Underground]] at the age of 12.  He recorded his first album at 15 years old.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.dubcnn.com/interviews/sanquinn/|title=San Quinn Interview (September 2008) : West Coast News Network|publisher=Dubcnn.com|access-date=20 December 2014}}</ref> In addition to a solo career, Brooks is also a member of the San Francisco rap group '''Get Low Playaz'''<ref name="sfbv">{{cite news |title=The Quinngorithm: an interview wit' the prolific Frisco lyricist San Quinn |url=https://sfbayview.com/2023/04/the-quinngorithm-an-interview-wit-the-prolific-frisco-lyricist-san-quinn/ |access-date=1 May 2023 |work=[[San Francisco Bay View]] |date=17 April 2023}}</ref> with [[JT The Bigga Figga]], D-Moe, and Seff Tha Gaffla.<ref>{{cite web |title=Get Low Playaz |url=https://www.discogs.com/artist/332457-Get-Low-Playaz |website=[[Discogs]] |access-date=1 May 2023}}</ref> One of Brooks' most notable tracks is "Shock the Party", which [[Sampling (music)|samples]] [[Whodini|Whodini's]] "[[Back in Black (Whodini album)|One Love]]".<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.whosampled.com/sample/view/22031/San%20Quinn-Shock%20the%20Party_Whodini-One%20Love/|title=San Quinn's Shock the Party sample of Whodini's One Love - WhoSampled|work=WhoSampled|access-date=20 December 2014}}</ref>  The video for "Shock the Party" was filmed in the Fillmore district of San Francisco in the now-defunct Buena Vista Plaza East public housing towers, commonly referred to as OC (Outta Control) Housing Projects.<ref>{{cite news| url=http://articles.sfgate.com/2005-03-28/news/17364577_1_public-housing-housing-towers-housing-authority | work=The San Francisco Chronicle | first=Ilene | last=Lelchuk | title=They don't call the project Outta Control anymore / Success of renewal using HOPE VI is evident all around | date=June 24, 2011|access-date=20 December 2014}}</ref>
