'''Curtis Williams'''  (born January 19, 1976), more commonly known as '''Spider Loc''', is an American rapper and actor. He was formerly signed to [[Interscope Records]] and [[50 Cent]]'s [[G-Unit Records]].

==Biography==
{{BLP sources|date=February 2011}}

Spider Loc was discovered by [[Suge Knight]] and tagged along with [[Death Row Records]], he later appeared on the [[Death Row Records]] released soundtrack to the movie [[Dysfunktional Family (soundtrack)|Dysfunktional Family soundtrack]], and also was affiliated with [[Kurupt]]'s Against Tha Grain record label under the name "Spider", but never signed an agreement. In September 2004 Spider Loc met rapper [[Young Buck]] in [[Atlanta]] and performed for him. A week later in Los Angeles, Young Buck introduced him to 50 Cent on the set of the "[[Shorty Wanna Ride]]" video and he performed a freestyle rap. After four months the rapper signed a record deal with [[G-Unit]].

Spider Loc has since been featured on many G-Unit mixtapes such as ''G-Unit Radio Part 18 - Rags to Riches'' and has also released his own mixtape G-Unit West title ''Bangadoshish'' with artists Young Buck, [[Big Syke]] (from Thug Life), 50 Cent, and [[Olivia (singer)|Olivia]].

In September 2007, he released a street album, ''[[West Kept Secret: The Prequel]]'' and the following June released a follow-up project ''[[Da 1 U Luv 2 Hate]]''.

In an interview with HipHopDX released March 27, 2011, Spider Loc confirmed that he is still signed to G-Unit and did not move to EMI.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/news/id.14538/title.spider-loc-40-glocc-talk-graveyard-shift-release-status-of-crips-bloods |title=Spider Loc & 40 Glocc Talk "Graveyard Shift" Release, Status Of Crips & Bloods &#124; Get The Latest Hip Hop News, Rap News & Hip Hop Album Sales |publisher=HipHop DX |date=March 26, 2011 |access-date=March 30, 2012}}</ref> In 2011 Spider stated that G-Unit West was back and up and running with the arrival of [[Slim da Mobster]].

Spider Loc was dropped from G-Unit in late 2011.

On June 8, 2017, [http://www.datwav.com/remember-g-unit-signee-spider-loc-well-wheel-fortune-video/ Spider Loc appeared on ''Wheel of Fortune'']. During the episode, he stated he is currently employed as a security officer and is the father of 6 kids.<ref>{{Cite web | url=http://www.datwav.com/remember-g-unit-signee-spider-loc-well-wheel-fortune-video/ | title=Remember G-Unit Signee Spider Loc? Well, He Was on Wheel of Fortune (Video)| date=June 17, 2017}}</ref> He also runs his own record label, BAYMAAC Records.

In 2022 he launched a podcast entitled "Factz Ova Feelinz" on [[Alex Alonso]]'s LAnd media Network.
