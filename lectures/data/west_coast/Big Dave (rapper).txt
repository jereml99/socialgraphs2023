'''Big Dave''' (real name David Lewis Blevins) is an Australian [[rapper]], [[record producer]], [[radio host]] and [[Advocate|Community Advocate]]. He is also the founder and owner of [[KP Records]].<ref>{{Citation | last = Verity | first = William| title = Sins of the Father | journal = Illawarra Mercury Weekender | pages = 6 | date = 5 September 2009 }}</ref><ref>{{Citation | last = Skobavac | first = Petal | title = Big Dave Parsons | journal = BMA Magazine | date = 4 September 2008 | url = http://www.bmamag.com/authors/petal-skobavac/# | access-date = 19 September 2009 | archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20110708074356/http://www.bmamag.com/authors/petal-skobavac/ | archive-date = 8 July 2011 | url-status = dead }}</ref><ref>{{Citation |last=Burke |first=Tristan |title=Big Dave - The Raw Storyteller |journal=3D World |date=8 September 2008 |url=http://www.threedworld.com.au/music/features/2008/Big-Dave--The-Raw-Storyteller-_1/ |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20080916163009/http://www.threedworld.com.au/music/features/2008/Big-Dave--The-Raw-Storyteller-_1/ |archivedate=16 September 2008}}</ref>

Since his release from [[Long Bay Correctional Centre|Long Bay Jail]] in 2005,<ref name=telegraph>{{cite web|last=Holmes|first=Peter|title=Internet voice for inmates|url=http://www.dailytelegraph.com.au/news/sydney-nsw/internet-voice-for-inmates/story-e6freuzi-1111113431347|work=The Telegraph|accessdate=1 June 2011|date=2007-04-29}}</ref> Big Dave has gone on to become a well-known Australian [[Rapping|MC]].

==Career==
Big Dave has toured nationally performing with international artists like [[Ice Cube]], [[D12]], [[Tech N9ne]], [[Xzibit]], [[Bone Thugs N Harmony]], [[Kurupt]], [[Obie Trice]], [[KRS-One]], [[De La Soul]], [[Outlawz]], and [[Kool Keith]].<ref name=bma-real>{{cite web|last=Skobavac |first=Pete |title=Big Dave Parsons - Real subject matter |url=http://www.bmamag.com/article/features/20080904-big-dave-parsons-real-subject-matter/ |archive-url=https://archive.today/20131130123319/http://www.bmamag.com/article/features/20080904-big-dave-parsons-real-subject-matter/ |url-status=dead |archive-date=30 November 2013 |work=BMA Magazine |accessdate=1 June 2011 |date=4 September 2008 }}</ref> On top of these international acts Big Dave has also shared the bill with a large list of Australia's top hip hop acts.

Big Dave's debut solo album ''Self Made'' was first released via KP Records/WIDEawake Entertainment LLC on 25 September 2012 in a limited format and was later released in full via KP Records and MGM Distribution on 4 April 2013 with the full release featuring the Big Dave/Snoop Dogg collaboration single "The Original".<ref>{{cite news | url=http://www.news.com.au/entertainment/music/the-aussie-ex-con-snoop-wants-to-make-a-star/story-e6frfn09-1226601505681 | title=Meet Big Dave, the ex-con Snoop Dogg wants to make a star | first=Daniel | last=Piotrowski | date=20 March 2013 | publisher=news.com.au | accessdate=4 April 2013 }}</ref> The album has been described as a blend of Hip Hop, House, street culture and commercial dance music. The album is produced by in-house KP producer Grantwho? and features guest appearances from [[Snoop Dogg]], [[Joell Ortiz]] ([[Slaughterhouse (group)|Slaughterhouse]]), [[WC (rapper)|WC]] ([[Westside Connection]]), [[Kurupt]] ([[Tha Dogg Pound]]), [[Necro (rapper)|Necro]], [[Kyza]] and KP artists Kitty B, Mibs, Pug D and Eitha. ''Self Made'' was mastered by Sameer Sengupta of Ministry of Sound at [[Studios 301]] Sydney.
The first single from "Self Made" titled "The Good Life" ft Kitty B was released on 25 September 2012 and featured on 160 commercial top 40 Australian radio stations.

Big Dave has been a regular feature in the Australian media in recent times including an ABC special on his life and music titled "Beats And Bars".
The 30-minute documentary also featured Ice Cube who spoke of his pride in Big Dave's achievements, KRS One and John Payne.
Other highlights include News Limited press, Fairfax Media press and various print, web, TV appearances and radio broadcasts.

Big Dave is also part-time host and producer of ''Jailbreak Radio'' on Sydney's [[2SER]] Radio.<ref name=actnow>{{cite web|last=msav89|title=Prisoner health in Australia|url=http://www.actnow.com.au/Issues/Prisoner_health_in_Australia.aspx|work=ActNow|accessdate=1 June 2011|date=2 September 2008|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20110720075433/http://www.actnow.com.au/Issues/Prisoner_health_in_Australia.aspx|archivedate=20 July 2011|url-status=dead}}</ref><ref name=crcnsw-radio>{{cite web|last=Owens|first=Angela|title=JAILBREAK SPECIAL|url=http://www.crcnsw.org.au/services/services/Jailbreak.htm|work=Community Restorative Centre Inc.|accessdate=1 June 2011|date=2008-10-23}}</ref><ref name=bma-radio>{{cite web|title=tidbits|url=http://issuu.com/bmamagazine/docs/bma_333|work=BMA Magazine Issue 333|accessdate=1 June 2011|page=10|date=2009-09-17}}</ref> He has been a part of the team since 2009, the ''Jailbreak'' programme became syndicated nationally in Australia via the Community Radio Network service.<ref name=cbaa>{{cite web|last=Walters |first=M |title=Jailbreak (CRN) |url=http://www.cbaa.org.au/news/Jailbreak-CRN |work=Community Broadcasting Association of Australia |accessdate=1 June 2011 |date=7 September 2007 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20110726221356/http://www.cbaa.org.au/news/Jailbreak-CRN |archivedate=26 July 2011}}</ref>

Big Dave also manages various community projects including the infamous ''Behind The Walls'' project which supports Australian prison inmates and the ''Workshop Program'', a hip hop based course aimed at helping troubled teens in the [[Australian Capital Territory|ACT]]. He was a speaker on [[Triple J]]'s ''The Hack Half Hour'' television program—the ''Lock Up'' special on 24 November 2008.<ref name=jjj-lockup>{{cite web|last=Cannane|first=Steve|title=The Hack Half Hour – Lock Up|url=http://www.abc.net.au/triplej/hackhalfhour/|work=Triple J|publisher=Australian Broadcasting Corporation|accessdate=1 June 2011|date=2008-11-24}}</ref>

Big Dave released a single titled "Artificial Insemination" in December of 2022. This song was debuted as "The hottest song of 2022."
