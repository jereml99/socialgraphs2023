'''Marvin Watson Jr.''' (born March 19, 1976),<ref name=cbi>{{cite web|title=California Birth Index, 1905–1995|url=http://www.familytreelegends.com/records/calbirths?c=search&first=marvin&last=watson&spelling=Exact&4_year=&4_month=3&4_day=19&5=&7=&SubmitSearch.x=63&SubmitSearch.y=19&SubmitSearch=Submit|publisher=Family Tree Legends Records Collection (Online Database)}}</ref> better known by his [[stage name]] '''Messy Marv''', is an American [[rapper]] who began his career in the [[Fillmore District]] of [[San Francisco, California]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.siccness.net/wp/cash-lord-mess-california-live-presents-the-second-round-on-me-cash-lords-tour|title=Cash Lord Mess 'The Second Round On Me Cash Lords Tour''|website=Siccness.net|language=en-US|access-date=2018-09-26}}</ref> Messy was also known as a collaborator in the rap group Kaalman's Krew.{{Citation needed|date=May 2023}}

==Career==

Messy Marv released his first rap album, ''[[Messy Situationz]]'', in 1996, when he was in 10th grade. ''[[Explosive Mode]]'', his 1998 collaboration album with [[San Quinn]], sold more than 50,000 copies.<ref name="mmm">{{cite news |last1=Caples |first1=Garrett |title=Making Messy Marv |url=https://sfbgarchive.48hills.org/sfbgarchive/2006/11/21/making-messy-marv/ |access-date=8 May 2023 |work=[[San Francisco Bay Guardian]] |date=21 November 2006}}</ref>

In 2005, Messy Marv started his own record label, Scalen LLC,<ref>{{cite web |title=SCALEN LLC |url=https://opencorporates.com/companies/us_ca/200514710037 |website=[[OpenCorporates]] |access-date=9 May 2023}}</ref> and released a barrage of albums in 2004-2006. During the [[Hyphy|Hyphy movement]], his songs "Get on My Hype" and "So Hood" received frequent radio airplay.<ref name="mmm"/>

On November 2, 2007, Messy Marv was released from jail after serving twelve months on weapons charges.<ref name="dream deferred">{{cite news |last1=Arnold |first1=Eric K. |title=For S.F. rappers, another dream deferred |url=http://sfgate.com/cgi-bin/article.cgi?f=/c/a/2007/05/15/DDG47PPKBT1.DTL |access-date=8 May 2023 |work=[[San Francisco Chronicle]] |date=15 May 2007}}</ref> Before, during and following his jail sentence, he released several albums which made the [[Billboard charts|charts]]: ''[[Cake & Ice Cream 2]]'', released in 2009 and peaked on the [[R&B/Hip-Hop Albums]] chart at #39 and on the [[Rap Albums]] chart at #12, and ''[[Blow: Blocks and Boat Docks]]'', a collaboration album with [[Berner (rapper)|Berner]] released in 2010 which peaked at #48 on the R&B/Hip-Hop Albums chart.{{Citation needed|date=May 2023}}

In the spring of 2012, Messy Marv released the mixtape, ''Da Frank Lucas Dat Neva Wore Da Mink Coat'' and the single, "You Gotta Pay Me". In 2013, he announced the release of his newest LP "Playboy Gangsta" under his new moniker "Lil Paper'd Up Mess", and released 3 singles as promotionals for the album: "We Killas", "Beautiful Kalifornia", & "A Girlfriend Ain't What I Need". Marv confirmed via Twitter a release date of July 23, 2013 for the LP.  Early in 2014  with a  guest feature on single "World Wide Mob" alongside  Montana Montana Montana, [[Joe Blow]], Ap.9, and Fed X<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.thizzler.com/blog/2014/8/4/montana-montana-montana-ft-messy-marv-joe-blow-ap9-fed-x-wor.html |title=Montana Montana Montana ft. Messy Marv, Joe Blow, AP9, Fed-X - World Wide Mob |website=Thizzler.com |date=2014-08-04 |access-date=2020-03-31}}</ref>

On July 3, 2018, Messy Marv and San Quinn released a 12 song compilation titled ''Joc Nation (Well Connected)''.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Messy Marv & San Quinn Present: Jocnation (Well Connected) by JocNation, San Quinn & Messy Marv|date=2018-07-03|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/messy-marv-san-quinn-present-jocnation-well-connected/1407066804|website=Itunes.apple.com|language=en-US|access-date=2018-07-07}}</ref>

On December 3, 2018, Messy Marv aka Cashlord Mess signed to Mozzy Records.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Diddy |first1=Shay |title=Mozzy Signs Messy Marv To Mozzy Records! |url=https://kmel.iheart.com/featured/shay-diddy/content/2018-12-04-mozzy-signs-messy-marv-to-mozzy-records/ |website=[[KMEL]] |access-date=9 May 2023 |date=4 December 2018}}</ref>

==Personal life==
As a teenager, Messy Marv [[Dropping out|dropped out]] of high school because of family problems. Rapper [[San Quinn]] and his mother took Messy Marv in when his living situation was unstable. As his music became more popular, Messy Marv struggled with [[substance abuse]] and addiction.<ref name="mmm"/><ref>{{cite web |title=Messy Marv Talks About Smoking Coke, Says He Gave His Chain Back to Mozzy |url=https://www.vladtv.com/article/254099/messy-marv-talks-about-smoking-coke-says-he-gave-his-chain-back-to-mozzy |website=[[DJ Vlad|VladTV]] |access-date=9 May 2023 |date=16 April 2019}}</ref>

In recent years, Messy Marv has been in and out of prison.<ref name="at large">{{cite news |last1=Caples |first1=Garrett |title=Messy Marv at large |url=https://sfbgarchive.48hills.org/sfbgarchive/2008/01/16/messy-marv-large/ |work=[[San Francisco Bay Guardian]] |access-date=8 May 2023 |date=16 January 2008}}</ref><ref name="dream deferred"/> He was arrested again in 2019 for robbery, among other charges.<ref>{{cite web |title=Arrest Information for Marvin Watson Jr |url=https://www.localcrimenews.com/welcome/detail/42918276/marvin-watson-jr-arrest.html |website=localcrimenews.com |access-date=8 May 2023 |date=6 December 2019}}</ref>

==Feuds==
Messy Marv has feuded with a number of other rappers over the years.

===Obie Trice===
In 2005, Messy Marv was involved in a feud with [[G-Unit]], which escalated when [[Obie Trice]], a member of [[Shady Records]], said in a radio interview on [[KMEL]]: "in San Francisco I have to cover my ass."

Following this, Guce and Messy Marv released a diss track, "50 Explanations", on the album ''Pill Music: The Rico Act, Vol. 1''. [[50 Cent]] and [[Sha Money XL]] then exchanged words over the phone with Guce's camp. 
Guce stated afterwards, "it's over with, and it never kicked off".<ref>{{cite web |title=Guce Biography |url=http://www.beatmp3.net/artist/guce.html |website=Beatmp3.net |access-date=14 May 2023 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150923183057/http://www.beatmp3.net/artist/guce.html |archive-date=23 September 2015 |url-status=dead}}</ref>

===San Quinn===
On September 22, 2008, Messy Marv initiated a feud with his cousin San Quinn, by calling him a [[Informant|snitch]]. On December 11, Quinn told HipHopDX that he "loves" Messy Marv despite the [[diss tracks]] that they have both exchanged.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/interviews/id.1278/title.san-quinn-fillmores-finest|title=San Quinn: Fillmore's Finest|author=HipHopDX|date=12 December 2008|work=HipHopDX|access-date=20 March 2015|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130313180058/http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/interviews/id.1278/title.san-quinn-fillmores-finest|archive-date=13 March 2013|url-status=dead}}</ref> In a 2010 interview with WordofSouth, he stated the reason for the beef was because he was "talking too much", but that they are attempting to squash the feud.<ref>[http://www.wordofsouth.com/myblock/showthread.php?85017-Messy-Marv-Interview ]{{dead link|date=March 2020}}</ref>

===Spider Loc===
On April 19, 2007, rapper [[Spider Loc]] released a diss track towards Marv, and [[Game (rapper)|Game]] called "Ova Killa" featuring Papa Smurf.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/singles/id.1915/title.spider-loc-f-papa-smurf-ova-kill-the-game-messy-marv-e-40-diss|title=Spider Loc f. Papa Smurf|author=HipHopDX|date=20 April 2007|work=HipHopDX|access-date=20 March 2015|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20121104062208/http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/singles/id.1915/title.spider-loc-f-papa-smurf-ova-kill-the-game-messy-marv-e-40-diss|archive-date=4 November 2012|url-status=dead}}</ref>
