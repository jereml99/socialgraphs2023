'''Aulsondro "Novelist" Hamilton''', better known by his stage name '''Emcee N.I.C.E.''', is an American [[Christian hip hop]] recording artist.[[rapper]], singer, songwriter, record and [[film producer]], record executive, actor, an American Inventor and a Entrepreneur and former lead vocalist/rapper of the duo [[KansasCali]].
He is the co-creator of the animated music series [[Da Jammies]], The co-founder of The Stellar Award winning radio station [[God's House of Hip Hop Radio|God’s House of Hip Hop Radio]]<ref>{{cite web|url =https://lasentinel.net/emcee-n-i-c-e-and-chani-g-takes-christian-hip-hop-global.html|title=Emcee N.I.C.E. & Chanie G take Christian Hip Hop Global|website=lasentinel.net|date=April 10, 2019|accessdate=April 20, 2023}}</ref> powered by [[Dash Radio]], A Best Selling Author for his book “Music Release University”.<ref>{{cite web|url =https://www.thehypemagazine.com/2022/08/from-billboard-hits-to-music-business-emcee-n-i-c-e-releases-new-book/|title=Music Release University: The Indies' Guide to Releasing Music|website=thehypemagazine.com|date=August 23, 2022|accessdate=April 20, 2023}}</ref><ref name="rapzilla.com">{{cite web|url =https://rapzilla.com/2022-08-emcee-n-i-c-e-releases-indie-artist-strategy-guide-book-it-reaches-no-1/|title=Music Release University: The Indies' Guide to Releasing Music Reaches #1|website=Rapzilla.com|date=August 23, 2022 |accessdate=August 26, 2022}}</ref> He is president of two non-profit organizations, The iTL Foundation (Information, Technology & Literacy) of Los Angeles <ref>{{cite web|url=http://itlfoundation.org/about-us/|title=Information Technology & Literacy Foundation|website=itlfoundation.org|date=January 1, 2013|accessdate= April 20, 2023}}</ref> and Urbanomics 101,<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.urbanomics101.org/company/|title=Urbanomics 101 Foundation|website=urbanomics101.org|date=January 1, 2010|accessdate= April 20, 2023}}</ref> the Managing Editor for RYZE Magazine,<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.ryzemagazine.com/masthead|title=RYZE Magazine|website=ryzemagazine.com|date=September 2, 2020|accessdate= April 20, 2023}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.prlog.org/12836441-ryze-magazine-announces-emcee-nice-as-new-managing-editor.html|title=Official Press Release|website=prlog.org|date=September 2, 2020|accessdate= April 20, 2023}}</ref> a christian hip hop lifestyle magazine and an American Inventor.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://image-ppubs.uspto.gov/dirsearch-public/print/downloadPdf/20060254936|title=United States Patent & Trademark Office|website=uspto.gov|date=November 16, 2006|accessdate= April 20, 2023}}</ref> He is the co-producer of 2 Pac's [[Thugz Mansion]] acoustic, the first single released from the certified triple platinum album [[Better Dayz]] and the Nas acoustic [[Thugz Mansion|Thugz Mansion (N.Y.)]], off of the certified platinum album [[God's Son (album)|God's Son]]

Emcee N.I.C.E.’s debut recording as a Christian Hip Hop Recording artist came when the album [[Praise]] was released in 2017 and became his first solo project to reach No. 1 on the [[Billboard Charts|Billboard Top Gospel Albums]] and “Billboard Gospel Album Sales” the album would yield Five No. 1 hit records on the ''Billboard'' charts in "I Got Angels"<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/charts/gospel-digital-song-sales/2017-11-18|title=Billboard Digital Song Sales Chart|website=billboard.com|date=November 28, 2013|accessdate= September 21, 2021}}</ref><ref>{{cite magazine|url=http://www.billboard.com/biz/charts/hot-singles-sales|title=Billboard Hot Single Sales Chart|magazine=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20171025235344/http://www.billboard.com/biz/charts/hot-singles-sales|accessdate= September 21, 2021|archive-date=2017-10-25}}</ref> claiming two positions along with "Glory to God" (ft. Fred Hammond)<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/charts/gospel-digital-song-sales/2018-05-11|title=Billboard Hot Single Sales Chart|website=billboard.com|date=November 28, 2013|accessdate=September 21, 2021}}</ref> & [[MRC Data|Nielsen BDS]] including a No. 1 [[Gospel music|gospel]] album in ''[[Praise (album)|Praise]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/charts/gospel-albums/2017-12-02|title=Billboard Top Gospel Albums Chart|website=billboard.com|date=January 2, 2013|accessdate=September 21, 2021}}</ref>

Emcee N.I.C.E. received nominations for Rap Hip-Hop Gospel CD of the Year and Gospel Hip Hop Radio Station of Year at the [[34th Annual Stellar Awards|34th]] [[Stellar Awards|Annual Stellar Awards]], the last of which he won. He is also a 12 time "The Gospel Spin Awards" winner, in the categories of "Internet Radio Personality of the Year," 2 time "Best Radio Show 2 or More – (God's Calamari w/ Emcee N.I.C.E. & The God Squad)," 2 time "Voice of the Year, Station Manager/Program Director of the Year" are the notables, he received a total of 17 Nominations in Gospel radio from the Gospel Spin Award and in 2021 Emcee N.I.C.E. was honored as "Radio Man of the Year."{{cn|date=June 2023}}

Emcee N.I.C.E. also known as Novelist with the duo KansasCali, has been on six motion picture soundtracks, Oscar Award Winning film [[Crash (2004 film)|Crash]] song "If I...", Mr. & Mrs. Smith (International Soundtrack)<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.imdb.com/title/tt0356910/soundtrack/|title= Mr. & Mrs. Smith (2005 Soundtracks)|website=IMDB.com|date=June 10, 2005|accessdate=April 20, 2023}}</ref> song "If I Never See You Again", [[Haven (soundtrack)|Haven Original Soundtrack]] song "Uuuh", [[Kickin' It Old Skool]]<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.imdb.com/title/tt0772178/soundtrack/|title= Kickin' It Old Skool (Soundtrack)|website=IMDB.com|date=April 27, 2007|accessdate=April 20, 2023}}</ref> song "The Life", [[Once in a Lifetime: The Extraordinary Story of the New York Cosmos]] song "U Gotta Fight!"
