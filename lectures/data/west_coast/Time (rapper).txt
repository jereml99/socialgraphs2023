'''Christopher Steele''', better known by his [[stage name]] '''Time''', is an American [[indie hip hop]] artist from [[Denver]], [[Colorado]].<ref name=westword2013/> He is one of the original members of Dirty Laboratory Productions.<ref name=westword2013/> He is also one half of the hip hop group [[Calm (music group)|Calm]].<ref name=westword2013/> Aside from music, Steele is also an investigative journalist.

==Life and career==
Time released his first album, ''Litterture'', in 2004.<ref name=westword2013>{{cite web|url=http://www.westword.com/2013-10-31/music/chris-steele-time-newstalgia/full/|title=Time makes memories with Newstalgia|work=[[Denver Westword]]|first=Tom|last=Murphy|date=October 31, 2013}}</ref> He then went on to release the album, ''Anti-Smiles'', in 2006 under the moniker [[Calm (music group)|Calm]] with producer Awareness.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.rapreviews.com/archive/2006_12_antismiles.html|title=Calm :: Anti-Smiles :: Dirty Laboratory Productions|work=RapReviews|first=Jordan|last=Selbo|date=December 19, 2006}}</ref> ''[[Denver Westword]]'' gave the album a favorable review, commenting that "[although] ''Anti-Smiles'' reflects the dark side of our collective psyche, it's also a statement of hope in the face of fear and self-doubt."<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.westword.com/2007-04-12/music/calm/|title=Calm - Anti-Smiles - Dirty Laboratory|work=[[Denver Westword]]|first=Tom|last=Murphy|date=April 12, 2007}}</ref> In 2007, he released his solo album, ''The Fantastic Reality''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.rapreviews.com/archive/2007_12_fantasticreality.html|title=Time :: The Fantastic Reality :: Dirty Laboratory Productions|work=RapReviews|first=Matt|last=Jost|date=December 4, 2007}}</ref> ''Benzine Magazine'' gave the album 3 out of 5.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.benzinemag.net/2007/12/20/chroniques-express-45/|title=Chroniques Express 45|work=Benzine Magazine|first=Benoit|last=Richard|date=December 20, 2007}}</ref>

Time currently serves as an associate professor of History at [[Regis University]] in Denver, Colorado.  Time's scholarly areas include politics and tort law, ethics and social justice, pedagogy, and the history of social change.  Steele received a Doctor's of Jurisprudence (J.D.,) from Creighton University.  Time teaches courses on History and Social Change.<ref>[https://www.regis.edu/RC/Academics/Departments-and-Faculty/History-and-Politics/Faculty/Steele-Chris] {{dead link|date=May 2023}}</ref>

Time released his solo album, ''[[Naked Dinner]]'', in 2009.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://blogs.westword.com/backbeat/2009/10/qa_with_time.php|title=Q&A with Time (1/2)|work=[[Denver Westword]]|first=Dave|last=Herrera|date=October 6, 2009}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://blogs.westword.com/backbeat/2009/10/qa_with_time.php?page=2|title=Q&A with Time (2/2)|work=[[Denver Westword]]|first=Dave|last=Herrera|date=October 6, 2009}}</ref> It featured guest appearances from Damon Jevon, [[Sole (hip hop artist)|Sole]], [[C-Rayz Walz]] and Extra Kool.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.rapreviews.com/archive/2009_09_nakeddinner.html|title=Time :: Naked Dinner :: Dirty Laboratory|work=RapReviews|first=Justin|last=Chandler|date=September 15, 2009}}</ref> The album was described by ''Denver Westword'' as "a dark affair tonally reminiscent of ''[[There's a Riot Goin' On]]'', with a similar socially critical agenda."<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.westword.com/2009-10-08/music/time/full/|title=Time - Naked Dinner - Dirty Laboratory|work=[[Denver Westword]]|first=Tom|last=Murphy|date=October 8, 2009}}</ref>

In 2010, Time released 12 EPs in 12 months called the ''12 Headed Hydra'' series for free download.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://blogs.westword.com/backbeat/2011/07/time_releases_new_video_for_wh.php|title=A first look at Time's new video for "Why My Money Ghost" from his 12 Headed Hydra series|work=[[Denver Westword]]|first=Cory|last=Lamz|date=July 14, 2011}}</ref> In 2013, he released ''[[Newstalgia]]'', an album featuring contributions from the likes of [[Ceschi]], [[Jake One]], [[Factor (producer)|Factor]], Budo, Man Mantis, and [[Xiu Xiu]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.urb.com/2013/09/19/premiere-time-streams-newstalgia-lp/|title=Premiere: Time Streams 'Newstalgia' LP|work=[[URB (magazine)|URB]]|first=Brett|last=Uddenberg|date=September 19, 2013}}</ref>

As a journalist, Chris Steele has written for ''[[Examiner.com|The Examiner]]'' as a progressive politics reporter.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.heyreverb.com/2011/02/11/the-mile-high-makeout-hip-hop-artist-time-completes-year-long-12-part-opus/|title=The Mile High Makeout: Hip-hop artist Time completes year-long, 12-part opus|work=Reverb|first=Eryc|last=Eyl|date=February 11, 2011}}</ref> He has written on topics ranging from UFOs to the death of [[Fred Hampton]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://blogs.westword.com/backbeat/2013/05/westword_freeloader_time_they_call_us_the_irish.php|title=Listen to "They Call Us the Irish," a new track from Time's forthcoming album, Newstalgia|work=[[Denver Westword]]|first=Noah|last=Hubbell|date=May 24, 2013}}</ref> In 2011, he has received attention for writing in favor of the [[Occupy Wall Street]] movement and was interviewed on Mike Feder's XM radio show ''Occupied Territory''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://occupiedterritory.podbean.com/2011/11/30/why-are-the-police-out-of-control-or-are-they-in-control-and-doing-just-what-they-were-ordered-to-d/|title=Why are the police out-of-control?|work=Occupied Territory|first=Mike|last=Feder|date=November 30, 2011}}</ref>
