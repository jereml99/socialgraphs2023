'''Nicholas Neil Carter''' (born March 16, 1978), better known by his [[stage name]] '''Murs''', is an American [[rapping|rapper]]. His name is an [[acronym]] (or [[backronym]]) for which he himself has created multiple meanings, such as "Making the Universe Recognize and Submit" or "Making Underground Raw Shit."<ref>{{cite web|url=http://laist.com/2008/10/07/laist_interview.php|title=LAist Interview: MURS|publisher=LAist|date=October 7, 2008|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20081211032229/http://laist.com/2008/10/07/laist_interview.php|archive-date=December 11, 2008}}</ref>

Murs is a former member of the rap group [[Living Legends (group)|Living Legends]], along with [[Luckyiam]], [[Sunspot Jonz]], [[The Grouch (rapper)|The Grouch]], [[Scarub]], [[Eligh]], Aesop, Bicasso and Arata.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.xlr8r.com/features/2008/04/living-legends-hip-hop-masterpie|title=The Living Legends' Hip-Hop Masterpiece|publisher=XLR8R|author=Eric K. Arnold|date=April 1, 2005|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131026140852/http://www.xlr8r.com/features/2008/04/living-legends-hip-hop-masterpie|archive-date=October 26, 2013}}</ref> He is currently a member of several groups: [[3 Melancholy Gypsys]] (along with Scarub and Eligh),<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.huffingtonpost.com/2012/05/18/lacma-hip-hop-concerts-mu_n_1526438.html|title=LACMA Hip Hop Concerts: Murs Of 3MG Kicks Off Summer Series (VIDEO, PHOTOS)|work=The Huffington Post|author=Anna Almendrala|date=May 18, 2012}}</ref> [[Felt (hip hop group)|Felt]] (along with [[Slug (rapper)|Slug]]),<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.urb.com/2009/11/30/felt-felt-3-a-tribute-to-rosie-perez-review/|title=Felt - Felt 3: A Tribute to Rosie Perez|publisher=URB|author=Adam Figman|date=November 30, 2009|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100213162621/http://www.urb.com/2009/11/30/felt-felt-3-a-tribute-to-rosie-perez-review/|archive-date=February 13, 2010}}</ref> and Melrose (along with [[Terrace Martin]]).<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ballerstatus.com/2011/01/05/murs-terrace-martin-on-melrose-music-video/|title=Murs & Terrace Martin: On Melrose (Music Video)|publisher=Baller Status|date=January 5, 2011}}</ref> He is also a vocalist in The Invincibles, along with [[Whole Wheat Bread (band)|Whole Wheat Bread]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.okayplayer.com/news/murs-whole-wheat-bread-are-the-invincibles-free-ep-video.html|title=Murs & Whole Wheat Bread Are The Invincibles (Free EP + Video)|publisher=Okay Player|date=July 6, 2012}}</ref>

On October 13, 2016, Murs set a [[Guinness World Record]] for [[rapping]] for 24 hours non-stop during a [[live stream]] on [[Twitch (service)|Twitch]].

On June 11, 2013, Murs released an album as lead vocalist of a band called [[The White Mandingos]]. The album is entitled ''The Ghetto is Tryna Kill Me''.<ref name=":0">Frannie Kelley (August 13, 2013). "[https://www.npr.org/blogs/therecord/2013/08/13/211396093/the-white-mandingos-music-that-defies-definition The White Mandingos: Music That Defies Definition]". National Public Radio.</ref>

Murs signed with Strange Music in 2014 and has released three studio albums through the label. His record deal with Strange Music was a three-album deal.

==Career==
Murs formed [[3 Melancholy Gypsys]] with fellow [[Alexander Hamilton High School (Los Angeles)|Alexander Hamilton High School]] classmates [[Scarub]] and [[Eligh]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://the5thelementmag.com/2012/05/31/lacma-through-the-mic-featuring-murs-and-3mg/|title=LACMA: Through The Mic featuring Murs and 3MG|publisher=The 5th Element Magazine|date=May 31, 2012}}</ref> The group became friends with [[Mystik Journeymen]], and joined them in the [[Living Legends (group)|Living Legends]] collective in 1996.<ref name="allmusic">{{cite web|url=http://www.allmusic.com/artist/murs-mn0000934439|title=MURS Biography|website=Allmusic|author=Bradley Torreano}}</ref> His solo debut album, ''[[F'Real]]'', was released on Veritech in 1997.<ref>{{cite magazine|url=http://exclaim.ca/Interviews/WebExclusive/murs|title=Murs - Interviews|magazine=Exclaim!|author=Luke Fox|date=November 2011}}</ref>

Alongside [[Slug (rapper)|Slug]], Murs has been part of the group [[Felt (hip hop group)|Felt]] since 2002.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://pitchfork.com/news/36391-aesop-rock-atmospheres-slug-and-murs-team-for-felt-3/|publisher=Pitchfork Media|title=Aesop Rock, Atmosphere's Slug, and Murs Team for Felt 3|author=Tom Breihanon|date=September 3, 2009}}</ref> ''[[The End of the Beginning (MURS album)|The End of the Beginning]]'', his debut album on [[Definitive Jux]], was released in 2003.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://pitchfork.com/reviews/albums/5513-the-end-of-the-beginning/|publisher=Pitchfork Media|title=Murs: The End of the Beginning|author=Matt Kallman|date=April 23, 2003}}</ref> Murs released ''[[Murs 3:16: The 9th Edition]]'', an album entirely produced by [[9th Wonder]], in 2004.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://pitchfork.com/reviews/albums/5514-murs-316-the-9th-edition/|publisher=Pitchfork Media|title=Murs: Murs 3:16 - The 9th Edition|author=Rollie Pemberton|date=March 15, 2004}}</ref> After another collaborative album with 9th Wonder, ''[[Murray's Revenge]]'', in 2006,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://pitchfork.com/reviews/albums/5515-murrays-revenge/|publisher=Pitchfork Media|title=Murs: Murray's Revenge|author=Peter Macia|date=March 21, 2006}}</ref> Murs signed a contract with [[Warner Music Group|Warner Bros.]], his debut album for the label being ''[[Murs for President]]''.<ref>{{cite magazine|url=http://consequenceofsound.net/2008/10/album-review-murs-for-president/|title=Album Review: Murs – Murs For President|magazine=Consequence of Sound|author=Michael Denslow|date=October 15, 2008}}</ref> It was preceded by ''[[Sweet Lord]]'' which was given away free to fans.<ref>{{cite magazine|url=http://exclaim.ca/Reviews/HipHop/murs_9th_wonder-sweet_lord|title=Murs & 9th Wonder - Sweet Lord|magazine=Exclaim!|author=Omar Mouallem|date=September 2008}}</ref>

Murs released ''Varsity Blues 2'', a follow-up to his ''[[Varsity Blues (EP)|Varsity Blues]]'' EP, with production duties handled by [[Aesop Rock]], [[Terrace Martin]] and [[Droop-E]], in 2011.<ref>{{cite magazine|url=http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/news/id.14723/title.murs-releases-sequel-varsity-blues-2-production-by-aesop-rock-droop-e|title=Murs Releases Sequel "Varsity Blues 2," Production By Aesop Rock, Droop-E|magazine=Hip Hop DX|author=Jake Paine|date=April 13, 2011|access-date=December 14, 2012|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140903125750/http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/news/id.14723/title.murs-releases-sequel-varsity-blues-2-production-by-aesop-rock-droop-e|archive-date=September 3, 2014|url-status=dead}}</ref> He released ''[[This Generation (Murs and Fashawn album)|This Generation]]'', a collaborative album with [[Fashawn]], in 2012.<ref>{{cite magazine|url=http://exclaim.ca/Reviews/HipHop/murs_fashawn-this_generation|title=Murs & Fashawn - This Generation|magazine=Exclaim!|author=Luke Fox|date=September 24, 2012}}</ref>

Murs was invited by [[Sacha Jenkins]], a hip hop journalist, to join the group [[The White Mandingos]], a collaboration between Jenkins and [[Bad Brains]] bassist [[Daryl Jenifer]].<ref>Jay Balfour (July 5, 2013). [http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/interviews/id.2139/title.the-white-mandingos-analyze-adam-yauch-s-role-in-their-formation "The White Mandingos Analyze Adam Yauch's Role In Their Formation"] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140808061212/http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/interviews/id.2139/title.the-white-mandingos-analyze-adam-yauch-s-role-in-their-formation |date=2014-08-08 }}. Hip Hop DX.</ref> They released their first album, ''The Ghetto is Tryna Kill Me'', on June 11, 2013 under the Fat Beats record label.<ref>Jake Crates (March 4, 2013). [http://allhiphop.com/2013/03/04/murs-and-new-group-the-white-mandingos-announce-new-concept-album/ "Murs and New Group “The White Mandingos” Announce New Concept Album"]. All Hip Hop.</ref>

On February 7, 2014, it was announced via [[Tech N9ne]]'s Facebook page that Murs was signed to his record label [[Strange Music]].<ref>{{cite web|last=Rys |first=Dan |url=http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2014/02/murs-signs-tech-n9ne-strange-music/ |title=Murs Signs With Tech N9ne's Strange Music - XXL |publisher=Xxlmag.com |date=2014-02-07 |access-date=2014-07-13}}</ref> Later that year, Murs teamed up with fellow Strange Music members Mayday! to drop the ''!Mursday¡'' album<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/rap-music/reviews/2014/06/mayday-murs-mursday-album-review/|title=¡Mayday! And Murs Craft A Ripe Summer Album With '¡MURSDAY!' - XXL|website=XXL Mag|language=en|access-date=2018-04-10}}</ref> as his first release on the label. He then released ''[[Have a Nice Life (album)|Have a Nice Life]]'' on May 18, 2015, ''[[Captain California]]'' on March 10, 2017, and ''A Strange Journey into the Unimaginable'' on his birthday in 2018 – all through Strange Music. In between projects, Murs partnered once again with 9th Wonder to create ''[[Brighter Daze]]'', which was released on December 31, 2015.
