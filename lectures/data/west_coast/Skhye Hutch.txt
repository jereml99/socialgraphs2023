'''Derrick Hutchins, Jr.''', better known as his stage name '''Skhye Hutch''', is an American [[hip hop music|hip hop]] record producer from [[Los Angeles]], [[California]]. The two-time Grammy nominee attended [[Pacific Palisades High School]] and graduated from the Musicians Institution in 2004.

He began working with the production R&B duo [[The Underdogs (duo)|The Underdogs]]. During this time, he met Rob Knox, now a member of [[The Y's]]. During his apprenticeship he met [[1500 or Nothin']], [[Tyrese Gibson|Tyrese]], Siege Monstracity and [[Curtiss King]].

==Career==
In 2007, Skhye signed his first development deal with Mad Heaven as a producer and artist for the group Reign Major. The group released the album ''Growing Pains'', on which all but one song was produced by Skhye.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Major|first1=Reign|title=Growing Pains (Album)|url=http://www.tower.com/growing-pains-reign-major-cd/wapi/116429145|date=2007}}</ref> The [[Digi+Phonics]] affiliate was introduced to [[Carson, California|Carson]]-based independent record label [[Top Dawg Entertainment]] (TDE) recording artists [[Black Hippy]] by Grammy Winning producer [[Tae Beast]], during the [[Section 80]] mix-tape era.

In 2010, Skhye Hutch produced "Space Bars" by [[Curtiss King]] featuring [[Ab-Soul]] & Young Rook.<ref>{{cite web|title=New Music|url=http://bricktoyaface.com/2010/08/25/curtiss-king-scumbags-ft-ab-soul-stevie-crooks-prod-jansport-j-video/|date=Aug 25, 2010|access-date=February 2, 2016|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20101207093846/http://bricktoyaface.com/2010/08/25/curtiss-king-scumbags-ft-ab-soul-stevie-crooks-prod-jansport-j-video/|archive-date=December 7, 2010|url-status=dead}}</ref>

2012 proved to be a banner year for Skhye as he began to work with TDE artists on solo projects. Hutch produced three hip hop tracks for Ab-Soul's debut album ''Control System''.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Flex|first1=Funkmaster|title=Ab-Soul – Featuring Kendrick Lamar "ILLuminate" [VIDEO]|url=http://www.hot97.com/news/funkmaster-flex/ab-soul-–-featuring-kendrick-lamar-illuminate-video|website=www.hot97.com|date=Jan 17, 2013}}</ref> The highly successful “calm and stoic song” single "ILLuminate". He also produced the album tracks "Empathy" featuring Alori Joh, Javonte and "Beautiful Death" featuring TDE Vice President Punch and [[Ash Riser]].<ref>{{cite web|author1=Jordan Sargent|title="ILLuminate" [ft. Kendrick Lamar]|website=[[Pitchfork (website)|Pitchfork]]|url=http://pitchfork.com/reviews/tracks/13575-ab-soul-illuminate-ft-kendrick-lamar/|date=May 25, 2012}}</ref>

Building on his relationships, he landed a single on [[Kendrick Lamar]]’s 2012 highly anticipated, ''[[Good Kid Maad City|Good Kid M.A.A.D. City]]'' producing ''Dying of Thirst''. The album was nominated at the [[56th Annual Grammy Awards]] for five awards including [[Grammy Album of the Year|Album of The Year]], [[Best Rap Album]], [[Best New Artist]], [[Best Rap/Sung Collaboration]] and [[Best Rap Performance]].<ref>{{cite news|author1=Hannah Karp|title=Grammy Nominations 2014: Jay Z Leads Field|newspaper=Wall Street Journal|url=https://blogs.wsj.com/speakeasy/2013/12/06/jay-z-leads-nominees-for-2014-grammy-awards-complete-list/|date=Dec 6, 2013}}</ref>

Linking back with Curtiss King for one of the “most highly anticipated independent releases from the west coast.”, ''Atychiphobia'', Hutch produced "The Bottom" featuring Brooke Taylor.<ref>{{cite web|author1=Will|title=Curtiss King – "Atychiphobia (The Fear of Failure)|url=http://westcoastrydaz.com/2012/12/05/curtiss-king-atychiphobia-the-fear-of-failure/|date=Dec 5, 2012}}</ref>

Venturing into co-producing, Skhye Hutch and Tae Beast formulated the music production company WeirrdPeople in 2013. The production duo worked with J-GO on the ''Mr.Goins II'' album producing tracks Underdog, Kush & 2k, Down and Catch Me.

In 2014, he rejoined Ab-Soul on the ''[[These Days...]]'' album producing "Feeling Us" featuring Jay Rock. The year continued with a feature on iTunes as the best hip hop album of the year, ''Under Pressure'' by [[Def Jam Recordings]] & Visionary Music Group rapper [[Logic (rapper)|Logic]].<ref>{{cite web|author1=Staff Writer|url=http://www.undergroundhiphop.com/logic-under-pressure/DEFB002212602CD/|website=/www.undergroundhiphop.com|date=2014|title = New Music}}</ref> The song "Growing Pains III" was co-produced by Tae Beast, 6ix, and Skhye Hutch, and was released in October 2014.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/rap-music/reviews/2014/10/logic-under-pressure-album-review/|website=XXLMag.com|date=Oct 2014|title = New Music}}</ref> Reaching out to the [[Bronx]] emerging artist [[Euro League]], Hutch delivered “an atmospheric beat” on the single "Gold Chains In a Dungeon" featuring Doley Barnays off the ''Euro Trip: Continuum'' EP of the New York collective ReeLife.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Soderberg|first1=Brandonderberg|title=Hear Euro League's Atmospheric Bronx Banger, "Gold Chains In a Dungeon" New track features Doley Bernays and a supporting player from Jay Z's pastBrandon Soderberg|url=http://www.spin.com/2014/03/euro-league-gold-chains-in-a-dungeon/|date=Mar 5, 2014}}</ref> In addition, Skhye produced "Eat Sleep and Die"<ref>{{cite web|author1=Steven Solis|title=MC Jin ''XIV:LIX'' Album Cover, Tracklisting & Release Date Revealed|url=http://www.rapzilla.com/rz/news/74-behind-the-scenes/9229-mc-jin-xiv-liv-album-cover-tracklisting-release-date-revealed|website=www.rapzilla.com/|date=Oct 7, 2014}}</ref> on [[MC Jin]]'s album, ''XIV:LIX''.

In 2015, he branched out to work with the DMV native [[Jay IDK]],<ref>{{cite web|author1=Dharmic X|url=http://www.complex.com/music/2014/03/euro-league-ft-doley-bernays-pain-in-da-ass-gold-chains-in-a-dungeon|website=www.complex.com|date=Mar 9, 2014|title = New Music}}</ref> where the two collaborated on the tracks "Dirty Scale" and "God Said Trap" with additional production from Gamebrand, which serves as the second official leak from the highly anticipated ''SubTrap LP''.<ref>{{cite web|author1=Jahn Parker|title=Jay IDK – SubTrap [Album Review]|url=http://earmilk.com/album-reviews/250541/jay-idk/subtrap/}}</ref> He then produced for Jay Rock's first album in over 4 years, [[90059 (album)|90059]]. The [[Loop (music)|loopy]] beat<ref>{{cite magazine|author1=Daniel Kreps|title=Hear Kendrick Lamar, Jay Rock Reunite on Fiery 'Easy Bake' DANIEL KREPS|magazine=[[Rolling Stone]]|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/news/hear-kendrick-lamar-jay-rock-reunite-on-fiery-easy-bake-20150827|date=Aug 27, 2015}}</ref> on "Easy Bake" featuring Kendrick Lamar & [[SZA (singer)|SZA]] premiered on [[Sirius XM]]'s [[Shade 45]].<ref>{{cite web|title=LISTEN TO JAY ROCK'S "EASY BAKE" F/ KENDRICK LAMAR|url=http://thesource.com/2015/08/27/listen-to-jay-rocks-easy-bake-f-kendrick-lamar/|website=THESOURCE.com|date=Aug 27, 2015}}</ref> Continuing to reach out to emerging artists, Skhye Hutch joined the [[Inglewood, California|Inglewood]] rapper Shawn Chrys<ref>{{cite web|author1=Mika|title=New Music|url=http://thetimbalandpassion.blogspot.com/2015/04/shawn-chrystopher-no-fcks-skhyehutch-15.html?m=1|website=thetimbalandpassion.blogspot.com|date=Apr 2015}}</ref> on "NO FCKS" released on Timbaland's Imprint label.<ref>{{cite web|author1=Matthew Garner|title=Shawn Chrystopher "NO FCKS"|url=http://www.youngcalifornia.com/2015/04/shawn-chrystopher-no-fcks/|date=Apr 24, 2015}}</ref> He also produced ‘''Flicker''’ by Ash Riser.<ref>{{cite web|author1=Depratt M|title=ASH RISER – Flicker (prod. Skhye Hutch)|url=http://bluntiq.com/2015/11/10/ash-riser-flicker-prod-skhye-hutch/|date=Nov 10, 2015}}{{Dead link|date=November 2018 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}</ref>
