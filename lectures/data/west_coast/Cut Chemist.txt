'''Lucas MacFadden'''<ref>{{cite web|first=Andrew|last=Drever|url=http://www.smh.com.au/entertainment/music/cut-chemist-and-dj-shadow-salute-afrika-bambaataa-with-archives-mix-20150303-13qnth.html|title=Cut Chemist and DJ Shadow salute Afrika Bambaataa with archives mix|work=[[The Sydney Morning Herald]]|date=February 27, 2015|access-date=March 10, 2018}}</ref> (born October 4, 1972),<ref name=allmusic>{{cite web|first=John|last=Bush|url=http://www.allmusic.com/artist/cut-chemist-mn0000786627/biography|title=Cut Chemist|work=[[AllMusic]]|access-date=March 10, 2018}}</ref> better known as '''Cut Chemist''', is an American DJ and record producer.<ref>{{cite web|first=Natalie|last=Kuchik|url=http://www.axs.com/news/dj-shadow-cut-chemist-announce-additional-fall-tour-dates-20352|title=DJ Shadow & Cut Chemist announce additional fall tour dates|work=[[AXS (company)|AXS]]|date=September 19, 2004|access-date=March 10, 2018}}</ref> He is a former member of [[Jurassic 5]] and [[Ozomatli]].<ref>{{cite web|first=Victoria|last=Hernandez|url=http://hiphopdx.com/news/id.29739/title.dj-shadow-cut-chemist-announce-renegades-of-rhythm-tour-additional-date-opening-acts-added|title=DJ Shadow, Cut Chemist Announce "Renegades of Rhythm" Tour; Additional Date, Opening Acts Added|work=[[HipHopDX]]|date=August 20, 2014|access-date=March 10, 2018}}</ref> He has collaborated with [[DJ Shadow]] on a number of projects.<ref>{{cite web|first=Tal|last=Rosenberg|url=http://www.stylusmagazine.com/reviews/cut-chemist/the-audiences-listening.htm|title=Cut Chemist - The Audience's Listening|work=[[Stylus Magazine]]|date=July 17, 2006|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120509184743/http://www.stylusmagazine.com/reviews/cut-chemist/the-audiences-listening.htm|archive-date=May 9, 2012|access-date=March 10, 2018}}</ref>

==Early life==
Cut Chemist graduated from [[University of California, Los Angeles]] in 1997.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://alumni.ucla.edu/stories/lucas-macfadden-97/|title=Lucas MacFadden '97|work=[[University of California, Los Angeles]]|date=May 22, 2015|access-date=March 10, 2018}}</ref> Cut Chemist also attended Glendale Community College and took several drawing and life drawing classes in the early 1990's.

==Career==
Cut Chemist became known through the Los Angeles-based rap group Unity Committee.<ref name=allmusic/> The group merged with another group, Rebels of Rhythm, to form [[Jurassic 5]].<ref name=allmusic/>

In 2004, Cut Chemist released ''The Litmus Test'', which included his solo tracks, remixes, and Jurassic 5 tracks.<ref>{{cite web|first=Cameron|last=Macdonald|url=http://pitchfork.com/reviews/albums/1781-the-litmus-test/|title=Cut Chemist: The Litmus Test|work=[[Pitchfork (website)|Pitchfork]]|date=September 23, 2004|access-date=March 10, 2018}}</ref>

He left Jurassic 5 in 2004.<ref>{{cite web|first=Nick|last=Patch|url=http://exclaim.ca/music/article/cut_chemist_works_backwards|title=Cut Chemist Works Backwards|work=[[Exclaim!]]|date=July 2006|access-date=March 10, 2018}}</ref> In a 2006 interview with ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'', he stated that he had left the group in order to get his solo album finished.<ref name="billboard-leftgroup">{{cite magazine|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/58486/cut-chemist-finds-new-audience-on-solo-debut|title=Cut Chemist Finds New 'Audience' On Solo Debut|magazine=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|date=May 10, 2006|access-date=November 4, 2007}}</ref>

His first solo studio album, ''[[The Audience's Listening]]'', was released on July 11, 2006.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.metacritic.com/music/the-audiences-listening/cut-chemist|title=The Audience's Listening by Cut Chemist|work=[[Metacritic]]|access-date=March 10, 2018}}</ref> In 2015, it was listed by ''[[Vice (magazine)|Vice]]'' as the 49th greatest dance album of all time.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://thump.vice.com/en_ca/article/the-99-greatest-dance-albums-of-all-time|title=The 99 Greatest Dance Albums of All Time|work=[[Vice (magazine)|Vice]]|date=July 14, 2015|access-date=March 10, 2018}}</ref> "The Audience Is Listening Theme Song," the song from the album, has been featured in an [[iPod Nano]] advertisement.<ref>{{cite web|first=Tom|last=Smith|url=http://www.cyclicdefrost.com/2006/12/cut-chemist-the-audiences-listening-warner/|title=Cut Chemist – The Audience's Listening (Warner)|work=[[Cyclic Defrost]]|date=December 12, 2006|access-date=March 10, 2018}}</ref>

In 2010, he released a DJ mix, ''Sound of the Police''.<ref>{{cite web|first=Nate|last=Patrin|url=http://pitchfork.com/reviews/albums/14716-sound-of-the-police/|title=Cut Chemist: Sound of the Police|work=[[Pitchfork (website)|Pitchfork]]|date=October 12, 2010|access-date=March 10, 2018}}</ref> It was recorded using a turntable, a mixer, and a loop pedal.<ref>{{cite web|first=David|last=Jeffries|url=http://www.allmusic.com/album/sound-of-the-police-mw0002016168|title=Sound of the Police - Cut Chemist|work=[[AllMusic]]|access-date=March 10, 2018}}</ref>

His second solo studio album, ''Die Cut'', was released on March 2, 2018.<ref>{{cite magazine|first=Jason|last=Scott|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/hip-hop/8225523/cut-chemist-interview-die-cut-jurassic-5|title=Cut Chemist on First Solo Album in 12 Years, Importance of His Father's Memory & More|magazine=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|date=March 3, 2018|access-date=March 10, 2018}}</ref> It featured guest appearances from [[Chali 2na]], [[Biz Markie]], [[Mr. Lif]], and [[Myka 9]], among others.<ref>{{cite web|first=Kyle|last=Eustice|url=http://hiphopdx.com/news/id.44836/title.cut-chemist-announces-die-cut-lp-with-1st-single-work-my-mind|title=Cut Chemist Unleashes 1st Solo LP In 12 Years|work=[[HipHopDX]]|date=March 2, 2018|access-date=March 10, 2018}}</ref>
