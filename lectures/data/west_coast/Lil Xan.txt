'''Nicholas Diego Leanos'''<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.instagram.com/p/Bsc1QXnnR8e/|author=xanxiety|title=fuck it i'll do a Q n A on live rn|date=January 10, 2019|website=[[Instagram]]|access-date=January 13, 2019}}</ref> (born September 6, 1996), better known as '''Lil Xan''' ({{IPAc-en|z|æ|n}} {{Respell|ZANN}}) or simply '''Diego''', is an American rapper, singer, and songwriter from [[Redlands, California]].<ref>{{cite magazine|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/latin/8061630/hottest-latino-mcs-hip-hop-today|title=10 Hottest MCs of Latin Descent in Hip-Hop Today|magazine=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|last=Elibert|first=Mark|date=December 5, 2017|access-date=December 20, 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2017/12/18/arts/music/eminem-g-eazy-lil-xan-nf.html|title=3 Rappers Peeking at Eminem's Playbook|work=[[The New York Times]]|last=Caramanica|first=Jon|date=December 18, 2017|access-date=December 20, 2017}}</ref> He is best known for his song "[[Betrayed (Lil Xan song)|Betrayed]]", which was certified platinum by the [[Recording Industry Association of America|RIAA]]<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/lil-xans-betrayed-has-gone-platinum-news.49606.html|title=Lil Xan's "Betrayed" Has Gone Platinum|work=HotNewHipHop|access-date=May 10, 2018}}</ref> and peaked at number 64 on the [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]]. On April 6, 2018, Leanos released his debut studio album, ''[[Total Xanarchy]]''.

==Youth==
Growing up, Leanos was [[poverty|poor]] and his family lived in motels during most of his childhood.<ref name=":0" /> He attended [[Redlands East Valley High School]], but dropped out of high school in his freshman year, and spent several years at home unemployed.<ref>{{cite interview|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=s0zJub4oD-g|title=The LIL XAN Interview|author=Back2Back Hip Hop|publisher=[[YouTube]]|date=August 11, 2017|access-date=November 29, 2017}}</ref><ref name=nojumper>{{cite interview|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XzXBq8KCw8I|title=The Lil Xan Interview|publisher=YouTube|author=No Jumper|date=January 27, 2018|access-date=February 1, 2018}}</ref> Leanos took a job as a street cleaner and sold drugs<ref>{{Citation|title=Lil Xan Would Like to Make You Sober: Noisey Raps|date=December 15, 2017|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=LGLpAdzqGtk|author=Noisey|publisher=YouTube|access-date=February 1, 2018}}</ref> before he began rapping.<ref name="Billboard2">{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/8094303/lil-xan-betrayed-you-should-know-video-interview|title=Get to Know 'Betrayed' Rapper Lil Xan|last=Schiller|first=Rebecca|date=January 16, 2018|publisher=Billboard|access-date=January 19, 2018}}</ref> Leanos later pursued a photography career in support of several friends who were rappers. He eventually had his camera stolen, and chose to begin rapping in lieu of investing in a new camera.<ref name="XXL2">{{cite web|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2017/11/the-break-presents-lil-xan/|title=The Break Presents: Lil Xan|last=C.M.|first=Emmanuel|date=November 10, 2017|website=[[XXL (magazine)|XXL]]|access-date=November 29, 2017}}</ref>

== Career ==
[[File:Lil Xan.png|thumb|Lil Xan performing in 2018]]
His stage name is derived from Xanax,<ref>{{cite web|url=https://toofab.com/2020/09/09/lil-xan-on-sobriety-that-name-change-and-working-with-tekashi-6ix9ine-exclusive/|author=toofab|title=Lil Xan on Sobriety, That Name Change and Working with Tekashi 6ix9ine (Exclusive)|quote="Lil Xan famously derives his name from Xanax, a drug he was formerly addicted to"}}</ref> the trade name of the prescription drug [[alprazolam]]. Leanos began to gain recognition through platforms such as [[SoundCloud]] and [[YouTube]]. His popularity grew after the release of the music video for his song "Betrayed" in August 2017.<ref>{{cite magazine|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/chart-beat/8014759/pandora-predictions-lil-xan-lyrica-anderson-mahalia|title=Pandora Predictions to Know: Lil Xan, Lyrica Anderson & Mahalia|last=Blake|first=Emily|date=October 26, 2017|magazine=Billboard|access-date=December 12, 2017}}</ref> The song peaked at number 64 on the [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]].<ref name="Hot 1002">{{cite magazine|url=https://www.billboard.com/charts/hot-100/2018-02-17|title=Billboard Hot 100: February 17, 2018|magazine=Billboard|date=January 2, 2013|access-date=February 13, 2018}}</ref> In an interview with ''[[XXL (magazine)|XXL]]'', Leanos announced his debut album ''[[Total Xanarchy]]''.<ref name="XXL2" /> The album includes collaborations with artists such as [[Diplo]] and [[Swae Lee]].<ref name=":0">{{cite magazine|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/hip-hop/8094841/lil-xan-betrayed-interview-chartbreaker|title=Chartbreaker: Lil Xan on Face Tattoos and Overcoming Addiction|last=Haithcoat|first=Rebecca|date=January 22, 2018|magazine=Billboard|access-date=February 1, 2018}}</ref> In December 2017, Leanos announced his ''Total Xanarchy'' tour,<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/lil-xan-reveals-dates-for-total-xanarchy-tour-news.41208.html|title=Lil Xan Reveals Dates For "Total Xanarchy" Tour|last=F|first=Matt|date=December 21, 2017|work=HotNewHipHop|access-date=February 1, 2018}}</ref> which sold out in five hours according to ''Billboard''.<ref name=":0" /> In 2018, Leanos considered changing his stage name to Diego in order to support his anti-drug message;<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2018/01/lil-xan-plans-changing-name/|title=Lil Xan Plans on Changing His Name|last=Coleman II|first=C. Vernon|date=January 13, 2018|publisher=XXL|access-date=January 13, 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/lil-xan-officially-changes-name-to-diego-on-big-boys-neighborhood-news.42216.html|title=Lil Xan Officially Changes Name To Diego On Big Boy's Neighborhood|last=P.|first=Milca|date=January 14, 2018|publisher=HotNewHipHop|access-date=January 15, 2018}}</ref> however, he has since expressed uncertainty toward officially going through with the pseudonym change.<ref name=nojumper />

Leanos released his debut studio album, ''Total Xanarchy'' on April 6, 2018. The album received mostly negative reviews from critics but was a moderate commercial success, debuting at number ten on the ''Billboard'' 200 and selling 28,000 copies in its first week.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/rap-music/new-music/2018/04/lil-xan-total-xanarchy-album/|title=Lil Xan Shares 'Total Xanarchy' Album – XXL|website=XXL Mag|language=en|access-date=November 14, 2018}}</ref> The following month in May 2018, Leanos announced the mixtape ''Heartbreak Soldiers''<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/lil-xan-shares-heartbreak-soldiers-tracklist-and-release-date-news.51389.html|title=Lil Xan Shares "Heartbreak Soldiers" Tracklist & Release Date|work=HotNewHipHop|access-date=November 14, 2018}}</ref> which was released on July 8, 2018.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://hypebeast.com/2018/7/lil-xan-heartbreak-soldiers-ep-stream|title=Lil Xan Surprises Fans With New 'Heartbreak Soldiers' Project|work=HYPEBEAST|access-date=November 14, 2018}}</ref> In September 2018, Leanos announced he had been working on an album titled ''Be Safe'' as a tribute to [[Mac Miller]], as these were the last words he said to Leanos before his death.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.complex.com/music/2018/09/lil-xan-says-mac-miller-inspired-title-of-his-next-project|title=Lil Xan Says Mac Miller's Last Words to Him Inspired the Title of His Next Project|last=Jarreau|first=Khal|date=September 26, 2018|website=Complex|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180927114429/https://www.complex.com/music/2018/09/lil-xan-says-mac-miller-inspired-title-of-his-next-project|archive-date=September 27, 2018|url-status=live|access-date=February 18, 2019}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.vulture.com/2018/11/lil-xan-is-making-a-mac-miller-tribute-album.html|title=Lil Xan Is Making a Mac Miller Tribute Album|last=Harris|first=Hunter|work=Vulture|access-date=November 14, 2018|language=en}}</ref>

Leanos has given a number of contradictory statements regarding his yet-unreleased second album. Initially, he planned on releasing his ''Be Safe'' project in 2019, but the album entered production limbo and was eventually scrapped. In November 2019, he announced his next album would be titled ''When September Ends'' and released an accompanying album cover, but this also never came to fruition.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://people.com/music/lil-xan-quit-xanax-cold-turkey-had-seizures/|title=Lil Xan Opens Up About Having Seizures After Quitting Xanax and Hydrocodone 'Cold Turkey'|website=PEOPLE.com}}</ref> In January 2020, Leanos announced that his album was finished and that it would be called ''Sorry I Didn't Quit'', revealing a new album cover that took inspiration from [[Lil Wayne]]'s ''[[Sorry 4 the Wait]]'', but this project appears to have been scrapped as well.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://genius.com/albums/Lil-xan/Sorry-i-didnt-quit|title=Sorry I didn't Quit ! by Lil Xan|website=Genius}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.xxlmag.com/news/2019/12/lil-xa-not-quitting-rap/|title=Lil Xan Claims He Was Kidding About Quitting Rap}}</ref>

On June 24, 2022, Leanos released the EP ''have a nice day'' – his first project in over three years – as a collaboration with fellow emo rapper [[Chris Miles (musician)|Chris Miles]]. The EP includes a feature from [[Lil Tracy]] and was supported by the single "Miss Me".<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://variancemagazine.com/sounds/12614-chris-miles-lil-xan-miss-me|title=Chris Miles links with Lil Xan for new breakup single 'Miss Me'|last=Heidt|first=Dustin|work=Variance|access-date=September 16, 2022|language=en}}</ref>

== Artistry ==
Leanos has listed [[Pharrell Williams]] and [[N.E.R.D.]] as early influences in [[Hip hop music|hip hop]], with other influences including [[Arctic Monkeys]], [[Cage the Elephant]] and [[Queens of the Stone Age]].<ref name="Pigeons&Planes2">{{Cite news|url=http://pigeonsandplanes.com/interviews/2017/12/lil-xan-interview-2017|title=People Can Change: An Interview With Lil Xan|last=Rindner|first=Grant|date=December 22, 2017|work=Pigeons & Planes|access-date=February 1, 2018}}</ref> Leanos has also described [[Drake (musician)|Drake]] and [[Mac Miller]] as musical inspirations.<ref name="Billboard2" />

According to ''Pigeons & Planes'', Leanos' music started off as "typical trap" and later shifted towards "a murkier, dream-like sound".<ref name="Pigeons&Planes2" /> ''[[The New Yorker]]'' has described Leanos as part of a "sad rap" movement.<ref>"{{cite magazine|url=https://www.newyorker.com/magazine/2018/01/08/lil-xan-and-the-year-in-sad-rap|title=Lil Xan and the Year in Sad Rap|last=Battan|first=Carrie|date=January 8, 2018|magazine=[[The New Yorker]]|access-date=February 1, 2018}}</ref>

== Personal life ==
Leanos started dating singer and actress [[Noah Cyrus]] on June 30, 2018.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.yahoo.com/entertainment/dont-accept-noah-cyrus-rapper-lil-xan-go-public-fans-arent-happy-220117784.html|title='I don't accept this': Noah Cyrus and rapper Lil Xan go public, and fans aren't happy about it|access-date=July 31, 2018|language=en-US}}</ref> The couple released the collaboration "Live or Die" in August 2018. They broke up in September 2018, with both parties accusing the other of being unfaithful; Leanos also claimed that the relationship was forced by the couple's record label, Columbia,<ref>{{Citation|last=adam22|title=Lil Xan speaks on Noah Cyrus, Mac Miller and quitting music|date=September 8, 2018|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=i9H5-aXfyRQ|access-date=September 9, 2018}}</ref> though he later took responsibility for the breakup and said he has "nothing but love for the Cyrus family."<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.elitedaily.com/p/why-did-lil-xan-break-up-with-noah-cyrus-the-rapper-admitted-it-was-his-fault-12062673|title=Lil Xan Just Admitted His Breakup With Noah Cyrus Was His Fault For A Sad Reason|last=Boyle|first=Kelli|date=September 27, 2018|website=Elite Daily|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190130161856/https://www.elitedaily.com/p/why-did-lil-xan-break-up-with-noah-cyrus-the-rapper-admitted-it-was-his-fault-12062673|archive-date=January 30, 2019|url-status=live|access-date=February 18, 2019}}</ref>

Leanos has been open about his former addiction to [[benzodiazepine]]s (particularly [[alprazolam|Xanax]]) and [[opiate]]s. He overcame his Xanax addiction after two years.<ref name=":2">{{cite news|url=https://djbooth.net/features/2017-10-19-lil-xan-xanax-addiction|title=A Rapper Named Lil Xan Talks About His Painful Xanax Addiction & Recovery|date=October 19, 2017|work=DJBooth|access-date=November 29, 2017}}</ref> Leanos is outspoken against Xanax abuse and urges people to stop using the drug altogether.<ref name=":3">{{cite web|url=https://www.highsnobiety.com/p/lil-xan-interview/|title=Lil Xan Is the Unlikely Inspiration for Getting Kids off Xanax|last=Hodge|first=Kyle|date=January 18, 2018|publisher=[[Highsnobiety]]|access-date=January 19, 2018}}</ref><ref name=":4">{{cite web|url=http://trl.mtv.com/news/3057388/lil-xan-on-substance-abuse-in-rap/|title=Lil Xan On Substance Abuse In The Hip-Hop Community: 'You'd Be Stupid To Keep Glorifying It'|last=Gore|first=Sydney|date=January 9, 2018|publisher=[[Total Request Live|TRL]]|access-date=January 19, 2018|archive-date=January 10, 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180110033710/http://trl.mtv.com/news/3057388/lil-xan-on-substance-abuse-in-rap/|url-status=dead}}</ref>

In February 2019, Leanos announced that he and his then-fiancée Annie Smith were expecting a child.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.eonline.com/au/news/1015931/lil-xan-expecting-his-first-child-5-months-after-noah-cyrus-breakup|title=Lil Xan Expecting His First Child 5 Months After Noah Cyrus Breakup|last=Grossman|first=Lena|date=February 17, 2019|website=E! News|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190218090558/https://www.eonline.com/au/news/1015931/lil-xan-expecting-his-first-child-5-months-after-noah-cyrus-breakup|archive-date=February 18, 2019|url-status=live|access-date=February 18, 2019}}</ref> Smith, however, claimed to have had a [[miscarriage]],<ref name="auto">{{Cite web|url=https://www.eonline.com/ca/news/1030377/lil-xan-s-fiancee-reveals-she-had-a-miscarriage|title=Lil Xan's Fiancée Reveals She Had a Miscarriage|date=April 6, 2019|website=E! News|language=en-CA|access-date=April 7, 2019}}</ref> sharing the information about the incident via a video and photographs on Instagram.<ref name="auto" /> In a ''[[Adam22|No Jumper]]'' interview weeks later, Leanos said he had suspicions that Smith had been faking her pregnancy after a number of the rapper's fans on social media pointed out that Smith's supposed [[ultrasound]] photos looked identical to ones that could be found using a [[Google Images]] search. The couple would eventually split.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://people.com/music/lil-xan-questions-annie-smith-faked-pregnancy-miscarriage/|title=Lil Xan Questions Whether Partner Annie Smith Faked Her Pregnancy and Miscarriage: 'I'm 50/50'|website=People.com}}</ref>
