'''Dacoury Dahi Natche''' (born March 10, 1983), professionally known as '''Dahi''', is an American record producer, songwriter, [[disc jockey]] and singer from [[Inglewood, California]]. Dahi is best known for producing "[[Money Trees]]" by American rapper [[Kendrick Lamar]], “[[Worst Behavior]]" by Canadian rapper [[Drake (musician)|Drake]], and "[[I Don't Fuck with You]]" by American rapper [[Big Sean]], which reached the [[Top 40|top 20]] of the US Hot 100 chart.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.vibe.com/article/interview-producers-scoop-deville-dj-dahi-and-thc-kendrick-album|title=Interview: Producers Scoop Deville, DJ Dahi, And THC Have Started Working On Kendrick Lamar's Next Album|date=January 31, 2014|access-date=September 30, 2016}}</ref> Throughout his career Dahi has [[hip hop production|produced]] for a wide range of artists including [[Kendrick Lamar]], [[Drake (musician)|Drake]], [[J. Cole]], [[Dr. Dre]], [[Travis Scott]], [[21 Savage]], [[Don Toliver]], [[Mac Miller]], [[Nas]], [[Madonna]], [[Childish Gambino]], [[Kali Uchis]], [[Steve Lacy (singer)|Steve Lacy]], [[Baby Keem]], [[Vampire Weekend]], [[SZA]], [[Big Sean]], [[Pusha T]], [[Brent Faiyaz]], [[Post Malone]], [[Schoolboy Q]], [[Kehlani]], [[Lupe Fiasco]], [[Vince Staples]], [[Big Boi]], and [[Freddie Gibbs]] among others.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.hiphopcanada.com/2013/07/rich-kidd-id-be-lying-video/|title=Rich Kidd – I'd Be Lying [Video]|date=July 5, 2013|access-date=September 30, 2016|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20151023001538/http://www.hiphopcanada.com/2013/07/rich-kidd-id-be-lying-video/|archive-date=October 23, 2015|url-status=dead}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://theboombox.com/20-best-dj-dahi-beats-so-far/ |title=20 Best DJ Dahi Beats |publisher=Theboombox.com |date=September 26, 2013 |access-date=February 11, 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.gq.com/story/dj-dahi-donald-glover-interview|title=Donald Glover Needed Something New. Enter DJ Dahi|date=April 8, 2020|publisher=GQ|access-date=October 21, 2020}}</ref> Nominated for [[Grammy Awards]] four times in the [[Grammy Award for Album of the Year|Album of the Year]] and [[Grammy Award for Best Rap Album|Best Rap Album]] categories and once for [[Grammy Award for Producer of the Year, Non-Classical|Producer of the Year, Non-Classical]], Dahi won the [[Grammy Award for Best Rap Song]] in 2020 for the [[21 Savage]] song [[A Lot (song)|A lot]]''.''<ref name=":4">{{Cite web |title=DJ Dahi at Grammy Awards |url=https://www.grammy.com/artists/DJ-Dahi/24536 |access-date=2023-08-06 |website=www.grammy.com}}</ref>

Aside from his solo production career, Dahi is also a member of production team D.R.U.G.$.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.complex.com/music/2012/11/25-new-producers-to-watch-out-for/drugs|title=25 New Producers To Watch Out ForD.R.U.G.S.|website=[[Complex Networks]]|access-date=September 30, 2016}}</ref>

== Musical career ==
Dahi learned disc jockeying while he was attending the [[University of California, Santa Cruz]], where he majored in Film and American studies.<ref>{{cite web | url=https://www.thefader.com/2013/10/31/beat-construction-dj-dahi | title=Beat Construction: DJ Dahi }}</ref> After returning to Los Angeles in 2007, he started collaborating with local rappers such as [[Pac Div]], TiRon, and [[Fashawn]].<ref name=":0">{{Cite web |last=Wete |first=Brad |title=DJ Dahi: Making The Move From Bass Line To Top Billing |url=https://www.npr.org/sections/therecord/2016/02/23/466130812/dj-dahi-making-the-move-from-bass-line-to-top-billing |website=[[NPR Music]]}}</ref> Dahi also collaborated with [[Dom Kennedy]] and [[Schoolboy Q|ScHoolboy Q]] before he achieved his first commercial success in 2012 with "[[Money Trees]]" from [[Kendrick Lamar]]'s platinum album ''[[Good Kid, M.A.A.D City]]''.<ref name="complex1">{{cite web|url=http://www.complex.com/music/2013/09/best-rap-producers/dj-dahi |title=9. DJ Dahi — The 10 Best Rap Producers Right Now |publisher=Complex |date=September 25, 2013 |access-date=February 11, 2014}}</ref><ref name=":2">{{Cite web |last=Turnerpublished |first=Danny |date=2022-11-22 |title=Who are the five nominees for Producer of the Year at the 2023 Grammy Awards? |url=https://www.musicradar.com/news/grammy-producer-of-the-year-2023-who-are-they |access-date=2023-08-06 |website=MusicRadar |language=en}}</ref> The album received several nominations including one for Grammy Album of the Year and Rap Album of the Year in [[56th Annual Grammy Awards|2014]],<ref name=":3" /> while "Money Trees" was named by ''[[Complex (magazine)|Complex]] as'' the ninth best instrumental of the previous five years.<ref>{{cite web |date=September 3, 2013 |title=9. Kendrick Lamar f/ Jay Rock "Money Trees" (2012) — The 25 Best Rap Beats of The Last 5 Years |url=http://www.complex.com/music/2013/09/best-rap-beats-last-5-years/kendrick-lamar-jay-rock-money-trees |access-date=February 11, 2014 |publisher=Complex}}</ref><ref name="complex1" />

In 2013, Dahi produced "[[Worst Behavior]]" by [[Drake (rapper)|Drake]] that charted at number 89 on the US [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]] after its release in 2014.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.mtv.com/news/articles/1714247/drake-nothing-was-the-same-worst-behaviour.jhtml |title=Drake Changes Up His 'Rap Flow' On NWTS's 'Worst Behaviour' – Music, Celebrity, Artist News |publisher=MTV.com |date=September 18, 2013 |access-date=February 11, 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.thefader.com/2013/10/31/beat-construction-dj-dahi|title=Beat Construction: DJ Dahi|access-date=September 30, 2016}}</ref> The following year, he co-produced the [[Big Sean]] single [[I Don't Fuck with You]], which reached the top of the US [[Hot R&B/Hip-Hop Songs|Billboard Hot R&B/Hiphop songs]] chart<ref>{{Cite web |title=Big Sean {{!}} Biography, Music & News |url=https://www.billboard.com/artist/big-sean/ |access-date=2023-08-06 |website=Billboard |language=en-US}}</ref> and received 8x Platinum certification from [[Recording Industry Association of America|RIAA]].<ref name="Gold & Platinum">{{Cite web |title=Gold & Platinum |url=https://www.riaa.com/gold-platinum/ |access-date=2023-08-06 |website=RIAA |language=en-US}}</ref> Dahi was also credited as a songwriter and producer for five tracks in Madonna's 2015 album ''[[Rebel Heart]]''.<ref name=":0" /> The same year Dahi co-produced three tracks on the album ''[[Compton: A Soundtrack]]'' album by [[Dr. Dre]].<ref name=":2" />

In 2016, Dahi co-produced "U with Me?" for the album ''Views'' by Drake, which earned him his second Grammy nomination for Album of the Year.<ref name=":4" /> He also put forward a tentative release date for his own solo album titled ''The Good Seed'', but the album did not materialize in 2016.<ref name=":0" />

Dahi collaborated a second time with Kendrick Lamar in 2017 and produced five tracks in the album ''DAMN'' that included "[[Loyalty (Kendrick Lamar song)|Loyalty]]" featuring Rihanna and "[[XXX (Kendrick Lamar song)|XXX]]" featuring [[U2]].<ref>{{Cite web |last=Josephs |first=Brian |date=2017-04-24 |title=Producer DJ Dahi Talks the 5 Songs He Made For Kendrick Lamar’s ‘DAMN.’ |url=https://www.billboard.com/music/rb-hip-hop/producer-dj-dahi-kendrick-lamar-damn-7768208/ |access-date=2023-08-06 |website=Billboard |language=en-US}}</ref> The album received several critical accolades including a [[Pulitzer Prize for Music]] and the [[Grammy Award for Best Rap Album|Best Rap Album]] at the [[60th Annual Grammy Awards|2018 Grammy Awards]]. In 2020, the album was ranked 175th on ''[[Rolling Stone]]''{{'}}s updated list of the [[Rolling Stone's 500 Greatest Albums of All Time|500 Greatest Albums of All Time]].<ref>{{Cite web |date=2020-09-22 |title=The 500 Greatest Albums of All Time |url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-lists/best-albums-of-all-time-1062063/ |access-date=2023-08-06 |website=Rolling Stone |language=en-US}}</ref>

In 2018, Dahi produced two songs in 21 Savage's studio album ''[[I Am Greater than I Was]].'' One of the songs, "a lot", earned him the 2020 Grammy Award for Rap Song of the Year.<ref name=":4" />

In 2019, Dahi collaborated with indie pop band [[Vampire Weekend]] for their album ''[[Father of the Bride (album)|Father of the Bride]]'' and co-produced the single "Big Blue", which charted number 33 in US [[Hot Rock & Alternative Songs]] (''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'').<ref>{{Cite web |title=Vampire Weekend {{!}} Biography, Music & News |url=https://www.billboard.com/artist/vampire-weekend/ |access-date=2023-08-06 |website=Billboard |language=en-US}}</ref> He also co-produced the 2020 Childish Gambino album ''[[3.15.20]]'' that included [[Time (Childish Gambino song)|"Time]]" featuring Ariana Grande and "[[12.38]]" featuring 21 Savage, Ink and [[Kadhja Bonet]] among seven other tracks co-produced by him''.''<ref>{{Cite web |date=2020-04-08 |title=How One Producer Helped Donald Glover Make His Most Dynamic Album Yet |url=https://www.gq.com/story/dj-dahi-donald-glover-interview |access-date=2023-08-06 |website=GQ |language=en-US}}</ref> In 2021, he produced "[[Gravity (Brent Faiyaz and DJ Dahi song)|Gravity]]", a single by Brent Faiyaz and himself featuring [[Tyler, the Creator]], which reached a peak position on 71 in the US Billboard Hot 100 chart and was certified Platinum by RIAA.<ref name="Gold & Platinum"/>

Dahi teamed up with Kendrick Lamar again in 2022 and co-produced five tracks for ''[[Mr. Morale and the Big Steppers]].'' The album received nomination for Album of the Year and won in the Best Rap Album category in the [[65th Annual Grammy Awards|2023 Grammy Awards]].<ref name=":4" /> Dahi also received a Producer of the Year nomination for his work in ''Mr. Morale & the Big Steppers, [[Gemini Rights]]'' (Steve Lacy album) and ''[[Ramona Park Broke My Heart|RAMONA PARK BROKE MY HEART]]'' (Vince Staples album).''<ref name=":4" />''

In 2023, Dahi appeared in the [[documentary film]] ''Anthem'' directed by [[Peter Nicks]], which followed Dahi and composer [[Kris Bowers]] on a musical [[road trip]] across the United States in a bid to reimagine the [[The Star-Spangled Banner|national anthem]].<ref>{{Cite news |last=Lawrence |first=Andrew |date=2023-06-30 |title=Is it time for America to adopt a new national anthem? |language=en-GB |work=The Guardian |url=https://www.theguardian.com/film/2023/jun/30/national-anthem-documentary-hulu-ryan-coogler-star-spangled-banner |access-date=2023-08-04 |issn=0261-3077}}</ref> The film premiered at [[Tribeca Festival]] in June 2023 and was acquired by [[Onyx Collective]] for [[Hulu]].<ref>{{Cite web |last=Dalton2023-01-21T23:59:00+00:00 |first=Ben |title=Disney label Onyx Collective buys Questlove’s Sly Stone documentary, Ryan Coogler-produced 'Anthem' for Hulu |url=https://www.screendaily.com/news/disney-label-onyx-collective-buys-questloves-sly-stone-documentary-ryan-coogler-produced-anthem-for-hulu/5178360.article |access-date=2023-08-04 |website=Screen |language=en}}</ref>

Dahi has a publishing contract with [[Sony Music Publishing]]<ref>{{Cite web |last=Facts |first=Radio |date=2023-02-07 |title=Sony Music Publishing Songwriters Win Top Awards At 65th Annual GRAMMYs (PICS) - Radio Facts |url=https://radiofacts.com/sony-music-publishing-songwriters-win-top-awards-at-65th-annual-grammys-pics/ |access-date=2023-08-06 |language=en-US}}</ref> and is a [[SESAC]] member.<ref>{{Cite web |date=2020-01-27 |title=SESAC Celebrates 2020 GRAMMY® Winners |url=https://www.musicconnection.com/sesac-celebrates-2020-grammy-winners/ |access-date=2023-08-06 |website=Music Connection Magazine |language=en-US}}</ref>
