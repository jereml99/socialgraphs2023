'''Jason L. Martin''' (born May 8, 1987), better known by his [[stage name]] '''Problem''', is an American rapper, songwriter and record producer. He has released nineteen mixtapes in his career, the most recent being ''S2'', released 2018. His debut [[Extended play|EP]], ''[[Understand Me]]'', was released on December 10, 2013. He is best known for his single "[[Like Whaaat]]" and for featuring on [[E-40]]'s "[[Function (song)|Function]]".

== Early life ==
Problem was born in [[Würzburg]], [[West Germany]] and grew up in [[Compton, California]].<ref name="autogenerated1">{{cite web|url=http://www.vibe.com/article/problem-talks-welcome-mollywood-getting-started-rap-and-more |title=Problem Talks 'Welcome To Mollywood,' Getting Started in Rap and More |publisher=Vibe |date=2012-05-22 |access-date=2013-06-24}}</ref> He entered the industry by writing a verse for producer [[Terrace Martin]], for a song called "Be Thankful", featured on [[Snoop Dogg]]'s compilation album ''The Big Squeeze''.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.amazon.com/Snoop-Dogg-Presents-The-Squeeze/dp/B000NDFLVM |title=Snoop Dogg Presents The Big Squeeze: Snoop Dogg: Music |website=Amazon |access-date=2013-06-24}}</ref> The track garnered praise from notable artist such as [[Nas]] and [[Talib Kweli]] amongst others.<ref name="autogenerated1"/> Problem began recording records with various [[West Coast hip hop|West Coast]] artists such as [[Dat Nigga Daz|Daz]], [[Kurupt]], Snoop Dogg, and [[DJ Quik]]. Problem's first big break was when Snoop Dogg heard a record which was also produced by Terrace Martin called "Neva Hafta To Wurry About That".<ref name="autogenerated1"/> He went on to write three records on Snoop Dogg's ''[[Ego Trippin']]'' album.<ref name="autogenerated1"/>

==Career==
Prior to his career as a solo artist, Martin served as a [[ghostwriter]] in 2008, the first of which being the single "Head of My Class" by rapper Scooter Smiff featuring [[Chris Brown]]. He also made a guest appearance on fellow California artist [[Snoop Dogg]]'s album, ''[[Malice N Wonderland]]''.

Later that year, Problem launched the independent label, Diamond Lane Music Group.<ref name="autogenerated1"/> Then in 2009, he signed an unsuccessful two-single major label deal with [[Universal Music Group|Universal]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.complex.com/music/2013/07/25-new-rappers-to-watch-out-for/problem |title=Problem — 25 New Rappers to Watch Out For |publisher=Complex |access-date=2013-08-29}}</ref> However his breakout moment would be when he was featured on [[E-40]]'s single "[[Function (song)|Function]]" along with [[YG (rapper)]] and [[Iamsu!]]. Since that time, Problem has worked with the various artists such as [[Jamie Foxx]], [[Game (rapper)|Game]], [[Kendrick Lamar]], [[Bobby V]], [[9th Wonder]], [[Chris Brown (American singer)|Chris Brown]], [[Wiz Khalifa]], [[Nipsey Hussle]], [[Warren G]], [[Childish Gambino]], [[Pharrell Williams]], [[Jim Jones (rapper)|Jim Jones]], [[Travis Barker]], and [[John Legend]] among many others.<ref name="thefader1">{{cite web|url=http://www.thefader.com/2013/04/17/interview-problem/ |title=Interview: Problem « The FADER |publisher=Thefader.com |date=2013-04-17 |access-date=2013-06-24}}</ref> Over his career Problem has released seven mixtapes.<ref name="thefader1"/> On February 13, 2013, Problem released a collaborative mixtape with Iamsu! titled ''Million Dollar Afro'' with guest appearances from [[Juvenile (rapper)|Juvenile]], [[Too Short]] and [[Omarion]].<ref>{{cite web|author=JoJo Kool says |url=http://www.thefader.com/2013/02/15/download-problem-and-iamsus-million-dollar-afro-mixtape/ |title=Download Problem and IamSu!'s Million Dollar Afro Mixtape « The FADER |publisher=Thefader.com |date=2013-02-15 |access-date=2013-06-24}}</ref>

On June 13, 2013, he released a mixtape titled ''The Separation'' with guest appearances from [[Wale (rapper)|Wale]], [[T.I.]], Snoop Dogg, [[The Game (rapper)|Game]], [[Chris Brown (American singer)|Chris Brown]], [[Tank (American singer)|Tank]], [[Tyga]] and Wiz Khalifa among others.<ref>{{cite web|author=DiAmante' Astillero |url=http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/news/id.24312/title.problem-the-separation-mixtape-tracklist-cover-art |title=Problem "The Separation" Mixtape Tracklist, Cover Art &#124; Get The Latest Hip Hop News, Rap News & Hip Hop Album Sales |publisher=HipHop DX |date=2013-06-13 |access-date=2013-06-24}}</ref> His 2013 song "[[Like Whaaat]]," featuring Bad Lucc, peaked at #20 on the Bubbling Under [[Hot 100 Singles|Hot 100 singles]] chart, becoming his most successful song. On July 23, 2013, he released "Say That Then," featuring [[Glasses Malone]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/say-that-then-feat.-glasses/id675314451 |title=iTunes - Music - Say That Then (feat. Glasses Malone) - Single by Problem |publisher=Itunes.apple.com |date=2013-07-23 |access-date=2013-08-29}}</ref> “Say That Then” and “Do it Big” featuring Bad Lucc, [[Sage the Gemini|Sage The Gemini]], and [[Iamsu!|Iamsu]]! were featured on the 2013 hit game [[Grand Theft Auto V]] by [[Rockstar Games]] on the fictional radio station “[[Radio Los Santos]].”

In November 2013, Problem announced that he would release his debut album independently through Diamond Lane Music Group. He revealed that he would be primarily producing the album along with League of Starz.<ref>{{cite web|last=Jackson |first=Dan |url=http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2013/11/problem-interview/ |title=Problem Keeps It Independent With His Next Album - XXL |publisher=Xxlmag.com |date=2013-11-07 |access-date=2014-08-10}}</ref> On December 10, 2013, Problem released a retail [[Extended play|EP]] called ''[[Understand Me]]'', featuring songs such as "Like Whaaat", "Say That Then" and "Understand Me".<ref>{{cite web|title=Understand Me - EP Problem|url=https://itunes.apple.com/th/album/id762707746|work=iTunes Preview|publisher=Apple Inc|access-date=14 February 2014|date=10 December 2013}}</ref> Problem appeared in a branded music video alongside [[Neil Patrick Harris]] for the drink product Neuro in February 2014.<ref>{{cite web|title=Morning Update: Neil Patrick Harris in branded music video for Neuro; Mini kicks off global creative review; Cadbury can boogie in new ad|url=http://mumbrella.com.au/morning-update-neil-patrick-harris-branded-music-video-neuro-mini-kicks-global-creative-review-206551|work=Mumbrella|publisher=Focal Attractions|access-date=14 February 2014|date=14 February 2014}}</ref>

Problem made a splash for his hometown city when he partnered with the [[Los Angeles Rams]] to create the team's theme song, "My Squad," for the 2016-17 NFL season.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://hiphopdx.com/news/id.40496/title.problem-links-with-los-angeles-rams-for-team-anthem-my-squad|title=Problem Links With Los Angeles Rams For Team Anthem, "My Squad"|work=HipHopDX|access-date=2018-04-10|language=en-US}}</ref> The release included an official music video that was shot to look like [[Snapchat]].

After gaining much success on the mixtape circuit, Problem finally released his debut album, ''Selfish'', in November 2017.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.latimes.com/entertainment/music/la-et-ms-problem-selfish-20171107-story.html|title=Compton-raised artist Problem looks inward on 'Selfish'|last=Hernandez|first=Victoria|website=[[Los Angeles Times]]|date=8 November 2017|access-date=2018-04-10}}</ref> The project showcases some of his most personal work yet. As well as he released his second album ''S2'' in November 30, 2018.

On April 4, 2020, Problem released a [[short film]] titled "A Compton Story". The short film is narrated by comedian [[Mike Epps]] and has appearances from [[Snoop Dogg]], [[Slink Johnson]], and [[Jackie Long]]. [[Terrence "Punch" Henderson]] is credited as the [[Executive producer (television)|executive producer]] on the project.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Rumford|first=Laetitia|date=2020-04-05|title=Problem Explains The Making of His Short Film "A Compton Story"|url=https://www.vibe.com/2020/04/l-a-rapper-problem-explains-the-making-of-his-short-film-a-compton-story-and-releases-new-single-dont-be-mad-at-me|access-date=2020-07-17|website=Vibe.com|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|date=2020-04-04|title=A Compton Story on Tidal|url=https://listen.tidal.com/artist/3640789/video/136082084|access-date=2020-07-16|website=listen.tidal.com|language=en}}</ref>
