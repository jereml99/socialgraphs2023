'''Damani Nkosi''' is an American rapper. Nkosi has worked with well-known artists including [[Dr. Dre]], [[Swizz Beatz]],<ref name=rs/> [[Snoop Dogg]], [[Pusha T]] and [[No Malice|Malice]] of [[Clipse]].<ref name=cent/>

==Early life==
Nkosi was brought up in [[Inglewood, California]].<ref name=rs/> His father chose an African name for him. "Damani" means "be famous" and "Nkosi" means "Chief, ruler or king".<ref name=hype/> He became part of Los Angeles' hip-hop underground.<ref name=rs/>

==Career==
Nkosi recorded his first track in 1999.<ref name=cake/> In 2001, Nkosi was featured on Kurupt's album ''Space Boogie: Smoke Odyssey''.<ref name=hip/><ref name=all/><ref name=la/> He released "Move", a track featuring [[Pusha T]] and [[No Malice|Malice]] in 2002. The song was featured on hip-hop radio mixshows in Los Angeles including [[KRRL|92.3 FM, The Beat]].<ref name=this/> In 2002, he released the mixtape ''The Street Album''.<ref name=rs/><ref name=this/>

Nkosi released ''Congratulations Player'', a mixtape featuring the song "Gotta Stay Paid" in 2006. The song was featured on The Beat as well as [[KPWR|Power 106]]. Later that year, he signed with [[Sony Urban Music]] under My Block Entertainment.<ref name=this/><ref name=la/> In 2007, Nkosi appeared on the television series [[Monk (TV series)|''Monk'']].<ref name=imdb/> Nkosi formed the group [[Dubb Union]] (originally called Westurn Union) with Bad Lucc and producer Soopafly. [[Snoop Dogg]] signed the group under Doggystyle Records. The group toured with Snoop Dogg and released ''Snoop Dogg Presents: Dubb Union'' in 2008.<ref name=this/><ref name=la/>

In 2009, Nkosi partnered with [[Adidas]] on its off-the-court EQT Campaign, which surrounded the relaunch of the EQT B-Ball Low. The limited edition shoes created included part of Nkosi's logo and the words "Congratulations Player" on the heel. He recorded "My Adidas" as part of the campaign.<ref name=ball/><ref name=slam/><ref name=sneak/> In 2010, Nkosi released a single and video, "Here Comes Damani" featuring Snoop Dogg.<ref name=this/><ref name=la/>

Nkosi released the album ''On Vacation From Vacation'' in 2010.<ref name=hip/> The album featured Snoop Dogg, [[Crooked I]], [[Mitchy Slick]], [[Mr. Short Khop|Short Khop]] and [[Daz Dillinger|Daz]].<ref name=dub/> In 2012, Nkosi left his previous record labels, took a hiatus to travel internationally.<ref name=cent/><ref name=source/><ref name=red/> He went to China as part of a shoe design deal and was influenced by the economic gap between the rich and the poor.<ref name=red/>

Nkosi produced a single, "Now That's Love" featuring [[Robert Glasper]] and [[Musiq Soulchild]] in 2014 produced by [[Warryn Campbell]]. "Now That's Love" was the first single from his album ''Thoughtful King''. The album included other featured artists such as [[PJ Morton]], and [[BJ the Chicago Kid]].<ref name=rs/><ref name=cent/><ref name=cake/>
 
In 2015, he produced the single "Stay Black" featuring Preston Harris, Ill Camille and Aneesa Strings.<ref name=up/> Nkosi appeared in the documentary "Manchild: The Schea Cotton Story" in 2016.<ref name=man/>
