'''Khalil Abdul-Rahman Hazzard''' (born October 16, 1973), professionally known as '''DJ Khalil''', is an American [[music producer]] and [[disc jockey|DJ]] from [[Los Angeles]]. He is the instrumental half of the hip hop duo Self Scientific (along with rapper Chace Infinite) and a member of the group [[The New Royales]], which also includes Liz Rodrigues, [[Erik Alcock]] and Pranam [[Chin Injeti]]. He produced the albums ''[[Recovery (Eminem album)|Recovery]]'' and ''[[The Marshall Mathers LP 2]]'' by the rapper [[Eminem]], as well as the multi-platinum single "[[The Man (Aloe Blacc song)|The Man]]" by [[Aloe Blacc]]. He is the third born son of former UCLA player and coach and NBA player [[Walt Hazzard]].

==Early life==
DJ Khalil was born Khalil Abdul-Rahman in [[Seattle, Washington]], and raised in Los Angeles, California. His father, [[Walt Hazzard]], who later changed his name to Mahdi Abdul-Rahman, was a professional basketball player in the [[National Basketball Association]]. At age 13, at a party thrown by his parents, he met [[Dr. Dre]], for whom he would later become a staff producer.<ref>{{cite web|title=DJ Khalil Interview|url=http://aftermathmusic.com/cms/2014/06/dj-khalil-interview-urban-daily/|website=aftermathmusic.com}}</ref> Khalil played basketball at the high school and collegiate level, as a point guard at [[North Hollywood High School]] and [[Morehouse College]].<ref>{{cite web|title=NBA free agency might end up as boring rerun|url=http://articles.latimes.com/2010/jul/01/sports/la-sp-0701-crowe-20100701|website=[[Los Angeles Times]]|date=July 2010 }}</ref>

==Career==
Khalil began his career as a [[disc jockey]] (DJ), and graduated to producing music. He began creating tracks on the [[Ensoniq ASR-10]] sampler workstation, later working in Propellerhead [[Reason (software)|Reason]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Artist Stories|url=https://www.propellerheads.se/substance/artist-stories/index.cfm?article=dj-khalil&fuseaction=get_article|website=www.propellerheads.se/}}</ref> He has produced for a large number of major artists in the hip-hop, R&B and pop genres, including [[Kanye West]], [[50 Cent]], [[Pink (singer)|Pink]], [[The Game (rapper)|The Game]], [[ASAP Rocky]], [[Drake (rapper)|Drake]], [[Eminem]], [[G-Unit]], [[Wale (rapper)|Wale]], [[Usher (singer)|Usher]] and Guess Who.
