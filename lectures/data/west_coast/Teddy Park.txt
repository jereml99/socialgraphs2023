'''Teddy Park''' (born '''Park Hong-jun''' on September 14, 1978)<ref name=":0">{{cite web |url=https://channel-korea.com/teddy-park-profile/ |title=GET TO KNOW BETTER YG ENTERTAINMENT PRODUCER, TEDDY PARK |publisher=CHANNEL KOREA}}</ref> is a [[Korean Americans|Korean–American]] [[Rapping|rapper]], [[songwriter]], and [[record producer]]. Born in [[Seoul]], [[South Korea]], Park moved to the [[United States]] with his family as a young child. When he was 17 years old, Park and his friend [[Taebin|Danny]] flew to South Korea during summer vacation to audition for [[YG Entertainment]].<ref name=":0" /> Both were signed immediately, and after finishing high school in the States, moved to Korea to pursue music.

In 1998, Park debuted in the [[hip hop music|hip hop]] group [[1TYM]] with his friend Danny, Jinhwan, and Baekyoung.<ref name=":0" /><ref name=":1">{{Cite news|url=http://seoulbeats.com/2012/08/exploring-hit-makers-ygs-teddy-park/|title=Exploring Hit-Makers: YG's Teddy Park|date=August 2, 2012|work=Seoulbeats|access-date=July 9, 2018|language=en-US}}</ref><ref name="Teddy Park Interview">{{cite web |url=http://star.mt.co.kr/view/stview.php?no=2009073010134528688&type=1 |script-title=ko:테디 "아이돌 때보다 프로듀서인 지금이 더 행복"(인터뷰) |trans-title=Teddy, "Idol producer, now more than happy" (Interview) |language=ko |author=Gil, Hye-seong |date=July 30, 2009 |access-date=November 6, 2010 |work=Star News |publisher=MoneyToday}}</ref> Together, the group recorded five studio albums with Park as their primary songwriter and producer. After his performing career with 1TYM ended in January 2006, Park became an in-house producer for [[YG Entertainment]] and co-founded [[The Black Label]] in 2016. He has co-produced and co-written songs for YG artists such as [[Jinusean]], [[Seven (Korean singer)|Seven]], [[Big Bang (band)|Big Bang]], [[2NE1]], [[Lee Hi]], [[Blackpink]] and [[Jeon Somi]], as well as [[Uhm Jung-hwa]] and [[Sunmi]].<ref name="major works">{{cite web |url=http://star.mt.co.kr/view/stview.php?no=2009052507523288772&type=1&outlink=1 |title='원타임' 테디, 2NE1 위해 레이디가가 작곡 '거절' |trans-title='1TYM' Teddy, rejects working with Lady Gaga because of 2NE1 |language=ko |author=Lee, Su-hyeon |date=May 25, 2009 |access-date=November 6, 2010 |work=Star News |publisher=MoneyToday}}</ref><ref name="2NE1 works">According to the content on ''[[2NE1 (2009 EP)|2NE1]]'' and ''[[To Anyone]]''{{'}}s album jackets.</ref>

== Life and career ==

=== Early life ===
Park was born in [[Seoul]], [[South Korea]], but moved to [[New York City]] as a young child.<ref name=":0" /><ref name=":1" /> He learned the piano as a child, but as he grew up, he was more interested in playing the guitar and beatboxing.<ref name=":0" /><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.dt.co.kr/contents.html?article_no=2009071602019922601006|title=테디 "빅뱅ㆍ투애니원은 新 힙합세대죠"|date=July 16, 2009|access-date=July 17, 2020}}</ref> After his father was transferred to [[Diamond Bar]], [[California]], Park attended [[Diamond Bar High School]] and became friends with future bandmate [[Taebin|Danny Im]].<ref name=":1" /> Growing up, the two sang together at karaoke bars and tinkered with recording equipment.<ref name=":2">{{Cite web|url=http://www.koreatimesus.com/danny-from-l-a-is-more-than-a-one-time-deal/|title=Danny from L.A. is more than a one-time deal|last=Hong|first=Tae|date=August 21, 2014|website=[[The Korea Times]]|language=en-US|access-date=July 9, 2018}}</ref> They were discovered when they were teenagers by a producer who worked with [[Yang Hyun-suk]], founder of YG Entertainment.<ref name=":0" /><ref name=":2" /> After auditioning for Yang and sending in demo tapes, the two were signed to his newly established label, and moved to [[South Korea]].<ref name=":3">{{Cite web|url=http://www.koreatimesus.com/danny-from-l-a-is-more-than-a-one-time-deal/|title=Danny from L.A. is more than a one-time deal|last=Hong|first=Tae|date=August 21, 2014|website=The Korea Times US|access-date=May 12, 2016}}</ref> Although Park enrolled in a university when he returned to South Korea, he dropped out to focus on his career.<ref name=":1" />

=== 1998–2006: Work with 1TYM ===

{{Main|1TYM}}
Park and Im were joined by rappers Jinhwan and Baekyoung and debuted as 1TYM in 1998 with the album, ''One Time for Your Mind''.<ref name=":0" /> It became one of the best-selling albums of the year and won several major awards.<ref name=":0" /><ref name=":1" /><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.kpopstarz.com/articles/68511/20131206/k-pop-rewind-1tym-one-time-for-your-mind.htm|title=K-Pop Rewind: 1TYM "1TYM (One Time for Your Mind)"|date=December 6, 2013|website=KpopStarz|access-date=May 12, 2016}}</ref> After the first album, Park began to have more input into the songs, and had a more prominent role in producing. In 2000, 1TYM released their second album ''2nd Round''. The songs 'One Love'<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Bzv4XX0lUDc  |archive-url=https://ghostarchive.org/varchive/youtube/20211215/Bzv4XX0lUDc |archive-date=2021-12-15 |url-status=live|title=1TYM - ONE LOVE |publisher=[[YG Entertainment]] |via=[[YouTube]]}}{{cbignore}}</ref> and '쾌지나 칭칭' (Let's Sing and Dance Together),<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=muk7KrB9YRY  |archive-url=https://ghostarchive.org/varchive/youtube/20211215/muk7KrB9YRY |archive-date=2021-12-15 |url-status=live|title=1TYM - Let's Sing and Dance Together, 원타임 - 쾌지나 칭칭, Music Camp 20000729 |date=July 29, 2000 |publisher=[[MBC TV (South Korean TV channel)|MBC TV]] |via=[[YouTube]]}}{{cbignore}}</ref> which he wrote and produced, won first place on TV music programs. ''This was followed by Third Time Fo' Yo' Mind'' in 2001. In 2003, 1TYM released their fourth album ''Once N 4 All,'' which yielded two singles: "Hot 뜨거",<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QTMyIgdaEGE  |archive-url=https://ghostarchive.org/varchive/youtube/20211215/QTMyIgdaEGE |archive-date=2021-12-15 |url-status=live|title=1TYM - Hot, 원타임 - Hot 뜨거, Music Camp 20040221 |date=February 21, 2004 |publisher=[[MBC TV (South Korean TV channel)|MBC TV]] |via=[[YouTube]]}}{{cbignore}}</ref> an up-beat hip-hop track with reggae rhythms, and "Without You", an emotional R&B ballad.<ref name=":1" /> The group went on a two-year hiatus in which member Im embarked on a brief solo career under the name of Taebin. The group reunited for the album ''One Way'' in 2005, their fifth and last studio album, before going on hiatus due to Jinhwan's [[Conscription in South Korea|mandatory military service]].<ref name=":1" /><ref name=":3" /> Their albums have sold a combined total of 780,000 copies.<ref name=":0" /><ref name=":3" />

Though they were predominately hip hop artists, 1TYM was influenced by [[reggae]] and [[R&B]]. 1TYM along with the duo [[Jinusean]] were credited for bringing hip hop music and YG Entertainment to the mainstream.<ref name=":1" /> At 22 years old, Park also began producing for other artists on the label, most of whom were much older and more experienced than him.<ref name="1TYM discography">{{cite web|url=https://www.ygfamily.com/ygf/1tym.html|title=1TYM|work=YG Entertainment|publisher=YG Entertainment|language=ko|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100303233208/http://www.ygfamily.com/ygf/1tym.html|archive-date=March 3, 2010|url-status=dead|access-date=November 6, 2010}}</ref>

=== 2006–present: Producer for YG Entertainment ===
With 1TYM on hiatus, Park transitioned into the role of producer for other YG artists, his first major contribution being "La La La" for Seven's album ''Sevolution''.<ref name="Productions">{{cite web |url=http://star.mt.co.kr/stview.php?no=2010100115132779154&type=3 |script-title=ko:9월 음원차트, ★작곡가 이름값 '제대로' 했다 |trans-title=September music charts, popular composers respected |language=ko |first=Kim |last=Gwan-myeong |date=October 1, 2010 |access-date=November 6, 2010 |work=Star News |publisher=MoneyToday}}</ref> He also collaborated with [[Big Bang (band)|Big Bang]] for several of their songs,<ref name="Teddy Park Interview" /> most notably "[[Sunset Glow]]."<ref name="major works" /> When BigBang member [[Taeyang]] embarked on a solo career in 2008, Park produced his [[extended play]], ''[[Hot (EP)|Hot]].''<ref name="Teddy Park Interview" /> The following year, Park produced "[[Lollipop (Big Bang and 2NE1 song)|Lollipop]]" for a collaboration between BigBang and [[2NE1]]. The song went on to top the [[Gaon Chart]] during April.<ref name="2NE1 Gaon">{{cite web|url=http://gaonchart.co.kr/main/section/search/list.gaon?Search_str=2NE1&x=0&y=0|title=Gaon Chart Search: 2NE1|work=Gaon Chart|publisher=Korea Music Content Industry Association|language=ko|access-date=October 30, 2010}}</ref>

Park would go on to have a hand in producing a majority of 2NE1's work, including their debut [[extended play]], ''[[2NE1 (2009 EP)|2NE1]]''<ref name="2NE1 works" /> (2009) and studio album, ''[[To Anyone]]'' (2010). Two of the three main singles, "[[Can't Nobody (2NE1 song)|Can't Nobody]]" and "[[Go Away (2NE1 song)|Go Away]]," from the latter were also composed by Park.<ref name="Productions" /> To continue producing for 2NE1, Park rejected an offer to work with [[Lady Gaga]].<ref name="major works" /> He collaborated with Seven again to produce the [[promotional single]] "Better Together," in addition to other songs for the EP ''[[Digital Bounce]]'' (2010)<ref name=":1" /> and was featured in [[G-Dragon]]'s song "The Leaders."<ref name="The Leaders">According to the content on ''[[Heartbreaker (G-Dragon album)|Heartbreaker]]''{{'}}s album jacket.</ref> He also had a role in Taeyang's solo album [[Solar (Taeyang album)|''Solar'']], including the singles "Where U At" and "Wedding Dress."<ref name=":1" /> At the end of the year, Park also participated in the production of [[GD & TOP]]'s [[GD & TOP (album)|album]], and co-composed both "High High" and "Oh Yeah".<ref name=":1" />

In 2011, he wrote and produced 2NE1's second EP ''[[2NE1 (2011 EP)|''2NE1'']]'', scoring three number-one hits: "[[Lonely (2NE1 song)|Lonely]]", "[[I Am the Best]]", and "Ugly".

In the first half of 2013, Park produced [[Lee Hi]] hit single "Rose," 2NE1's leader CL's solo debut single "[[The Baddest Female]]," and [[Kang Seung-yoon]]'s YG debut "Wild & Young." The following year, he wrote and produced 2ne1's second and final studio album, [[Crush (2NE1 album)|Crush]].

In 2015, he wrote and produced [[Big Bang (band)|Big Bang]]'s special project single albums ''[[M (Big Bang album)|M]]'', ''[[A (Big Bang album)|A]]'', ''[[D (Big Bang album)|D]]'', and ''[[E (Big Bang album)|E]]'' which became major hits.

In 2016, Park began working with girl group [[Blackpink]] and produced their first two single albums ''[[Square One (single album)|Square One]]'' and ''[[Square Two]]'', consisting of number-one hit "[[Whistle (Blackpink song)|Whistle]]" and top-ten hits "[[Boombayah]]", "[[Playing with Fire (Blackpink song)|Playing with Fire]]", and "[[Stay (Blackpink song)|Stay]]".<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.inquisitr.com/3642984/han-ye-seul-and-teddy-park-breakup-k-drama-actress-of-madame-antoine-and-yg-entertainment-producer-call-it-quits-after-4-years/|title=Han Ye Seul And Teddy Park Breakup: K-Drama Actress Of 'Madame Antoine' And YG Entertainment Producer Call It Quits After 4 Years|last=Omega|first=Jan|date=October 26, 2016|work=The Inquisitr|access-date=July 9, 2018|language=en-US}}</ref> That same year, he founded [[The Black Label]], a [[subsidiary]] of [[YG Entertainment]].<ref name=":0" /> The label currently houses [[Zion.T]], Korea's second bestselling artist of 2015 after BigBang, and Korean-Canadian singer and former [[I.O.I]] member [[Jeon Somi]].

In 2017, Park wrote and produced the hit single "[[As If It's Your Last]]" for Blackpink. In 2018, he wrote and produced their debut EP, ''[[Square Up (EP)|Square Up]]'', including the number-one single "[[Ddu-Du Ddu-Du]]" as well as "[[Forever Young (Blackpink song)|Forever Young]]". Park also wrote and produced Blackpink member [[Jennie (singer)|Jennie]]'s number-one hit song "[[Solo (Jennie song)|Solo]]". Park produced for the first time for a non-YG Entertainment artist with "Just Dance," the theme song for the [[reality television]] competition ''[[Mix Nine]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://pop.heraldcorp.com/view.php?ud=201710290904101430349_1|title='믹스나인', 오늘(29일) 첫 방송..놓칠 수 없는 관전포인트 4|website=Herald Pop|date=October 29, 2017 |language=ko|access-date=January 26, 2018}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=http://entertain.naver.com/read?oid=311&aid=0000786328|title='믹스나인' 측 "테디가 대표곡 작업…쇼케서 첫 공개"|last=Park|first=So-hyun|date=October 20, 2017|access-date=July 9, 2018|language=ko}}</ref> Participants singing and dancing to the song was released<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.newsen.com/news_view.php?uid=201710291540050410|title=[TV와치]'믹스나인' 베일벗은 센터 누구? 온앤오프 효진X페이브 이수진에 쏠린 관심|last1=Hwang|first1=Hye-jin|website=Newsen|language=ko|access-date=January 26, 2018}}</ref> and three versions of the song were released overall: a male version led by Hyojin of [[ONF (band)|ONF]], a female version led by Lee Sujin from Fave Entertainment, and a co-ed version performed by both teams.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://osen.mt.co.kr/article/G1110779328|title=[공식입장] '믹스나인', 오늘 '저스트댄스' 男女 버전 음원+MV 공개|last1=이소담 기자|website=Osen|date=November 19, 2017 |language=ko|access-date=January 26, 2018}}</ref> He produced two songs for singer [[Sunmi]]: "[[Gashina]]" and "[[Heroine (Sunmi song)|Heroine]]".

In 2019, Park wrote and produced Blackpink's second EP, ''[[Kill This Love]]''. He also wrote and produced singer [[Jeon Somi]]'s debut single "[[Birthday (Jeon Somi song)|Birthday]]".

In 2020, Park played a major role in writing and producing Blackpink's debut studio album, ''[[The Album (Blackpink album)|The Album]]'', which consisted of the number-one song "[[How You Like That]]" as well as hit singles "[[Ice Cream (Blackpink and Selena Gomez song)|Ice Cream]]" and "[[Lovesick Girls]]".

In 2021, Park wrote and produced Blackpink members [[Rosé (singer)|Rosé]] and [[Lisa (rapper)|Lisa]]'s respective debut solo singles "[[On the Ground]]" and "[[Lalisa (song)|Lalisa]]". He also wrote and produced many of the songs on Jeon Somi's debut studio album ''[[XOXO (Jeon Somi album)|XOXO]]'', which included top-ten single "[[Dumb Dumb (Jeon Somi song)|Dumb Dumb]]".

== Musical style and influences ==
Park's production style often incorporates [[contemporary R&B]], especially when working with Taeyang and Seven.<ref name=":1" /> He also features [[reggae]] in his songs,<ref name=":1" /> acknowledging the genre's influence on 2NE1's [[2NE1 (2009 EP)|debut EP]].<ref name="2NE1 EP style">{{cite web |url=http://star.mt.co.kr/stview.php?no=2009070109164226325&type=3 |title=2NE1 첫 미니 타이틀 'I don't care' 1일 전격 공개 |trans-title=2NE1 first mini album titled 'I Don't Care', public blitz |language=ko |author=Moon, Hwa-sik |date=July 1, 2009 |access-date=November 6, 2010 |work=Star News |publisher=MoneyToday}}</ref> His work with 2NE1's first [[To Anyone|full-length album]] and [[2NE1 (2011 EP)|2011 EP]] contained songs that are [[pop music|pop]] and [[Dance music|dance]], in addition to featuring [[house music|house]] elements.<ref name="To Anyone style">{{cite web |url=http://star.mt.co.kr/stview.php?no=2010091013294498802&type=3 |title='컴백' 2NE1, 댄스 타이틀곡 '고 어웨이' 뮤비 공개 |trans-title='Comeback', 2NE1 dance song "Go Away" music video released |language=ko |author=Park, Yeong-ung |date=September 10, 2010 |access-date=November 6, 2010 |work=Star News |publisher=MoneyToday}}</ref> The song "[[Lonely (2NE1 song)|Lonely]]" by 2NE1 was praised for its [[Acoustic Song|acoustic]] sound.<ref name=":1" /> Park also experimented with [[electronic music]] when working with BigBang.<ref name=":1" />

At the end of 2009, 10Asia named Park as one of the top 10 people of 2009 for his participation in some of the year's biggest [[k-pop]] hits.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://omonatheydidnt.livejournal.com/2310526.html|title=top 10 people of 2009, according to 10Asia|access-date=June 24, 2018}}</ref> In 2018, ''Seoul Sports'' published a list of "Most Influential Power People of K-pop," which was ranked by music industry executives. Park placed fifth in the category of best producer.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.sportsseoul.com/news/read/649835|title=[창간특집 가요설문①]'파워피플' 방탄소년단, 최고 프로듀서 방시혁, 최고아이돌 방탄소년단-트와이스|last=Hong|first=Seung-han|date=June 22, 2018|work=스포츠서울|access-date=July 9, 2018|language=ko-KR}}</ref> That same year, he and [[G-Dragon]] tied for the most earned royalties for lyrics writing and song composition within the field of popular music from the Korean Music Copyright Association.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.yonhapnews.co.kr/bulletin/2018/02/21/0200000000AKR20180221182000005.HTML?input=1195m|title=지드래곤·테디, 작년 작사·작곡 저작권료 수입 1위|last=Lee|first=Eun Jung|date=February 21, 2018|work=[[Yonhap News]]|access-date=July 9, 2018|language=ko-KR}}</ref> Park gets paid about US$850,000 a year from the Korean Music Copyright Association.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.news1.kr/articles/?2029299|title=한예슬 연인 테디, 1년 저작권료만 9억원대 '저작권 재벌'|date=January 2, 2015}}</ref>

== Controversy ==
In 2018, there were concerns that [[Sunmi]]'s "[[Heroine (Sunmi song)|Heroine]]" has plagiarized [[Cheryl Cole|Cheryl]]'s debut solo single "[[Fight for This Love]]".<ref>{{cite magazine|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/k-town/8095083/sunmi-new-song-heroine-video-gashina-sequel|title=The Show Goes On With Sunmi's 'Gashina' Sequel 'Heroine' Despite Plagiarism Concerns|last=Herman|first=Tamar|date=January 19, 2018|magazine=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|publisher=[[Eldridge Industries]]|access-date=May 9, 2018}}</ref>
Sunmi's agency said that they were investigating the issue.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.koreaherald.com/view.php?ud=20180119000737|title=[Trending] #Sunmi #Teddy #Heroine #plagiarism|last=Park|first=Ju-young|date=January 19, 2018|website=[[The Korea Herald]]|access-date=May 9, 2018}}</ref> It later concluded with stating, "We unequivocally reveal that 'Heroine' is 100 percent an original creative work with absolutely no reference to the song that has been named in the controversy."<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.soompi.com/2018/01/19/black-label-responds-accusations-plagiarism-sunmis-heroine/|title=The Black Label Responds To Accusations Of Plagiarism In Sunmi's "Heroine"|author=J. == Personal life ==
After becoming an in-house producer for YG Entertainment, Park has refused to appear on the air. In 2014, he purchased a building in [[Hongdae, Seoul]] worth US$9 million and opened his own cafe called Twosome Studio in that building.<ref name=":0" /> In 2016, he purchased a US$6 million house in [[Hannam-dong|Hannam-dong, Seoul]] from [[Hyundai Group]] founder's nephew.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://superich.heraldcorp.com/view.php?ud=20170412000313&sec=01-74-01|title=한남동 '재벌 집' 67억에 산 YG테디, '자기자본'은 얼마나 들였을까|date=April 12, 2017}}</ref>
