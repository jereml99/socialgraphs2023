'''Psychosiz''' is an American [[video game designer]] and [[rapper]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://exclaim.ca/Reviews/HipHop/thirsty_fish-watergate|title=Thirsty Fish - Watergate|publisher=[[Exclaim!]]|first=Thomas|last=Quinlan|date=28 May 2011}}</ref> He is a member of hip hop groups [[Thirsty Fish]], Swim Team,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.urb.com/2009/05/06/mp3-swim-team-members-open-mike-eagle-dumbfoundead-sahtyre-and-alpha-mc/|title=MP3: Swim Team Members Open Mike Eagle, Dumbfoundead, Sahtyre, and Alpha MC|publisher=[[URB (magazine)|URB]]|date=6 May 2009|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20121021131938/http://www.urb.com/2009/05/06/mp3-swim-team-members-open-mike-eagle-dumbfoundead-sahtyre-and-alpha-mc/|archive-date=21 October 2012}}</ref> and Customer Service.<ref>{{cite book|editor1-first=Darnell|editor1-last=Hunt|editor2-first=Ana-Christina|editor2-last=Ramon|first=Jooyoung|last=Lee|chapter='Blowing Up' at Project Blowed: Rap Dreams and Young Black Men|title=Black Los Angeles: American Dreams and Racial Realities|page=129|publisher=[[New York University Press]]|year=2010}}</ref>

==Career==
Shortly after being introduced to [[Project Blowed]] by SP83, Psychosiz requested membership in the newly formed group Customer Service alongside SP83, Kail, Y-Not, JoeSue, A-ok, and group leader [[Nocando]].<ref name="noc">{{cite web|url=http://theurbandecadence.com/category/reviews/page/2/|title=Reviews « The Urban Decadence|publisher=Theurbandecadence.com|date=|access-date=4 February 2010|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120303041840/http://theurbandecadence.com/category/reviews/page/2/|archive-date=3 March 2012|url-status=dead}}</ref>

Psychosiz formed [[Thirsty Fish]] alongside [[Open Mike Eagle]] and [[Dumbfoundead]]. The trio released the debut album ''[[Testing the Waters]]'' in 2007.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://insidepulse.com/2008/04/01/mgf-presents-the-thirsty-fish-testing-the-waters-roundtable-review-extravaganza/|title=MGF Presents the Thirsty Fish Testing the Waters Roundtable Review Extravaganza!|publisher=Inside Pulse|first=J R.|last=Fernandez|date=1 April 2008}}</ref> The second album, ''[[Watergate (album)|Watergate]]'', was released on [[Mush Records]] in 2011.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.theaureview.com/albums/thirsty-fish-watergate-2011-lp-0|publisher=The AU Review|title=Thirsty Fish - Watergate (2011 LP)|first=James|last=O'Doherty|date=15 October 2011}}</ref>

Shortly after the formation of Thirsty Fish, Psychosiz expanded the roster to include several more Los Angeles rappers to the Project Blowed scene.<ref name="ome">{{cite web|url=http://theurbandecadence.com/2009/04/30/mike-eagle/|title=MIKE EAGLE. « The Urban Decadence|publisher=Theurbandecadence.com|date=30 April 2009|access-date=4 February 2010|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090626180612/http://theurbandecadence.com/2009/04/30/mike-eagle/|archive-date=26 June 2009|url-status=dead}}</ref>

Psychosiz has been featured on several solo projects by Swim Team members including Sahtyre's debut album ''High Saht''.<ref name="saht">{{cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/high-saht/id307844419 |title=High Saht by Sahtyre - Download High Saht on iTunes|publisher=[[iTunes]]|date=14 March 2009|access-date=4 February 2010}}</ref>
